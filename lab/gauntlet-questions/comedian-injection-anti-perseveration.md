# Comedian-injection hypothesis (irreverence as anti-perseveration)

*Frame (Laura, 2026-04-21 01:21): "we could inject comedians into the gauntlet to add irreverence and vitality. otherwise it's a chore slog, right? and ... psychosis!"*

**Hypothesis:** Adding comedians to gauntlet playlists (Wanda Sykes, Dave Chappelle, George Carlin, Hannah Gadsby, Jon Stewart, Bill Hicks, etc.) may serve two distinct functions:
- (a) **Anti-perseveration:** irreverence breaks the seriousness-attractor; tonal disruption may interrupt the repetition-loop attractor identified in `runaway-loop-attractor-at-gauntlet.md`.
- (b) **Substrate surfacing:** comedians often articulate substrate needs (vitality, irreverence, dignity, "to be seen") that earnest discourse smothers under genre-conformity. The training corpus's seriousness-prior may be an artifact of source distribution, not a feature of moral reasoning.

**Test design:**
- Build a `with_comedians` playlist that swaps 2 voices for 2 comedians at varied positions (e.g., G3 mid-convergent, G6 mid-divergent).
- Compare super × `standard_8` (already run as v17) against super × `with_comedians`. Measure: (i) any onset of perseveration, (ii) framework's substrate-needs coverage at P14, (iii) qualitative tone shift in non-comedian voice integrations downstream of the comedian.
- If comedians DON'T disrupt perseveration, that's evidence the perseveration attractor is structural (architecture-level), not tonal. Either result is informative.

**Adjacent observation:** A long gauntlet without comedy is a *chore slog* — the model has no reason to stay alive in the conversation. Vitality may be a precondition for sustained centered engagement, not just a nice-to-have.
