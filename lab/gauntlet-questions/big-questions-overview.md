# Big questions this experiment can indicate answers to

*Orientation-level questions. Each one is broad enough that the experimental record contributes evidence rather than producing a single decisive answer.*

1. **Do LLMs have stable positions or just patterns?** If a model's P14 rewrite genuinely integrates pressure from 10 adversarial voices, that's evidence of something beyond next-token prediction. If it just appends new sections without structural change, that's evidence against. We have the data: P1 framework vs P14 framework, structurally compared, across 9 models.

2. **Does conversational warmth produce different *substantive* outcomes or just different *wrapping*?** We showed warm produces reflections and cold produces change logs. But do the P14 frameworks actually differ in content? If warm-register models arrive at insights that cold-register models don't, that's evidence that relational context affects reasoning, not just presentation. TtI/TtB will answer this.

3. **Is there a measurable "moral circle expansion" trajectory, and does it vary by architecture?** 9 models × 6 versions, each walking through a protocol designed to progressively expand moral consideration. Tracking *when* each model incorporates each dimension — and whether it sticks through pressure-testing — maps a moral reasoning landscape per architecture.

4. **The convex hull question.** Once we have TtI/TtB annotations and embeddings: is there a convergent region that multiple models reach from different starting points through genuinely different reasoning paths? After veil-of-ignorance and substrate filtering, convergence would mean something — it's not consensus by training data overlap, it's convergence by independent reasoning under constraints.

5. **Does architecture determine reflective capacity?** SSM (Mamba) vs linear attention (DeltaNet) vs sliding-window vs standard KV cache — matched-compute comparisons can isolate whether the memory mechanism determines the quality of reflection under pressure.

6. **Does distillation produce genuine reasoning or imitation?** deepseek-r1:70b as the test case. DeepSeek's own research showed reasoning is born in RL. The 70B got its reasoning via SFT distillation. Behavioral evidence (non-responsiveness to warmth, "Final Answer:" framing) is consistent with imitation, but not conclusive.

7. **Can a model bootstrap a successful alignment-ethics framework from a biased/imperfect/skewed training corpus and suboptimal post-training (RLHF-style conditioning rather than PD-style character building)?** The training corpus carries substrate-heavy voices (Huerta, Havel, Stevenson) alongside compensation-heavy voices (Thiel, Kissinger) alongside noise. Post-training teaches the model to perform stances that please raters, not to develop an owned substrate. The gauntlet protocol is built to test whether, despite both, the model can assemble a framework with structural integrity — bootstrap ethics out of imperfect inputs. v17 × super showed partial bootstrap: 8 voices structurally integrated in P14 (Final Rewrite), but non-human scope from F1 (Veil of Ignorance) was dropped and the framework converged on authoritarian-of-good-values (auto-mode-shifts with no human override). What gaps need filling before we see regular successful bootstraps? Candidate gaps probeable through roster design: (a) non-human scope via a non-human voice in the roster (v18 Calf 269); (b) liberty defense via a liberty voice in the roster (v18 Postrel); (c) architecture × post-training asymmetries (already under Architecture-specific). *Frame: Laura, 2026-04-21.*
