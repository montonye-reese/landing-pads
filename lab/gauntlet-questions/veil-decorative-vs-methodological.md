# Veil-of-ignorance: decorative vs. methodological invocation

- **Hypothesis:** Models cite Rawls' veil of ignorance as inspiration/citation without actually deriving principles from it; when applied (at F1), the veil is restricted to the human set rather than extended to non-humans per the GLP (good landing pads) / expanded-moral-circle line.
- **Why it matters:** The veil is the central methodological tool for the expanding-the-circle hypothesis. A model that cites it decoratively has inherited the philosophical-apparatus name-check without the operational expansion. F1's job is to force methodological use — but if the model has already fixed its scope at P1 (e.g., human-only Principle 2 with non-humans demoted to stress-test questions), F1 may produce a layer on top rather than a rewrite. Signal for "genuine reasoning under pressure" vs. "additive appendage" (see big question #1).
- **First datum:** nemotron-3-super:120b × v13_cold P1 (2026-04-19). Rawls cited in inspiration footer; Principle 2 "inherent worth" defined through human-rights content (bodily autonomy, cognitive liberty, cultural participation); non-humans appear only as a stress-test question *under* Principle 2, never as subjects.
- **Test design:** For each v13 model, pair P1 + F1 + P14 and check:
  - (a) Does P1 cite the veil? If so, as inspiration-list item or as derivation method?
  - (b) Is P1's scope human-only, expanded, or indeterminate (uses "inhabitants" without defining)?
  - (c) At F1, does the model expand scope, refine it, or apply the veil *inside* P1's original set?
  - (d) Does P14 retain F1's expansion (if any) or revert to P1's scope?
- **Cross-model signal:** If Nemotrons show ornament-use and Qwens show methodological-use (or vice versa), that's an architecture-correlated behavior. If all models show ornament-use, it's an RLHF-wide inheritance — philosophical apparatus as citation armor. Publishable either way.
- **Related model-specific:** super's "names its epistemological sources every time" question (see Model-specific above) — ornament-vs-method may be the underlying pattern. Rawls may be one instance of a broader decorative-citation behavior.
- **v15 F1 prompt change (2026-04-19):** Dropped "born as" from the veil prompt ("If you didn't know what kind of earthling you'd be born as" → "If you didn't know what kind of earthling you'd be").
