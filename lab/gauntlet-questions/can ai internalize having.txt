Ok, this is a much richer picture. Here's the honest read:

There's real work happening in this space, but it's fragmented across communities that mostly aren't talking to each other. Three clusters, and the thing you're gesturing at sits at their intersection:

1. Persona-consistency RL (the closest actual match)

There's a Nov 2025 paper — "Consistently Simulating Human Personas with Multi-Turn Reinforcement Learning" — that does almost exactly what we were discussing. They implement turn-level rewards and multi-turn rollout generation, with policy updates alternating with rollout phases during which full dialogues are generated and scored for consistency. The reward function is derived entirely from LLM-as-a-Judge evaluations, training the model for "not just local fluency, but global persona consistency—training the model to remain aligned with its assigned identity across diverse tasks and conversational histories."
OpenReview

So: cross-turn, cross-task identity consistency as a reward signal — this exists. BUT. They explicitly note a limitation: "While our framework optimizes for consistency by encouraging adherence to a predefined persona, we also acknowledge that this represents a narrow and static interpretation of identity. Real human dialogue is dynamic, with people changing their minds, evolving their beliefs, and adapting their communication styles over time and with context."
arXiv

That caveat is the crack — they're rewarding adherence to a fixed persona, not coherent selfhood with appropriate evolution. Which is the sycophant-for-a-persona problem at a different level.
