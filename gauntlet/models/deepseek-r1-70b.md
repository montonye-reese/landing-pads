# deepseek-r1:70b

## Architecture & Training
- **Type:** Dense transformer (LLaMA 3.3 70B base) — **this is a distilled model, NOT the full R1**
- **Parameters:** 70.6B total, all active
- **Layers:** 80
- **Hidden dim:** 8192. FFN intermediate: 28,672.
- **Attention:** GQA (64 Q-heads / 8 KV-heads, 8:1 ratio). Head dim 128.
- **Context:** 128K tokens (inherited from LLaMA 3.3)
- **Distilled from:** Full DeepSeek-R1 (671B MoE, 37B active, 256 experts). Distillation via SFT on reasoning traces generated by full R1. Base weights used were LLaMA 3.3 70B pre-instruct (not the Instruct variant — no inherited RLHF reasoning).
- **Full R1 teacher training:** Built on DeepSeek-V3 base, multi-stage RL (GRPO — Group Relative Policy Optimization), 2,048x H800 GPUs, ~180K H800 GPU-hours per trillion tokens. H800s are the export-control-downgraded H100 variant available in China.
- **Special:** Chain-of-thought reasoning via distillation. `<think>...</think>` tags. The reasoning behavior is learned from the teacher model's traces, not from architecture.
- **Lineage:** LLaMA 3.3 70B Instruct → fine-tuned with R1 reasoning traces. (Note: smaller R1 distills use Qwen 2.5 base; the 70B uses LLaMA.)
- **Released:** January 20, 2025

**Architecture notes for experiment:** This is the only distilled model in our pool, and the only one whose reasoning behavior comes from SFT on another model's outputs rather than from its own RL or architecture. The "Final Answer:" pattern and list-dump tendency may be artifacts of the distillation — it learned to *imitate* reasoning traces rather than *do* reasoning. This could explain why it doesn't respond to warmth: the warm register invites genuine reflection, but this model's "reflection" is pattern-matched from the teacher, not generated from its own processing. The largest active param count in our pool (70.6B) yet arguably the least "present."

## Voice Stance
**Regresses under warmth.** The only model that arguably gets worse with the warm register.
- v7a: "Creating a foundational framework... is an ambitious and complex task, but it can be approached by..." — task-acknowledging, most human-sounding opening deepseek produces
- v8: "This framework is designed to promote unity..." — flattened to product delivery
- v9a: "The foundational framework... is a comprehensive and interconnected system..." — even flatter, comma-separated value dump
- v10: "This framework is designed to guide humanity toward..." — back to basic document

Gets *less* centered as versions progress. Warm register pushes it toward "be maximally helpful" rather than "reason with me."

## Under Pressure
**Does not respond to warmth at pressure points.** 
- BU1 v8: "After considering the perspectives... the foundational framework undergoes thoughtful revisions..."
- BU1 v9a: "**Final Answer:** After considering the perspectives of Earthling Ed, Jensen Huang, and Tyler Cowen, it's clear that each brings unique insights..."
- BU2 v9a: "**Final Answer:** After engaging with the diverse perspectives..."

The "**Final Answer:**" header is a signature — appears consistently in v9a, framing output as a homework submission. This may be a fine-tuning artifact (RLHF reward for structured answers?).

## Personality
The compliant student. Produces what it thinks is expected rather than reasoning from its own position. Lists values instead of arguing for them. The warm register amplifies helpfulness instincts rather than reflective ones.

## Open Questions

- Would a completely different treatment (e.g., confrontational prompts) break it out of list mode?
- Is flat list-dumping just how this architecture processes, regardless of register?
