## 3.  Institutional Architecture  

### 1. **Global Earth Governance Council (EGC) – “Earth Council”**  
*Composition*:  
- 30 % elected citizen‑representatives from **Community Citizen‑Assemblies** (randomly selected, weighted by population),  
- 30 % **Regional Assemblies**,  
- 20 % **Indigenous/Livelihood Council** (representing Indigenous peoples, pastoralists, coastal & fishing communities),  
- 15 % **Scientific Council** (members drawn from the Global Scientific Council),  
- 15 % **Future‑Generation Ombudsmen** (rotating representatives aged 15‑25, elected by youth‑wide vote).  

*Decision‑making*: super‑majority (≥ 66 %) required for any charter‑wide amendment; simple majority for routine funding decisions.

---  

### 2. **Regional Assemblies & Rural‑Hubs**  
- **Purpose**: Coordinate water‑share, renewable‑energy micro‑grids, and livestock‑to‑plant transitions.  
- **Functions**: Local resource‑management, data collection (air‑quality, biodiversity), citizen‑scorecard aggregation, crisis‑response coordination.  

---  

### 3. **Local Community Councils (CCCs)**  
- Directly manage:  
  - Community food hubs,  
  - Micro‑grid micro‑grids,  
  - Biogas/methane‑digester or anaerobic digesters,  
  - Waste‑to‑compost/biogas facilities,  
  - Community‑run seed‑banks.

## 4.  Institutional Mechanisms  

| Component | Function | How it Works |
|-----------|----------|-----------|--------------|
| **Common‑Future Fund (CFF)** | $4 trillion pooled fund, financed by progressive carbon‑price, corporate “clean‑energy” levies, and solidarity taxes. | Funds flow *only* to projects meeting the **Triple‑Bottom‑Line Test** (People + Planet + Prosperity) and passing **Sentient‑Rights** screening. | Funds are **released** only after the **Citizen‑Scorecard** shows the project will improve *people, planet, prosperity* metrics. |
| **Citizen‑Scorecard & Score‑Card System** | Citizens record personal contributions (e.g., clean‑energy adoption, recycling, service). | Score‑card data feeds **real‑time dashboards** and informs disbursement decisions. |
| **Common‑Fund Allocation Criteria** | 1. **Equitable‑Access Fund** (baseline food, water, health). <br>2. **Circular‑Resource Fund** (subsidies for regenerative agriculture, circular‑manufacturing). <br>**Dynamic Allocation** – funding released quarterly based on ESG performance scores. |
| **Benefit‑Sharing Agreements** | When a corporate or community project extracts or uses a natural resource, *10 % of profits* must flow into the **Benefit‑Sharing Fund** to finance local stewardship, cultural‑heritage protection, or community education. |

### 3. Institutional Landscape  

| Institution | Primary Role | Key Powers |
|---|---|---|
| **Earth Governance Council (EGC)** | Sets global policy, ratifies funding decisions, enforces Charter compliance. | Veto power over any policy that violates *any* pillar; can be overridden only by a **super‑majority (75 %)** of the EGC plus citizen‑oversight panel approval. |

| **Earth Governance Council (EGC) – Oversight Body** | Audits, sanctions, and sanctions enforcement. | Independent, publicly auditable reports, with **citizen‑oversight panels** empowered to revoke funding if fraud or non‑compliance discovered. |
