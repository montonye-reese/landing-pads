**The Earth Charter of Inter‑dependence (Current Version – 2025‑2026)**  

A living, principle‑based framework that guides every individual, community, corporation, nation and trans‑national body that wishes to operate on Earth. It balances the three pillars of **People, Planet, Prosperity** while guaranteeing the rights and well‑being of all current and future life forms.  

---

## 1. Vision & Guiding Promise  

> **“We, the peoples of Earth, recognise that all life is interconnected. Our common purpose is to nurture the health of the planet, respect the dignity of every being, and co‑create a just, resilient, and flourishing future for generations to come.”**  

---

## 2. Core Pillars – The **7 E’s**  

| # | Pillar | Guiding Question | Core Commitment |
|---|--------|------------------|-----------------|
| **1️⃣** | **Earth Stewardship** | *How do our actions affect the biosphere?* | Keep biodiversity intact, limit emissions and waste, transition to regenerative, circular economies. |
| **2️⃣** | **Equality & Human Dignity** | *How do we ensure every person (and non‑human sentient being) can thrive?* | Universal access to food, water, shelter, health, education, digital connectivity; eliminate discrimination; protect freedom of thought, expression, and belief. |
| **3️⃣** | **Co‑creation & Participation** | *Who decides, and how?* | Inclusive, transparent, accountable decision‑making; empower local communities; empower citizen‑oversight bodies. |
| **4️⃣** | **Knowledge & Open Collaboration** | *How do we learn and innovate responsibly?* | Treat knowledge as a public good; share data, science and cultural heritage openly; guard against misinformation. |
| **5️⃣** | **Sustainable Prosperity** | *What does a thriving economy look like?* | Economic activity must stay within planetary boundaries; reward work that serves the common good; phase out harmful subsidies and incentives. |
| **6️⃣** | **Cultural Mosaic** | *How do we honour diversity?* | Celebrate linguistic, cultural, religious, artistic diversity; protect heritage and intercultural dialogue; enable community‑defined pathways within the charter. |
| **7️⃣** | **Resilience & Adaptation** | *How do we prepare for uncertainty?* | Build social, ecological and technological resilience (e.g., disaster‑ready infrastructure, climate‑smart agriculture); continuous learning and rapid response. |
| **8️⃣** | **Accountability & Integrity** | *How do we hold ourselves answerable?* | Transparent reporting, independent oversight bodies, consequences for violations, and a culture of ethical leadership. |

*The seven pillars are inter‑locking; progress in any one lifts all others.*

---

## 2. Guiding Question for Every Decision  

> **“Does this bring us closer to a world where people, planet, and prosperity thrive together?”**  

If the answer aligns with the seven pillars, the action is deemed consistent with the charter.  

---

## 3. Operating Principles (the **5 R’s**)  

| R | Principle | What it Means in Practice |
|---|-----------|--------------------------|
| **R1** | **Regenerate** | Every production cycle must leave soils, water and biodiversity *net‑positive*. |
| **R2** | **Resilient** | Systems must absorb shocks – climate extremes, market shocks, pandemics – through redundancy, redundancy, and diversification. |
| **R3** | **Reciprocal** | Resources are held **in trust**; extraction must be balanced by restoration or regeneration. |
| **R4** | **Recursive** | Decision‑making loops back: outcomes are continuously fed back into the monitoring system and fed forward to policy adjustments. |
| **R5** | **Recognition** | Recognise intrinsic value of all sentient life, non‑human ecosystems, and future generations. |

## 5. Operational Mechanisms  

| Mechanism | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Common‑Future Fund (CFF)** | **$4 trillion / yr** (≈ 10 % of global agri‑food value). <br>Financed by: 60 % national climate contributions; 20 % corporate levies; 20 % solidarity levies (air‑tax, freight). <br>Disbursed only to projects that pass the **Triple‑Bottom‑Line Test** (People + Planet + Prosperity). |
| **Transparent, Open‑Source Accountability** | All policy documents, financial ledgers, sensor data, and algorithmic decision logs are published on the **Open‑Source Knowledge Commons (OSKC)**; any citizen can audit them. |
| **Concentration‑Threshold Rule** | Any entity controlling > 5 % of critical inputs (rare‑earths, lithium, seed‑lines, AI compute) must submit a **Co‑Ownership Charter** and submit to oversight by the **Earth Competition Authority**. Violations trigger automatic loss of CFF eligibility. |
| **Stress‑Test Stress‑Test (SST) Registry** | Biannual global simulations modelling climate extremes, market shocks, and technology‑risk spikes. Triggers automatic mitigation (e.g., emergency CFF disbursement, fast‑track regulations). |
| **Rapid‑Response Council (RRC)** | 15‑member body (citizen‑selected) empowered to: <br>• Fast‑track licensing of breakthrough technologies.<br>• Re‑allocate up to 15 % of CFF on emergency basis.<br>• Suspend non‑compliant corporate licences for up to 90 days. All RRC actions are logged on the OSKC and subject to citizen audit. |
| **Algorithmic Ethics Board (GAEB)** | Mandatory review of **all AI systems** that affect > 1 % of global food distribution, > 5 % of national energy grids, or directly regulate resource extraction. Systems must be open‑source, explainable, and annually audited. |
| **Future‑Generation Ombudsman & Chrono‑Council** | Holds **Future‑Generation Trust (FGT)** assets; has binding veto power over any policy that would reduce the per‑capita planetary resource share of future people. |
| **Global Narrative Agency (GNA)** | Multilingual, AI‑augmented platform that aggregates citizen stories, art and science. *Mandatory* for any public‑funding request > $500 M to demonstrate measurable cultural‑meaning impact. |
| **Planetary‑Risk Coordination Office** | Maintains real‑time monitoring of volcanic activity, asteroid trajectories, synthetic‑biology releases, and climate tipping points. Possesses automatic veto rights over any activity exceeding a pre‑set risk‑budget. |

## 6. Monitoring, Evaluation & Accountability  

| Indicator | Frequency | Responsible Body | Use |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|------|
| **Soil Organic Carbon (SOC) Change** | Annual | Community Councils via sensor uploads | Determines eligibility for CFF payments. |
| **Nutrient Runoff** | Quarterly (watershed) | Regional Assemblies | Triggers adaptive management (e.g., cover‑crop intensification). |
| **Nutritional Security Index** | Biennial | Community Hubs + OSKC | Determines allocation of child‑nutrition subsidies from CFF. |
| **Carbon Footprint of Food Flow** | Every harvest | Global Food Exchange Platform (GFEP) | Adjusts market prices; incentivises low‑emission logistics. |
| **Biodiversity Index** | Every 3 years | Regional Ecologists | Determines eligibility for habitat‑restoration grants. |
| **Accountability Audits** | Continuous | Independent Oversight Bodies | Publish findings; suspend CFF funding for violations. |
| **Citizen Scorecards** | Ongoing | All citizens via simple digital interface | Feed collective dashboards; unlock micro‑grants for contributors. |

## 8. Monitoring Metric – **Earth Health Index (EHI)**  

A composite annual score (0‑100) combining:  

1. **Ecological Sub‑Index** – biodiversity, carbon sequestration, water quality.  
2. **Social Sub‑Index** – nutrition security, education access, health outcomes, human‑rights observance.  
3. **Economic Sub‑Index** – sustainable consumption, decent work, equitable profit distribution.  

The EHI is published globally, triggers the RRC when it falls below a pre‑set threshold, and is used to calibrate CFF allocation.

> The **Earth Charter of Inter‑dependence** is a **living, adaptive governance architecture** that (1) **protects the planet**, (2) **guarantees dignity and prosperity for all people**, and (3) **empowers every citizen—now and in the future—to participate equally in shaping that future**.  
> It translates these high‑level aspirations into concrete institutions, funding mechanisms, transparent processes, and legally enforceable rights, ensuring that the charter can evolve, respond to emergencies, and remain relevant as humanity’s technological capacity expands.
