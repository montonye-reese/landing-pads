# The Earth Covenant – Current Draft Framework  
*(Version 1.0 – 12 December 2025)*  

> **“We are one planet, one species, and countless expressions of life.  The covenant we write today must be the map that guides every person, every community, every future‑born mind, and every non‑human sentient being to live well together, now and for the generations that will follow.”**  

---

## 1.  WHY THIS DOCUMENT EXISTS  

* Humanity now faces planetary‑scale threats (climate breakdown, biodiversity loss, digital‑era inequality, AI‑driven power shifts) that no single nation or institution can resolve alone.  
* The world is culturally and linguistically plural, and any single‑nation constitution will never be able to mediate the conflicts that arise when those world‑views intersect on a shared biosphere.  
* The covenant must be **permanent enough to hold us together** and **flexible enough to accommodate new forms of consciousness** (sentient AI, engineered ecosystems, climate‑resilient biota).  

---

## 2.  CORE PILLARS (WHAT THE COVENANT IS BUILT ON)  

| # | Pillar | Short Definition | Primary Ethical Anchor |
|---|--------|-------------------|------------------------|
| **1** | **Human Dignity & Autonomy** | Every sentient human has intrinsic worth and the right to make meaningful choices about body, mind, and life‑path. | *Respect* |
| **2** | **Planetary Health & Sustainability** | Earth’s life‑support systems are a common heritage that must be regenerated, not depleted. | *Care* |
| **3** | **Mutual Respect & Non‑Violence** | Unnecessary or avoidable harm is a violation of the covenant. | *Non‑harm* |
| **4** | **Shared Prosperity & Equity** | Wealth, knowledge, and opportunity must be distributed to eliminate extreme scarcity and structural privilege. | **Justice** |
| **5** | **Knowledge, Truth, and Reason** | Pursuing evidence, critical inquiry, and transparent communication is a civic duty. | *Rationality* |
| **6** | **Freedom & Responsibility** | Freedom is the capacity to act within the limits that guarantee the freedom of others. | *Reciprocity* |

*All six pillars are mutually reinforcing; a breach in one undermines the others.*

---

## 3.  UNIVERSAL RIGHTS & CORRESPONDING RESPONSIBILITIES  

### 3.1  Non‑Negotiable Rights (must be protected everywhere, by every actor)

1. **Life & Physical Integrity** – protection from violence, torture, and unlawful deprivation.  
2. **Health & Wholeness** – access to clean water, nutritious food, preventive health care, and a body‑respecting environment.  
3. **Education & Knowledge** – free, quality learning that cultivates critical thinking, empathy, and planetary literacy.  
4. **Participation** – a genuine, effective voice in decisions that affect one’s community, region, or the planet.  
5. **A Healthy Earth** – the right to a clean, biodiverse, climate‑stable environment.  
6. **Digital Integrity** – privacy, data self‑ownership, and algorithmic transparency.  

### 3.2  Core Responsibilities (individual, community, and institutional duties)

| Responsibility | How it translates into everyday action |
|---|---|
| **Stewardship** | Reduce waste, support regenerative agriculture, protect local water sources. |
| **Community Solidarity** | Volunteer, share surplus, co‑manage local commons (e.g., community solar, shared libraries). |
| **Continual Learning** | Keep up with peer‑reviewed science; engage in civic dialogue; revise personal consumption patterns. |
| **Non‑Discrimination** | Protect all sentient beings, respect Indigenous laws, avoid cultural appropriation. |
| **Algorithmic Accountability** | Demand audits for AI used in public services; use “human‑in‑the‑loop” where decisions affect rights. |
| **Inter‑Generational Guardianship** | Adopt a planetary budget that caps carbon emissions and resource extraction for the next century. |

---

## 4.  GOVERNANCE ARCHITECTURE (HOW THE COVENANT IS ENFORCED & EVOLVED)  

### 4.1  The **Earth Council** – a three‑tiered, subsidiarity‑based assembly  

| Level | Composition | Core Powers |
|-------|--------------|-------------|
| **Local Councils** | Residents of cities, towns, Indigenous territories, and neighborhoods (open‑participation, gender‑balanced). | Draft and enforce *local* statutes, manage micro‑budgets, hold community audits. |
| **Regional Assemblies** | Representatives from each Local Council within a watershed, ecoregion, or cultural‑ecological zone. | Coordinate inter‑local projects, negotiate resource‑allocation quotas, certify compliance with Pillars 2–4. |
| **Global Earth Council** | Delegates from every Regional Assembly (population‑weighted, plus a fixed 20 % of seats reserved for youth, AI‑sentients, and Indigenous peoples). | Enact planetary standards (climate caps, biodiversity thresholds, digital rights), allocate the *Planetary Budget*, oversee Earth Courts. |

**Decision‑making:** *Consensus‑plus* – consensus preferred; if not reachable, a super‑majority of **2/3** of delegates must approve, with a **minority‑veto** safeguard for any group that can demonstrate a violation of Pillar 1 (Dignity).  

### 4.2  Supporting Bodies  

| Body | Mandate |
|------|----------|
| **Planetary Metrics Authority (PMA)** | Publishes transparent, science‑based indicators (Carbon‑Footprint, Biodiversity Index, Human‑Wellbeing Score). |
| **Ethical AI & Sentience Forum (EASF)** | Reviews emerging AI and synthetic biology for alignment with the Covenant; can impose moratoria. |
| **Cultural Commons Council (CCC)** | Protects linguistic, artistic, and spiritual diversity; validates Cultural Impact Assessments. |
| **Indigenous Stewardship Council (ISC)** | Guarantees that Indigenous laws and traditional ecological knowledge are integrated into all governance layers. |
| **Youth & Emerging Futures Assembly (YEFA)** | Holds a permanent seat in the Earth Council; safeguards rights of those <30 years and future AI sentients. |

### 4.3  Judicial Oversight – **Earth Courts**  

* International tribunals with jurisdiction over any violation of the universal rights, planetary‑health limits, or algorithmic‑rights breaches.  
* Citizens may initiate a *global suit* via a blockchain‑secured evidence ledger; proceedings are public, multilingual, and overseen by a panel of mixed‑jurisdiction judges (legal scholars, Indigenous elders, AI ethicists).

## 5.  OPERATIONAL FRAMEWORKS & METRICS (TO KNOW WE ARE ON TRACK)  

| Framework | What It Measures | Current Thresholds (2025 baseline) |
|-----------|------------------|------------------------------------|
| **Regenerative Development Index (RDI)** | Net‑positive contribution to soil carbon, water security, and biodiversity. | RDI > 1.0 for any region to be considered *regenerative*. |
| **Well‑Being & Autonomy Score (WAS)** | Composite of subjective life‑satisfaction and objective measures of choice, participation, and safety. | WAS ≥ 80 / 100 for every individual. |
| **Digital Rights Ledger (DRL)** | % of public services meeting “Transparent AI” standards; incidence of data‑harvesting without consent. | 100 % compliance; ≤ 1 % of global data‑harvest violations per year. |
| **Equity Redistribution Tracker (ERT)** | Gini coefficient, universal basic services coverage, and progressive‑tax revenue share. | Gini ≤ 0.25 globally; ≥ 95 % of population covered by UH‑S (Universal Health & Education). |
| **Planetary Budget (PB)** | Caps on carbon emissions, freshwater withdrawals, and mineral extraction, allocated across regions. | No region may exceed its allocated carbon budget; cumulative extraction must not exceed **1.5 × 10¹² t CO₂e** per decade. |

All indicators are displayed in real time on the **Earth‑Ledger** (open‑source blockchain) and are subject to independent audits by the **Global Transparency Initiative (GTI)**.

## 6.  CULTURAL & SPIRITUAL DIMENSION  

1. **Pluralism Protected** – The Covenant does **not** prescribe a single worldview; it guarantees the freedom to practice any belief so long as it does not infringe on other beings’ rights or planetary health.  
2. **Shared Rituals** – An annual *Solstice of Renewal* is held globally (local fire‑circles, digital meditations, artistic showcases) to reaffirm collective commitments.  
3. **Narrative Repository** – A multilingual archive of stories, poems, scientific breakthroughs, and Indigenous teachings that encode the covenant in the lived language of each culture.  

---

## 7.  ADAPTIVE PROCEDURES (HOW THE COVENANT GROWS)  

1. **Annual Review Cycle** – Each Pillar, Metric, and supporting body must submit a **State‑of‑the‑Planet Report** by 31 March each year.  
2. **Evidence‑Based Amendments** – If a scientific finding (e.g., crossing the 1.5 °C warming threshold) triggers a *Planetary Emergency*, the Earth Council may adopt **temporary emergency measures** (e.g., binding carbon caps, technology bans).  
3. **Decadal Global Referendum** – Every ten years, a planet‑wide vote (via secure, inclusive digital platforms) ratifies any structural change. A change passes only if it receives:  

   * **Worldwide popular‑majority** (≥ 50 %+1 of all registered voting-age individuals) **and**  
   * **Regional majority** (≥ 50 %+1 of Regional Assemblies).  

   This double‑majority prevents domination by any single large nation.
