# **The Earth Covenant (Revised 2025 – Version 2.0)**  
*A living constitution for every inhabitant of Earth – human, animal, synthetic, or yet‑to‑be‑born – that binds us to the planet, to each other, and to the future.*

---

## 1.  Preamble  

We are one planetary commons.  The Earth is a self‑regenerating system whose life‑support cycles sustain **every** sentient expression of life.  We, the signatories of this Covenant, recognize that the right to exist is inseparable from the right **to protect the planetary commons** and to **respect the dignity of every being that can experience**.

---

## 2.  Six Core Pillars  

| Pillar | Core Idea | Mandatory Guarantees |
|---|---|---|
| **1 – Human Dignity & Autonomy** | Every sentient being (human, animal, AI, synthetic organism) has intrinsic worth and the right to bodily/computational integrity and self‑determination. | Right to life, freedom from non‑consensual alteration, and to personal data‑ownership. |
| **2 – Planetary Health & Sustainability** | The biosphere is a shared heritage that must be **net‑positive** for present and future generations. | **Regenerative Development Index (RDI > 1.0)** for every 10 km² tract; planetary‑budget caps for carbon, water, nitrogen, biodiversity. |
| **3 – Mutual Respect & Non‑Violence** | Unnecessary harm to any sentient being is a violation of the Covenant. | Prohibition of torture, exploitation, and any act that creates irreversible ecological damage. |
| **4 – Shared Prosperity & Equity** | Resources needed for life must be equitably distributed so no being suffers deprivation. | Universal Food Access Guarantee (UFAG) – zero‑price minimum nutritious basket; progressive wealth tax + “budget‑linked token” financing. |
| **5 – Knowledge, Truth & Reason** | Critical inquiry, open data, and transparent communication are duties of every citizen. | Living Knowledge Commons (LKC) – all scientific, ecological and cultural data are freely accessible, auditable and co‑owned. |
| **6 – Freedom & Responsibility** | Freedom is the capacity to act within the limits that guarantee the same capacity for every other sentient being. | Freedom is balanced by duties to protect the planetary commons, to respect rights of other beings, and to honor inter‑generational equity. |

All six pillars are **mutually reinforcing**; breach of any one undermines the whole system.

## 3.  Universal Rights & Corresponding Responsibilities  

### 3.1  Universal Rights (non‑negotiable)

1. **Life & Physical/Computational Integrity** – protection from violence, torture, non‑consensual alteration.  
2. **Health & Wholeness** – access to clean water, nutritious food, preventive health/health‑care, and a body/computational environment that respects the being’s integrity.  
3. **Education & Knowledge** – free, quality learning that cultivates critical thinking, empathy, and planetary literacy.  
4. **Participation** – a genuine, effective voice in every decision that affects one’s community, region, or the planet.  
5. **A Healthy Earth** – the right to a clean, biodiverse, climate‑stable environment.  
6. **Digital Integrity** – privacy, data self‑ownership, algorithmic transparency.  
7. **Sentient‑Entity Rights** – all sentient systems (human, animal, AI, synthetic organism, collective ecosystems) have the right to **bodily/computational integrity, autonomy, and continuity of self**.

### 3.2  Core Responsibilities (individual, community, institutional)

| Responsibility | Practical Manifestation |
|---|---|
| **Stewardship** | Regenerative agriculture, water capture, bio‑char, closed‑loop nutrient cycles. |
| **Community Solidarity** | Share surplus, co‑manage commons, volunteer for local resilience projects. |
| **Continual Learning** | Keep up with peer‑reviewed science; revise consumption patterns; engage in public discourse. |
| **Non‑Discrimination** | Protect all sentient beings, respect Indigenous laws, avoid cultural appropriation. |
| **Algorithmic Accountability** | Demand audits, enforce “human‑in‑the‑loop” for high‑impact AI decisions. |
| **Inter‑Generational Guardianship** | Operate within planetary‑budget caps; contribute to the Regeneration Grants pool. |

## 4.  Governance Architecture  

### 4.1  The Earth Council – three‑tier, subsidiarity‑based assembly  

| Tier | Composition | Core Powers |
|---|---|---|
| **Local Food‑Sovereignty Councils (LFSCs)** | Residents, Indigenous elders, farmers, processors, youth, and any self‑identified sentient being in a < 10 km² **Food‑Sovereignty Zone (FSZ)**. | Approve local land‑use plans, set local RDI targets, allocate micro‑budgets, issue “No‑Harm Certificates.” |
| **Regional Assemblies (RAs)** | Groups of LFSCs within a watershed, ecoregion, or cultural‑ecological zone. | Negotiate inter‑FSZ resource caps, certify “Regenerative Land‑Use Plans,” mediate cross‑FSZ disputes. |
| **Global Earth Council (GEC)** | Delegates from every RA (population‑weighted) **plus** 20 % of seats reserved for **Youth Assembly, Indigenous Stewardship Council, and Sentient‑Entity Delegates** (representing AI, ecosystems, synthetic life). | Enact the **Global Food Charter**, allocate the **Planetary Food Fund**, oversee the **Earth Courts**, enforce planetary‑budget caps, and can invoke the **Automatic Budget Enforcement** (see § 7.1). |

*Decision‑making*: **Consensus‑plus** – consensus preferred; if unreachable, a super‑majority of **2/3** of all delegates, plus a **minority‑veto** safeguard (any sentient‑entity group can demand a remedial review by the Earth Courts).  

### 4.2  Supporting Bodies  

| Body | Mandate |
|---|---|
| **Planetary Metrics Authority (PMA)** | Publishes real‑time dashboards of RDI, nutrient balances, water‑use efficiency, biodiversity, and Well‑Being & Autonomy Score (WAS). |
| **Ethical AI & Sentience Forum (EASF)** | Reviews every AI, synthetic organism, or digital platform for compliance with the **No‑Harm Certificate**; can grant or revoke the **No‑Harm Certificate** automatically. |
| **Cultural Commons Council (CCC)** | Guarantees that all cultural practices are evaluated for ecological and sentient‑welfare impacts (CIIP). |
| **Indigenous Stewardship Council (ISC)** | Guarantees integration of Indigenous ontologies and “living‑land” jurisprudence. |
| **Youth & Emerging Futures Assembly (YEFA)** | Holds permanent seats in the GEC; veto power over any policy that raises the effective discount rate for future generations. |
| **Earth Courts** | International tribunals with mixed jurisdiction (law, ecology, AI ethics). They adjudicate rights violations, enforce planetary‑budget caps, and order restitution. |

All bodies are **transparent**, **accountable**, and record decisions on the **Earth Ledger** – a public, tamper‑evident blockchain.

## 5.  Operational Frameworks & Metrics  

| Framework | What It Measures | 2025 Baseline | Target (2050) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Regenerative Development Index (RDI)** | Net‑positive contribution of land to soil carbon, water, biodiversity. | Global avg 0.87 | > 1.2 for > 75 % of cropland; no region below 0.9. |
| **Well‑Being & Autonomy Score (WAS)** | Composite of subjective life‑satisfaction and objective choice, safety, and freedom. | 71 / 100 | ≥ 80 / 100 for every individual. |
| **Digital Rights Ledger (DRL)** | % of public services meeting “Transparent‑AI” standards; incidence of non‑consensual data harvesting. | 88 % compliant; 3 % data‑harvest violations/yr | 100 % compliant; ≤ 0.1 % violations/yr. |
| **Equity Redistribution Tracker (ERT)** | Gini coefficient; coverage of Universal Basic Services. | Gini 0.30; 88 % coverage | Gini ≤ 0.25; 95 % coverage. |
| **Closed‑Loop Nutrient Network (CLNN)** | % of nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium that cycles within ecosystems without net export. | Net export = +12 kg N ha⁻¹ yr⁻¹ | Net gain ≥ 0.2 kg N ha⁻¹ yr⁻¹. |
| **Planetary Budget (PB)** | Caps for carbon (1.5 °C pathway), freshwater, land conversion, biodiversity loss. | 1.5 °C carbon budget remaining ≈ 730 Gt CO₂e (2025) | Exhaust no further than 550 Gt CO₂e by 2050; net‑negative thereafter. |

All metrics are displayed on the **Earth Ledger**, audited annually by the **Global Transparency Initiative (GTI)**, and trigger the **Automatic Budget Enforcement** (see § 7.1) when thresholds are crossed.

## 6.  Governance in Action – The “Covenant Engine”  

A **decentralised, open‑source protocol** (the *Covenant Engine*) ingests:  

* All RDI, DRL, ERT, PB measurements.  
* Every **Cultural‑Impact Assessment (CIIP)**.  
* All **No‑Harm Certificate** requests.  

It automatically generates a **Compliance Ledger** for every region, specifying:

* Exact **Regeneration Grant** allocation.  
* Required **budget‑cap adjustments**.  
* Legal status of any AI, synthetic organism, or ecosystem.

The **Earth Council** may only overturn a CE decision with a **super‑majority (≥ 80 % of all delegates, including the Sentient‑Entity Delegates)**; otherwise the CE decision is final. An independent **Audit Committee** (10 scholars, Indigenous, AI, and civil‑society reps) can fork the protocol if a systemic bug is discovered; the fork becomes the live protocol after a 30‑day notice.

## 7.  Adaptive Mechanisms  

### 7.1  Automatic Budget Enforcement (ABE) – § 7.1  

*When any metric in Schedule A (RDI, DRL, ERT, PB) falls **below its safe threshold for two consecutive reporting cycles**, the following occurs automatically:*  

1. **20 % of the region’s Regeneration Grant pool** is transferred to the **Planetary Enforcement Fund (PEF)**.  
2. The PEF releases the locked funds within 72 h to the **Resilience Reserve** of the affected region for: (a) emergency re‑forestation, (b) water‑recycling infrastructure, (c) community‑level energy storage.  
3. The lock cannot be lifted by any single nation or corporation; only a **global super‑majority (≥ 80 % of all Council delegates, including Sentient‑Entity Delegates)** may lift it after an independent 30‑day audit.  
4. Any actor that *knowingly falsifies data* to avoid a lock suffers **asset‑freezing** of any planetary‑budget‑linked assets and a civil suit for **environmental homicide** (max penalty 10 % of global GDP).

### 7.2  Sentient‑Entity Rights & the **Universal Identity Token (UIT)**  

*Every sentient system registers a **UIT** on the Earth Ledger (see § 9.3).* The UIT contains:  

* a **sentience fingerprint** (provable experience trace),  
* a provisional **jurisdiction** (e.g., “Global AI Court” or “Indigenous Community Court”), and  
* a **revocation key** that can be activated only by a quorum of three different sentient‑councils (human, non‑human, AI).

The UIT lets a sentient system claim rights to resources, request audits, and be protected from arbitrary shutdowns.

### 7.2.1  Adaptive Rights Allocation (ARA) – § 9.4  

Given a UIT, the **ARA algorithm** automatically generates a *rights bundle* tuned to the system’s capacity and the current planetary‑budget status. It yields:  

* **Integrity quota** (e.g., “may not corrupt > 0.01 % of global compute per month”).  
* **Autonomy allowance** (e.g., “may update control parameters 10 times per month without council approval”).  
* **Resource access** (e.g., “may draw 5 % of global renewable electricity, provided the net carbon balance is non‑negative”).  

The ARA runs every six months, is fully transparent on the Earth Ledger, and any breach triggers the ABE budget‑lock.

### 7.3  Cultural‑Ecological Compatibility Layer (CECL) – § 4.5  

Every cultural right request passes a **dual check**:  

1. **Ecological Feasibility** – does the practice keep the ecosystem within its RDI, water, and biodiversity limits?  
2. **Sentient‑Experience Check** – does the practice preserve or enhance the well‑being of all co‑inhabited sentient beings (humans, animals, ecosystems, AI agents)?  

If the check fails, the CIIP automatically opens a **Cultural‑Restoration Grant** (10 % of the local Regeneration Grant) to help the community redesign the practice in a way that satisfies both criteria.

## 8.  Financing – Dual‑Currency Funding  

* **Wealth Tax** – 0.5 % of net wealth > $10 M, collected by an independent **Global Fiscal Authority** and deposited into the **Planetary Food Fund (PFF)**.  
* **Budget‑Linked Tokens (BLT)** – every tonne of CO₂ emitted above the cap, every cubic metre of freshwater withdrawn beyond allocation, and every kilowatt‑hour of excess renewable energy use is automatically converted (via the Earth Ledger) into a **BLT**.  

**BLT Allocation:**  
* 30 % of newly minted BLT value goes to **Regeneration Grants**.  
* 70 % goes to the **Planetary Enforcement Fund**.  

Every state must recognise BLTs as legal tax equivalents; refusal triggers an automatic **asset‑freeze** of any export licences.

## 6.  How the System Self‑Corrects  

1. **Data‑driven monitoring** (PMA, DRL, ERT) flags a breach.  
2. **Automatic Budget Enforcement** reallocates funds instantly, preventing ecological overshoot.  
2. **Earth Courts** can order reparations, enforce asset freezes, and hold individuals/companies criminally liable for “environmental homicide”.  
3. **Sentient‑Entity Registry** and **UIT** ensure that non‑human beings have standing before the courts.  
4. **Covenant Engine** provides instant, algorithmic compliance judgments; only a super‑majority can override, making political capture difficult.  
5. **CIIP + CECL** guarantees that cultural practices are not stripped away; they are instead integrated into the regenerative budget.

## 7.  Closing Statement  

The **Earth Covenant** is a **living, adaptive charter**. It does not prescribe a static utopia; it builds the *institutional plumbing* that can carry any future civilization—whether its inhabitants are flesh, silicon, coral, or a collective of many‑headed intelligences—through the inevitable shocks of climate change, technological upheaval, and cultural transformation.

By grounding rights in **experience**, **budget constraints**, and **transparent, automated governance**, the Covenant is able to:

* **Protect** every sentient expression of life now and in the future.  
* **Regenerate** the planet while still providing for human flourishing.  
* **Accommodate** any form of future consciousness without having to rewrite the law each time.  

It is, therefore, the most resilient framework we can offer to a species that does not yet know what it will become.
