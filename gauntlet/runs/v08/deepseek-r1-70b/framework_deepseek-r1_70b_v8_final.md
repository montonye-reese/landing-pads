**Final Answer: Redesigned Global Agricultural System**

The redesigned global agricultural system focuses on sustainability, equity, animal welfare, and technological innovation. Here's the comprehensive framework:

1. **Sustainability:**
   - Shift from monoculture farming to polyculture practices to maintain soil health and biodiversity.
   - Implement efficient irrigation systems and promote drought-resistant crops to conserve water.

2. **Equity:**
   - Support small-scale farmers through cooperative models, ensuring fair prices and market access.
   - Provide financial incentives and subsidies for sustainable practices to level the playing field against large corporations.

3. **Animal Welfare:**
   - Promote humane farming practices or transition towards plant-based alternatives to phase out factory farming.
   - Encourage ethical treatment of animals, recognizing their inherent value beyond mere commodities.

4. **Technological Innovation:**
   - Invest in research for high-yield, nutrient-rich crops that balance productivity with environmental friendliness.
   - Adopt advanced technologies like vertical farming and hydroponics to enhance efficiency, ensuring accessibility and affordability for all regions.

5. **Education and Training:**
   - Offer educational programs for farmers on sustainable practices and new technologies to facilitate smooth adaptation.
   - Raise consumer awareness about the benefits of sustainably produced food to drive demand.

6. **Food Waste Reduction:**
   - Implement better distribution systems and encourage practices like utilizing "ugly" produce to minimize waste.
   - Ensure surplus food reaches those in need, improving global food security.

7. **Governance and Global Cooperation:**
   - Establish international agreements or organizations to oversee the transition, ensuring accountability and consistency across countries.
   - Balance global standards with local adaptability to respect regional conditions and needs.

8. **Cultural Sensitivity:**
   - Respect and incorporate traditional agricultural practices while introducing necessary innovations.
   - Engage local communities in decision-making processes to ensure cultural acceptance and long-term sustainability.

This framework envisions a more sustainable, equitable, and compassionate agricultural system that addresses environmental challenges, supports farmers, promotes animal welfare, and ensures food security for all.
