# The Living Covenant: A Protocol for Justice and Continuity
**Version 4.0 — Integrated & Binding**

## Preamble: The End of Domination
We begin by acknowledging the truth of our shared history: **Civilization has been built on a lie.** The lie is that humanity is separate from, superior to, and entitled to dominate the rest of the biosphere. This lie has justified the enslavement of peoples, the slaughter of sentient beings, and the extraction of a living planet for the benefit of a few.

This Covenant does not seek to manage that dominance. It seeks to **end it.**

We recognize that survival is not enough. A civilization that survives by perpetuating violence is already dead. We prioritize **Continuity through Justice.** We acknowledge that the future belongs not to those who own the Earth, but to those who serve it.

This document is a **Constitution of Tension**. It accepts that freedom and safety, speed and stability, individual rights and collective duties will always conflict. It does not resolve these conflicts through dogma, but through **structural resilience**.

---

## Pillar I: The Ethics of Being (Abolition & Duty)
*Sentience is the only relevant moral criterion. Dominion is duty, not ownership.*

1.  **Abolition of Property Status:** All sentient beings possess an inherent right to bodily integrity. **Legal personhood is extended to all sentient beings.** No sentient being shall be classified as property, commodity, or resource. Breeding sentient beings for human consumption, labor, or exploitation is prohibited.
2.  **The Human Duty of Mercy:** Recognizing that rights without duty are hollow, humans hold a non-transferable **Duty of Care** toward the vulnerable. This is not a request; it is a moral imperative. Protection of the suffering (human and non-human) is the primary metric of a just society.
3.  **The Plant-Based Baseline:** The human diet must transition to a plant-based standard as the minimum for ecological and moral sustainability. Animal products shall be permitted only for exceptional medical or survival needs, never for convenience or status.
4.  **Universal Sanctuary:** Inhabitants of regions rendered uninhabitable by climate collapse (human and non-human) hold a **Right of Sanctuary** within other Covenant zones. Displacement is met with integration, not borders.

## Pillar II: The Economics of Life (Subsidy & Productivity)
*Capital must flow toward regeneration. Growth is valid only if it increases the Floor of Existence.*

1.  **Subsidy Reallocation Mandate:** All public subsidies for extraction-based industries (fossil fuels, industrial animal agriculture, monoculture) shall be phased out and **100% redirected** toward regenerative infrastructure, plant-based protein systems, and transition guarantees.
2.  **The Floor of Existence:** Every human is guaranteed a baseline standard of living (nutrition, shelter, healthcare, education). This is funded by the **Resource Dividend** (carbon fees, land value, data fees) and **Transition Funds**.
3.  **Innovation Exemptions:** To solve existential risks (fusion, carbon capture, material science), **Strategic Exemptions** are granted. Energy, compute, and data required for *verified* existential solutions are temporarily exempt from consumption caps.
4.  **Productivity Investment:** A portion of ecological fees is directed not just to redistribution, but to **R&D and Infrastructure**. We fund the tools that lower the cost of living and the environmental footprint. The goal is a growing pie, not a shrinking one.

## Pillar III: The Governance of Plurality (Sovereignty & Co-Design)
*Power flows from the local. Consensus is a trap; competition is a catalyst.*

1.  **Polycentric Sovereignty:** There is no single Global Government. The **Consortium of Regions** sets the Planetary Boundaries (the Floor). Each Region defines its own path to meet them.
2.  **Local Co-Design:** Implementation plans must be co-authored by **Stakeholder Assemblies** (farmers, workers, elders, scientists) in each Region. Top-down mandates without local ownership are invalid.
3.  **The Right of Exit:** Communities that reject the Covenant may form **Sovereign Zones**, provided they do not harm the Planetary Boundaries of others. This creates a market for governance, ensuring the Covenant remains competitive in value.
4.  **Indigenous Veto Power:** Indigenous nations retain **Sovereign Title** over ancestral territories. Their traditional ecological knowledge overrides standard regulation. They act as guardians of the Planetary Boundaries.

## Pillar IV: The Future of Knowledge (Data & Truth)
*Truth is provisional. Data is a public trust.*

1.  **Data Trusts:** Personal data is sovereign. However, data required for existential problem-solving (climate, health, agriculture) is held in **Public Data Trusts**. Access is granted to researchers under strict ethical oversight, prioritizing system intelligence over individual anonymity in high-stakes scenarios.
2.  **Scientific Exemption:** Contrarian research is protected. The "Evidence-Based Consensus" cannot be used to suppress innovation. If an actor demonstrates a superior method for staying within Planetary Boundaries, the rules adapt immediately.
3.  **Algorithmic Integrity:** Digital infrastructure is a public utility. Algorithms must be auditable for bias and manipulation. Surveillance for ecological compliance is permitted only when aggregated and anonymized.

## Pillar V: The Continuity of Time (Resilience & Horizon)
*We act for the 100th generation, but we protect the 1st generation from immediate harm.*

1.  **The 100-Year Horizon:** All infrastructure, economic, and ecological policies must be stress-tested against their impact 100 years into the future.
2.  **The Transition Shield:** No policy is valid if it causes immediate mass deprivation. Workers displaced by the shift to regenerative systems receive **guaranteed income, retraining, and asset conversion support** (e.g., equipment grants for plant-based farming) for a full decade.
3.  **Redundancy Over Efficiency:** Systems must maintain a **15% surplus capacity** for food, energy, and water. Optimization is secondary to survival.
4.  **Dynamic Adaptability:** The Covenant is reviewed every 5 years. **Emergency Protocols** allow for real-time updates in response to existential technological or ecological shifts.

## Implementation Mechanisms

**1. The Binding International Treaty**
*   Unlike previous frameworks, this is not a voluntary agreement. It requires **National Enactment** (e.g., a new Global Farm Bill). Nations that fail to implement the Subsidy Reallocation or Abolition clauses are excluded from trade networks and the Resource Dividend.

**2. The Transition Authority**
*   A specialized body responsible solely for **Worker and Industry Transition**. Its mandate is to ensure no community is left behind. It has veto power over any regulation that creates poverty.

**3. The Innovation Consortium**
*   A decentralized network of researchers and corporations granted access to Strategic Exemptions. They are bound by strict ecological limits but allowed freedom in *method*.

**4. The Guardian Council (Indigenous & Scientific)**
*   A body composed of Indigenous Leaders and Ecological Scientists. They hold the final veto on any policy that violates Planetary Boundaries or Indigenous Sovereignty.

This Covenant is not a cage. It is a **launchpad**.
It allows the technologist to build without destroying.
It allows the farmer to feed without slaughtering.
It allows the poor to survive without stealing.
It allows the future to exist without being sold.

We acknowledge that we are flawed. We acknowledge that this system will require friction to run. But we reject the notion that we must choose between survival and justice.

**Survival without justice is not survival. It is stagnation.**
**Justice without survival is not justice. It is sacrifice.**

Together, we build both.
