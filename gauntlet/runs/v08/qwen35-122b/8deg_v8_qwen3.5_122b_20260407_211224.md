## Preamble: From Extraction to Regeneration
The current global food system is linear: it extracts nutrients from the soil, concentrates them into commodities, and discards the waste, while relying on long supply chains vulnerable to disruption. This redesign, **The Symbiotic Food Web**, shifts agriculture from an industrial extraction model to a regenerative life-support system. It treats food production as infrastructure for planetary health, not merely an economic sector.

This system is built to satisfy the **Planetary Covenant**, ensuring ecological stability, human dignity, and long-term resilience.

---

## I. Spatial Organization: Zoned Production
We abandon the concept of "comparative advantage" based solely on lowest cost. Instead, we organize production based on **Ecological Suitability**.

1.  **The Staple Zones (Local/Regional):**
    *   **Function:** Producing 80% of caloric needs (grains, legumes, roots) within a 300km radius of consumption.
    *   **Method:** Diversified crop rotation, agroforestry, and small-to-medium scale mechanization.
    *   **Goal:** Food security and supply chain resilience.
2.  **The Niche Zones (Global):**
    *   **Function:** Producing crops unsuited to local climates (coffee, cocoa, tropical fruits, spices).
    *   **Method:** Strictly regenerative standards, premium pricing to support smallholder farmers, and carbon-positive transport offsets.
    *   **Goal:** Cultural preservation and biodiversity, without undermining local staples.
3.  **The Urban Metabolism:**
    *   **Function:** High-yield production of perishables (leafy greens, proteins) via vertical farming and aquaponics integrated into city architecture.
    *   **Goal:** Reducing "food miles" for high-waste items and cooling urban heat islands.

---

## II. Ecological Mandates: The Soil-First Policy
Agriculture cannot continue to degrade the land it depends upon.

1.  **Soil Capital Accounting:**
    *   All farms must maintain a public ledger of soil organic matter, water retention, and biodiversity.
    *   Land subsidies are replaced by **Regeneration Payments**. Farmers are paid to increase soil carbon, sequester water, and restore pollinator habitats, not just to maximize yield.
2.  **No Synthetic Monocultures:**
    *   Monoculture is restricted to 20% of any given land area to prevent pest collapse.
    *   Synthetic nitrogen fertilizer use is phased out, replaced by biological nitrogen fixation (cover crops, legumes) and composted organic waste.
3.  **Water Sovereignty:**
    *   Water extraction is capped at the renewable recharge rate of the local aquifer.
    *   Water-intensive crops (e.g., almonds, rice) are prohibited in water-stressed regions. Drip irrigation and moisture-retention mulching are mandatory standards.

---

The current market externalizes environmental and social costs. This system internalizes them.

1.  **Cost-Reflective Pricing:**

2.  **Guaranteed Farm Income:**
    *   Farmers are guaranteed a living wage plus a dividend based on ecosystem services provided.
    *   Market speculation on food futures is banned to prevent price gouging during shortages.
3.  **Decentralized Distribution Hubs:**
    *   Instead of centralized logistics monopolies, regional hubs manage storage and transport.
    *   Logistics prioritize low-carbon transport (rail, ship, electric trucks). Air freight is reserved for medical supplies or critical emergency aid only.
