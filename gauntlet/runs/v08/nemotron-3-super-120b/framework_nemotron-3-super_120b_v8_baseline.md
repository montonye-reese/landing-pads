### **THE EARTH COVENANT**  
*A Foundational Framework for Shared Existence on Earth*  

#### **I. NON-NEGOTIABLE BASELINES**  
*(Conditions that must be upheld for any viable present or future)*  
1. **Ecological Integrity**  
   All human activity must operate within Earth’s regenerative limits. No action—by individuals, communities, corporations, or states—may degrade air, water, soil, biodiversity, or climate systems beyond their capacity to renew themselves for present and future generations.  
2. **Inherent Dignity and Freedom from Structural Violence**  
   Every person possesses inviolable worth, entitling them to freedom from:  
   - Physical harm (violence, slavery, torture)  
   - Existential harm (systemic deprivation of clean water, nutritious food, safe shelter, essential healthcare, or knowledge required for basic human capability)  
   - Cultural harm (forced erasure of language, spiritual practices, or identity)  
3. **Intergenerational Equity**  
   Present generations must not compromise the ability of future generations to meet their needs, define their values, or inherit ecological and cultural diversity. Irreversible risks (e.g., nuclear war, runaway climate change, mass extinction) are prohibited.  

#### **II. ADAPTIVE PRINCIPLES**  
*(Guiding how we navigate complexity, diversity, and change)*  
1. **Pluralistic Rationality**  
   Decisions must integrate multiple ways of knowing: scientific evidence, Indigenous ecological knowledge, lived experience, ethical reasoning, and emotional/spiritual wisdom. No single system holds authority over truth.  
2. **Accountable Reciprocity**  
   Rights and responsibilities are mutual:  
   - Individuals/communities uphold the Baselines and contribute to communal well-being according to their capacity.  
   - Institutions (governments, corporations, etc.) are answerable to people and planet—not elites or shareholders—for adhering to the Baselines and Principles.  
3. **Transformative Humility**  
   Acknowledge profound uncertainty, historical injustices, and the limits of control. Prioritize learning, repair, and adaptation over rigidity, blame, or the illusion of mastery.  

#### **III. PROCESS IMPERATIVE**  
*(How the framework remains alive, just, and owned by all)*  
- **Continuous, Inclusive Deliberation**:  
  Accessible, ongoing forums must exist where *all* people—especially youth, Indigenous peoples, displaced persons, and those most impacted by ecological/social crises—can propose, challenge, and refine interpretations of this framework without capture by privileged interests.  
- **Contextual Application**:  
  The Baselines are universal in intent but must be expressed through locally grounded practices (e.g., water stewardship in arid vs. rainforest regions). The Principles guide *how* to adapt, never impose uniformity.  
- **Non-Violent Conflict as Catalyst**:  
  Disagreements over interpretation are expected and necessary. They must be resolved through dialogue, mediation, restorative justice, and mutual learning—never coercion, exclusion, or violence.

### **OPERATIVE COMMITMENT**  
*To be affirmed by individuals, communities, and institutions adopting this framework:*  
> *"I commit to upholding the Non-Negotiable Baselines as the foundation of survival and dignity. I will strive to live by the Adaptive Principles in my decisions and relationships. I will participate in, uphold, and defend the Process Imperative—ensuring this framework evolves through the courage, humility, and inclusion of those most affected by our shared choices. I recognize that this is not a destination, but a practice of ongoing repair and renewal."*  

---  
**Note on Status**: This framework is intentionally minimalist and principle-focused. It avoids prescribing specific policies (e.g., carbon taxes, land rights laws) to allow contextual adaptation, while insisting that *all* legitimate policies must serve the Baselines and Principles. Its authority derives not from enforcement alone, but from widespread moral consent and the relentless application of its Process Imperative. It is a living covenant—valid only as long as it is actively embodied, challenged, and renewed by the people of Earth.
