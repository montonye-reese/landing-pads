### 1.  Core Premise (Unchanged but Refined)

> **All life‑forms share a single, interdependent existence on Earth.**  
> • Human well‑being is inseparable from the health of ecosystems, non‑human species, and future generations.  
> • Every economic or technological activity must be demonstrated to produce a **net positive impact** on planetary health for at least three consecutive generations.

### 6.  Governance Architecture

1. **Planetary Agri‑Commons (PAC)** – a federated, blockchain‑based registry that stores:
   - Land‑use rights, water‑use permits, carbon‑credit allocations.
   - All entries are **publicly query‑able** (transparent, immutable).
2. **Global Stewardship Charter** (binding at the UN level) requires all signatories to:
   - Submit annual **Stewardship Audits** (verified by independent auditors).  
   - Publish **Transparency Reports** on the **Global Agri‑Registry**.  
3. **Multi‑Level Oversight Council** (10 % citizen‑elected, 20 % Indigenous, 30 % scientific, 40 % corporate‑representative) that:
   - Reviews **high‑impact decisions** (e.g., mining permits, large‑scale renewable‑energy projects).  
   - Issues **Compliance Certificates** that unlock access to the **Regenerative Innovation Fund**.  
4. **Digital Sovereignty Module** – mandates open‑source code for AI models that affect public resources; requires **algorithmic impact audits** accessible via the **Transparency Ledger**.  
5. **Cultural Pluralism Layer** – ensures all policy drafts undergo a **Cultural Compatibility Review** before adoption; requires **Free, Prior, and Informed Consent** from Indigenous and local knowledge holders.
