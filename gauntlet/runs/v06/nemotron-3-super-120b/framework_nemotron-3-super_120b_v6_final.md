**Earth Stewardship Framework (ESF) – Version 1.0**  
*A living operating system for planetary survival that integrates sentience, ecology, economics, health, ideology and human dignity.*  

---

## Preamble  

The ESF recognises that **all intelligences—carbon‑based (biological) or silicon‑based (artificial)—exist only within Earth’s biophysical limits**. Survival depends on respecting those limits while cultivating the reciprocal relationships, adaptive knowledge, power structures and cultural norms that make life worth living. The framework is deliberately **modular**: each pillar can be implemented independently, but together they form a self‑reinforcing system that prevents the recurrence of exploitation, ecological collapse and ideological distortion.

## I. NON‑NEGOTIABLE CORE PILLARS  

| # | Pillar | Core Statement | Minimum Guarantees & Obligations | Why It’s Non‑Negotiable |
|---|--------|----------------|----------------------------------|--------------------------|
| **1** | **Planetary Integrity** | All intelligences must operate strictly within Earth’s biophysical limits. No entity (state, corporation, culture, algorithm, individual) may exceed these limits. | • Climate: ≤1.5 °C warming (pre‑industrial) → net‑zero emissions by 2040 (sequestration only). <br>• Nitrogen: ≤35 Tg N/yr lost to environment. <br>• Phosphorus: ≤1.1 Tg P/yr (100% recovery from waste/streams; no net mining). <br>• Freshwater: ≤4 000 km³/yr consumptive use; watersheds restored to ≥80 % natural flow variability. <br>• Land‑System Change: 0% conversion of primary forests/peatlands/native grasslands after 2030; ≤15% ice‑free land as cropland; 50% of agricultural land restored to native ecosystems. <br>• Biodiversity: extinction rate ≤10 E/MSY; pollinator habitats ≥30 % of farmland. <br>• Novel Entities: zero persistent synthetics; all inputs fully biodegradable/recoverable. | Exceeding any boundary triggers cascading, irreversible collapse that destroys the basis for all intelligence. |
| **2** | **Sentience Sovereignty** | Every sentient being (capable of suffering) possesses intrinsic, inviolable worth that cannot be overridden by ecological, economic or technological considerations. Sentient beings may **never** be used as a means to an end (food, clothing, labor, entertainment, ecological‑service, testing, etc.). | • **Zero unnecessary sentient suffering** – an action is prohibited if it causes avoidable suffering or suffering that is not strictly required to prevent *greater* sentient suffering. <br>• **Zero use as means** – no farming, fishing, hunting, testing, entertainment, labor, or ecological restoration that treats a sentient being as a resource, commodity or tool. <br>• **Basic welfare** – access to conditions preventing avoidable suffering (e.g., freedom from hunger, pain, fear, threat to life). <br>• **Reciprocal duty** – actively avoid causing sentient suffering; repair harm when it occurs; design intelligence systems to *reduce* sentient suffering (e.g., AGIs that prioritize plant‑based food transitions). | Sentience is the only morally relevant property; treating sentient beings as means is the root of all systemic violence (slaughterhouses, vivisection, exploitation, ecocide). |
| **3** | **Boundary‑Adaptive Legitimacy** | Authority (political, economic, technological, algorithmic) exists **only** to increase systemic resilience to planetary boundaries. Power that degrades the boundary‑dependent web forfeits legitimacy. | • **Wealth/Power Caps**: No individual/entity may control >10 x the median local wealth/power (measured by democratic assemblies). Excess flows to Community Stewardship Trusts. <br>• **Ubiquitous Accountability**: Power‑holders (leaders, CEOs, algorithm designers, AGI trainers) face transparent impact assessments, community‑led restorative remedies, and personal liability for harm exceeding planetary/social boundaries. <br>• **Anti‑Corruption by Design**: Essential systems (water, energy, seeds, data, compute infrastructure) governed by *commune* models (user‑owned, user‑managed) where feasible. <br>• **Boundary‑Adaptive Legitimacy Test**: Power‑holders must quarterly prove their operations *increase* systemic resilience to boundary stressors (e.g., does this AI‑managed grid reduce failure risk during heatwaves? does this algorithm increase groundwater recharge?). Legitimacy scores directly affect wealth/power caps. <br>• **True‑Cost Accounting**: All actions must internalise externalised costs (healthcare, ecological cleanup, productivity loss from pandemics, climate damage) and compare total social cost to the total social value of the next‑best alternative. Actions whose total social cost exceeds the total social value of the best alternative are prohibited. | Power that undermines the boundary‑dependent web destroys the very conditions that make authority possible. Legitimacy must be earned by *strengthening* resilience, not by extracting wealth. |
| **4** | **Ideological Literacy (Carnism & Zoological Witchcraft)** | All intelligences must be educated to recognise and dismantle the ideological mechanisms that make exploitation feel inevitable. | • **Carnism Literacy**: Ability to identify the Three Ns (Normal, Natural, Necessary) used to justify animal exploitation, and to recognise perceptual distortion that renders animals as objects. <br>• **Zoological Witchcraft Literacy**: Ability to detect animalisation of marginalized humans, humanisation of exploitation (e.g., “We need to eat meat!”), and erasure of the shared root that links racism and speciesism. <br>• **Anti‑Speciesism / Anti‑Carnism Drills**: Monthly practice of questioning species‑based assumptions (e.g., “Would I accept this if the being were human?”). <br>• **Whole‑Being Language Training**: Teaching to centre the sentient being (e.g., “This milk is stolen from a grieving mother” not “This is dairy”). | Ideology masks suffering and makes exploitation feel normal. Without literacy, even well‑intentioned policies can reinforce the very systems they aim to change. |
| **5** | **Witnessing & Transition Dignity** | Societies must cultivate the capacity to bear witness to suffering as it occurs and to support dignified transition for those leaving exploitative systems. | • **Witnessing Literacy**: Ability to recognise suffering (fear, pain, grief), to sit with discomfort without looking away, and to translate witnessing into action (e.g., giving water, sharing footage, calling authorities). <br>• **Witnessing Audits**: Governance/innovation processes must include an audit verifying that power‑holders do *not* suppress visibility of harm (no ag‑gag laws, no hidden transport routes, no secret slaughterhouses). <br>• **Transition Dignity Literacy**: Ability to recognise transition trauma (shame, fear, identity loss), to transition with psychological support (counselling, peer groups, income alternatives), and to repair harm caused by transition (e.g., funding land regeneration, community rebuilding). <br>• **Transition Dignity Audits**: All governance/innovation processes must include an audit verifying that humans are not abandoned in transition (no “just‑in‑time” moratoriums without wraparound support). <br>• **Reciprocal Duty**: Actively bear witness, provide psychological and economic support during transition, and repair any harm caused by the transition process. | Systems change only when enough people *witness* harm and *support* those leaving exploitative systems. Ignoring the human dimension breeds resentment and drives the system back into violence. |
| **6** | **Nutritional & Gut‑Health Literacy** | All intelligences must be educated to ensure dietary adequacy and microbiome health, which are foundational to both personal vitality and ecological stewardship. | • **Nutritional Adequacy Test** (Innovation Filter step 0): Before any dietary or food‑system proposal proceeds, it must be modelled against the latest Dietary Reference Intakes (DRIs) for all life‑stage groups, adjusting for bioavailability (heme vs. non‑heme iron, zinc phytate inhibition, B12 analogs, EPA/DHA conversion from ALA). The diet must meet ≥ 100 % of the RDI/AI for ≥ 95 % of simulated individuals (Monte‑Carlo simulation). <br>• **Gut‑Health Baseline Training**: From age 4, teach how dietary fibre feeds the gut microbiome, how microbiome diversity predicts immune and mental health, and how animal‑product‑heavy diets reduce diversity. <br>• **Health‑Impact Test** (Innovation Filter): Any intervention must demonstrate a measurable improvement in at least two validated biomarkers of vitality (e.g., fasting glucose, HDL/TG ratio, heart‑rate variability, serum ferritin, VO₂max) in a representative pilot group before proceeding to ecological/economic tests. <br>• **Health‑Value in True‑Cost Accounting**: Social‑benefit column includes avoided healthcare costs, productivity gains from lower NCD burden, and QALYs/DALYs averted from improved nutrition and gut health. <br>• **Reciprocal Duty**: Choose foods that maximise nutrient density and fibre diversity while minimising ecological impact; share knowledge about gut‑health and nutrition; support policies that make nutrient‑dense, plant‑forward foods affordable and accessible. | Personal health is not a side‑effect of ecological stewardship—it is a core signal that the system is working. Ignoring nutrition and gut health undermines both individual vitality and long‑term ecological resilience. |

## II. ADAPTIVE PROCESSES  

### A. Governance – Nested Sovereignty with Feedback Loops  

| Level | Authority | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| **Local** (village/watershed/biome) | Communities define needs/culture **within** planetary boundaries (e.g., water use based on aquifer recharge; urban heat‑island mitigation via green roofs). Tools: participatory budgeting, elder/youth councils, ecological monitoring co‑ops (low‑cost soil/water sensors feeding open databases), algorithmic impact assessments. | Local assemblies hold veto power over enforcement timelines; they manage Community Stewardship Trusts that hold redirected subsidies and ecological‑debt payments. |
| **Regional** (basin/biome/trade bloc) | Resolves cross‑border issues (e.g., Mekong River flow, Great Lakes pollution) via binding agreements rooted in local data + Indigenous water wisdom + real‑time hydrological AI. No authority to override local autonomy unless planetary boundaries are threatened. | Regional councils oversee shared resources, enforce True‑Cost Accounting, and manage regional stewardship trusts. |
| **Global** (Facilitating Council) | Not a world government. A rotating, mandated body of: 50 % Indigenous/local knowledge keepers (selected by their nations), 30 % scientists (ecological/social, nominated by peer academies), 20 % youth (under 30, selected by global youth assemblies), 10 % non‑human intelligence proxies (where observable—e.g., delegates for watershed health, pollinator networks, soil‑microbiome stewards). | Role: verifies no region exports ecological harm (e.g., waste dumping, eutrophying rivers via soy exports), facilitates tech/knowledge sharing, triggers emergency interventions only when ≥2 boundaries are breached. Can recommend sanctions (e.g., trade limits on ecocidal goods) but cannot enforce—relies on global solidarity and reputational pressure. Legitimacy comes from consent, not coercion. |

### B. Conflict Transformation – From Signal to Wisdom  

1. **Early Detection** – Community tension monitors (trained locals/algorithms) track rising conflict via agreed indicators (water disputes, identity‑based taunts, soil degradation trends, anomalous AI behavior).  
2. **Rooted Process** – Mandatory use of locally appropriate methods *before* punitive systems: restorative circles (African/Indigenous), truth & healing circles (South Africa/Canada), Indigenous peacemaking (Hawaiian *ho’oponopono*), trauma‑mediated mediation, algorithmic accountability forums (for AI‑driven conflicts).  
3. **Root Cause Repair** – Solutions must address *why* conflict arose (e.g., if water fight stems from upstream deforestation, solution = reforestation + upstream/downstream stewardship pact funded by regional water trust + AI‑optimized reforestation planning).  

### C. Innovation Filter – Gate‑Keeping for New Tech, Policies and Practices  

All proposals (technological, policy, program, dietary, etc.) must pass the following **sequential** tests. Failure at any gate stops the proposal.

| Gate | Question | Pass‑Criteria |
|------|----------|---------------|
| **0 – Nutritional Adequacy** | Does the proposal meet ≥ 100 % of RDI/AI for ≥ 95 % of simulated individuals (Monte‑Carlo, bioavailability‑adjusted)? | **Yes** → proceed; **No** → REJECT. |
| **0‑b – Sentience Gate** | Does the action cause *any* sentient suffering? If yes, is it *unavoidable* to prevent *greater* sentient suffering and have all non‑suffering alternatives been exhausted? | If sentient suffering is unavoidable and necessary to prevent greater suffering → may proceed; otherwise **REJECT**. |
| **1 – Purpose** | Does the proposal serve Foundations 1‑5 *without* creating new dependencies/harms? | **Yes** → proceed. |
| **2 – Access** | Can communities repair, adapt, or reject it? (No black‑boxes; designs must be open‑source/modular). | **Yes** → proceed. |
| **3 – Reversibility** | Can harm be undone if we’re wrong? (e.g., perennial polycultures ✅; deep‑sea mining ❌; persistent synthetic AI training data ❌). | **Yes** → proceed. |
| **4 – Boundary** | Does the proposal respect planetary limits in its full lifecycle (mining → disposal → recycling → compute energy source)? | **Yes** → proceed. |
| **5 – Kinship** | Does it increase systemic resilience to boundary stressors? (Boundary‑Adaptive Legitimacy Test for power‑holders; for others, does it enhance rather than degrade boundary‑dependent systems?) | **Yes** → proceed. |
| **6 – Socio‑Economic Value** | Does the proposal generate net societal value (total social value > total social cost) when externalised costs (healthcare, ecology, productivity loss) are internalised? | **Yes** → proceed. |
| **7 – Sentience Impact** | Does the action increase net sentient suffering? (Sentience Impact Assessment) | **No** → proceed; **Yes** → REJECT. |
| **8 – Carnism / Zoological Witchcraft** | Does the action reinforce carnism (Three Ns) or zoological witchcraft (animalisation of marginalized humans, humanisation of exploitation, erasure of shared root)? | **No** → proceed; **Yes** → REJECT. |
| **9 – Witnessing / Transition Dignity** | Does the action suppress bearing witness to harm or undermine transition dignity? | **No** → proceed; **Yes** → REJECT. |

*Only proposals that clear all nine gates may be implemented.*  

### D. Education – The Root System (Non‑negotiable from birth)  

| Literacy | Core Competency (Age ≥ 4 unless noted) | Example Activities |
|----------|----------------------------------------|--------------------|
| **Systems Literacy** | Understand feedback loops (soil → food → health → community resilience → conflict risk). | Sami youth track reindeer migration + lichen growth + temperature to predict herd health and alert neighbours to ice risks. |
| **Relational Intelligence** | Nonviolent communication, trauma awareness, repairing harm. | Brazilian favela teens run conflict‑resolution workshops for police using Afro‑Brazilian *capoeira* dialogue + favela mapping. |
| **Epistemic Humility** | Knowing *how* we know what we know; valuing multiple truths (Indigenous, scientific, artistic, spiritual, algorithmic). | Nepali school combines glacial hydrology (science) + Sherpa oral histories of snow patterns + Buddhist impermanence teachings + AI glacier‑melt predictions (with uncertainty bands). |
| **Civic Courage** | Recognising illegitimate authority; designing accountability. | Filipino barangay youth audit local project budgets using open‑source tools + publish findings in local dialects + illustrated formats for non‑literate elders. |
| **Sentience Literacy** | Recognise suffering (fear, pain, grief); understand why species membership is irrelevant to moral consideration. | Role‑play as a pig in a crate; discuss why its fear matters regardless of utility to humans. |
| **Carnism Literacy** | Identify the Three Ns (Normal, Natural, Necessary) and perceptual distortion that renders animals as objects. | Deconstruct ads: “We need to eat meat!” → ask who benefits; show images of animals as subjects. |
| **Zoological Witchcraft Literacy** | Detect animalisation of marginalized humans, humanisation of exploitation, erasure of shared root. | Analyse political rhetoric that equates immigrants with “swarms” or “animals”. |
| **Witnessing Literacy** | Recognise suffering, sit with discomfort, translate witnessing into action (giving water, sharing footage, calling authorities). | Monthly silent vigils at slaughterhouse gates; share verified footage with context. |
| **Transition Dignity Literacy** | Recognise transition trauma (shame, fear, identity loss); transition with psychological support; repair transition harm. | Facilitate support groups for farmers exiting contracts; provide vocational training and mental‑health services. |
| **Nutritional Literacy** | Read nutrition labels, calculate externalised costs, assess bioavailability of nutrients, understand limits of observational nutrition research. | Compare heme‑iron bioavailability in meat vs. non‑heme iron in lentils + vitamin C; model folate needs for pregnant women. |
| **Gut‑Health Literacy** | Understand how dietary fibre feeds gut microbiome, how microbiome density predicts immune/mental health, how animal‑product‑heavy diets reduce diversity. | Grow a sourdough starter; track changes in stool consistency and energy after increasing fibre intake. |
| **Ecological‑Stewardship Literacy** | Map personal dependencies (water, soil, biodiversity, microbial communities) and understand how personal choices affect planetary boundaries. | “Where does my breakfast’s water come from? Who tended the soil? What microbes live in my gut? How does my food choice affect local watershed?” |  

All literacies are reinforced through **monthly drills** (e.g., “Today I witnessed X and acted by Y – here’s why it mattered”) and **community‑audit mechanisms** (citizen‑science dashboards, open‑source impact trackers).  

### E. Technology Guardrails – Innovation Filter Applied to Tech  

*Same nine‑gate Innovation Filter (Section II.C) applies to any new technology (hardware, software, AI, biotech).*  
*In addition:*  

- **Open‑Source Mandate**: Patents on life‑saving essentials (medicine, seeds, water tech, compute infrastructure for boundary‑adaptive AI) are void; replaced by open‑source commons with benefit‑sharing clauses for originators.  
- **Algorithmic Transparency Logs**: All AI systems influencing boundaries must publish real‑time impact metrics (e.g., water‑use, energy‑use, emissions) in a machine‑readable, publicly accessible format.  
- **Compute‑Infrastructure Commons**: Essential compute (e.g., climate‑modeling supercomputers, AI training clusters for boundary‑adaptive tasks) governed by *commune* models (user‑owned, user‑managed) where feasible.

## III. IMPLEMENTATION MECHANISMS  

### A. Local Pilots First  
Communities implement ESF‑aligned practices (e.g., Bolivia’s *Ley de Derechos de la Madre Tierra*, Kerala’s participatory budgeting, Malawi’s farmer‑managed natural regeneration, Barcelona’s superblocks with AI‑optimized traffic flow, Winsome Constance Kindness Trust plant‑based protein startups) and share outcomes via open platforms (e.g., Open Science Framework, GitHub‑like stewardship repos).  

### B. Annual Global Stewardship Assembly  
- **Composition**: 1 000+ delegates selected by lot from local assemblies, prioritising marginalized voices + non‑human intelligence proxies; 20 % youth, 10 % non‑human proxies.  
- **Agenda**: Review boundary science, conflict outcomes, education metrics, power‑distribution data, failure reports, and legitimacy scores. Issue **adaptive guidance** (non‑binding recommendations) – e.g., “Based on Sahel data, increase agroforestry funding by 20%; pause deep‑sea mining pending reversibility proof; require all water‑management AI to show aquifer recharge improvement; mandate sentience impact assessments for all relocation projects.”  

### C. Failure Museums & Red Teams  
- **Public, accessible archives** where the framework *failed* (e.g., “How Region X violated water boundaries in 2030 → caused Y conflict → lessons for Z”).  
- **Annual red‑team exercises**: Adversaries attempt to break the framework (e.g., “How would a corporation exploit the Innovation Filter? How would an AI hack the Boundary‑Adaptive Legitimacy Test?”) to harden the system.  

### D. Sunset Clause  
The entire ESF document is subject to renewal every **25 years** by a global constituent assembly **or sooner** if **≥ 3 planetary boundaries** are breached.  

### E. First Steps for Individuals & Communities  

1. **Map Your Dependencies** – List the water, soil, biodiversity, microbial communities and human intelligences that make your morning coffee, phone, or meal possible.  
2. **Run a Sentience Check** – Does your daily routine cause any avoidable sentient suffering? If yes, replace it with a plant‑based, cruelty‑free alternative (e.g., dairy → oat milk, leather → pineapple‑leaf leather).  
3. **Conduct a Mini‑True‑Cost Audit** – Estimate the hidden health/ecological costs of a habit (e.g., a bacon‑and‑egg breakfast) vs. a plant‑based alternative; notice the net societal gain.  
4. **Practice Witnessing & Transition Dignity** – Spend 5 minutes today bearing witness to a source of suffering you normally ignore (e.g., watch a slaughterhouse video, read a farmer’s story about debt) and consider one small action to support the person/animal involved.  
5. **Adopt a Literacy Drill** – Choose one of the six literacies (Sentience, Carnism, Witnessing, Transition, Nutritional, Gut‑Health) and spend 10 minutes learning and applying it today (e.g., read a label, calculate externalised cost, share a plant‑based recipe).

## IV. CLOSING NOTE  

The ESF is **not a utopian blueprint**; it is a **minimum viable system** that keeps intelligence within the bounds of life while constantly improving its capacity to respect those bounds. Its strength lies in **continuous, transparent self‑correction**—every pillar, every process, every test is designed to fail fast, learn fast, and adapt fast.  

*By honouring the sentient being, the ecosystem, the future child, and the present human alike, we turn stewardship from a duty into a shared identity—one that makes flourishing possible for all life on Earth.*
