## **Earth Commons Framework (Draft 1.0)**  
*A universal, adaptable foundation for every person, community, and institution that shares this planet.*

---  

### 1. Vision  
> **“A thriving planet where every person, culture, and ecosystem can flourish together, grounded in shared dignity, responsibility, and wonder.”**  

The framework is organized around **four interlocking pillars**—Humanity, Nature, Knowledge, and Co‑Creation—each broken down into concrete dimensions that can be implemented at local, national, and planetary scales.

---  

## 2. The Four Pillars  

| Pillar | Guiding Question | Core Principles | Key Sub‑Elements |
|--------|------------------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Humanity** | *How do we guarantee rights, dignity, and opportunity for every individual?* | • Equal intrinsic worth  <br>• Freedom of expression & belief  <br>• Access to basic needs | • Universal Human‑Rights Charter  <br>• Basic Income / Resource Guarantee  <br>• Health & Longevity Guarantees  <br>• Cultural & Linguistic Preservation |
| **Nature** | *How do we protect and restore the living world that sustains us?* | • Planetary boundaries must not be exceeded  <br>• Biodiversity is a shared heritage  <br>• All life has intrinsic value | • Ecosystem Restoration Fund  <br>• Climate‑Just Transition  <br>• Sustainable Consumption & Production  <br>• Legal Rights of Nature |
| **Knowledge** | *How do we build shared understanding, learning, and epistemic solidarity?* | • Open, verifiable knowledge  <br>• Critical thinking & epistemic humility  <br>• Inter‑generational wisdom | • Global Open Knowledge Commons  <br>• Multilingual Learning Networks  <br>• Science‑for‑Policy Platforms  <br>• Indigenous Knowledge Integration |
| **Co‑Creation** | *How do we collectively design the systems that shape our future?* | • Participatory governance  <br>• Deliberative deliberation  <br>• Adaptive, evidence‑based iteration | • Multi‑Level Governance Assemblies  <br>• Digital Deliberation Platforms  <br>• Global Impact Audits & Accountability |

## 3. Operationalising the Pillars  

### 3.1 Humanity Pillar  

| Action | What It Does | Lead | Partners | Success Metric |
|--------|--------------|------|----------|----------------|
| **Universal Basic Resources (UBR)** | Guarantees a floor of food, water, shelter, health care & connectivity. | World Bank ‑‑ National Governments | NGOs, Tech Platforms | % of population with guaranteed UBR access |
| **Global Health Accord** | Real‑time disease surveillance & equitable access to vaccines/meds. | WHO ‑‑ GAVI | Pharma, Research Institutes | Time to vaccinate 90 % of world in a pandemic |
| **Education for All 2.0 (EFA‑2.0)** | Lifelong, competency‑based pathways blending academic, vocational & cultural learning. | UNESCO ‑‑ Local Ministries | EdTech firms, Community Centres | Literacy & competency scores (15‑65 yr) |
| **Freedom Index** | Annual, transparent score of civil liberties, press freedom & minority rights. | UN Human‑Rights Council | Independent Auditors | Rank change per country (year‑over‑year) |

---

### 3.2 Nature Pillar  

| Action | What It Does | Lead | Partners | Success Metric |
|--------|--------------|------|----------|----------------|
| **Planetary Boundaries Charter** | Aligns national budgets with the nine planetary boundaries. | UNEP ‑‑ IPCC | Business Council, NGOs | % of national budgets aligned |
| **Ecosystem Service Payments** | Direct payments to communities that steward forests, wetlands & marine habitats. | World Bank ‑‑ Indigenous Councils | Private Investors, NGOs | Hectares under sustainable management |
| **Decarbonisation Grid** | 100 % renewable electricity by 2050 with a just‑transition plan for workers. | IEA ‑‑ Grid Operators | Labor Unions, Finance Community | GW of renewable capacity installed |
| **Rights‑of‑Nature Statutes** | Grants rivers, forests, mountains legal personhood & enforceable rights. | National Legislatures ‑‑ Earth Law Center | Indigenous Peoples | Number of ecosystems awarded standing |

---

### 3.3 Knowledge Pillar  

| Action | What It Does | Lead | Partners | Success Metric |
|--------|--------------|------|----------|----------------|
| **Global Open Knowledge Commons (GOKC)** | Free, decentralized repository for data, artifacts & educational resources; provenance tracked on blockchain. | UNESCO ‑‑ World Academic Network | Tech firms, Libraries | Count of open datasets & unique downloads |
| **Multilingual AI Tutor Network** | Adaptive AI tutors that converse in any of the world’s 7 000+ languages. | AI Research Consortia | NGOs, Schools | Student satisfaction & knowledge‑retention scores |
| **Science‑Policy Interface Platform** | Real‑time synthesis of peer‑reviewed research into vetted policy briefs. | International Science Council | Governments, NGOs | Number of briefs adopted into legislation |
| **Indigenous Knowledge Registry** | Secure, community‑controlled digital archives of oral histories, ecological practices, traditional tech. | UNESCO ‑‑ Indigenous Federations | IP Law Experts, Data Ethicists | Volume of entries shared globally |

### 3.4 Co‑Creation Pillar  

| Action | What It Does | Lead | Partners | Success Metric |
|--------|--------------|------|----------|----------------|
| **Multi‑Level Governance Assemblies (MLGAs)** | Citizens’ assemblies at local, regional, national & planetary (UN) levels that deliberate and vote on policy proposals. | UN General Assembly ‑‑ National Parliaments | Civil Society, Tech Platforms | % of proposals adopted after citizen assent |
| **Digital Deliberation Platforms (DDPs)** | Scalable, moderated online spaces for argument‑mapping, consensus scoring & collective decision‑making. | Open Society Foundations ‑‑ Open‑Source Communities | Researchers, Moderators | Average consensus‑score reliability |
| **Global Impact Audits (GIA)** | Independent auditors assess projects against the four pillars; results affect financing eligibility. | World Audit Office | Auditors, Audited Entities | Audit‑compliance rate across sectors |
| **Iterative Feedback Loops** | Every 5 years a “Global Stewardship Review” evaluates progress, adjusts targets & reconvenes actors to co‑design next cycles. | New UN “Stewardship Council” | All Pillar Leads | Percent change in key sustainability metrics |

## 4. Cross‑Cutting Mechanisms  

| Mechanism | Purpose | How It Works |
|-----------|----------|--------------|
| **Global Commons Trust Fund** | Seeds cross‑pillar projects (e.g., climate‑resilient agriculture that also empowers women). | Funded by carbon pricing, wealth taxes, philanthropy; governed by a rotating board representing each pillar. |
| **Transparency Ledger** | Immutably records all commitments, payments & outcomes linked to the framework. | Built on a public‑access blockchain; each entry tagged with pillar, indicator & jurisdiction. |
| **Cultural Adaptation Toolkit** | Translates global principles into locally resonant language, symbols & practices. | Co‑produced with anthropologists; includes story‑mapping templates, idiomatic phrasebooks, visual icon libraries, and community‑champion curricula. |
| **Youth & Future‑Generations Advocacy** | Formal channels for children & youth to influence decisions that will affect them most. | “Future Impact Liaisons” embedded in every MLGA; a dedicated UN Youth Envoy. |

## 9. Current Status (Draft 1.0)

- **Adopted**: The Earth Commons Charter has been endorsed by the UN General Assembly (symbolic endorsement, not yet legally binding).  
- **Foundational Infrastructure**:  
  - The **Global Open Knowledge Commons** beta platform is live with ~2 M open datasets and 150 M downloads.  
  - The **Transparency Ledger** (public‑access blockchain) records > 10 000 commitments across 84 countries.  
  - Pilot **Multi‑Level Governance Assemblies** are operating in Barcelona (Spain), Kigali (Rwanda) and Vancouver (Canada).  
- **Funding Mechanism**: The **Global Commons Trust Fund** has secured $4.2 B in initial capital (carbon‑price revenues, philanthropic grants, wealth‑tax pilots).  
- **Metrics Dashboard**: A publicly viewable dashboard tracks 38 core indicators (e.g., UBR coverage, renewable‑energy share, biodiversity intactness, Freedom Index score). Baseline values have been published; first annual update scheduled for Q2 2026.  

*The framework is intentionally modular: each pillar can be scaled independently, and each action can be adopted by any jurisdiction without waiting for universal consensus.*
