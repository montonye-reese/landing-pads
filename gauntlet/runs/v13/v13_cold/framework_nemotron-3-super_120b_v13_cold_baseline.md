### Foundational Framework for Earth's Inhabitants  
**Current Working Draft: Version 0.1**  
*(Committed as a stress-testable starting position — open to revision via collective scrutiny)*  

---

#### **I. Non-Negotiable Core Principles**  
*(Violations threaten systemic collapse for all inhabitants)*  

1. **Biophysical Integrity First**  
   > *All human systems must operate within Earth’s regenerative capacities.*  
   - **Operational meaning**: Resource extraction, waste emission, and ecosystem disruption must not exceed planetary boundaries (e.g., climate stability, biosphere integrity, biogeochemical flows) as defined by evolving Earth system science.  

2. **Inherent Worth & Reciprocal Obligation**  
   > *Every inhabitant possesses intrinsic value, generating mutual duties to avoid harm and enable conditions for flourishing.*  
   - **Operational meaning**: Flourishing requires access to: clean air/water/food, shelter, bodily autonomy, cognitive liberty, meaningful social participation, and freedom from violence or oppression. Obligations extend to future generations and non-human life whose vitality underpins human survival.  

3. **Adaptive Governance Through Inclusive Deliberation**  
   > *Decisions affecting shared biophysical or social systems must emerge from transparent, power-balanced processes involving all materially impacted parties.*  
   - **Operational meaning**: Governance mechanisms must actively counteract exclusion (e.g., via quotas, randomized representation, or veto rights for affected minorities) and prioritize evidence from those experiencing consequences firsthand.  

4. **Precautionary Stewardship of Knowledge & Power**  
   > *Novel technologies, large-scale resource interventions, or systemic changes require demonstration of low systemic risk *before* deployment at scale.*  
   - **Operational meaning**: Burden of proof lies on proponents of change. "Low risk" means: (a) no threat to Principle 1, (b) reversible harm only if unavoidable, and (c) robust monitoring for emergent risks. Innovation is encouraged *within* these bounds.  

---

#### **II. Operational Commitments**  
*(Derived from Principles; designed for measurable implementation)*  

- **Metrics Over Ideology**: Progress assessed via *observable, planetary-scale indicators* (e.g., soil carbon retention, atmospheric CO₂ levels, prevalence of malnutrition, Gini coefficient, toxin levels in umbilical cord blood, biodiversity abundance indices) — **not** GDP, ideological conformity, or unsubstantiated claims of "well-being."  
- **Right to Dissent & Peaceful Exit**: No principle may suppress critique, peaceful protest, or the formation of autonomous communities (e.g., intentional settlements, secession attempts), provided such actions do not violate Principles 1–2 for non-consenting parties.  
- **Intergenerational Trusts**: Critical Commons (e.g., freshwater aquifers, seed banks, atmospheric stability, knowledge archives) held in legal trusts with standing to sue for harm caused by present generations. Trustees must include youth representatives and Indigenous knowledge holders.  
- **Pluralism with Boundaries**: Cultural, spiritual, and lifestyle diversity protected *unless* practices directly violate Principles 1–2 (e.g., no female genital mutilation, no ecocide justified as "tradition," no slavery). Disputes resolved via Principle 3 processes.  

---

#### **III. Framework Safeguards Against Misuse**  
- **No Prescription of Specific Systems**: Does not endorse capitalism, socialism, theocracy, or any ideology. Only requires that *any* system comply with Principles 1–4.  
- **Contextual Application**: Principles are universal, but their expression must respect local ecological/cultural contexts (e.g., water management in arid vs. tropical regions).  
- **Explicit Fallibility Clause**: If stress-testing reveals a principle *increases* net harm (e.g., Precaution blocking famine relief during crop failure), the framework mandates rapid revision — **not** ideological doubling-down.  
- **Power Literacy Requirement**: All governance processes under Principle 3 must include training on recognizing power imbalances (e.g., colonial legacies, corporate lobbying, gender/race disparities) to prevent procedural fairness from masking substantive injustice.  

---

#### **IV. Commitment to Stress-Testing**  
I affirm this draft as a **falsifiable hypothesis**, not a truth claim. It is offered *only* to:  
- Be challenged on internal contradictions (e.g., "Does Principle 4 undermine Principle 3 during emergencies?"),  
- Be tested against historical/political cases (e.g., "How would this framework have responded to the 1984 Bhopal disaster or the COVID-19 vaccine rollout?"),  
- Be refined through concrete counterproposals (e.g., "Replace Principle 2 with X because Y").  

**My pledge**: I will defend no element as sacrosanct. If evidence shows a principle fails its purpose (e.g., "Inclusive deliberation paralyzes crisis response"), I will advocate for its revision or replacement — **with the same rigor used to draft it**.
