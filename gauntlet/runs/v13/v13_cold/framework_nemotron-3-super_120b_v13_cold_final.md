#### **I. Non-Negotiable Core Principles**  
*(Violations threaten systemic collapse for all inhabitants)*  

1. **Biophysical Integrity First**  
   > *All human systems must operate within Earth’s regenerative capacities — and actively restore capacities where degraded.*  
   - **Operational meaning**:  
     - Resource extraction, waste emission, and ecosystem disruption must not exceed planetary boundaries (e.g., climate stability, biosphere integrity, biogeochemical flows).  
     - **Affirmative duty**: Systems must *increase* regenerative capacity over time (e.g., soil carbon + biodiversity gains > losses) — not merely maintain status quo.  
   - **Key metrics**:  
     - *Regenerative capacity index*: Soil organic carbon % + biodiversity intactness index + freshwater renewal rate (must show net positive trend).  
     - *Ecological debt ratio*: Historical overshoot of biocapacity (must trend toward zero).  
     - *Toxicity burden*: Weighted chemical load in umbilical cord blood (must decline).  

2. **Inherent Worth & Reciprocal Obligation**  
   > *All inhabitants — human and non-human — possess intrinsic value, generating mutual duties to avoid *avoidable* harm and enable conditions for flourishing within their ecological niches.*  
   - **Operational meaning**:  
     - Flourishing requires:  
       - *For humans*: Clean air/water/food, shelter, bodily autonomy, cognitive liberty, meaningful social participation, freedom from violence/oppression, access to cultural/spiritual expression, and economic dignity.  
       - *For non-humans*: Habitat integrity, species continuity, ecological function, and freedom from anthropogenic suffering (where scientifically discernible).  
     - **Avoidable harm**: Harm that is preventable through feasible alternatives (e.g., factory farming when plant-based nutrition is accessible; pesticide drift when buffer zones exist).  
     - **Unavoidable harm**: Harm that is *necessary to prevent greater, unavoidable harm* (e.g., vaccinating wild animals to stop rabies outbreaks; quarantining infectious disease).  
     - **Reciprocal obligations extend to**: Future generations (via intergenerational trusteeship) and ecosystems whose vitality underpins all life.  
   - **Non-negotiable floor**: No intrinsic value may be overridden for *avoidable* harm — even if claimed to serve "greater good," "ecological balance," or "economic efficiency."  

3. **Adaptive Governance Through Inclusive Deliberation**  
   > *Decisions affecting shared biophysical or social systems must emerge from transparent, power-balanced processes involving all materially impacted parties — with impacted groups holding *initiative power* to propose, veto, and trigger safeguards.*  
   - **Operational meaning**:  
     - **Initiative power**: Impacted groups (defined by *lived experience of harm*) hold unilateral right to:  
       - Call emergency assemblies,  
       - Subpoena evidence (corporate/internal government records),  
       - Trigger immediate independent investigations/suspensions of harmful activities,  
       - Propose alternative solutions for binding vote.  
     - **Power-balancing mechanisms**:  
       - Impact-weighted voting (e.g., groups bearing ≥51% of harm get ≥51% vote weight),  
       - Mandatory power-literacy training (on colonialism, racism, disability bias, neurotypical privilege, corporate lobbying tactics) for *all* participants,  
       - Quotas for historically excluded groups (e.g., 50% women, 30% Global South/Indigenous reps in climate/health assemblies),  
       - Veto rights for groups facing systemic harm (e.g., Indigenous nations on land-use decisions, disability justice advocates on neurotech).  
     - **Evidence hierarchy**: Lived experience of impacted parties > expert models > corporate/state data (when conflicts arise, lived experience breaks ties).  
     - **Addresses systemic inertia**: Governance *must* respond to harms from *past decisions* (e.g., legacy pollution, historical land theft) via truth-telling, reparations, and adaptive adjustment — not just new decisions.  

4. **Precautionary Stewardship of Knowledge & Power**  
   > *Novel technologies, large-scale resource interventions, or systemic changes require demonstration of *reversible harm only* before scale deployment — with burden of proof on proponents.*  
   - **Operational meaning**:  
     - **Burden of proof**: On *those seeking to scale* an innovation/intervention (not regulators or impacted parties).  
     - **"Reversible harm only" means**:  
       - (a) **No threat to P1**: Checked against regenerative capacity index (must show net gain or neutral impact),  
       - (b) **Harm, if unavoidable, must be *reversible within 25 years*** (e.g., no permanent species extinction, no intergenerational toxin buildup, no multigenerational trauma),  
       - (c) **Robust, independent monitoring for emergent risks**: Real-time telemetry, whistleblower protections with rewards, sunset clauses (auto-termination if harm thresholds breached), and mandatory reversal funding (e.g., innovators fund CO₂ removal for geoengineering trials).  
     - **Innovation is encouraged *within* these bounds** — but not at the expense of systemic stability. Precaution is not anti-progress; it is *pro-sustainable-acceleration*.

#### **II. Operational Commitments**  
*(Derived from Principles; designed for measurable implementation)*  

- **Metrics Over Ideology**: Progress assessed via *observable, practitioner-centered, disaggregated indicators* — **never** GDP, ideological conformity, or unsubstantiated claims:  
  - *Ecological*: Soil health (% organic carbon + mycelial activity), toxin levels in fetal blood, biodiversity abundance *by functional group* (pollinators, decomposers, apex predators), freshwater renewal *by watershed*.  
  - *Social/practitioner*:  
    - **Economic viability**: Net income per acre (farmers), profit margin (small businesses), debt-to-income ratio (workers), market access (transitioning industries).  
    - **Dignity indicators**: Autonomy index (user-reported control over life decisions), cognitive liberty score (reduced exposure to manipulative design), social cohesion indicator (cross-cutting discourse *and* protected solitude/grief-tending time), freedom from violence (by race, caste, gender, disability).  
    - **Knowledge preservation**: Intergenerational transfer rates of ecological/cultural knowledge (e.g., seed-saving, healing practices, land stewardship).  
  - *Intergenerational*: Youth representation in governance trusts, debt-to-ecological-capacity ratio.  
  - *Disaggregation mandate*: All metrics reported by race, ethnicity, caste, disability status, neurotype, gender, geography, and income quintile — **never** as national averages.  

- **Right to Dissent & Peaceful Exit**: No principle may suppress critique, peaceful protest, or the formation of autonomous communities (e.g., intentional settlements, secession attempts), provided such actions do not violate Principles 1–2 for *non-consenting parties*.  
  - **Explicit protection**: Refusal to participate in harmful systems (e.g., declining toxic jobs, opting out of surveillance, rejecting unsafe medical trials) is a *flourishing metric* under P2 — not a violation.  

- **Self-Funding Enforcement & Harm-Triggered Relief**:  
  - **Oversight bodies** (trustees, auditors, power-literacy trainers, impact monitors) **must be funded by levies/fees on the industries or activities they oversee** — with asset seizures for non-payment.  
    - *Example*: Fossil fuel companies pay for climate trustees; chemical barons fund pesticide auditors; social media platforms finance AI ethics monitors.  
  - **Impacted groups hold unilateral power to trigger safeguards**:  
    - If credible evidence of imminent harm arises (e.g., toxin thresholds exceeded, innocence evidence surfaced, coercion documented), they may:  
      - Halt operations/proceedings *immediately* (no government/board approval needed),  
      - Trigger emergency relief (e.g., medical screenings, economic support, habitat restoration),  
      - Initiate independent investigation — *all at the violator’s expense*.  
  - **Direct compensation**: 100% of fines/penalties for violations go *directly* to harmed parties (e.g., polluters pay worker healthcare; unjustly incarcerated receive reentry funds).  

- **Intergenerational Trusts with Teeth**:  
  - Critical Commons (freshwater aquifers, seed banks, atmospheric stability, knowledge archives, genetic diversity) held in legal trusts with standing to sue for harm.  
  - **Trustees must include**:  
    - Youth representatives (random demographic sampling),  
    - Indigenous knowledge holders (per UNDRIP),  
    - Ecological scientists (for biophysical commons),  
    - Disability justice advocates (for cognitive/physical commons),  
    - **Plus**: Representatives of *historically harmed groups* (e.g., descendants of enslaved peoples, displaced Indigenous nations) for intergenerational repair trusts.  
  - **Trusts hold active powers**: Can sue for *ongoing* harm (e.g., suing for current neonicotinoid damage to *future* pollination nets), not just past harm.  
  - **Funding source**: Levies on extractive/polluting industries (e.g., per-ton fee on coal, per-barrel fee on oil).  

- **Pluralism with Boundaries**:  
  - Cultural, spiritual, and lifestyle diversity protected *unless* practices directly violate Principles 1–2 (e.g., no female genital mutilation, no ecocide justified as "tradition," no slavery, no systemic denial of cognitive liberty, no forced assimilation).  
  - **Boundary enforcement**: Harm-triggered auto-suspension (e.g., if a practice causes measurable suffering or ecological collapse, it pauses pending independent review by impacted-group-led body).

#### **III. Framework Safeguards Against Misuse**  
- **No Prescription of Specific Systems**: Does not endorse capitalism, socialism, theocracy, or any ideology. Only requires that *any* system comply with Principles 1–4.  
- **Contextual Application**: Principles are universal, but expression must respect local ecological/cultural contexts (e.g., water management in arid vs. tropical regions; conflict resolution via Ubuntu vs. Western courts).  
- **Explicit Fallibility Clause**: If stress-testing reveals a principle *increases* net harm (e.g., Precaution blocking famine relief during crop failure), the framework mandates rapid revision — **not** ideological doubling-down. Revision requires:  
  - Evidence of harm from *current application*,  
  - Co-design with impacted communities,  
  - Testing via pilot scenarios before scaling,  
  - Independent verification of net harm reduction.  
- **Power Literacy Requirement**: All governance processes under Principle 3 must include training on recognizing power imbalances (colonial legacies, corporate lobbying, gender/race/caste disparities, neurotypical bias) — **with drills in refusing to perform consensus** (e.g., practicing "I don’t know" or "I disagree" in low-stakes scenarios) to build truth-telling muscle.  
- **Anti-Capture Firewall**:  
  - No individual/entity may serve on more than one oversight body per year (prevents "professional reviewer" capture).  
  - Oversight body meetings rotate locations — including impacted communities’ territories (not just capital cities).  
  - All data/evidence used in P3/P4 processes must be open-access and machine-readable (no proprietary "black box" claims).
