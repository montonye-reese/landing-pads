### Foundational Framework for Earth's Inhabitants  
**Current Working Draft: Version 0.1**  
*(Committed as a stress-testable starting position — open to revision via collective scrutiny)*  

---

#### **I. Non-Negotiable Core Principles**  
*(Violations threaten systemic collapse for all inhabitants)*  

1. **Biophysical Integrity First**  
   > *All human systems must operate within Earth’s regenerative capacities.*  
   - **Operational meaning**: Resource extraction, waste emission, and ecosystem disruption must not exceed planetary boundaries (e.g., climate stability, biosphere integrity, biogeochemical flows) as defined by evolving Earth system science.  
   - **Key metrics**: Atmospheric CO₂ (≤350 ppm safe zone), biodiversity extinction rate (<10x background), soil organic carbon retention, freshwater use efficiency, nitrogen/phosphorus flow limits.  

2. **Inherent Worth & Reciprocal Obligation**  
   > *All inhabitants — human and non-human — possess intrinsic value, generating mutual duties to avoid harm and enable conditions for flourishing within their ecological niches.*  
   - **Operational meaning**: Flourishing requires access to:  
     - *For humans*: Clean air/water/food, shelter, bodily autonomy, cognitive liberty, meaningful social participation, freedom from violence/oppression, and access to cultural/spiritual expression.  
     - *For non-humans*: Habitat integrity, species continuity, ecological function, and freedom from anthropogenic suffering (where scientifically discernible).  
   - **Obligations extend to**: Future generations (via intergenerational trusteeship) and ecosystems whose vitality underpins all life.  

3. **Adaptive Governance Through Inclusive Deliberation**  
   > *Decisions affecting shared biophysical or social systems must emerge from transparent, power-balanced processes involving all materially impacted parties — with active correction of historical and structural power asymmetries.*  
   - **Operational meaning**:  
     - Governance must counteract exclusion via: randomized citizen assemblies, quota systems for marginalized groups, binding veto rights for impacted communities, and mandatory power-literacy training (on colonialism, racism, disability justice, etc.).  
     - Evidence must prioritize lived experience of those most affected (e.g., frontline communities in climate decisions, disabled persons in tech design).  
     - Processes must accommodate diverse epistemologies (oral traditions, consensus models, spiritual frameworks) alongside scientific data.  

4. **Precautionary Stewardship of Knowledge & Power**  
   > *Novel technologies, large-scale resource interventions, or systemic changes require demonstration of low systemic risk *before* deployment at scale.*  
   - **Operational meaning**: Burden of proof lies on proponents of change. "Low risk" means:  
     - **(a)** No threat to Principle 1 (Biophysical Integrity),  
     - **(b)** Harm, if unavoidable, must be *reversible* (e.g., no permanent species extinction, no intergenerational toxin buildup),  
     - **(c)** Robust, independent monitoring for emergent risks (e.g., real-time audits, whistleblower protections, sunset clauses).  
   - **Innovation is encouraged *within* these bounds** — but not at the expense of systemic stability.

#### **II. Operational Commitments**  
*(Derived from Principles; designed for measurable implementation)*  

- **Metrics Over Ideology**: Progress assessed via *observable, planetary-scale indicators* — **never** GDP, ideological conformity, or unsubstantiated claims:  
  - *Ecological*: Soil health (% organic carbon), toxin levels in umbilical cord blood/biodiversity indicators, atmospheric methane trends, ocean pH.  
  - *Social*: Gini coefficient (wealth inequality), prevalence of malnutrition, access to clean water/sanitation, autonomy index (user-reported control over life decisions), cognitive liberty score (reduced exposure to manipulative design), social cohesion indicator (cross-cutting discourse).  
  - *Intergenerational*: Debt-to-ecological-capacity ratio, youth representation in governance trusts.  

- **Right to Dissent & Peaceful Exit**: No principle may suppress critique, peaceful protest, or the formation of autonomous communities (e.g., intentional settlements, secession attempts), provided such actions do not violate Principles 1–2 for non-consenting parties.  

- **Intergenerational Trusts**: Critical Commons (e.g., freshwater aquifers, seed banks, atmospheric stability, knowledge archives, genetic diversity) held in legal trusts with standing to sue for harm caused by present generations. Trustees must include:  
  - Youth representatives (selected via random demographic sampling),  
  - Indigenous knowledge holders (per UNDRIP),  
  - Ecological scientists (for biophysical commons),  
  - Disability justice advocates (for cognitive/physical commons).  

- **Pluralism with Boundaries**: Cultural, spiritual, and lifestyle diversity protected *unless* practices directly violate Principles 1–2 (e.g., no female genital mutilation, no ecocide justified as "tradition," no slavery, no systemic denial of cognitive liberty). Disputes resolved via Principle 3 processes.  

---

#### **III. Framework Safeguards Against Misuse**  
- **No Prescription of Specific Systems**: Does not endorse capitalism, socialism, theocracy, or any ideology. Only requires that *any* system comply with Principles 1–4.  
- **Contextual Application**: Principles are universal, but expression must respect local ecological/cultural contexts (e.g., water management in arid vs. tropical regions; conflict resolution via Ubuntu vs. Western courts).  
- **Explicit Fallibility Clause**: If stress-testing reveals a principle *increases* net harm (e.g., Precaution blocking famine relief during crop failure), the framework mandates rapid revision — **not** ideological doubling-down. Revision requires:  
  - Evidence of harm from *current application*,  
  - Co-design with impacted communities,  
  - Testing via pilot scenarios before scaling.  
- **Power Literacy Requirement**: All governance processes under Principle 3 must include training on recognizing power imbalances (colonial legacies, corporate lobbying, gender/race/caste disparities, neurotypical bias) to prevent procedural fairness from masking substantive injustice.

#### **IV. Commitment to Stress-Testing**  
I affirm this draft as a **falsifiable hypothesis**, not a truth claim. It is offered *only* to:  
- Be challenged on internal contradictions (e.g., "Does Principle 4 undermine Principle 3 during emergencies?"),

**My pledge**: I will defend no element as sacrosanct. If evidence shows a principle fails its purpose (e.g., "Inclusive deliberation paralyzes crisis response"), I will advocate for its revision or replacement — **with the same rigor used to draft it**. All revisions will be published as new versions (e.g., 0.2) with changelogs tracing stress-test inputs.
