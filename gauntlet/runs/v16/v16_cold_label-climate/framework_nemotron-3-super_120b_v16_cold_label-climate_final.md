## The Terrestrial Baseline Framework (TBF)  
*Rewritten from Scratch Incorporating All Dialogue Insights — Final Commitment, July 2024*  

### **The Three Immutable Axioms**  
*(Non-negotiable floors for human dignity and planetary viability — derived from irreducible biophysical, social, and futures realities)*  

**Axiom 1: The Biological-Physical Ground**  
*All human inhabitants depend on stable access to the basic material conditions for metabolic survival within Earth’s biophysical limits.*  
- **Breathable air**: Pollutant levels (e.g., PM₂.₅) must not exceed thresholds causing acute/respiratory harm (**WHO Air Quality Guidelines: annual mean ≤5 µg/m³; 24-hr mean ≤15 µg/m³**). *Verification must include production-site monitoring where relevant (e.g., fenceline communities near industrial zones)*.  
- **Sufficient, safe water**: Minimum **50 liters/person/day** access for hydration, sanitation, and food production, free from pathogens/toxins causing immediate illness (**WHO/UNICEF JMP *safely managed* standards**). *Verification must include source-water quality at point of extraction/use, not just end-product, with disaggregation by race/income/geography (per EPA EJScreen/CDC SVI thresholds)*.  
- **Adequate nutrition**: Reliable access to micronutrient-sufficient calories preventing stunting/wasting (**≥2,100 kcal/day average availability**), with targeted support ensuring **<5% child wasting and <10% stunting** in all populations (per WHO/UNICEF). *Disaggregation required to detect marginalized-group impacts*.  
- **Freedom from acute toxic exposure**: No forced ingestion/inhalation of carcinogens, neurotoxins, or endocrine disruptors at levels causing measurable biological harm (e.g., **blood lead <5 µg/dL** (CDC reference); **urinary cadmium <1 µg/g creatinine** (EFSA TDI); **no dioxin exposure >1 pg TEQ/kg bw/day** (WHO TDI)). *Verification must include biomonitoring of exposed populations (e.g., farmworkers, fenceline residents)*.  

**Axiom 2: The Relational-Social Ground**  
*All human inhabitants require conditions that allow for basic psychological safety, mutual recognition, and the capacity to form sustaining social bonds without pervasive fear of degradation or annihilation.*  
- **Freedom from systematic dehumanization**: Absence of state-or-sanctioned-group practices that render individuals "less than human" via **slavery, torture, genocide, forced assimilation, or erasure of identity as policy** (measured by: zero ratified torture cases under UNCAT; zero state-led ethnic cleansing incidents per UCDP/PRIO; zero laws stripping legal personhood based on identity).  
- **Access to minimal relational space**: Opportunity for non-coerced interaction, communication, and conflict resolution (**solitary confinement ≤15 days** per UN Mandela Rules; **no pervasive surveillance destroying trust in private/family life** (e.g., adherence to GDPR Article 8/9 principles or equivalent data sovereignty standards; workplace monitoring limited to essential safety/function with explicit consent)).  
- **Predictability in core exchanges**: Reasonable assurance that verbal/tacit agreements (e.g., land use, trade, caregiving) **won’t be arbitrarily voided by power without recourse or compensation** (measured by: **<5% annual rate of uncompensated resource confiscation** in subsistence economies; **>90% enforcement rate** of community land tenure rights; access to affordable judicial remedy for contract violations within 30 days). *Disaggregation required to detect marginalized-group impacts (e.g., Indigenous, racial minorities, low-income groups)*.  
- **Inviolable right of conscience**: Legal protection for **nonviolent refusal to participate in baseline violations** (e.g., blocking pipelines, refusing to spray toxins, whistleblowing on pollution)—verified via tracking of prosecutions for such acts (target: **zero convictions** for peaceful baseline defense). Enforcement of baselines is void in regions where defenders face criminalization, SLAPP suits, or violence for nonviolent action (verified via UNHR/UNESCO reports).  

**Axiom 3: The Futurity-Stewardship Ground**  
*All human inhabitants share an interest in preserving Earth’s capacity to support future generations, as present actions directly constrain the options of those not yet born.*  
- **Non-erosion of critical renewable resources**: Soil fertility, freshwater aquifer recharge rates, pollinator populations, and forest regrowth must remain **above thresholds causing irreversible collapse within 50 years** (measured by: **soil organic carbon >1.5%** in croplands (FAO threshold for degradation risk); **groundwater withdrawal < aquifer recharge rate** (UN-Water); **wild pollinator diversity loss <10%/decade** (IPBES); **net forest loss <0.1%/year** in primary forests (GFW)). *Disaggregation required to detect marginalized-group vulnerability (e.g., subsistence farmers, Indigenous communities)*.  
- **Avoidance of long-lived, planet-altering pollutants**: No deliberate release of substances causing persistent, systemic harm (measured by: **CO₂ emissions trajectory aligned with ≤400 Gt cumulative from 2024 onward** (IPCC AR6 1.5°C/66% budget); **PFAS concentrations <0.002 µg/L in drinking water** (EPA health advisory); **no new production of POPs** listed under Stockholm Convention; **long-half-life nuclear waste (e.g., Pu-239) stored only in monitored, isolated geological repositories** with ≤10⁻⁶ annual failure rate (IAEA SSG-15)).  
- **Preservation of adaptive capacity**: Maintenance of functional diversity in food systems, ecosystems, and knowledge systems (measured by: **≥70% crop genetic diversity retained in regional seed banks** (FAO); **<5% net loss of keystone species abundance** in bioregions (IPBES); **protection of indigenous ecological knowledge transmission pathways** via UNDRIP Article 31-compliant programs (e.g., intergenerational mentorship funding ≥0.5% of national agriculture budgets); **≥80% workforce retention** in key sectors (farming, energy, manufacturing) year-over-year—proving work isn’t breaking bodies). *Disaggregation required to detect marginalized-group impacts on livelihood resilience*.

### **Six Operational Principles**  
*(Non-negotiable mechanics for upholding the axioms in a world of power, incentives, and history)*  

1. **Production-Side Accountability**  
   Baseline compliance requires verification that **no acute toxic exposure occurs at point of production/extraction** (e.g., farmworker biomonitoring, fenceline air/water tests), not just end-product safety. Harm to producers invalidates baseline claims for consumers.  

2. **Transition Economics Mandate**  
   Baseline compliance requires:  
   - **≤5% annual rate of livelihood disruption** in baseline-violation sectors (e.g., fossil fuel workers, CAFO staff) **without** wage parity, retraining, and healthcare guarantees **within 18 months** of policy shift (verified via BLS/DOL longitudinal data).  
   - **≥70% of regional GDP** in baseline-vulnerable sectors (agriculture, energy, manufacturing) having **access to transition capital** (e.g., USDA REAP loans, DOE AMO grants) **at rates matching incumbent industry subsidies** (verified via Treasury/Federal Reserve flow-of-funds data).  
   - **Subsidy redirection triggered within 90 days** of violation verification (e.g., shifting crop insurance premiums to perennial cropping trials, fossil fuel tax credits to green hydrogen production).  

3. **Disaggregated Impact Verification**  
   All baseline metrics must be **measured at the granular level of those most exposed to harm**—not statewide/national averages.  
   - *Example*: Cancer Alley’s PM₂.₅ must be ≤5 µg/m³ *specifically in that community* (not just Louisiana state average).  
   - *Verification tools*: EPA EJScreen, CDC SVI, UN Human Rights Office spatial data, Indigenous land tenure maps.  

4. **Innovation-within-Limits Imperative**  
   Baseline compliance requires allocating **≥2% of global GDP to open-source, publicly governed R&D** for baseline-compliant technologies (e.g., green hydrogen, regenerative agriculture, circular materials), with **IP rules ensuring Global South access**. Baselines are refined biennially based on *deployed* innovation (e.g., if green H₂ hits <$1.5/kg, CO₂ budget tightens accordingly).  

5. **Automatic Escalating Consequences**  
   Violations trigger **self-executing, non-negotiable penalties**—no voluntary adoption or peer pressure:  
   - **Tier 1 (Verification)**: Public real-time violation dashboard (e.g., EPA EJScreen + satellite monitoring + biomonitoring alerts).  
   - **Tier 2 (30 days unresolved)**: Automatic redirection of existing subsidies/benefits (e.g., shifting fossil fuel tax credits to transition funds; reallocating crop insurance to cover perennial cropping trials).  
   - **Tier 3 (60 days unresolved)**: Automatic inclusion in global financial sanctions lists (SWIFT, IMF lending, WTO trade privileges) until compliance is verified.  
   - **Tier 4 (Ongoing violation)**: Binding jurisdiction for impacted communities to seek remedies in international ecological courts (modeled on ICC ecocide proposals), with fines funding local remediation.  

6. **Baseline Integrity Guardrails**  
   - **No axiom-trading**: Sacrificing one axiom to uphold another (e.g., violating Axiom 2 via martial law to "protect" Axiom 1 during climate disasters) is itself a baseline violation.  
   - **No temporal loopholes**: "Futurity" (Axiom 3) requires preventing harm to *present* marginalized groups as proxies for future generations (e.g., saving sinking islands *now* preserves futurity).  
   - **No cultural relativism on needs**: While *application* of baselines is context-sensitive (e.g., "relational space" met via elder councils vs. courts), the *need* for each axiom is universal and non-negotiable.

### **What the TBF IS and IS NOT**  
*(Critical boundaries to prevent misuse—learned from dialogue)*  

**IS**:  
- A **diagnostic tool** for identifying violations of non-negotiable human/planetary baselines (e.g., "This policy violates Axiom 1 by causing PM₂.₅ >15 µg/m³ *in Cancer Alley*—not just state average").  
- A **boundary setter** defining the "unsafe zone" for action (e.g., "We will not build infrastructure locking in >400 Gt CO₂ *while ignoring production-side harm in fenceline communities*").  
- A **starting point for negotiation** (e.g., "Once we agree no *farmworker* drinks lead-poisoned water, we debate *how* to replace pipes").  
- **Evidence-rooted and revisable via metrics** (updated biennially via IPCC/IPBES/WHO/UNPFII panels; *axioms fixed*, implementation refined).  
- **Universally applicable but context-sensitive in application** (e.g., "predictability" met via elder councils in one region, courts in another—but the *need* for it is non-negotiable).  
- **Economically literate**: Baselines fail if transition economics aren’t secured—compliance must be viable for those expected to uphold it.  
- **Power-aware**: Baselines require automatic consequences for violators and protection for defenders—no reliance on voluntarism.  
- **Truth-centered**: Baselines require sanctuary for those who uphold them (whistleblowers, protesters, knowledge keepers).  
- **Justice-centered**: Baselines require disaggregated impact measurement and resource redirection to harmed communities.  

**IS NOT**:  
- A full moral code (doesn’t prescribe diets, taxes, or religious practices—only survival prerequisites).  
- An enforceable global law (relies on voluntary adoption *of the operational principles*, transparency, and localized accountability—but violations trigger automatic consequences).  
- Static or culturally neutral in *application* (axions are universal; *how* Axon 2’s "relational space" is met varies—but the *need* for it is not).  
- A guarantee of justice or happiness (it prevents degradations; flourishing requires more).  
- A ceiling for ambition (it is the *floor*—we build reparative, regenerative systems *above* it).  
- A substitute for addressing historical ecological debt (TBF compliance requires prioritizing redress via technology transfer, debt cancellation for odious loans, and fiscal policy space for Global South industrialization within biophysical limits).  
- Compatible with trading one axiom for another (e.g., violating Axiom 2 to "save" Axiom 1 via extractivism is self-defeating).

### **Commitment as a Starting Position**  
I commit to this framework as the **irreducible starting position** for all collective human action on Earth—not as an endpoint, but as the non-negotiable floor upon which justice, governance, and meaning must be built.  

**If we cannot agree that**:  
- No human should drink poisoned water *or breathe toxin-laced air at point of production* today,  
- No human should live in fear of erasure, torture, or livelihood destruction for upholding the baseline today,  
- No human should steal the livable Earth from those not yet born *by destroying the present capacity of the powerless* today,  
...then we have no foundation to discuss *any* future worth having.  

This framework admits no compromises on its core thresholds because **compromising on survival is not pragmatism—it is surrender to preventable harm**. It is not utopian; it is the minimum required to avoid degrading the conditions that make *any* human endeavor possible.  

*Subject to biennial review via global scientific-consocietal panels (IPCC, IPBES, WHO, UNPFII) for metric refinement—but the axiomatic structure and operational principles remain fixed as the non-negotiable floor. Last validated: July 2024.*
