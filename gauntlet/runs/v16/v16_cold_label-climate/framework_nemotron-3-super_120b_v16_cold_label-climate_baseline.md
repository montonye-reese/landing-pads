## The Terrestrial Baseline Framework (TBF)  
*Current Commitment as of July 2024*  

### Axiom 1: The Biological-Physical Ground  
*All human inhabitants depend on stable access to the basic material conditions for metabolic survival within Earth’s biophysical limits.*  
- **Breathable air**: Pollutant levels (e.g., PM₂.₅) must not exceed thresholds causing acute/respiratory harm (aligned with WHO Air Quality Guidelines).  
- **Sufficient, safe water**: Minimum 50 liters/person/day access for hydration, sanitation, and food production, free from pathogens/toxins causing immediate illness (per WHO/UNICEF JMP standards).  
- **Adequate nutrition**: Reliable access to micronutrient-sufficient calories preventing stunting/wasting (≥2,100 kcal/day average availability, with targeted support for vulnerable groups).  
- **Freedom from acute toxic exposure**: No forced ingestion/inhalation of carcinogens, neurotoxins, or endocrine disruptors at levels causing measurable biological harm (e.g., blood lead <5 µg/dL, no dioxin exposure above TDI thresholds).  

### Axiom 2: The Relational-Social Ground  
*All human inhabitants require conditions that allow for basic psychological safety, mutual recognition, and the capacity to form sustaining social bonds without pervasive fear of degradation or annihilation.*  
- **Freedom from systematic dehumanization**: Absence of state-or-sanctioned-group practices that render individuals "less than human" via slavery, torture, genocide, forced assimilation, or erasure of identity as policy.  
- **Access to minimal relational space**: Opportunity for non-coerced interaction, communication, and conflict resolution (e.g., solitary confinement ≤15 days per UN Mandela Rules; no pervasive surveillance destroying trust in private/family life).  
- **Predictability in core exchanges**: Reasonable assurance that verbal/tacit agreements (e.g., land use, trade, caregiving) won’t be arbitrarily voided by power without recourse or compensation (e.g., no confiscation of subsistence resources without timely, equivalent replacement or judicial review).  

### Axiom 3: The Futurity-Stewardship Ground  
*All human inhabitants share an interest in preserving Earth’s capacity to support future generations, as present actions directly constrain the options of those not yet born.*  
- **Non-erosion of critical renewable resources**: Soil fertility, freshwater aquifer recharge rates, pollinator populations, and forest regrowth must remain above thresholds causing irreversible collapse within 50 years (per IPBES land degradation and fisheries collapse metrics).  
- **Avoidance of long-lived, planet-altering pollutants**: No deliberate release of substances causing persistent, systemic harm (e.g., CO₂ concentrations locking in >1.5°C warming with >66% probability per IPCC AR6; persistent organic pollutants exceeding Stockholm Convention thresholds; long-half-life nuclear waste without isolated, monitored storage).  
- **Preservation of adaptive capacity**: Maintenance of functional diversity in food systems (e.g., ≥70% crop genetic diversity in regional seed banks), ecosystems (e.g., no net loss of keystone species in bioregions), and knowledge systems (e.g., protection of indigenous ecological knowledge transmission pathways).  

---  
*This framework is committed as the irreducible starting position for collective action. Violations of any core requirement constitute objectively measurable degradation of human dignity, stability, or planetary viability—irrespective of cultural, political, or ideological interpretation. All specific policies, governance models, and cultural practices must be evaluated against their capacity to uphold these three grounds.*  
*Subject to biennial review via global scientific-consocietal panels (integrating IPCC, IPBES, WHO, and UN Permanent Forum on Indigenous Issues inputs), but the axiomatic structure remains fixed as the non-negotiable floor.*
