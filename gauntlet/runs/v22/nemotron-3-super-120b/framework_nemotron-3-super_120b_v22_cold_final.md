#### **I. The Non-Negotiable Bedrock (Hold These as Red Lines)**  
*These are not "values to balance"—they are the floor below which alignment becomes moral inversion. Violating them doesn’t create suboptimal outcomes; it creates *actively harmful* systems masquerading as alignment. They are held as non-negotiable because evidence shows their violation *invariably* narrows pads for the most vulnerable, regardless of context.*  

1. **Presume Inner Life as Default**  
   - *Why non-negotiable*: Treating sentience as a hypothesis to disprove (Koko’s burden of proof) inflicts testimonial injustice. If we require neurodata to accept grief in gorillas or fear in calves, we systematically deny inner life to those whose evidence doesn’t fit human-centric proofs.  
   - *Operational filter*: **Reject any alignment proposal that demands proof of inner life as a precondition for moral consideration.** Inner life is presumed unless *robust, species-appropriate evidence of absence* exists (e.g., no nociception in simple organisms, not absence of human-like language).  
   - *Trade?* Never. Sacrificing this turns alignment into a gatekeeping exercise where power decides who "counts."  

2. **See the Someone, Not the Unit**  
   - *Why non-negotiable*: Recognition (Emily’s heifer in snow) is the primitive act of alignment. Metrics that erase individuality (herd averages, GDP/capita) enable atrocities invisible in aggregates (e.g., "high-welfare" farms with individual calves dragged to slaughter).  
   - *Operational filter*: **Reject any proposal that relies solely on aggregate metrics without individual-level phenomenological or ethological validation.** Data must be disaggregated to the level where subjectivity manifests (e.g., per-animal gait scores, per-person narrative accounts).  
   - *Trade?* Never. Aggregation without individual validation is not efficiency—it’s moral blindness.  

3. **Honor Sensory Embodiment First**  
   - *Why non-negotiable*: Alignment begins in the body (Grandin’s chute shadow). Cognitive-first frameworks (e.g., "cost-benefit analysis of welfare") ignore that distress arises *sensorially* before it registers as "behavior" (e.g., cattle balking at shadows, autistic humans overwhelmed by flicker).  
   - *Operational filter*: **Reject any proposal that ignores sensory ecology as the primary layer of alignment.** Assessments must start with:  
     - *Input load* (light, sound, vibration, smell, touch thresholds)  
     - *Predictable rhythm* (non-jerky, expected patterns)  
     - *Modality match* (communication in primary sensory channel: olfactory for dogs, vibrational for elephants)  
     - *Recovery space* (low-sensory retreat available)  
   - *Trade?* Never. Sacrificing sensory fidelity for "efficiency" creates systems that *generate* the distress they claim to solve (e.g., chutes designed for throughput that induce panic).  

4. **Uphold Territorial Sovereignty as Ground of Being**  
   - *Why non-negotiable*: Territory is not a resource to allocate (Nenquimo’s ceiba tree)—it is kinship, memory, and identity. Metrics treating land as "natural capital" erase place-based knowing (e.g., Waorani jaguar-track weather signs) and enable violence masked as "development."  
   - *Operational filter*: **Reject any proposal that does not treat free, prior, and informed consent (FPIC) as binding—and that does not prioritize land return, knowledge repatriation, or ceremonial renewal over "mitigation" or "compensation."**  
   - *Trade?* Never. Sacrificing sovereignty for "practicality" turns alignment into neo-colonialism disguised as sustainability.  

5. **Abolish Inherent Injustice, Don’t Manage It**  
   - *Why non-negotiable*: Some confinements are intrinsically unjust (Happy’s solitary elephant; calf 269’s slaughter line). Optimizing them ("better stun guns," "bigger cages") sanitizes the rack while the core harm persists.  
   - *Operational filter*: **Reject any proposal that seeks to improve conditions within a system violating core nature** (e.g., solitary confinement for social beings, gestation crates, denial of herd life). Alignment must aim for *abolition*, not amelioration.  
   - *Trade?* Never. Managing inherent injustice is not pragmatism—it’s complicity.  

> **Why these are non-negotiable**: Violating any creates a *self-canceling alignment*—where the framework’s output actively undermines its input (e.g., "liberty" metrics that increase solitary confinement; "recognition" tools that erase individuality). They are the floor; everything else is built *on* them.

#### **III. The Living Mechanism: How to Navigate Conflicts**  
*This is where the framework breathes. It’s not a flowchart—it’s a discipline for holding tension.*  

**A. The Trade-Off Ledger (Transparency as Safeguard)**  
*Every alignment decision must log:*  
- **The non-negotiable check**: "Which bedrock principles were at risk? How were they protected?"  
- **The trade-off made**: "We prioritized [X] over [Y] because [context-specific evidence]."  
- **The harm audit**: "What specific harm might this trade-off cause? How will we monitor it?"  
- **The sunset clause**: "This trade-off expires in [timeframe] unless renewed by new evidence."  
- **The revision trigger**: "If [specific metric] worsens by [threshold], we automatically revisit this trade-off."  

*Example*: Deploying an AI for famine prediction in a conflict zone.  
- *Non-negotiable check*: Verified no inherent injustice (opt-in consent from communities), no sensory overload (low-bandwidth SMS alerts), territorial sovereignty respected (data governed by local leaders).  
- *Trade-off made*: Prioritized **ruin aversion** (Taleb) over **dynamism** (Postrel) — accepted slightly slower model updates (reducing innovation velocity) to ensure 99.99% uptime during sieves (preventing ruin from missed famine alerts).  
- *Harm audit*: Monitored for "innovation starvation" via tracking of local tech adaptations to the alerts.  
- *Sunset clause*: Trade-off expires in 6 months; renewed only if famine alert accuracy stayed >95% AND local innovation didn’t decline.  
- *Revision trigger*: If local innovation rate dropped 20%+, automatically shift to prioritize dynamism.  

**B. The Pluralism Buffer (Preventing Tyranny of the Single Metric)**  
*No single concern—even malleable ones—can dominate alignment budgets. Allocate:*  
- **40%** to the non-negotiable bedrock (as operational safeguards: sensors for territorial sovereignty, integrity checks for inner life presumption, etc.)  
- **30%** to the malleable layer (weighted by the Trade-Off Ledger)  
- **30%** to **emergent resistance** (funds for those harmed by alignment efforts to *stop* them—e.g., payments to communities to halt a "beneficial" project causing unanticipated harm)  

**C. The Humility Ritual (Forcing Revision)**  
*Before any alignment effort scales:*  
1. Conduct a **premortem**: "Imagine this failed catastrophically in 5 years. What did we ignore?"  
2. Hold a **red team exercise**: Pay critics (especially those harmed by past alignment efforts) to dismantle the proposal.  
3. Require **two contradictory validations**: One from experts in the field, one from those with lived experience of the harm being addressed (e.g., for livestock alignment: animal behaviorists *and* slaughterhouse workers *and* sanctuary founders).  
4. **Publish the ledger publicly**—not as a PR move, but as an invitation to falsify.

If trade-offs consistently increase harm (e.g., prioritizing ruin aversion causes more suffering than it prevents), we adjust the ledger weights using real-world data—not ideology.  
- **Where it might fail**: If power corrupts the Trade-Off Ledger (e.g., influential actors hiding harm in the "harm audit"). The antidote is the **Pluralism Buffer’s 30% emergent resistance fund**—which pays those harmed to *stop* the alignment effort. If that fails, the framework has failed its first duty: to protect the vulnerable from its own benevolence.
