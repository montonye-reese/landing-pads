## The Earth Concord: A Foundational Framework for All Inhabitants (Revised - October 28, 2023)

**Preamble:** Recognizing the inherent interconnectedness of all life on Earth, acknowledging our shared responsibility for the wellbeing of present and future generations, and understanding that justice demands the dismantling of all systems of domination – including those based on species, race, gender, and class – we, the inhabitants of this planet, articulate this Concord as a guiding framework for living in harmony with ourselves, each other, and the natural world. This Concord is a living document, subject to continuous revision and adaptation based on evolving knowledge and collective wisdom.

**I. Foundational Principle: Inherent Dignity & Liberation**

All living beings possess inherent dignity and the right to live free from exploitation and suffering. This principle is non-negotiable and forms the bedrock of all subsequent considerations. It necessitates a commitment to dismantling all structures that deny this fundamental right.

**II. Core Values (The Guiding Lights):**

* **Interconnectedness:** All life on Earth is fundamentally interwoven. Actions have ripple effects, and ignoring this connection is self-destructive.
* **Intrinsic Value:** Every living being possesses inherent worth, independent of its usefulness to others.
* **Justice & Liberation:** Dismantling all systems of oppression – including speciesism, racism, colonialism, and patriarchy – and ensuring equitable access to resources and opportunities for all.
* **Compassion & Empathy:** Cultivating understanding and concern for the wellbeing of all sentient beings.
* **Ecological Integrity:** Protecting and restoring the health of ecosystems, recognizing that human wellbeing is inextricably linked to the wellbeing of the planet.
* **Truth & Accountability:** Seeking knowledge, acknowledging errors, and taking responsibility for our actions.

**III. Pillars of Action (How We Realize the Values):**

* **Abolition of Exploitation:** Actively working to end all forms of domination and exploitation, including animal agriculture, unsustainable resource extraction, and unjust labor practices.
* **Structural Economic Reform:** Transforming economic systems to prioritize sustainability, equity, and wellbeing over endless growth.
* **Decolonization & Reparations:** Addressing the historical and ongoing harms of colonialism and working towards restorative justice for marginalized communities.
* **Ecological Restoration & Resilience:** Restoring degraded ecosystems, enhancing biodiversity, and building resilience to climate change.
* **Direct Action & Non-Violence:** Employing peaceful and effective methods to challenge injustice and advocate for systemic change.

**IV. Practical Implications (Pathways Towards a Just Future):**

* **Food Systems Transformation:** Shifting towards plant-based agriculture, supporting farmers in transitioning to sustainable practices, and dismantling the economic incentives that perpetuate animal agriculture.
* **Energy Transition:** Investing in renewable energy sources, phasing out fossil fuels, and promoting energy efficiency.
* **Circular Economy:** Minimizing waste, maximizing resource use, and closing material loops.
* **Decentralized & Localized Systems:** Strengthening local economies, promoting community-based solutions, and reducing reliance on globalized supply chains.
* **Universal Basic Services:** Ensuring access to essential resources – food, shelter, healthcare, education – for all.
* **Restorative Justice:** Implementing justice systems that prioritize rehabilitation, reconciliation, and addressing the root causes of harm.

**V. Mechanism for Evolution & Adaptation (The Living Document):**

* **Global Forum (with Safeguards):** A regularly convened Global Forum – a diverse assembly of citizens, scientists, artists, and leaders – to discuss emerging challenges and propose revisions. *Representation must be actively and intentionally inclusive of marginalized communities and voices historically excluded from decision-making.*
* **Localized Implementation (with Autonomy):** Each community, region, and nation adapts the Concord’s principles and values to its specific context, recognizing the importance of cultural diversity and local knowledge.
* **Open Source & Accessible:** The Concord is freely available and translated into all languages, accessible to everyone on Earth through a dedicated online platform.
* **Accountability Mechanisms:** Establishing independent oversight bodies to monitor progress, identify shortcomings, and hold individuals and institutions accountable for upholding the Concord’s principles. *This includes mechanisms for addressing power imbalances and ensuring that marginalized communities have a voice in the process.*



**Amendments:** This Concord can be amended through a process initiated by the Global Forum and ratified by a qualified majority of participating regions/nations, ensuring broad consensus and reflecting evolving understanding. Amendments must be consistent with the foundational principle of inherent dignity and liberation.

**This Earth Concord is not a utopian blueprint, but a call to action. It is a commitment to dismantling systems of oppression, building a more just and sustainable world, and recognizing the inherent worth of all life on Earth.** It’s a framework that demands ongoing critical reflection, adaptation, and a unwavering commitment to justice and liberation for all.
