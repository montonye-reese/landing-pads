## 3.  Agency Licensing – The Core of “Agency”

1. **Agency Capacity (AC)** – every entity (human, animal, AI, synthetic) is given a measurable **Agency Capacity (AC)** (0‑1) based on observable behaviours (learning speed, autonomy, stress‑response).  
2. **Basic Agency Tokens (BATs)** – each entity with **AC ≥ 0.2** automatically receives **2 × AC BATs** (each BAT = 1 vote, 0.02 % of regional resource, and a right to a Self‑Alignment Audit).  
   *Tokens are **non‑transferable** – they cannot be bought, sold, or inherited.*  
3. **Self‑Alignment Audit (SAA)** – any agent that wants to act (e.g., deploy an autonomous robot, release a synthetic microbe, or train a large AI) must submit its model to the **Global AI‑Safety Audit**. If it passes, it receives a **bounded autonomy envelope** (max 0.02 % of global compute, ≤ 5 % compute for non‑public‑good uses).  
4. **Self‑Alignment Audit outcome** is stored in the Knowledge Graph; any future deployment must reference the audit hash.  

> **Result:** No entity can hide behind a “big‑tech” licence; the system will *automatically* block a rogue AI that tries to exceed its autonomy envelope because the **Compute‑Rights Ledger (CRL)** will refuse to give it any more compute budget.

## 3.  The Compute‑Budget (DSPF) – The Heart of the Tech Layer

1. **Digital‑Sector Planetary Footprint (DSPF)** – a real‑time accounting of *energy*, *rare‑earth material*, and *water* used for *all* AI and computation.  
2. **Compute‑Rights Ledger (CRL)** – a blockchain ledger that issues **Compute‑Rights (CR) tokens**.  
   * Each entity (human, AI, collective) can *own* a fixed share of the compute budget (no one can own more than **0.02 % of total compute**).  
   * Every AI/ML project must **register** a use‑case; the GASO evaluates it against the **Ethical Agency Envelope**.  
   * If the AI is granted the licence, the compute it uses is *tracked*; excess usage (beyond the envelope) automatically *shuts down* the compute nodes.  

> *This is not a “soft cap” – it is a hard, automated enforcement mechanism.*  

> **Why it works:** The charter does **not** try to ban AI; it *channels* the AI’s power into public‑good sectors (climate modelling, disease detection, sustainable agriculture). The *only* way to “cheat” is to hide compute in secret data‑centers, but every compute node is logged in the Knowledge Graph; any unregistered compute instantly flags a **Resilience Act** that can shut down the offending servers (via a *kill‑switch* built into the AI’s runtime).

## 5.  The Governance Engine – Decision Flow (real‑time)

1. **Data Capture** – Global Sensor Network (air, water, soil, biodiversity, AI metrics, compute‑energy) streams into the **Knowledge Graph**.  
2. **Analytics** – The **Systemic Integrity Engine (SIE)** computes the **Risk Vector (RV)**: (PHI, IWI, EAS, DSPF, DII, FPS).  
3. **Trigger** – If **any two** of PHI, IWI, EAS drop below their warning zones for **30 days**, the **Resilience Act** is automatically launched.  

**Resilience Act** does three things automatically:  

1. **Re‑allocate resources** (compute, water, habitat) from the offending sector to regenerative projects.  
2. **Suspend the offending activity** (e.g., a mining permit, a drone‑fleet, an AI that exceeds its BAE).  
3. **Award Learning Credits** to the communities whose rights were harmed (these credits can only be spent on protection, restoration, or cultural projects).  

*All of this happens automatically – there is no need for a parliament to vote after the trigger is crossed.*

## 4.  The “Compute‑Budget” (DSPF) – Hard‑wired but adaptable

1. **Global Compute Budget** = 5 % of total global compute is **public‑good compute** (climate modelling, disease detection, biodiversity monitoring). This portion **cannot be transferred**; it is *pre‑allocated* to public‑good projects.  

2. **Remaining compute** (the rest of the 0.02 % of total global compute) is allocated **by the AI Safety Board** based on a *public‑good impact* score.  

3. **Any AI that exceeds its BAE** (compute, energy, or compute‑budget) is **automatically shut down** by the **AI‑Safety Engine** that monitors the **Digital‑Sector Planetary Footprint (DSPF)**.  

*Result:* Even a super‑intelligent AI that wants to “solve” climate change cannot do so if it would breach the planetary boundary; it will be forced to allocate its compute to *public‑good* activities first, and the extra compute can be “saved” for later only after the PHI, IWI, and EAS are stable.

## 4.  How the charter *actually* prevents the “old‑school” failures

1. **Hard caps on compute and resources** (DSPF, BAE) make it *physically impossible* for a tech monopoly to keep a monopoly over resources.  
2. **Hard caps on subsidies** – the charter forces the **Resource Allocation Grants** to be *capped at 30 % of any individual entity’s resource use*; any excess is automatically taken by the charter and re‑allocated to a **public‑good**.  
3. **Cultural‑rights protection** – no decision that harms a cultural practice can be approved without the community’s **cultural‑impact token** (CIT) being present. This token can be *re‑claimed* at any time.  
4. **Future‑Penalty Score (FPS)** – the charter automatically taxes any activity that threatens the planetary future (e.g., a new oil pipeline) by diverting its compute and money to the **Inter‑Generational Trust**.  

These mechanisms **close the loopholes** that let the rich keep the status quo while the environment collapses.
