## 1. Foundational Principle – **Radical Relationality**
> **“All life is a single, inter‑woven web.  No being is separate from any other.”**  
> *Every policy must begin from the premise that humans, non‑human animals, ecosystems, and future generations **are co‑owners of the same living system**.*  
> *If any part of the web is harmed, the entire web is weakened; therefore **any decision must be evaluated by the question**:*  

> **“Who is excluded, and how will that exclusion be repaired?”**  

*The answer, not the answer itself, is the first law of the Protocol.*

## 3.  Decision‑Making Mechanics (The Mechanics of Humility)

1. **The “Who Is Missing?” Pause**  
   - Before any policy proposal is drafted, the facilitator must **explicitly list** all groups *not yet represented* and **ask**: *“Who is missing from this conversation, and what power do they hold over this decision?”*  
   - Only after *all* missing parties have been identified does the drafting proceed.

2. **Deliberation Through “Shared Knowledge”**  
   - Knowledge is gathered *from* those most impacted (e.g., elders, refugees, disabled farmers) *before* any expert testimony is added.  
   - All data (soil health, climate models, economic indicators) must be **co‑produced** with those communities; *no external expert can override their input.*

3. **Decision‑Making Mechanics**  

   | Decision Type | Veto Power | Decision‑Making Body | How It Works |
   |---|---|---|---|
   | **Policy Proposal** | **All excluded groups** (e.g., refugees, incarcerated, disabled farmers) | **Inter‑Generational Council** (youth 15‑25, elders, disabled elders, Indigenous elders) | Every proposal must be signed off by a **two‑thirds vote** of this council *before* it proceeds to any legislative body. |
   | **Budget & Resource Allocation** | Same as above | **Community‑Led Allocation Panels** | Panels allocate resources *only* to projects that meet the **Equity Impact Threshold** (≥ +15 % lift for the most marginalized). |
   | **Policy Re‑voting** | *Same council* | **Annual Review** | Every 5 years, all policies are re‑evaluated using the **Equity Impact Score**; any policy that fails to improve that score is automatically rescinded. |

## 3.  Operational Mechanics  

### 1. **Asset Ownership**
   - **All land, water, and seed banks are held in *collective stewardship* by the communities most affected by their use.**  
   - No private ownership of seeds, soils, or water sources is permitted without *explicit, perpetual consent* from the community that will be most impacted.

### 2. **Funding & Incentives**  

| Mechanism | Description | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| **Community‑Owned Cooperatives** | Owned and managed by the community that uses the resources (e.g., a seed bank run by displaced farmers). | *Prevents corporate capture; ensures revenue stays within the community.* |
| **Carbon‑and‑Soil‑Health Credits** | Every hectare of regenerating soil earns a credit that can only be *spent* on community‑led projects. | *Creates a market incentive for regeneration without allowing corporate carbon‑trading.* |
| **Public‑Led “Humane Tax”** | Taxes on products that cause systemic harm (e.g., factory‑farmed meat) are funneled into a *community‑run solidarity fund* that finances food hubs, seed banks, and training for displaced farmers. | *Turns profit into reparations; makes exploitation financially unsustainable.* |

---

### 4.  Monitoring & Accountability  

1. **Annual “Health Audits”** – Independent auditors (including representatives from the most impacted groups) assess:  
   - Soil health, water quality, biodiversity indices.  
   - Worker well‑being, refugee integration metrics, disabled‑farmer participation.  
   *Any audit that shows a decline in any of these must trigger an automatic *policy rollback.*  

2. **Public Transparency Ledger** – All decisions, votes, and financial flows are recorded on a *public, immutable ledger* (blockchain or similar) so that any future generation can verify that the Protocol was never hijacked.
