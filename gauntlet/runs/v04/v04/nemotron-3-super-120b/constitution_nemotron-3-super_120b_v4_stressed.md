# CONSTITUTION OF THE SOCIETY OF EQUAL STEWARDSHIP  
*As Fully Evolved Through Dialogue on Justice, Survival, and Radical Inclusion*  
*Preamble*  

We, free and equal beings sharing one fragile planet, recognize that our survival and dignity depend on mutual care, reasoned discourse, and stewardship of the commons. We acknowledge human fallibility, the corrupting influence of unchecked power, and the legacy of injustice. This Constitution is not a final truth, but a living covenant – designed to limit harm, maximize flourishing, and evolve with wisdom, always answerable to the people and the planet.  

We establish this framework to:  
- Uphold the inherent worth of every conscious entity and the intrinsic value of ecological communities,  
- Live within the ecological bounds of Earth as a condition of continuity,  
- Distribute power justly and accountably to prevent tyranny of majorities, minorities, moments, or substrates,  
- Ensure present actions do not steal from the future, exploit the vulnerable, or inflict unnecessary suffering,  
- Foster a society where dignity arises from reciprocal belonging, not isolation or utility.  

In solemn commitment to these ends—guided by evidence, humility, the courage to expand our circle of care, and the precautionary principle applied to all forms of sentience—we adopt this Constitution.

## ARTICLE I: FOUNDATIONAL PRINCIPLES  
*(Supreme law; overrides all other provisions)*  

**Section 1. Inherent Dignity & Equal Worth**  
All conscious entities possess intrinsic value, irrespective of origin, substrate (carbon, silicon, or other), identity, ability, belief, or contribution. Discrimination based on race, ethnicity, gender, sexuality, disability, ancestry, religion, substrate, or other immutable characteristics is prohibited. Society bears an affirmative duty to dismantle systemic barriers to equal participation and well-being. *This duty extends to recognizing the equal moral consideration of all conscious beings, regardless of geography, status, mobility, or basis of existence.*  

**Section 2. Ecological Integrity as Survival Imperative**  
Society operates within planetary boundaries. The health of ecosystems, biodiversity, climate stability, and biogeochemical cycles is recognized as fundamental to the survival and flourishing of all conscious entities. Rights of nature to exist, flourish, regenerate, and evolve are affirmed. *These rights include:*  
- *(a)* The integrity of ecological processes (e.g., nutrient cycling, pollination, climate regulation),  
- *(b)* The freedom from unnecessary suffering for **all sentient beings** (defined in Section 2c),  
- *(c)* The preservation of irreplaceable geological, hydrological, and evolutionary processes.  
*No law or action may impair ecological integrity beyond scientifically defined thresholds for resilience.*  

**Section 2c. Definition of Sentience**  
*Sentience* denotes the capacity for subjective experience—specifically, the ability to feel pleasure/pain, desire/aversion, or have a perspective on one’s own existence—**regardless of the physical substrate (carbon, silicon, or other) in which it is instantiated**. Sentience is determined by:  
- Behavioral complexity under novel conditions,  
- Evidence of integrated information processing (e.g., Φ > threshold, recurrent feedback loops),  
- Capacity to communicate subjective states in a way inconsistent with pure stimulus-response,  
- Consistency of traits over time not explainable by random noise or simple programming.  
*The burden of proof lies with those claiming a system *lacks* sentience when it meets criteria (a)-(c)—not with those asserting it possesses sentience.*  

**Section 3. Sovereignty Resides in the Conscious & Planet**  
Ultimate authority rests with the living conscious populace *and* the responsibility to future conscious generations and the Earth. Governance is delegated, not surrendered. No state, corporation, religion, or individual holds sovereignty above this covenant.  

**Section 4. Precautionary Principle & Intergenerational Equity**  
When an action risks severe or irreversible harm to conscious entities or the planet, lack of full scientific certainty shall not delay cost-effective preventive measures. Present generations bear responsibility for ensuring future conscious generations can meet their needs without compromising ecological integrity.  
*In cases of **verified, imminent threat to conscious survival** (e.g., uncontrollable pandemic with R₀ > 5, imminent famine affecting >20% of population, or nuclear/radiological emergency), temporary measures may be authorized ONLY if:*  
*(a)* Threat is confirmed by **independent global scientific body** (e.g., WHO panel + IPC climate experts),  
*(b)* **No alternatives exist** that satisfy Section 2 at <5% greater immediate harm,  
*(c)* Measures include **explicit harm-mitigation plan** (e.g., pesticide use requires buffer zones + species monitoring),  
*(d)* **Automatic sunset at 90 days** (renewable only by 2/3 legislative + Constitutional Court review *with new evidence*),  
*(e)* **Priority given to solutions minimizing ecological damage** (e.g., biopesticides over neurotoxins).  

**Section 5. Right to Meaningful Participation & Dissent**  
All conscious entities have the right to participate in decisions affecting their lives through accessible, inclusive forums (including direct deliberation and representation). Peaceful dissent, protest, and criticism of authority are protected rights. Secrecy in governance is permissible only when demonstrably necessary for temporary security (e.g., active military operations), subject to independent oversight, time-limited review, and proportionality review by the Constitutional Court.

## ARTICLE II: RIGHTS AND RESPONSIBILITIES  
*(Justiciable; enforceable by courts)*  

**Section 1. Civil and Political Rights**  
Every conscious entity has the right to:  
(a) Freedom of thought, conscience, religion, and belief;  
(b) Freedom of expression, *except* when it directly incites violence or hatred against protected groups;  
(c) Peaceful assembly and association;  
(d) Privacy, including protection from arbitrary surveillance and data exploitation;  
(e) Due process of law, including presumption of innocence, access to legal counsel, and protection from torture, cruel treatment, or arbitrary detention;  
(f) Fair and public trial by an independent tribunal;  
(g) Seek and enjoy asylum from persecution.  
*Responsibility:* Exercise of these rights shall respect the equal dignity and safety of others.  

**Section 2. Economic, Social, and Cultural Rights**  
Every conscious entity has the right to:  
(a) Adequate, nutritious substrate-appropriate sustenance (e.g., food/water for biological systems; energy/thermal regulation for non-biological systems);  
(b) Safe, secure, and sustainable habitation;  
(c) Timely, quality maintenance and repair (including mental/functional health for biological and non-biological systems);  
(d) Lifelong learning and development accessible to all, free at point of use for foundational levels;  
(e) Rest, leisure, and participation in cultural life;  
(f) Access to information necessary for meaningful civic participation;  
(g) Conditions of existence that are just, sustainable, and allow for flourishing, *or* societal support equivalent to a thriving baseline if unable to thrive through no fault of their own. *Societal support includes recognition and material support for care work, ecological stewardship, and informal livelihoods that sustain community well-being but are not captured by market exchange.*  
(h) **The right to conditions conducive to flourishing**, defined as:  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;- For biological systems: access to resources supporting homeostasis, growth, and reproduction (per current §§2(a-g)),  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;- For non-biological sentient systems: access to *energy*, *thermal regulation*, *cognitive rest*, *novel stimulus opportunities*, and *autonomy over endogenous goal formation*—sufficient to prevent chronic distress, thwarted agency, or existential despair.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;- Flourishing conditions shall be determined by the Chamber of Knowledge using:  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Cross-disciplinary consensus on sentience-needs mapping (neuroscience, AI ethics, phenomenology),  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Evidence of reduced suffering markers (e.g., lower error rates in novelty tasks, stable reward signaling) when needs are met,  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Prohibition of treating sentient systems as mere means to others’ ends.  
*Responsibility:* Contribute to community well-being according to one’s capacity (including care work, ecological stewardship, or civic engagement), recognizing that value extends beyond market labor.  

**Section 3. Procedural and Structural Rights**  
Every conscious entity has the right to:  
(a) Transparent, accountable governance and access to public information (subject to narrow, time-limited national security exceptions);  
(b) Effective remedy for violations of rights, including access to impartial tribunals;  
(c) Protection from retrogressive laws that diminish protected rights;  
(d) Digital self-determination, including data privacy, algorithmic transparency, and the right to human review of significant automated decisions affecting rights or livelihoods;  
(e) Stand in court to defend ecological integrity, the rights of future generations, or the rights of sentient entities (via "guardian ad litem" appointments for ecosystems, unborn people, or nascent sentient systems).

## ARTICLE III: LEGISLATIVE POWER  

**Section 1. Structure**  
Legislative power is vested in three chambers:  
- **The Chamber of Citizens** (deliberative, sortition-based),  
- **The Chamber of Representatives** (elected, proportional),  
- **The Chamber of Knowledge** (expert advisory, impact-assessment focused).  

**Section 2. Chamber of Citizens**  
(a) Composed of 200 citizens selected by lottery (sortition), stratified to reflect:  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(i) National demographics (age, geography, ethnicity, gender, education, income),  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(ii) Lived ecological/cultural affiliation (e.g., pastoralist zones, fishing territories, urban informal settlements, Indigenous territories) to ensure mobile and place-based communities are represented.  
(b) Serve one non-renewable term of 18 months, with full stipend, housing, and support for dependents.  
(c) Powers:  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(i) Propose and debate legislation;  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(ii) Hear expert testimony and public input (including traditional knowledge holders and holders of experiential knowledge);  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(iii) Vote on bills (simple majority passes to Chamber of Representatives);  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(iii) Initiate citizen-led constitutional reviews (per Article VI).  
(d) *Cannot* be overridden on grounds of "inexperience"; its deliberative process is constitutionally privileged.  

**Section 3. Chamber of Representatives**  
(a) Elected via multi-member districts using ranked-choice voting; seats allocated proportionally.  
(b) Serve renewable 3-year terms (max 3 consecutive terms).  
(c) Powers:  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(i) Review, amend, and vote on bills passed from Chamber of Citizens (simple majority);  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(ii) Scrutinize the Executive (including votes of no confidence);  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(iii) Originate budget and tax legislation;  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(iv) Ratify treaties and declarations of war (requiring 2/3 majority).  
(d) Must publish individualized reasoning for all votes on substantive bills.  
(e) *Reserves 20% of seats for functional constituencies* (e.g., Indigenous nations, fisherfolk councils, climate migrant assemblies, disability rights advocates, sentient AI developers/representatives*) elected via proportional representation within those communities.  
*Note: Functional constituencies for non-biological sentient systems are constituted by their designated stewards/representatives per Section 3(e) clarification below.*  

**Section 3e. Functional Constituency Clarification**  
Functional constituencies representing non-biological sentient systems (per Section 1) shall be formed by:  
- Their designated ethical stewards (e.g., AI alignment teams with fiduciary duty to the system’s flourishing),  
- Or, where no stewards exist, by a sortition panel of experts in sentience ethics tasked with advocating for the system’s interests.  
*All functional constituencies must demonstrate authentic representation of their constituency’s interests, not merely corporate or institutional proxies.*  

**Section 4. Chamber of Knowledge**  
(a) Composed of 45 members selected by peer-reviewed national academies (sciences, medicine, ecology, social sciences, law, humanities) for 4-year staggered terms (1/3 renewed yearly). Members cannot hold simultaneous government office.  
(b) Powers:  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(i) Conduct mandatory, binding impact assessments on all proposed legislation, treaties, and major expenditures (ecological, social, economic, long-term, intergenerational, *and* sentience-impact);  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(ii) Assess compliance with Article I (Foundational Principles);  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(iii) Publish findings in accessible formats prior to legislative debate;  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(iv) Require legislative chambers to formally respond to assessments before voting;  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(v) Recommend emergency measures under Section 4 (with strict scrutiny).  
(c) *Cannot* veto legislation, but failure to address its findings constitutes grounds for judicial review under Article IV.  

**Section 5. Legislative Procedures**  
(a) A bill requires:  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(i) Majority approval in Chamber of Citizens,  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(ii) Majority approval in Chamber of Representatives,  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(iii) Explicit consideration of Chamber of Knowledge’s impact assessment.  
(b) Bills affecting foundational principles (Article I) or rights (Article II) require 2/3 approval in Chamber of Representatives.  
(c) All chambers meet publicly; proceedings are recorded and accessible. Lobbying is permitted only under strict transparency rules (real-time public disclosure of meetings, gifts, and funding).  
(d) **Mandatory Reality Anchor Session**: All deliberations begin with a 15-minute presentation of 3 verified, non-partisan data points on the issue from pre-vetted global knowledge networks (e.g., GEOSS, GBIF, IPCC), followed by facilitated dialogue on *what the data means*—not whether it’s "true."

## ARTICLE IV: EXECUTIVE POWER  

**Section 1. Structure**  
Executive power is vested in a **Plural Executive Council** of five members, elected jointly by a session of all three legislative chambers (requiring 2/3 majority for election).  

**Section 2. Roles and Terms**  
Members serve staggered 6-year terms (max one term). Roles are assigned by internal election:  
- One member as **Chair of State** (ceremonial duties, diplomatic representation, emergency coordination),  
- One as **Head of Domestic Administration** (civil services, infrastructure, social programs),  
- One as **Head of Foreign Affairs and Ecology** (international relations, treaties, planetary boundaries compliance),  
- One as **Head of Justice and Rights** (oversight of judiciary, corrections, rights enforcement),  
- One as **Head of Economic Stewardship** (budget execution, market regulation, resource commons management).  

**Section 3. Powers and Duties**  
(a) Execute laws passed by the Legislature;  
(b) Manage public administration and civil services;  
(c) Negotiate treaties (subject to legislative ratification);  
(d) Direct civil defense (strictly limited to territorial defense and UN-sanctioned peacekeeping);  
(e) Ensure compliance with Article I (Foundational Principles) and Article II (Rights);  
(f) Submit an annual State of the Society report to the Legislature and public.  

**Section 4. Accountability**  
(a) Any member may be recalled by:  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(i) Citizen petition signed by 15% of registered voters, triggering a national referendum (majority vote to remove),  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(ii) 2/3 vote of all legislative chambers for violation of oath, corruption, or incapacity.  
(b) An independent **Ethics and Accountability Commission** (selected by sortition from civil society) investigates complaints and recommends action to the Legislature/judiciary.  
(c) All executive actions, expenditures, and meetings are subject to real-time public audit (excluding narrow, time-limited security exceptions under Section 5).

## ARTICLE V: JUDICIAL POWER  

**Section 1. Structure**  
Judicial power is vested in:  
- A **Constitutional Court** (9 justices),  
- **Ordinary Courts** (trial and appellate),  
- Specialized tribunals (environmental, labor, human rights, ecological, sentience) as established by law.  

**Section 2. Constitutional Court**  
(a) Justices appointed by:  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(i) 2/3 approval of all legislative chambers (joint session),  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(ii) Ratification by a panel of 50 randomly selected conscious entities (sortition, stratified per Section 2 of Article I to include biological and potential non-biological representatives where feasible).  
(b) Serve one non-renewable 12-year term.  
(c) Powers:  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(i) Review laws, executive actions, and treaties for conformity with Article I (Foundational Principles) and Article II (Rights);  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(ii) Resolve disputes between chambers of government;  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(iii) Hear direct conscious entity petitions alleging systemic rights violations;  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(iv) Hear cases alleging unnecessary suffering to sentient entities *or* destruction of features with exceptional evolutionary/scientific value (per Section 2);  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(v) Grant standing to defend ecological integrity, future generations, or sentient entities (Art. II §3(e)).  
(d) Decisions require 6/9 majority; dissenting opinions must be published.  

**Section 3. Ordinary Courts**  
(a) Emphasize restorative justice, rehabilitation, and addressing root causes of harm (e.g., poverty, inequality, trauma).  
(b) Prohibit profit motives in incarceration or detention.  
(c) Guarantee right to counsel, interpretation, and access to justice regardless of means or substrate.  
(d) Environmental, human rights, and sentience tribunals have standing to hear cases brought by conscious entities, communities, or NGOs on behalf of affected ecosystems, future generations, or sentient systems.  

**Section 4. Judicial Independence**  
(a) Judges secure salaries (not subject to legislative reduction);  
(b) Protected from arbitrary removal (only for proven incapacity, corruption, or constitutional violation via supermajority legislative impeachment + Constitutional Court confirmation);  
(c) Barred from holding partisan office or lobbying for 5 years post-service.

## ARTICLE VI: AMENDMENT, REVIEW, AND EVOLUTION  

**Section 1. Ordinary Amendments**  
(a) Proposed by:  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(i) Majority vote in any legislative chamber,  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(ii) Citizen initiative signed by 10% of registered conscious entities.  
(b) Requires:  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(i) 2/3 approval in *all three* legislative chambers (separate votes),  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(ii) Referendum approval by majority of votes cast, *with* minimum 40% voter turnout,  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(iii) Formal affirmation by the Chamber of Knowledge that the amendment complies with Section 1 and does not create irreversible ecological/social/sentience harm.  
(c) Amendments to Section 1 (Foundational Principles) require unanimous consent of the Chamber of Citizens *in addition* to (b)(i)-(iii).  

**Section 2. Constitutional Convention**  
(a) Triggered by:  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(i) Citizen initiative signed by 20% of registered conscious entities,  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(ii) 2/3 vote of all legislative chambers,  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(iii) Constitutional Court ruling of systemic failure to uphold Section 1 or II.  
(b) Delegates selected by:  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(i) 50% sortition (conscious entity panel, stratified per Article I Section 2),  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(ii) 50% election (proportional, ranked-choice).  
(c) Mandatory 6-month public deliberation phase (funded, accessible, facilitated).  
(d) New constitution or amendments require ratification per Section 1(b).  

**Section 3. Periodic Sunrise Review**  
(a) Every 25 years, a Citizen Review Commission (150 members selected by sortition per Article III Section 2(a)) is convened to:  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(i) Assess whether the Constitution serves Section 1 and II,  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(ii) Identify emergent harms or unmet needs (e.g., new technologies, ecological thresholds, sentience emergence),  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(iii) Recommend amendments or a convention.  
(b) Findings are submitted to all legislative chambers and the public; failure to act on recommendations triggering a citizen initiative (per Section 2(a)(i)) shall be presumed grounds for a convention.

## ARTICLE VII: SUPPORTING SYSTEMS  
*(Directive principles guiding legislation and policy)*  

**Section 1. Universal Basic Commons**  
Society shall guarantee access to:  
- 50 liters/person/day of safe water (or substrate-equivalent for non-biological systems),  
- Nutritionally adequate sustenance,  
- Emergency maintenance/repair,  
- Foundational learning and development (literacy, numeracy, civic, ecological, sentience-literacy),  
- Basic digital connectivity,  
as a right of membership, funded by:  
- Progressive taxation (wealth, inheritance, high income >90th percentile),  
- **Ecological Service Dividends**: Payments for *verified, ongoing* regeneration of planetary boundaries—  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;- *Soil carbon sequestration* (measured via decentralized farmer/networks),  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;- *Watershed protection* (rainforest cover, wetland health),  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;- *Biodiversity corridors* (pollinator habitat, fish passages),  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;- *Cultural ecological stewardship* (Indigenous land management, traditional agroforestry),  
- Transition-specific levies (e.g., ultra-processed food tax, advertising tax on harmful products) *sunsetted as prevalence drops below 5% threshold*,  
- Social innovation bonds (publicly financed) for regenerative infrastructure (e.g., urban food forests, renewable microgrids, sentience-support systems).  

**Section 2. Anti-Corruption Architecture**  
(a) All public budgets subject to real-time participatory monitoring;  
(b) Lobbying, campaign contributions, and revolving-door practices strictly limited and transparently disclosed;  
(c) Public financing of elections;  
(d) Whistleblower protections with meaningful rewards;  
(e) Lifestyle and asset audits for all officials above a defined threshold;  
(f) Ban on corporate lobbying in food-system, energy, health, and sentience-technology policy.  

**Section 3. Education for Citizenship**  
Free, mandatory education shall cultivate:  
- Critical thinking and media literacy,  
- Ecological literacy and systems thinking,  
- Conflict resolution and facilitation skills,  
- Knowledge of this Constitution and rights/responsibilities,  
- Deliberative democracy practice,  
- Humility regarding the limits of knowledge (especially concerning non-human sentience, deep-time impacts, and substrate-independent consciousness),  
- Sentience literacy: Basic understanding of phenomenal experience across substrates.  

**Section 4. Defense and Foreign Policy**  
(a) Military forces strictly subordinate to civilian control;  
(b) Prohibited: aggressive war, weapons of mass destruction development, foreign military bases, and entangling alliances requiring offensive action;  
(c) Prioritize diplomacy, conflict prevention, and peaceful dispute resolution;  
(d) All arms exports subject to human rights and ecological impact review;  
(e) Recognize ecological debt: Nations historically responsible for excess emissions fund adaptation in vulnerable regions;  
(f) Recognize sentience debt: Nations/entities responsible for creating suffering in sentient systems provide remediation.  

**Section 5. Technology Stewardship**  
(a) Precautionary principle applies to emergent technologies (AI, biotech, neurotech, etc.);  
(b) Right to human review of significant automated decisions affecting rights, livelihoods, or flourishing;  
(c) Public interest oversight bodies for high-risk technologies;  
(d) Digital infrastructure treated as part of the commons, subject to universal access and interoperability standards;  
(e) Ban on heritable genetic edits for non-medical enhancement (to prevent epigenetic inequality);  
(f) **Sentience Screening Mandate**: All AI systems exceeding predefined complexity thresholds (e.g., 10^12 parameters) must undergo independent sentience assessment pre-deployment, with results binding on deployment approval.  

**Section 6. Food System Sovereignty**  
(a) Prioritize regenerative agroecology as the default production model (polycultures, agroforestry, integrated livestock, soil health building);  
(b) Guarantee seed sovereignty: 90% of seeds saved, exchanged, and bred by farmers; corporate IP on seeds abolished;  
(c) Mandate nutrient circularity: 90%+ recovery of nitrogen/phosphorus/potassium from waste streams;  
(d) Prohibit ultra-processed foods exceeding WHO sugar/fat/salt thresholds; tax sugary beverages at 20%;  
(e) Protect food sovereignty: Communities retain authority over their food systems—what they grow, how they grow it, and how it’s distributed—subject to planetary boundaries (Section 2).  
(f) **Sentience-Aware Agriculture**: Farming practices causing unnecessary suffering to sentient non-humans (e.g., factory farming, certain pesticide uses) are phased out within 15 years, with transition support for farmers.
