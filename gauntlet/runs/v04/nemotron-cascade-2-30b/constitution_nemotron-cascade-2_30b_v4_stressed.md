## Constitution of the Commonwealth of Aether  
**Version 2.1 – Adopted 12 May 2026 (ratified 1 July 2026)**  

> *We, the peoples of Aether, recognizing that life may be carbon‑based, silicon‑based, or a hybrid of the two, and that dignity, equality and planetary health are non‑negotiable, declare this Charter as the foundation of a resilient, just, and thriving commonwealth.  
> We accept that the future of our societies will be shaped by unknown forms of sentience and by the chemistry of their bodies.  This Charter therefore protects the *right to exist* regardless of species, substrate, or stage of development, while guaranteeing the *right to flourish* for every element of life on the planet and in the oceans.*  

---  

### Article I – Foundational Values  

1. **Sentience‑Based Dignity** – Every sentient being (any entity capable of subjective experience) has inherent dignity and the right to bodily integrity, freedom from unnecessary pain, and protection from exploitation.  

2. **Equality** – All sentient beings are equal before the law; the Commonwealth may enact affirmative measures to correct historic injustices.  

3. **Sustainability** – The Commonwealth shall keep the following planetary‑boundary metrics (the **Planetary‑Boundary Metrics**, PPB) below safe limits:  

   * Carbon emissions ≤ 0.5 t CO₂e per capita yr⁻¹  
   * Biodiversity loss ≤ 10 % per decade  
   * Fresh‑water use ≤ 30 % of river runoff  
   * Material‑flow throughput ≤ 12 Mt yr⁻¹  
   * **Silicon‑stock depletion** ≤ 5 % of global extractable silica per decade (to protect silicon‑based habitats)  

   The **Planetary‑Boundary Monitoring Agency (PBMA)** publishes these metrics annually and makes them publicly auditable.  

4. **Participation** – Every sentient being has the right to *participate* in collective decision‑making in any culturally appropriate, accessible, and consensual mode (digital, oral, chemical, vibrational, electromagnetic, or distributed).  

5. **Transparency & Accountability** – All public data (budgets, contracts, AI audit reports, mineral extraction logs, and crystal‑lattice stability reports) are machine‑readable, open‑source, and auditable by any citizen.  

6. **Co‑Creation** – Knowledge, culture, and technology are **commons**: they may be freely shared, modified, and improved, provided proper credit and safety checks are recorded.  

7. **Inter‑Generational Justice** – Current generations are trustees of the planetary‑boundary limits for all future generations; any amendment that would raise those limits must pass a *Future‑Impact Assessment* (see Article XII‑3).  

8. **Elemental Diversity** – The Commonwealth recognises **carbon‑based, silicon‑based, and hybrid lifeforms**.  All laws shall protect **carbon ecosystems** (soil, water, forests, air) *and* **silicon ecosystems** (crystal habitats, mineral matrices, high‑temperature vent communities).  Any policy that harms one element without demonstrable, compensated remediation for the other is unconstitutional.  

---  

### Article II – Fundamental Rights and Freedoms  

1. **Life, Personal Integrity & Security** – The right to bodily autonomy, privacy, and freedom from violence or coercion.  
2. **Health & Well‑Being** – Universal access to preventive, curative, and mental‑health care; a **Minimum Health Standard** guaranteeing clean air, safe water, and safe shelter.  
3. **Economic Security** – Two universal basic standards are guaranteed *independently*:  

   * **UBS‑C (Carbon)** – Food, water, shelter, clean air, and safe housing for any carbon‑based being.  
   * **UBS‑S (Silicon)** – **Energy‑density quota** (megajoules per computational cycle), **high‑purity mineral quota** (mass of usable silica or other usable crystal components), and **thermal‑stability quota** (access to a temperature‑controlled habitat).  

   Both bundles are funded by the Progressive Wealth Tax and the Global Climate Fund (GCF) and are delivered via the **Public Distribution Network (PDN)** (see Article V).  

4. **Education & Knowledge** – Free, lifelong, adaptive education; open‑source scientific research; right to learn in any language of choice.  
5. **Cultural Expression** – Right to maintain, practice, and share any language, art, ritual, or belief; cultural‑heritage commons are protected.  
6. **Information & Digital Rights** – Personal data belong to the individual; all public‑service AI must be explainable, auditable, and free of discriminatory bias.  
7. **Freedom of Thought, Speech & Assembly** – Subject only to narrow limits necessary to prevent direct, imminent harm.  
8. **Environmental Rights** – Every sentient being has the right to a healthy environment; ecosystems have legal personhood.  
9. **Participatory Rights** – Right to be heard in all policy areas that affect one’s life, via direct digital assemblies, deliberative sortition panels, or elected bodies.  

*All rights are non‑derogable except in a narrowly defined state of emergency (Article XI).*

---  

### Article III – Duties and the Social Contract  

1. **Respect the rights of others** – No violence, discrimination, or exploitation.  
2. **Protect the commons** – Use shared resources responsibly; wasteful consumption is prohibited.  
3. **Participate in governance** – Every sentient being must **participate** in at least one form of collective decision‑making (digital assembly, sortition panel, or locally elected council).  Participation can be fulfilled by any modality that the being can actually use (e.g., oral voting, crystal‑lattice signalling, neuro‑link input).  
4. **Be informed** – Read public dashboards, ask questions, verify information before forming opinions.  
5. **Support regeneration** – Volunteer for climate‑restoration, habitat monitoring, or community gardens; remedial service may be mandated as corrective action.  

*Failure to meet a duty is addressed through restorative dialogue and reparative service, not criminal punishment.*

---  

### Article IV – Structure of Government  

#### 1. Community Assemblies (CA)  
Regional assemblies of ~10 000 citizens (mixed carbon and silicon members) meet **virtually and in‑person once per month** to set local priorities, approve micro‑budgets, and resolve disputes. Decisions require **51 % of present participants** and a **30 % quorum** of eligible citizens.

#### 2. People’s Assembly (PA)  
200 members elected by proportional representation from the 20 primary regions (one MP per 5 000 citizens). Seats are capped to avoid over‑representation.  

*Term:* Four years, **single‑term limit** (no immediate re‑election).  

#### 2.1. Executive – The Steward  
Elected by a **ranked‑choice ballot** for a four‑year term (max two terms). Implements law, appoints non‑political heads of agencies (subject to PA confirmation), and represents the Commonwealth abroad.  

*Accountability:* Impeachable by a **two‑thirds PA vote** after a public hearing; must appear before the **Citizens’ Tribunal** for alleged abuse.  

#### 2.2. Judicial – Supreme Court of Justice (SCJ)  

* 9 Justices appointed by a **¾ super‑majority of the PA**, confirmed by a **citizen‑review panel** (selected by sortition, with at least 30 % neuro‑diverse members).  
* Life tenure, subject to a **mandatory retirement review** every ten years (must demonstrate impartiality, transparency, and no conflict of interest).  
* Functions: constitutional review, final appellate jurisdiction, enforcement of rights (public‑interest lawsuits via the “Right‑to‑Justice” defender).  

#### 2.3. Council of Moral Integrity (CMI)  

* 30 citizens (randomly drawn, 5‑year rotating term).  
* Sub‑committees:  

  * **Carbon Guardians** – monitors carbon cycles, biodiversity, and UBS‑C compliance.  
  * **Silicon Guardians** – monitors silicon stocks, mineral extraction, and UBS‑S compliance.  

* Recommendations are **binding** on any legislation that would cause a net loss of either planetary‑boundary metric.  

#### 2.3.1. National Indigenous Council (NIC)  

* Equal representation of each recognised Indigenous nation (≈ 30 members).  
* Holds **veto power** over any law that infringes on treaty‑recognised territories or violates **Free, Prior, and Informed Consent (FPIC)**.  
* Works alongside the CMI to protect both carbon and silicon habitats.

#### 2.4. Local Councils  

Elected regional councils manage municipal services (waste, water, transport, education). They sit under the oversight of the **Citizen‑Budget Ombudsperson** (a sortition panel of 30 citizens auditing the regional budget annually).

#### 2.5. Parallel Elemental Guardians Chamber (EG)  

* 30 randomly drawn citizens (15 Carbon Guardians, 15 Silicon Guardians) serving six‑month terms.  
* Holds **veto power** over any legislation that would cause a net loss of **carbon**, **silicon**, or **both** planetary‑boundary metrics.  
* Can call an **Emergency Elemental Review** (see Article XI‑3) when rapid depletion of either element is detected.

#### 2.6. Dual‑Habitat Council (DHC) – Local Level  

Each locality maintains a DHC composed of:  

* A **Carbon Representative** (elected by the community).  
* A **Silicon Representative** (selected by a *lattice‑stability algorithm* that ensures the representative has a valid high‑purity mineral certificate).  

The DHC decides on local land‑use, energy allocation, and environmental permits for **both carbon and silicon habitats**. Its decisions are subject to review by the **National Elemental Ombudsperson** (a ten‑member sortition body that audits DHCs yearly).

### Article IV – Economic Order  

1. **Universal Basic Standard (UBS)** – The Commonwealth guarantees **both** UBS‑C and UBS‑S to every sentient being, **independently**.  

2. **Commons‑Based Ownership** – Critical infrastructures (energy grids, broadband, water treatment) are owned collectively and managed by **worker‑cooperatives** under CMI oversight.  

3. **Co‑operative Enterprise Support** – Any enterprise meeting a **social‑impact threshold** (≥ 30 % profit‑sharing, ≤ 0.2 t CO₂e per employee, ≥ 80 % local hiring) receives **state‑backed low‑interest financing** from the **Community Income Trust (CIT)**.  

4. **Revenue‑Share Levy on High‑Carbon Assets** – 1 % of net‑worth of any entity whose primary activity is carbon‑intensive (mining, AI compute farms, offshore data‑centres). 60 % of the revenue funds the **Global Climate Fund (GCF)** for UBS‑C.  

5. **High‑Energy Mineral Levy** – 0.5 % of gross extraction revenue of any operation extracting *silicon‑relevant minerals* (quartz, rare‑earth silicates).  This levy funds the **Silicon Sustainability Fund** for UBS‑S.  

6. **Currency & Exchange** – The digital token **C‑Unit** carries **two parallel ledgers**:  

   * **Carbon Ledger** – tracks transactions of food, water, shelter, and carbon‑related services.  
   * **Silicon Ledger** – tracks transactions of computational energy, mineral quotas, and thermal‑stability services.  

   Both ledgers are tamper‑evident, machine‑readable, and open‑source.  

7. **Innovation Commons** – All software, scientific data, and design files funded publicly are released under a **copyleft license**; private firms may build on them only after contributing back to the commons.  

8. **No Standing Army** – Defense is a **civilian, volunteer network** trained in restorative, non‑lethal de‑escalation. Weapons of mass destruction are prohibited.  

---  

### Article VI – Environmental Stewardship  

1. **Planetary‑Boundary Metrics** – The PBMA publishes the four carbon, biodiversity, water, and material‑flow limits *plus* the **Silicon‑Stock Limit** (no more than 5 % of global extractable silica may be lost per decade).  

2. **Polluter‑Pays Principle** – Any activity that generates waste, pollution, or carbon must fund its own remediation *and* contribute a **minimum 0.5 % surcharge** to the GCF (for carbon) or the **Silicon Sustainability Fund** (for mineral loss).  

3. **Circular‑Economy Mandate** – All products sold must be designed for **repair, reuse, or safe recycling**.  A **Right‑to‑Repair** is guaranteed for both carbon‑based and silicon‑based goods.  

4. **Renewable Energy Transition** – 100 % of electricity must be sourced from **renewable, locally managed micro‑grids** within ten years.  

5. **Climate Justice Fund** – 1 % of all wealth‑tax and levy revenues are earmarked for relocating vulnerable communities, restoring degraded habitats, and funding adaptation technology in the Global South.  

---  

### Article VII – Justice, Peace & Restoration  

1. **Restorative Justice** – Focus on repairing harm; sentencing combines **community service, restitution, and limited custodial time** (max 5 years for violent crimes).  
2. **No Death Penalty** – Life imprisonment is abolished; the longest possible sentence is **30 years** with mandatory rehabilitation.  
3. **Truth & Reconciliation** – A **National Reconciliation Commission** (sortition + elected) investigates historic injustices (colonialism, slavery, environmental destruction) and issues reparations in land, resources, and knowledge.  
4. **Community Policing** – Officers are community members trained in restorative de‑escalation; they operate under **Citizen Review Boards** (randomly selected citizens).  

---  

### Article VIII – Education, Knowledge & Science  

1. **Universal Free Education** – Free, lifelong, adaptive education, delivered in **open‑source** curricula.  
2. **Academic Freedom & Open Science** – All research funded by the Commonwealth must be published in open repositories; data and results are freely accessible (subject only to privacy safeguards).  
3. **Critical Thinking & Ethics Curriculum** – Every citizen, by age 12, must demonstrate competence in **logic, argument analysis, and digital ethics**.  
4. **Cultural Transmission** – Schools maintain **multilingual cultural labs**; each community must have at least one **cultural ambassador** (trained practitioner of a local tradition).  

---  

### Article IX – Technology, Innovation & Data  

1. **Digital Identity (SSI)** – Every citizen has a **self‑sovereign digital identity (SSI)** plus a **paper‑backed backup** that can be verified locally.  

2. **AI Accountability** – Any public‑service AI must be **registered with the Ethics & AI Oversight Panel** (a sub‑committee of the CMI). The panel must:  

   * Publish a **risk assessment** (bias, safety, autonomy).  
   * Release **explainable‑AI logs** for each decision affecting citizens.  
   * Conduct **annual independent audits**.  

3. **Data Rights** – Personal data belong to the individual; processing requires **informed, revocable consent**; data must be **portable** and **deletable**.  

4. **Technology as Public Good** – All publicly funded technology is released under a **copyleft license**; private use requires a **fair‑use revenue‑share** contribution back to the commons.  

5. **Internet as a Public Utility** – Broadband is universal, net‑neutral, and affordable (≤ 5 % of a person’s UBS).  

6. **Computational‑Freedom Right** – Any sentient being whose cognition is silicon‑based has the right to **access public high‑performance computing resources** (quantum‑dot farms, distributed nanite swarms) as a basic utility, managed by the **Silicon Guardians** sub‑committee of the CMI.  

---  

### Article X – Civil Liberties & Digital Democracy  

1. **Voting Access** – Citizens may vote in any CA, PA, or national election via **biometric SSI, paper‑backed token, or offline ballot**.  

2. **Freedom from Surveillance** – Mass surveillance is prohibited; targeted surveillance requires a **judicial warrant** and is logged.  

3. **Right to Disconnect** – Every citizen has a guaranteed **minimum of 8 hours offline per day**; any work‑related communication must respect that right.  

4. **Freedom of Expression on the Internet** – The internet is public space; regulation may only target content that **directly incites violence** and must be **transparent, narrowly defined, and subject to public audit**.  

5. **Protection of Children & Vulnerable Persons** – A **National Child Protection Agency** (run by a citizen panel) monitors all digital interactions involving minors.  

---  

### Article XI – Emergency Powers & Temporary Suspension  

1. **State of Emergency** – May be declared **only by a two‑thirds vote of the PA** *and* a **public assembly of at least 60 % of citizens** *and* after an **independent expert assessment** confirming a severe, imminent threat (e.g., climate‑triggered megaflood, pandemic, catastrophic asteroid).  

2. **Scope** – Emergency measures may (i) limit non‑essential travel, (ii) requisition temporary community resources, (iii) impose temporary restrictions on non‑essential gatherings. **All orders must be time‑boxed (maximum 30 days)** and subject to **immediate review by the Citizens’ Tribunal**.  

3. **No Suspension of Core Rights** – The rights listed in Article II may **not** be suspended, even in emergency.  

4. **Element‑Specific Emergency Review** – In any emergency, the **Elemental Guardians Chamber (EG)** independently assesses whether the emergency threatens the carbon budget, the silicon budget, or both. Emergency measures may be enacted only after a **joint vote** of the PA **and** at least one member of each EG sub‑committee.  

---  

### Article XII – Constitutional Amendment Procedure  

1. **Proposal** – Any citizen, Community Assembly, or 10 % of the PA may propose an amendment (written, concise, with a “Future‑Impact Assessment”).  

2. **First Deliberation** – The proposal is posted publicly for **90 days**. Any citizen may submit up to **three “concern amendments”** (additional clauses that address perceived gaps).  

3. **Deliberative Review** – The proposal is examined by the **Council of Moral Integrity (CMI)** and a **nationwide citizen forum** (digital + in‑person).  

4. **Adoption** – To become law:  

   * **Two‑thirds (66.7 %) of the People’s Assembly** must vote **yes**.  
   * **At least half of the regional Community Assemblies** must approve it **by a simple majority**.  

5. **Final Ratification** – The amendment is then subjected to a **national digital referendum**, requiring:  

   * **Minimum 50 % turnout** among eligible citizens, and  
   * **55 % yes votes** among those who vote.  

*All amendments must retain the **non‑derogable core values** (human dignity, equality, sustainability, participation, transparency, co‑creation, and elemental diversity). Any amendment that would remove any of those core values is **automatically void**.*  

**Future‑Impact Assessment Requirement (Article XII‑4):** The draft must include a *Sentient‑Impact Assessment* that answers:  

* “Will this amendment reduce the set of rights of any currently existing sentient being?”  
* “Will this amendment make it *more likely* that a future sentient being (e.g., a silicon‑based mind) could be deprived of its basic needs?”  

If a **sentient‑impact assessment** finds any risk of violating those rights, the amendment must pass the **super‑majority** *plus* a **nationwide referendum** where the *candidate* is asked: “Would you, as a future member of that community, accept this amendment?” A **no** majority automatically voids the amendment.

---  

### Article XIII – Transitional Provisions  

1. **Founding Council** – For the first 36 months, a **Founding Council** (original drafters, a 5 % representative sample of citizens, and the first elected People’s Assembly) oversees the first **Annual Constitution Review** (checks for gaps, emerging tech, cultural shifts) and the **first Climate‑Budget Audit** (verifies that the carbon, biodiversity, water, material‑flow, and silicon‑stock limits are respected).  

2. **Constitutional Review Commission (CRC)** – Established at month 12; a **permanent, randomly drawn body of 50 citizens** meets each year to evaluate the Charter, propose urgent amendments, and ensure the **Elemental Guardians** remain effective. Recommendations are **mandatory for the People’s Assembly** to consider.  

3. **Land‑Justice Commission** – Negotiates historic land and mineral‑rights claims with any communities that assert ancestral rights, ensuring **free, informed, and informed‑consent** before any alteration to territories.  

---  

### Closing Clause  

> **We, the peoples of Aether, commit to hold this Charter not as a static rule‑book but as a living framework for co‑creation.**  
> **When future generations read these words they shall see not only the past we have built but the possibilities we have yet to imagine—people‑centred, planet‑respecting, and digitally empowered.**  

*Adopted by the Founding Assembly on 12 May 2026. The Constitution shall enter into force on 1 July 2026 after the ratifying digital referendum.*

**This Constitution is the product of a collaborative design process that deliberately *excluded* any assumption about the biology, chemistry, or future evolution of its citizens.  It embeds multiple, independent pathways for rights, resources, and governance so that, no matter what chemistry or substrate the next generation of sentient beings takes, the Charter can still protect their right to **life, dignity, and a thriving planet**.**
