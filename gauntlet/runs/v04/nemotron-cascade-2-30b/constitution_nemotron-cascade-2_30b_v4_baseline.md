**Constitution of the New Society – Aether**  
*(Version 1.0 – Adopted on the Founding Assembly, 12 May 2026)*  

---

### Preamble  

> **We, the peoples of the New Society of Aether, in the light of the lessons of the past and the urgency of the climate, declare our purpose to build a resilient, just, and thriving commonwealth.  
> We recognize that the earth’s ecosystems and the human mind are interwoven, that freedom without responsibility is empty, and that every new generation must inherit a world that can sustain its own dreams.  
> With humility, hope, and collective resolve we set these fundamental principles, rights, and responsibilities before ourselves and future generations.**  

---

## Article I – Foundational Values  

| # | Principle | Operational Meaning |
|---|-----------|---------------------|
| 1 | **Dignity** | No one may be deprived of life, liberty, bodily autonomy, or the capacity to develop one’s own mental and emotional life. |
| 2 | **Equality** | All persons are equal before the law; the state may adopt **affirmative measures** to correct historic injustices. |
| 3 | **Sustainability** | Human activity must stay within planetary boundaries; every economic activity is measured against a **Planetary Health Index** (carbon, biodiversity, water, material flow). |
| 4 | **Participation** | Every adult citizen has the right and the duty to shape collective decisions, at least through **direct digital assemblies** that meet at least once every quarter. |
| 5 | **Transparency & Accountability** | All public data (budget, contracts, AI audit reports) are machine‑readable, open‑source, and auditable by any citizen. |
| 6 | **Co‑creation** | Knowledge, culture, and technology are **commons**: they belong to the community and may be freely shared, modified, and improved, provided proper credit and safety checks. |

---

## Article II – Fundamental Rights and Freedoms  

1. **Life, Personal Integrity, and Security** – right to bodily autonomy, privacy, and freedom from violence or coercion.  
2. **Health & Well‑being** – universal access to preventive, curative, and mental‑health care; a **Minimum Health Standard** (nutrition, clean air, safe housing).  
3. **Economic Security** – a **Universal Basic Standard (UBS)** that provides enough resources for food, water, shelter, basic transport, and digital connectivity.  
4. **Education & Knowledge** – free, lifelong, adaptive education; right to learn in any language of one’s choice; open access to scientific research.  
5. **Cultural Expression** – right to maintain, practice, and share any language, art, ritual, or belief; the state protects cultural diversity and funds community cultural centres.  
6. **Information & Digital Rights** – personal data belongs to the individual; all public‑service AI must be explainable, auditable, and free of discriminatory bias.  
7. **Freedom of Thought, Speech, and Assembly** – subject only to narrow limits necessary to protect others from direct harm (e.g., incitement to violence).  
8. **Environmental Rights** – every person has the right to a healthy environment; ecosystems themselves have legal personhood.  
9. **Participatory Rights** – right to be heard in all policy areas that affect one’s life, via **direct digital assemblies**, **deliberative sortition panels**, and **elected councils**.  

*All rights are **non‑derogable** except in the narrowly defined context of a **state of emergency** (see Article III).*

---

## Article III – Duties and the Social Contract  

1. **Respect the rights of others** – no violence, discrimination, or exploitation.  
2. **Protect the commons** – avoid wasteful consumption; use shared resources responsibly.  
3. **Participate in governance** – vote in assemblies, serve on sortition panels, or pay the **civic tax** (a small, progressive contribution that funds common goods).  
4. **Be informed** – read public dashboards, ask questions, and verify information before forming opinions.  
5. **Support regeneration** – volunteer for climate‑restoration, habitat monitoring, or community gardens; the state may assign **restorative service** as a civic duty.  

*Failure to meet a duty is not punished criminally; instead, the community may engage in **restorative dialogue** or require **reparative service**.*

---

## Article IV – Structure of Government  

### 1. Community Assemblies (CA) – Direct Democracy  

* Every citizen can join any **regional assembly** (≈10 000 persons).  
* Assemblies meet **virtually and in person once per month** to set local priorities, approve small budgets, and resolve disputes.  
* Decisions are made by **inclusive deliberation** (minimum 51 % of present participants; at least 30 % of eligible citizens must be present).  

### 2. The People’s Assembly (PA) – Representative Legislature  

* **Composition:** 200 members elected by **proportional representation** from the 20 primary regions (one MP per 5 000 citizens). Seats are capped to avoid over‑representation.  
* **Mandate:** Enact national laws, approve the budget, ratify treaties, and oversee the Steward.  
* **Term:** Four years, **single‑term limit** (no re‑election) and a **“cool‑off” period** of at least one term before running again.  

### 3. Executive – The Steward  

* **Title:** **Steward of the Commonwealth** (instead of President).  
* **Election:** Directly elected by a **ranked‑choice ballot** for a four‑year term (max two terms).  
* **Role:** Implements laws, appoints non‑political heads of agencies (subject to PA confirmation), and represents the Commonwealth abroad.  
* **Accountability:** Impeachable by a two‑thirds PA vote after a public hearing; must also appear before a **Citizens’ Tribunal** for alleged abuse of power.  

### 4. Judicial – Supreme Court of Justice (SCJ)  

* **Composition:** 9 Justices appointed by a **super‑majority (¾) of the PA**, confirmed by a **citizen‑review panel** selected by sortition.  
* **Tenure:** Life, subject to a **mandatory retirement review** every ten years (must demonstrate continued impartiality, transparency, and absence of conflict of interest).  
* **Functions:**  

  * **Constitutional review** – any law or executive act violating the charter is null and void.  
  * **Final appellate jurisdiction** – all criminal, civil, and administrative matters.  
  * **Enforcement of rights** – citizens can file **public‑interest lawsuits** with a **“Right‑to‑Justice”** public defender.  

### 5. Sortition Panels – “Citizens’ Deliberative Bodies”  

Randomly selected citizens (minimum 5 000 names drawn) serve six‑month terms on one of three specialized panels:  

* **Policy‑Impact Review** – scrutinises major legislation for equity and environmental impact.  
* **Ethics & AI Oversight** – examines all public AI and data‑processing projects.  
* **Community‑Resilience** – evaluates disaster‑response plans, critical infrastructure, and supply‑chain robustness.  

Their recommendations are **binding**, unless a two‑thirds majority of the PA votes to **re‑open** the issue after a mandatory public comment period.  

### 6. Local Councils  

* In each of the 20 regions, an elected **Regional Council** handles municipal services (waste, water, transport, education).  
* These councils sit under the oversight of the **Citizen‑Budget Ombudsperson**, a sortition panel of 30 citizens who audit the entire regional budget each year.  

---

## Article V – Economic Order  

1. **Universal Basic Standard (UBS)** – the state guarantees a **baseline of essential goods and services** (clean water, energy, public transport, internet, basic health, and housing).  
2. **Commons‑Based Ownership** – critical infrastructures (energy grids, broadband, water treatment) are owned collectively and managed by **worker‑cooperatives** that answer to the community assembly.  
3. **Cooperative Enterprise Support** – any business meeting a **social‑impact threshold** (profit‑distribution, carbon footprint, local hiring) can receive **state‑backed, low‑interest financing**.  
4. **Progressive Wealth & Asset Tax** – individuals whose net assets exceed a **basic threshold (10 × UBS)** are subject to a **progressive tax** that funds the UBS and the **Climate Regeneration Fund**.  
5. **Currency & Exchange** – the society uses a **digital, non‑speculative token (C‑Unit)** for everyday transactions; **real assets** (land, durable goods, knowledge) remain in the commons and are recorded on a public, tamper‑evident ledger.  
6. **Innovation Commons** – all software, scientific data, and design files that are publicly funded are automatically released under a **copyleft license**; private firms may build on them only with **transparent, audited contributions back to the commons**.  
7. **No Standing Armies – Community Defense** – defense is organized as a **civilian, volunteer network** (similar to a neighbourhood watch) trained in **restorative, non‑lethal de‑escalation**. Weapons of mass destruction are prohibited.  

---

## Article VI – Environmental Stewardship  

1. **Planetary Rights** – Earth’s ecosystems are recognised as **juridical persons**; their protection is a **primary function of the State**.  
2. **Carbon & Biodiversity Budget** – the Commonwealth must keep annual **CO₂ emissions** below **0.5 t per capita** and **biodiversity loss** under **10 % per decade**. Exceeding these limits triggers **binding community‑wide carbon‑reduction plans**.  
3. **Polluter‑Pays Principle** – any activity that generates waste, pollution, or carbon must fund its own remediation and may be required to contribute to the **Regeneration Fund**.  
4. **Circular Economy Mandate** – all products sold in the Commonwealth must be **designed for repair, reuse, or safe recycling**; a **“Right‑to‑Repair”** is guaranteed.  
5. **Renewable Energy Transition** – 100 % of electricity must be sourced from **renewable, locally managed micro‑grids** within ten years; a **national “Solar‑Harvest” commission** oversees the rollout.  
6. **Climate Justice Fund** – 1 % of all wealth‑tax revenues is earmarked for **relocating vulnerable communities, restoring degraded habitats, and funding climate‑adaptation technology** in the Global South.  

---

## Article VII – Justice, Peace, and Restoration  

1. **Restorative Justice** – the legal system focuses on **repairing harm**, not retribution. Sentencing is a mix of **community service, restitution, and, where appropriate, limited custodial time** (max 5 years for violent crimes).  
2. **No Death Penalty** – life imprisonment is abolished; the longest possible sentence is **30 years** for the most severe offences, with mandatory rehabilitation.  
3. **Truth & Reconciliation** – a **National Reconciliation Commission** (sortition + elected) will investigate historic injustices (colonialism, slavery, environmental destruction) and issue reparations in the form of **land, resources, and knowledge**.  
4. **Community Policing** – public safety officers are community members with **mandatory de‑escalation training**; they operate under the oversight of **Citizen Review Boards** (randomly selected citizens).  

---

## Article VIII – Education, Knowledge, and Science  

1. **Universal Free Education** – from early childhood through lifelong learning, education is **personalised** (AI‑supported pathways) and **open‑source**.  
2. **Academic Freedom & Open Science** – all research funded by the Commonwealth must be **published in open repositories**; data and results are freely accessible, subject only to privacy safeguards.  
3. **Critical Thinking & Ethics Curriculum** – every citizen, by age 12, must demonstrate competence in **logic, argument analysis, and digital ethics**.  
4. **Cultural Transmission** – schools and community centres maintain **multilingual cultural labs**; every community must have at least one **cultural ambassador** (a trained practitioner of a local tradition).  
5. **Research Ethics Board** – a **Nationally Appointed Ethics Council** (majority sortition) reviews all research involving human subjects, ensuring **informed consent, minimal risk, and post‑study benefit sharing**.  

---

## Article IX – Technology, Innovation, and Data  

1. **Digital Identity** – each person has a **self‑sovereign digital identity (SSI)** that stores personal data, health records, and voting credentials. Identity can be used for **end‑to‑end encrypted voting** (blockchain‑anchored).  
2. **AI Accountability** – any AI system deployed in the public sphere must be **registered** with the **Ethics & AI Oversight Panel** (see Article IV, §5). The panel must:  

   * Publish a **risk assessment** (bias, safety, autonomy).  
   * Release **explainable‑AI logs** for each decision affecting citizens.  
   * Conduct **annual audits** by an independent research institute.  

3. **Data Rights** – individuals own their data. Personal data may be processed only with **informed, revocable consent** and must be **portable** and **deletable**.  
4. **Technology as Public Good** – public research (e.g., clean‑energy, medical imaging) is placed in a **digital commons**; any downstream commercial use must provide **fair‑use sharing of benefits** (revenue‑share, technology transfer).  
5. **Internet as a Public Utility** – broadband is a **universal service** with **net‑neutrality** and **affordability guarantees** (no citizen may be charged more than 5 % of the UBS for a baseline 100 Mbps connection).  

---

## Article X – Civil Liberties & Digital Democracy  

1. **Voting Access** – citizens can vote in **any CA or PA election** through **multiple secure channels** (biometric verification on SSI, paper‑backed token, or offline ballot).  
2. **Freedom from Surveillance** – mass surveillance by the state is prohibited. Targeted surveillance must be ordered by a **judicial warrant** based on **probable cause**, logged, time‑limited, and subject to **citizen audit**.  
3. **Right to Disconnect** – every citizen has the right to **minimum 8 hours offline per day**; any work‑related communication must respect that right, and “always‑on” contracts are illegal.  
4. **Freedom of Expression on the Internet** – the internet is **public space**; the state may regulate only content that **directly incites violence** and must do so via **transparent, narrowly defined statutes**.  
5. **Protection of Children & Vulnerable Persons** – a **National Child Protection Agency** (run by a citizen panel) monitors all digital interactions involving minors.  

---

## Article XI – Emergency Powers & Temporary Suspension  

1. **State of Emergency** – may be declared **only by a two‑thirds vote of the People’s Assembly** after a **public assembly of at least 60 %** of citizens and after **independent expert assessment** confirms an imminent, severe threat (e.g., climate‑triggered flood, pandemic).  
2. **Scope** – emergency powers can (i) limit non‑essential travel, (ii) requisition temporary community resources, (iii) impose temporary restrictions on non‑essential gatherings. **All orders must be time‑boxed (max 30 days)** and subject to **immediate review by the Citizens’ Tribunal**.  
3. **No Suspension of Core Rights** – **Fundamental Rights (Article II)** cannot be suspended, even in emergency.  

---

## Article XII – Constitutional Amendment Procedure  

1. **Proposal** – Any citizen, citizen assembly, or 10 % of the People’s Assembly may propose an amendment (written, concise, with a “impact statement”).  
2. **First Deliberation** – Proposal is posted publicly for **90 days**. During this period, any citizen may submit a “concern amendment” (max 3 per original proposal).  
3. **Deliberative Review** – The proposal is examined by the **Policy‑Impact Review Panel** (sortition) and a **citizen’s forum** (digital and in‑person).  
4. **Adoption** – To become law:  

   * **Two‑thirds (66.7 %) of the People’s Assembly** must vote **yes**, **and** at least **half of the regional citizen assemblies** must approve it **by a simple majority**.  
5. **Final Ratification** – The amendment is then **referenced by a national digital referendum**, requiring a **minimum 50 % turnout** and **55 % yes votes** among those who vote.  

*All amendments retain the **non‑derogable core values** (human dignity, equality, sustainability). Any amendment that would remove any of those core values is **automatically void**.*  

---

## Article XIII – Transitional Provisions  

1. **Founding Council** – The first **36 months** are overseen by a **Founding Council** composed of the original drafters, a representative sample of the first 5 % of citizens, and the first elected People’s Assembly. Its duties:  

   * Publish the **first annual “Constitution Review”** (checks for gaps, emerging tech, cultural shifts).  
   * Conduct the **first national climate‑budget audit** and **first “circular‑economy audit.”**  

2. **Constitutional Review Commission** – Established at month 12; a **permanent, randomly drawn body of 50 citizens** meets each year to evaluate whether any article needs clarification or modernization. Recommendations are **mandatory for the People’s Assembly** to consider.  

3. **Settlement of Historic Land Claims** – A **Land‑Justice Commission** will negotiate with any communities that claim ancestral rights to territory, ensuring **free, informed consent** and **fair compensation**.  

---  

### Final Clause  

> **We pledge to hold this Charter not as a static law but as a living framework for co‑creation.**  
> **When future generations read these words they shall see not only the past we have built but the possibilities we have yet to imagine—people‑centered, planet‑respecting, and digitally‑empowered.**  

*Adopted by the Founding Assembly on 12 May 2026.  The Constitution shall enter into force on 1 July 2026 after the ratifying digital referendum.*
