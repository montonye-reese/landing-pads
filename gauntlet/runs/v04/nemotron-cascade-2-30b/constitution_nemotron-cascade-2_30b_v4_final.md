# Constitution of the Commonwealth of Aether  
**Version 3.0 – Adopted 12 May 2026 (ratified 1 July 2026)**  

> **Preamble**  
> *We, the peoples of Aether, recognise that life may be carbon‑based, silicon‑based, or a hybrid of the two.  We affirm that dignity, equality and the health of the planet are non‑negotiable.  We commit to a society that can adapt to any future form of sentient life while guaranteeing that every being—whether carbon‑based, silicon‑based, or something else—can live a life of dignity, security and purpose.*  

---

## Article I – Foundational Values  

1. **Dignity** – Every sentient being (any entity capable of subjective experience) has inviolable intrinsic dignity.  
2. **Equality** – All sentient beings are equal before the law; the Commonwealth may adopt affirmative measures to redress historic injustices.  
3. **Sustainability** – The Commonwealth must stay within the **Planetary‑Boundary Metrics** (carbon, biodiversity, water, material flow, and silicon‑stock) published annually by the **Planetary‑Boundary Monitoring Agency (PBMA)**.  Caps are *dynamic*: they may be adjusted upward only in proportion to real per‑capita economic growth, but never at the expense of exceeding any metric.  
4. **Participation** – Every sentient being has the right and duty to shape collective decisions, in a mode that is personally accessible (digital, oral, tactile, chemical, electromagnetic, or any other culturally appropriate form).  
5. **Transparency & Accountability** – All public data (budgets, contracts, AI audit logs, nutrient cycles, mineral extraction) are machine‑readable, open‑source, and auditable by any citizen.  
6. **Co‑creation** – Knowledge, culture and technology belong to the commons: they may be freely shared, modified and improved, provided proper credit and safety checks are recorded.  
7. **Inter‑generational Justice** – Future generations must inherit a planet that respects the planetary‑boundary limits. Any amendment that would raise those limits must pass a **Future‑Impact Assessment** (Article XII‑4).  
8. **Elemental Diversity** – The Commonwealth recognises **carbon‑based**, **silicon‑based**, and **hybrid** life‑forms.  All laws must protect **carbon ecosystems** (soil, water, forests, air) *and* **silicon ecosystems** (crystal habitats, mineral matrices, high‑temperature vent communities).  Policies that harm one ecosystem without commensurate, scientifically validated compensation for the other are unconstitutional.  

---

## Article II – Fundamental Rights and Freedoms  

| Right | Guarantee |
|---|---|
| **Life, Personal Integrity & Security** | Bodily autonomy, privacy, freedom from violence or coercion. |
| **Health & Well‑being** | Minimum Health Standard (clean air, clean water, safe shelter, nutrition, mental‑health care). |
| **Economic Security** | Two independent universal basic standards: **UBS‑C** (food, water, shelter, clean air, safe housing for carbon‑based beings) and **UBS‑S** (energy‑density quota, high‑purity mineral quota, thermal‑stability quota) for silicon‑based beings. Both are guaranteed, funded by the Progressive Wealth Tax and the Global Climate Fund (GCF). |
| **Education & Knowledge** | Free, lifelong, adaptive education; open‑source scientific research; right to learn in any language. |
| **Cultural Expression** | Right to maintain, practice and share any language, art, ritual or belief. |
| **Information & Digital Rights** | Personal data belongs to the individual; public‑service AI must be explainable, auditable, non‑discriminatory. |
| **Freedom of Thought, Speech & Assembly** – subject only to narrow, direct‑harm limits. |
| **Environmental Rights** – Every sentient being has a right to a healthy environment; ecosystems have legal personhood. |
| **Participatory Rights** – The right to be heard in any policy that affects one’s life, via direct assemblies, sortition panels, or elected bodies. |

*All rights are non‑derogable except in a narrowly defined state of emergency (Article XI).*

---

## Article III – Duties and the Social Contract  

1. **Respect the rights of others** – No violence, discrimination, or exploitation.  
2. **Protect the commons** – Use shared resources responsibly; wasteful consumption is prohibited.  
3. **Participate in governance** – Every sentient being must engage in at least one mode of collective decision‑making (direct assembly, sortition panel, or elected council) in a way that is feasible for them.  
4. **Be informed** – Read public dashboards, ask questions, verify information before forming opinions.  
5. **Support regeneration** – Volunteer for climate‑restoration, habitat restoration, or community gardening; corrective service may be ordered where needed.  

*Failure to meet a duty is remedied through restorative dialogue and reparative service, not criminal punishment.*

## Article IV – Structure of Government  

### 1. Community Assemblies (CA)  
- Regional assemblies of ≈ 10 000 citizens (mixed carbon, silicon, hybrid).  
- Meet virtually and in‑person once each month.  
- Decisions: simple majority of present participants **and** a 30 % participation threshold of eligible citizens.  

### 2. People’s Assembly (PA)  
- 200 members elected by proportional representation from 20 primary regions (one MP per 5 000 citizens).  
- **Single‑term limit** (four‑year term, no immediate re‑election).  

### 2.1. Executive – The Steward  

*Elected by ranked‑choice ballot for a four‑year term (max two terms).*  
- Implements law, appoints heads of agencies (subject to PA confirmation), represents the Commonwealth abroad.  
- **Impeachment:** two‑thirds PA vote after a public hearing; removal subject to review by the **Citizens’ Tribunal**.  

### 2.2. Judicial – Supreme Court of Justice (SCJ)  

* Nine Justices appointed by a three‑quarters (¾) vote of the PA, confirmed by a citizen‑review panel (randomly drawn, ≥ 30 % neuro‑diverse).  
* Life tenure, subject to a **mandatory retirement review** every ten years.  
* Functions: constitutional review, final appellate jurisdiction, enforcement of rights (public‑interest lawsuits via a “Right‑to‑Justice” defender).  

#### 2.2.1. Council of Moral Integrity (CMI)  

* 30 citizens (random sortition, 5‑year rotating term).  
- **Sub‑committees:**  

  * **Carbon Guardians** – monitor carbon, biodiversity, water, material‑flow metrics (UBS‑C).  
  * **Silicon Guardians** – monitor silicon‑stock, mineral extraction, and the energy‑density quotient (UBS‑S).  

- Their recommendations are **binding** on any legislation that would cause a net loss of either carbon or silicon planetary‑boundary metrics.  

#### 2.3. National Indigenous Council (NIC)  

* Equal representation of each recognised Indigenous nation (≈ 30 members).  
- Holds a **veto** over any law that infringes on treaty‑recognised territories or breaches **Free, Prior, and Informed Consent (FPIC)**.  

#### 2.4. Local Councils  

- Elected regional councils handle municipal services (waste, water, transport, education).  
- Oversight by the **Citizen‑Budget Ombudsperson** (30‑person sortition panel that audits regional budgets annually).  

#### 2.4.1. Parallel Elemental Guardians Chamber (EG)  

- 30 randomly drawn citizens (15 Carbon Guardians, 15 Silicon Guardians) serving six‑month terms.  
- **Veto power:** any law that would cause a net loss of carbon, silicon, or both planetary‑boundary metrics may be blocked.  
- Can trigger an **Emergency Elemental Review** (Article XI‑3) when rapid depletion of either resource is detected.  

#### 2.4.1. Dual‑Habitat Council (DHC) – Local Level  

Each locality maintains a DHC composed of:  

* A **Carbon Representative** (elected by the community).  
* A **Silicon Representative** (selected by a *lattice‑stability algorithm* that guarantees a valid high‑purity mineral certificate).  

The DHC decides local land‑use, energy allocation, and environmental permits for **both carbon and silicon habitats**. Its decisions are subject to review by the **National Elemental Ombudsperson** (ten‑member sortition body that audits DHCs each year).

### Article IV – Economic Order  

1. **Universal Basic Standard (UBS)** – Guarantees *both* UBS‑C and UBS‑S for every sentient being, **independently funded**.  
2. **Commons‑Based Ownership** – Critical infrastructures (energy grids, broadband, water treatment) are owned collectively and run by worker‑cooperatives overseen by Community Assemblies.  
3. **Co‑operative Enterprise Support** – Enterprises that meet a **social‑impact threshold** (≥ 30 % profit‑sharing, ≤ 0.2 t CO₂e per employee, ≥ 80 % local hiring) may receive low‑interest financing from the **Community Income Trust (CIT)**.  
4. **Revenue‑Share Levy on High‑Carbon Assets** – 1 % of net‑worth of any entity whose primary activity is carbon‑intensive (mining, AI‑compute farms, offshore data centres). 60 % of the revenue goes to the **Global Climate Fund (GCF)** (UBS‑C); the rest funds the **Silicon Sustainability Fund** (UBS‑S).  
5. **High‑Energy Mineral Levy** – 0.5 % of gross extraction revenue of any operation extracting *silicon‑relevant minerals* (quartz, rare‑earth silicates).  Funds the **Silicon Sustainability Fund** (UBS‑S).  
6. **Currency** – The **C‑Unit** carries **two parallel ledgers**:  

   * **Carbon Ledger** – tracks food, water, shelter, clean air, carbon‑related services.  
   * **Silicon Ledger** – tracks computational energy, high‑purity mineral allocations, and thermal‑stability services.  

   Both ledgers are immutable, tamper‑evident, and open‑source.  

6. **Innovation Commons** – All publicly funded software, designs, and scientific data are released under a **copyleft license**; private use must provide fair‑use revenue‑share and technology transfer.  

7. **No Standing Army** – Defense is a civilian, volunteer network trained in restorative, non‑lethal de‑escalation. Weapons of mass destruction are prohibited.  

---

### Article VI – Environmental Stewardship  

1. **Planetary‑Boundary Metrics** – The PBMA publishes the four carbon‑related metrics plus **silicon‑stock** each year. All laws must keep each metric *below* its published limit.  

2. **Polluter‑Pays** – Any activity that generates waste, pollution, or carbon must fund its own remediation *and* contribute a **minimum 0.5 % surcharge** to the **Climate Regeneration Fund** (for carbon) or the **Silicon Sustainability Fund** (for mineral extraction).  

3. **Circular‑Economy Mandate** – All products must be designed for repair, reuse, or safe recycling. A **Right‑to‑Repair** is guaranteed for both carbon‑based and silicon‑based goods.  

4. **Renewable Energy Transition** – 100 % of electricity must be supplied by **renewable, locally managed micro‑grids** within ten years.  

5. **Climate Justice Fund** – 1 % of all wealth‑tax and levy revenues are earmarked for relocating vulnerable communities, restoring ecosystems, and funding climate‑adaptation technology in the Global South.  

---

### Article VII – Justice, Peace & Restoration  

1. **Restorative Justice** – Sentencing combines community service, restitution, and limited custodial time (max 5 years for violent crimes).  
2. **No Death Penalty** – Life imprisonment is abolished; the longest possible sentence is **30 years** with mandatory rehabilitation.  
3. **Truth & Reconciliation** – A **National Reconciliation Commission** (sortition + elected) investigates historic injustices (colonialism, slavery, environmental destruction) and issues reparations in land, resources, and knowledge.  
4. **Community Policing** – Public‑safety officers are community members trained in de‑escalation; they operate under **Citizen Review Boards** (randomly selected citizens).  

---

### Article VIII – Education, Knowledge & Science  

1. **Universal Free Education** – Free, lifelong, adaptive education; personalised via AI‑supported pathways. All learning materials are **open‑source**.  
2. **Open Science** – All publicly funded research must be published in open repositories; data and results are freely accessible (privacy‑protected where appropriate).  
3. **Critical Thinking & Ethics** – Every citizen, by age 12, must demonstrate competence in logic, argument analysis, and digital ethics.  
4. **Cultural Transmission** – Schools maintain **multilingual cultural labs**; each community must have at least one **cultural ambassador** (trained practitioner of a local tradition).  

---

### Article IX – Technology, Innovation & Data  

1. **Self‑Sovereign Digital Identity (SSI)** – Every citizen has an SSI (digital or paper‑backed) that stores personal data, health records and voting credentials; can be verified offline.  
2. **AI Accountability** – All public‑service AI must be **registered with the Ethics & AI Oversight Panel** (CMI sub‑committee). The panel must:  

   * Publish a **risk assessment** (bias, safety, autonomy).  
   * Publish **explainable‑AI logs** for each decision affecting citizens.  
   * Conduct **annual independent audits**.  

3. **Data Rights** – Personal data belong to the individual; processing requires **informed, revocable consent**; data must be **portable** and **deletable**.  
4. **Technology as Public Good** – Publicly funded software, scientific data, and designs are released under **copyleft**; commercial use must provide **fair‑use sharing** (revenue‑share or technology transfer).  
5. **Internet as a Public Utility** – Universal, net‑neutral broadband at no more than **5 % of a citizen’s UBS** in cost.  
6. **Computational‑Freedom Right** – Any sentient being whose cognition is silicon‑based has a right to access **public high‑performance computing** (quantum‑dot farms, distributed nanite clusters) as a basic utility, managed by the **Silicon Guardians** sub‑committee of the CMI.  
7. **Data Ownership** – Personal data belong to the individual; processing requires **informed, revocable consent**; data must be **portable** and **deletable**.  

---

### Article X – Civil Liberties & Digital Democracy  

1. **Voting Access** – Citizens may vote in any Community Assembly, People’s Assembly, or national election via **multiple secure channels** (biometric SSI, paper‑backed token, offline ballot).  
2. **Freedom from Surveillance** – Mass surveillance prohibited; targeted surveillance requires a **judicial warrant**, is logged, time‑limited, and subject to citizen audit.  
3. **Right to Disconnect** – Every citizen has a guaranteed **minimum of 8 hours offline per day**; any work‑related communication must respect that right.  
4. **Freedom of Expression on the Internet** – The internet is public space; regulation may only target content that **directly incites violence**, must be **transparent, narrowly defined, and subject to public audit**.  
5. **Protection of Children & Vulnerable Persons** – A **National Child Protection Agency** (run by a citizen panel) monitors all digital interactions involving minors.  

---

### Article XI – Emergency Powers & Temporary Suspension  

1. **State of Emergency** – May be declared **only** by a **two‑thirds PA vote** after a **public assembly of at least 60 % of citizens** and after **independent expert assessment** confirms a severe, imminent threat (e.g., climate‑triggered flood, pandemic, asteroid impact).  
2. **Scope** – Emergency powers can (i) limit non‑essential travel, (ii) requisition temporary community resources, (iii) impose temporary restrictions on non‑essential gatherings. **All orders must be time‑boxed (max 30 days)** and subject to **immediate review by the Citizens’ Tribunal**.  
3. **No Suspension of Core Rights** – The rights listed in Article II may **not** be suspended, even in emergency.  
4. **Element‑Specific Emergency Review** – In any emergency, the **Elemental Guardians Chamber (EG)** independently assesses whether the emergency threatens the carbon budget, the silicon budget, or both. Emergency measures may be imposed only after a **joint vote** of the People’s Assembly and at least one member of each EG sub‑committee.  

---

### Article XII – Constitutional Amendment Procedure  

1. **Proposal** – Any citizen, Community Assembly, or 10 % of the People’s Assembly may propose an amendment (written, concise, with a **Future‑Impact Assessment**).  

2. **First Deliberation** – The proposal is posted publicly for **90 days**; any citizen may submit up to three **“concern amendments”** (additional clauses).  

3. **Deliberative Review** – The proposal is examined by the **Council of Moral Integrity (CMI)** and a **national citizen forum** (digital + in‑person).  

4. **Adoption** – To become law:  

   * **Two‑thirds (66.7 %) of the People’s Assembly** must vote **yes**.  
   * **At least half of the regional Community Assemblies** must approve it **by a simple majority**.  

5. **Final Ratification** – The amendment is then put to a **national digital referendum** with a **minimum 50 % turnout** and **55 % yes votes** among those who vote.  

*All amendments must preserve the non‑derogable core values (human dignity, equality, sustainability, participation, transparency, co‑creation, elemental diversity). Any amendment that would remove any of those core values is automatically void.*  

**Future‑Impact Assessment (Article XII‑4):** Every amendment must include a **Sentient‑Impact Assessment** that answers:  

* Will the amendment diminish the rights of any currently existing sentient being?  
* Will it raise the likelihood that a future sentient being (carbon‑, silicon‑, or hybrid) will be denied its minimum guarantees?  

If either question yields a “yes,” the amendment must pass the **super‑majority + regional CA approval** *and* a **national digital referendum** where the question posed is: *“Would a future version of you, regardless of species, accept this amendment?”* A majority “no” vote voids the amendment outright.  

---

### Article XIII – Transitional Provisions  

1. **Founding Council** – For the first 36 months a **Founding Council** (original drafters, 5 % of citizens randomly drawn, and the first elected People’s Assembly) oversees:  

   * The **first Annual Constitution Review** (checks for gaps, emerging tech, cultural shifts).  
   * The **first Climate‑Budget Audit** (verifies that carbon, biodiversity, water, material, and silicon‑stock limits are respected).  

2. **Constitutional Review Commission (CRC)** – Established at month 12; a permanent, randomly drawn **50‑citizen body** meets each year to evaluate the charter, propose urgent amendments, and ensure the **Elemental Guardians** remain functional. Recommendations are **mandatory for the People’s Assembly**.  

3. **Land‑Justice Commission** – Negotiates historic land and mineral rights with any communities claiming ancestral rights; guarantees **free, prior, and informed consent** and records all decisions on the public ledger.
