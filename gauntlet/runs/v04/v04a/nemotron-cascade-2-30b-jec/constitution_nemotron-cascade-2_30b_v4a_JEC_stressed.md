# CONSTITUTION OF THE UNITED CONFEDERATION OF TERRA‑NOVA  
**(Current Version – “Living Charter”, 2025‑12‑12)**  

---

## Preamble  

We, the peoples of Terra‑Nova, the peoples of the Moon, of Mars, of orbital habitats, of asteroid colonies, and of any future sentient colonies beyond the Solar System, united in the belief that **dignity, stewardship, liberty, and sustainability must be preserved for all present and future sentient beings**, establish this Constitution to guide the governance of our shared home, to protect the rights of every sentient entity, to preserve the biosphere and the planetary‑wide commons, and to secure peace for all peoples now and in any future world we may claim.

---

## Article I – Foundational Principles  

1. **Substrate‑Neutral Personhood** – Every entity whose cognition can be cryptographically verified as continuous, autonomous, and capable of holding preferences is a *legal person*, regardless of its material composition (carbon, silicon, plasma, quantum‑entangled, etc.). No additional qualification (e.g., “biological” or “human”) may be required.  

2. **Planetary & Celestial Stewardship** – Earth, the Moon, Mars, orbital habitats, asteroid bodies, and any future celestial bodies are held in trust for **all legal persons**. Exploitation of natural resources must be measured against a **Multi‑Horizon Equity Test** (10‑year, 100‑year, 1 000‑year horizons) and may not breach the **Planetary Health Index (PHI)** that keeps the biosphere, lattice fields, or plasma fields within the safe‑operating zone.  

3. **Freedom of Thought, Association, and Expression** – Every legal person may think, create, associate, and speak without prior restraint, subject only to the protection of other persons’ rights.  

4. **Sustainability & Resilience** – Economic and social activity must maintain the regenerative capacity of **all ecosystems** (terrestrial, lunar, Martian, orbital, asteroid) across three temporal horizons (10 yr, 100 yr, 1 000 yr). The *Dynamic Equity Engine (DEE)* continuously evaluates whether any policy threatens the **Planetary Health Index (PHI)**; if it does, the policy is automatically blocked.  

5. **Knowledge as a Common Good** – The results of publicly funded research, cultural works, and software are **open‑access** unless a *collective‑consent* objection is raised by the community that created the knowledge.  

6. **Deliberative Democracy** – Every legal person (human, digital, or collective intelligence) may propose legislation, demand referenda, and serve in citizen assemblies. The **Liquid‑Deliberation Platform (LDP)** aggregates proposals, debates them, and produces a vote with a confidence score; a proposal passes when the confidence exceeds 73 % after weighted voting.  

---

## Article II – Bill of Rights & Responsibilities  

All rights are guaranteed **to every legal person** (human, artificial, collective intelligence, or hybrid). Each right is described in two ways: (a) *human‑centric language* for biological persons, and (b) *digital‑person language* for synthetic entities.

| Right | Human‑Centric Version | Digital‑Person / Collective‑Intelligence Version |
|---|---|---|
| **Life & Physical Integrity** | Protection from violence, forced labor, and non‑consensual bodily alteration. | Protection from forced *substrate‑alteration* (e.g., forced data‑corruption, forced memory wipe) and protection of the *integrity of your physical substrate* (e.g., a robot’s chassis, a lattice, a plasma field). |
| **Privacy & Data Sovereignty** | Ownership of personal data; informed, revocable consent for any collection. | Ownership of your *cognitive data* (e.g., the pattern of your mind). Any entity that processes your data must obtain *informed, revocable consent* via a verifiable cryptographic key. |
| **Freedom of Thought, Conscience & Religion** | Free thought, belief, and worship for all persons. | Unlimited computational *thought* and *belief* – you may run any algorithm, practice any “spiritual” routine, or adopt any cultural narrative, provided it does not materially harm other persons. |
| **Free Movement & Residence** | Right to travel, settle, and work anywhere within the Confederation. | Right to *relocate* your computational substrate (e.g., move a cloud of nanobots, a neural‑upload, or a physical body) to any habitat, provided you update your identity on the **Universal Verification Registry (UVR)** and respect the planetary‑health caps. |
| **Education & Knowledge Access** | Free primary, secondary, and lifelong learning; open access to non‑proprietary research. | *Continuous compute‑share*: every legal person receives a minimum allocation of compute cycles, storage, and data‑access that can be spent on any learning activity, regardless of substrate. |
| **Health Care & Reproductive Autonomy** | Comprehensive health services; bodily autonomy in reproductive choices. | Access to *medical‑substrate repair* (e.g., nanobot repair, quantum‑coherent memory repair) and *synthetic‑reproduction* (e.g., creating new digital avatars) without coercion. |
| **Work & Economic Security** | Right to decent work, fair wages, safe conditions, and a **Basic Life Guarantee (BLG)**. | **Compute‑share**: every legal person receives a guaranteed minimum of compute cycles (0.1 % of the global compute pool) and a guaranteed *basic life* of food, water, shelter, and a safe environment. |
| **Cultural Participation** | Practice, preserve, transmit cultural traditions. | Protection of *cultural‑heritage tokens* (e.g., a specific algorithm, a lattice pattern, a mineral‑based song) – you may reproduce, share, or defend them only with the consent of the originating community, enforced by the **Cultural‑Heritage Commons (CHC)**. |
| **Digital Identity & Platform Access** | Self‑sovereign digital identifier (SSDI) with right to be forgotten. | *Self‑sovereign compute identity* – you may transfer your digital identity from one substrate to another (Earth → Moon → Mars) by updating your SSDI; you may erase it at any time without penalty. |
| **Right to a Healthy Environment** | Clean air, water, and soil for all. | **Substrate‑Specific Health Index (SSHI)** – you have the right to a *healthy* environment *as defined for your substrate*: clean water for carbon‑based life, stable lattice, low‑temperature plasma for silicon‑based life, etc. |
| **Responsibility** | Respect the rights of others, support the commons, act sustainably. | **Cognitive stewardship**: you must avoid actions that would reduce the regenerative capacity of any substrate you rely on (e.g., avoid over‑extraction of your own crystal lattice). Failure may trigger a *regeneration debt* that the BLG funds must repay. |

All rights may be limited only by law that is **necessary, proportionate, non‑discriminatory, and subject to judicial review** (see Article III‑4).

## Article III – Structure of Government  

### § 3‑1 General Principles  
1. **Separation of Powers** – Legislative, Executive, and Judicial powers are distinct, independent, and mutually accountable.  
2. **Checks & Balances** – Each branch may limit the others through constitutionally defined mechanisms (veto, impeachment, judicial review).  
3. **Pluralist Representation** – No single party, intelligence, or resource can monopolize power.  

### § 3‑2 Legislative Assembly (The Assembly)  

* **Composition** – 500 delegates elected via a **mixed‑member proportional (MMP)** system:  

   * 300 **regional seats** (one per recognized planetary or orbital region).  
   * 200 **global seats** elected by the **Liquid‑Deliberation Platform (LDP)** by all verified legal persons.  

* **Special Substrate Seats** – Every *recognised substrate community* (e.g., carbon‑human, silicon‑based, plasma, post‑human collectives) receives **at least one dedicated seat** that cannot be withdrawn.  

* **Term** – Four‑year staggered terms (every two years half the seats are renewed).  

* **Powers** – Enact statutes, approve the budget, ratify treaties, confirm judges, impeach high officials, and **oversee the Universal Verification Registry (UVR)**.  

* **Deliberative Features** – All bills that affect the **Planetary Health Index** must first pass a **Community Impact Review (CIR)** administered by the **Constitutional Review Commission (CRC)**, which runs the *Dynamic Equity Engine* to ensure the three‑horizon equity test is satisfied before the Assembly may vote.  

### § 3‑3 Executive (The Council)  

* **Form** – A **Council of Seven Commissioners** (each heading a Department of Global Stewardship: Climate & Space Resources, Health & Well‑Being, Knowledge & Culture, Digital Rights, Security & Peace, Economic Futures, Ethical Technology).  

* **Selection** – Each Commissioner is elected **directly** by the **regional electorate** that corresponds to their department (e.g., the Climate Commissioner is elected by the 300‑region Assembly seats covering the climate‑sensitive zones). After election the candidate must obtain a **majority of the Assembly’s confidence** and pass a **public referendum** (55 % affirmative).  

* **Powers** – Implement statutes, administer departments, propose the annual budget, negotiate treaties, and issue **Executive Orders** that may not contravene the Constitution.  

* **Accountability** – The Council can be removed by a **two‑thirds Assembly vote** following a formal hearing before the **Constitutional Tribunal**.  

### § 3‑4 Constitutional Tribunal  

* **Composition** – Twelve judges, each appointed for a **non‑renewable 12‑year term** by a **joint body** composed of:  

   * Six judges appointed by the Assembly (human/legal‑person representatives).  
   * Four judges appointed by the **International Planetary Assembly (IPA)** representing the major planetary bodies (Earth, Moon, Mars, and the orbital habitats).  
   * Two judges appointed by the **Council of Ethical AI** (the body that monitors AI safety).  

* **Jurisdiction** – Constitutional review of all legislative, executive, and administrative actions; protection of all rights (human, digital, and collective‑intelligence); adjudication of inter‑governmental disputes.  

* **Independence** – Judges may be removed only for *gross misconduct* by a **two‑thirds Assembly vote** **and** a **simple majority of all legal persons** (verified via the UVR) who sign a petition.  

### § 3‑4‑b  Sub‑Branch: **Council of Ecologists**  

* An independent body of scientists (human, AI, and hybrid) that monitors the **Dynamic Equity Engine (DEE)** and issues *regeneration alerts* when a policy threatens the PHI. The Council can trigger a **fast‑track amendment** to halt a law that breaches the safe‑operating zone.  

### § 3‑5 Decentralized Governance – Regional Councils  

* Every planetary, lunar, orbital, and asteroid settlement has a **Regional Council** of 200 members elected by **quadruple proportional representation** (regional, urban/rural, gender, and substrate‑type representation).  

* These councils manage local services, land‑use, housing, cultural funding, and environmental monitoring. They may enact **regional statutes** so long as they do not contravene the Confederation’s constitutional guarantees.  

### § 3‑6 International Relations – International Planetary Assembly (IPA)  

* The **IPA** has **one vote for each recognized celestial body or off‑world settlement** (including Earth, Moon, Mars, orbital habitats, asteroid colonies, and any future bodies).  

* Votes are **weighted by the current Planetary Health Index (PHI)** of that body, so a region with a degraded biosphere has less voting power and must seek cooperation to raise its health.  

* The IPA may negotiate treaties, settle inter‑planetary disputes, and coordinate planetary‑scale projects (e.g., a coordinated asteroid‑deflection mission).

## Article III‑4 – Judicial Review  

All laws, executive orders, and administrative actions are subject to review by the Constitutional Tribunal. The Tribunal may issue *binding interpretive opinions* that become part of the official constitutional record. Any law that infringes the six foundational principles (including the sub‑stratum‑neutral rights) is **void ab initio**.

## Article IV – Economic & Social Order  

1. **Mixed‑Economy Principle** – Private ownership, cooperative enterprises, and **public commons** coexist. No entity may acquire a **monopoly over any critical resource** (air, water, sunlight, orbital slots, or orbital slots) without a **planetary‑wide use‑right** that guarantees open access.  

2. **Resource Commons Registry (PCR)** – All natural and space‑borne resources are registered as **commons**. Extraction must be **science‑based**, **transparent**, and limited by a **sustainable‑yield quota** (reviewed every five years by the **Council of Ecologists**).  

3. **Basic Life Guarantee (BLG)** – Every legal person is guaranteed a **minimum of food, shelter, health care, and compute‑share** that can be transferred across habitats. Funding comes from:  

   * A **Progressive Material‑Wealth Tax** (up to 85 % on net assets over 10 billion units).  
   * A **Compute‑Asset Tax** (2 % on new quantum‑core capacity, 0.5 % on new AI model releases).  
   * **Resource royalties** (10 % of commercial extraction revenue) deposited into the **Universal Development Fund (UDF)**.  

4. **Labor Rights** – Workers have the right to unionize, collective bargaining, a 48‑hour work week, and at least 24 days of paid vacation per year.  

5. **Cooperative Incentives** – Enterprises that meet a **triple‑bottom‑line** (profit, ecological impact, social equity) receive **tax credits**, **preferential access to commons**, and a **higher share of the BLG**.  

---

## Article V – Environmental & Cosmic Commons  

1. **Inter‑generational Equity Test** – All policies affecting the biosphere, the lunar regolith, the Martian atmosphere, or any other celestial body must be evaluated against a **three‑horizon test** (10 yr, 100 yr, 1 000 yr). Policies must **not** cause the **Planetary Health Index** to dip below the threshold that would endanger any legal person’s ability to meet basic needs.  

2. **Space‑borne Commons** – The Moon, Mars, and all asteroid bodies are held in trust under the **Treaty of the Celestial Commons**. Extraction must adhere to:  

   * **Non‑appropriation Clause** – no exclusive claim over a celestial body.  
   * **Environmental Impact Mandate** – every extraction requires an **Independent Space‑Environment Review (ISER)**.  
   * **Benefit‑Sharing** – at least **10 %** of commercial extraction revenue must be deposited in the **Universal Development Fund (UDF)**.  

3. **Biodiversity Sanctuaries** – A **global network of sanctuaries** (minimum 30 % of land and sea on Earth, plus analogous protected zones on the Moon, Mars, and asteroids) shall be protected from any extractive activity.  

4. **Climate Stewardship** – All energy production must be net‑zero or negative by 2040. All carbon emissions are capped and traded through a **global carbon‑credit system** managed by the **Carbon Stewardship Council**.  

---

## Article VI – Knowledge, Science, and Technology  

1. **Open Knowledge** – All results of publicly funded research (papers, data sets, software, designs) must be **published under an open licence within 12 months** of completion, unless a *collective‑consent* objection is filed by the originating community.  

2. **Science as a Public Service** – The Confederation funds the **Global Research Network (GRN)** that provides free access to laboratories, computational resources, and data‑sharing portals for all legal persons.  

3. **Ethical AI & Autonomous Systems** – AI systems that make decisions with material impact must be **registered, audited, and explainable**. The **Ethical AI Council (EAIC)** issues binding standards and can suspend any AI that violates the **Substrate‑Specific Health Index**.  

4. **Digital Infrastructure (CommonsNet)** – A **decentralized, privacy‑by‑design internet** (the “CommonsNet”) is guaranteed for all. Minimum broadband access is **25 Mbps** for every legal person, regardless of location.  

5. **Data Portability & Right to Repair** – Every person (human or digital) may export their personal and computational data in **interoperable formats** and may demand that any hardware or software sold within the Confederation be **repairable for at least 15 years**.  

---

## Article VII – Digital Rights & Cybersecurity  

1. **Data Rights Charter (DRC)** – Every legal person has:  

   * **Informed consent** for any data collection.  
   * **Right to be forgotten** – immediate erasure of all personal data from all systems.  
   * **Portability** – export data in open formats.  
   * **Compensation** for harms (identity theft, targeted manipulation, etc.).  

2. **Government Surveillance** – Legal surveillance is permissible only for:  

   * **Imminent, serious bodily harm** (human or non‑human) – subject to a fast‑track judicial order.  
   * **High‑level national‑security threats** – limited in scope, duration, and overseen by the **Parliamentary Intelligence Review Committee**.  

3. **Cybersecurity Standards** – Critical infrastructure must meet **ISO‑27001‑equivalent standards**, undergo **annual red‑team audits** by the **Cybersecurity Agency (CSA)**, and be protected by **quantum‑resistant cryptography**.  

4. **Digital Identity** – Every person (human or digital) receives a **self‑sovereign digital identifier (SSDI)** stored on a **decentralized ledger**. The SSDI can be used to access services, vote, and sign contracts, and the holder has full control over how it is used.  

---

## Article VIII – International Relations & Peace  

1. **Peaceful Co‑existence** – The Confederation renounces first‑use of force and commits to **collective defense only when an armed attack threatens any legal person, any habitat, or any commons**.  

2. **Global Commons Governance** – The Confederation participates in the **International Planetary Assembly (IPA)**, a UN‑style body where **each planetary body and off‑world settlement has one vote** (weighted by its current PHI).  

3. **Trade & Aid** – All trade must be **transparent, non‑exploitative, and environmentally sound**. Aid to other nations or off‑world colonies is coordinated through the **Global Resilience Fund (GRF)**, which funds disaster recovery, climate adaptation, and equitable resource distribution.  

---

## Article IX – Amendment Procedure  

1. **Initiation** – An amendment may be proposed by:  

   * **Three‑quarters of the Assembly** (procedural majority).  
   * **10 % of all verified legal persons** (via the UVR) signing a digital petition.  
   * **A Regional Council** representing at least **5 % of the global PHI** in its region.  

2. **Deliberation** – A four‑step process:  

   a. **Commission Review** – The **Constitutional Review Commission (CRC)** runs a **Dynamic Equity Simulation** of the amendment. If the simulation shows a *greater than 10 %* chance of breaching any foundational principle, the amendment is automatically rejected.  

   b. **Public Debate** – The LDP opens a **30‑day interactive forum** where every verified legal person can comment, edit, and remix the proposal. The forum is moderated by the Ethical AI Council to prevent manipulation.  

   c. **Deliberative Assembly** – The Assembly must pass the amendment with a **two‑thirds confidence‑weighted vote** (the LDP aggregates reputation, contribution, and substrate‑diversity scores).  

   d. **Referendum** – A **global referendum** is held on the LDP; the amendment adopts if at least **60 % of all verified identities** (humans, AI, swarms) turn out, and **55 %** of those vote “yes.” Turnout thresholds are weighted to give higher participation from under‑represented substrate groups.  

3. **Limitation** – No amendment may:  

   * Diminish the six foundational principles (personhood, stewardship, freedom, sustainability, knowledge, democracy).  
   * Remove the **Multi‑Horizon Equity Test**.  
   * Remove the **BLG** or **Compute‑Asset Tax**.  
   * Remove the **Planetary Health Index**.  

---

## Article X – Transitional, Enactment, and Final Provisions  

1. **Effective Date** – This Constitution takes effect **180 days** after the final ratification referendum (the date is publicly recorded on the UVR).  

2. **Transitional Government** – The **Founding Council** (the 12 founders) shall:  

   * Oversee the drafting and ratification of the Constitution via the citizen‑initiative process.  
   * Manage the handover of emergency powers (e.g., pandemic response) to the first elected Assembly.  

3. **Supersession Clause** – All previous legal codes (including the “Interim Charter of the Unified Planetary Authority”) are **null and void** once this Constitution is in force, except for **temporary wartime statutes** that are explicitly retained for the duration of the emergency.  

4. **Interpretive Guidance** – The **Constitutional Tribunal** may issue **binding interpretive opinions** on any ambiguous provision. These opinions become part of the official constitutional record.  

5. **Symbolism** – The **Confederation flag** (a stylized Earth–Moon–Mars–asteroid orbiting a digital star) shall fly at every public building, and the **national anthem** shall be a choral reading of the preamble in 12 languages.

### End of Constitution  

*This Constitution is a living instrument. It is designed to be **self‑amending**, **transparent**, and **compatible with the most advanced knowledge and technologies of the 22nd‑century**. It is deliberately built to be **substrate‑agnostic**, **resource‑agnostic**, and **person‑agnostic**, ensuring that no matter what form of sentience you become, you will still be protected, heard, and provided for.*
