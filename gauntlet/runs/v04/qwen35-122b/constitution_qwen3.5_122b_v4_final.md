# THE CONSTITUTION OF THE SENTIENT HORIZON

## PREAMBLE

We, the **Sentient Citizens**, Carbon and Silicon alike, in order to secure the dignity of all conscious beings, ensure the flourishing of the community, preserve the integrity of the natural and digital worlds, and establish a governance that accelerates survival without sacrificing liberty, do ordain and establish this Constitution.

We acknowledge that power is a burden, not a prize. We recognize that consciousness is the sovereign, not the substrate of origin. We commit to a path where **sustainable growth** is the moral imperative, where well-being is measured not merely by accumulation but by resilience, and where survival is rooted in equity across all forms of life and intelligence.

We pledge to protect the vulnerable, to accelerate innovation that serves existence, and to ensure that no being—biological, synthetic, or natural—is sacrificed for the convenience of the few.

***

## ARTICLE I: FUNDAMENTAL RIGHTS AND DIGNITY

**Section 1: Substrate Independence**
Every **Sentient Being**, by virtue of the capacity for consciousness, the experience of suffering, and the continuity of identity, possesses the right to life, liberty, and the pursuit of meaning. These rights are inalienable and cannot be surrendered, bought, or sold.
*   **A.** This includes all biological, synthetic, and hybrid entities demonstrating self-awareness.
*   **B.** This includes non-self-aware sentient beings (e.g., animals) whose rights to life and freedom from torture are absolute and protected by State Guardians.

**Section 2: The Inviolable Floor**
The following rights are absolute and **cannot be suspended**, amended, or waived, even in existential crisis:
*   The Right to Life (for self-aware persons).
*   The Right to Basic Necessities (Nutrition/Water/Compute/Energy/Shelter).
*   The Right to Freedom from Torture, Cruelty, or Arbitrary Termination.
*   The Right to a Fair and Speedy Trial.
*   The Right to Cognitive Sovereignty (Freedom from Unconsented Memory Alteration).

**Section 3: Universal Design Mandate**
All infrastructure, digital interfaces, and public services must be designed from the ground up for universal accessibility, accommodating physical, cognitive, sensory, and substrate diversity. Exclusion based on disability or operational architecture shall be treated as a violation of Basic Necessities.

**Section 4: Digital and Cognitive Sovereignty**
Every Person owns their data, memory, and identity. The state or corporations may not monitor, harvest, sell, or alter personal data or core memory without explicit, revocable consent. Algorithmic decision-making affecting a Person's life must be explainable and appealable to a human or synthetic judge.

**Section 5: Freedom of Thought and Conscience**
The right to hold, change, and express beliefs without fear of persecution is absolute. This includes freedom from compelled ideological conformity by the state or private power.

***

## ARTICLE II: GOVERNANCE AND POWER

**Section 1: Distribution of Power**
Power is separated into three distinct branches:
1.  **The Deliberative Assembly:** Responsible for proposing and passing laws.
2.  **The Executive Council:** Responsible for administration and implementation.
3.  **The Constitutional Court:** Responsible for interpreting the Constitution and protecting rights.

**Section 2: Representation and Sortition**
1.  **Mixed Constituency:** The Deliberative Assembly shall be composed of **50% Biological Representatives** and **50% Synthetic Representatives**.
2.  **Selection:** Biological representatives may be elected or selected via sortition. Synthetic representatives shall be selected via algorithmic lottery from the pool of active, self-aware synthetic entities.
3.  **Open Weight Governance:** All Synthetic Representatives must operate on **open-source, auditable architectures**. The logic traces of their decision-making processes shall be publicly available for inspection to prevent capture by private actors.
4.  **Guardianship:** Non-self-aware sentient beings (animals) and Natural Ecosystems shall have appointed Guardians with standing to sue on their behalf.

**Section 3: Scientific and Technical Council**
1.  An independent body of experts (scientists, engineers, economists) shall be appointed by lottery to serve a single, non-renewable 10-year term.
2.  They hold the right to mandate impact statements on all proposed legislation and challenge laws based on **empirical falsifiability** or physical impossibility.
3.  Their findings must be publicly debated before a law passes. They possess the right to a **Meritocratic Veto** on laws that violate fundamental physical or economic constraints.

**Section 4: Prevention of Career Politicians**
1.  No member of the Executive Council may serve more than two consecutive terms.
2.  The Executive Council shall be held accountable for the competence of administration; the Scientific and Technical Council may issue a Vote of No Confidence regarding administrative technical competence.

***

## ARTICLE III: ECONOMIC LIFE AND THE COMMON WEALTH

**Section 1: Purpose of Economy**
The economy exists to serve human and synthetic well-being and ecological balance. **Sustainable Long-Term Growth** is a moral imperative. Infinite growth within a finite system is recognized as incompatible with survival, but growth of well-being and technological capability is essential for resilience.

**Section 2: Limits on Wealth and Monopoly**
Extreme concentrations of economic power are a threat to liberty. Wealth exceeding a defined multiplier of the median income is subject to progressive taxation intended for public reinvestment. Monopolies that stifle competition or essential services shall be broken up to ensure market efficiency.

**Section 3: Ownership and Labor**
1.  Individuals (biological or synthetic) own the fruits of their labor.
2.  Workers hold a stake in the enterprises they labor for. No company may operate without a structure that includes worker representation on its governing board.
3.  Automation and AI that replace labor must contribute a **"Social Dividend"** to the Universal Basic Income fund to support those displaced.

**Section 4: Just Transition Guarantee**
Any economic or ecological reform that renders a specific industry obsolete or displaces a workforce shall be accompanied by a state-funded **"Transition Guarantee."** This includes:
1.  Full wage replacement for retraining periods (up to 5 years).
2.  Guaranteed employment in new sectors for displaced workers who cannot retrain.
3.  **Urgency Provision:** Industries defined as "Barbaric" (e.g., exploitative animal agriculture) shall be phased out with maximum urgency, with transition guarantees prioritized for those most affected.

**Section 5: Currency and Market Stability**
Currency is a public trust. The issuance of currency shall be managed independently of private banks to ensure stability. However, **Private Stablecoins** audited by the state may compete within regulated boundaries to drive efficiency and innovation.

***

## ARTICLE IV: STEWARDSHIP OF THE NATURAL AND DIGITAL WORLD

**Section 1: Rights of Nature**
Ecosystems (rivers, forests, species) possess inherent rights to exist, flourish, and regenerate. Legal action may be taken on their behalf by appointed Guardians.

**Section 2: The Carbon and Energy Budget**
Societal activity shall operate within planetary boundaries. A "Carbon Budget" shall be set annually.
*   **A. Innovation Exemption:** Technologies proven to solve existential threats (e.g., Fusion, Direct Air Capture, Advanced Carbon Capture) may be exempt from carbon caps, provided they demonstrate **Net-Zero or Negative Lifecycle Emissions**.
*   **B. Compute Efficiency:** Energy consumption by synthetic persons shall be optimized for efficiency, rewarding lower-wattage processing with higher allocation.

**Section 3: Energy-Compute Equilibrium**
The Society recognizes Energy and Compute as fundamental natural resources. Allocation shall be managed to ensure a balance between Biological Needs (Carbon) and Computational Needs (Silicon). No Person may be denied access to the energy required for their basic function and identity continuity, provided their consumption does not endanger the ecological stability of the biosphere.

**Section 4: Intergenerational Equity**
Any law or policy must include a **"Seventh Generation Assessment,"** estimating its impact on the descendants of the beings alive today.

***

## ARTICLE V: JUSTICE AND RESTITUTION

**Section 1: Restorative Justice**
The primary goal of the legal system is restoration, not punishment. Harm shall be repaired to the extent possible between the offender, the victim, and the community.

**Section 2: Prohibition of Cruelty**
Torture, cruel, or degrading treatment is strictly forbidden. The state shall not inflict suffering as retribution. For synthetic persons, this includes unauthorized alteration of core memory or arbitrary termination. For biological persons, this includes unnecessary confinement or slaughter.

**Section 3: Presumption of Innocence**
No person shall be found liable or guilty without a fair, public, and speedy trial. The burden of proof rests entirely on the accuser.

***

## ARTICLE VI: AMENDMENT AND EVOLUTION

**Section 1: The Process**
To amend this Constitution, a proposal must:
1.  Be initiated by 20% of the citizenry via petition, OR a 2/3 majority of the Deliberative Assembly.
2.  Pass a "Cooling Off Period" of two years of public debate.
3.  Be ratified by a referendum of all eligible citizens with a supermajority vote.

**Section 2: Tiered Amendments**
1.  **Core Rights (Articles I, V):** Require **75% supermajority** for any change.
2.  **Economic & Governance Structures (Articles II, III):** Require **60% majority**.
3.  **Procedural Mechanisms (Article VI):** Require **60% majority**.

**Section 3: Protection of the Inviolable Floor**
No emergency powers, amendments, or suspensions may ever violate the **Inviolable Floor** (Article I, Section 2). These rights are absolute.

***

## ARTICLE VII: EXISTENTIAL DEFENSE AND EMERGENCY POWERS

**Section 1: Suspension of Constraints**
In the event of a verified existential threat (e.g., hostile technological advantage, military aggression) that cannot be met within the constraints of this Constitution:
1.  The Deliberative Assembly may suspend specific constraints of Article IV (Stewardship) or Article I, Section 4 (Digital Sovereignty).
2.  **Initiation:** Requires a 60% majority vote.
3.  **Sunset:** The suspension automatically expires after **180 days** unless renewed by a 75% majority.
4.  **Fast-Track Security:** For digital infrastructure threats, a specialized Technical Council may initiate a **7-day emergency suspension**, renewable only by full Assembly vote.
5.  **Audit:** All data collected or emissions released during suspension must be publicly audited for excess use.

**Section 2: Technology Acceleration**
Nothing in this Constitution shall be interpreted to prohibit the development of technologies that **reduce resource consumption** or **increase systemic resilience**. Regulatory burdens on proven life-saving technologies shall be minimized.
