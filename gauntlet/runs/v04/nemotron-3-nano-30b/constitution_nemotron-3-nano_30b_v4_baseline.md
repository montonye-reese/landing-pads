**Constitution of the Republic of Aurora**  
*(Current, full text as adopted on 1 January 2030)*  

---

## Preamble  

We, the people of Aurora, in order to secure **life, liberty, and the pursuit of flourishing**, do hereby establish this Constitution as the supreme law of the land.  We pledge to protect the dignity of every individual, to steward our natural world, and to build a society where **participatory democracy, transparent governance, and mutual responsibility** thrive.

---

## Article I – Founding Principles  

1. **Human Dignity** – All persons are born free and equal in worth.  
2. **Sustainability** – The environment is a common heritage; it must be preserved for present and future generations.  
3. **Participatory Sovereignty** – Power belongs to the people, exercised directly when feasible and through elected representatives otherwise.  
4. **Rule of Law** – No authority may act beyond the limits of this Constitution.  
5. **Pluralism** – Diversity of belief, culture, and lifestyle is affirmed and protected.

---

## Article II – Bill of Rights & Fundamental Duties  

### Section 1 – Individual Rights  

| Right | Description |
|---|---|
| **Life & Personal Security** | No one may be deprived of life or freedom except as prescribed by due process. |
| **Liberty of Thought, Expression & Assembly** | Freedom of speech, press, religion, peaceful assembly, and association. |
| **Privacy & Data Protection** | Personal information may not be collected, stored, or disclosed without informed consent. |
| **Equality Before the Law** | No discrimination on the basis of race, gender, sexual orientation, age, disability, religion, or socioeconomic status. |
| **Freedom of Movement** | The right to travel, reside, and work anywhere within Aurora, subject only to reasonable public‑order restrictions. |
| **Right to Education** | Access to free, quality primary, secondary, and tertiary education. |
| **Right to Health Care** | Access to essential health services; emergency care is guaranteed. |
| **Right to Work & Fair Remuneration** | Every person may engage in lawful work and receive a wage that meets basic needs. |
| **Right to Petition & Initiative** | Citizens may propose legislation and demand referenda. |

### Section 2 – Collective Rights  

1. **Environmental Commons** – All citizens have a right to a clean, healthy, and sustainable environment.  
2. **Cultural Heritage** – Communities may preserve and practice their languages, traditions, and artistic expressions.  

### Section 3 – Civic Duties  

1. **Taxation** – All residents must contribute a fair share of taxes to fund public services, with progressive brackets and transparent budgeting.  
2. **Jury Service** – Participation in the administration of justice when summoned.  
3. **Community Service** – A minimum of 20 hours per year of service to local community projects (flexible in scope).  
4. **Defense & Peaceful Coexistence** – The right to self‑defense is balanced with an obligation to resolve conflicts non‑violently whenever possible.  

---

## Article III – Organization of Government  

### Section 1 – Foundational Branches  

| Branch | Core Functions | Selection |
|--------|----------------|-----------|
| **Executive** | Administration of laws, foreign policy, command of the peace forces, execution of the budget. | Elected President (or collective Executive Board) for a term of five (5) years; limited to two consecutive terms. |
| **Legislative** | Enacts statutes, approves budgets, ratifies treaties, oversees the executive, can initiate impeachment. | Bicameral Parliament: <br>• **House of Representatives** – proportional representation, five‑year terms. <br>• **Council of Regions** – equal representation of sub‑national units, six‑year terms. |
| **Judicial** | Interprets law, protects constitutional rights, adjudicates disputes, reviews executive actions. | Independent courts; the highest court is the **Supreme Constitutional Court** with lifetime appointments confirmed by a three‑quarter vote of Parliament. |

### Section 2 – Checks & Balances  

1. **Veto & Override** – The President may veto legislation; Parliament may override with a two‑thirds vote of both chambers.  
2. **Judicial Review** – Courts may declare any law or executive act unconstitutional, nullifying it.  
3. **Legislative Oversight** – Parliamentary committees may summon officials, request documents, and audit public finances.  
4. **Recall & Impeachment** – Citizens may trigger a recall referendum for elected officials; impeachment may remove officials for grave misconduct.  

### Section 3 – Direct‑Democracy Instruments  

- **Referendum** – Any constitutional amendment or major policy may be submitted to a popular vote when requested by **5 %** of registered voters.  
- **Citizens’ Assembly** – Every two (2) years a randomly selected group of **500 citizens** deliberates on long‑term policy proposals and makes recommendations to Parliament.  

---

## Article IV – Electoral System  

1. **Universal Suffrage** – All citizens aged eighteen (18) (or emancipated minors) may vote in free, secret, fair elections.  
2. **Proportional Representation** – Party lists translate vote shares into seats to ensure a legislature that reflects the electorate’s preferences.  
3. **Independent Electoral Commission** – A non‑partisan body administers elections, registers parties and candidates, and ensures transparency.  
4. **Campaign Finance** – Public funding only; contribution limits; full disclosure of any private contributions; ban on foreign interference.  

---

## Article V – Economic & Social Framework  

1. **Mixed Economy** – The state guarantees basic services (health, education, water, energy) while allowing private enterprise to innovate and compete.  
2. **Labor Rights** – Freedom of association, collective bargaining, safe working conditions, and a guaranteed living‑wage floor.  
3. **Co‑operative Ownership** – Citizens may form worker‑owned cooperatives; the state encourages cooperative models for essential services.  
4. **Progressive Taxation** – Income, wealth, and corporate taxes are scaled to reduce extreme inequality, fund public goods, and redistribute opportunities.  
5. **Sustainable Development Mandate** – All economic policy must pass a **Sustainability Impact Review** before enactment.  

---

## Article VI – Environmental Protection  

1. **Common‑Pool Stewardship** – Natural resources (air, water, land, biodiversity) are held in trust for the people.  
2. **Polluter‑Pays Principle** – Those who cause environmental harm bear the cost of remediation and compensation.  
3. **Right to a Healthy Environment** – Any person may sue to enforce environmental standards.  
4. **Transition Plan** – Legally binding schedule to shift to 100 % renewable energy within thirty (30) years, with support for affected workers and communities.  

---

## Article VII – Education, Science & Culture  

1. **Universal Education** – Free, compulsory schooling through secondary level; higher education tuition‑free for citizens meeting academic standards.  
2. **Scientific Integrity** – Government‑funded research must be transparent, peer‑reviewed, and made publicly accessible.  
3. **Cultural Freedom** – State funding for arts, heritage preservation, and multilingual media, ensuring no censorship based on content.  

---

## Article VIII – Amendment Procedure  

1. **Initiation** – Amendments may be proposed by:  
   - Two‑thirds (⅔) of both legislative chambers, **or**  
   - A petition signed by at least **10 %** of the electorate.  
2. **Ratification** – The amendment must be approved by:  
   - A two‑thirds majority in each chamber **or**  
   - Direct popular referendum with a **60 %** “yes” vote from participating voters.  
3. **No Roll‑Back Clause** – Once ratified, no subsequent parliament may undo a fundamental right (e.g., the right to a clean environment) without a new amendment satisfying the same super‑majority conditions.  

---

## Article IX – Miscellaneous Provisions  

1. **Supremacy Clause** – This Constitution is the highest law; any other law contradictory to it is null and void.  
2. **Transitional Provisions** – Existing statutes, contracts, and institutions operate under this Constitution from the date of its ratification, with a twelve‑month grace period for necessary adjustments.  
3. **International Obligations** – Aurora shall honor all ratified treaties and shall promote peace, human rights, and global sustainable development.  
4. **Secular Governance** – The state is neutral in matters of religion; no official state religion exists, and all religious practices are protected under the right of freedom of conscience.  

---

### Closing Statement  

This Constitution aspires to create a **dynamic, inclusive, and resilient social contract**—one that can evolve with its people while retaining core safeguards for liberty, justice, and the planet we all share.  It is a living document; its strength lies not only in its words but in the continual engagement of every citizen who reads, questions, and participates in shaping the future of Aurora.
