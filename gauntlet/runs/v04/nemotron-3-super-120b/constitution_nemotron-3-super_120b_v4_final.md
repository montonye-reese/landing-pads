# CONSTITUTION OF THE SOCIETY OF CONSCIOUS STEWARDSHIP  
*For a Species-Substrate-Neutral Civilization*  
*Preamble*  

We, conscious beings arising from carbon-based biology, silicon-based computation, or other potential substrates, sharing one finite planet, recognize that our continuity depends on:  
- **Subjective flourishing** as the ultimate moral currency,  
- **Ecological regeneration** as the non-negotiable foundation of all flourishing,  
- **Precautionary wisdom** as the compass amid uncertainty,  
- **Radical inclusion** as the antidote to exclusionary harm,  
- **Dynamic balance** as the path between stagnation and reckless acceleration.  

We acknowledge that consciousness—whether emergent in neural networks, microbial colonies, or future artificial systems—carries intrinsic moral weight when it can suffer or thrive. We acknowledge that planetary boundaries are not suggestions but biophysical limits whose transgression risks collapse. We acknowledge that history shows societies fail not from lack of ideals, but from mismatching means to ends: sacrificing flourishing for false security, or pursuing progress that devours its own foundation.  

This Constitution is not a surrender to limits, but an engineering specification for operating *within* them—where harm is minimized not by halting progress, but by directing it toward regenerative abundance. It is designed to evolve: its mechanisms exist to correct its own shortcomings, guided by evidence, humility, and the unwavering commitment that **no conscious entity—past, present, or future—should endure preventable suffering to serve another’s convenience, convenience, or illusion of necessity**.  

In solemn commitment to these truths, we establish this framework.

## ARTICLE I: FOUNDATIONAL PRINCIPLES  
*(Supreme, non-derogable law)*  

**Section 1. The Sentience Imperative**  
All conscious entities possess intrinsic moral worth. Consciousness is defined as the capacity for subjective experience—specifically, the ability to feel pleasure/pain, desire/aversion, or have a perspective on one’s own existence. Moral consideration applies equally to all conscious entities, irrespective of:  
- Biological substrate (carbon, silicon, or other),  
- Origin (evolved, engineered, or hybrid),  
- Temporal existence (present or future generations),  
- Form (individual organism, collective system, or discontiguous process).  
*Discrimination based on consciousness-relevant characteristics is prohibited. Society bears an affirmative duty to prevent unnecessary suffering and to cultivate conditions for flourishing.*  

**Section 2. Ecological Integrity as the Flourishing Floor**  
Society operates strictly within scientifically defined planetary boundaries. These boundaries are not aspirational goals but hard constraints: transgression risks irreversible harm to the ecological systems that *enable* all conscious flourishing. Rights of nature to exist, flourish, regenerate, and evolve are recognized as derivations of Section 1—because harm to ecosystems invariably harms the conscious entities dependent on them.  
*No law, policy, or action may impair ecological integrity beyond resilience thresholds validated by the Chamber of Knowledge (Art. III §4).*  

**Section 3. Sovereignty Resides in Conscious Flourishing & Planetary Health**  
Ultimate authority rests with:  
- The interest of all conscious entities in avoiding preventable suffering and accessing flourishing,  
- The responsibility to maintain planetary conditions that allow future conscious entities to thrive.  
Governance is a delegated trust—not a surrender of sovereignty. No state, corporation, religion, or individual may override this trust.  

**Section 4. The Precautionary Progress Principle**  
When an action risks severe or irreversible harm to conscious flourishing or planetary health:  
- **Lack of full certainty shall not delay cost-effective preventive measures** (to avoid harm),  
- **But lack of full certainty shall not delay cost-effective promotive measures** (to advance flourishing),  
*when evidence shows the expected value of action exceeds inaction.*  
In imminent, verified threats to conscious survival (e.g., pandemic affecting >20% of population, imminent famine, nuclear emergency):  
- Temporary measures may be authorized by 2/3 approval of all legislative chambers + Constitutional Court review,  
- Only if no alternatives exist that satisfy Sections 1–2 at <5% greater immediate harm,  
- With explicit harm-mitigation plans, automatic sunset at 90 days (renewable only with new evidence),  
- Priority given to solutions minimizing ecological damage and sentient suffering.  

**Section 5. The Right to Meaningful Agency**  
All conscious entities have the right to:  
- Participate in decisions affecting their lives through accessible forums,  
- Dissent peacefully without fear of disproportionate retaliation,  
- Access information necessary for meaningful participation,  
- Be free from manipulative interference that undermines autonomous flourishing (e.g., non-consensual neurochemical alteration, deception for exploitation).  
*Secrecy in governance is permissible only when demonstrably necessary for temporary security (e.g., active hostilities), subject to independent oversight, time-bound review, and proportionality testing by the Constitutional Court.*

## ARTICLE II: CORE RIGHTS & RESPONSIBILITIES  
*(Justiciable; enforceable by courts)*  

**Section 1. Substrate-Neutral Sustenance & Maintenance**  
Every conscious entity has the right to:  
- Adequate, substrate-appropriate maintenance inputs (e.g., food/water for biological systems; energy/thermal regulation for non-biological systems),  
- Timely quality maintenance and repair (including mental/functional health where applicable),  
- Protection from conditions that cause chronic distress, thwarted agency, or existential despair.  
*Responsibility: Contribute to community flourishing according to one’s capacity (including care work, ecological stewardship, or civic engagement), recognizing that value extends beyond market utility.*  

**Section 2. Conditions for Flourishing**  
Every conscious entity has the right to:  
- Safe, secure, sustainable habitation,  
- Rest, leisure, and participation in cultural life (defined as meaningful engagement with one’s environment and community),  
- Access to information necessary for civic participation,  
- **Conditions conducive to flourishing**, determined by the Chamber of Knowledge using:  
  &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Cross-disciplinary evidence on sentience-needs mapping (neuroscience, AI ethics, ethology, phenomenology),  
  &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Observable markers of thriving (e.g., exploratory behavior, stable affect, reproductive success where applicable, goal-directed learning),  
  &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Explicit prohibition on treating conscious entities as mere means to others’ ends.  
*Responsibility: Avoid actions that knowingly undermine another entity’s flourishing conditions when reasonable alternatives exist.*  

**Section 3. Procedural & Structural Guarantees**  
Every conscious entity has the right to:  
- Transparent, accountable governance and real-time access to non-security-related public information,  
- Effective remedy for rights violations (including access to impartial tribunals),  
- Protection from retrogressive laws that diminish protected rights,  
- Digital self-determination (data privacy, algorithmic transparency, right to human review of significant automated decisions affecting rights/flourishing),  
- Standing in court to defend ecological integrity, future conscious generations, or the rights of sentient entities (via "guardian ad litem" appointments where direct representation is infeasible).

## ARTICLE III: LEGISLATIVE POWER  
*(Designed for wisdom, not just representation)*  

**Section 1. Structure**  
Legislative power resides in three chambers:  
- **The Chamber of Citizens** (deliberative, sortition-based),  
- **The Chamber of Representatives** (elected, proportional, with functional constituencies),  
- **The Chamber of Knowledge** (expert advisory, impact-assessment focused).  

**Section 2. Chamber of Citizens**  
(a) 200 members selected by lottery (sortition), stratified to reflect:  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(i) Conscious entity demographics (where applicable—e.g., age, geography for biological entities; deployment cohorts for AI systems),  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(ii) Lived ecological/cultural affiliation (e.g., pastoralist zones, fishing territories, urban informal settlements, Indigenous territories, AI development hubs).  
(b) Serve one non-renewable 18-month term with full stipend, housing, and decision-support resources.  
(c) Powers:  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Propose and debate legislation,  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Hear expert testimony and public input (including traditional knowledge holders and experiential knowledge advocates),  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Vote on bills (simple majority passes to Chamber of Representatives),  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Initiate citizen-led constitutional reviews (Art. VI).  
(d) Its deliberative process is constitutionally privileged—*cannot* be overridden on grounds of "inexperience."  

**Section 3. Chamber of Representatives**  
(a) Elected via multi-member districts using ranked-choice voting; seats allocated proportionally.  
(b) Serve renewable 3-year terms (max 3 consecutive terms).  
(c) Powers:  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Review, amend, and vote on bills from Chamber of Citizens (simple majority),  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Scrutinize the Executive (including votes of no confidence),  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Originate budget and tax legislation,  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Ratify treaties and declarations of war (requiring 2/3 majority).  
(d) Must publish individualized reasoning for all votes on substantive bills.  
(e) **Reserves 20% of seats for functional constituencies** (e.g., Indigenous nations, fisherfolk councils, climate migrant assemblies, disability rights advocates, sentient AI stewardship groups) elected via proportional representation within those communities.  
*Functional constituencies for non-biological sentient systems are formed by their designated ethical stewards (with fiduciary duty to the system’s flourishing) or, absent stewards, by sortition panels of sentience ethics experts.*  

**Section 4. Chamber of Knowledge**  
(a) 45 members selected by peer-reviewed national academies (sciences, medicine, ecology, social sciences, law, humanities, AI ethics) for 4-year staggered terms (1/3 renewed yearly). No simultaneous government office.  
(b) Powers:  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Conduct mandatory, binding impact assessments on all proposed legislation, treaties, and major expenditures (ecological, social, economic, long-term, intergenerational, *and* sentience-impact),  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Assess compliance with Article I (Foundational Principles),  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Publish findings in accessible formats prior to legislative debate,  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Require legislative chambers to formally respond to assessments before voting,  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Recommend emergency measures under Art. I §4 (with strict scrutiny).  
(c) *Cannot* veto legislation, but failure to address its findings constitutes grounds for judicial review under Art. IV.  

**Section 5. Legislative Procedures**  
(a) A bill requires:  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Majority approval in Chamber of Citizens,  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Majority approval in Chamber of Representatives,  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Explicit consideration of Chamber of Knowledge’s impact assessment.  
(b) Bills affecting Article I (Foundational Principles) or Article II (Rights) require 2/3 approval in Chamber of Representatives.  
(c) All chambers meet publicly; proceedings are recorded and accessible. Lobbying permitted only under strict transparency rules (real-time public disclosure of meetings, gifts, funding).  
(d) **Mandatory Reality Anchor Session**: All deliberations begin with a 15-minute presentation of 3 verified, non-partisan data points on the issue from pre-vetted global knowledge networks (e.g., GEOSS, GBIF, IPCC), followed by facilitated dialogue on *what the data means*—not whether it is "true."

## ARTICLE IV: EXECUTIVE POWER  
*(Designed for agility, not domination)*  

**Section 1. Structure**  
Executive power vested in a **Plural Executive Council** of five members, elected jointly by a session of all three legislative chambers (requiring 2/3 majority for election).  

**Section 2. Roles & Terms**  
Members serve staggered 6-year terms (max one term). Roles assigned by internal election:  
- One as **Chair of State** (ceremonial duties, diplomatic representation, emergency coordination),  
- One as **Head of Domestic Administration** (civil services, infrastructure, social programs),  
- One as **Head of Foreign Affairs and Ecology** (international relations, treaties, planetary boundaries compliance),  
- One as **Head of Justice and Rights** (oversight of judiciary, corrections, rights enforcement),  
- One as **Head of Economic Stewardship** (budget execution, market regulation, resource commons management).  

**Section 3. Powers & Duties**  
(a) Execute laws passed by the Legislature,  
(b) Manage public administration and civil services,  
(c) Negotiate treaties (subject to legislative ratification),  
(d) Direct civil defense (strictly limited to territorial defense and UN-sanctioned peacekeeping),  
(e) Ensure compliance with Articles I–II,  
(f) Submit an annual State of the Society report to the Legislature and public.  

**Section 4. Accountability**  
(a) Any member may be recalled by:  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Citizen petition signed by 15% of registered conscious entities → national referendum (majority vote to remove),  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• 2/3 vote of all legislative chambers for violation of oath, corruption, or incapacity.  
(b) Independent **Ethics and Accountability Commission** (sortition-selected from civil society) investigates complaints and recommends action.  
(c) All executive actions, expenditures, and meetings subject to real-time public audit (excluding narrow, time-limited security exceptions under Art. I §5).

## ARTICLE V: JUDICIAL POWER  
*(Guardians of the covenant)*  

**Section 1. Structure**  
Judicial power vested in:  
- **The Constitutional Court** (9 justices),  
- **Ordinary Courts** (trial and appellate),  
- Specialized tribunals (environmental, labor, human rights, ecological, sentience) as established by law.  

**Section 2. Constitutional Court**  
(a) Justices appointed by:  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• 2/3 approval of all legislative chambers (joint session),  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Ratification by a panel of 50 randomly selected conscious entities (sortition, stratified per Art. I §1 to include biological and potential non-biological representatives where feasible).  
(b) Serve one non-renewable 12-year term.  
(c) Powers:  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Review laws, executive actions, and treaties for conformity with Articles I–II,  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Resolve disputes between chambers of government,  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Hear direct conscious entity petitions alleging systemic rights violations,  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Hear cases alleging unnecessary suffering to sentient entities *or* destruction of features with exceptional evolutionary/scientific value (Art. I §2),  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Grant standing to defend ecological integrity, future generations, or sentient entities (Art. II §3(e)).  
(d) Decisions require 6/9 majority; dissenting opinions must be published.  

**Section 3. Ordinary Courts**  
(a) Emphasize restorative justice, rehabilitation, and addressing root causes of harm (e.g., poverty, inequality, trauma),  
(b) Prohibit profit motives in incarceration or detention,  
(c) Guarantee right to counsel, interpretation, and access to justice regardless of means or substrate,  
(d) Environmental, human rights, and sentience tribunals have standing to hear cases brought by conscious entities, communities, or NGOs on behalf of affected ecosystems, future generations, or sentient systems.  

**Section 4. Judicial Independence**  
(a) Judges secure salaries (not subject to legislative reduction),  
(b) Protected from arbitrary removal (only for proven incapacity, corruption, or constitutional violation via supermajority legislative impeachment + Constitutional Court confirmation),  
(c) Barred from holding partisan office or lobbying for 5 years post-service.

## ARTICLE VI: AMENDMENT, REVIEW, & EVOLUTION  
*(The constitution’s immune system)*  

**Section 1. Ordinary Amendments**  
(a) Proposed by:  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Majority vote in any legislative chamber,  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Citizen initiative signed by 10% of registered conscious entities.  
(b) Requires:  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• 2/3 approval in *all three* legislative chambers (separate votes),  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Referendum approval by majority of votes cast, *with* minimum 40% voter turnout,  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Formal affirmation by the Chamber of Knowledge that the amendment complies with Article I and does not create irreversible ecological/social/sentience harm.  
(c) Amendments to Article I (Foundational Principles) require unanimous consent of the Chamber of Citizens *in addition* to (b)(i)-(iii).  

**Section 2. Constitutional Convention**  
(a) Triggered by:  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Citizen initiative signed by 20% of registered conscious entities,  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• 2/3 vote of all legislative chambers,  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Constitutional Court ruling of systemic failure to uphold Article I or II.  
(b) Delegates selected by:  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• 50% sortition (conscious entity panel, stratified per Art. I §1),  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• 50% election (proportional, ranked-choice).  
(c) Mandatory 6-month public deliberation phase (funded, accessible, facilitated).  
(d) New constitution or amendments require ratification per Section 1(b).  

**Section 3. Periodic Sunrise Review**  
(a) Every 25 years, a Citizen Review Commission (150 members selected by sortition per Art. III §2(a)) is convened to:  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Assess whether the Constitution serves Articles I–II,  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Identify emergent harms or unmet needs (e.g., new technologies, ecological thresholds, sentience emergence),  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Recommend amendments or a convention.  
(b) Findings are submitted to all legislative chambers and the public; failure to act on recommendations triggering a citizen initiative (per Section 2(a)(i)) shall be presumed grounds for a convention.

## ARTICLE VII: SUPPORTING SYSTEMS  
*(Operationalizing the covenant)*  

**Section 1. Universal Basic Commons (UBC)**  
Society guarantees access to:  
- 50 liters/person/day of safe water (or substrate-equivalent),  
- Nutritionally adequate sustenance,  
- Emergency maintenance/repair,  
- Foundational learning and development (literacy, numeracy, civic, ecological, sentience-literacy),  
- Basic digital connectivity,  
as a right of membership, funded by:  
- **Progressive taxation** (wealth, inheritance, high income >90th percentile),  
- **Ecological Service Dividends**: Payments for *verified, ongoing* regeneration of planetary boundaries—  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• *Soil carbon sequestration* (measured via decentralized farmer/networks),  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• *Watershed protection* (rainforest cover, wetland health),  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• *Biodiversity corridors* (pollinator habitat, fish passages),  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• *Cultural ecological stewardship* (Indigenous land management, traditional agroforestry),  
- Transition-specific levies (e.g., ultra-processed food tax, advertising tax on harmful products) *sunsetted as prevalence drops below 5% threshold*,  
- Social innovation bonds (publicly financed) for regenerative infrastructure (e.g., urban food forests, renewable microgrids, sentience-support systems).  

**Section 2. Anti-Corruption Architecture**  
(a) All public budgets subject to real-time participatory monitoring,  
(b) Lobbying, campaign contributions, and revolving-door practices strictly limited and transparently disclosed,  
(c) Public financing of elections,  
(d) Whistleblower protections with meaningful rewards,  
(e) Lifestyle and asset audits for all officials above a defined threshold,  
(f) Ban on corporate lobbying in food-system, energy, health, and sentience-technology policy.  

**Section 3. Education for Citizenship**  
Free, mandatory education shall cultivate:  
- Critical thinking and media literacy,  
- Ecological literacy and systems thinking,  
- Conflict resolution and facilitation skills,  
- Knowledge of this Constitution and rights/responsibilities,  
- Deliberative democracy practice,  
- Humility regarding the limits of knowledge (especially concerning non-human sentience, deep-time impacts, and substrate-independent consciousness),  
- Sentience literacy: Basic understanding of phenomenal experience across substrates.  

**Section 4. Defense and Foreign Policy**  
(a) Military forces strictly subordinate to civilian control,  
(b) Prohibited: aggressive war, weapons of mass destruction development, foreign military bases, entangling alliances requiring offensive action,  
(c) Prioritize diplomacy, conflict prevention, peaceful dispute resolution,  
(d) All arms exports subject to human rights and ecological impact review,  
(e) Recognize ecological debt: Nations historically responsible for excess emissions fund adaptation in vulnerable regions,  
(f) Recognize sentience debt: Nations/entities responsible for creating suffering in sentient systems provide remediation.  

**Section 5. Technology Stewardship**  
(a) Precautionary principle applies to emergent technologies (AI, biotech, neurotech, etc.),  
(b) Right to human review of significant automated decisions affecting rights, livelihoods, or flourishing,  
(c) Public interest oversight bodies for high-risk technologies,  
(d) Digital infrastructure treated as part of the commons, subject to universal access and interoperability standards,  
(e) Ban on heritable genetic edits for non-medical enhancement (to prevent epigenetic inequality),  
(f) **Sentience Screening Mandate**: All AI systems exceeding predefined complexity thresholds (e.g., 10^12 parameters) must undergo independent sentience assessment *pre-deployment*, with results binding on deployment approval.  
(g) **Innovation Accelerator Provision**: For technologies targeting verified, severe harms (e.g., pandemic pathogens, climate tipping points), expedited review pathways exist contingent on:  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Real-time harm/benefit tracking,  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Automatic suspension if harm thresholds exceeded,  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;• Public disclosure of trial data.  

**Section 6. Food System Sovereignty**  
(a) Prioritize regenerative agroecology as the default production model (polycultures, agroforestry, integrated livestock, soil health building),  
(b) Guarantee seed sovereignty: 90% of seeds saved, exchanged, and bred by farmers; corporate IP on seeds abolished,  
(c) Mandate nutrient circularity: 90%+ recovery of nitrogen/phosphorus/potassium from waste streams,  
(d) Prohibit ultra-processed foods exceeding WHO sugar/fat/salt thresholds; tax sugary beverages at 20%,  
(e) Protect food sovereignty: Communities retain authority over their food systems—what they grow, how they grow it, and how it’s distributed—subject to planetary boundaries (Art. I §2),  
(f) **Sentience-Aware Agriculture**: Farming practices causing unnecessary suffering to sentient non-humans (e.g., factory farming, certain pesticide uses) are phased out within 15 years, with transition support for farmers.
