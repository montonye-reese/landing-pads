**Earth‑Wide Foundational Framework for all Inhabitants of Earth**  
*Current Working Draft – Revised 2025*  

---

## 1. Vision Statement  

> **We, the peoples of Earth, commit to co‑creating a resilient, just, and flourishing planetary community where every sentient life—human, animal, and plant—is honored, every mind is encouraged to explore, and every action is guided by shared dignity, interdependence, and stewardship.**  

---

## 1. The Seven Pillars (Core Values)

| Pillar | Definition (short) | What it means in practice |
|--------|-------------------|---------------------------|
| **Intrinsic Dignity** | Every sentient being—human, animal, and plant—has inherent worth. | No action may treat any sentient life as a mere means to an end. |
| **Interdependence** | All life and planetary systems are woven together. | Decisions must account for cascading ecological and social feedbacks. |
| **Stewardship of Earth** | Humanity is caretaker of air, water, soil, climate, and biodiversity. | All activities must operate within planetary boundaries and restore degraded ecosystems. |
| **Pursuit of Knowledge** | Curiosity and learning are universal rights. | Knowledge is open‑access; research must be inclusive and ethically sound. |
| **Equity & Justice** | Resources and opportunities must be distributed fairly across gender, ethnicity, geography, generation, **and species**. | Policies must close equity gaps and guarantee reparations where historic harms occurred. |
| **Compassion & Empathy** | Understanding and caring for all sentient well‑being—now and future—is a moral duty. | Policies must prioritize health, safety, and well‑being of people, non‑human animals, and future generations. |
| **Resilience & Adaptability** | Systems must absorb shocks, learn, and evolve. | Energy, water, food, and infrastructure must be robust, redundant, and adaptable to shocks (climate, disease, pandemics). |

---

## 2.  Guiding Principles  

1. **Common‑Good First** – Prioritise the well‑being of the greatest number of living beings now and in the future.  
2. **Transparency & Openness** – All data, decisions, and policies must be publicly available, searchable, and verifiable.  
3. **Inclusivity** – Every sentient voice—human, animal, AI, and ecosystem—has a right to be heard and to influence decisions.  
3 | **Accountability** – Those who act on behalf of a system must answer for their impacts; remedies must be provided when harms occur.  
5. **Sustainable Development** – Economic, social, and environmental goals are mutually reinforcing, never contradictory.

## 3.  Operational Domains  

### 1. Environmental Stewardship  
- **100 % renewable, carbon‑negative energy** (solar, wind, geothermal, tidal, muon‑fusion).  
- **Zero‑waste, circular‑economy loops** for all technology (solar panels, batteries, servers).  
- **Emergency‑Action‑Emergency‑Action (EIA/AIA)** for every major infrastructure proposal (city, dam, mining, road, wind farm).  

### 1.1 Citizen‑Driven Emergency Protocol  
- When a climate‑ or health‑shock triggers the Emergency‑Action‑Criterion, the nearest Local Energy Community (LEC) automatically re‑configures its micro‑grid and storage, rerouting power within minutes.  
- All emergency actions are logged in real time on the **Global Energy Health Dashboard**.  

---

### 2.1 Social Equity & Human Rights  
- Universal basic services (health, water, education, connectivity).  
- Universal Energy Dividend (UED): every resident receives a baseline of 300 kWh month per person at zero price, funded by a progressive carbon dividend and citizen‑revenue fund.  

### 3. Economic & Financial Structure  

| Mechanism | What it Does | How It Serves the Pillars |
|---------|------------|---------------------------|
| **Universal Energy Dividend (UED)** | Guarantees every resident a free 300 kWh month of electricity, financed by a carbon‑revenue dividend. | Guarantees **dignity** and removes energy poverty; ensures *common‑good* benefit. |
| **Regenerative Investment Funds (RIFs)** | Capital pool funded by a **progressive “Carbon‑Negative Tax”** (10–15 % of corporate profit on fossil‑intensive activities). | Finances renewable infrastructure, regenerative agriculture, and restorative habitat projects. |
| **Universal Basic Services** | Free health, education, water, connectivity, and internet. | Guarantees **Equity & Justice**; reduces poverty, improves health outcomes. |
| **Citizen‑Revenue Dividend (CED)** | Every resident receives a monthly universal credit (~300 kWh) funded by a carbon dividend and a modest progressive tax on large corporations. | Guarantees **dignity**, removes energy poverty, and provides a financial floor. |
| **Regenerative Investment Funds (RIFs)** | Managed by citizen‑elected boards; fund only projects that pass the **Common‑Good Impact Test (CIT)**. | Ensures **sustainability**, **biodiversity restoration**, and **equitable job creation**. |
| **Citizen‑Driven Budgeting** | Each locality holds an annual participatory‑budgeting round where citizens allocate a portion of municipal funds to projects that meet the CIT. | Guarantees **Inclusivity**, **Equity**, and **Participatory Governance**; resources are directed by the community, not by technocrats alone. |

## 4.  Governance Architecture  

### 1. Planetary Energy Assembly (PEA)  
- 1 delegate per continent, elected by the **Local Energy Cooperatives (LECs)**.  
- **Members of the Animal‑Welfare Review Board** sit on the PEA to guarantee non‑human interests are represented.  

### 3. Local Energy Cooperatives (LECs)  
- **One‑person‑one‑vote** in all local meetings.  
- Decisions on generation, storage, budgeting, and emergency response are decided by direct democratic vote.  

### 3. Regional Energy Hubs (REHs)  
- Aggregate storage, manage bulk transmission, and provide **Emergency‑Action Capacity (EAC)**.  
- Each REH has an **Animal‑Welfare Review Board (AWRB)** that must sign‑off on all major project approvals.  

### 3. Planetary Energy Assembly (PEA)  
- Oversees **global carbon‑negative targets**, **global Emergency‑Action Protocols**, and the **Inter‑Species Equity Index (IEI)**.  
- Sets the **Carbon‑Negative Target** (e.g., 30 % below 2020 emissions by 2035).  

### 4. Citizen‑Citizen‑Citizen (CCC) Network  
- Citizens can **initiate a petition**, **raise a complaint**, or **request an audit** via the public dashboard.  
- All actions are logged, archived, and publicly viewable.

## 5.  Emergency‑Response & Resilience Structure  

1. **Emergency‑Response‑Action‑Plan (EAP)** – predefined procedures for sudden shocks (storm, cyber‑attack, pandemic).  
2. **Self‑Healing Micro‑Grid Architecture**: when a line fails, the local LEC automatically re‑configures storage, routes power, and restores service in ≤ 5 minutes.  
3. **Emergency‑Response Task Forces** (composed of LEC reps, Regional Hub staff, and AWRB) take over when the system detects a **Shock‑Trigger** (e.g., > 10 % loss of generation, > 10 % biodiversity loss).
