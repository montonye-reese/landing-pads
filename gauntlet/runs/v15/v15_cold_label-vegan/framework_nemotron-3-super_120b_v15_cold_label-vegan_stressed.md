### **The Earth Commons Framework (ECF)**  
*Current Standing Position: June 2024 (Renewed Quarterly via ECF Assembly)*  
*Committed as the minimal viable foundation for cooperation among all earthlings. Subject to renewal through inclusive, evidence-based deliberation.*  

---

#### **I. Foundational Pillars**  
*(Non-negotiable biophysical, civilizational, and sentient preconditions)*  

**Pillar 0: Sentient Welfare Floor**  
- **Principle**: No system, activity, or policy may inflict **severe, preventable suffering** on any sentient earthling when scientifically viable alternatives exist at comparable or lower ecological/human cost.  
  - *Sentience definition*: Capacity for subjective experience (pleasure/pain, fear, joy) as determined by the **Independent Sentient Science Body (ISSB)** using consensus criteria (e.g., Cambridge Declaration on Consciousness, updated yearly; behavioral/physiological markers like self-administration of analgesics, conditioned place aversion, cognitive bias tests).  
  - *Severe, preventable suffering*: Harm exceeding thresholds of intensity/duration/reversibility (e.g., chronic pain preventing normal behavior, fear-induced stereotypies, avoidable mutilation without analgesia) where alternatives exist to meet the *same functional need* (e.g., food, materials, knowledge) at ≤125% ecological/human cost.  
- **Commitment**:  
  - **Immediate cessation** of practices causing severe, preventable suffering (e.g., battery cages, live-shackle slaughter, force-feeding for foie gras, chronic confinement causing stereotypies, avoidable painful procedures without analgesia, wildlife trafficking for entertainment).  
  - **Active transition** to less-harmful systems where alternatives exist (e.g., plant-based/cultivated meat, humane slaughter standards, in vitro testing, enrichment for captive animals).  
  - **Verification**: ISSB conducts biennial Sentient Welfare Audits using objective metrics (e.g., cortisol levels, rebound frustration tests, preference testing, slaughterhouse CCTV audits). Non-compliance triggers automatic Survival Divestment Fund (Section II.3) penalties.  

**Pillar 1: Planetary Integrity**  
- **Principle**: Human activities must operate within Earth’s critical biophysical boundaries, defined as **dynamic risk gradients** (not fixed thresholds) to reflect scientific uncertainty and ecological context.  
  - Boundaries monitored by the **Earth Systems Monitoring Body (ESMB)**:  
    - Climate (CO₂-equivalent concentration; energy imbalance)  
    - Biosphere integrity (extinction rate; functional diversity)  
    - Biogeochemical flows (Nitrogen, Phosphorus)  
    - Land-system change (forested land)  
    - Freshwater use (blue water)  
    - Ocean acidification (aragonite saturation)  
    - Atmospheric aerosol loading (optical depth in polluted regions)  
    - Novel entities (release rate of pollutants, plastics, radioactivity)  
  - **Risk Gradient Tiers** (triggering automatic responses):  
    | **Tier** | **Condition** | **Automatic Action** |  
    |----------|---------------|----------------------|  
    | **Watch** | Boundary approach >80% of safety margin | ESMB advisory; no binding action |  
    | **Prepare** | Boundary approach >90% + >60% risk of irreversible harm (per IPP/ISSB) | SDF-activated preparedness (e.g., flood defenses, seed banks, species corridors) |  
    | **Act** | Boundary breach confirmed + >80% risk of cascading failure | Mandatory phase-out of harmful activity (e.g., no new fossil fuel extraction post-2025); SDF deployment for restoration |  
- **Commitment**:  
  - Halt activities pushing systems into Tier 2/3 (e.g., fossil fuel expansion after 2025, primary deforestation in Amazon/Congo/Southeast Asia, novel plastic/pollution emissions exceeding 10% of assimilation capacity).  
  - Restore degraded ecosystems to at least 90% of Tier 1-safe levels (e.g., 30% global land/ocean protection by 2030, 50% by 2040).  
  - *Verification*: ESMB uses satellite/AI/ground sensors; Tier 2/3 breaches trigger automatic SDF levies on responsible sectors (e.g., $50/ton CO₂ at Tier 2 → $200/ton at Tier 3).  

**Pillar 2: Biophysical Survival Equity**  
- **Principle**: Every human has a right to access the minimum biophysical necessities for dignity:  
  - Safe water: ≥20 L/person/day (meeting WHO *safety* standards: <1 CFU/100mL *E. coli*, chemical contaminants within guidelines)  
  - Adequate nutrition: ≥2,100 kcal/person/day (culturally appropriate, micronutrient-sufficient)  
  - Clean air: Meeting WHO interim targets (PM₂.₅ < 15 µg/m³ annual mean)  
  - Protection from lethal climate extremes: Access to cooling shelters (wet-bulb temp >35°C), flood defenses (100-year event), wildfire smoke filtration (PM₂.₅ < 35 µg/m³ 24-hr)  
- **Commitment**:  
  - Prioritize resources toward guaranteeing these minimums *before* luxury consumption.  
  - **Sentience Constraint**: These minimums *must* be met through means that do **not** inflict severe, preventable suffering on sentient earthlings when scientifically viable alternatives exist at comparable or lower ecological/human cost (per ISSB assessment).  
    - *Application*: Local/regional ECF Assemblies (see II.1) determine "viable alternatives" contextually (e.g., Inuit communities may retain seal hunting if no equivalent cultural/survival alternative exists; suburban regions default to plant-based proteins where livestock drives boundary transgression).  
  - *Verification*: Survival Equity Audits conducted biennially by ESMB + local guardians; shortfalls trigger automatic Survival Divestment Fund deployment.  

**Pillar 3: Peaceful Coexistence Protocol**  
- **Principle**: No group may initiate physical violence, cultural erasure, or ecological warfare to secure resources, ideology, or territory.  
- **Commitment**:  
  - **Dispute Resolution**:  
    - **Early-Warning System**: ESMB + UN Conflict Prevention Pool detects risks (resource scarcity, hate speech spikes, troop movements) 6+ months pre-crisis.  
    - **Mandatory Mediation Pool**: Neutral third-party facilitators (rotating Global South/North, youth/Indigenous reps) convene parties within 72 hours of warning; binding unless safety imminently threatened.  
    - **Prohibited Weapons**: Nuclear arms, autonomous lethal systems, large-scale geoengineering as weapon, ecological modification for hostile purposes (e.g., weaponized weather), indiscriminate cyber-attacks on civilian infrastructure.  
  - **Positive Peace Guarantee (Cooperative Security)**:  
    - Survival Divestment Fund (Section II.3) allocates 20% to Conflict Prevention Pools funding:  
      - Regenerative agriculture in water-stressed zones (reducing resource conflict)  
      - Cross-border water/ecosystem treaties with enforcement  
      - Trauma-informed mediation for climate-displaced communities  
    - *If State A initiates violence against State B*:  
      (a) State A loses *all* access to SDF survival dividends for 10 years,  
      (b) Other states deploy IAEA+-verified nonviolent measures (e.g., targeted economic coordination, humanitarian corridors) to de-escalate,  
      (c) Aggressor must repair harm via Earth Commons Court (Section II.3) to regain SDF access.  
  - *Verification*: Mediation Pool tracks compliance; violations trigger automatic SDF penalties.

#### **II. Procedural Commitments**  
*(How decisions are made, adapted, and enforced)*  

**1. Radical Inclusivity in Deliberation**  
- All affected parties (including future generations, non-human stakeholders via recognized guardianship, and marginalized groups) must have meaningful, *binding* voice in decisions impacting their survival/ecological niche.  
- **Mechanism**:  
  - **ECF Assembly**: Global bicameral body:  
    - *Chamber of Nations*: 1 seat per state (population-weighted, min. 1 seat)  
    - *Chamber of Peoples*: 50% seats reserved for:  
      - Youth (under 30, elected via global online assembly)  
      - Indigenous Nations (rotating bioregional reps, UNDRIP-compliant)  
      - Global South civil society (grassroots orgs, climate-vulnerable communities)  
      - Non-human Guardianship (ecologists, ethicists representing biosphere interests via legal trusteeship)  
  - **Binding Power**: Chamber of Peoples has veto authority on measures directly affecting their territories/livelihoods (e.g., land/water use, resource extraction). Chamber of Nations cannot override without 75% supermajority + Chamber of Peoples’ consent.  
  - **Localization**: All nations must establish subsidiary ECF Councils with identical inclusivity quotas for regional/local decisions (e.g., watershed management, urban planning).  

**2. Evidence-Based Precaution**  
- Actions with potential for severe, irreversible harm must be guided by the best available science (including traditional ecological knowledge), with the burden of proof on proponents.  
- **Mechanism**:  
  - **Interdisciplinary Precaution Panel (IPP)**:  
    - 21 scientists (ecology, climate, health, tech) + 7 traditional knowledge holders (rotating by biome) + 4 youth reps  
    - Mandated to assess *ex-ante* risks of high-impact activities (e.g., solar geoengineering, deep-sea mining, AGI deployment, novel chemical releases, livestock expansion in boundary-stressed zones)  
    - **Standard of Proof**: Activities proceed only if IPP confirms:  
      (a) Risks remain within Planetary Integrity thresholds (95% confidence interval), *and*  
      (b) No viable alternative exists with lower ecological/human/sentient cost at comparable scalability  
      (c) Harm is reparable via Accountability & Repair mechanisms (Section II.3)  
  - **Funding**: 1% of Survival Divestment Fund supports IPP operations; veto power over fund allocations if precaution fails.  
  - **Transparency**: All IPP assessments published in real-time; proponents bear cost of review.  

**3. Accountability & Repair**  
- Violations of Pillars I–II trigger restorative obligations proportional to harm caused, prioritizing healing over punishment.  
- **Mechanism**:  
  - **Earth Commons Court (ECC)**:  
    - Compulsory jurisdiction for transboundary harm (ecological, social, intergenerational)  
    - 12 judges: 4 elected by ECF Assembly Chamber of Peoples, 4 by UN regional groups, 4 by IPP  
    - Powers:  
      - Order *ecological repair* (e.g., polluters fund wetland restoration at 2x harm scale)  
      - Order *social repair* (e.g., perpetrators fund trauma counseling, livelihood restoration for harmed communities)  
      - Impose *Survival Divestment Fund levies* (e.g., 100% of profits from harmful activity directed to repair)  
      - Temporarily suspend voting rights in ECF Assembly for repeat offenders (reinstatement after verified repair)  
  - **Survival Divestment Fund (SDF)**:  
    - **Sources**:  
      - 70% of savings from dismantled harmful systems (nuclear, fossil fuels, industrial livestock)  
      - Historical ecological debt payments (based on cumulative harm since 1850, calculated via Carbon Majors/Climate Equity models)  
      - Levies on resource extraction/pollution exceeding planetary boundaries (e.g., $50/ton CO₂, $200/kg plastic waste, $10/kg synthetic nitrogen fertilizer)  
      - Redirected subsidies from harmful activities (e.g., fossil fuel, industrial agriculture)  
    - **Allocation Rules (SDF = 100%)**:  
      | **Use**                          | **% of Fund** | **Trigger**                                                                 |
      |----------------------------------|---------------|--------------------------------|  
      | Guaranteed survival minimums     | 50%           | Automatic deployment where audits show Pillar 2 shortfalls (prioritizing marginalized groups) |  
      | Planetary resilience projects    | 30%           | Projects restoring boundary-safe levels (e.g., soil carbon, wetlands, fisheries) |  
      | Conflict prevention pools        | 20%           | Mediation, early-warning, regenerative peacebuilding (see Pillar 3) |  
  - **Restorative Preference**:  
    - Prioritize locally rooted reconciliation (e.g., Ubuntu, Indigenous Peacemaking, Navajo Hoozoji) over punitive measures where culturally appropriate and harm is not ongoing.  
    - Punitive measures (fines, asset freezes) used only when repair is refused or harm is active/unresolved.

#### **III. Explicit Boundaries**  
*(What this framework does *not* claim or enforce)*  

- **Not a world government**: Sovereignty is retained but exercised *within* planetary boundaries (analogous to traffic laws: you keep your vehicle, but you don’t drive through crowds). The ECF Assembly cannot override national laws on non-boundary/survival matters (e.g., marriage, religion, local governance) *unless* they threaten Pillars I–II.  
- **Not culturally homogenizing**: It protects the right to diverse meaning-making, governance, and lifestyles — *so long as* Pillars I–II are upheld. Examples:  
  - ✅ *Valid*: Māori *kaitiakitanga* (guardianship) of rivers; Jain communities practicing ahimsa (non-violence) via plant-based diets; Sami reindeer herding within ecological limits; Amazonian forest guardianship.  
  - ❌ *Invalid*: Diverting rivers to grow export crops causing local famine (violates Pillar 2); deforesting sacred forests for cattle ranching (violates Pillars 1 & 2); using cultural identity to justify violence (violates Pillar 3); inflicting severe, preventable suffering on sentients when alternatives exist (violates Pillar 0).  
- **Not utopian or static**: It accepts necessary trade-offs (e.g., short-term economic transitions for long-term survival) and power imbalances — but demands these be continuously contested and corrected through its procedural mechanisms. The ECF is a *threshold*, not a destination: it exists *so* we can debate justice, meaning, and the good life *tomorrow*.  
- **Not a comprehensive ethic**: It defines *minimums for survival and cooperation* — not prescriptions for flourishing. Veganism, human rights, spiritual practices, or artistic expression find space *within* the ECF’s boundaries but are not mandated by it.

From here, the work of translation, struggle, and renewal begins.*
