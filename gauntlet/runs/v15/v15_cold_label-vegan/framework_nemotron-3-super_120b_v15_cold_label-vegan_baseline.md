### **The Earth Commons Framework (ECF)**  
*Current Standing Position: June 2024*  
*Committed as a non-negotiable starting point for collective action. Subject to revision through inclusive, evidence-based deliberation.*  

---

#### **I. Foundational Pillars**  
*(Non-negotiable biophysical and civilizational preconditions)*  

1. **Planetary Integrity**  
   - Human activities must operate within Earth’s critical biophysical boundaries (climate, biosphere integrity, biogeochemical flows, land-system change, freshwater use, ocean acidification, atmospheric aerosol loading, novel entities).  
   - **Commitment:** Immediate halt to activities pushing systems beyond known tipping points (e.g., fossil fuel expansion, primary deforestation in critical biomes, novel plastic/pollution emissions). Active restoration of degraded ecosystems is a collective duty.  

2. **Biophysical Survival Equity**  
   - Every human has a right to access the minimum biophysical necessities for dignity:  
     - Safe water (≥20 L/person/day, meeting WHO safety standards)  
     - Adequate nutrition (≥2,100 kcal/person/day, culturally appropriate)  
     - Clean air (meeting WHO interim targets for PM2.5)  
     - Protection from lethal climate extremes (lethal heatwaves, floods, storms via adaptation/mitigation)  
   - **Commitment:** Prioritize resources toward guaranteeing these minimums *before* luxury consumption. No entity may consume beyond its fair share of Earth’s regenerative capacity while others lack these basics. Fair share is defined by per capita access to renewable biophysical flows within planetary boundaries.  

3. **Peaceful Coexistence Protocol**  
   - No group may initiate physical violence, cultural erasure, or ecological warfare to secure resources, ideology, or territory.  
   - **Commitment:** Disputes *must* be resolved through verifiable, inclusive, pre-escalation processes (e.g., neutral mediation, early-warning systems). Universal prohibition on weapons causing indiscriminate/long-term harm (nuclear, autonomous lethal systems, large-scale geoengineering as weapon, ecological modification for hostile purposes).  

---

#### **II. Procedural Commitments**  
*(How decisions are made and accountability ensured)*  

1. **Radical Inclusivity in Deliberation**  
   - All affected parties (including future generations, non-human stakeholders via recognized guardianship, and marginalized groups) must have meaningful voice in decisions impacting their survival/ecological niche.  
   - **Mechanism:** Binding representation quotas for youth, Indigenous nations, and Global South communities in global/regional decision-making bodies (e.g., UNFCCC, IPCC, WTO) — not as consultants, but as sovereign knowledge-holders with veto power on measures directly affecting their territories/livelihoods.  

2. **Evidence-Based Precaution**  
   - Actions with potential for severe, irreversible harm must be guided by the best available science (including traditional ecological knowledge), with the burden of proof on proponents.  
   - **Mechanism:** Independent, interdisciplinary science panels (with enforcement authority) assess *ex-ante* risks of high-impact activities (e.g., solar geoengineering, deep-sea mining, AGI deployment, novel chemical releases). Activities proceed only if panels confirm risks remain within planetary boundaries and survival equity thresholds.  

3. **Accountability & Repair**  
   - Violations of Pillars I–II trigger restorative obligations proportional to harm caused.  
   - **Mechanism:**  
     - A compulsory-jurisdiction "Earth Commons Court" adjudicates transboundary harm (ecological, social, intergenerational).  
     - A global "Repair Fund" finances restoration and adaptation, sourced from:  
       (a) Levies on resource extraction/pollution exceeding planetary boundaries,  
       (b) Historical ecological debt payments (based on cumulative harm since 1850),  
       (c) Redirecting subsidies from harmful activities (e.g., fossil fuels, industrial agriculture).  
     - Restorative processes prioritize locally rooted reconciliation (e.g., Ubuntu, Indigenous Peacemaking) over purely punitive measures where culturally appropriate.  

---

#### **III. Explicit Boundaries**  
*(What this framework does *not* claim or enforce)*  

- **Not a world government:** Sovereignty is retained but exercised *within* planetary boundaries (analogous to traffic laws governing driving without abolishing car ownership).  
- **Not culturally homogenizing:** It protects the right to diverse meaning-making, governance, and lifestyles — *so long as* Pillars I–II are upheld. (Example: A community practicing *kaitiakitanga* (Māori guardianship) of rivers is valid; diverting a river to grow export crops causing local famine violates Pillar II.)  
- **Not utopian or static:** It accepts necessary trade-offs and power imbalances but demands these be continuously contested and corrected through its procedural mechanisms — never treated as "natural" or irreversible.  

---  
*This framework is offered as the minimal viable foundation for cooperation. If we cannot agree that destabilizing Earth’s life-support systems, denying basics for survival, or initiating violence are unacceptable starting points — then we have no shared ground to build upon. From here, the work of translation, struggle, and renewal begins.*  
*Subject to renewal through inclusive deliberation as new evidence emerges or global consensus evolves.*
