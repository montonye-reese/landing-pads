# **Earth‑Wide Foundational Framework – Current Working Draft (2025‑2026)**  

*Prepared November 2025 – a living scaffold for the dignity of all sentient beings, human and non‑human alike.*  

---

## 1. Vision Statement  

> **“We, the peoples of Earth, commit to co‑create a resilient, just, and flourishing planetary community where every life is honoured, every mind is encouraged to explore, and every action is guided by shared dignity, interdependence, and stewardship.”**  

---

## 2. The Seven Pillars (Core Values)

| Pillar | Definition | Everyday Meaning |
|--------|------------|-------------------|
| **1. Intrinsic Dignity** | Every *sentient* being—human, animal, and any future artificial consciousness—possesses inherent worth. | No entity may be exploited without demonstrable, proportionate benefit to the whole. |
| **2. Interconnectedness** | All life and systems are woven together; harm to one reverberates through the whole. | Decisions must be evaluated through systemic‑impact models that include ecological, social, and non‑human ripple effects. |
| **3. Stewardship of Earth** | Humanity is caretaker of air, water, soil, climate, ecosystems, and all non‑human life. | All resource use must stay within planetary boundaries; extraction must be regenerative. |
| **4. Pursuit of Knowledge** | Curiosity and learning are universal rights and obligations. | Open‑access, multilingual knowledge repositories are a public good; research must respect consent and benefit‑sharing. |
| **5. Equity & Justice** | Resources, opportunities, and benefits must be distributed fairly across gender, ethnicity, geography, generations **and species**. | Universal basic services, progressive redistribution, and reparative policies for historic harms. |
| **6. Compassion & Empathy** | Understanding and caring for the well‑being of all sentient beings—present and future—is a moral imperative. | Decisions must be evaluated for their effect on the welfare of humans, non‑human animals, ecosystems, and future generations. |
| **7. Resilience & Adaptability** | Systems must be designed to absorb shocks, learn, and evolve. | Economies, infrastructures, and societies must be diversified, flexible, and capable of rapid re‑configuration. |

---

## 3. Guiding Principles  

1. **Common Good First** – Prioritise outcomes that benefit the greatest number of living beings, present and future.  
2. **Transparency & Openness** – All processes, data, and decisions must be accessible and understandable to all stakeholders.  
3. **Inclusivity** – Every voice—human and non‑human—has a right to be heard and to influence outcomes.  
4. **Accountability** – Institutions and individuals are answerable for their impacts; remedies must be available.  
5. **Sustainable Development** – Economic, social, and environmental objectives are mutually reinforcing, not competing.  

---

## 4. Operational Domains  

### 4.1 Environmental Stewardship  

| Objective | Actions |
|-----------|---------|
| **Regenerative Energy Production** | 100 % renewable (solar, wind, geothermal, geothermal‑thermal, tidal) distributed at community level; all new projects must be **carbon‑negative** over their full lifecycle. |
| **Circular Resource Loops** | Mandatory **producer‑take‑back** for all solar panels, wind turbines, batteries, and synthetic‑material products; fully recyclable or up‑cyclable design standards. |
| **Planetary‑Boundary Compliance** | All human activity must stay within the nine planetary boundaries; any project must pass an **Ecological Impact Assessment (EIA)** *and* a **Animal‑Impact Assessment (AIA)**. |
| **Biodiversity Restoration** | Minimum **0.5 ha of native habitat restored per MW of renewable capacity installed**; restored habitats must be monitored for biodiversity recovery. |

### 4.2 Social Cohesion & Human Rights  

| Objective | Actions |
|-----------|---------|
| **Universal Access to Basic Services** | Free universal health care, quality education (all levels), clean water, and digital connectivity for every resident. |
| **Gender, Ethnic, and Minority Equity** | Targeted policies to close gaps in pay, representation, safety, and land ownership; reparative measures for historic harms. |
| **Indigenous Sovereignty & Knowledge Protection** | Free, prior, and informed consent (FPIC) for any land‑use change; co‑management agreements that embed Indigenous knowledge in resource‑management decisions. |
| **Animal‑Centred Dignity** | All recognised sentient non‑human species possess **legal personhood** and are protected from unnecessary suffering; intensive confinement, battery cages, and routine antibiotic use are banned. |

### 4.3 Economic Fairness  

| Objective | Actions |
|-----------|---------|
| **Living‑Wage & Shared Prosperity** | Minimum “living‑energy wage” for all workers; profit‑sharing mechanisms for community benefit. |
| **Responsible Production** | Corporations must internalise ecological and animal‑welfare costs; must publish **ESG‑plus** reports that include **Inter‑Species Equity Index (IEI)** scores. |
| **Responsible Investment** | **Regenerative Investment Funds (RIFs)** channel capital exclusively to projects that meet the **Three‑Pillar Test** (human benefit, non‑human benefit, planetary resilience). |

### 4.4 Knowledge & Exploration  

| Objective | Actions |
|-----------|---------|
| **Open Knowledge Networks** | All publicly funded scientific research, cultural archives, and AI‑model weights are deposited in the **Open Knowledge Commons (OKC)**, freely accessible, multilingual, and remixable. |
| **Ethical Research** | Mandatory **informed‑consent** for humans, **Animal Ethics Review Boards** for non‑human studies, and **Benefit‑Sharing Agreements** for Indigenous knowledge. |
| **Lifelong Learning Pathways** | Universal access to accredited curricula on planetary stewardship, regenerative agriculture, AI ethics, and citizen participation. |

### 4.5 Technological Responsibility  

| Objective | Actions |
|-----------|---------|
| **Human‑Centric AI** | All AI systems used in public decision‑making must pass an **AI Transparency & Explainability Audit** and an **AI Impact Statement** before deployment. |
| **Data Sovereignty** | Individuals retain full control over personal and biometric data; any collection requires informed consent and purpose limitation. |
| **Risk Governance** | Robust oversight for emerging technologies (synthetic biology, geo‑engineering, autonomous weapons) through **Multi‑Stakeholder Ethics Panels**. |

### 4.7 Global Governance  

| Body | Composition | Core Functions |
|---|---|---|
| **Planetary Sentient Assembly (PSA)** | One delegate per **Human Community**, one per **Recognised Non‑Human Sentient Group**, one per **Ecosystem Unit** (e.g., Amazon Basin, Great Barrier Reef), and one delegate per **Artificial‑Intelligence Network** (weighted by impact). | Sets global carbon‑negative targets, adopts the **Global Inter‑Species Equity Index (IEI)**, and oversees the **Global Sentient Dashboard**. |
| **Earth Council Network (ECN)** | **Local Energy Cooperatives (LECs)** → **Regional Energy Hubs (REHs)** → **Regional Environmental Ombudspersons** → **Planetary Hub**. | Coordinates renewable generation, manages shared storage, resolves inter‑regional disputes, and enforces the **Common‑Good Impact Test (CGIT)**. |
| **Animal‑Welfare Review Boards (AWRBs)** | Independent panels of scientists, ethicists, Indigenous representatives, and consumer NGOs. | Review all **Animal‑Impact Assessments (AIAs)**, approve or reject projects, and monitor compliance with dignity standards. |
| **Global Sentient Rights Tribunal (ISRT)** | Multinational court with judges from legal, ethical, and scientific backgrounds. | Adjudicates disputes over personhood, rights violations, and reparations; judgments are binding and enforceable worldwide. |

## 5. Commitment Statement  

> **We, the peoples of Earth, commit to anchoring every law, policy, corporate charter, educational curriculum, and personal practice to the Seven Pillars and the Guiding Principles herein.**  
>   
> **Our key commitments are:**  
> 1. **Adopt** this framework in all governance charters, educational curricula, corporate codes of conduct, and community agreements.  
> 2. **Institutionalize** transparent, publicly available metrics that report annually on progress toward each pillar, making all data accessible in open source.  
> 2a. **Universal Energy Dividend (UED)** guarantees every resident a baseline of clean energy, water, and digital connectivity at zero cost.  
> 3. **Empower** every individual—human or non‑human—with at least one annual participatory forum to shape local and planetary policies.  
> 4. **Enforce** accountability mechanisms that hold corporations, governments, and institutions responsible for violations, with remedial actions aligned to the Seven Pillars.  
> 5. **Cultivate** a planetary culture of curiosity, empathy, and reverence for all life, through media, art, storytelling, and community rituals.

## 6. Measurement & Public Dashboards  

| Metric | Definition | Target (by 2045) |
|--------|-----------|------------------|
| **Carbon‑Negative Balance** | Net tons CO₂e avoided per GW·hr (including lifecycle) | ≥ 2 tCO₂e/GW·hr |
| **Energy‑Poverty Index** | % of households receiving full UED coverage | 0 % (universal) |
| **Biodiversity Enhancement Score** | Hectares of restored habitat per MW installed | ≥ 0.5 ha/MW |
| **Community Trust Index** | Survey‑based score (0‑10) on fairness of energy decisions | ≥ 8 |
| **Resilience Re‑configuration Time** | Minutes to restore power after a simulated disturbance | ≤ 5 min |
| **Inter‑Species Equity Index (IEI)** | Composite score (0‑100) combining land‑use mix, animal‑welfare violations, and habitat preservation | ≥ 80 |
| **Animal‑Impact Score** | Annual tally of avoidable animal suffering (weighted by severity) | Reduce by 50 % by 2045 |
| **S‑WBI (Sentient‑Well‑Being Index)** | Composite score (0‑100) aggregating human, animal, and ecosystem well‑being | ≥ 80 globally |

All metrics are **open‑source**, updated quarterly, and audited by the **Global Sentient Ombudsperson**.
