#### **Irrevocable Commitment**  
We affirm that the continued flourishing of *all* inhabitants of Earth—**human beings, sentient non-human beings, present and future generations**—depends on upholding three non-negotiable pillars. These pillars are derived from:  
- **Objective biophysical realities** (Earth’s finitude, interconnected systems, thermodynamic limits),  
- **Verified sentience science** (cross-species capacity for suffering/flourishing),  
- **Inescapable interdependence** (shared risks/opportunities that render isolation suicidal).  

This framework is a **living starting position for enforceable cooperation**—not a wishlist, but a *operating system* with automatic consequences for violations. It rejects all exemptions for privilege, convenience, or "necessary evil." Behind the veil of ignorance (not knowing if you’d be human or non-human, present or future, powerful or marginalized), these pillars represent the *minimum conditions* for a civilization worthy of survival.  

**Failure to uphold any pillar triggers automatic, graduated corrective measures**—enforced via market mechanisms, knowledge access, and legal accountability—not voluntary goodwill.

### **II. The Dignity Imperative**  
*All sentient beings possess inherent worth requiring the minimization of **preventable, avoidable suffering** and the maximization of conditions for meaningful agency. Agency requires: (a) freedom from coercion/manipulation, (b) access to conditions for flourishing, and (c) the **right to disengage** from imposed systems of modernity.*  
**Starting Commitments (with Enforcement Triggers)**:  

| **Commitment**                                                                 | **Automatic Trigger if Violated**                                                                 | **Pillar Upheld** |  
|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------|  
| **1. Zero Tolerance for Avoidable Sentient Suffering**: Prohibit **all** practices causing *verifiable* suffering to sentient beings (per Cambridge Declaration on Consciousness + species-specific ethology), **except only** when:<br>  - Survival of *another sentient being* is imminently at stake (e.g., life-saving medicine with no alternatives),<br>  - Suffering is strictly proportional/time-bound,<br>  - Approved by an independent sentience ethics board (50% non-human welfare scientists, 50% public citizens) with veto power.<br>**No exemptions** for culture, tradition, convenience, or economic gain. | • If violation confirmed:<br>  - *Immediate suspension of related subsidies/permits*<br>  - *Redirect funds to alternatives (e.g., precision fermentation for animal testing)*<br>  - *Referral to ICC for crimes against sentience*<br>  - *Public naming/shaming via Global Sentience Monitor* | Dignity (ends normalization of cruelty) |  
| **2. Universal Human Foundations** (non-derogable):<br>  - Clean air, water, sanitation<br>  - Sufficient, culturally appropriate nutrition<br>  - Access to quality healthcare (incl. mental health), education, and energy<br>  - Freedom from violence, oppression, torture, unjust detention<br>  - Meaningful participation in decisions affecting one’s life/livelihood/environment (especially for impacted groups: Indigenous, Afro-descendant, peasants, women, youth, PWDs, LGBTQ+)<br>  - **Wage parity + guaranteed retraining** for workers displaced by pillar-compliant transitions (e.g., fossil fuel/livestock → renewables/agroecology) | • If foundational right denied:<br>  - *Suspension of state eligibility for global climate/health funds*<br>  - *Trigger for UN Special Rapporteur investigation*<br>  - *Redirect of diverted funds to frontline mutual-aid networks* | Dignity (prevents suffering → enables agency) |

### **Operational Principles (Non-Negotiable)**  
1. **Enforceability First**: Commitments trigger **automatic, graduated responses**—not hope, diplomacy, or voluntary compliance. Power respects cost.  
2. **Context Without Compromise**: Implementation respects ecological/cultural diversity (e.g., protein transition in Sahel vs. Japan) but **never** violates pillar core intent (e.g., no CAFOs permitted anywhere, even if "culturally traditional").  
3. **Dynamic Revision**: Biennial reviews by **Global Framework Council** (50% Indigenous/local knowledge holders, 30% Global South scientists, 20% Northern scientists; gender-balanced; youth quotas). Early review triggered by:  
   - ≥2 planetary boundary transgressions (PIK assessment)  
   - UN-declared famine/conflict affecting ≥10M people  
   - Formal petition from ≥500,000 inhabitants across ≥30 nations  
4. **Accountability**: Real-time monitoring via satellite/AI + community observatories (Global Forest Watch, Ocean Health Index). Restorative justice for harms (land return, cultural revitalization, species recovery). Ecocide/intergenerational injustice prosecutable by ICC/national courts.

### **Boundary Conditions: What This Is and Is Not**  
- ✅ **IS**: A minimal, enforceable baseline for coexistence—not a cultural/spiritual/economic prescription. Creates *space* for diverse lifeways *within* planetary/sentient-boundaried limits.  
- ❌ **IS NOT**: A mandate for uniformity (e.g., water governance differs by watershed; protein sources vary by ecology/culture).  
- ❌ **IS NOT**: A replacement for Indigenous sovereignty, grassroots autonomy, or place-based wisdom—it exists to **protect and amplify** them within planetary boundaries.  
- ⚠️ **ACKNOWLEDGES**: Power asymmetries exist; upholding these pillars requires confronting entrenched interests (fossil fuel lobbies, agribusiness conglomerates, wealth extractors). Its value lies in arming inhabitants with a **self-enforcing reference point** where defection costs more than compliance.
