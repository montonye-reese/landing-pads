**Earth‑Common Framework (ECF‑2.0) – Current Operating Version**  
*Adopted 2025 – a living, legally‑binding charter for a regenerative, resilient, and equitable planetary system.*

---

## 1. Vision  

> **We commit to a thriving, resilient, and equitable planet where human flourishing and ecological integrity reinforce each other, and where every sentient life‑form—human, non‑human, and future generations—has the right to a safe, clean, and just environment.**  
> • All human and non‑human activity must stay within Earth’s planetary‑boundary limits.  
> • All people and communities shall have universal access to essential resources (energy, water, food, health, education) as a basic human right.  
> • The framework is designed to be **self‑correcting**, **institutionally transparent**, and **adaptable** so it can survive any future shock.

---

## 2. Core Principles  

| # | Principle | What it obligates us to do |
|---|-----------|---------------------------|
| 1 | **Interdependence** | Recognise that all life—human, non‑human, and future generations—is tightly coupled; every policy must consider ripple effects across ecosystems, societies, and time. |
| 2 | **Regenerative Justice** | All resource extraction, production, and consumption must **restore** more than they take; damaged ecosystems must be revived to at least pre‑disturbance health. |
| 3 | **Scientific Integrity** | Policies must be based on peer‑reviewed, openly‑auditable evidence; data, models, and impact assessments must be publicly available and peer‑reviewed. |
| 4 | **Adaptive Resilience** | Systems must be designed with redundancy, diversity, and continuous learning loops to absorb shocks and evolve. |
| 5 | **Human Dignity & Inclusion** | Every person, regardless of identity, geography, or ability, has the right to basic services, equitable opportunity, and meaningful participation. |
| 6 | **Stewardship of Commons** | Atmosphere, oceans, biodiversity, digital data, and other shared resources are finite; they must be managed as **common‑property trusts** with transparent, participatory governance. |
| 7 | **Ethical Technological Use** | Technologies must serve life‑affirming goals, avoid systemic risk, and be subject to an **Ethical Impact Test (EIT)** before deployment. |
| 7a| **Rights of Non‑Human Sentience** | Ecosystems, species, and individual non‑human organisms possess intrinsic rights that cannot be overridden by any economic or technological pursuit. |
| 8 | **Temporal Equity** | Present generations hold a fiduciary duty to preserve planetary health for all future generations; any policy must pass a **Long‑Term Impact Assessment** (≥ 50‑year horizon). |
| 9 | **Economic Power Restriction** | No private entity may own > 30 % of strategic natural resources (fossil fuels, critical minerals, water basins) without mandatory co‑ownership by community trusts. |

## 4. Operational Pillars  

| Pillar | Core Function | Key Implementation Mechanisms |
|--------|--------------|------------------------------|
| **Ecological Restoration** | Return degraded lands, waters, and species to net‑positive health. | • Mandatory **Living‑Systems Impact Test (LSIT)**; projects that fail lose all permits.<br>• Restoration credits (e.g., reclaimed mines, pumped‑hydro hubs) fund biodiversity offsets. |
| **Circular Economy** | Keep materials in use, eliminate waste, regenerate ecosystems. | • Product‑as‑a‑Service models, **design‑for‑disassembly**, **industrial symbiosis** clusters.<br>• Recycling quota: ≥ 70 % of post‑consumer waste must be re‑introduced into production. |
| **Universal Basic Well‑Being (UBW)** | Guarantee a baseline of energy, water, health, education, and connectivity as a right, not a privilege. | • **Universal Basic Energy (UBE)**: 1 kWh/day per person, supplied free by community micro‑grids.<br>• **Universal Basic Services (UBS)**: free health, water, and digital access for all citizens. |
| **Participatory Governance** | Decisions must be co‑created by those most affected. | • **Citizen Assemblies** at community level; **Regional Grid Trusts** with elected citizen boards.<br>• **Free, Prior and Informed Consent (FPIC)** charter grants Indigenous nations veto power over any infrastructure on their territories. |
| **Data Commons** | Share all relevant environmental, economic, and social data openly. | • **Open‑Data Platform** on a public blockchain; data are timestamped, immutable, and accessible via API.<br>• **Data‑Sovereignty Ledger** secures provenance and prevents monopolisation. |
| **Ethical Innovation** | Prioritise technologies that enhance planetary health. | • **Ethical Impact Test (EIT)**: scores ≥ 8/10 on carbon, labour, biodiversity, cultural‑impact; failure blocks public funding.<br>• **Copyleft Energy License** ensures all downstream users keep technologies open and non‑exclusive. |
| **Cultural Preservation & Creativity** | Preserve and celebrate diverse cultural practices and knowledge systems. | • **Cultural Knowledge Council** (representatives of Indigenous, diaspora, urban, and emerging epistemic traditions) must co‑sign every major infrastructure charter. |
| **Demilitarisation of Energy** | Energy infrastructure mustnot be defended by armed forces. | • **Demilitarisation Charter**: UN‑mandated oversight; any armed protection of energy assets is subject to UN Security Council review and sanctions. |

### Governing Bodies  

| Body | Composition | Authority |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| **Planetary Energy Commons Council (PECC)** | 1 delegate per nation + 1 seat per Indigenous Nation + 1 seat for Non‑Human Guardian Council + 1 seat for Future‑Generation Trust | Sets global charter, carbon caps, equity standards; ratifies “Rights‑of‑Nature” charter; appoints Ombudsperson. |
| **Regional Grid Trusts** | Mixed board: citizen representatives, union reps, scientific experts, Indigenous delegates | Own/operate transmission, manage cross‑border flows, enforce FPIC, run AI dispatch. |
| **Local Energy Co‑ops** | Member‑owners (≥ 30 % from historically marginalized groups) | Produce/consume renewable energy, hold UBE accounts, manage micro‑grid resilience. |
| **Non‑Human Guardian Council (NGHC)** | NGOs, Indigenous custodians of sacred sites, independent scientists | Issues **Living‑Systems Impact Test** vetoes; can halt any project that breaches intrinsic rights of ecosystems. |
| **Future‑Generation Trust (FGT)** | 5‑person board appointed for renewable terms, ratified by UN General Assembly | Holds legal standing to sue for planetary‑boundary breaches; can veto any decision that jeopardises future‑generation rights. |
| **International Energy Commons Court (IECC)** | Judges appointed by PECC + civil‑society panel | Adjudicates disputes; enforces sanctions, asset freezes, and revocation of commons access rights. |
| **Global Compliance Ombudsperson** | Independent auditor elected by citizen‑assembly bodies | Conducts 5‑yearly audits; issues warnings, fines, or revokes commons‑access rights. |

## 5.1. Governance Mechanisms  

| Mechanism | Function | Enforcement |
|-----------|----------|--------------|
| **Annual Compliance Audit** | Independent review of carbon accounting, biodiversity metrics, equity indices. | Findings bind the Sanctions Matrix; non‑compliance triggers automatic fines or suspension of commons‑access rights. |
| **Citizen‑Jury Veto** | Any major infrastructure project must win a binding vote of a randomly selected citizen jury (≥ 5 % of electorate). | Veto overrides all other approvals; project cannot proceed without a new risk‑mitigation redesign. |
| **FPIC Charter** | Indigenous Nations have absolute veto over any project intersecting their territories. | Veto is legally binding; violation triggers sanctions under the International Energy Commons Court. |
| **Sanctions Matrix** | Tiered penalties (Warning → Financial fine → Suspension → Revocation). | Enforced by the Global Compliance Ombudsperson; sanctions are automatically triggered by compliance audits. |
| **Sanctions Matrix** | Tiered penalties (warnings → financial penalties → revocation of commons‑access rights). | Enforced by the International Energy Commons Court; penalties can include asset freezes, trade restrictions, and loss of co‑op voting rights. |

## 7. Ethical Impact Test (EIT) – Scoring System  

All proposed technologies must meet **all** of the following criteria (each scored 0‑10).  

| Dimension | Minimum Score | What it Measures |
|-----------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Carbon Life‑Cycle** | ≥ 8 | Full‑life‑cycle GHG emissions, avoided emissions, and avoided resource extraction. |
| **Labour & Labour‑Rights** | ≥ 8 | Fair wages, safe conditions, worker representation. |
| **Biodiversity Impact** | ≥ 8 | Avoidance of habitat loss, restoration offset ratios. |
| **Cultural‑Cultural Impact** | ≥ 8 | Respect for Indigenous knowledge, cultural heritage sites, and community consent. |
| **Economic Equity** | ≥ 8 | Distribution of benefits across income groups; avoidance of monopolistic concentration. |
| **Temporal Equity** | ≥ 8 | Does the technology benefit future generations without compromising their options? |  

Projects scoring **< 8** are ineligible for any public funding or licensing.

## 8. Enforcement & Sanctions  

| Tier | Trigger | Penalty |
|------|----------|---------|
| **Tier 1 – Warning** | First minor non‑compliance (e.g., minor reporting lapse) | Verbal warning; must submit corrective plan within 30 days. |
| **Tier 2 – Financial Penalty** | Repeated non‑compliance or moderate breach of environmental/equity standards. | Fine up to **5 % of the entity’s global revenue**; funds go to the Perpetual Maintenance Fund. |
| **Tier 3 – Sanctions** | Severe or repeated violation (e.g., FPIC violation, breach of planetary‑boundary thresholds). | Revocation of **commons‑access rights** (loss of UBE eligibility, loss of co‑op board seats), asset seizure, and referral to **International Energy Commons Court** for asset seizure or trade restrictions. |
| **Perpetual Sanctions** | Systemic violation that endangers planetary boundaries. | Permanent exclusion from all commons‑governance structures; mandatory divestiture of all regulated assets. |

## 9. Perpetual Commons Maintenance Fund  

*Financed automatically* by **2 % of every energy transaction** (sale, transmission fee, storage‑service charge).  
- **Capital** invested in a diversified portfolio of green bonds, impact‑linked securities, and sovereign green‑bond funds.  
- **Returns** are the only source of funding for maintenance, restoration, and resilience projects.  
- **Withdrawal** of funds requires a **unanimous vote** of the Planetary Energy Commons Council; thus funds remain insulated from short‑term political pressures.

## 9. Implementation & Accountability  

1. **Annual Global Audit** performed by the **Global Compliance Ombudsperson** (independent, citizen‑elected).  
2. **Real‑time Compliance Dashboard** – all financial flows, carbon accounting, and impact metrics are publicly visible.  
3. **Citizen‑Ombudsman Hotline** – any citizen can file a complaint; the Ombudsman must investigate within 14 days and issue a compliance report.  
4. **Annual “Charter Review”** – every 5 years the PECC must submit a *Comprehensive Charter Review* to the International Energy Commons Court, which may amend any charter provision with a two‑thirds vote.

## 9. Synthesis – Why This Version Is Resilient  

| What the original lacked | How ECF‑2.0 fixes it |
|--------------------------|----------------------|
| No enforcement; “soft” commitments | **Binding sanctions, legal personality of the Future‑Generation Trust, and a Sanctions Matrix**. |
| Assumed universal human‑centric governance | **Explicit Rights of Nature**, **FPIC Charter**, and **Non‑Human Guardian Council** give non‑human life binding authority. |
| No quantitative thresholds with automatic corrective actions | **Metrics Dashboard** ties every target to an automatic corrective trigger. |
| Reliance on ad‑hoc governance | **Layered, multi‑scale governance** (city → regional → planetary) with clear competence boundaries. |
| Tokenistic inclusion | **Mandated quotas, co‑governance seats, and veto powers** for marginalized groups. |
| No protection against capture | **Public‑Interest Capital Mandate**, **Financial‑Capital Caps**, and **Transparent Audits** prevent capture. |
| Vulnerability to technological lock‑in | **Diversified storage hubs**, **dual‑use infrastructure**, and **Resilience‑Reserve Reserve** ensure redundancy. |
| No protection against data capture | **Data‑Sovereignty Ledger** and **Digital‑Sovereignty Charter** protect data as a commons. |
