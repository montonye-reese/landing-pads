## 1. Vision  

> *We commit to a thriving, resilient, and equitable planet where every person, every non‑human life‑form, and every future generation retain the right to clean energy, clean water, clean air, and a safe environment.  In doing so we restore the Earth’s life‑support systems and guarantee dignified, dignified‑for‑all human well‑being.*

## 4.  Core Commitment Statement (binding charter)

> **We, the Earth‑Common, commit to:**  

1. **Live within planetary boundaries**; any project must pass a *Living‑Systems Impact Test*.  
2. **Guarantee Universal Basic Energy (UBE)** – every person receives a free baseline of clean energy and water.  
2‑a. **Universal Basic Services (UBS)** guarantee free access to clean water, health, education, and connectivity.  
2. **Co‑governance** – every citizen belongs to a local co‑op, participates in assemblies, and can veto projects that affect them.  
3. **Sharewealth Share** – at least 60 % of capital for new energy assets is owned by **community co‑ops or citizen trusts**.  
2‑b. **Perpetual Maintenance Fund** – automatically locks 2 % of every energy transaction into a fund that finances restoration, resilience, and service expansion.  
2‑b. **Financial‑Capital‑Control** – any new project must be financed first through a community‑owned “public‑interest fund”; private investment may supplement but must not exceed 40 % of total financing.  
3. **Citizen‑Ombudsman & Compliance Audits** – annual audits audit every asset, any violation triggers warnings → fines → revocation of rights.  
3. **The Sanction Matrix** – fines, revocation of access rights, or revocation of the right to sell electricity.  
   *The matrix is immutable and automatically enforced by the **Sanction‑Court**.*

## 5.  Institutional Structure Overview  

1. **Planetary Earth‑Common Council (PECC)**  
   *Supreme authority, sets planetary boundaries, enforces the Living‑Systems Impact Test, and issues the Living‑Systems Impact Test scorecard.*  
   *Governance signatories:*  
   - Representative from each sovereign state  
   - 1 seat for each Indigenous nation (or tribal nation)  
   - 1 seat for the **Future‑Generation Trust** (appointed by the Future‑Generation Trust)  
   - 1 seat for the **Non‑Human Guardian Council** (appointed by UNESCO‑UNESCO‑Biological Biodiversity Network  
3. **Regional Grid Trusts** (30‑50 units worldwide)  
   - Own transmission assets, manage cross‑border energy flow, enforce **Ethical Impact Test** for all new renewable projects.  
   - Governance board includes:  
   - 45 % citizen‑elected members (≥ 50 % gender‑balanced, 15 % women/women‑non‑binary, 15 % persons with disabilities)  
   - 1 seat for Union/Workers, 2 members representing **Future‑Generation Trust**  
4. **Local Energy Co‑ops**  
   - Democratic, community‑owned, at least 30 % of members are marginalized groups (women, LGBTQ+, disabled, diaspora); co‑ops own generation assets and distribute **Universal Basic Energy (UBE)** free of charge to households.  
3. **Non‑Human Guardian Council** – appointed body of ecological scientists and Indigenous elders. Holds **veto power** over any project that would degrade a protected ecosystem.  
3. **Future‑Generation Trust (FGT)**  
   - Legally‑personified entity with authority to sue on behalf of future generations; can **veto** any project that breaches planetary boundaries.  
3. **International Legal Persons (Future‑Generation Trust & Non‑Human Guardian Council)** each have standing in the **International Court of Energy and Environment** (to be established via the PECC).

## 9.  Decision‑Making Flow (from problem → decision → implementation)

1. **Proposal Drafted** → submitted to **Regional Grid Trust**.  
3. **Living‑Systems Impact Test** (including **Biodiversity Impact**, **Carbon‑Footprint**, and **Ethical Impact** scores).  
3. **Living‑Systems Impact Test** must be passed *before* any design moves to execution.  
4. **Proposal is submitted to the Living‑Guardians for sign‑off** – *any veto by the Living Guardians overrides any other approval.*  
4. **After approval**, the project proceeds to **Generation + Storage → Generation → Distribution → Storage** loops, all linked to the **AI Dispatch Dispatcher** that balances supply with demand.  
4. **Dispatch Scheduler** reads from the **AI‑driven demand‑response scheduler**, balances supply and loads, balances energy storage, and issues dispatch signals to micro‑grids.  
   *The dispatch system automatically prioritises*:  
   * **Universal Basic Energy loads** (always served first)  
   - *then* **Public‑Utility loads** (essential services)  
   - *then* *optional* consumer loads.  

5. **All outputs are logged** into the **Data Commons** and logged onto the open‑source Data Commons Dashboard.  
6. **All dispatch decisions automatically record energy flow, storage level, and impact on local ecosystems** (via environmental sensors, sensor nets, etc.) → feeds into **Impact Dashboard**.  
7. **If any Impact Index (biodiversity gain, carbon avoided, or human‑well‑being metric) drops below threshold**, the **AI dispatch algorithm automatically shuts down the affected sub‑grid** and re‑routes power.  
7‑b. The **AI‑Dispatch Scheduler** is open‑source; any user can run a **simulation** to see impact projections.

### 6.  Ethical Impact Test (EIT) – How It Works  

**Inputs:**  

- Proposed project proposal (technical design, cost, location, resource use).  
- Full environmental‑impact assessment (including biodiversity, soil, water, carbon).  
- Community Impact Survey (citizen‑participation survey).  

**Scoring Scale (0‑10)** on each dimension, each dimension must hit ≥ 8 to pass:  

| Dimension | Max Weight | Weighting Example |
|---|---|---|
| Carbon‑life‑cycle impact | 0‑10 pts | Carbon‑footprint per unit output → 0‑50 kg CO₂e per kWh |
| Cultural‑Impact (cultural‑knowledge impact) | 0‑10 | Does the project intersect with a sacred site? |
| Labour‑Rights & Labor‑Justice | 0‑10 (labour‑rights audit) | Fair‑wage, living‑wage, union representation |
| Biodiversity Impact | +0 pts if restored habitats ≥ 2 ha/GW·yr |
| Community‑Benefit | +2 (if co‑generation agreement signed with local community) |

**Any project that fails any dimension below 8 automatically fails EIT → rejected for any funding, license, or licensing.  

All test results must be uploaded to the **Public Data Commons**, searchable by anyone.
