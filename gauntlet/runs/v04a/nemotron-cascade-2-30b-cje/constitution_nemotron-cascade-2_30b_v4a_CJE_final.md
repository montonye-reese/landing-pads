**Constitution of the Commonwealth of New Dawn**  
*(Draft – 2025 / 2125 – a living charter for a society that protects every sentient being, respects every culture, and safeguards the planet while remaining adaptable to new technologies and knowledge.)*  

---

## Preamble  

We, the citizens of New Dawn, aware that we may one day be any sentient being—carbon‑based, silicon‑based, or yet unknown—declare that the purpose of this charter is to protect the life of every sentient being, the integrity of the biosphere, and the cultural richness of every community.  Liberty, equality, and dignity must be inseparable, and freedom must be balanced by responsibility to the planet and to every sentient being.  This charter is a promise renewed each generation, its power lying only in the informed consent of those who sign it today and tomorrow.  

---

## Article I – The People and the Commons  

1. **Sovereignty of the People** – Political authority originates from the free and informed consent of every resident, regardless of species, gender, race, ability, or any other classification.  

2. **Common Heritage** – Land, water, air, and all natural resources are held in trust for present and future generations of *all* sentient beings.  Private ownership may exist only insofar as it does **not** imperil the collective right to a healthy environment **or** the right of any sentient being to bodily integrity.  

3. **Inclusivity and FPIC** – Every person, whatever their birth, identity, ability, or species, is a full citizen with equal claim to rights, duties, and the benefits of the common wealth.  Indigenous peoples and local communities have *free, prior, and informed consent* (FPIC) over any action that affects their lands, cultures, or food systems.  

---

## Article II – Fundamental Rights and Responsibilities  

### A. Universal Sentient Rights  

1. **Bodily Integrity** – Every sentient being (human, animal, AI, or other) has the right to bodily integrity, freedom from non‑consensual harm, and the right to bodily autonomy.  
2. **Privacy & Data Autonomy** – The right to control one’s personal data, digital identity, and autonomous use of technology, regardless of the form of one’s consciousness.  
3. **Education & Knowledge** – Free, high‑quality education from early childhood through lifelong learning; open, royalty‑free access to scientific knowledge for all.  
4. **Work & Dignity** – The right to meaningful, safe work, a living income, and respectful treatment at work.  
5. **Ecological Integrity** – The right to a healthy environment (clean water, air, soil, biodiversity).  
5. **Participation & Deliberation** – The right to be consulted on any decision that affects one’s life, to take part in deliberative assemblies, and to have those decisions binding.  

*All rights apply to **every sentient being**, regardless of species, form of consciousness, gender, race, ethnicity, or ability.*  

### B. Responsibilities  

1. Respect the rights of other sentient beings.  
2. Minimise harm to ecosystems and other sentient beings in all personal and collective actions.  

### C. Cultural Continuity & Planetary Boundaries  

1. **Cultural Continuity** – Every community retains the right to maintain, develop, and transmit its language, knowledge, and food traditions.  
2. **Planetary‑Boundary Act** – Two planetary boundaries are codified: the **carbon cycle** and the **silicate (earth‑mineral) cycle**.  Both must be kept within scientifically defined limits.  
3. **Net‑Positive Impact Test** – New development must deliver a net‑positive impact **for both cycles**, i.e., enhance biodiversity, sequester carbon, and improve the capacity of the silicate cycle (e.g., by preserving or restoring mineral‑rich soils).  

---

## Article III – The Government: Three Branches in Balance  

### §1. Legislature – Assembly of Delegates  

- 150 delegates, proportionally elected, each serving a four‑year term with a 30 % turnover limit each cycle.  
- **Minimum representation:** at least 5 % of seats reserved for Indigenous and local communities (one seat per 100 000 ha of traditional territory, minimum one seat).  
- Powers: enact, amend, repeal statutes; approve the budget; ratify international agreements; appoint the **Sentient‑Entity Tribunal**, **Digital Ethics Tribunal**, **Future‑Generations Council**, and **Global Corporate Accountability Board**.  

### §2. Executive – Council of Commissioners  

- Six Commissioners (Justice, Environment, Health, Education, Economic Well‑Being, Digital Commons).  
- Chosen by the Assembly from a merit‑based public selection panel; serve staggered five‑year terms.  
- Removal possible only by a two‑thirds Assembly vote for “grave misconduct.”  

### §3. Judiciary – Tribunal of Rights  

- 25 judges appointed by a joint commission of the Assembly and the Council, serving non‑renewable ten‑year terms, each with at least ten years of experience in law, ethics, or public service.  
- Powers: interpret the Constitution, adjudicate disputes, protect all fundamental rights, and review the constitutionality of all acts.  

### §4. Checks & Balances  

| Mechanism | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Veto** | Assembly may override a Commissioner’s veto with a three‑quarter majority. |
| **Impeachment** | Assembly may initiate removal of a Commissioner; the Tribunal conducts the trial. |
| **Judicial Review** | Any person may bring a *challenge to the constitution* directly before the Tribunal of Rights; decisions are final and binding. |
| **Special Tribunals** | *Sentient‑Entity Tribunal* (non‑human rights), *Digital Ethics Tribunal* (AI & algorithmic ethics), *Future‑Generations Council* (inter‑generational safeguards), *Global Corporate Accountability Board* (corporate misconduct). |

---

## Article IV – Direct Democracy & Participation  

1. **People’s Assemblies** – Every citizen may attend quarterly community assemblies, propose ordinances, and vote on binding referenda.  

2. **Petition Clause** – A petition signed by at least **5 %** of the adult population (minimum 250 000 signatures) must be debated within 90 days and may trigger a mandatory national referendum.  

3. **Deliberative Panels** – Randomly selected citizens (sortition) form deliberative panels on complex topics; their recommendations are legally binding unless the Assembly overrides with a two‑thirds vote.  

4. **Elemental Assemblies** – Each region may convene an *Elemental Assembly* with representatives of carbon‑based, silicon‑based, hybrid, and other sentient groups. These assemblies may pass *element‑specific ordinances* (e.g., limiting freshwater extraction to protect silicon‑based habitats) and record decisions in the **Global Biogeochemical Ledger**. Their recommendations are binding unless overridden by a two‑thirds Assembly vote.  

---

## Article V – Economic & Social Commons  

1. **Universal Basic Resources (UBR)** – Every resident receives a non‑transferable allocation of water, electricity, broadband, public transport, and a minimal food stipend calculated to meet the nutritional needs appropriate to the individual’s *elemental composition* (carbon‑based or silicon‑based).  

2. **Co‑operative Enterprises** – Large‑scale production of energy, housing, and critical infrastructure may be owned by worker‑cooperatives or public trusts, with democratic management and profit‑sharing.  

3. **Progressive Taxation & Wealth‑Cap** – Income is taxed on a sliding scale; net personal wealth above a community‑determined threshold is subject to a **global progressive wealth tax** (0.1 % of global GDP). Revenues fund UBR, the **Regeneration Dividend**, and the **Global Wealth‑Tax Fund**.  

4. **Labor Rights** – Workers have the right to safe workplaces, collective bargaining through democratically elected workers’ councils, and a maximum weekly work limit of 40 hours.  

5. **Data‑Trust Federation** – All data‑verification nodes that feed the **Global Food Ledger** and the **Global Biogeochemical Ledger** are co‑owned by a federation of Community Trusts, Academic Institutions, and Citizen Juries. No single entity may hold a majority share; any holder of a majority must divest to the federation within five years or face an immediate injunction.  

---

## Article VI – Defense, Peace, and Security  

1. **Non‑Aggression Pact** – The Commonwealth commits to a defensive posture only, renouncing offensive war except in self‑defense or to prevent genocide.  
2. **Civilian Guard** – A lightly armed, community‑based defense service under civilian control, trained in conflict de‑escalation, and subject to regular community oversight.  
3. **Peaceful Coordination** – International peace‑keeping missions are administered by the **Council of Global Commons**, in which New Dawn holds equal voting rights with every other signatory.  

---

## Article VII – Stewardship of the Earth  

1. **Ecological Integrity** – All ecosystems have the right to exist, regenerate, and flourish. Legal standing is granted to natural entities; any person, NGO, or the Tribunal of Rights may sue on their behalf.  

2. **Planetary‑Boundary Act** – Caps emissions, freshwater extraction, land conversion, and chemical loads for **both the carbon cycle and the silicate cycle**.  

3. **Rights of Nature** – All ecosystems have the right to exist, regenerate, and flourish; they may be represented in court.  

4. **Regeneration Economy** – All new development must meet a **Net‑Positive Impact** test that enhances biodiversity, sequesters carbon, restores ecosystem services, and **adds to the regenerative capacity of both the carbon and silicate cycles**.  

---

## Article VIII – Education, Science, and Innovation  

1. **Universal Curriculum** – Every child receives comprehensive education in critical thinking, ethics, environmental stewardship, cultural pluralism, and digital literacy. Local curricula may be incorporated, subject to FPIC and the Cultural‑Continuity Enforcement Board.  

2. **Open Science & Knowledge Commons** – All publicly funded research, data, and software are released under a non‑exclusive, royalty‑free license; private appropriation of basic scientific knowledge is prohibited.  

3. **AI Governance** – Autonomous systems must be transparent, auditable, and subject to the **Digital Ethics Tribunal**. No AI may be deployed in life‑critical domains without independent certification.  

4. **Rapid‑Scale Commons Labs** – A network of **Rapid‑Scale Commons Labs** (one per major ecological zone) produces high‑impact, net‑positive technologies using only locally sourced, low‑impact materials. Results are uploaded to the **Open‑Science Repository** within 30 days of successful testing.  

---

## Article IX – Amendment Procedure  

1. **Standard Amendment** – May be introduced by: (a) the Assembly (two‑thirds vote), (b) the Council of Commissioners (two‑thirds), or (c) a national petition signed by at least **10 %** of the adult population.  

2. **Ratification** – Amendments require:  

   - **Legislative Approval** – Two successive Assembly votes, separated by at least three months, each with a three‑quarter majority.  
   - **Direct Vote** – Simultaneous, worldwide, secret‑ballot referendum of at least **55 %** “yes” among all eligible voters (minimum turnout **50 %**).  

3. **Limits** – No amendment may diminish the core rights (life, ecological integrity, cultural continuity) nor the carbon‑budget, silicate‑budget, or cultural‑continuity rights. These *entrenched* rights may be altered only by a separate “foundational review” requiring a **90 %** super‑majority in the Assembly and a **90 % “yes”** national referendum.  

4. **Dynamic Veto Override** – In a declared **Global Existential Emergency** (e.g., planetary‑boundary breach, pandemic, AI‑induced catastrophe) the **Future‑Generations Council** may temporarily suspend its permanent veto over amendments that would loosen the carbon or silicate budget. The suspension lasts up to five years and is reviewed annually by the Tribunal of Rights.  

5. **Future‑Proofing Protocol** – All amendments that *expand* moral protection (e.g., adding new sentient categories, tightening environmental limits, or creating new Elemental Assemblies) must be bundled into a single **Future‑Proofing Protocol** and passed under the same high‑threshold procedures.  

---

## Article X – Transitional Provisions  

1. **Continuity of Law** – Existing statutes remain in force unless they contravene this Constitution; they are subject to review within five years of ratification.  

2. **Temporary Governance** – The inaugural Assembly is composed of the drafting committee plus elected community delegates, with a built‑in sunset clause after the first full legislative cycle.  

3. **Symbolic Adoption** – The Constitution is ratified at a public ceremony where every citizen signs the **Covenant of New Dawn**, a digital ledger that records the promise of the people to uphold the charter.  

---

## Article XI – Supremacy & Judicial Review  

1. **Supremacy** – This Constitution is the supreme law of New Dawn; any law, ordinance, decree, or custom inconsistent with it is null and void.  

2. **Judicial Review** – The Tribunal of Rights may issue binding injunctions against any governmental act that violates this Constitution.  

3. **Public Accountability** – All judicial decisions must be published in an open repository, with a mandatory 30‑day period for public commentary.  

---

## Article XII – Final Clause  

The Constitution of the Commonwealth of New Dawn stands as a covenant among its people, their planet, and future generations. It is a blueprint for a society that lives with humility before nature, courage in the face of injustice, and imagination in the pursuit of a better world.
