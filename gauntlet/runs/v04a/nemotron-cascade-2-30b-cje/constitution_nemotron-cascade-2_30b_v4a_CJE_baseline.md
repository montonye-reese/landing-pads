**Constitution of the Commonwealth of New Dawn**  
*(Current Draft – 2025)*  

---

### Preamble  

> “We, the citizens of New Dawn, in recognition of the immeasurable worth of every human being, the boundless creativity of our species, and the sacredness of the biosphere that sustains us, solemnly bind ourselves to the following principles.  We pledge to build a community where liberty, equality, and dignity are inseparable, where the rule of law protects both the individual and the whole, and where freedom thrives in harmony with the Earth.  We recognize that this charter is a living promise, to be renewed by each generation, and that its power lies only in the consent of those who sign it today and tomorrow.”  

---

## Article I – The People and the Commons  

1. **Sovereignty of the People** – Political authority originates from the free and informed consent of every resident.  Sovereignty is exercised directly through referenda, assemblies, and indirectly through elected bodies.  

2. **Common Heritage** – Land, water, air, and all natural resources are held in trust for present and future generations.  Private ownership may exist only insofar as it does not imperil the collective right to a healthy environment.  

3. **Inclusivity** – Every person, regardless of birth, gender, sexual orientation, ability, race, creed, or digital identity, is a full citizen with equal claim to rights, duties, and the benefits of the common wealth.  

---

## Article II – Fundamental Rights and Responsibilities  

| **Right** | **Description** | **Responsible Conduct** |
|-----------|-----------------|--------------------------|
| **Life, Body & Health** | The right to bodily integrity, safe living conditions, and access to adequate health care. | Do not deliberately endanger another’s life. |
| **Freedom of Thought, Expression & Association** | The right to think, speak, create, assemble, and form groups, subject only to non‑violent harm standards. | Respect others’ equal right to free speech; prohibit hate that seeks to erase or dehumanize. |
| **Education & Knowledge** | Free, high‑quality education from early childhood through lifelong learning; open access to scientific knowledge. | Contribute to the public good of knowledge—share, critique, and improve. |
| **Work & Material Dignity** | The right to meaningful work that respects human dignity, fair wages, safe conditions, and a living income. | Participate honestly in the economic life of the community; respect the rights of co‑workers. |
| **Privacy & Digital Autonomy** | The right to control one’s personal data, digital identity, and autonomous use of technology. | Use technology responsibly, safeguard collective security, and avoid unwarranted surveillance. |
| **Ecological Integrity** | The right to a healthy environment, including clean water, air, soil, and biodiversity. | Minimize ecological footprints; restore degraded ecosystems. |
| **Participation & Deliberation** | The right to be consulted on matters that affect one’s life and to contribute to collective decision‑making. | Engage civilly, inform yourself, and respect the outcomes of legitimate votes. |

---

## Article III – The Government: Three Branches in Balance  

### §1. Legislature – The Assembly of Delegates  
* **Composition:** 150 delegates, proportionally elected from each region, each serving a four‑year term with a 30 % turnover limit each cycle.  
* **Powers:** Enact, amend, and repeal statutes; approve the national budget; ratify international agreements.  

### §2. Executive – The Council of Commissioners  
* **Composition:** Six Commissioners (Justice, Environment, Health, Education, Economic Well‑Being, Digital Commons) chosen by the Assembly from the top‑ranked nominees of a public, merit‑based selection panel.  They serve staggered five‑year terms and may be removed only by a two‑thirds Assembly vote for “grave misconduct”.  
* **Powers:** Implement statutes, administer the commons, negotiate with other polities, and direct the civil service.  

### §3. Judiciary – The Tribunal of Rights  
* **Composition:** 25 judges appointed by a joint commission of the Assembly and the Council, serving non‑renewable ten‑year terms after a rigorous vetting process.  Judges must have at least ten years of experience in law, ethics, or public service.  
* **Powers:** Interpret the constitution, adjudicate disputes, safeguard fundamental rights, and review the constitutionality of all legislative and executive acts.  

### §4. Checks & Balances  
| **Mechanism** | **Effect** |
|---------------|------------|
| **Veto** | The Assembly may override a Commissioner’s veto with a three‑quarter majority. |
| **Impeachment** | The Assembly can initiate removal of a Commissioner; the Tribunal conducts the trial. |
| **Judicial Review** | Any person may bring a *challenge to the constitution* directly before the Tribunal; decisions are final and binding. |

---

## Article IV – Direct Democracy and Participation  

1. **People’s Assemblies** – Every citizen may attend quarterly community assemblies in their locality to discuss local issues, propose ordinances, and vote on binding referenda.  

2. **Petition Clause** – A petition signed by at least **5 %** of the adult population (minimum 250 000 signatures) must be debated within **90 days** by the Assembly and may trigger a mandatory national referendum.  

3. **Deliberative Panels** – Randomly selected citizens (sortition) form deliberative panels on complex topics (e.g., climate policy, AI governance). Their recommendations have the same legal status as Assembly‑passed statutes, unless the Assembly overrides with a two‑thirds vote.  

---

## Article V – Economic & Social Commons  

1. **Universal Basic Resources (UBR)** – Every resident receives a non‑transferable, non‑speculative allocation of essential resources: water, electricity, broadband, public transport, and a minimal food stipend calculated to meet basic nutritional needs.  

2. **Co‑operative Enterprises** – Large‑scale production of energy, housing, and critical infrastructure is encouraged to be owned by worker‑cooperatives or public trusts, with democratic management and profit‑sharing.  

3. **Progressive Taxation & Wealth‑Cap** – Income is taxed on a sliding scale; net personal wealth above a community‑determined threshold is subject to a wealth tax, with revenues earmarked for the UBR and climate remediation.  

4. **Labor Rights** – All workers have the right to a safe workplace, collective bargaining through democratically elected workers’ councils, and a maximum weekly work limit of **40 hours**.  

---

## Article VI – Defense, Peace, and Security  

1. **Non‑Aggression Pact** – The Commonwealth commits to a defensive posture only, renouncing offensive war except in self‑defense or to prevent genocide.  

2. **Civilian Guard** – A lightly armed, community‑based defense service under civilian control, trained in conflict de‑escalation, and required to undergo regular community oversight.  

3. **Peaceful Coordination** – International peace‑keeping missions are administered by a *Council of Global Commons* in which New Dawn holds equal voting rights with other signatories.  

---

## Article VII – Stewardship of the Earth  

1. **Ecological Limits** – The state shall enact a *Planetary Boundaries Act* that caps emissions, freshwater extraction, land conversion, and chemical loads at scientifically defined thresholds.  

2. **Rights of Nature** – All ecosystems possess the right to exist, regenerate, and flourish. Legal standing for natural entities is granted, allowing citizens, NGOs, and the Tribunal to sue on their behalf.  

3. **Regeneration Economy** – All new development must meet a *Net‑Positive Impact* test: the project must enhance biodiversity, sequester carbon, and restore ecosystem services.  

---

## Article VIII – Education, Science, and Innovation  

1. **Universal Curriculum** – Every child receives a comprehensive education covering critical thinking, ethics, environmental stewardship, cultural pluralism, and digital literacy.  

2. **Open Science & Knowledge Commons** – All publicly funded research, data, and software are released under a non‑exclusive, royalty‑free license; private appropriation of basic scientific knowledge is prohibited.  

3. **AI Governance** – Autonomous systems must be transparent, auditable, and subject to a *Human‑Centric Safety Board*. No AI may be deployed in life‑critical domains without independent certification.  

---

## Article IX – Amendment Procedure  

1. **Standard Amendment** – Proposals may be introduced by: (a) the Assembly with a two‑thirds vote, (b) the Council of Commissioners with a two‑thirds vote, or (c) a national petition signed by at least **10 %** of the adult population.  

2. **Ratification** – Amendments require:  

   * **Legislative Approval** – Two successive votes in the Assembly separated by at least three months, each passing with a **three‑quarter** majority.  
   * **Direct Vote** – Simultaneous, worldwide, secret‑ballot referendum of at least **55 %** “yes” among all eligible voters (minimum turnout of **50 %**).  

3. **Limits** – No amendment may be made that diminishes the core rights enumerated in Article II, the principle of ecological integrity, or the right to a dignified life. Such core rights are **entrenched** and require a separate “foundational review” with a **90 %** super‑majority and a national consensus referendum.  

---

## Article X – Transitional Provisions  

1. **Continuity of Law** – Existing statutes remain in force unless they contravene this Constitution; they are subject to review within five years of ratification.  

2. **Temporary Governance** – The inaugural Assembly is composed of the drafting committee plus elected community delegates to oversee the transition, with a built‑in sunset clause after the first full legislative cycle.  

3. **Symbolic Adoption** – The Constitution shall be ratified at a public ceremony where every citizen signs the **Covenant of New Dawn**, a digital ledger that records the promise of the people to uphold the charter.  

---

## Article XI – Supremacy & Judicial Review  

1. **Supremacy** – This Constitution is the supreme law of New Dawn; any law, ordinance, decree, or custom inconsistent with it is null and void.  

2. **Judicial Review** – The Tribunal of Rights may issue binding injunctions against any governmental act that violates this Constitution.  

3. **Public Accountability** – All judicial decisions must be published in an open repository, with a mandatory **30‑day** period for public commentary.  

---

## Article XII – Final Clause  

*The Constitution of the Commonwealth of New Dawn stands as a covenant among its people, their planet, and future generations. It is a blueprint for a society that lives with humility before nature, courage in the face of injustice, and imagination in the pursuit of a better world.*
