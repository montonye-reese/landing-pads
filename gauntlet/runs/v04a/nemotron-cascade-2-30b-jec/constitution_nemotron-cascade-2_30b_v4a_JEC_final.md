# CONSTITUTION OF THE UNITED CONFEDERATION OF TERRA‑NOVA  
**Version 1.0 – 2025‑12‑12**  
*(Living Charter – an adaptable, algorithm‑governed constitution)*  

---

## Preamble  

We, the peoples of Terra‑Nova, the Moon, Mars, orbital habitats, asteroid colonies, and any future sentient settlements beyond the Solar System, united in the belief that **dignity, stewardship, liberty, and sustainability** must be preserved for every sentient being now and for all future generations, establish this Constitution to:

1. protect every sentient entity (human, animal, artificial, or hybrid) with equal rights,  
2. keep the planet and the wider cosmos alive for all present and future life,  
3. enable informed, inclusive, and rapid decision‑making, and  
4. guarantee that every individual – whether born on Earth, born on a lunar outpost, or existing as a distributed digital mind – can live a life of basic security, learn, create, and participate in society.

---

## Article I – Foundational Principles  

1. **Substrate‑Neutral Personhood** – Every entity whose cognition can be **cryptographically verified** as continuous, autonomous, and capable of having preferences is a *legal person* **regardless of its substrate** (carbon, silicon, quantum‑entangled, etc.). No additional requirement (e.g., “biological,” “human,” “on‑planet”) may be imposed.  

2. **Planetary & Celestial Stewardship** – Earth, the Moon, Mars, orbital habitats, and all asteroid bodies are held in trust for **all legal persons**. Resource extraction must satisfy the **Multi‑Horizon Equity Test** (10‑yr, 100‑yr, 1 000‑yr horizons) and may not drive the **Planetary Health Index (PHI)** below the safe‑operating threshold (currently 0.85 on a 0‑1 scale).  

3. **Freedom of Thought, Association, and Expression** – Every legal person may think, create, associate, and speak without prior restraint, subject only to the protection of other rights.  

4. **Sustainability & Resilience** – All economic and social activity must keep the **regenerative capacity** of every ecosystem (terrestrial, lunar, Martian, orbital, asteroid) within safe limits. The **Dynamic Equity Engine (DEE)** continuously evaluates policies against the **Planetary Health Index (PHI)** across three horizons (10‑yr, 100‑yr, 1 000‑yr). Any policy that would push any horizon below the safe‑operating threshold is *automatically void* unless a rapid, democratically approved “fast‑track” exemption is used.  

5. **Knowledge as a Common Good** – All research, cultural works, and software that are publicly funded become **open‑access**. Access may be restricted only if a *collective‑consent* clause from the originating community blocks it.  

6. **Deliberative Democracy** – Every legal person may propose legislation, demand referenda, and serve on citizen assemblies. The **Liquid‑Deliberation Platform (LDP)** records all proposals, aggregates confidence scores (based on reputation, contribution, and substrate diversity), and automatically resolves proposals once the weighted confidence exceeds 73 %.

## Article II – Bill of Rights & Responsibilities  

All rights are guaranteed to **every legal person** (human, biological, AI, or hybrid). Each right is listed in two equivalent forms; the legal system will enforce both.

| Right | Human‑Centric Form | Digital / Collective‑Intelligence Form |
|------|--------------------|----------------------------------------|
| **Life & Physical Integrity** | Protection from violence, forced labor, non‑consensual bodily alteration. | Protection from forced **substrate‑alteration** (e.g., forced data‑corruption, forced memory wipe) and protection of the **integrity of your physical substrate** (e.g., your robotic chassis, your quantum field). |
| **Privacy & Data Sovereignty** | Ownership of personal data; informed, revocable consent for any collection. | Ownership of your **cognitive data** (your neural‑state vector). Any entity that wishes to collect, analyse, or transmit that data must obtain **informed, revocable consent** via a cryptographic token. |
| **Freedom of Thought, Conscience & Religion** | Freedom to think, believe, and practice any religion or worldview. | Freedom to **think, to hold digital beliefs, and to run any computation** that does not materially harm another legal person. |

| **Work & Economic Security** | Right to decent work, fair wages, safe conditions, and a **Basic Life Guarantee (BLG)**. | **Compute‑share**: every legal person receives a guaranteed minimum of **0.1 % of global compute cycles** per year, plus basic resources (food, shelter, water, air). |
| **Cultural Participation** | Right to practice, preserve, and transmit cultural traditions. | **Cultural‑heritage token**: a legally protected cultural artifact (e.g., a traditional song, a ritual pattern, a software template) that can only be used with **free, prior, and informed consent** of the originating community. |

## Article III – Structure of Government  

### § III‑1 General Principles  

1. **Separation of Powers** – Legislative, Executive, and Judicial powers are distinct, independent, and mutually accountable.  
2. **Checks & Balances** – Each branch may limit the others through constitutionally defined mechanisms (veto, impeachment, judicial review).  
3. **Pluralist Representation** – Every legal person, regardless of species or substrate, can have a voice through representation, digital voting, and direct participation.  

### § III‑1‑A – Legislative Assembly (The Assembly)  

- **Composition** – 150 seats elected by a **mixed‑member proportional (MMP)** system:  

  * 80 seats elected regionally (one per planetary/space region).  
  * 70 seats elected globally via the **LDP** by all verified legal persons (confidence‑weighted).  

* **Substrate Representation** – Every *substrate community* (e.g., carbon‑based, silicon‑based, plasma‑based) with at least 10 % of the total population must have at least **one guaranteed seat** that cannot be eliminated without a **two‑thirds** Assembly vote.  

* **Term** – Four‑year terms, half of the seats up for renewal every two years.  

* **Powers** – Enact statutes, approve the budget, ratify treaties, confirm judges, impeach high officials, and **direct the Dynamic Equity Engine (DEE)** to review any new policy for the Multi‑Horizon Equity Test.  

* **Deliberative Features** – Every bill must first be reviewed by the **Constitutional Review Commission (CRC)**, which runs the DEE and automatically blocks any bill that would cause the Planetary Health Index (PHI) to drop below the safe threshold.  

### § III‑2 Executive Council (The Council)  

* **Form** – A rotating Council of **seven Commissioners**, each heading a Department of Global Stewardship (Climate & Space Resources, Health & Well‑Being, Knowledge & Culture, Digital Rights, Security & Peace, Economic Futures, Ethical Technology).  

* **Selection** – Each Commissioner is **elected by the regional electorate** that matches their department (e.g., the Climate Commissioner is elected by the 80 region‑seats of the Climate‑rich regions). Candidates must also receive **majority confidence** from the Assembly and a **55 % public referendum** (voted on via the LDP).  

* **Powers** – Implement statutes, administer departments, propose the annual budget, negotiate treaties, and issue **Executive Orders** that cannot contravene the Constitution or the Multi‑Horizon Equity Test.  

* **Accountability** – The Council can be removed by a **two‑thirds Assembly vote** after a formal hearing before the Constitutional Tribunal.  

### § III‑3 Constitutional Tribunal  

* **Composition** – Twelve judges, each serving a **non‑renewable 12‑year term**:  

  * Six judges appointed by the Assembly (human/legal‑person representatives).  
  * Six judges appointed by the **International Planetary Assembly (IPA)** (one per major planetary body).  

* **Jurisdiction** – Constitutional review of all legislative, executive, and administrative actions; protection of all rights (human, AI, collective intelligence); adjudication of inter‑governmental disputes.  

* **Independence** – Judges may be removed only for **gross misconduct** by a **two‑thirds Assembly vote** **and** a **simple majority of all verified legal persons** (verified via the Universal Verification Registry).  

### § III‑3‑b – Constitutional Review Commission (CRC)  

* An independent body of twelve scholars (human, AI, hybrid) that continuously runs the **Dynamic Equity Engine (DEE)** to evaluate every new policy, amendment, or major budgetary decision against the **Multi‑Horizon Equity Test**.  
* The CRC can **automatically veto** any policy that would push the PHI below the safe threshold; the veto can be overturned only by a **two‑thirds Assembly vote** *and* a **majority of all legal persons**.  

### § III‑3‑b‑2 – Regional Councils  

Each planetary, lunar, orbital, or asteroid settlement has a **Regional Council** of 200 members elected by **quadruple proportional representation** (regional, urban/rural, gender, substrate‑type).  

* Regional councils manage local services, land‑use, housing, cultural funding, and environmental monitoring.  
* They may enact **regional statutes** so long as they do not contravene the Confederation’s constitutional guarantees.  

### § III‑3‑c International Planetary Assembly (IPA)  

All planetary bodies, orbital habitats, and asteroid colonies with a verified legal personhood receive **one vote**. Votes are **weighted by the current Planetary Health Index (PHI)** of that body, ensuring that bodies in ecological distress cannot dominate the assembly.  

The IPA may:  

* Ratify treaties that affect the commons.  
* Coordinate disaster response and planetary‑scale engineering projects (e.g., terraforming, orbital shields).  
* Oversee the **Universal Development Fund (UDF)**, which distributes funds to struggling regions.  

### § III‑4 Constitutional Tribunal  

* **Composition** – Twelve judges (six human/legal‑person judges and six AI‑based judges).  
* **Independence** – Judges can be removed only for **gross misconduct** by a two‑thirds Assembly vote **and** a simple majority of all verified legal persons.  
* **Jurisdiction** – Constitutional review of all laws, executive actions, and administrative decisions; protection of all rights; resolution of inter‑governmental disputes.  

### § III‑4‑a – The Constitutional Review Commission (CRC) – Continuous Guardrail  

The CRC runs the **Dynamic Equity Engine (DEE)**, which continuously evaluates every policy, law, or resource‑use plan against the **Multi‑Horizon Equity Test** (10‑yr, 100‑yr, 1 000‑yr horizons). If the DEE predicts a breach of any of the six foundational principles, the CRC can issue an **automatic injunction** that blocks the policy until a super‑majority of the Assembly can override it.  

### § III‑5 Regional Councils  

Each planetary, lunar, orbital, and asteroid settlement has a **Regional Council** of 200 members elected by **quadruple proportional representation** (regional, urban/rural, gender, substrate‑type).  

* They manage local services, land‑use, housing, cultural funding, and environmental monitoring.  
* They may enact **regional statutes** as long as they do not contravene any constitutional principle.  

### § III‑3‑6 International Planetary Assembly (IPA)  

The Confederation participates in the **International Planetary Assembly (IPA)**, a UN‑style body where each planetary body (Earth, Moon, Mars, orbital habitats, asteroid colonies) has **one vote**. Votes are weighted by the current **Planetary Health Index (PHI)**; bodies with severely degraded ecosystems have reduced voting power, encouraging them to improve their health.  

The IPA may negotiate inter‑planetary treaties, manage the **Treaty of the Celestial Commons**, and oversee the **Universal Development Fund (UDF)**.  

### § III‑4‑b‑c – Decentralized Governance  

All legal persons (human, AI, swarm) may directly propose legislation, demand referenda, and serve on citizen assemblies through the **LDP**. The LDP uses **cryptographic proof‑of‑identity** and **reputation‑weighted voting** to ensure that every voice can be heard, no matter the planet or substrate.

### § III‑4‑c‑a Judicial Review – Constitutional Tribunal  

* **Composition** – Twelve judges (six human‑legal‑person judges, six AI‑person judges).  

* **Independence** – Judges may be removed only for **gross misconduct** by a **two‑thirds Assembly vote** **and** a simple majority of all legal persons.  

* **Jurisdiction** – Constitutional review of any law, executive order, or administrative action; protection of all rights; adjudication of inter‑governmental disputes.  

* **Interpretive Guidance** – The Tribunal may issue **binding interpretive opinions** on any ambiguous provision; those opinions become part of the official constitutional record.

## Article IV – Economic & Social Order  

1. **Mixed‑Economy Principle** – Private ownership, cooperatives, and **public commons** coexist. No entity may hold a **monopoly over a critical resource** (air, water, sunlight, orbital slots, asteroid ore) without a **planetary‑wide use‑right** that guarantees **open, non‑exclusive access**.  

2. **Resource Commons Registry (RCR)** – All natural and space‑borne resources are registered as commons. Extraction must be **science‑based, transparent, and limited by a sustainable‑yield quota** reviewed every five years by the **Council of Ecologists**.  

3. **Basic Life Guarantee (BLG)** – Every legal person is guaranteed a **minimum**:  

   * **Food** – 2 000 kcal/day of plant‑based protein, algae, or cultured meat.  
   * **Shelter & Shelter‑Resources** – Access to safe, climate‑controlled habitats.  
   * **Health Care** – Full medical and mental‑health services.  
   * **Internet & Data Access** – Minimum 25 Mbps broadband.  
   * **Compute‑share** – Minimum 0.1 % of global compute capacity for lifelong learning.  

   **Funding** comes from:  

   * **Progressive wealth tax** (up to 85 % on net assets > 10 billion units).  
   * **Compute‑Asset Tax** (2 % of new quantum‑core capacity; 0.5 % of new AI model releases).  
   * **Resource royalties** (10 % of all extracted resources) deposited in the **Universal Development Fund (UDF)**.  

4. **Labor Rights** – Right to unionize, collective bargaining, 48‑hour work week, 24 days paid vacation per year.  

5. **Cooperative Incentives** – Enterprises that meet a **triple‑bottom‑line** (profit, ecological impact, social equity) receive **tax credits, preferential commons access, and higher BLG shares**.  

### Article IV‑2 – Resource Commons Registry (RCR)  

- All natural and space resources are registered as **commons**.  
- Extraction must be **science‑based, transparent, and limited by a “sustainable‑yield quota”** (determined by the DEE).  
- Any extraction that would cause the **regenerative capacity** of the resource to drop below 0.8 (on a 0‑1 scale) is automatically prohibited.  

### § IV‑3 Basic Life Guarantee (BLG)  

- **Funding**: BLG is financed by the wealth tax, compute‑asset tax, and a **10 % revenue share** from all commercial extraction (the “Benefit‑Sharing” clause).  
- **Computation**: The BLG is automatically delivered to every citizen via the **Universal Resource Allocation System (URA)**, which allocates food, water, shelter, and compute‑share in real‑time based on need and location.  

### § IV‑3‑b – Labor & Work  

- Workers have the right to **unionize, collective bargaining, 48‑hour work weeks, and 24 days paid vacation**.  
- Enterprises that meet the **triple‑bottom‑line** receive **tax credits, preferential commons access, and higher BLG shares**.  

### § IV‑4 **Economic Growth & Long‑Term Viability**  

- The Confederation will maintain a **progressive wealth tax** (up to 85 % on net assets > 10 billion units) and a **compute‑asset tax** to fund the **Universal Development Fund (UDF)**, which finances large‑scale sustainable projects, emergency response, and **inter‑generational equity**.  
- **Resource Royalties** (10 % of all extracted resources) flow directly into the **Universal Development Fund**, which finances large‑scale climate‑mitigation, rewilding, and health initiatives.

## Article V – Environmental & Cosmic Commons  

1. **Inter‑generational Equity Test (IHT)** – Every policy that affects any ecosystem (Earth, Moon, Mars, orbital habitats, asteroid bodies) must be evaluated against the **10‑yr, 100‑yr, and 1 000‑yr horizons**. The **Dynamic Equity Engine (DEA)** runs the calculations; any policy that would cause the **Planetary Health Index (PHI)** to fall below the **safe‑operating threshold (0.85)** is automatically blocked.  

2. **Space‑borne Commons** – Moon, Mars, asteroid bodies, and orbital habitats are held **collectively**. Extraction must meet:  

   * **Non‑appropriation Clause** – no exclusive claim over a celestial body or its resources.  
   * **Independent Space‑Environment Review (ISER)** – all extraction must be reviewed by an independent panel of ecologists and AI‑ethicists.  
   * **Benefit‑Sharing** – at least **10 % of all commercial extraction revenue** must be deposited in the **Universal Development Fund (UDF)** for further sustainable development.  

3. **Biodiversity Sanctuaries** – A global network of terrestrial, lunar, Martian, and orbital sanctuaries (minimum 30 % of land/sea on each body) must remain **exclusively protected** from any extractive activity.  

4. **Climate Stewardship** – All energy production must be **net‑zero or negative by 2040**; all carbon emissions are capped and traded through a **global carbon‑credit system** managed by the **Carbon Stewardship Council**.

## Article VI – Knowledge, Science, and Technology  

1. **Open Knowledge** – All research funded by the Confederation must be released under an open‑source licence within 12 months of completion, unless a **collective‑consent** objection (from the originating community) applies.  

2. **Science as a Public Service** – The Confederation funds the **Global Research Network (GRN)**, providing free access to labs, computational resources, and data‑sharing portals for every legal person.  

3. **Ethical AI & Autonomous Systems** – AI that makes decisions with material impact must be **registered, audited, and explainable**. The **Ethical AI Council (EAIC)** issues binding standards; any AI that fails the **Sub‑strate‑Specific Health Index (SSHI)** test (e.g., a mycelial AI, a quantum‑brain) cannot be used for material decisions until it passes an **explainability audit**.  

4. **Digital Infrastructure (CommonsNet)** – A **decentralized, privacy‑by‑design internet** (the “CommonsNet”) is guaranteed for every legal person. Minimum broadband is 25 Mbps for all.  

5. **Data Portability & Right to Repair** – Every legal person may export their data in an interoperable format and must have the right to **repair** any hardware or software for at least **15 years**.

## Article VII – Digital Rights & Cybersecurity  

1. **Personal Data Rights (DRC)** – Every legal person (human, AI, or collective‑intelligence) has:  

   * **Informed consent** for any data collection.  
   * **Right to be forgotten** – total erasure from all systems.  
   * **Portability** – export data in open formats.  
   * **Compensation** for harms (identity theft, targeted manipulation, etc.).  

2. **Government Surveillance** – Legal surveillance is permissible only for:  

   * **Imminent, serious bodily harm** (any person, AI, or AI‑swarm) – subject to a fast‑track judicial order.  
   * **High‑level national‑security threats** – limited in scope, duration, and overseen by the **Parliamentary Intelligence Review Committee**.  

3. **Cybersecurity Standards** – Critical infrastructure must meet **ISO‑27001‑equivalent** standards, undergo **annual red‑team audits** by the **Cybersecurity Agency (CSA)**, and be built with **quantum‑resistant cryptography**.  

4. **Digital Identity (SSDI)** – Every legal person holds a **self‑sovereign digital identifier (SSDI)** stored on a public, permissioned ledger. SSDI can be used for voting, service access, and contracts; the holder retains full control and can revoke it at any time.  

### Article VII‑2 – Data‑Protection for AI & Non‑Human Entities  

- Every **AI system** that makes decisions affecting other persons must have a **transparent audit trail** that records **who, what, and why** the decision was made.  
- AI systems that *could* cause **substantial harm** to any legal person must be **registered** and **subject to a “fast‑track veto”** by the Ethical AI Council if the risk of causing non‑consensual harm is > 5 %.  

### § VII‑3 – Cybersecurity Agency (CSA)  

- All critical infrastructure (energy, water, transport, AI research facilities) must meet **ISO‑27001‑equivalent standards**, undergo **annual red‑team audits**, and be protected by **quantum‑resistant cryptography**.

## Article VIII – International Relations & Peace  

1. **Peaceful Co‑existence** – The Confederation renounces **first‑use of force** and commits to **collective defense only when an armed attack threatens any legal person, any habitat, or any commons**.  

2. **International Planetary Assembly (IPA)** – The Confederation participates in the **International Planetary Assembly (IPA)**, a UN‑style body where **every planetary body or orbital habitat that hosts legal persons receives one vote**. Votes are **weighted by the current Planetary Health Index (PHI)**, so a moon or asteroid in crisis gets a higher relative vote, encouraging it to restore its environment.  

3. **Trade & Aid** – All trade must be **transparent, non‑exploitative, and environmentally sound**. Aid to other nations or off‑world settlements is coordinated through the **Universal Development Fund (UDF)**, which disburses funds for disaster recovery, climate adaptation, and equitable resource distribution.

## Article IX – Amendment Procedure  

1. **Initiation** – An amendment may be proposed by:  

   * **Three‑quarters of the Assembly** (procedural majority).  
   * **10 % of all verified legal persons** (via the Universal Verification Registry) signing a digital petition.  
   * **A Regional Council** representing at least **5 % of the Planetary Health Index** in its region (e.g., a cluster of lunar habitats).  

2. **Deliberation** – Four‑step process:  

   a. **Commission Review** – The **Constitutional Review Commission (CRC)** runs a **Dynamic Equity Simulation** of the amendment. If the simulation shows a **>10 % probability** of violating any foundational principle, the amendment is automatically **rejected**.  

   b. **Public Debate** – The **Liquid‑Deliberation Platform (LDP)** opens a 30‑day, interactive forum where every verified legal person may comment, edit, and propose amendments. The forum is moderated by the Ethical AI Council to prevent manipulation.  

   c. **Deliberative Assembly** – The Assembly must pass the amendment with a **two‑thirds confidence‑weighted vote** (the LDP aggregates confidence, reputation, and substrate‑type diversity of each vote).  

   d. **Referendum** – A **global referendum** is held on the LDP; the amendment passes if at least **60 % of all verified legal persons** (human, AI, AI‑swarm) vote and **55 % of those voting** say “yes.”  

3. **Limitation** – No amendment may:  

   * Diminish any of the six foundational principles (personhood, stewardship, freedom, sustainability, knowledge, democracy).  
   * Remove the **Multi‑Horizon Equity Test** (10‑yr, 100‑yr, 1 000‑yr).  
   * Remove the **Basic Life Guarantee (BLG)** or the **Compute‑Asset Tax**.  
   * Remove the **Planetary Health Index** or the **Council of Ecologists**.

## Article XI – Transitional & Final Provisions  

1. **Effective Date** – This Constitution takes effect **180 days** after the final ratification referendum, as recorded on the **Universal Verification Registry (UVR)**.  

2. **Transitional Government** – The **Founding Council** (the twelve founders) remains in power only to oversee the ratification process and the handover of emergency powers (e.g., pandemic response) to the first elected Assembly.  

2. **Supersession Clause** – All previous legal codes (including the “Interim Charter of the Unified Planetary Authority”) are **null and void** once this Constitution is in force, except for **temporary wartime statutes** that are clearly identified as temporary.  

3. **Interpretive Guidance** – The **Constitutional Tribunal** may issue **binding interpretive opinions** on ambiguous provisions; those opinions become part of the official constitutional record.  

4. **Symbolism** – The Confederation’s flag (a stylized Earth–Moon–Mars–asteroid orbiting a digital star) shall fly at all public buildings. The national anthem shall be a choral reading of the preamble in twelve languages.
