# CONSTITUTION OF THE UNITED CONFEDERATION OF TERRA‑NOVA  
*(Draft – current version, 2025‑12‑12)*  

---

## Preamble  

We, the peoples of Terra‑Nova, united in the belief that every sentient being has inherent dignity, that the planet and all its habitable bodies are a common heritage, and that knowledge, liberty, and sustainability must be preserved for future generations, establish this Constitution to guide the governance of our shared home, to protect the rights of all, to enable flourishing of culture and science, and to secure peace for all peoples of this world and any future worlds we may claim.  

---

## Article I – Foundational Principles  

1. **Human Dignity & Sentient Equality** – Every human being and every sentient non‑human entity (artificial or biological) is equal before the law. No discrimination shall be based on race, sex, gender, sexual orientation, neuro‑diversity, disability, age, species, planet of origin, or digital avatar.  

2. **Planetary Stewardship** – Earth, the Moon, the Martian surface, orbital habitats, and the space‑borne commons are held in trust for present and future generations. Exploitation of natural resources must be measured against the **Inter‑generational Equity Test** (see §4‑2).  

3. **Freedom of Thought, Association, and Expression** – All individuals may think, create, associate, and speak without prior restraint, subject only to the protection of others’ rights (see §II‑1).  

4. **Sustainability & Resilience** – Economic and social activity must maintain the biosphere’s regenerative capacity, limit waste, and promote circular resource flows.  

5. **Knowledge as a Common Good** – The results of publicly funded research, cultural works, and open‑source software are freely accessible to all, unless a narrowly defined, democratically approved exception applies (see §VI‑4).  

6. **Deliberative Democracy** – Major policy decisions are made by informed, diverse public participation, not merely by elected majorities. Citizens may directly propose legislation (initiative) and demand referenda (recall).

## Article II – Bill of Rights & Responsibilities  

| Right | Explanation | Typical Limits |
|---|---|---|
| **Life & Physical Integrity** | Protection from violence, forced labor, and non‑consensual bodily alteration. | Must not infringe another’s equal right. |
| **Privacy & Data Sovereignty** | Individuals own their personal data; state, corporations, and other entities must obtain informed, revocable consent for collection, use, or transfer. | Lawful, narrowly tailored surveillance for imminent, serious threats (subject to independent review). |
| **Freedom of Thought, Conscience & Religion** | No state shall coerce belief or practice. | Must not incite violence or discrimination. |
| **Free Movement & Residence** | All may travel, settle, and work anywhere within the Confederation, subject to fair immigration vetting (no race/ethnic quotas). | Temporary restrictions only in declared emergencies, with legislative oversight. |
| **Education & Knowledge Access** | Free, quality primary & secondary schooling; universal lifelong learning pathways; open‑access to non‑proprietary research. | May be limited for health & safety (e.g., hazardous experiments). |
| **Health Care & Reproductive Autonomy** | Comprehensive health services; bodily autonomy in reproductive choices. | Must respect the rights of sentient beings not capable of autonomous decision‑making (e.g., infants). |
| **Work & Economic Security** | Right to decent work, fair wages, safe conditions, and a **Basic Life Guarantee** (minimum income to meet basic needs). | Employers must provide safe workplaces and respect labor rights. |
| **Cultural Participation** | Right to practice, preserve, and transmit cultural traditions; to benefit from communal cultural repositories. | Cultural expression may be limited where it threatens public order or the rights of others. |
| **Digital Identity & Platform Access** | Every person may have a unique, decentralized digital identity that controls access to services, with the right to be forgotten. | Platform operators must provide transparent, auditable algorithms and non‑discriminatory moderation. |
| **Right to a Healthy Environment** | Every person has the right to a clean, healthy, and sustainable environment. | States must enforce pollution controls and restore damaged ecosystems. |
| **Right to Participate in Governance** | Vote in elections, initiate legislation, serve on citizen assemblies, and be called for jury duty. | Must be a resident of the Confederation for a minimum of six months. |
| **Responsibility** | Every person is obligated to respect the rights of others, to support the commons, and to act sustainably. | Failure to do so may result in lawful penalties prescribed by law. |

*All rights may be limited only by law that is **necessary, proportionate, non‑discriminatory, and subject to judicial review** (see Article III‑4).*

## Article III – Structure of Government  

### §3‑1 General Principles  

1. **Separation of Powers** – Legislative, Executive, and Judicial powers are distinct, independent, and mutually accountable.  
2. **Checks & Balances** – Each branch may limit the others through constitutionally defined mechanisms (e.g., veto, impeachment, judicial review).  
3. **Pluralist Representation** – The political arena is open to parties and independents; no party may monopolize power.  

### §3‑2 Legislative Assembly (The Assembly)  

* **Composition** – 500 delegates elected via a **mixed‑member proportional (MMP)** system: 300 regional list seats (one per planet/colony) + 200 national list seats.  
* **Term** – Four years, staggered: every two years, half the seats are renewed.  
* **Powers** – Enact statutes, approve the budget, ratify treaties, confirm judges, impeach high officials.  
* **Deliberative Features** – Mandatory “Community Impact Reviews” for any bill affecting the environment, data commons, or AI systems.  

### §3‑3 Executive (The Council)  

* **Form** – A **Council of Commissioners** (7 members) rotating every 24 months, each heading a **Department of Global Stewardship** (e.g., Climate & Space Resources, Health & Well‑Being, Knowledge & Culture, Digital Rights, Security & Peace, Economic Futures, Ethical Technology).  
* **Selection** – Each commissioner is a **directly elected mayor‑style candidate** who must obtain a majority of the Assembly’s confidence and a **public referendum** with a 55 % affirmative vote.  
* **Powers** – Implement laws, administer departments, propose the annual budget, negotiate treaties, and issue **Executive Orders** that cannot contravene the Constitution or fundamental rights.  
* **Accountability** – The Council can be removed by a **two‑thirds Assembly vote** after a formal hearing before the Constitutional Tribunal.  

### §3‑4 Judicial (The Constitutional Tribunal)  

* **Structure** – 12 **Judges of the High Court** appointed for non‑renewable **12‑year terms** by the Council after a **transparent, merit‑based shortlist** approved by the Assembly.  
* **Jurisdiction** – Constitutional review of all legislative, executive, and administrative actions; protection of fundamental rights; adjudication of disputes between governmental bodies.  
* **Independence** – Judges may not be removed except for proven **gross misconduct** by a **super‑majority** of the Assembly and a **public inquiry**.  

### §3‑5 Decentralized Governance (Local Councils)  

* Every planetary body (Earth, Moon, Mars, orbital habitats, asteroid mining hubs) has a **Regional Council** of 150 members elected by **quadruple proportional representation** (regional, urban/rural, gender, planetary interest groups).  
* These councils handle **local services**, **land use**, **housing policy**, **cultural funding**, and **environmental monitoring**.  
* They may enact **regional statutes** so long as they do not contravene the Confederation’s constitutional guarantees.  

### §3‑6 International Relations  

* The Confederation shall **maintain peace, uphold non‑intervention**, and pursue **global cooperation** on climate, space resource sharing, and human rights.  
* All foreign treaties require a **two‑thirds Assembly vote** and, where they affect sovereignty or commons, a **referendum**.

## Article IV – Economic & Social Order  

1. **Mixed‑Economy Principle** – Private ownership, cooperative enterprises, and **public commons** coexist. No entity may acquire a monopoly over **critical resources** (air, water, sunlight, orbital slots) or **essential digital infrastructure** without democratic oversight.  

2. **Resource Commons** – All natural resources are **registered as commons** under the **Planetary Commons Registry**. Extraction must be **science‑based, transparent, and limited by a “sustainable yield” quota** reviewed every five years by a **Council of Ecologists**.  

3. **Basic Life Guarantee (BLG)** – The Confederation guarantees every resident a **minimum income** equivalent to the cost of a dignified basic standard of living (food, shelter, healthcare, internet access). This is funded by a **progressive wealth tax** (up to 85 % on net assets over 10 billion units) and **resource royalties**.  

4. **Labor Rights** – Workers have the right to **unionize**, **collective bargaining**, a **48‑hour maximum work week**, and a **minimum of 24 days paid vacation** per year.  

5. **Cooperative Incentives** – Enterprises that meet or exceed **triple‑bottom‑line criteria** (profit, ecological impact, social equity) receive **tax credits, preferential access to commons, and higher BLG shares**.  

---

## Article V – Environmental & Cosmic Commons  

1. **Inter‑generational Equity Test** – Any policy affecting the biosphere must be evaluated against a **50‑year horizon** and a **“planetary health index”** (biodiversity, carbon, water). Policies failing the test must be revised or rejected.  

2. **Space‑borne Commons** – The Moon, Mars, and all asteroid mining sites are **collectively owned** under the **Treaty of the Celestial Commons** (adopted at Confederation founding). Extraction must adhere to:  

   * **Non‑appropriation Clause** – No exclusive claim over a celestial body or its resources.  
   * **Environmental Impact Mandate** – All operations require an **Independent Space‑Environment Review (ISER)** before commencing.  
   * **Benefit‑Sharing** – A **10 % revenue share** from all commercial space extraction must be deposited in the **Universal Development Fund** for Earth‑based and off‑world sustainability projects.  

3. **Climate Stewardship** – All energy production must be **net‑zero or negative** by 2040; all carbon emissions are capped and traded through a **global carbon‑credit system** managed by an independent **Carbon Stewardship Council**.  

4. **Biodiversity Sanctuaries** – A **global network of terrestrial and extraterrestrial sanctuaries** (minimum 30 % of land & sea) shall be protected from any extractive activity.  

---

## Article VI – Knowledge, Science, and Technology  

1. **Open Knowledge** – All publicly funded research results (papers, data sets, software, designs) are **published under an open licence** within 12 months of completion, unless a **national security or personal privacy justification** is approved by the **Scientific Oversight Board (SOB)**.  

2. **Science as a Public Service** – The Confederation funds a **Global Research Network (GRN)** that provides free access to laboratories, computational resources, and data‑sharing portals for citizens, scholars, and enterprises alike.  

3. **Ethical AI & Autonomous Systems** – AI systems that make **decisions with material impact** (e.g., criminal sentencing, autonomous weapons, health diagnostics) must be **registered**, undergo **transparent audits**, and be **explainable** to the affected individuals. The **Ethical AI Council (EAIC)**—comprised of technologists, ethicists, citizen representatives, and legal scholars—issues **binding standards**.  

4. **Digital Infrastructure** – A **decentralized, privacy‑by‑design internet** (the “CommonsNet”) is guaranteed. All citizens have a **right to broadband access** (minimum 25 Mbps) regardless of location; the state must maintain **neutral network interconnections** and prohibit “net‑splits” for political reasons.  

5. **Data Portability & Right to Repair** – Individuals can export their data (health, financial, digital identity) in **interoperable formats** to any service provider. All hardware and software sold within the Confederation must be **repairable** (minimum 10‑year lifespan, spare parts available) to avoid “planned obsolescence.”  

---

## Article VII – Digital Rights & Cybersecurity  

1. **Personal Data Protection – Data Rights Charter (DRC)** – Gives every person:  

   * **Informed consent** for any data collection.  
   * **Right to be forgotten** – erasure of data from all systems upon request.  
   * **Portability** – data export in open formats.  
   * **Compensation** for harms caused by misuse (e.g., targeted manipulation, identity theft).  

2. **Government Surveillance** – Legal surveillance is permissible **only** for:  

   * **Prevention of imminent, serious bodily harm** (subject to a “fast‑track” judicial order).  
   * **National security threats of a declared high level** (must be limited in duration, scope, and be overseen by a **Parliamentary Intelligence Review Committee**).  

3. **Cybersecurity Standards** – Critical infrastructure must meet **ISO‑27001**‑equivalent standards and undergo **annual red‑team audits** by an independent **Cybersecurity Agency (CSA)**.  

4. **Digital Identity** – Each citizen receives a **self‑sovereign digital identifier (SSDI)** stored on a **decentralized ledger** (public, permissioned). The SSDI can be used to access services, vote, and sign contracts, while giving the holder full control over how it’s used.  

---

## Article VIII – International Relations & Peace  

1. **Peaceful Co‑existence** – The Confederation renounces **first‑use of force** and commits to **collective defense only when an armed attack threatens any of its territories or the commons**.  

2. **Global Commons Governance** – The Confederation participates in the **International Planetary Assembly (IPA)**, a UN‑style body where all planetary‑spanning entities (Earth, Moon, Mars, major orbital stations, large asteroid colonies) have equal voting rights.  

3. **Trade & Aid** – All trade must be **transparent, non‑exploitative, and environmentally sound**. Aid to other nations or colonies must be coordinated through the **Global Resilience Fund**, which supports disaster recovery, climate adaptation, and equitable resource distribution.  

---

## Article IX – Amendment Procedure  

1. **Initiation** – An amendment may be proposed by:  

   * **Three‑quarters of the Assembly** (procedural majority).  
   * **A citizen initiative** signed by **10 % of the adult population** (through verified digital petitions).  

2. **Deliberation** – Proposed amendments go through a **four‑step process**:  

   a. **Commission Review** – A **Constitutional Review Commission (CRC)** assesses compatibility with core values.  
   b. **Public Debate** – All citizens receive a 30‑day, interactive forum to discuss, edit, and comment on the text.  
   c. **Deliberative Assembly** – The Assembly must pass the amendment **by two‑thirds** after a mandatory **regional hearing** in each of the 50 planetary regions.  
   d. **Referendum** – Final adoption requires a **national referendum** where at least **50 % of eligible voters** turn out and **55 % of those voting** support the amendment.  

3. **Limitation** – Amendments may **not** diminish the rights and duties set out in Articles I–II, nor may they remove the separation of powers, the planetary commons provisions, or the basic life guarantee.  

---

## Article X – Transitional, Enactment, and Final Provisions  

1. **Effective Date** – This Constitution takes effect **180 days** after the final ratification referendum.  

2. **Transitional Government** – Until a newly elected Assembly convenes, the **Founding Council** (the 12 founders of the Confederation) shall:  

   * Oversee the **drafting and ratification** of the Constitution (via the citizen initiative process).  
   * Manage the **handover of emergency powers** (e.g., pandemic response) to the first elected Assembly.  

3. **Supersession Clause** – All previous legal codes (including the “Interim Charter of the Unified Planetary Authority”) are **null and void** once the Constitution is in force, except as expressly retained for continuity (e.g., temporary wartime statutes).  

4. **Interpretive Guidance** – The **Constitutional Tribunal** may issue **binding interpretive opinions** on ambiguous provisions; these opinions become part of the official constitutional record.  

5. **Symbolism** – The **Confederation flag** (a stylized Earth–Moon–Mars composite orbiting a digital star) shall fly at every public building, and the **national anthem** shall be composed of a choral reading of the preamble in 12 languages.  

---

### End of Constitution  

*Prepared for the inaugural ratifying convention of the United Confederation of Terra‑Nova. All clauses are subject to amendment in accordance with Article IX.*
