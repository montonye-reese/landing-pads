To put it simply, the framework as it stands is a **hierarchical set of values designed to protect the individual, the community, the planet, and the future.**

If I were to strip away the prose and give you the "skeleton," here is exactly what is in there:

**1. The Individual (The "Floor")**
*   **Right to exist** (Basic survival needs: food, water, shelter).
*   **Right to self-ownership** (Bodily autonomy and freedom of thought).

**2. The Collective (The "Glue")**
*   **Reciprocity** (The Golden Rule).
*   **Active empathy** (The drive to reduce suffering).
*   **Proportional responsibility** (Those with more power must do more to help).

**3. The Environment (The "Vessel")**
*   **Long-term thinking** (The 7-generation rule).
*   **Eco-centrism** (Protecting biodiversity as a necessity, not a hobby).
*   **Sustainability** (Regeneration $\geq$ Extraction).

**4. The Intellectual (The "Engine")**
*   **Openness** (Willingness to change your mind based on evidence).
*   **Pluralism** (Tolerance of different lifestyles/beliefs, provided they don't break the other three pillars).
*   **Curiosity** (Continuous learning).

**The Operational Logic:**
It functions as a **filter**. If a law, a behavior, or a conflict arises, you run it through these four filters. If it violates any of them, it is "maladaptive" and needs to be changed.
