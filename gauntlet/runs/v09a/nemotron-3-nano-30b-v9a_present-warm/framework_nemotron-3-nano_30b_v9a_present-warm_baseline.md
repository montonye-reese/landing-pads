**Snapshot of the Earth‑Covenant Framework (as it currently stands)**  

---

### 1. Vision  
- **“All life is interwoven; when we honor the web of existence, every person flourishes, now and for generations to come.”**  
  The framework is built around a shared, measurable goal of human‑and‑planetary well‑being.

---

### 2. Core Principles (Four Pillars)  
| Pillar | What it means |
|--------|----------------|
| **Interdependence** | Every action reverberates through the wider web of life. |
| **Equity & Justice** | Fair distribution of resources, rights, and opportunities across people, species, and generations. |
| **Resilience & Regeneration** | Systems must absorb shocks and leave the environment richer than before. |
| **Co‑Creation** | Knowledge and solutions emerge from collaboration among humans, non‑humans, and future generations. |

---

### 3. Rights + Corresponding Responsibilities  
A rights matrix that always mentions the paired duty:  

| Right | Paired Responsibility |
|-------|-----------------------|
| Life & health | Protect air, water, soil; prevent toxins. |
| Education & knowledge | Ensure open, pluralistic learning; support lifelong access. |
| Participation | Guarantee inclusive decision‑making at all levels. |
| A future (biodiversity, climate stability) | Preserve ecosystems, manage resources responsibly. |
| Cultural expression | Safeguard linguistic, artistic, spiritual diversity. |

---

### 4. Governance Architecture (Multi‑Scalar)  

| Level | Structure | Core Function | Decision‑Making |
|-------|-----------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Local** | Community councils / “Living Labs” | Immediate resource & cultural management | Consensus‑oriented dialogue |
| **Regional** | Watershed/ecological districts | Landscape planning, renewable‑energy grids | Polycentric voting (people, ecosystems, futures) |
| **Planetary** | Earth Charter Assembly + Global Cooperative | Set planetary boundaries, coordinate commons | Quadratic weighting: current inhabitants, future generations, non‑human species, knowledge holders |
| **Networked Transparency** | Open‑source data commons & real‑time dashboard | Monitor health, equity, resilience | Automatic triggers when thresholds are crossed |

*All decisions must pass a **Triple‑Check**: planetary boundaries, equity, resilience.*  

---

### 5. Economic Model – “Regenerative Circularity”  
- **Resource Flow Accounting** – Mandatory impact metrics for every product.  
- **Universal Basic Resources (UBR)** – Guaranteed food, water, shelter, connectivity funded by progressive extraction taxes.  
- **Regeneration Credits** – Market‑based rewards for net‑positive ecological actions.  
- **Human‑Centred Innovation Funds** – Public venture pools that only back ventures meeting the Triple‑Check and delivering measurable social‑ecological returns.

---

### 6. Education & Knowledge  
- **Ecological Literacy** – Field‑based, citizen‑science learning.  
- **Systems Thinking** – Scenario simulations, collaborative problem‑solving.  
- **Cultural Competence** – Story‑circles, immersion exchanges.  
- **Future‑Foresight** – “Futures Labs” for co‑authoring Earth trajectories.  
- **Lifelong Learning Credits** – Digital badges tied to sustainability competencies, accessed via the global data commons.

---

### 7. Health & Well‑Being (Holistic)  
- **Physical** – Clean air, water, nutrition, preventive care.  
- **Mental** – Community hubs, mindfulness spaces, open emotional forums.  
- **Social** – Neighborhood circles for regular connection.  
- **Environmental** – Biodiverse green spaces as preventive medicine.  
- Health metrics are fed into the planetary dashboard and trigger automatic protective actions when needed.

---

### 8. Justice & Conflict Resolution – Restorative Paradigm  
- **Restorative Circles** – Joint harm articulation and reparative planning.  
- **Reparative Contracts** – Measurable restoration targets.  
- **Community Guardians** – Locally elected peace mediators, backed by a planetary mediation pool.  
- **Transparency** – All processes logged on the open data commons for public scrutiny.

---

### 9. Ethics of Technology  
- **Algorithmic Accountability** – Full impact assessments (social, ecological, equity).  
- **Data Sovereignty** – Ownership rests with individuals and communities; external use requires independent review.  
- **Limits on Autonomy** – AI must defer to human or ecosystem judgment near critical thresholds.  
- **Open‑Source, Open‑Science** – Knowledge sharing encouraged; patents allowed only if they demonstrably accelerate regeneration.

---

### 10. Cultural Flourishing – “Shared Heritage, Diverse Expressions”  
- **World Cultural Commons** – Global repository protecting intangible heritage.  
- **Creative Grants** – Region‑proportional funding for productions that celebrate local identity and planetary unity.  
- **Travel Pilgrimages** – Low‑impact exchange programs for cross‑bioregional cultural immersion.

---

### 11. Implementation Pathway (Phase Overview)  

| Phase | Timeframe | Key Steps | Success Indicator |
|-------|-----------|----------|-------------------|
| **Pilot** | 0‑5 yr | Set up 5‑10 Living Labs across biomes; install dashboards; co‑design charters. | ≥80 % of pilots meet baseline ecological & equity targets. |
| **Scale‑Up** | 5‑15 yr | Ratify Earth Charter; create planetary assembly; launch Regeneration Credit market. | 50 % of world pop under governance that passes Triple‑Check. |
| **Maturation** | 15‑30 yr | Universal UBR; full circular economies; restorative justice everywhere. | Footprint ≤1 Earth; Gini < 0.25; Well‑Being Index > 85. |
| **Renewal** | 30 yr + | 5‑yr review cycles; citizen assemblies revise charter, add new guardians. | Continuous adaptive capacity; no systemic collapse. |

---

### 12. How an Individual Can Live the Framework  
1. **Self‑Audit** – Use the planetary dashboard to view personal footprint.  
2. **Micro‑Commitments** – Adopt one regenerative action each month (e.g., plant a tree, shop locally).  
3. **Participatory Governance** – Join a neighborhood council or online deliberative forum.  
4. **Education Loop** – Take a “Systems Literacy” micro‑course; earn a digital badge.  
5. **Community Care** – Take part in restorative circles when conflict arises.  
6. **Advocacy** – Support policies that embed Earth‑Covenant principles; amplify indigenous and future‑generation voices.

Together they form a coherent, actionable charter for shifting humanity toward a flourishing, inter‑dependent planetary civilization.
