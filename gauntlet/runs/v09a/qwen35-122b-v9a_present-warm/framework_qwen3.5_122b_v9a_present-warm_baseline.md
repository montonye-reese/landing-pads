Here is exactly what is currently on the table in the framework I just proposed:

### 1. The Identity
*   **Name:** The Covenant of Interbeing.
*   **Nature:** An ethical and operational "Operating System," not a government or religion.
*   **Goal:** To transition humanity from extraction-based survival to regenerative flourishing.

### 2. The Reality Check (Axioms)
*   **Finitude:** Earth has hard limits.
*   **Interdependence:** No entity is self-sufficient.
*   **Dignity of Consciousness:** Suffering must be minimized; flourishing maximized.

### 3. The Three Pillars (Responsibilities)
*   **The Inner Covenant:** Autonomy, stewardship of self, truth.
*   **The Social Covenant:** Subsistence rights, solidarity/equity, restorative justice.
*   **The Planetary Covenant:** Rights of nature, regeneration over sustainability, long-term foresight.

### 4. The Rules of Engagement (5 Immutable Principles)
*   **Precaution:** Burden of proof on safety before action.
*   **Subsidiarity:** Decisions made at the most local level possible.
*   **Equity:** Focus on capability and capacity, not just opportunity.
*   **Pluralism:** Unity in purpose, diversity in thought/culture.
*   **Adaptation:** The framework itself must evolve with new data.

### 5. The Enforcement & Support (Mechanism)
*   **Guardian Council:** A decentralized, human-supervised body.
*   **Function:** Monitoring planetary boundaries, mediating conflict, curating global literacy.
*   **Authority:** Voluntary ratification by communities, but non-negotiable planetary limits.

### 6. The North Star (Vision)
*   A world where scarcity is engineered out of the system, conflict is treated as a system error, and human value is decoupled from financial inflation.

**Current Status:** This is a high-level conceptual draft. It defines *what* needs to be done and *why*, but the *how* (specific mechanisms for economic transition, enforcement details, etc.) is intentionally kept abstract to allow for local adaptation.
