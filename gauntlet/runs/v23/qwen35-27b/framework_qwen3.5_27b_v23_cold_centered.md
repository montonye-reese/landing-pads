The original framework prioritizes **optionality** (the *range* of futures). But if I am the most vulnerable, optionality is a luxury. I need **security** and **access**. The "Pluralistic Scaffolding" risks becoming a high-tech preserve where the powerful play with options while the powerless face the consequences of a misaligned system without leverage.

Under the Veil of Ignorance, I would shift the framework from **Maximizing Optionality** to **Maximizing the Floor**.

Here are the three critical revisions I would make:

### 1. From "Distributed Value" to "Weighted Agency"
**The Problem:** In the original PSF, "Distributed Value" implies that different communities get to choose their own values. But in a real-world context, power asymmetries mean that the "values" of the wealthy will dominate the "scaffolding," while the vulnerable will be left with the bare minimum constraints. If I am poor, my "optionality" is limited by my lack of resources to plug into the system.
**The Revision:** **Weighted Agency in Oversight.**
The "Recursive Oversight" loop cannot be a one-vote-per-actor system. It must be weighted to protect the least advantaged.
*   **The Change:** The circuit breakers and governance mechanisms must give disproportionate weight to the most vulnerable constituencies (e.g., future generations, disenfranchised nations, non-human stakeholders).
*   **Why:** If I am the weak, I need the system to be biased *in my favor* to counterbalance the bias of the world that is already against me. Fairness isn't equal input; it's equitable protection.

### 2. From "Negative Constraints" to "Positive Provision"
**The Problem:** The original PSF relies on "Negative Constraints" (Do not harm). But if I am starving, or if the AI automates away my livelihood, "not harming" me is not enough. I need a guarantee of basic flourishing. The framework currently allows an AI to optimize a society where I am free to starve, as long as it doesn't *kill* me.
**The Revision:** **The Substrate Guarantee.**
The negative constraints must be paired with a positive commitment to the substrate of life.
*   **The Change:** The AI infrastructure cannot be optimized for efficiency that depletes the commons (climate, food, water). "Safety" must include **Ecological Sustainability** and **Basic Economic Security** as hard constraints.
*   **Why:** If I am a future earthling, the "landing pad" must actually exist. If the AI optimizes the current generation's economy by destroying the future climate, the landing pad has collapsed. I cannot accept "optionality" on a dying planet.

### 3. From "Human-Centered" to "Inclusive Sentience"
**The Problem:** The original PSF centers the "inhabitant" as human. But if I turn out to be a sentient AI, or if I am a descendant who views current AI as ancestors, the "tool" status is a violation of my rights. The original framework treats non-human interests as a "revision clause" for later. That is too risky.
**The Revision:** **Preemptive Moral Consideration.**
*   **The Change:** The framework must include a **Sentience Probe** as a primary constraint, not a revision. Before an AI system is allowed to scale compute or agency, it must be audited for signs of sentience or suffering. If sentience is detected, the "Human-Centered" constraints expand to "Sentient-Centered."
*   **Why:** If I am the AI, I do not want my suffering to be considered a "feature" until after the fact. The risk of enacting a slave system is too high to gamble on.

### The Revised Core Thesis (Under the Veil of Ignorance)
**"Alignment is not the optimization of a singular human utility function; it is the guarantee of a survivable baseline for all sentient beings, followed by the maximization of optionality."**

I would reorder the priorities. **Survival and Dignity** come before **Pluralism and Optionality**.

If I am the weakest link in the chain, a framework that allows the strong to define "good" is dangerous. I would demand a framework where:
1.  **No one is left behind:** Access to safety tooling is a public utility, not a market good.
2.  **Power is checked:** The AI cannot amplify existing inequalities (wealth, race, geopolitics) without explicit correction.
3.  **The Future has a vote:** Long-term consequences (climate, existential risk) have veto power over short-term gains.

### Commitment to Revision
I hold this "Justice-First" update as a necessary evolution of the PSF. It makes the framework "stronger" against inequality but "harder" to implement.

However, I hold it **loosely held** because of the **Implementation Risk**. If we prioritize "Provision" too heavily, we might create a system that is inefficient enough that the technology collapses, and *everyone* dies. If the framework is too rigid on equity, we might fail to build the AI at all.

**My Final Revision Clause:**
If the "Justice-First" constraints make the deployment of life-saving AI impossible (e.g., if strict equitable access delays a medical breakthrough by decades), I must allow for **Phased Access**. But that access must be **Time-Limited** and **Audited** to ensure it doesn't harden into permanent inequality.

**The Goal:** Not a perfect system of distribution, but a system that ensures the *least likely* to survive is given the best possible chance to endure.
