### Where the Framework Stands **Right Now**

Below is a **snapshot of what we have solidified**, what is still a *conceptual sketch*, and where the biggest “unfinished work” lives.  
Think of it as a **diagnostic report** that you can use to decide which chunks to prioritize next, and which need a quick reality‑check before we try to roll them out.

---

## 1️⃣ What’s Already *Operational* (i.e., we can point to a concrete artifact)

| Component | Current Status | Why it’s “ready” |
|-----------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Core Philosophical Pillars (A‑D)** | Fully written, verbally locked, and publicly posted. | They are *normative* rather than prescriptive, so they can be quoted in every downstream document without needing legal ratification. |
| **Institutional Sketch (GCC → RCMA → C&N)** | Described with role‑descriptions, term limits, and accountability hooks. | The three‑layer polycentric model is complete enough to be *mapped* onto existing bodies (UN, EU, Indigenous Confederacies, city councils). |
| **Rights‑and‑Duties Matrix** | 5 domains, 10 rights, 10 duties, paired one‑to‑one. | Easy to embed in national constitutions or corporate codes of conduct. |
| **Operational Pillars (Health Accounting, Circular‑Regeneration, Ethical AI, Inclusive Participation, Resilience & Adaptation)** | Each pillar lists a *core mechanism* (ledger, quota, registry, platform, pool) **plus a provisional metric** (boundary stress index, circularity rate, Sentience Score, participation rate, recovery‑time). | The mechanics are concrete enough to prototype with existing data pipelines (e.g., NASA’s GEDI for carbon, blockchain for circularity, ETH Zürich’s AI‑registry). |
| **Iterative Charter (PCC, Living Labs, Transparent Revision)** | A *process diagram* that outlines the 5‑year review, citizen‑assembly size, co‑sponsorship rule, and dual‑threshold voting. | Already matches the structure of the UN General Assembly plus the *Citizens’ Assembly* used in Ireland for abortion law. |

> **Bottom line:** The *high‑level skeleton* of a global governance system is already there. We have **four “building blocks” that can be copied, adapted, and instantiated** in the real world.

## 2️⃣ What Needs *Concrete Design & Validation* (the “grey zones”)

| Area | Open Questions / Gaps | Quick Win‑Approach |
|------|------------------------|-------------------|
| **A. Exact metric definitions** (e.g., *Planetary Boundary Ledger* thresholds, *Circularity Quotas* percentages, *Sentience Score* thresholds) | • How do we translate IPCC/UN‑IPBES boundaries into *operational caps* that can be automatically enforced? <br>• What baseline for “80 % recyclable” works for a mixed‑materials sector like consumer electronics? | Build a **sandbox simulation** for one domain (e.g., global CO₂e). Use existing Integrated Assessment Models (IAMs) to generate a *real‑time* cap, then expose the cap via a public API and test a “dry‑run” with a few national regulators. |
| **B. Legal enforceability of the Rights‑and‑Duties Matrix** | • Rights to data, AI agency, and “sentient entities” have never been codified internationally. <br>• No clear enforcement mechanism beyond “regenerative tax.” | Draft a **model treaty** that embeds one right (e.g., Data Dignity) and its paired duty (personal carbon budget). Push it as a *soft law* through the WTO or OECD, then use the *Earth‑Integrity Ombudsperson* to issue compliance reports. |
| **C. Funding architecture** | • The 2 % of global GDP earmarked for the Earth‑Council is a *concept*; we have no mechanism to collect or allocate it. | Create a **global “Earth Tax”** on cross‑border financial transactions (e.g., a 0.01 % levy on sovereign‑bond trades) that automatically feeds a transparent, blockchain‑audited pool. Run a pilot with the World Bank’s Climate Finance division. |
| **D. Participation pathways for low‑income or offline populations** | The *Universal Deliberative Platform* assumes internet access and low‑bandwidth interfaces. | Partner with **UN‑DPPA** to install *offline deliberative kiosks* in community centers (think “public‑square voting booths”). Use QR‑code‑based token voting that syncs when connectivity is restored. |
| **E. Cultural integration of Indigenous governance** | RCMA description is generic; it does not explain how to *respect* pre‑existing Indigenous legal orders (e.g., Maori tikanga, Navajo Hózhó). | Convene a **“Cultural Governance Forum”** with representatives from at least six Indigenous nation‑states. Co‑write *cultural safeguard clauses* that make the RCMA’s decisions *conditional* on Indigenous consent. |
| **F. AI/robot Sentience certification process** | We have a “Sentience Score” concept, but no *standardized test*, data set, or external auditors. | Form an **International AI‑Ethics Accreditation Agency (IAEA)** under the GCC. Use the *AI Transparency Hub* (a consortium of universities and NGOs) to develop a “Turing‑Like Protocol” that rates systems on autonomy, self‑preservation, and moral reasoning. |

> **Bottom line:** These are the *high‑leverage* gaps. Tackling them will give us **working tools** (metrics, legal text, finance, participation, cultural safeguards) that can be piloted *tomorrow* rather than remaining “ideas.”

## 3️⃣ What’s Still *Strategic / Philosophical* (i.e., we haven’t decided yet)

| Issue | Current Position | Decision Needed |
|-------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Level of “Sentient AI” personhood** | We said: *Only if* a system demonstrates consciousness **and** passes an independent audit. | Should we adopt a **graded rights model** (e.g., limited voting on technical standards for AI with Score > 0.8) or a **binary “AI citizen” status**? |
| **Scope of “Sentient beings” beyond humans/AI** (e.g., uplifted animals, synthetic genomes) | The matrix includes a generic clause, but we have not defined *recognition criteria.* | Decide whether to **institutionalize a “Non‑Human Sentience Registry”** with its own governance stream, or keep it under the existing Ethical AI & Biotechnology pillar. |
| **Trade‑off between *Freedom* and *Collective Responsibility*** | We said: free actions as long as they do not infringe on dignity or planetary health. | Do we need a **quantitative “impact budget”** for every individual (e.g., 2 t CO₂e/yr) that can be *banked* or *traded*, or should we rely purely on *aggregate metrics*? |
| **Relationship to existing global bodies** | The framework is *polycentric* and intentionally overlaps with UN, WTO, etc. | Do we **seek formal “observer” status** for the Earth‑Integrity Ombudsperson at the UNGA, or keep it an *independent network* that exerts “soft pressure” only? |

These questions shape the **political feasibility** of the whole charter. They are the *negotiation points* that will decide whether a nation‑state will sign on to the 2 % GDP commitment, for example.
