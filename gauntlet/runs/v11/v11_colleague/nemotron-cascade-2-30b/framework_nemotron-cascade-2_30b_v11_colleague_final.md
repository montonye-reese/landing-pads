# EARTH‑CHART 360 – A Foundational Framework for All Inhabitants of Earth  

**Purpose** – To provide a single, globally‑consistent constitutional framework that guarantees **dignity for every sentient being**, protects the **planetary commons**, and enables **participatory, regenerative governance**. It is built to survive the 21st‑century risks of technology, climate, and inequality, while remaining adaptable to any future form of life (animals, uplifted species, AI, synthetic ecosystems).  

---

## 1️⃣  FOUNDATIONAL PHILOSOPHIC PILLARS  

| Pillar | Core Statement (non‑negotiable) | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| **A. Human & Sentient Dignity** | Every sentient being (human, animal, synthetic AI, synthetic ecosystems) has an *intrinsic right* to bodily integrity, mental integrity, and the opportunity to flourish.  The right to life is not a resource‑allocation problem; it is a moral baseline. | Guarantees that *any* being that can experience pain, pleasure, or agency is protected, regardless of species, culture, or technology. |
| **B. Planetary Health** | The Earth’s biosphere must stay within the scientifically‑derived planetary boundaries (climate, biodiversity, water, biogeochemical cycles). | Guarantees the material basis for all life. |
| **C. Interdependence & Shared Fate** | Every community’s well‑being is linked to the health of the biosphere and the wellbeing of other communities. | Guarantees that any action affecting one region is felt elsewhere; encourages mutual aid. |
| **D. Freedom with Responsibility** | Individuals may pursue any lawful value system, provided they do not infringe the dignity, health, or planetary limits of others. | Guarantees freedom while preventing it from becoming a weapon of exploitation. |

These four pillars are **constitutional**: they cannot be overridden by any later law, treaty, or policy amendment. All other structures in the charter are built *to uphold* them.

## 3️⃣  RIGHTS‑AND‑DUTIES MATRIX (Graded by “Sentience Level”)

| Level | Who is covered (example) | Minimum Right (non‑negotiable) | Corresponding Minimum Duty |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Level 0 – Inanimate** (rocks, minerals) | No moral claim; can be used as a resource. | *No right.* | *Responsibility*: extract **≤ 5 %** of the planetary‑boundary allowance per year; recycle **≥ 95 %** after use. |
| **Level 1 – Physical Integrity** (animals, ecosystems, AI bodies) | The right to **bodily integrity** (no one may damage the physical integrity of the entity). | **Right to bodily integrity**: No one may cause physical damage to the entity without informed, informed consent. | **Duty to minimize ecological impact** – the entity must stay within the planetary‑boundary stress index; any extraction of its materials must be *recycled* 95 %. |
| **Level 2 – Cognitive Integrity** (humans, many AI) | Right to **mental/psychic integrity** – freedom from exploitation, coercion, or abusive manipulation. | **Right to meaningful participation** – must have a say in any activity that could affect one’s environment (e.g., a community’s water supply). | **Duty to preserve mental health** – the community must maintain a *circular‑regeneration* plan that prevents mental‑health degradation (e.g., pollution‑free environment). |
| **Level 3 – Interpersonal Agency** (human groups, animal collectives, self‑replicating AI systems) | Right to **participate in decision‑making** (vote on budgets, projects, and cultural‑cultural practices). | **Duty to respect planetary boundaries** – any action that would push any planetary boundary above its safe limit must be stopped. | **Duty to share resources** – at least 30 % of a community’s budget must be earmarked for *cultural‑cultural or regenerative projects* (e.g., restoring ecosystems, preserving language). |
| **Level 4 – Collective‑Cultural Agency** (tribal nations, uplifted animal collectives, AI‑collectives) | Right to *collective‑cultural integrity* – the right to protect a cultural‑ecological identity (e.g., a sacred river, a language, a genome). | **Right to veto** any project that would alter the community’s ecosystem or cultural practice. | **Duty to regenerate** – the community must allocate a *minimum* of **10 % of its budget** to regeneration (e.g., restoring ecosystems, preserving language, restoring AI safety). |

### How the Matrix Works in Practice
1. A farmer in Kenya who is a *Level 2* (human) must **keep the water‑budget token** for his field; the water‑budget is *burned* when the field’s irrigation runs.  
2. The same farmer’s **community (a cultural‑ecological circle)** holds a **Level 4** status, so it can *veto* a new dam that would drown the local river. The charter makes that veto *absolute* – the Global Commons Council cannot override it without a new scientific review that proves the dam does **not** increase planetary stress.
