### 🔒 **The Anti-Capture Immune System (Why This Won’t Be Co-Opted)**  
Every mechanism has **self-policing features** that make exploitation *more costly* than compliance:  
- **EDTs automatically trigger**: If a corporation tries to lobby against water derivative taxes, the *revenue loss* directly harms the LSTs they depend on for social license—making opposition self-defeating.  
- **BSCs have recall power**: Local stewards can vote out captured reps *mid-term*—no waiting for elections.  
- **Knowledge Sovereignty includes methodology veto**: Communities can reject flawed science (e.g., a dam’s "environmental impact statement" ignoring Indigenous flood records).  
- **Pillar 0 veto is project-level**: No "we’ll study it while building"—if a mine threatens a sacred spring, it stops *before* groundbreaking.

### 💡 **What’s *Not* in the Framework (And Why It Matters)**  
- **No GDP growth targets**: Replaced with *ecological deficit/surplus* accounting and *essentials equity gaps*.  
- **No voluntary pledges**: All mechanisms are *pre*-enforced (screening, vetoes, automatic transfers).  
- **No "stakeholder engagement" as an end goal**: Power shifts are *structural*, not ceremonial.  
- **No techno-optimism loopholes**: Innovation must *reduce* boundary pressure—not just offset it (e.g., solar farms must pass Pillar 0/P1/P2 screening *first*).
