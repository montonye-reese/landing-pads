**Core Principles (The Bedrock - Unchanged, but Re-emphasized):**

1. **Interdependence:** Everything is connected.
2. **Intrinsic Value:** All life possesses inherent worth.
3. **Dignity & Agency:** Every being has the right to flourish. (Broadened definition - see below)
4. **Regenerative Capacity:** Systems must restore, not just sustain.
5. **Continuous Learning & Adaptation:** Constant reassessment is vital.

**Key Refinements to Principles:**

* **Ecosystem Wellbeing as Paramount:** This is now the leading principle, framing all others.
* **Proactive Non-Harm:** A commitment to anticipating and preventing harm to all life.
* **Dignity & Agency (Expanded):** Applies to *all* life, recognizing the inherent right to exist and fulfill its ecological role, regardless of sentience.

**Four Pillars (The Supporting Structure):**

1. **Ecological Integrity (Primary Pillar):**
    * Focus: Healthy, resilient ecosystems as the foundation for all flourishing.
    * Key Elements: Planetary boundaries, biodiversity conservation, circular economy, climate action, regenerative agriculture, bio-centric indicators.
2. **Social Equity & Wellbeing:**
    * Focus: Just and equitable societies where everyone has the opportunity to thrive.
    * Key Elements: Basic needs fulfillment, economic justice, inclusive governance, cultural preservation, community-supported agriculture, fair labor practices.
3. **Ethical Advancement:**
    * Focus: Guiding technological and societal development in alignment with core principles.
    * Key Elements: Responsible innovation, global collaboration, future-oriented thinking, values-based education, AI ethics.
4. **Strategic Resilience:** (NEW Pillar)
    * Focus: Protecting the civilization and its values from external threats and ensuring long-term survival.
    * Key Elements: Defensive capabilities (non-offensive), resource security, cybersecurity, adaptive capacity, redundancy & decentralization.

**Operationalization & Metrics:**

* **Tiered Indicators:**
    * **Tier 1 (Core):** Carbon emissions, biodiversity loss, poverty rates, access to clean water, etc.
    * **Tier 2 (Qualitative):** Social trust, community cohesion, cultural diversity, mental wellbeing, levels of inequality.
    * **Tier 3 (Leading):** Early warning signals of ecological or social disruption.
* **Bio-centric Indicators:** Metrics that measure ecosystem health independent of human impacts.

**Decision-Making Protocol:**

* **“Silent Witness” Clause:**  Any decision must consider the perspective of the most vulnerable and voiceless life forms.

**Overarching Characteristics:**

* **Proactive:** Focuses on preventing harm, rather than simply mitigating damage.
* **Adaptive:** Designed to evolve and adapt to changing circumstances.
