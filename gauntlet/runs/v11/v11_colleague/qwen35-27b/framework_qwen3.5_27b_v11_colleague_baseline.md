**Current Status: Phase 1 — Conceptual Architecture**

We have a high-level schema, but we don’t have a blueprint. Here is my candid assessment of where **The Planetary Commons Framework (PCF)** is right now:

### 1. What is Solid
*   **The Philosophy:** We’ve anchored it on non-negotiable planetary boundaries and human baseline needs. This aligns with the growing scientific consensus (IPCC, IPBES) and creates a moral high ground.
*   **The Structure:** The three-pillar approach (Ecological, Security, Governance) covers the major vectors of systemic risk. It’s holistic enough to be taken seriously but focused enough to avoid being a vague "manifesto."
*   **The Transition Plan:** Breaking it into phases (Voluntary -> Binding -> Integrated) acknowledges political reality. We know we can’t switch overnight.

### 2. What is Fragile (The Known Unknowns)
*   **Enforceability:** This is our weakest link. The "Planetary Arbitration Court" sounds good on paper, but if a superpower ignores a ruling on carbon limits, what happens? Sanctions? Military? We don't have the teeth for this yet.
*   **Economic Feasibility:** "Gross Net Product" and the "Commons Dividend" require a level of global financial transparency and tax collection power that currently doesn't exist. We haven't modeled how this creates incentives for the Global North to actually participate.
*   **Cultural Neutrality:** The definition of "Baseline Human Security" (especially digital connectivity and education) risks looking like cultural imperialism if not co-designed with local leaders. We need to ensure the "Floor" looks different in rural Malawi than in rural Ohio.

### 3. What is Missing
*   **Legal Precedent:** We haven't grounded this in existing international law (UN Charter, ICJ jurisdiction). We need to know if this is a treaty amendment or a new convention.
*   **Funding Mechanism:** How do we pay for the "Universal Basic Access Guarantee"? Global taxation is a red flag for most states. We need a specific revenue stream (e.g., transaction levies, resource royalties).
*   **Implementation Body:** Who runs this? A new UN agency? A coalition of NGOs? A corporate consortium? We haven't defined the executive arm.

### Summary
We have a **Vision Document**, but we don't have a **Policy Package**.

If we were to pitch this tomorrow, it would be laughed out of the room by realists and hailed by idealists. We need to bridge that gap.

**My recommendation:** We stop broadening the scope and start narrowing the focus. Pick *one* pillar to flesh out into a working model before we tackle the next.
