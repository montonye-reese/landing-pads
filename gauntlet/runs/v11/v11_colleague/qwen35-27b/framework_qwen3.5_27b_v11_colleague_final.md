# The Planetary Commons Framework (PCF)
**Version 3.0 — Final Architecture**

## I. PREAMBLE: The Mandate

We recognize that the Earth is a single, interdependent system. No nation, corporation, or individual can thrive while the biosphere collapses. No human can claim dignity while another suffers preventable harm. No technology can progress while the cost is paid in blood, land, and life.

This framework is not a treaty to regulate nations. It is a **Constitution for Civilizational Survival**.

It is founded on four principles:

1.  **Sentience is the Boundary:** The capacity to suffer is the only valid criterion for moral consideration. Human and non-human life are both protected.
2.  **Security is the Floor:** Every inhabitant has an inalienable right to survival, dignity, and participation.
3.  **Power is the Check:** No institution, state, or corporation can control the Commons without accountability.
4.  **Conscience is the Guard:** Compliance must not crush the human spirit. The right to dissent is protected.

---

## II. PILLAR ONE: ECOLOGICAL INTEGRITY

### A. The Hard Limits
*   **Planetary Boundaries:** Carbon, nitrogen, water, biodiversity, and land use are capped by the physical limits of Earth systems. These limits are non-negotiable.
*   **Sentience Threshold:** Industrial animal agriculture, wildlife exploitation, and habitat destruction are prohibited. This protects the non-human beings whose suffering enables human consumption.
*   **Enforcement:** The **Planetary Resource Ledger** tracks resource throughput in real-time. All actors (states, corporations, individuals) must account for their resource use.

### B. The Market Signal
*   **Carbon & Resource Pricing:** Instead of hard bans, the framework enforces **Price Floors** on carbon and resource use. Actors may exceed the baseline only by paying a premium into the **Commons Dividend Fund**.
*   **Technology Efficiency:** The **Global Innovation Fund** subsidizes technologies that reduce resource intensity. The goal is to decouple prosperity from extraction.

### C. The Just Transition
*   **Conversion Grants:** Any industry transitioning from non-compliant practices (e.g., livestock to plant-based) receives targeted funding. No sector is criminalized without an economic lifeline.
*   **Worker Support:** Displaced workers receive living-wage retraining, universal basic income supplements, and priority access to the Global Innovation Fund.

---

## III. PILLAR TWO: BASELINE HUMAN SECURITY

### A. The Floor (Tier 1 Essentials)
*   **Guaranteed Access:** Every inhabitant is guaranteed access to clean water, nutrition, primary education, basic healthcare, and digital connectivity.
*   **Labor Dignity:** "Nutrition" and "Economic Activity" include the right to **Collective Bargaining**, **Living Wages**, and **Safe Working Conditions**. Exploitative labor (child labor, wage theft, unsafe conditions) is prohibited.
*   **Humanitarian Access:** **Tier 1 Essentials cannot be embargoed.** Even non-compliant states must receive food, medicine, and water. The "Pariah State" risk is eliminated.

### B. The Essential Technology Protocol (ETP)
*   **Tier 1 (Survival):** Pharmaceuticals, clean water tech, renewable energy infrastructure, climate adaptation tools. **Open Source, Royalty-Free.**
*   **Tier 2 (Economic):** Software, industrial machinery, luxury goods. Standard IP protections apply.
*   **Tier 0 (Infrastructure):** **AI, Compute, and Chip Architecture** are classified as "Critical Infrastructure." Access for Commons-related tasks (climate modeling, medical discovery) is guaranteed at cost. Hardware remains proprietary, but usage for public good is subsidized.

### C. The Incentive Model
*   **Bounties over Patents:** Innovation for Tier 1 is funded by **Pre-Market Bounties** (Global Innovation Fund), not patent monopolies. R&D is rewarded with guaranteed payout; access is guaranteed to all.
*   **Innovation Dividend:** Core breakthrough technology retains IP protection for 15 years to incentivize discovery. After that, it enters the Commons.

---

## IV. PILLAR THREE: EQUITABLE GOVERNANCE

### A. Multi-Scalar Accreditation
Power is distributed across four levels:
1.  **Nation-States:** Signatory power for treaties, sovereignty over internal affairs.
2.  **Indigenous Territories:** Binding veto power over land/resource use within their jurisdictions.
3.  **Municipalities:** Direct accreditation to access the Global Innovation Fund, bypassing hostile central governments.
4.  **Labor Representatives:** 25% voting share in the **Ethics Council**. Workers are not subjects; they are partners.

### B. The Chamber of Inhabitants
*   **Representation:** Weighted by population, not wealth. Prevents small nations from dictating planetary terms.
*   **Indigenous & Labor Seats:** Permanent seats for Indigenous stewards and worker representatives.
*   **Citizen Juries:** Randomly selected citizens (2-year terms) serve on enforcement panels to prevent elite capture.

### C. The Ethics of Access Council
*   **Composition:** 50% Independent Scientists, 25% Human Rights Reps, 25% Civil Society.
*   **Authority:** Maintains the **Essential Registry** (what qualifies as Tier 1 vs. Tier 2).
*   **Anti-Capture:** No private entity may fund enforcement activities. All Council decisions are publicly auditable via blockchain.

---

## V. PILLAR FOUR: ENFORCEMENT & JUSTICE

### A. The Anti-Capture Protocol
*   **Decentralized Verification:** The **Planetary Resource Ledger** is a publicly auditable blockchain. No single body owns the data; all actors can verify compliance.
*   **No Private Funding:** Enforcement is funded by **Carbon Dividends** (tax on extraction), not corporate donations.
*   **Whistleblower Protection:** Anyone who exposes non-compliance is protected. The framework protects truth-tellers.

### B. The Restorative Justice Protocol
*   **Corrective Action Plans:** Non-compliance triggers a 5-year transition period with funding, not immediate bans.
*   **Proximity in Justice:** Cases are judged by regional representatives close to the affected community.
*   **Re-Entry:** No permanent exclusion. Penalties are paid to the harmed ecosystem or population. The goal is restoration, not punishment.

### C. The Right to Dissent
*   **Conscience Protection:** Individuals and communities may express moral objection to the framework without sanctions, provided they do not cause structural harm.
*   **No Slogan Compliance:** Participation must be voluntary, not coerced. The framework protects the human spirit, not just the human body.

### D. The Mutual Defense Pact
*   **Collective Security:** If any PCF member is attacked (militarily or economically) for compliance, all members commit to economic and security support.
*   **Shared Defense Tech:** Military R&D is pooled. No member can outgun the bloc.

## VI. ECONOMIC TRANSITION

### A. The Commons Dividend
*   **Funding Source:** 1% levy on global carbon trade + cross-border financial transactions + resource royalties.
*   **Distribution:** Pooled to redistribute wealth to the Global South, fund the Just Transition, and support Tier 1 essentials.
*   **Audit:** All dividend disbursements are publicly tracked.

### B. Gross Net Product (GNP)
*   **Metric:** Deducts environmental degradation and social inequality costs from growth.
*   **Incentive:** Nations are measured by **Redundancy**, not **Efficiency**. Survival > Speed.

### C. The Compute Commons
*   **Infrastructure Access:** Guaranteed access to AI/compute for climate modeling, medical discovery, and public infrastructure.
*   **Energy Standard:** All training/deployment must meet energy efficiency standards. No unbounded AI expansion.

## VII. TRANSITION & PHASING

### A. Phase 1: Voluntary (Years 0–5)
*   Focus: **Baseline Human Security**.
*   Mechanism: NGOs, Municipalities, and Indigenous groups access the Global Innovation Fund directly.
*   Goal: Prove the model works at the ground level.

### B. Phase 2: Binding (Years 5–15)
*   Focus: **Ecological Integrity**.
*   Mechanism: Carbon/Resource Ledgers become legally binding. Trade tariffs linked to compliance.
*   Goal: Scale the system to the national level.

### C. Phase 3: Integrated (Years 15+)
*   Focus: **Full Governance**.
*   Mechanism: Chamber of Inhabitants integrated into UN system. Dual Chamber Assembly operational.
*   Goal: Make the PCF the default operating system for civilization.

## VIII. CORE TENSIONS & SAFEGUARDS

### A. The Freedom/Security Balance
*   **Tension:** We cannot guarantee survival without some compliance, but compliance cannot crush dissent.
*   **Safeguard:** **Right to Dissent** (Havel). Compliance is required for *access*, but moral objection is protected.

### B. The Growth/Protection Balance
*   **Tension:** We need innovation to solve problems, but innovation must not violate boundaries.
*   **Safeguard:** **Innovation Dividend** (Cowen). IP protection for breakthroughs, but open access for survival tech.

### C. The Power/Accountability Balance
*   **Tension:** We need enforcement, but enforcement can become corrupt.
*   **Safeguard:** **Anti-Capture Protocol** (Butler). Public ledgers, citizen juries, no private enforcement funding.

### D. The Justice/Mercy Balance
*   **Tension:** We need to punish harm, but we cannot permanently exclude people.
*   **Safeguard:** **Restorative Justice** (Stevenson). Corrective action over punishment. Re-entry over exile.

### E. The Worker/Producer Balance
*   **Tension:** We need to transition industries, but we cannot punish workers.
*   **Safeguard:** **Just Transition** (Reese/Huerta). Funded exit, living wages, collective bargaining.
