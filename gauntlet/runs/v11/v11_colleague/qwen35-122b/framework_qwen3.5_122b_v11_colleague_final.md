# The Planetary Covenant for Resilience & Flourishing (v3.3)
### **A Constitution for Civilizational Survival**

## **Preamble**
We, the inhabitants of Earth, acknowledge that our civilization stands at a threshold. The systems of the past—built on extraction, inequality, and short-term gain—have brought us to the edge of collapse. We recognize that survival is not merely a technical challenge but a moral imperative.

We do not build this framework to constrain the future, but to liberate it. We acknowledge the debt of the past. We respect the rights of the voiceless. We trust in the capacity of the human spirit to choose dignity over domination.

This Covenant is not a treaty of the powerful. It is a covenant of the common. It binds us not by force, but by shared interest and shared conscience. It is designed to ensure that **no human is left behind, no species is erased without cause, and no generation inherits a broken world.**

---

## **Article I: Foundational Principles**
**1. Planetary Integrity (The Boundary)**
Human activity must remain within the biophysical boundaries of the Earth system. The atmosphere, oceans, freshwater, and biodiversity are not resources to be exhausted, but life-support systems to be maintained.
*   *Constraint:* Irreversible ecological tipping points are prohibited.
*   *Mechanism:* Resource caps based on regenerative capacity, not consumption demand.

**2. Universal Dignity (The Floor)**
Every human being possesses inherent worth. Survival essentials—food, water, energy, shelter, healthcare, connectivity, and care—are rights, not commodities.
*   *Constraint:* No human may be excluded from basic survival due to compliance failure, poverty, or geography.
*   *Mechanism:* Guaranteed Universal Basic Assets (UBA) for all Earthlings.

**3. Relational Equity (The Power)**
Power and resources must be distributed to prevent concentration. Justice requires acknowledging historical harm and repairing structural inequity.
*   *Constraint:* Systemic exploitation based on race, geography, or gender is prohibited.
*   *Mechanism:* Historical Liability Taxes fund the Global Transition; decision-making requires Proximity Quotas.

**4. Epistemic Humility (The Truth)**
Decisions must be grounded in the best available evidence, scientific consensus, and diverse knowledge systems. We acknowledge the limits of what we know.
*   *Constraint:* No single authority holds a monopoly on truth.
*   *Mechanism:* Pluralistic Truth Mechanisms integrating science, indigenous knowledge, and local wisdom.

**5. Intergenerational Stewardship (The Time)**
Current generations hold the system in trust for future generations. We cannot mortgage their survival for our convenience.
*   *Constraint:* Long-term risks (carbon debt, nuclear waste, biodiversity loss) are priced into current decisions.
*   *Mechanism:* Future Generations Representative with veto power over existential risks.

**6. Radical Pluralism & Intrinsic Value (The Scope)**
The value of a being is not determined by its utility to humans. Sentient life and ecosystems possess intrinsic rights. Human diversity of culture, thought, and being is essential for resilience.
*   *Constraint:* Exploitation of sentient beings and ecosystems is prohibited unless necessary for survival where no alternative exists.
*   *Mechanism:* Legal standing for Nature; Guardianship for Sentient Beings; Right to Subsistence Exit.

---

## **Article II: The Planetary Ledger Economy (PLE)**
**1. True Cost & Subsidy Reallocation**
*   **True Cost Pricing:** All transactions reflect ecological and social liability. Externalities are internalized at the source.
*   **Subsidy Shift:** All public subsidies supporting extraction, pollution, or animal agriculture are redirected toward regeneration, plant-based infrastructure, and renewable energy.
*   **Historical Liability Tax:** Wealth generated from historical extraction (colonialism, fossil fuels) is taxed to fund the Transition.

**2. Circular & Regenerative Design**
*   **Material Ownership:** Manufacturers retain ownership of materials in products; "waste" is a design failure.
*   **Plan for Obsolescence:** Planned obsolescence is prohibited. Durability and repairability are mandated.

**3. Labor Sovereignty & Ownership**
*   **Worker Ownership:** New critical infrastructure (energy, food, transport) must be co-owned by the workers (Minimum 51% cooperative/public trust).
*   **Care Infrastructure:** Care work (human and ecological) is valued and credited in the ledger alongside labor and capital.
*   **Just Transition:** Workers in declining sectors receive guaranteed income, retraining, and ownership stakes in the new economy.

**4. Technology as Infrastructure**
*   **Compute & Data:** Advanced AI and clean energy infrastructure are treated as public utilities. Access is guaranteed; pricing is based on efficiency, not profit maximization.
*   **Open-Source Verification:** Compliance data must be verifiable via decentralized, open-source protocols (zero-knowledge proofs), preventing central surveillance.

---

## **Article III: Governance Architecture**
**1. Polycentric Coordination**
*   **Local Autonomy:** Decisions are made at the most local level possible. Communities have the right to opt out of higher-level regulations if they can prove self-sufficiency and adherence to ecological baselines.
*   **Global Coordination:** Binding agreements exist only for cross-border issues (climate, oceans, pandemics, AI safety).

**2. Proximity & Representation**
*   **Proximity Quota:** Decision-making bodies must include 50% representatives from the communities most impacted by the decisions.
*   **Guardianship Councils:** Independent bodies representing Nature and Vulnerable Populations hold veto power over projects that violate Red Lines.

**3. Restorative Compliance**
*   **First Response:** Breaches of Red Lines trigger Support and Repair mechanisms first. Sanctions or exclusion are a last resort.
*   **Historical Context:** Compliance assessments must account for historical exploitation and capacity constraints.

**4. Right to Conscience & Dissent**
*   **Protected Dissent:** Individuals may publicly dispute Red Lines or verify data without penalty, provided they do not cause immediate irreversible harm.
*   **Human Appeal:** Algorithmic decisions may be appealed to human juries. The Algorithm is a tool, not a judge.

---

## **Article IV: Rights & Protections**
**1. Universal Basic Assets (UBA)**
*   Guaranteed access to essentials for every human on Earth, regardless of national compliance status.
*   Funded by Global Liability Taxes and redirected subsidies.

**2. Sentience Protection**
*   Commercial commodification of sentient animals is prohibited.
*   Subsistence use is protected only if no viable alternative exists for nutritional survival and proven suffering is minimized.
*   Animal welfare is a core metric of Planetary Integrity.

**3. The Right to Subsistence Exit**
*   Communities may live outside the commercial "Club" economy if they maintain ecological baselines.
*   No penalty may be imposed on those who opt for low-impact, low-tech livelihoods.

**4. Data Privacy & Body Integrity**
*   Biometric and behavioral data for compliance cannot be used for social control or commercial targeting.
*   Surveillance is limited strictly to ecological and safety verification.

---

## **Article V: The Transition Protocol**
**1. The Global Transition Fund**
*   **Purpose:** To subsidize the shift from extraction to regeneration.
*   **Funding:** Historical Liability Tax, Externality Taxes, Financial Transaction Taxes.
*   **Use:** Just Transition Grants for farmers, debt relief for developing nations, retraining for workers.

**2. Agriculture & Land Use**
*   **Subsidy Reallocation:** Immediate shift from animal agriculture subsidies to plant-based perennials and regenerative soil management.
*   **Conversion Grants:** Financial support for farmers to convert infrastructure to sustainable models.

**3. Debt Restructuring**
*   Sovereign debts are restructured based on ecological contribution and capacity to pay.
*   "Climate Debts" held by the Global South are cancelled.

**4. Timeline**
*   **Phase 1 (Stabilization):** Immediate halting of new extraction; funding of UBA and Transition Fund.
*   **Phase 2 (Transition):** 10-year period to align all major sectors with Regenerative standards.
*   **Phase 3 (Equilibrium):** Maintenance of regenerative capacity; focus shifts to innovation and flourishing.

---

## **Article VI: Oversight & Ratification**
**1. The Planetary Assembly**
*   A rotating body of citizens, workers, scientists, and elders convened to review the Covenant every 10 years.
*   Authority to amend Red Lines based on new evidence (with Future Generation veto).

**2. Ratification**
*   This Covenant is ratified when adopted by a coalition of nations representing 60% of the global population and 50% of the global economy.
*   Upon ratification, the Transition Protocol activates immediately.
