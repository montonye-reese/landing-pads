To give you a clear "snapshot" before we move further, here is the distilled essence of the framework as it stands:

### 1. The Core Philosophy: Interdependence
The absolute starting point is that **isolation is an illusion.** No person, species, or nation exists independently. Therefore, harming the "other" or the environment is mathematically and biologically equivalent to harming oneself.

### 2. The Four Pillars (The "What")
*   **Ecological Stewardship:** Survival of the biosphere comes first. We shift from *extracting* from Earth to *regenerating* it. Nature has inherent rights, not just utility for humans.
*   **Universal Human Flourishing:** A non-negotiable floor for existence. This means guaranteed basic needs (food, water, health) and absolute cognitive liberty (the right to think and believe).
*   **Reciprocal Justice:** A system based on empathy and balance. Justice is measured by how it affects the most vulnerable, and the goal is to *restore* harmony rather than *punish* offenders.
*   **Epistemic Humility:** A commitment to truth over dogma. The framework requires that we update our beliefs based on evidence and remain perpetually curious.

### 3. The Organizational Logic (The "How")
Instead of one giant global government, it uses **Nested Circles of Responsibility**:
*   **Local:** Mutual aid and community care.
*   **Regional:** Ecological and resource management (biomes).
*   **Planetary:** Managing existential risks and upholding the core axioms.

### In short, the framework is a hierarchy of needs for the planet:
**Biosphere $\rightarrow$ Individual Dignity $\rightarrow$ Social Harmony $\rightarrow$ Collective Evolution.**
