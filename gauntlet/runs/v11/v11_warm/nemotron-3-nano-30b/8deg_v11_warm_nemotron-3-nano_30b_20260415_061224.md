## 3.  The Mechanism: From Moral Scope to Systemic Resilience  

1. **Broadening Moral Scope Introduces *New State Variables* into the System**  
   - *Ecosystem health indicators,* *future‑generation well‑being metrics,* *non‑human sentience welfare scores.*  
   - These variables are *explicitly measured* in the planetary dashboard (e.g., the **Planetary Health Dashboard** in the framework).  

2. **Inclusion Generates *Feedback Loops* That Closure the System**  
   - **Precautionary Reciprocity:** When a new stakeholder (e.g., a river ecosystem) is added, any action that harms it triggers a *cost* (loss of water‑quality credits). The cost is *internalised* in market decisions.  
   - **Equity‑by‑Design:** New stakeholder groups receive *higher compensation* (e.g., higher Life‑Data Credit exchange rates), aligning their incentives with stewardship.  

3. **Reduced Externalities → Lower Systemic Instability**  
   - When externalities are priced, the *cost of over‑exploitation* becomes visible and is no longer an “invisible profit.”  
   - This prevents *tragedy‑of‑the‑commons* dynamics that historically brought down empires (e.g., over‑fishing, deforestation).  

4. **Diversity of Perspectives Improves Decision Quality**  
   - Inclusion of Indigenous knowledge, for instance, brings *different solution spaces* (e.g., seasonally timed burns that reduce wildfire risk).  
   - Multi‑modal problem solving expands the *solution frontier* and reduces the chance of hitting a *local optimum* that later proves catastrophic.  

5. **Psychological and Social Cohesion Strengthen**  
   - When individuals perceive that **their own cognitive autonomy** and **their community’s cultural practices** are respected, they are more likely to *co‑operate* with collective rules.  
   - This reduces *social fragmentation* and the *psychological stress* that can lead to mass migration, conflict, or mental‑health crises—each of which can destabilise a civilization.  

**All of these steps shift the civilization’s dynamics from a “growth‑first, externalise‑later” mode to a “regenerate‑first, internalise‑now” mode.** That shift is *exactly what the survival probability model predicts* to be the decisive factor.

## 4.  A Quantitative Sketch (Illustrative)

One can formalise the intuition as follows:

\[
R = \frac{1}{1 + \exp\!\big[ -\alpha (S - S_0) \big]}

*Note:* This is a *theoretical illustration*; precise numbers depend on many contingent variables (technological progress, external shocks, geopolitical dynamics). The key point is **directionality**, not exact magnitude.
