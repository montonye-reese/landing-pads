## The Framework – Current State (Version 2.0)

Below is the **complete, up‑to‑date description** of the planetary‑wide operating system after the full round of stresses, critiques, and redesigns.  Think of it as a single, coherent “operating manual” that can be read by any stakeholder—policy‑maker, technologist, community organizer, or citizen—who wants to understand **what the framework is, how it works, and why it is built to survive the hardest tests** (economic competition, moral scrutiny, technological shock, cultural pluralism, and existential risk).

---

### 1. Foundational Vision (The “Why”)

> **Core Vision‑Pillars (unchanged):**  
> 1. **Regenerative Co‑existence** – All human activity must leave ecosystems more alive than before.  
> 2. **Equitable Flourishing** – Every sentient being can meet basic needs, develop capacities, and participate in decisions that affect it.  
> 3. **Interconnected Learning & Adaptation** – Knowledge about the Earth system is continuously generated, shared, and applied.  
> 4. **Stewardship, Not Ownership** – All natural and informational resources are held in trust for present and future life.  

*The four pillars remain the north‑star; everything else is built around them.*

---

### 2. Guiding Principles (The “How‑We‑Think”)

| Principle | What It Means in Practice |
|-----------|---------------------------|
| **Interdependence** | No decision is evaluated in isolation; every action is mapped onto its ripple effects across ecological, economic, and social subsystems. |
| **Radical Transparency** | All metadata about data collection, consent, financial flows, and impact scores are publicly queryable on the Planetary Data Registry (PDR). |
| **Precautionary Reciprocity** | When uncertainty exists, the system automatically imposes *penalties* (credits debits, tariffs, or caps) before damage can accumulate. |
| **Diversity & Redundancy** | Multiple governance channels, audit bodies, and data‑storage pathways exist so that the failure of any one does not collapse the whole system. |
| **Feedback‑First Design** | Every policy, market mechanism, or technology includes a measurable feedback loop that can be **paused, scaled, or reversed** as soon as a metric breaches a threshold. |
| **Equity‑by‑Design** | Allocation rules (e.g., Life‑Credit exchange rates) give *higher* economic rewards to historically marginalized groups, ensuring that the system *rewards* inclusion. |

### 6. Succinct Summary (What It Is Now)

> **The framework is a *planetary‑scale, multi‑layered socio‑economic system* that:**
> 1. **Operates on a set of six moral principles** (interdependence, radical transparency, precautionary reciprocity, diversity & redundancy, feedback‑first design, equity‑by‑design).  
> 2. **Organizes everything into five interlocking layers** (bio‑regulation → economy → governance → education/culture → individual/community).  
> 3. **Uses concrete, token‑based instruments** (Life‑Data Credits, Regenerative‑Data Bonds, Open Knowledge Commons, Restorative Courts) to translate abstract principles into measurable actions.  
>  **All rules are tied to a dynamic feedback loop** that automatically prices externalities, enforces planetary boundaries, and can be overridden only by citizen‑driven assemblies or restorative courts.  
>  **It is built to be resilient:** redundant analog backups, shock‑proof governance, and a built‑in mechanism for continual self‑audit and cultural‑pluralistic update.  
>  **Its moral compass is humility‑aware:** every principle is tagged with a confidence level and an update trigger, ensuring that as new knowledge or cultural world‑views emerge, the framework can *re‑calibrate* without collapsing.*
