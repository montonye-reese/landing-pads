Okay, let's distill what's in the "Earthweave" framework as it stands right now. Here's a breakdown, categorized for clarity:

**I. Core Principles (The Foundational "Why") - Non-Negotiable:**

*   **Interdependence:** Everything is connected – ecological, social, economic, philosophical. Well-being is linked.
*   **Finite Resources:** Earth's resources are limited. Sustainable living is a necessity, not just a virtue.
*   **Sentience & Dignity:** All life exhibits awareness and inherent value, deserving of respect (varying degrees of consideration).
*   **Continuous Learning & Adaptation:**  The universe is dynamic. Rigidity leads to failure. We must learn, question, and adapt.
*   **Potential for Growth:** Life strives to grow and evolve, both individually and collectively. Support this growth within boundaries.

**II. Supporting Values (The "How" - Guiding Behavior)**

These *flow from* the core principles. They're more nuanced and allow for cultural expression.

*   **Empathy & Compassion:** Understanding and alleviating suffering.
*   **Justice & Equity:** Fair distribution of resources & opportunities; addressing past imbalances (access, not necessarily outcome).
*   **Truthfulness & Integrity:** Honesty and trust-building.
*   **Responsibility & Accountability:** Owning actions and consequences.
*   **Creativity & Innovation:** Finding better solutions; driving adaptation.
*   **Beauty & Wonder:** Appreciating the intrinsic beauty of the world; inspiring positive action.
*   **Respect for Diversity:** Valuing different perspectives and cultures for resilience.

**III. Practical Implications (The "What" - Manifestation in Daily Life)**

These are areas where the framework would *influence* existing systems. It's about *orientation*, not imposition.

*   **Education:** Systems thinking, ecological literacy, emotional intelligence, critical thinking, intercultural understanding. Focus on *how* to learn.
*   **Economics:** Shift to regenerative economics, circular economies, valuing natural capital, local resilience.
*   **Governance:** Decentralized, participatory, transparent, accountable governance; restorative justice.
*   **Technology:** Sustainable, equitable, accessible technology; prioritizing human connection.
*   **Healthcare:** Holistic, preventative, accessible healthcare for all.
*   **Food Systems:** Regenerative agriculture, local food security.
*   **Conflict Resolution:** Diplomacy, mediation, restorative justice; addressing root causes.
*   **Personal Practices:** Mindfulness, gratitude, connection to nature, conscious consumption.

**Key Characteristics (Overarching Qualities):**

*   **Non-Dogmatic:** A framework, not a rigid system.
*   **Inclusive:** Respects cultural diversity.
*   **Dynamic:**  Evolves with understanding.
*   **Holistic:** Recognizes interconnectedness.
*   **Action-Oriented:** Provides guidance for action.



**Essentially, it's a framework built on the idea that recognizing our interconnectedness and the limits of our planet requires a fundamental shift in how we live, learn, govern, and relate to each other and the natural world.** It’s aiming for a balance between universal principles and the freedom for diverse expression.
