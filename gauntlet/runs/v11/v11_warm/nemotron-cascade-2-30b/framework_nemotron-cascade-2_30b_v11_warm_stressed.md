## The Earth‑Wide Foundational Framework (EFF) – What It Looks Like **After All the Pressure‑Testing**

Below is a **single‑page snapshot** of the framework as it stands today.  
Everything you will see is the result of the “what‑if” analyses you asked for, the stakeholder‑mapping, and the desire to survive a civilization that races ahead without the EFF.  

---

### 1. Vision (the “why”)

> **All sentient and non‑sentient beings that exist on Earth (including future intelligences, synthetic life‑forms, and off‑world habitats) share a single planetary home that must remain a thriving, self‑regenerating ecosystem.**  
> **The EFF translates this moral vision into a single, globally‑applicable accounting language – Regeneration Units (RU) – that turns every action into a measurable contribution (or deficit) to the planet’s health.**  

---

### 2. Core Principles (the “what”) – unchanged

| # | Principle | Short formulation |
|---|-----------|-------------------|
| **P1** | **Interdependence** | No being can flourish while the biosphere collapses. |
| **P2** | **Life‑Centred Rights** | Every organism, species, functional ecosystem, AI collective, and synthetic organism has the right to exist, to be restored, and to be represented. |
| **P3** | **Regeneration over Sustainability** | We must give back more than we take, measured across carbon, water, biodiversity, soil, and cultural‑ecological integrity. |
| **P4** | **Adaptive Learning** | All rules are data‑driven, revisable every five years, and can be tightened instantly when a planetary boundary is breached. |
| **P5** | **Shared Stewardship & Equity** | Benefits and burdens are allocated fairly across generations, peoples, and non‑human life. |
| **P6** | **Co‑creative Technology Governance** | AI, synthetic biology, and space‑based activities are part of the Earth system and are governed by the same planetary limits. |

### 3. Universal Accounting – **Regeneration Units (RU)**  

*One RU ≈ 1 % of the global carbon budget + 1 % of the global biodiversity budget + 1 % of the global water‑use budget + 1 % of the global social‑equity budget.*  

**RU is the only currency that can be spent, saved, or transferred.**  

| Sub‑category | What it measures | How it is calculated (example) |
|---|---|---|
| **Carbon RU** | Net‑positive or negative CO₂eq. | 1 t CO₂eq avoided → +0.03 RU; 1 t CO₂eq emitted → –0.03 RU |
| **Water RU** | Net‑positive water returned to a watershed. | 100 m³ of fresh water restored → +0.01 RU |
| **Biodiversity RU** | Species‑abundance retained or increased. | 0.5 % increase in a threatened pollinator species → +0.02 RU |
| **Soil‑Carbon RU** | Increase in Soil Organic Carbon (SOC). | 1 % rise in SOC on 1 ha of farmland → +0.005 RU |
| **Cultural‑Ecological Resilience RU (CERR‑RU)** | Intact cultural‑ecological mosaics (e.g., poly‑crop farms, language‑linked habitats). | Preservation of a 10‑ha multi‑species agroforestry system with three indigenous languages → +0.015 RU |
| **Social‑Resilience RU (SERR‑RU)** | Strength of community networks, mental‑health metrics, mutual‑aid. | 20 % rise in inter‑household support → +0.01 RU |
| **Care‑Economy RU** | Verified unpaid care, community repair, informal recycling. | 10 h of community‑repair work → +0.2 RU |
| **Human‑Health Impact RU (HHRU)** | DALYs averted or created. | 0.1 DALY averted → +0.005 RU |
| **Cultural‑RU (C‑RU)** | Rights‑based credit for protected cultural‑ecological sites. | A sacred river restored → +0.02 RU (cannot be transferred, only used to protect the river) |

*All sub‑categories feed into the **single RU total** for any activity.  A project is legally allowed **iff its total RU balance ≥ 0**.*

### 4. Institutions – The **Poly‑centric Loop**

#### 4.1. **Local Commons Assembly (LCA)**
* Every village, town, or mining concession has one.  
* Elects:  
  * **Human‑Ecological Trustee** (local Indigenous guardian, farmer, etc.)  
  * **AI‑Caretaker Trustee** (a certified, audited AI model that runs on the local RU‑calculator).  
* Produces a **Local Impact Report (LIR)** that contains the RU debit/credit for every proposed project.  

#### 4.2. **Regional Commons Assembly (RCA)**
* 20–30 LCAs in a region (e.g., Amazon basin, the Great Barrier Reef) feed their LI­Rs into a **Regional Impact Statement (RIS)**.  
* Runs the **Adaptive Policy Engine (APE)** to test scenarios against planetary caps.  

#### 4.3. **Planet‑Scale Commons Council (PCC)**
* 200 seats: 40 % biome delegates, 25 % Indigenous confederations, 10 % AI‑Caretaker seats, 15 % rotating citizen‑juries (5 000 random global citizens each year).  
* Issues **Regeneration Licences** (net‑positive RU) and can **nullify** any licence that would push any planetary boundary below its safety margin.  

#### 4.4. **Knowledge Commons & Planetary Health Registry (KCPHR)**
* Federated, permissioned blockchain.  
* Stores: satellite imagery, IoT sensor streams, oral histories (structured), AI‑caretaker audit logs, RU‑transactions.  
* Provides a **public RU Dashboard** that shows, in real time, the net RU balance for every planetary boundary.

#### 4.5. **Regeneration Toolkit (RT)**
* **Ecological Impact Calculator (EIC)** – maps raw flows (kg of ore, m³ of water, etc.) → RU debit/credit.  
* **Social Equity Ledger (SEL)** – measures distributional impact; must be *non‑negative* for a licence.  
* **Digital Rights Interface (DRI)** – every AI that can affect a boundary must submit a **cryptographically signed RU‑impact signature** every 5 minutes; the signature can be vetoed by the **Synthetic‑Life Trust (SLT)**.  
* **Regeneration Licence Generator (RLG)** – issues a blockchain‑based licence that automatically locks the activity to a net‑positive RU balance.

### 5. Legal‑Personhood Architecture

| Entity | Legal status | Who can veto / act for it? |
|---|---|---|
| **Ecosystem (e.g., Amazon rainforest)** | *Resource Trust* – a non‑human legal person. | **Human‑Ecological Trustee** (Indigenous or scientific) **and** **AI‑Caretaker** must both sign off on any activity that touches the ecosystem. |
| **Autonomous AI swarm** | *Digital Person* under the **Synthetic‑Life Trust (SLT)**. | **Synthetic‑Life Trust** holds veto power; the AI’s own **self‑audit** must show a non‑negative RU before it can continue operating. |
| **Synthetic organism (gene‑drive, engineered microbe)** | *Biological Person* under the **Synthetic‑Life Trust**. | SLT veto can halt deployment if RU deficit is detected; must fund a compensatory RU‑positive restoration within 30 days or lose personhood. |
| **Space‑based settlement (e.g., lunar base)** | *Territorial Trust* (human‑centric but with an **AI‑caretaker** representing the settlement’s compute infrastructure). | **Council of Regenerative Commons** (regional) + **PCC** must approve any RU‑deficit activity; the settlement must pay RU‑positive restoration on Earth. |

### 6. Key Safeguards that Stop a “Fast‑AI, Data‑Closed” Race

| Threat | Built‑in guardrail |
|---|---|
| **AI or synthetic life hides its impact** | **Energy‑Use Index (EUI)** – every kilowatt‑hour used, even by a closed‑source data centre, is *physically* measured by satellite and must be published as a signed hash on the KCPHR. The RU‑budget automatically flags any negative RU. |
| **One nation hoards RU or creates a monopoly** | **RU‑ownership caps** (≤ 0.5 % of total RU per legal person) + **non‑speculative RU** (each RU credit must be locked to a verified on‑ground restoration project for at least 3 years). |
| **Future‑generation RU reserve is drained early** | **Tiered FGRU**: any draw from the reserve must be matched *within 30 days* by a verified **net‑positive RU** restoration on Earth; the draw can only be used for planetary emergencies, not for funding new tech. |
| **Cultural‑ecological values get “priced out”** | **CERR‑RU** is a *hard floor* – a project cannot achieve a positive net RU if it reduces CERR‑RU. Cultural‑RU (C‑RU) credits are *non‑transferable* and must be spent on the very cultural practice that generated them. |
| **Fast‑AI tries to bypass the DRI** | **Dual‑mandate DRI**: an AI must satisfy *both* the **global AI‑caretaker** (operates on the KCPHR) **and** a **national AI‑caretaker** whose veto can be overridden only by a **global citizen‑jury** (≥ 5 000 randomly selected humans). |
| **Off‑world extraction threatens Earth’s limits** | **Space‑Boundary Budget** – any asteroid or lunar mining must *pay* a verified RU‑positive amount of Earth restoration (e.g., 1 t of space‑mined platinum → 0.5 tCO₂ removal on Earth). The **Synthetic‑Life Trust** for the asteroid (given personhood) can veto extraction that would breach the Earth budget. |

### 6. Regeneration Toolkit – The “How‑to‑Use” Suite (all are open‑source, version‑controlled, and can be run offline)

| Tool | Core function | Example of a decision it enables |
|---|---|---|
| **Ecological Impact Calculator (EIC)** | Converts raw flow data (tonnes of ore, m³ of water, ha of land) → RU debit/credit across the nine sub‑categories. | A mining company submits a plan to open a new lithium pit; the EIC shows a –120 RU deficit unless a 200 ha wetland restoration is added. |
| **Social Equity Ledger (SEL)** | Quantifies distributional impacts (who gains/loses) and must be ≥ 0 for any licence. | A proposed highway shows a net‑negative SEL because a low‑income neighbourhood will be displaced; the LCA must mitigate (e.g., build affordable housing) before a licence can be issued. |
| **Adaptive Policy Engine (APE)** | Monte‑Carlo simulation of planetary boundaries; updates caps every quarter. | APR shows that the projected RU deficit for the carbon boundary will be exceeded in 2032 unless carbon extraction is cut by 30 %; the PCC can immediately tighten the carbon‑RU cap. |
| **Digital Rights Interface (DRI)** | Provides the legal “veto” for any AI that can affect planetary limits; issues a signed RU‑impact signature every 5 minutes. | An autonomous drone swarm wants to spray a pollutant over a reef; the DRI signature shows a negative RU; the global AI‑caretaker veto forces an immediate halt. |
| **Regeneration Licence Generator (RLG)** | Issues a blockchain‑based licence that automatically embeds the required RU‑offsets, SEL compliance, and cultural‑RU commitments. | A renewable‑energy project receives a licence that automatically allocates 5 RU to a community‑led agroforestry restoration, ensuring a net‑positive RU. |

### 7. What Has *Stayed the Same*  

| Element | Why it survived the pressure test |
|---|---|
| **Six core principles** – they are abstract enough to be the moral spine for *any* Earth‑ling (human, AI, synthetic organism, space settler). |
| **RU as the universal accounting token** – the only thing that can turn “morality” into “something you can add up”. |
| **Poly‑centric governance loop** – local → regional → planetary – because that scale‑flexibility is essential when you don’t know whether you’ll be a village farmer or a planetary AI. |
| **Living charter and story‑RU** – still the cultural glue that turns numbers into a shared narrative. |
| **RU‑budget + planetary caps** – the hard limits that keep the whole system from slipping into an overshoot. |

### 7. What Has *Changed* (the big upgrades)

| Area | New element | Why it matters now |
|---|---|---|
| **Legal personhood** | **Synthetic‑Life Trust (SLT)** with veto power and forced re‑instatement; **dual‑caretaker trusts** for every non‑human entity. | Guarantees that any new form of life (AI, gene‑drive, space habitat) has *two* independent guardians that must agree before any action proceeds. |
| **RU accounting** | **Sub‑categories**, **ownership caps**, **non‑transferable cultural RU**, **hard RU‑ownership caps**. | Prevents a single corporation or AI from hoarding the resource and from turning cultural heritage into a tradable commodity. |
| **Data integrity** | **Energy‑Use Index (EUI)**, **cross‑ledger bridges**, **regular independent audits**. | Makes it technically impossible for a state or corporation to stay invisible while still consuming planetary resources. |
| **Cultural safeguards** | **CERR‑RU**, **non‑transferable C‑RU**, **Cultural‑Rights Trustee** in every licence. | Guarantees that the very cultural practices that give a community its identity cannot be erased by a “green” project that only looks good on a carbon spreadsheet. |
| **Adaptive speed** | **Quarterly APE updates**, **emergency RU issuance** (48‑hour response). | The system can react fast enough to an AI‑driven geo‑engineering deployment that would otherwise push the climate past a tipping point. |
| **Governance of AI** | **Dual‑mandate AI‑caretaker** (global + national, with citizen‑jury veto). | No single state or corporation can silently switch off the RU‑budget enforcement for its AI. |
| **Future‑generation safety** | **Tiered Future‑Generation RU Reserve** with strict borrowing rules and cultural‑RU protection. | Even if a fast‑tech civilization decides to spend all its RU on a new mining fleet, the reserve will still have a dedicated pool for restoring ecosystems after the inevitable tipping point is hit. |
