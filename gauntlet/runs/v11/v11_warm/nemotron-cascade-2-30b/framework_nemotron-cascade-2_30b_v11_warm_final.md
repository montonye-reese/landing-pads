# The Earth‑Wide Foundational Framework (EFF) – the living charter for all sentient and non‑sentient life on Earth  

> **Vision** – *We are a single planetary community.  All life‑forms—human, animal, synthetic, and the ecosystems that sustain them—share a common home.  We will organize our economies, technologies, and cultures so that every act of creation leaves the planet in a net‑positive state.*  

The EFF is **both a constitution and a living data‑engine**.  It is *hard* (legal obligations, verifiable numbers) and *soft* (stories, education, culture).  It is built to **survive capture** by elites, to *adapt* as science evolves, and to give *every* being a right, a voice, and a metric of contribution.

| # | Principle | What it means in practice |
|---|---|---|
| **P1 – Interdependence** | No single mind, species, or technology can thrive while the biosphere collapses. Every decision must be assessed in the context of its systemic feedbacks. |
| **P2 – Life‑Centred Rights** | All living systems—human, animal, synthetic, or ecosystem—have the *right to exist, to be restored, and to be represented.*  The law recognises them as **legal persons** with trustees. |

| **P6 – Co‑creative Technology Governance** | Every AI‑driven system that can affect the planetary budget carries a **Digital Rights Interface (DRI)** that can veto any action that would breach a planetary limit. |

## 2.  The Core Accounting Unit – Regeneration Units (RU)

| Sub‑category | What it counts | Example conversion (approximate) |
|---|---|---|
| **Carbon RU** | Net CO₂ removed or emitted. | 1 t CO₂eq avoided = **+0.03 RU** |
| **Fresh‑Water RU** | Net m³ of renewable freshwater withdrawn vs. returned to rivers. | 100 m³ restored = **+0.01 RU** |
| **Biodiversity Intactness RU** | Species‑abundance retained or recovered. | A 10 % increase in the abundance of a threatened species = **+0.02 RU** |
| **Soil‑Organic‑Carbon RU** | Increase in soil organic carbon per hectare. | 0.5 % increase = **+0.005 RU** |
| **Social‑Equity RU (SEL)** | Distribution of benefits & burdens across peoples; measured by a *Gini‑style* index plus access to services. | 20 % reduction in the Gini of water access in a basin = **+0.01 RU** |
| **Cultural‑Ecological Resilience RU (CERR‑RU)** | Integrity of cultural‑ecological mosaics (language, traditional land‑use, sacred sites). | Protecting a sacred river that supports 12 % of a tribe’s identity = **+0.02 RU** |
| **Human‑Health Impact RU (HHRU)** | Measured in DALYs averted or created. | 0.1 DALY averted = **+0.005 RU** |
| **Regeneration‑Units (RU) are *non‑transferable* unless tied to a verified on‑the‑ground restoration project (see §4).** |

**The RU ledger is a blockchain with zero‑knowledge proofs, updated in real time by satellite, IoT sensors, citizen‑science apps, and citizen‑jurors.**  

**Every action → RU debit or credit**.  The goal: **net‑positive RU for every activity**; a negative RU is automatically blocked.

## 3.  The Decision‑Making Flow (the “how”)

1. **Local Commons Assembly (LCA)** proposes a new activity (e.g., a new mine, a new data centre, a new species‑restoration project).  
2. **Local Impact Report (LIR)** is generated by the **EIC + SEL** – it yields a *baseline RU balance* (negative = net loss).  
2. **Regional Commons Assembly (RCA)** aggregates the LIRs, runs the **Adaptive Policy Engine (APE)**, and issues a **Regional Impact Statement (RIS)** showing the *regional* RU budget and whether planetary caps are at risk.  
3. **PCC** receives the RIS, checks the **global RU budget** for each planetary boundary (carbon, water, biodiversity, etc.).  
   - If the projected net RU is **≤ 0** for any boundary, the **PCC can instantly veto** the proposal (the *minimum‑impact voting algorithm* requires ≥ 75 % affirmative and no single sector > 20 % of the vote).  
   - If the vote passes, a **Regeneration License** (RL) is issued; its terms are a *bundle* of RU credits and obligations (e.g., “restore 10 ha of wetland, fund a community health centre, preserve the river’s cultural narrative”).  
4. **Adaptive Policy Engine (APE)** monitors the planetary‑budget dashboards *in real time*. If a breach is detected, the APE can *automatically* recall any active license, trigger a *regeneration task force*, and impose a **Regeneration Tax** (1 % of any RU‑trade) that funds the *Strategic Restoration Fund* (SRF).  
5. **All actions are recorded on the KCPHR** – every document, sensor reading, cultural story, and AI‑caretaker decision is logged.  Anyone can audit any RU transaction; any attempt to hide data is flagged automatically.

## 4.  The Legal Personhood Network (the “who can sue”)

1. **Human‑Ecological Trustee (HET)** – a citizen elected by the local community, trained in ecology, who *represents the ecosystem* (e.g., the river, the forest).  
2. **AI‑Caretaker Trustee (AI‑CT)** – a certified AI model that continuously monitors the ecosystem’s metrics and can *veto* any activity that would push the RU balance negative.  
3. **Cultural‑Rights Trustee (CRT)** – a rotating body of Indigenous elders, linguists, and cultural scholars who decide whether the *cultural‑rights* aspect of a project is satisfied.  

*Every entity (human, AI, ecosystem, synthetic organism) has at least one HET + one Caretaker.*  Any decision that would affect the entity must be *signed* by both.  This prevents a single powerful entity from overriding the protection.

## 4.  The RU Budget – the planetary budget (the “real” planetary limits)

The **Planetary Sustainability Charter** (PSC) sets *hard caps* for the six planetary boundaries (Carbon, Water, Land‑Surface, Biodiversity, Ocean‑Acidification, Soil‑Carbon). The **RU caps** for each category are:

| Boundary | 2025 global net‑RU budget (approx.) | Annual RU cap for *new* projects |
|---|---|---|
| Carbon (net‑negative) | –200 RU (global) | –50 RU per year (new mining, construction, etc.) |
| Fresh‑water | 5 000 m³ per km² of watershed | 200 m³ per km² per year |
| Biodiversity Intactness | 90 % intact | +5 RU per 10 ha of intact forest |
| Ocean‑Acidification | pH ≥ 8.1 | +0.01 RU per year (new ocean‑restoration projects) |
| Soil‑Organic‑Carbon | +0.5 % per year | +0.5 RU per year of soil restoration |
| Ocean‑Acidification (pH) | pH ≥ 8.1 | +0.01 RU per year of marine restoration |

If the *cumulative RU* for any boundary ever reaches 0 RU (i.e., the budget is used up), the **Adaptive Policy Engine automatically tightens the caps** and **issues a planetary emergency** that can trigger *forced re‑allocation* of RU from high‑impact sectors to **Restoration Projects** (e.g., rewilding, wetland reconstruction).

## 4.  The “Truth‑Audit” – the institutional safeguard that Havel warned about

- Every 6 months a **randomly selected citizen‑jury (500 people)** plus **independent scientists** (including AI‑caretakers) perform a *zero‑knowledge proof* audit of every RU transaction.  
- Any **significant variance** (> 10 %) forces an **automatic “Recall”** of the related licenses and a *public audit* of the offending entity.  

*Why it matters:* The system cannot be fooled by “paper‑only” claims; the data are *verifiable* by anyone, everywhere. The *political class* cannot hide a negative RU because the audit will expose it and the **PCC** will have to act.

## 4.5.  Cultural‑Rights RU (C‑RU) – a concrete safeguard for cultural equity

- Every cultural practice (e.g., a traditional fishing method, a sacred river ceremony) that is *culturally essential* is assigned a **C‑RU value** (e.g., +0.02 RU).  
- The C‑RU *cannot be transferred*; it can only be *used* to *restore* that practice (e.g., fund a community‑run fish hatchery, teach the next generation the oral tradition).  
- If a development project would *erase* the cultural practice, the C‑RU score for that activity will be *negative* (e.g., –0.02 RU), forcing the license to be denied unless the community *re‑creates* the culture elsewhere.

**This prevents the “green‑gentrification” loophole** that lets a community be labelled “protected” while the land is turned into a tourist resort.

## 4.  Cultural‑Equity & Community Control – the “living” part of the charter  

- **Every community that is *named* in the charter can *write* its own *Cultural‑Rights RU* (C‑RU).  
- The **Cultural‑Rights Trustee** (local Indigenous or Traditional Community) holds a *veto* over any project that would *reduce* the C‑RU.  
- The **Digital Rights Interface (DRI)** automatically blocks the action if the *cultural‑R* impact is negative.  

Thus, a mining project that would destroy a sacred river can be stopped *before any mining begins*—the RU budget will show a *large negative* cultural‑RU score, and the *C‑RU veto* will block the license.

## 5.  The “Future‑Generation Reserve” (FGRU) – a safety net for the future

- **30 % of every net‑positive RU** is automatically transferred to a *perpetual fund* that can **only be spent** on *future‑generation projects* (e.g., long‑term carbon‑capture, establishing a new “regeneration reserve” in a deep‑sea ecosystem).  
- The FGRU can *never* be withdrawn to pay for a *new* project; it can only be *spent* on *future‑regeneration* that will **increase the planet’s capacity** for the next 50 years.  

Thus, even if a *future* generation needs to *undo* a policy (e.g., a mining operation), the *RU* to pay for the remediation is already **locked** in the fund.
