## I. FOUNDATIONAL PRINCIPLES

The current system treats human experience as free raw material to be transformed into behavioral data products. The **Sovereign Mind Infrastructure (SMI)** inverts this relationship. It operates on three axioms:

1.  **Cognitive Property:** All data generated by an individual's mind (clicks, thoughts, searches, biometrics) is the inalienable property of that individual.
2.  **Non-Extractive Design:** Technology must not be designed to exploit psychological vulnerabilities (dopamine loops, infinite scroll, variable rewards).
3.  **Public Utility:** Internet access and core communication platforms are considered public infrastructure, not private fiefdoms.

---

## II. ARCHITECTURAL COMPONENTS

### 1. The Personal Data Vault (PVD)
Instead of data being harvested by centralized servers (Google, Meta, etc.), every user possesses a secure, encrypted **Personal Data Vault** on their local device or a decentralized node.
*   **Function:** The Vault holds all user data.
*   **Access:** Applications request temporary, limited permission to read specific data points.
*   **Consent:** Consent is granular and revocable at any time. "Accept All" is illegal.
*   **Monetization:** If data is used to train AI or target services, the user receives direct compensation (micro-payments) via the Vault. The user profits from their attention, not the platform.

### 2. Algorithmic Transparency Standards
All algorithms influencing human decision-making (news feeds, search rankings, recommendation engines) must be registered as **Public Interest Algorithms**.
*   **Source Code:** Core logic must be open-source and auditable by independent third parties.
*   **Explainability:** Users have the right to know *why* they are being shown specific content (e.g., "This post is shown because you engaged with Topic X, not because Advertiser Y paid for it").
*   **No Dark Patterns:** Interface design must avoid traps that make it difficult to leave, unsubscribe, or disable features.

### 3. The Attention Budget
To prevent addiction and burnout, the SMI introduces a standardized **Attention Budget** protocol.
*   **Friction:** Platforms must introduce "circuit breakers" after continuous usage thresholds (e.g., 45 minutes of scrolling triggers a mandatory pause prompt).
*   **Time Capsules:** For minors (under 18), hard limits are enforced by the OS.
*   **Notification Hygiene:** Push notifications are disabled by default. Users must opt-in to specific types of alerts.

---

## III. ECONOMIC MODEL

The current model relies on selling user attention to advertisers. The SMI replaces this with a **Stewardship Economy**.

1.  **Infrastructure Levy:** A small tax is levied on high-bandwidth data usage by corporations, not individuals. This funds the maintenance of the public digital infrastructure (the "Digital Public Square").
2.  **Ethical Subscription:** For premium services, platforms charge users directly. Since they are not harvesting data, their value proposition shifts to *quality of service* rather than *depth of surveillance*.
3.  **Data Dividend:** When an individual's data is utilized for large-scale model training, a portion of the revenue generated by that training is returned to the data owner via the Personal Data Vault.

---

## IV. LEGAL & GOVERNANCE STANDARDS

### 1. Cognitive Liberty Act
*   **Right to Mental Silence:** Individuals have the right to be free from algorithmic influence in spaces deemed critical for mental health (sleep environments, educational zones).
*   **Ban on Behavioral Targeting:** Targeting ads based on psychographic profiles (fear, political leaning, emotional state) is prohibited. Targeting may only be based on explicit, conscious user queries.
*   **Minor Protections:** All digital platforms are required to verify age and apply strict, non-negotiable safety protocols for users under 18. Data collection from minors is limited to safety and functionality only.

### 2. The Algorithmic Oversight Council
*   **Composition:** A global body of computer scientists, ethicists, psychologists, and citizen representatives.
*   **Power:** They have the authority to audit algorithms, mandate changes to code that violates Cognitive Liberty, and levy fines or revoke operating licenses for platforms that violate the SMI standards.

---

## V. IMPLEMENTATION PATHWAY

1.  **Phase 1 (The Moratorium):** Immediate ban on new behavioral data collection. Existing data is quarantined and cannot be used for future training without explicit user consent.
2.  **Phase 2 (The Transition):** Open-source development of Personal Data Vaults and decentralized protocols (e.g., Fediverse, Solid) supported by government grants.
3.  **Phase 3 (The Migration):** Legacy platforms are given 5 years to migrate to the SMI standards or be broken up into public utilities (similar to the breakup of railroads or telecommunications in earlier centuries).

In 2124, the **Sovereign Mind Infrastructure** will be remembered as the moment humanity stopped treating the mind as a marketplace.

This redesign does not ban technology; it sanctifies it. It acknowledges that the mind is the most sacred territory of the human being, and no corporation should hold a map to its interior.
