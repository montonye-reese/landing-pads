**Earth‑One 3.0 – The Full Blueprint**  
*A living, self‑correcting institutional architecture that guarantees the survival of humanity + all sentient life while guaranteeing justice, resilience and regeneration for the long term.*

---  

## 1. Vision  

**One Planet, One Future** – All sentient beings—human, animal, and emergent digital intelligences—share a single planetary commons.  Every decision must keep the planet within its ecological limits, guarantee equal dignity and opportunity for every sentient being, and leave the world richer in natural capital than it was found.  

---

## 2. Foundational Pillars (unchanged)

| Pillar | Core Idea | Why it matters |
|--------|----------|-----------------|
| **Interdependence** | All life‑forms and ecosystems are linked in a web of material and energy flows. | Guarantees we never treat nature as a “free resource.” |
| **Equity & Inclusion** | Every sentient being—human, animal, AI—has an equal right to thrive. | Guarantees dignity for all, now and for future generations. |
| **Resilience & Adaptation** | Systems must be able to absorb shocks and learn from feedback. | Prevents collapse when climate, economic or social shocks hit. |
| **Regeneration** | Human activity must **restore** rather than merely *conserve* natural capital. | Moves us from “sustainable use” to “restoring the planet”. |

---

## 3. The Seven Rules of Engagement (binding for every decision)

1. **Precautionary‑by‑Default** – No activity may be approved if any credible analysis shows > 5 % risk of irreversible harm.  
2. **Circular‑First** – Materials, energy and information must stay in use; waste streams become new resources.  
3. **Transparent Data** – All decisions must be logged on the **Earth Ledger** (public, tamper‑evident blockchain).  
3. **Bottom‑Up Co‑Creation** | Every decision affecting the commons must be preceded by a *local citizen‑assembly* that drafts the proposal; the community’s vote has *binding* weight. |  
| **Science‑Guided Ethics** | All projects undergo a peer‑reviewed **Ethical Impact Assessment (IAE‑2)** that scores on human‑rights, non‑human rights and carbon‑budget risk. | • Guarantees decisions are rooted in peer‑reviewed science. |
| **Capacity‑Building as Public Good** | Every citizen receives a **Baseline Right**: clean water, safe food, energy, connectivity and education. | Guarantees a baseline of human (and non‑human) well‑being. |
4. **Celebration of Diversity** | Cultural, linguistic, spiritual and ecological diversity are declared *strategic assets*, not merely “nice‑to‑have.” | Diversity is valued for its adaptive power, not just as a feel‑good add‑on. |

## 3. Earth‑One Governance – Layered but Flattened  

```
E1  →  Global Council (Earth‑Scale Assembly)
       │
E1 → Continental/Regional Hubs → Regional Energy Hubs
       │
E3  → Local Community Assemblies (Citizen Juries)
E4 – Micro‑Networks (Households, Co‑ops, Schools)
```

*Key Features*

| Layer | Main Function | Governance Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| **E1 – Earth‑Scale Council** | Sets planetary caps (e.g., carbon budget, biodiversity caps). Issues **Global Renewable Investment Fund (GRIF)**. | **Earth‑Scale Council** = 1 delegate per 10 M citizens, elected every 4 years; decisions require a **two‑thirds super‑majority** and **compliance with the **Ethical Impact Assessment** (IEIA). |
| **E2 – Continental Hubs** | Translate caps into regional plans; coordinate cross‑border utilities. | Operated by **Regional Earth Councils** (mix of elected politicians, Indigenous Knowledge Council reps and citizen‑juries). |
| **E3 – Community Assemblies** | Run **Participatory Budgeting**, co‑design micro‑grids, set local **Circular‑Credit** targets. | **Citizen Juries** (≥ 10 % of local pop.) decide on projects; results are logged on the **Earth Ledger**. |
| **E4 – Micro‑Network** | Households, co‑ops, schools run **micro‑grids** and **circular‑resource loops**. | Each household has a **Personal Energy Account** on the Earth Ledger, visible to all participants. |

**Governance Mechanisms**

* **Participatory Assemblies** – citizen‑juror panels that deliberate on local projects.  
* **Impact Audits** – certified auditors publish *before/after* metrics; violations trigger automatic sanctions.  
* **Dynamic Equity Index (DEI‑2.0)** – a composite index (social equity, environmental health, cultural‑preservation) that automatically triggers:  
  * Corrective Policy Directives when thresholds fall below 0.65 (0–1 scale).  
  * Automatic **Corrective Directive** that may temporarily suspend funding, impose sanctions, or require corrective redesign. |
| **Appeal & Restorative Panels** | Citizen juries that administer *restorative‑justice* settlements, payable in **Circular Credits**. | Restorative justice ensures harmed parties receive tangible compensation without punitive over‑punishment. |
| **Dynamic Equity Index (DEI‑2.0)** | Continuous real‑time metric tracking **environmental, social and distributional health**; updated hourly and published on the Earth Ledger. | Forces automatic policy correction whenever planetary or societal thresholds are breached. |

## 4.  Energy & Resource Economy  

| Component | Description | Key Features |
|---|---|---|
| **Regenerative Energy Mix** | 100 % renewable (solar, wind, geothermal, run‑of‑river hydro, *planned* BECCS when carbon‑negative). | Every megawatt is logged on the **Earth Ledger**; excess generation generates **Circular‑Credits** that are automatically divided among citizens (UBR‑E). |
| **Circular‑Design Standard (CDS‑2.0)** | Requires ≥ 90 % material recovery, **zero‑waste** design, and zero‑harm manufacturing processes. | Guarantees that *all* new infrastructure (solar panels, batteries, batteries, building components) is fully recyclable at end‑life. |
| **Circular‑Credits** | Earned when a facility *adds net positive* material to the commons (e.g., restored wetlands, restored wetlands, cleaned waste streams). | Credits are tradable on the **Earth Ledger**; profits are re‑invested into public services or community projects. |
| **Renewable‑Energy Revenue (RGR)** | Renewable‑energy projects feed into a **Regenerative Grid** that feeds *both* the micro‑grids and the global **Global Renewable Investment Fund (GRIF)**. | Returns are tied to **long‑term planetary‑health indicators**, not just financial return. |
| **Universal Basic Resilience – Energy (UBR‑E)** | Every household receives a **baseline allotment of clean energy** (≈ 150 kWh month⁻¹). | Funded by a **progressive levy** on fossil‑fuel extraction, speculative finance and high‑emission industries; guarantees *universal access* to clean power. |

## 5.  Economic Architecture  

| Component | Description | Moral Goal |
|---|---|---|
| **Circular‑Credits** | Earned by *restoring* resources, recycling materials, or restoring habitats; redeemable for goods, services, or time‑bank credits. | Rewards regeneration, not merely *use* of resources. |
| **Circular‑Credit Economy** | All transactions (goods, services, energy) must be recorded on the **Earth Ledger** with tags for *material source, carbon intensity, and social impact*. | Makes *externalities visible* and enforceable. |
| **Circular‑Credit Currency** | Not a separate currency, but a **reward token** that can be spent on any goods/services, but *only* if the transaction meets material‑recovery and environmental‑impact thresholds. |
| **Revenue Streams** | *Renewable‑Renewable Bonds* (linked to renewable‑energy output), *Carbon‑Positive Credit Sales*, *Circular‑Credit earnings*. |
| **Universal Basic Resilience – Energy (UBR‑E)** | Guarantees every household ≥ 150 kWh month⁻¹ of clean, reliable electricity (plus basic heating & sanitation). Funded by **progressive carbon‑tax levy + fossil‑fuel‑royalty revenues**. |
| **Circular‑Credit Currency** | Not a separate currency; rather, credits are *redeemed* for goods/services, *charged* based on *circular‑credit balance*. | Makes resource use *costly* when it generates waste, encouraging circular cycles. |
