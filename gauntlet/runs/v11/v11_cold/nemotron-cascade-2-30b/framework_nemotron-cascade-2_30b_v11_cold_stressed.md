# Earth‑First Foundational Framework (EFF) – Current Version (2025‑27)

**Purpose** – To provide a *living constitution* for all sentient life on Earth that is **self‑regulating, future‑proof, and inclusive of any future form of life or cognition**.  

The framework is built on four non‑negotiable ontological statements, twelve core articles (the **Earth Charter of Interdependence**), three mutually‑reinforcing pillars, and a polycentric governance system that is anchored in an **immutable planetary ledger** and **distributed, verifiable AI**.  

---

## 1.  Vision (single sentence)

> **All life‑forms on Earth, together with the people and any future‑born sentient entities, together steward a flourishing, resilient, and just planet.**  

Everything else in the framework exists to translate this vision into enforceable rights, duties, and decision‑making mechanisms.

## 2.  Core Ontology – The Four Statements (non‑negotiable spine)

| # | Statement | Immediate legal consequence |
|---|-----------|------------------------------|
| **1** | **The planet is a living system.** Earth’s life‑supporting processes (climate, water, carbon, nitrogen, biodiversity) have safe operating limits. | **Planetary Boundaries** are hard caps. Any human or non‑human activity that would push a boundary must be denied by an automatic smart‑contract on the Global Climate Ledger (GCL). |
| **2** | **Humans (and any future sentient forms) are embedded participants, not owners.** Our bodies, cultures, technologies, and economies are part of the Earth system. | No extraction or production may exceed the *allocation* granted by the **Resource Commons Trust (RCT)** – a public trust that holds all extraction licences. |
| **3** | **Every sentient entity has intrinsic worth.** Value is both instrumental (what we obtain) and inherent (what it is). | All ecosystems, geological formations, subterranean biospheres, and any future sentient processes are **legal persons** with the right to **integrity, evolution, and non‑exploitation**. |
| **4** | **Justice is temporal and spatial.** Equity must be measured across present, future, and deep‑time generations, and across all regions (including deep ocean, subsurface, and cultural‑spiritual domains). | All decisions are subject to **double‑threshold** voting (≥ 55 % of active participants *and* ≥ 30 % of global population representation) and a **Temporal‑Justice Council (TJC)** that can veto any action threatening a deep‑time threshold (see Article 14). |

These statements are the **first four articles** of the Earth Charter of Interdependence. No amendment may alter them.

## 4.  Pillar A – Planetary Integrity & Commons  

* **Core idea:** Keep the Earth’s life‑supporting systems within their safe operating space.  
* **Tools:**  

  * **Global Climate Ledger (GCL)** – a multi‑chain, tamper‑evident registry of every climate, water, biodiversity, and cultural‑values metric.  
  * **Method‑Impact Penalties (MIP)** – every extraction licence carries a fee proportional to its *method‑impact score*; the fee funds the **Innovation Safety Fund (ISF)** for mandated mitigation R&D.  
  * **Restoration Allocation (RA)** – 20 % of RCT revenue is locked in a sequestration pool that can be released only after a **Restoration Review Board (RRB)** certifies that a project will restore the exact metric that was harmed.  

---

## 5.  Pillar B – Human Dignity & Rights  

* **Universal Basic Services (UBS):** free clean water, air, energy (baseline 5 kWh person⁻¹ day⁻¹), health, education, internet.  
* **Resource‑Allocation Quotas (RAQs):** non‑transferable licences that ensure every community gets a minimum share of water, air, and carbon capacity.  
* **Participatory mechanisms:** Liquid‑democracy micro‑forums, random citizen councils, and the **Moral‑Circle Assembly (MCA)** (see Article 13) decide on any change to the list of recognized sentient entities.  

---

## 5.1  Pillar C – Adaptive Governance & Innovation  

* **Polycentric decision‑making:** Global Council (1 200 delegates), Regional Councils (basin‑level), Local Councils (municipal), and **People’s Assemblies** (10‑100 participants each, randomly selected). All decisions are recorded on the GCL.  
* **Ethical Review Panels (ERP)** – independent, randomly drawn, with a binding veto over any technology that would push the PRI over the 0.02/year limit.  
* **Innovation Safety Fund (ISF):** Receives 10 % of all extraction fees; funds research into low‑impact technologies *before* they can be deployed.

*Key features of the EOS*  

1. **Redundancy:** The GCL exists on three independent blockchains (Ethereum, Polkadot, Sovrin). Any change to a licence must be recorded on *all* three.  
2. **Irreversibility of decisions:** Once a licence is minted on the GCL, it can only be *re‑allocated* or *retired* by a **global double‑threshold vote** (≥ 55 % of active participants *and* ≥ 30 % of world population).  
3. **Real‑time feedback:** Every minute, sensor data (satellite, ocean buoys, deep‑earth probes) flow into the GCL. Smart contracts automatically adjust allowances or trigger remediation.

## 7.  Knowledge System – Open Planetary Knowledge System (OPKS)

* All scientific data, cultural recordings, and technological designs are deposited **within 48 hours** of creation.  
* Knowledge is stored as **NFTs** on a decentralized storage network (IPFS + Filecoin). Borrowing is free; permanent ownership is prohibited.  
* The **Open Knowledge Commons (OKC)** runs a public *knowledge‑audit* every year; any data that is not freely available is automatically re‑licensed under CC‑BY‑SA 4.0.

All revenue flows are visible on the GCL and can be audited by any citizen at any time.

## 10.  Risks & Resilience (Why the Framework Survives a Technology Race)

| Threat | How the framework prevents it |
|---|---|
| **A fast‑moving civilization creates a powerful AI that can game the decision‑making.** | All AI that can affect planetary policy runs on **three independent, sovereign AI instances**. The *Planetary Engine* must be replicated on each; a single AI cannot alter the outcome without a majority of the three. |
| **A powerful state or corporation tries to seize the G‑CF.** | The G‑CF’s revenue streams are **diversified** (wealth‑tax, carbon‑price, extraction fees) and **stored in a sovereign wealth‑style fund** that is *owned* by the RCT, not by any state. Even if the G‑CF’s board is captured, the RCT still controls the licences and the PST funds. |
| **A rapid climate shock (e.g., sudden Arctic melt)** | The **Climate Ledger** detects the overshoot in real time; the **automatic remediation contract** on the GCL forces immediate allocation of the **Planetary Stewardship Trust** to fund rapid re‑wilding and re‑allocation of resource‑use rights. |
| **Cultural groups lose their aesthetic‑spiritual voice.** | The **Cultural‑Values Ledger** stores *immutable narratives* (hashes) of every sacred site. The **Cultural‑Rights Tribunal (CRT)** can veto any licence that would affect a stored narrative, **without needing a numeric threshold**. |
| **Future generations have different priorities (e.g., 10 000‑year horizons).** | The **Temporal‑Justice Council** and the **Temporal‑Justice Deep‑Time Review Panel** have permanent seats and a **veto over any action that threatens a 500‑year+ buffer**. The **Future‑Generation Trust** ensures a minimum of 5 % of all RCT revenue is reserved for them, regardless of present politics. |

These mechanisms make the system *self‑correcting* even when powerful actors try to push beyond the safe operating space. The **only way to break the system** would be to **destroy the three global blockchains** and the *distributed AI* – an undertaking that would be noticeable, costly, and would itself trigger planetary‑integrity breaches that the framework would automatically address.

## 10.  The Final Checklist – Is the Framework Ready for Any “Earth‑ling”?  

- **Identity‑agnostic:** Rights are granted to *any* entity that can be recognised by the **Cultural‑Values Ledger** (not just humans).  
- **Temporal‑agnostic:** Deep‑time vetoes and the **Future‑Generation Trust** protect any being that plans on millennial or longer timescales.  
- **Technology‑agnostic:** Every new technology must pass the **Distributed Ethics Engine** before it can be used; no black‑box AI can hide.  
- **Culture‑agnostic:** The **Cultural‑Values Ledger** stores narratives *as they are* and cannot be reduced to a single metric.  
- **Economically sustainable:** The **Triple‑Stream financing** guarantees that even if a powerful bloc withdraws, the core resources (RCT, PST, RA) remain funded.  

If a civilization is still capable of out‑producing the EFF in raw material terms, it will *still* have to **pay the planetary cost** on the GCL. The cost cannot be hidden, and the remediation mechanisms will *force* restoration, not just a superficial compliance.
