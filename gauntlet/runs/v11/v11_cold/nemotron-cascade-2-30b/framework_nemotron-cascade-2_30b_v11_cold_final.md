**Earth‑First Foundational Framework (EFF) – Version 1.0**  
*The constitution of a planet that cares for its inhabitants, past and future, while giving every person and every sentient being a legal right to exist, thrive, and evolve.*

---

## 1.  Vision  

> **All sentient beings—human and non‑human, present and future—together steward a resilient, just, and thriving planet.**  

Everything in the framework is a tool for making that vision concrete, enforceable, and self‑correcting.

---

## 2.  Core Ontology – The Four Non‑Negotiable Statements  

| # | Statement | Operational Meaning (in the framework) |
|---|-----------|-------------------------------------------|
| **1** | **The planet is a living system.**  Earth’s life‑supporting processes (climate, water, carbon, nitrogen, biodiversity) have finite, scientifically defined **safe‑operating limits**. | All decisions must keep the **Planetary Risk Index (PRI)** below 0.20 (the threshold for safe operating space). |
| **2** | **Humans (and any future sentient entities) are embedded participants, not owners.** | Every decision must respect **the planetary budget** – you cannot extract more than the safe operating limits. |
| **3** | **Every sentient entity has intrinsic worth.** | The **Cultural‑Values Ledger (CVL)** records each ecosystem, ecosystem component, and sentient being as a legal person with a *right to existence, health, and evolution.* |
| **4** | **Justice is temporal and spatial.**  Every decision must be evaluated for its impact on the ability of **future generations and future non‑human life** to meet their needs. | The **Temporal‑Justice Council (TJC)** can veto any project that would push the planetary budget beyond its safe limits for *future* generations. |

These four statements are the **constitutional spine** of the Earth‑First Framework (EFF). No amendment may modify them.

## 3.  The Earth‑First Charter (12 Articles – self‑enforcing via the Global Climate Ledger)

| Article | Short Text | How it is enforced |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Interdependence** | All life exists in a web of mutual support; no entity may thrive by destroying another. | Every project must show a *net‑positive* effect on the **Ecological‑Integrity Index (EI)** on the Global Climate Ledger (GCL). If the index would fall, the licence is automatically revoked. |
| **2. Planetary Boundaries** | Human activity may not exceed safe‑operating limits for climate, water, nitrogen, biodiversity, and air quality. | The Global Climate Ledger (GCL) monitors the four planetary boundaries. When a metric exceeds its *Safety Band* (e.g., 1.5 °C warming), an **automatic remediation contract** is triggered and the offending activity’s licence is cancelled. |
| **3. Rights of Nature** | Every ecosystem, ecosystem component, and non‑human sentient entity is a legal person with the right to exist, thrive, and evolve. | The **Cultural‑Values Ledger (CVL)** stores immutable records of each ecosystem’s intrinsic worth. If a decision would lower the *Ecological Integrity Index* of a legal person below 0.9, the licence is automatically revoked and a *Restoration Funding Contract* is activated. |
| **4. Universal Dignity** | Every sentient being has the right to health, shelter, water, food, education, and meaningful participation. | **Universal Basic Services (UBS)**: free clean water, clean air, clean air, basic health care, education, internet. The **Universal Basic Services Agency (UBS‑Agency)** allocates resources from the **Global Commons Fund (G‑CF)**. |
| **5. Equitable Resource Allocation** | Commons (air, oceans, biodiversity) are allocated based on need, not on wealth or extraction capacity. | The **Resource‑Allocation Quotas (RAQs)** are non‑transferable, non‑tradeable, and must be approved by the *local community* via a liquid‑democracy vote. |
| **6. Common‑but‑Differentiated Responsibilities** | Nations that have contributed most to environmental degradation must shoulder a larger share of remediation. | The **Historical‑Debt Index (HDI)** automatically calculates each nation’s share of the *restoration budget*. The **Planetary Stewardship Trust (PST)** receives that share and funds restoration projects. |
| **6. Universal Access to Knowledge** | All scientific, cultural, and technological knowledge is a public good. | All knowledge is deposited in the **Open Knowledge Commons (OKC)** as a non‑fungible token (NFT). When a knowledge piece is deposited, it is free to remix; the license automatically revokes any proprietary claim. |
| **8. Peaceful Co‑existence** | Violence is prohibited except for direct self‑defense. | The **Conflict‑De‑Escalation Protocol (CDP)** on the GCL automatically opens a global deliberative forum within 24 h when a conflict‑risk index exceeds a threshold. |
| **9. Responsibility to Future Generations** | Every decision must keep the planet’s safe‑operating space for future generations. | The **Temporal‑Justice Council (TJC)** can issue a **deep‑time moratorium** on any activity that would push a boundary beyond its safe‑operating limit for >100 years. The decision must be approved by a *double‑threshold* of the Global Climate Ledger. |
| **10. Participatory Accountability** | Everyone can shape decisions; decisions are reversible. | Every policy is backed by a **Liquid‑Democracy Vote** (55 % yes + 30 % global representation) and is recorded on the immutable GCL. |
| **11. Innovation with Ethical Guardrails** | Any technology that could alter planetary cycles (AI, geo‑engineering, genetic drives) must pass a *Safety‑First* review before deployment. | The **Ethical Review Panel (ERP)** – a rotating panel of ethicists, scientists, and Indigenous keepers – can veto any project that would raise the **Planetary Risk Index (PRI)** by > 0.02 in a year. |
| **12. Planetary Stewardship Trust (PST)** – a sovereign wealth‑style fund that owns the *rights to remediate* and funds **Universal Basic Services** (clean water, clean air, energy, health, internet, education). | The PST can only release funds when a *restoration target* reaches a 99 % scientifically verified restoration index. | The **Planetary Stewardship Trust (PST)** automatically disburses money to restoration projects when the *Ecological‑Integrity Index* for the affected ecosystem climbs above 0.9. |

All 12 Articles are **self‑executing**: the moment a metric falls outside the allowed limits, a smart contract on the **Global Climate Ledger (GCL)** automatically triggers the appropriate corrective action. No parliament, no minister, no corporation can stop it.

## 5.  Funding – The “Triple‑Stream” Model

1. **Extraction‑Use Fees** (mineral, water, carbon) → 55 % of the total fund.  
   *Paid by the entity that extracts, the fee is automatically routed to the **Planetary Stewardship Trust (PST)**.*  

2. **Global Wealth & Carbon Tax** (15 % wealth‑tax, 15 % carbon tax) → 30 % of total funding.  
   - 40 % → **Global Commons Fund (G‑CF)** for large‑scale restoration.  
   - 35 % → **Innovation Safety Fund (ISF)** for low‑impact technologies.  
   - 15 % → **Cultural‑Values Fund (C‑Fund)** for preserving cultural narratives.  

3. **Method‑Impact Penalties (MIP)** – every activity that exceeds the allowed *method‑impact* (e.g., uses a fossil‑fuel‑intensive extraction technique) pays an additional fee that automatically goes to the **Innovation Safety Fund (ISF)**, which finances research that can *replace* the harmful practice with a low‑impact alternative.

All money moves are recorded on the **Global Climate Ledger (GCL)**, so you can see exactly where the money goes and why.

## 6.  Knowledge System – Open Planetary Knowledge System (OPKS)

- All scientific data, cultural narratives, and technology blueprints are deposited as **non‑fungible tokens (NFTs)** on the **Open Knowledge Commons (OKS)**.  
- The data is replicated on **Ethereum, Polkadot, and Sovrin** – three independent blockchains, each run by a different sovereign community.  
- Every metric that feeds the **Global Climate Ledger (GCL)** comes from the OPKS, so you cannot *hide* data.  
- **Open Knowledge Commons (OKS)** also houses the *legal documents* (charters, licences, contracts) – they are all public and can be edited only by the *randomly selected citizen councils*.
