### 1. The Economy of Externalities
**The Mechanism:**

*   **Narrow Morality:** If the forest, the river, the worker, or the atmosphere has no moral standing, they are **free resources** to be exploited until exhaustion. *   **Survival Impact:** Narrow morality maximizes short-term wealth but creates long-term existential debt. Broad morality ensures the capital (natural and social) remains intact for the future.

### 2. Social Cohesion and Conflict Resolution
**The Mechanism:**

*   **Narrow Morality:** "Us" is the tribe, the nation, or the corporation. "Them" is an enemy to be exploited or eliminated. This creates a **Zero-Sum Mindset**. Conflict becomes a primary mode of interaction. Constant internal or external warfare drains resources and distracts from problem-solving.
*   **Broad Morality:** "Us" expands to humanity, or even all sentient life. This fosters **Cooperation**. Large-scale cooperation (e.g., climate accords, pandemic response) is only possible when we view the health of the "other" as integral to our own.
*   **Survival Impact:** Civilizations trapped in narrow moral boundaries spend their energy managing conflict. Civilizations with broad moral circles spend their energy solving shared problems.

### 3. Risk Assessment and Blind Spots

*   **Survival Impact:** True power is not the ability to take, but the ability to endure. Endurance requires **Reciprocity**. You cannot extract without giving back. Expanding moral consideration is the only way to institutionalize that reciprocity.
