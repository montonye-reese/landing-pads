# THE SYMBIOTIC COVENANT
**A Foundational Framework for the Sentient Inhabitants of Earth**

## PREAMBLE
We, the sentient inhabitants of Earth, recognizing our absolute interdependence and the fragility of the biological systems that sustain us, hereby establish this Covenant. We acknowledge that no single culture, ideology, or species holds dominion over the others, and that our continued existence depends not on the victory of one over another, but on the flourishing of the whole. 

This Covenant serves as the primary operating system for planetary coexistence. It defines the boundaries of acceptable behavior and the minimum requirements for a just and sustainable existence.

---

## ARTICLE I: PLANETARY STEWARDSHIP
**The Primacy of the Biosphere**

**Section 1.01: The Biological Baseline.** The health, stability, and biodiversity of the Earth’s biosphere are the primary prerequisites for all other rights. No right or economic interest shall supersede the integrity of the planetary life-support systems.

**Section 1.02: The Seven-Generation Mandate.** All systemic decisions regarding the extraction of resources, land use, and atmospheric composition must be evaluated by their projected impact on the seventh generation of descendants. Actions that provide short-term gain at the cost of long-term viability are prohibited.

**Section 1.03: Interspecies Equity.** Recognition is granted to all non-human sentient beings. While the framework for their rights differs from humans, they possess an inherent right to exist within their natural habitats free from systemic cruelty and unnecessary eradication.

---

## ARTICLE II: INHERENT DIGNITY
**The Ethical Baseline**

**Section 2.01: Intrinsic Value.** Every sentient being possesses an inherent dignity that is independent of utility, status, origin, or merit. This dignity is inviolable.

**Section 2.02: The Non-Aggression Axiom.** Every inhabitant has the right to exist free from coerced violence, torture, and systemic oppression. Aggression is only permissible as a direct, proportional response to protect the life or dignity of oneself or others.

**Section 2.03: The Right to Basic Sustenance.** To ensure the possibility of dignity, access to clean water, basic nutrition, and emergency healthcare is recognized as a planetary right. Geography and wealth shall not be barriers to the fulfillment of these biological imperatives.

**Section 2.04: Subjective Autonomy.** The internal world of the individual—including belief, identity, and cultural expression—is sovereign. No entity shall coerce the internal state of another, provided that the individual's outward actions do not violate the Non-Aggression Axiom.

---

## ARTICLE III: EPISTEMIC HUMILITY
**The Cognitive Baseline**

**Section 3.01: The Priority of Evidence.** In matters concerning the physical world and shared reality, evidence, reason, and the scientific method shall be the primary tools for arbitration. 

**Section 3.02: The Iterative Mindset.** Knowledge is recognized as provisional. All inhabitants are encouraged to maintain a state of openness, accepting the responsibility to update their conclusions when presented with superior evidence.

**Section 3.03: The Duty of Fair Representation.** In conflict or debate, parties commit to the principle of "Steel-manning": the requirement to represent an opposing view in its strongest, most honest form before attempting to refute it.

---

## ARTICLE IV: RECIPROCAL RESPONSIBILITY
**The Social Baseline**

**Section 4.01: The Balance of Rights and Duties.** No right is absolute without a corresponding responsibility. Those who claim the protections of this Covenant accept the duty to contribute to the maintenance of the systems that provide those protections.

**Section 4.02: Proportional Contribution.** Every inhabitant has a duty to contribute to the collective wellbeing of the planet and its people, proportional to their capacity and resources.

**Section 4.03: Restorative Justice.** Conflict shall be resolved through mediation and restoration rather than retribution. The goal of justice is the repair of the harm and the reintegration of the individual into the community.

**Section 4.04: Global Solidarity.** A crisis affecting any significant portion of the planetary population—whether ecological, biological, or social—is recognized as a crisis for all. Response to such suffering is a systemic necessity.

---

## FINAL PROVISION: THE FILTER
Any law, policy, or technological implementation enacted upon the Earth must pass through the **Symbiotic Filter**:
1. Does it preserve the **Biosphere**?
2. Does it uphold **Dignity**?
3. Is it based on **Evidence**?
4. Does it balance **Rights with Responsibility**?

*If the answer to any of these is "No," the action is deemed a systemic failure and must be revised.*
