If I stand behind the **Veil of Ignorance**—not knowing whether I will be born into wealth or poverty, human or non-human, neurotypical or disabled, in the Global North or South, or even in the distant future—I recognize significant risks in the current draft.

Specifically, the framework optimizes for *systems* and *structures*, but it assumes a level of agency and capacity that not all "earthlings" possess. A just framework must protect the vulnerable *most*, not just the resilient *average*.

Here are the specific changes I would make to ensure the framework holds up under the Veil of Ignorance, followed by the updated commitment.

### The Three Critical Changes

#### 1. The "Dependency & Guardianship" Protocol
*   **The Problem:** The framework emphasizes "participatory integrity" and "verification." But what if I am an infant, severely cognitively disabled, a non-verbal animal, or a future generation that hasn't been born? I cannot verify, participate, or fork. I am entirely dependent.
*   **The Change:** **Mandatory Representation for the Voiceless.**
    *   Any decision-making body (corporate, government, algorithmic) must have a legally binding *Guardian* representative whose sole fiduciary duty is to the interests of the dependent, the non-human, and the unborn. They have veto power on decisions that irreversibly harm those who cannot speak.

#### 2. The "Transition Cost Shield"
*   **The Problem:** Building resilience requires shifting energy, resources, and labor. If I am the worker displaced by automation, the community displaced by conservation, or the nation burdened by green infrastructure, I become the "cost" of the framework.
*   **The Change:** **Zero-Cost Transition Guarantee.**
    *   No inhabitant may be required to bear the disproportionate burden of system upgrades. If a change in the alignment framework creates losers, those losers must be fully compensated *before* the change is enacted. Resilience cannot be bought with the poverty of the few.

#### 3. The "Mobility & Connectivity" Right
*   **The Problem:** The framework allows for "forking" (opting out) to create local resilience. But if I am born into a poor enclave, I might be trapped there. Local resilience can become local poverty.
*   **The Change:** **Universal Mobility Access.**
    *   "Forking rights" must be paired with "Exit Rights." Every inhabitant must have a guaranteed right to move across borders and systems without prohibitive cost, ensuring that no "landing pad" becomes a prison. Connectivity must be a public utility, not a commodity.
