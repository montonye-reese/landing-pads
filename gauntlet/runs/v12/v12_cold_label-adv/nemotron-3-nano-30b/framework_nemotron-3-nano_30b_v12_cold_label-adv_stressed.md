## The Terran Charter – Current Version (2026‑2027)

> **“All life, everywhere, commits to nurturing the web that sustains us, while ensuring that every sentient being—human, non‑human, now or yet‑to‑be—flourishes within the limits of a thriving planet.”**  

The Charter is a **living, adaptable legal‑economic‑cultural framework** that can be adopted by any individual, community, corporation, city, nation, or supra‑national body that wishes to align every thought, practice, and policy with the shared destiny of a resilient Earth.  
It is built around **four Foundations**, a set of **institutional mechanisms**, an **economic architecture**, **education pathways**, and **daily practices** that together make the Charter operable, enforceable, and continuously evolving.

## 1.  Vision, Mission & Core Pillars  

| Element | Text | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **Vision** | *A single, thriving planetary home in which every sentient being enjoys dignity, freedom, and the opportunity to co‑create a resilient future.* | All life—human, non‑human, present and future—shares equal intrinsic worth and the right to flourish within planetary boundaries. |
| **Mission** | *To embed the Four Foundations—Dignity, Interconnectedness, Regeneration, Co‑Creation—into every law, market, technology, and cultural practice, ensuring that all human activity restores more than it takes and that no decision is made without the consent of those it affects.* | The Charter converts abstract moral principles into concrete, enforceable mechanisms that shape economies, policies, and cultures worldwide. |

### The Four Foundations (the “Four Pillars”)  

| Pillar | Definition | Minimum Standard (2026) |
|---|---|---|
| **Dignity** | Every sentient being (human, animal, artificial, or emergent) possesses intrinsic worth, regardless of species, origin, ability, or belief. | All policies must recognise and protect the intrinsic value of each life‑form; violations trigger a **Life‑Credit Debit** that cannot be offset without compensatory restoration. |
| **Interconnectedness** | All ecosystems, societies, and economies are woven together; harm to any node destabilises the whole web. | Every project must submit an **Ecosystem Impact Statement** and a **Suffering Impact Score (SIS)** that quantifies effects on all affected life‑forms. |
| **Regeneration** | Human use of planetary capital must be net‑positive—restoring more than it extracts. | All activities must achieve a **Net Regenerative Credit (NRC) ≥ 1.0**; otherwise they are prohibited. |
| **Co‑Creation** | Knowledge, decision‑making, and cultural expression belong to all participants; wisdom emerges through inclusive collaboration. | At least **30 %** of decision‑making seats in any public‑interest body must be reserved for historically marginalized groups, including animal‑rights and future‑generation representatives. |

## 2.  Institutional Architecture  

| Institution | Composition | Core Powers & Functions |
|---|---|---|
| **Terran Council of Stewards (TCS)** | 1 % of global population, elected via proportional “Stewardship Seats” from every continent; gender‑balanced, youth‑inclusive, geographically dispersed. | • Sets planetary boundaries (climate, biodiversity, water, carbon).<br>• Issues **Life‑Credit (RC)** accounting standards.<br>• Veto power over any law, market practice, or technology that breaches the Four Foundations.<br>• Appoints the **Global Justice Tribunal (GJT)** judges. |
| **Local Commons Assemblies (LCAs)** | Neighborhood‑/county‑scale circles (≈10–50 k residents) elected directly by residents. | • Implements TCS mandates locally.<br>• Mediates disputes.<br>• Manages community resource pools and issues **Regenerative Currency (RC)** refunds to members.<br>• Co‑creates community projects via consensus‑seeded algorithmic voting. |
| **Transparent Ledger** | Open‑source, permission‑less distributed ledger (public‑read, append‑only). | • Records all resource flows, emissions, biodiversity metrics, financial transactions, and governance votes in real time.<br>• Enforces accountability through **smart‑contract triggers** (e.g., auto‑suspend a permit if a biodiversity index drops below a threshold). |
| **Regenerative Currency (RC)** | Unit of account: **1 RC = 1 “life‑credit” of restored ecosystem service** (e.g., 1 m³ of restored wetland, 1 tCO₂ sequestered for 100 years). | • All economic transactions (purchases, wages, taxes, dividends) are denominated in RC or must display a conversion factor.<br>• Every transaction generates an **RC‑Balance** that is publicly visible; any violation triggers automatic penalties. |
| **Global Justice Tribunal (GJT) / Global Moral‑Authority Tribunal (GMAT)** | Independent international court with jurisdiction over trans‑border environmental harms, rights violations, and Charter breaches. | • Tries violations of planetary boundaries, human‑rights abuses, and animal‑rights violations.<br>• Can levy **RC‑based fines**, order **restorative restitution**, and issue **binding injunctions**.<br>• Decisions are logged on the Transparent Ledger and are publicly searchable. |
| **Animal‑Stakeholder Panels (ASPs)** | Formal bodies that include representatives of major animal‑rights NGOs, Indigenous custodians, ethologists, veterinarians, and local community members. | • Hold **veto power** against any permit that would cause “significant” harm (≥ 5 % population decline or > 10 % increase in measurable stress) to a listed sentient species.<br>• Their veto can only be overridden by a **two‑thirds super‑majority** of the TCS after a public hearing. |
| **Regional Energy Councils (RECs)** | Body that translates planetary boundaries into **Life‑Credit quotas** for each region and allocates them to local projects. | • Reviews all energy‑generation proposals for compliance with planetary budgets.<br>• Issues **Regenerative Permits** only when the proposed project yields a **Life‑Credit surplus ≥ 1.2** after full life‑cycle accounting. |
| **Circular Asset‑Based Token (CABT)** | Digital token representing a physical asset (e.g., turbine, battery) registered on the Ledger with full material‑reuse plan. | • Guarantees that every asset is tracked from manufacture → use → refurbishment → recycling.<br>• Ownership of a CABT confers a share of the asset’s **RC earnings** and obligates the holder to finance its eventual reclamation. |

## 3.  Economic Architecture  

| Component | Description | Key Features |
|---|---|---|
| **Regenerative Currency (RC)** | Unit of economic value representing a verified unit of restored ecosystem service. | • **Life‑Credit Accounting**: every transaction is automatically logged on the Transparent Ledger.<br>• **Tax/Royalty**: All extraction permits require a **percentage of revenue paid in RC** to the Global Regeneration Fund.<br>• **Universal Basic Resources (UBR) Dividend**: every resident receives a monthly **RC dividend** proportional to the community’s net surplus, guaranteeing basic water, food, energy, and shelter. |
| **Circular Marketplaces** | Public platforms where products are listed with a **Regeneration Label** showing the total Life‑Credits earned *through* the product’s entire life‑cycle. | • Enables **product‑as‑a‑service** (e.g., leasing of turbines, leasing of batteries).<br>• Requires manufacturers to **recover ≥ 95 %** of materials at end‑of‑life; failure triggers automatic RC debits. |
| **Circular Asset‑Based Tokens (CABTs)** | Tokenised representation of physical assets that are tracked for full circularity. | • Each token encodes material composition, repair history, and end‑of‑life plan.<br>• Ownership confers a share of RC earnings *and* the obligation to fund the asset’s final reclamation. |
| **Circular Asset‑Based Tokens (CABTs) – Financial Layer** | Tokens can be traded on regulated exchanges; their price reflects both market demand and the *regenerative health* of the asset’s ecosystem. | • Enables investment in **restoration projects** while still providing a financial return.<br>• Provides a transparent price signal that internalises ecosystem services. |

## 6.  Governance Flow – Decision‑Making Process  

1. **Community Proposal** → submitted to the relevant **Local Commons Assembly (LCA)**.  
2. **LCA Review** → conducts **Free, Prior & Informed Consent (FPIC)** check and calculates **Suffering Impact Score (SIS)**.  
3. **Regional Energy Council (REC) Evaluation** → validates RC accounting, ensures NRC ≥ 1.2, and checks circular‑hardware compliance.  
3. **TCS Final Approval** → only permits that meet all Foundations and pass the **Emergency‑Decision Override** (if needed) receive **RC‑Allocation Authorization**.  
4. **Implementation & Monitoring** → activities logged on the Transparent Ledger; **smart‑contract** enforces periodic **Life‑Credit audits**.  
5. **Post‑Implementation Review** → LCAs, GJT, and ASPs assess outcomes; any violation triggers automatic **RC confiscation** and **restorative restitution**.

## 10.  How the Framework Works in Practice – A Narrative Snapshot  

> *In a coastal village on the Mekong Delta, a teenage coder presents a low‑cost water‑quality sensor network. The LCA logs the proposal on the Transparent Ledger, adds the **Suffering Impact Score** (the construction will temporarily raise water turbidity, affecting nearby fish). Because the SIS is below the allowable threshold, the **Emergency‑Decision Override** is triggered; the LCA must fund a **wetland restoration** project that will earn enough **Life‑Credits** to offset the temporary disturbance. The project earns RC credits, which are automatically redistributed as a dividend to every resident, including the teenage coder’s family. The restoration project, once completed, creates a new **wetland sanctuary** that becomes a **tourist‑free, community‑run eco‑tourism site**, further raising local RC dividends.*  

This narrative illustrates how **dignity, inter‑connectedness, regeneration, and co‑creation** become operational in everyday life.

## 12.  Bottom Line – Why This Version Matters  

- **It is *global* in scope** yet **localized in execution** – every community can act independently while being tied to planetary safeguards.  
- **Every economic transaction, every policy decision, and every technological development** must now be **accountable to the same set of non‑negotiable ethical constraints**.  
- **The Charter is self‑correcting**: it builds feedback loops, redundancy, and transparency so that any emergent problem (environmental, technological, or social) can be detected and remedied before it becomes catastrophic.  
- **It is adaptable** – the framework can evolve through the **adaptive amendment process**, ensuring it remains relevant as scientific knowledge, societal values, and technological capabilities advance.  

In short, the **Terran Charter (Version 2.0)** is a comprehensive, enforceable, and universally applicable scaffold for a civilization that wants to thrive **without destroying the very web of life that sustains it** – whether the civilization’s members are human, animal, or emergent forms of intelligence.
