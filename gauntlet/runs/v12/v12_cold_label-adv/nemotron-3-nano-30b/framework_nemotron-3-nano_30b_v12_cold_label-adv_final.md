# The Terran Charter (Version 3.0 – 2026)

> **A living, self‑correcting framework that binds humanity to the shared destiny of a thriving planet and to the dignity of every sentient being.**  

---

## 1. Vision, Mission & Pillars  

| Pillar | Definition | Daily Behaviour |
|---|---|---|
| **Dignity** | Every sentient being—human, non‑human, future‑being, AI—has intrinsic worth. | No action may be taken that violates the intrinsic worth of any sentient being without explicit, verifiable compensation (Life‑Credit). |
| **Interconnectedness** | All life, ecosystems, cultures, and societies are woven together; harm to one reverberates through the whole web. | Any action that degrades an ecosystem or community triggers an **Impact Accounting** penalty. |
| **Regeneration** | Human use of planetary capital must be *net‑positive*—we must restore what we take. | Every action must result in a **Life‑Credit (RC) gain of ≥ 1.0** after accounting for all extraction, waste, and pollution. |
| **Co‑Creation** | All parties whose actions are affected must have a meaningful voice in decisions that affect them. | Decisions are made only with **Free, Prior & Informed Consent (FPC)** from every affected group, including non‑human stakeholders. |

---

## 2.  Institutional Architecture  

| Institution | Composition | Core Function | Key Mechanism |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Terran Council of Stewards (TCS)** | 1 % of global population, elected by Local Commons Assemblies (LCAs). | Sets planetary‑scale limits (biodiversity, carbon, freshwater, water, etc.) and issues **Life‑Credit (RC) quotas**. | Operates a **Life‑Credit Allocation Engine** that translates planetary limits into quantifiable **Life‑Credits (RC)**. |
| **Regional Energy Councils (RECs)** | Locally elected bodies (≈ 5‑12 members) that manage sector‑specific policies (renewable projects, mining, land use). | Convert TCS limits into local *resource‑use caps* and allocate **Life‑Credit** budgeting for each community. | RECs verify **Restoration‑Restoration‑Asset‑Asset (RACR) contracts** before approving any new infrastructure. |
| **Local Commons Assembly (LCA)** | 10‑50 km community hub, elected by residents (including Indigenous and youth representatives). | Approves or rejects any proposed project, enforce **Free, Prior, Informed Consent (FPIC)**, and runs the **Local Restorative‑Revenue Fund**. | Decisions are logged in real‑time on the **Transparent Ledger**; any violation triggers automatic RC penalties. |
| **Transparent Ledger** | Public, open‑source, append‑only ledger (e.g., IOTA/Car­let‑Chain). | Records every **Life‑Credit transaction**, **resource extraction**, **emission flow**, and **maintenance event**. | Enables **instant, auditable audit** of any action; triggers **smart‑contract penalties** if a threshold is breached. |
| **Circular Asset Registry (CAR)** | Asset‑by‑asset digital ledger for any piece of hardware (pump, turbine, turbine‑blade, cable, etc.). | Tracks provenance, material composition, damage history, and recycling plan. | Guarantees 100 % material recovery or bio‑degradable return; assets can only be reclaimed if the **Restoration Compliance Index** ≥ 95 %. |
| **Global Justice Tribunal (GJT)** | Independent court with jurisdiction over all violations of the Charter, especially those that affect multiple nations or species. | Issues binding injunctions, imposes fines, and orders restitution. | Decisions are logged on the Ledger; enforcement actions (e.g., automatic seizure of RC assets) are executed automatically. |
| **Emergency‑Response‑Authority (ERA)** | Cross‑sector emergency committee (including citizen representatives) that can quickly sanction or shut down activities that threaten health, biodiversity, or critical infrastructure. | Issues **Stop‑Orders** when a sensor detects toxicity, structural failure, or any violation of the **Restoration‑Restoration‑Asset‑Asset (RBA) clause**. | Automatic “fail‑safe” triggers that halt any operation until compliance is restored. |

## 2.  The Pillars of the Charter

| Pillar | Definition | Enforcement Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| **Dignity** | All sentient beings have intrinsic worth. | *Dignity Impact Statement* (DIS) must be filed for any project; violation = mandatory corrective action. |
| **Interconnectedness** | The web of life is a single, integrated system. | **Suffering Impact Score (SIS)** quantifies effect on each species’ welfare; any SIS > 0 triggers a mandatory **Restoration‑Restoration‑Credit (RCr)** payout. |
| **Regeneration** | Human use of Earth’s capital must **exceed** the damage taken. | Every project must achieve **Net Life‑Credit ≥ 1.2**; otherwise the permit is denied. |
| **Co‑Creation** | Decision‑making must include all affected parties. | Minimum 30 % representation of historically marginalized groups on every LCA/Regional Energy Council/RED; mandatory **Participatory Design** for any infrastructure. |
