# The Earth Covenant – Current Framework  

*(A living, three‑tiered, polycentric system that translates humanity’s deepest values, the planet’s survival limits, and everyday social fabric into measurable norms, decision‑making pathways and cultural practices.)*  

---

## 1.  Core Architecture – Three Inter‑locking Tiers  

| Tier | What it is | Why it matters | Primary Outputs |
|------|------------|----------------|-----------------|
| **1️⃣ Human Core** | The set of **inalienable values and rights** that every sentient being possesses simply by being alive. | Provides the moral “heart” that cannot be bargained away. | *Human‑Well‑Being Index* (physical, mental, cultural health), *Freedom‑from‑Arbitrary‑Harm* metrics. |
| **2️⃣ Planetary Context** | The **boundary conditions** imposed by Earth’s physical and ecological systems (the nine Planetary Boundaries, climate trajectories, biodiversity status). | Defines the “sandbox” within which all human activity must stay. | *Planetary‑Boundary Status* (e.g., carbon budget remaining, freshwater use, biodiversity intactness). |
| **3️⃣ Community Nexus** | The **networks of relationships** where values and constraints are enacted daily (neighbourhoods, schools, workplaces, Indigenous territories, digital collectives). | The place where decisions become concrete actions and feedback loops. | *Pillar‑Status Scores* (Dignity, Stewardship, Knowledge, etc.) for each community; *Resilience & Regeneration* indicators. |

**Feedback rule:** Any breach in Tier 2 (e.g., exceeding the carbon budget) immediately lowers Tier 1 indicators (air‑quality‑related health) and propagates to Tier 3 (food insecurity, migration). The system constantly monitors these loops.

---

## 2.  The Seven Pillar Tracks  

Each pillar is a **track** that runs across all three tiers. The pillars are inter‑dependent; improving one often strengthens several others.

| Pillar (Track) | Core Idea | Key Indicators (illustrative) |
|----------------|-----------|-------------------------------|
| **1️⃣ Human Dignity & Rights** | Every person and sentient being has intrinsic worth; rights are universal. | Human‑Well‑Being Index, Freedom‑from‑Harm Score, Cultural‑Practice Sustainability. |
| **2️⃣ Ecological Stewardship** | Humans are part of Earth’s life‑support system; we must keep ecosystems functional. | Planetary‑Boundary Status (climate, biodiversity, freshwater, etc.), Net‑Regenerative Resource Flow. |
| **3️⃣ Knowledge Integration** | Truth is provisional; we co‑create models, share data, and keep open channels for revision. | Data‑Transparency Score, Open‑Source Contribution Index, Scientific‑Consensus Ratio. |
| **4️⃣ Creative Co‑Creation** | Societies thrive when they design together, not when top‑down edicts dominate. | Participation Index (deliberation events, co‑design projects), Diversity of Co‑Creation (cultural, disciplinary). |
| **5️⃣ Resilience & Redundancy** | No system should be a single point of failure; redundancy and adaptation are mandatory. | System‑Resilience Score (supply‑chain diversification, climate‑proofing), Recovery Time after Shocks. |
| **6️⃣ Justice & Equity** | Distributional equity is a prerequisite for collective flourishing. | Gini of Resource Access, Climate‑Justice Index, Indigenous‑Rights Compliance. |
| **7️⃣ Adaptive Governance** | Decision‑making is a learning loop, not a static vote. | Institutional‑Adaptability Index, Governance Transparency, Review Cadence (e.g., annual Pillar reviews). |

Each indicator is **computed at multiple scales** (local, regional, global) and displayed on the **Covenant Dashboard** (see § 4).

## 3.  Foundational Operating Principles  

These six principles are *hard‑coded* into every protocol, software tool, and civic practice.

1. **Interdependence** – All systems (biological, social, technological) are coupled; we treat impacts holistically.  
2. **Precautionary Integrity** – When credible catastrophic risk is identified despite uncertainty, we act *pre‑emptively* while preserving the right to refine.  
3. **Participatory Inclusion** – Every voice that can be reasonably heard has a seat at the table; decisions are proportional to the degree of affect.  
4. **Regenerative Thinking** – Actions must *give back* at least as much as they *take*; net‑positive is the baseline.  
5. **Transparency & Traceability** – Every choice, from supply‑chain to algorithmic recommendation, must be auditable in a publicly accessible ledger.  
6. **Reciprocity** – Consumption, labor, or data must be balanced by a contribution to the stewardship of the source.

## 4.  Operational Mechanisms  

### 4.1.  The **Covenant Dashboard**  

* A **global, interoperable data portal** (API‑first) that aggregates all pillar metrics for every community that has signed up.  
* Data sources: satellite remote sensing, citizen‑science apps, national statistics, blockchain‑verified supply‑chain logs, social‑well‑being surveys.  
* End‑users: municipal planners, school teachers, individual citizens, NGOs.  
* API example: `GET /pillar/ecological?region=XYZ&level=local` → returns **Ecological‑Footprint Score** (e.g., “+0.12 tCO₂e per capita – 15 % below the remaining carbon budget”).  

All dashboards are **styled for local languages** but share a common data schema, enabling seamless cross‑scale comparison.

### 4.2.  Nested Consensus Layers  

1. **Micro‑Layer (Household/Street)** – Daily actions guided by *Community Agreements* that are derived from the Dashboard (e.g., water‑use caps, waste‑reduction pledges).  
2. **Meso‑Layer (Neighbourhood/Institution)** – Local councils convene **Co‑Design Workshops** (minimum 30 % participation) to co‑create resource‑allocation plans. Decisions are recorded on an **open ledger** and automatically fed into the Adaptive Governance Engine.  
3. **Macro‑Layer (Nation/International)** – National policies must pass a **Pillar‑Weighted Impact Assessment** (PWIA). The PWIA requires a *justice‑weight* > 0.7 for any resource‑intensive project; otherwise the law is blocked.  

Each layer can **override** the one below only if the **Justice & Equity** score of the higher layer exceeds a pre‑set threshold (default 0.8). This guarantees that the most vulnerable have ultimate veto power.

### 4.3.  The **Planetary Lexicon**  

A **multilingual, multimodal digital dictionary** that defines every Covenant term *in context* (e.g., “Regeneration” → “Restoring more than you consume, with concrete examples for soils, data, language, and culture”).  

* Features:*  
  * Machine‑readable JSON‑LD version for APIs.  
  * Community‑curated edit cycles every 18 months.  
  * Embedded audio, pictograms and tactile icons for low‑literacy settings.  

Because the Lexicon is a **living contract**, each culture can map its own metaphors onto the same logical backbone.

## 6.  Governance – Polycentric, Yet Integrated  

1. **Local** – Community councils, Indigenous authorities, neighbourhood assemblies (decisions recorded on public ledgers).  
2. **Regional** – River‑basin authorities, state climate ministries, digital ecosystem regulators.  
3. **Global** – Global Consensus Forum, the Stewardship Fund, the Lexicon Oversight Board.  

**Contractual linkages:** A region may allocate water only within the planetary water‑budget; a city cannot exceed its carbon‑budget without a global sign‑off; a corporation must meet its regenerative‑resource target to receive a licence to operate.  

All layers are bound by the **Justice & Equity veto**: any single community that is *materially affected* (e.g., living on a critical ecological zone) can invoke a veto, requiring a super‑majority (≥ 85 %) and a justification that the action respects the planetary limits.

---

## 7.  Evolution – The Covenant Grows With Us  

* **Annual Review Sessions** – Each pillar is interrogated: “What new threats have emerged? What new capacities have we built?”  
* **Deliberative Updating** – Draft amendments are released to the Planetary Lexicon, debated for at least 60 days, and ratified with a global super‑majority (85 %). No amendment may erode Human Dignity or Ecological Stewardship; any proposal that does so triggers a **Planet‑Safeguard Committee** veto.  
* **Cultural Pluralism Safeguard** – The Lexicon always offers multiple cultural framings of the same logical rule, preventing a single worldview from dominating.  

Because the Covenant’s **governance rules** themselves are subject to the same six operating principles, the system can **learn to rewrite its own rules** while staying anchored to immutable ethics.

## 8.  What It Looks Like in Practice – A Day‑in‑the‑Life Snapshot  

**Mira, a mother in a semi‑urban Brazilian neighborhood** checks the Covenant Dashboard on her phone. The local **Well‑Being Index** is 73/100; the **Ecological‑Footprint** shows a 12 % overshoot of the regional freshwater boundary.

1. **Co‑Design Workshop (Meso‑Layer)** – Residents collectively design a rain‑water harvesting network and a community seed bank that protects Indigenous seed sovereignty.  
2. **Pillar Scores Updated** – Participation Index rises to 88 %; Regeneration Score for freshwater improves to –8 % (now below the regional budget).  
3. **Dashboard Auto‑Feedback** – The AI “Pillar Watch” flags the water improvement as a *global best practice* and pushes a notification to the Global Consensus Forum.  
4. **Six Months Later** – Mira’s community sees a 5‑point rise in well‑being, a 3 % reduction in water‑footprint, and a 70 % increase in community‑reported agency. The **Justice & Equity** score for the neighbourhood has climbed from 0.62 to 0.78, unlocking the ability to veto a proposed highway that would cut through a critical biodiversity corridor.

All of this happens **without a single top‑down decree**; it is the Earth Covenant operating in real time.
