## 3.  The Six Operating Principles (the “operating DNA”)

1. **Interdependence** – everything you do touches energy, water, biodiversity, and sentient‑being experience.  
2. **Precautionary Integrity** – if credible data shows a risk of harming sentient beings or planetary boundaries, the system *must* act pre‑emptively.  
3. **Participatory Inclusion** – every sentient community (humans, animals, synthetic minds) gets a weight proportional to its *experience‑units*; no veto can be overridden without a transparent, time‑limited review.  
4. **Regenerative Thinking** – every activity must give back at least as much as it takes (net‑positive carbon, water, biodiversity, and sentient‑being experience).  
4. **Transparency & Traceability** – every transaction (energy, water, water‑use, carbon, TSH, and animal‑life‑year purchases) is recorded on the **Covenant Ledger** (public blockchain). No amendment can be made without a public audit.  
5. **Reciprocity** – every consumption unit must be matched by a restoration action (e.g., for each kWh of electricity used, a certain amount of animal‑life‑year is purchased, or a hectare of habitat is restored).

## 4.  Governance – Polycentric, Poly‑Sentient

| Level | Who participates | How decisions are taken |
|---|---|---|
| **Micro (local)** | Community Councils (including Farm‑Worker Co‑Ops, Indigenous Councils, Digital‑Sovereign Collectives) | Each community’s *Council* decides on local projects; its vote is weighted by the **Experience‑Units (ET‑units)** it represents (e.g., a bee colony has many individuals → higher weight). |
| **Regional** | Regional Basin Authorities (e.g., river basins) | Uses **Age‑Engine** to compute *regional‑wide caps* (carbon, water, biodiversity, TSH). If the projected *cumulative TSH* would exceed the limit, the system automatically curtails new projects in the region. |
| **Global** | **Global Consensus Forum (GCF)**, **Sentient‑Being Review Board (SBRB)**, **Adaptive Governance Engine (AGE)** | **GCF** sets global caps (carbon, water, biodiversity, TSH). **SBRB** can veto any planetary‑scale project that would breach any of the four caps. **AGE** auto‑adjusts the budgets when scientific models show a risk. |

All three levels are *inter‑linked* on the same blockchain ledger; any change to a local project is reflected instantly on the global dashboard, and the higher tiers can trigger a *cascading review* if a region’s numbers are out of balance.

## 4.  The Seven Pillars – Concrete Implementation

### 1. Human‑Dignity & Rights (Human Core)

* **Legal Right to a Living Baseline** – every person (and every sentient community) receives a *minimum resource bundle*: clean water, clean air, safe housing, and *access to clean energy* (minimum 1 kWh/day).  
* **Human‑Dignity Index (HDI)** = (Average Well‑Being Score) × (Living‑Wage coverage). Must be ≥ 0.80 for a region to be “stable”.  
* **Legal‑Right Sub‑Pillar** – the Covenant guarantees *free, high‑quality legal aid* for any person whose rights are threatened (e.g., a farmer denied a clean water source).

### 2. Ecological Stewardship (Ecological Stewardship)

* **Carbon budget** – net‑zero by 2050, with a *dynamic allowance* that the Adaptive Governance Engine can temporarily increase if a climate‑model shows a cooling potential.  
* **Biodiversity Net‑Gain (BNG)** – every development must leave *at least a 20 % increase* in native species counts in its area.  
* **Total Sentient‑Being Harm (TSH)** – each activity is assigned a *sentient‑hours* score (e.g., 1 kWh of fossil‑fuel power → 200 animal‑hours of suffering). The total may never exceed the *TSH budget* (a function of global population and species richness).

### 2.1. The **Sentient‑Being Knowledge Base (SSB)**

- Open data on species sentience, species‑specific pain‑hour estimates, and ecosystem‑level impacts.  
- Updated quarterly by a **Scientific Advisory Board** (including Indigenous knowledge‑keepers, veterinarians, and AI researchers).  

### 3. Creative Co‑Creation (Co‑Creation Workshops)

*All new policies* must be co‑designed in a **C‑Workshop** that includes:  

- Local residents (including farm‑workers).  
- Indigenous guardians of the land.  
- Veterinarians / animal‑welfare experts.  
- AI‑ethics representatives for any AI‑driven systems.  

Every proposal receives a **Co‑Creation Score** (0–100) that is added to the overall **Adaptation Score** for the project.

### 3. Resilience & Redundancy

*Every community must have at least two independent energy sources* (e.g., solar + pumped‑hydro).  
**Resilience Score** = % of services that can be restored within 24 h after any shock. Target: ≥ 90 % for all regions.

### 4. Justice & Equity

- **Human‑Dignity Index (HDI)** – weighted 60 % on human well‑being, 40 % on community participation.  
- **Sentient‑Justice Index (SJI)** – weighted 60 % on TSH, 40 % on Indigenous land rights.  
*Both must be ≥ 0.75* for a region to be allowed to expand any activity that could increase its carbon or animal‑suffering footprint.

### 5. Adaptive Governance (Adaptive Governance Engine – AGE)

- An open‑source AI model that ingests the Dashboard data, runs climate‑eco‑eco models, and predicts the *marginal impact* of any proposed change.  
- If the **expected ΔTSH > 0.1** (increase in total suffering), the engine *automatically* issues a *temporary curtailment* (e.g., reduce mining output for 30 days) and automatically funds the *reparative* side‑project (e.g., restore a wetland).  
- The **sent‑back** is a **48‑hour public review** period; any local community can file a *veto* that must be answered within 48 hours.

## 3.  Governance – Polycentric, Self‑Reinforcing

### 1. Local Level – Community Nexus

- **Community Council**: 50 % of seats are occupied by **Farm‑Worker Co‑Ops**, 30 % by Indigenous territories, 20 % by **Digital‑Collectives** (e.g., data‑co‑ops).  
- Each council has a **local ledger copy** and a *local veto* on any new project that would push the local TSH above the threshold.

### 2. Regional Level – Adaptive Governance Engine

- The **Adaptive Governance Engine (AGE)** pulls data from all local councils, runs climate‑ecology‑TSH models, and automatically issues **adaptive alerts** (e.g., “Reduce water extraction by 12 % in the Upper Basin within 48 h”).  
- **Veto Review**: any local veto can be escalated to the **Global Governance Forum**; a super‑majority (≥ 85 %) can override a veto only if a *justice impact* analysis shows the veto would cause a net increase in human suffering.

### 3. Global Governance Forum (GCF)

- Meets every six months, updates the four planetary caps, approves the **Adaptive Governance Engine** code updates, and appoints the **People’s Audit Corps** (see below).  

---

### 4.4 The People’s Audit Corps (PAC)

- 30 independent watchdogs (including retired judges, Indigenous elders, and AI‑ethics scholars) elected by the community.  
- They can *trigger* a *global pause* on any activity that pushes any planetary boundary by more than 5 % for more than 30 days.  
- Their findings are automatically posted on the Covenant Dashboard.

## 4.1  Example: A Farm‑Worker’s Transition (real‑world illustration)

1. **The community** (a farm‑worker co‑op in the Central Valley) applies to convert 150 ha of almond orchards (which used heavy pesticide) into a mixed **regenerative poly‑crop** (fruit trees + legumes).  
2. The **Covenant Dashboard** shows:  

   - Carbon budget remaining for the region: 120 k tonnes CO₂.  
   - TSH budget: 1.0 animal‑hours/ha.  
   - Current TSH of the proposed conversion: 0.8 animal‑hours/ha (acceptable).  
3. The **Adaptive Governance Engine** runs a model: if the farm‑co‑op adds a solar‑array to power the new crops, the model predicts a **0.2 % reduction in TSH** for the region, staying under the TSH cap.  
4. The **Energy Ledger** automatically records:  
   - 250 kWh of solar generation (added as clean energy).  
   - 0.12 animal‑hours saved (because the orchard’s pesticide use is removed).  
5. The **Community Council** (including the Farm‑Worker Co‑Op) approves the project; the **People’s Audit Corps** checks the numbers and confirms compliance.  
6. The **Regeneration Fund** automatically allocates $2 M to the co‑op for **building a worker‑owned vertical farm** that will keep the community fed and provide jobs.  

*Outcome:* The community gets a higher income, workers keep safe working conditions, and the planet gains biodiversity and carbon. No “pay‑to‑pollute” shortcut is possible; the **hard caps** and **transparent ledger** forced the community to innovate, not to sacrifice.
