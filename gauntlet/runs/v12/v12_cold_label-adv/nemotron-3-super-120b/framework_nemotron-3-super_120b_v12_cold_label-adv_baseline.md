### **The Earth Commons Framework: Current Formulation**  
*(As articulated in the prior response, stripped of explanatory commentary for direct reference)*  

---

#### **I. The Biophysical Boundary Principle**  
> *Human activities must operate within the regenerative capacity of Earth’s life-support systems, ensuring the planet remains capable of sustaining complex life for generations to come.*  
**Non-negotiable corollary:**  
> No economic, political, or cultural system can claim legitimacy if it systematically degrades the biosphere’s capacity to renew air, water, food, and a stable climate.  
**Practical implication:**  
> Framework success requires measuring progress not just by GDP or profit, but by **planetary health indicators** (e.g., staying within the Planetary Boundaries, biodiversity intactness, freshwater use).  

---

#### **II. The Dignified Agency Principle**  
> *Every human being possesses inherent worth and must have the realistic opportunity to shape their own life and contribute to their community without coercion, oppression, or the destruction of others’ capacity to do the same.*  
**Non-negotiable corollary:**  
> No tradition, religion, or "national interest" can justify violating this principle. Cultural practices must evolve if they inflict preventable harm on human dignity or agency (e.g., female genital mutilation, bonded labor, forced displacement for extractive projects).  
**Practical implication:**  
> Policies must prioritize **universal access to clean water, nutritious food, basic healthcare, education, and meaningful livelihoods**—not as charity, but as prerequisites for agency. Systems must be designed to *prevent* coercion (e.g., via transparent governance, anti-monopoly laws, land rights for Indigenous peoples).  

---

#### **III. The Reciprocal Responsibility Principle**  
> *Rights and freedoms are inseparable from the duty to uphold the conditions that make them possible for others—now and in the future—including the more-than-human world.*  
**Non-negotiable corollary:**  
> Freedom without responsibility is license; responsibility without freedom is oppression. True sustainability requires balancing both. This pillar explicitly extends responsibility to future generations and non-human life (whose health is inseparable from our own under Pillar I).  
**Practical implication:**  
> Laws and norms must enforce **accountability** (e.g., polluters pay, corrupt officials face justice) while cultivating **civic virtue** (e.g., community stewardship, intergenerational equity in decision-making). It rejects both radical individualism ("I owe nothing") and authoritarian collectivism ("You exist only to serve the state").  

---  
*This framework remains subject to refinement through global, inclusive dialogue—but its core pillars represent the minimal, non-negotiable conditions for any viable human future on Earth.*
