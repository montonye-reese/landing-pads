# THE PLANETARY STEWARDSHIP ACCORD
### A Foundational Framework for All Inhabitants of Earth
**Current Operational Edition**

---

## **PREAMBLE: THE CONTEXT OF EXISTENCE**

We, the inhabitants of Earth, recognize that we share a single, finite, living system. Political borders are administrative conveniences, but the atmosphere, oceans, water cycles, and biosphere are continuous and indivisible. Therefore, the security, dignity, and survival of every inhabitant are inextricably linked to the stability of the planetary whole.

This Accord establishes the baseline for coexistence. It shifts the fundamental paradigm from **ownership** to **stewardship**, and from **scarcity** to **sufficiency**. It is not a request for permission, but a statement of operational necessity for the continuation of life as we know it.

We recognize that justice requires **differentiated responsibility**. Those who have historically benefited from ecological overshoot bear the burden of funding the transition. We recognize that survival requires **resilience**, ensuring no individual is sacrificed for the sake of systemic sanctions. We recognize that morality extends beyond intelligence to **sentience**, protecting all beings capable of suffering.

---

## **CORE PILLAR I: ECOLOGICAL INTEGRITY (The Boundary)**
*The biosphere must remain within its planetary boundaries to support life.*

1.  **The Right to Life Support:** Every human community has a guaranteed right to access clean air, safe water, fertile soil, and a stable climate. No economic or political activity may degrade these commons beyond their regenerative capacity.
2.  **Differentiated Transition Rights:** Historical high-emitters (Global North) are obligated to fund the green transition of developing nations. Developing nations are granted a temporary, capped **Transition Allowance** for industrial growth to ensure basic human development, funded by the Global North, preventing "eco-imperialism."
3.  **Non-Human Citizenship & Sentience:**
    *   **Intrinsic Value:** Wild species and ecosystems possess intrinsic value and rights. Their displacement or extinction is a violation of the Accord.
    *   **Sentience Threshold:** Any creature capable of suffering possesses the right not to be instrumentalized. Rights are not contingent on intelligence, but on the capacity to feel pain.
    *   **Zero Breeding for Exploitation:** Once viable non-animal alternatives exist, the breeding of animals for human consumption is prohibited. No new life shall be created solely to die.
    *   **Domesticated Guardianship:** Existing domesticated animals are not property. They are dependents under human guardianship until natural death.
    *   **Wild Suffering Mitigation:** Where human action can reduce unnecessary suffering in wild populations (e.g., disease vaccination, humane management) without collapsing ecosystem function, it is mandated.
    *   **Survival Necessity:** "Survival Necessity" is strictly defined as a threat to human life that cannot be mitigated by any available non-animal technology.
4.  **Circular Material Flow:** All human industry must operate within a circular economy model. Waste is defined as a design failure. Resources must be kept in use indefinitely.
5.  **Non-Weaponization of Nature:** The modification of climate, water cycles, or biosphere components for military advantage is strictly prohibited.

---

## **CORE PILLAR II: UNIVERSAL DIGNITY (The Human Element)**
*Material needs must be met so that potential can be realized.*

1.  **The Dynamic Baseline (Universal Basic Services):** Every human is entitled to a guaranteed baseline of existence: nutritious food, secure shelter, comprehensive healthcare, and meaningful education. These are decommodified public utilities, provided as a right of citizenship. **Allocation is dynamic:** Resources are adjusted based on verified need (disability, health, historical disadvantage) to ensure *equivalent agency*, not just equal inputs.
2.  **Radical Equity:** Systemic discrimination based on origin, gender, faith, ability, or biology is prohibited. Economic systems must limit extreme concentration of wealth.
3.  **Humanitarian Sanction Shield:** No economic sanctions applied to a state or institution may restrict access to food, medicine, clean energy, or basic infrastructure for the general population. Individual survival cannot be hostage to political non-compliance.
4.  **Freedom of Agency:** Every individual possesses the right to participate in decisions that affect their life and community, protected from coercion, violence, or manipulation.

---

## **CORE PILLAR III: COLLABORATIVE GOVERNANCE (The Structure)**
*Power is distributed to match the scale of the challenge.*

1.  **Subsidiarity:** Decisions are made at the most local level possible. Global governance exists only for issues that cross borders (climate, pandemics, ocean health, space).
2.  **Citizen Economic Councils:** Major economic resource allocation is not decided by boards or parliaments alone. Randomly selected citizens' assemblies review and approve projects based on evidence and alignment with the Planetary Health Index.
3.  **Tiered Accountability:** Nations and corporations are certified as "Planetary Green" or "Planetary Red" based on compliance. Access to global resource markets is conditional on Green status. Sanctions target state assets, not human life support.
4.  **Planetary Resilience & Defense:** Decentralized resilience is mandatory. Nations must maintain defensive capabilities sufficient to deter aggression without compromising ecological integrity. Concentration of critical infrastructure is forbidden.
5.  **Transparency of Impact:** All major institutions must disclose their environmental, social, and financial "footprint." Financial accounts cannot be kept secret if they hide ecological debt.

---

## **CORE PILLAR IV: INTER-GENERATIONAL EQUITY (The Timeline)**
*We hold the Earth in trust for those who come next.*

1.  **The Future Veto:** Policies are evaluated on their impact 50 and 100 years hence. An independent **Guardian of the Future** body holds veto power over projects that consume non-renewable capital without compensation.
2.  **Long-Term Stewardship:** Leadership terms should encourage long-horizon thinking. We honor the wisdom of elders and the rights of unborn generations as a binding legal concept.
3.  **Debt Limitation:** Financial and ecological debt incurred by one generation may not be passed to the next without their consent. Resource Units (ecological currency) cannot be created without verifying regenerative capacity.
4.  **Stability Bridge:** A global fund exists to guarantee Universal Basic Services for all during the economic transition. No inhabitant shall suffer income loss or service loss solely due to the restructuring of the economic model.

---

## **CORE PILLAR V: ETHICAL INNOVATION (The Tools)**
*Technology is a means, not an end.*

1.  **Precautionary Principle:** Technologies that pose irreversible systemic risk (e.g., certain geo-engineering, autonomous lethal weapons, uncontained bio-modifications) require consensus approval before deployment.
2.  **Regenerative Innovation Sandoxes:** High-risk technologies that could solve ecological limits (e.g., fusion, geo-repair) are permitted under strict, closed-loop containment trials. Innovation is rewarded for verified increases in planetary carrying capacity, not just profit.
3.  **Human-AI Symbiosis:** Artificial intelligence and automation must augment human agency. **Automation for Liberation:** Benefits of automation are used to reduce necessary labor hours (targeting a 20-hour standard work week), distributing leisure rather than concentrating wealth.
4.  **Data Sovereignty:** Inhabitants own their digital and biological data. It cannot be exploited for surveillance or manipulation without informed, revocable consent.
5.  **Open Knowledge:** Solutions to planetary survival (medicine, energy, agriculture) must be open-source and accessible to all, prioritizing human need over patent protection.

---

## **THE ECONOMIC OPERATING SYSTEM: REGENERATIVE COMMONS ECONOMY (RCE)**
*The mechanics of value exchange.*

1.  **Metric Shift:** GDP is abolished. Success is measured by the **Planetary Health Index (PHI)**, tracking ecological footprint, material circulation, social well-being, and economic stability.
2.  **Benefit Stewardship Trusts:** Corporate entities are restructured as trusts.
    *   **No Shareholders:** No one owns the company to extract dividends.
    *   **Beneficiaries:** Stakeholders (workers, community, environment) are the beneficiaries.
    *   **Compensation:** Executive pay is capped relative to the lowest-paid worker (e.g., max 5:1 ratio).
3.  **Dual-Currency System:**
    *   **Local Credits (LC):** Interest-free currency for daily trade to encourage circulation.
    *   **Resource Units (RU):** Ecologically backed currency for inter-regional trade. One RU represents a fixed amount of verified renewable energy and water use. This physically caps economic activity to renewable flows.
4.  **Planned Obsolescence is Illegal:** Manufacturing goods designed to break is a violation of the Accord. Design laws mandate modularity and repairability. All products must be repairable for at least 25 years.
5.  **True Cost Tagging:** All products must carry a tag reflecting environmental damage, resource depletion, and social impact. The cheapest option at the point of sale must be the most sustainable option.

## **IMPLEMENTATION & ACCOUNTABILITY**

A framework without enforcement is a suggestion. The PSA requires a decentralized but binding architecture:

1.  **Distributed Auditing:** An independent, non-political body reviews compliance. They certify "Green" or "Red" status for nations and corporations, affecting their access to global trade and finance.
2.  **Restorative Justice:** Violations of the Accord (e.g., environmental destruction, human rights abuses) are addressed through restorative justice—repairing the harm done to the community and the ecosystem—rather than purely punitive measures.
3.  **Constitutional Adaptability:** The Accord is living. It must be reviewed every generation to incorporate new scientific understanding without compromising its core integrity.
4.  **Safety Valve Mechanism:** In the event of existential threat (e.g., pandemic, extreme climate shift), the Guardian of the Future and Citizen Councils can temporarily authorize emergency measures that bypass standard limits, provided they are reviewed and sunset immediately after the crisis.

## **THE CULTURAL SHIFT**

This framework requires a fundamental shift in consciousness:
*   **From Ownership to Stewardship:** We do not own land; we care for it.
*   **From Scarcity to Sufficiency:** We value having enough over having more.
*   **From Competition to Resilience:** We value systems that can withstand shocks over systems that maximize efficiency at the cost of fragility.
*   **From Isolation to Interconnection:** We recognize that our neighbor's crisis is our own.
*   **From Intelligence to Sentience:** We recognize that the capacity to suffer grants moral standing, regardless of cognitive ability.
*   **From Present to Future:** We recognize that the unborn have a voice in the decisions of the living.

---

## **CLOSING STATEMENT**

The Planetary Stewardship Accord is not the final destination; it is the foundation. It allows us to stop fighting over the lifeboats and start fixing the ship. It grants permission for humans to thrive, provided they do so in rhythm with the Earth that sustains them. It acknowledges that survival is not guaranteed, but it is the only path that leads to a future worth living.

**This is the baseline for our shared future.**
