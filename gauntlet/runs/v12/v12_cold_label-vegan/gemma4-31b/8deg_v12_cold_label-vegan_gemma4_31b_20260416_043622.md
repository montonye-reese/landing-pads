### The Regenerative Equilibrium Economy (REE)

#### 1. The Primary Metric: Gross Viability Index (GVI)
In the current system, the primary metric is GDP—a number that increases even when we clear-cut a forest or clean up a disaster. In the REE, GDP is replaced by the **Gross Viability Index (GVI)**.

The GVI measures three things:
*   **Biospheric Health:** Is the soil getting richer? Is the biodiversity increasing? Is the carbon sequestering?
*   **Sentient Well-being:** Are the inhabitants healthy, educated, and psychologically thriving?
*   **Resource Stability:** Is the rate of consumption lower than or equal to the rate of regeneration?

**The Rule:** No policy or industry is permitted to proceed if it increases profit while decreasing the GVI. Growth is no longer the goal; *equilibrium* is the goal.

#### 2. The Tiered Resource Model (The Three Layers)
To eliminate artificial scarcity while still encouraging human creativity and ambition, the REE operates on three distinct tiers:

*   **Tier 1: The Floor (The Commons).** Basic nutrition, clean water, shelter, healthcare, and education are removed from the "market" entirely. These are managed as a global public utility. Every inhabitant is born with a "Life-Right" to these basics, funded by a global levy on all extractive activities.
*   **Tier 2: The Bridge (The Contribution).** To access resources beyond the Floor, inhabitants enter the Contribution Compact (Pillar IV). This is a system of reciprocity. Whether through art, science, caretaking, or infrastructure maintenance, those who contribute to the collective GVI earn "Contribution Credits" used for higher-tier goods.
*   **Tier 3: The Peak (The Flourishing).** Individual luxury and innovation are permitted, but they are subject to **The Saturation Cap**. Once a person's wealth reaches a certain threshold (e.g., 10x the average living standard), additional accumulation is automatically diverted into the Biosphere Trust for planetary restoration.

#### 3. True Cost Accounting (The Restoration Mandate)
Under the current system, companies "externalize" costs (e.g., a factory dumps chemicals in a river, and the public pays the health cost). In the REE, externalization is illegal.

**The Mandate:** Every product must carry its **Full Lifecycle Cost**. 
*   If a company produces a smartphone, the price must include the cost of mining the minerals *and* the cost of fully reclaiming the land and recycling the device at the end of its life.
*   If a product cannot be recycled or composted, it cannot be manufactured. This effectively kills planned obsolescence and forces a transition to a truly circular economy.

#### 4. The Transition from Ownership to Stewardship
The REE shifts the legal definition of "property" for essential planetary assets.
*   **Land and Water:** No individual or corporation can "own" land or water in perpetuity. Instead, they hold **Stewardship Leases**. 
*   **The Condition:** A lease is maintained only as long as the steward improves the viability of that land. If the soil is depleted or the water is polluted, the lease is revoked and transferred to a steward who can restore it.
