**The Charter of Inter‑Earth – Current Framework (Version 1.3 – October 2025)**  

---

## 1. Vision  
> *One planet, many lives.  In harmony with Earth’s limits we co‑create shared prosperity that honors every person, species, and ecosystem, now and for generations to come.*

---  

## 2. Core Pillars (The **Five E’s**)  

| Pillar | Essence | Quick‑check Questions |
|--------|---------|------------------------|
| **E**arth‑Centric Stewardship | All actions are filtered through their ecological footprint. | Does this preserve carbon, water, biodiversity, soil, and air quality? |
| **E**quitable Prosperity | Wealth, opportunity, and well‑being are distributed fairly. | Who gains? Who loses? How can benefits be shared more broadly? |
| **E**mpathy & Mutual Respect | Understanding and valuing the perspectives, rights, and needs of all beings. | What lived realities are affected? How can we amplify marginalized voices? |
| **E**volving Knowledge | Continuous, evidence‑based learning from science, art, and lived experience. | What new data or insights are emerging? How can we integrate them? |
| **E**xternal Resilience | Systems are designed to adapt, recover, and evolve under uncertainty. | How does this withstand shocks (climate spikes, pandemics, migrations)? |

*The pillars reinforce each other; weakening one erodes the others.*

## 3. Structural Architecture  

| Level | Constituents | Key Practices |
|-------|--------------|---------------|
| **Micro** (Individual & Household) | Daily habits, personal growth, skill‑building | Energy‑mindful consumption, local sourcing, bias‑checking, basic ecology & conflict‑mediation literacy |
| **Meso** (Community & Local Governance) | Neighborhood councils, cooperatives, mutual‑aid networks | Participatory planning, community asset mapping, regenerative/ circular economies |
| **Macro** (National & Trans‑National) | Parliaments, ministries, regional bodies | Policies anchored to the Five E’s, independent planetary oversight agencies, cross‑border justice mechanisms |
| **Meta‑Macro** (Planetary Governance) | Earth Charter 2.0, Inter‑Planetary Council of Stewards, Dynamic Simulation Engine | Global compliance audits, real‑time planetary health dashboard, open‑source knowledge commons |

## 4. Decision‑Making Lens – The **5‑Step Process**  

| Step | Core Question | Tool |
|------|---------------|------|
| **1. Vision Check** | Does it align with the planetary vision? | Vision‑alignment rubric |
| **2. Impact Scan** | What are the ecological, social, and economic footprints? | Life‑Cycle Assessment + Social‑Impact Matrix |
| **3. Equity Audit** | Who benefits, who bears costs, who is excluded? | Equity Impact Dashboard |
| **4. Resilience Test** | How does it perform under defined stress scenarios? | Scenario‑based stress‑testing simulation |
| **5. Learning Loop** | What data and insights emerge? How will we adapt? | Continuous feedback loop, version‑controlled knowledge base |

*If any step flags a negative outcome, the decision must be revised or abandoned.*

## 5. Measurement & Accountability  

| Dimension | Indicator | 2030 Target | 2050 Target | Reporting Cadence |
|-----------|-----------|------------|------------|-------------------|
| **Planetary Health** | Global mean temperature anomaly, Biodiversity Intactness Index, Freshwater use per capita | ≤ 1.5 °C ; > 85 % intactness ; ≤ 30 % reduction vs 2020 | Same thresholds, maintained |
| **Human Well‑Being** | Gini coefficient of income; Life‑expectancy equity; Subjective well‑being score | Gini ↓ 30 %; ↑ life‑expectancy equity to ≥ 85 % of global avg; Well‑being ↑ 10 % | Sustainable convergence |
| **Economic Inclusion** | Share of circular‑economy revenue; Access to basic services; Gender/ethnic labor participation | 60 % GDP from circular sources; 90 % of pop. with basic services; 50 % participation of marginalized groups | 80 % targets by 2050 |
| **Governance Transparency** | % of decisions with publicly accessible data; % citizen participation in deliberations | 70 % open‑data compliance; ≥ 40 % participation | Annual public dashboards |
| **Educational Reach** | % of schools & informal learning platforms offering planetary‑literacy curricula | ≥ 80 % coverage | 100 % coverage by 2050 |

All data are **open‑source** and **locally verifiable** to permit both bottom‑up validation and top‑down accountability.

## 8. The **Living Charter** – How It Evolves  

1. **Version‑Controlled Text** – Stored on a decentralized ledger; any participant may propose edits, which are reviewed by a multi‑disciplinary board (scientists, ethicists, representatives of major ecosystem types, and citizen jurors).  
2. **Adaptive Clauses** – Triggers automatic revision when planetary boundaries (climate, biodiversity, phosphorus, nitrogen) are breached, ensuring the Charter stays tied to Earth’s real‑time thresholds.  
3. **Polyphonic Voice** – Ecosystem “votes” are represented by expert panels (forest, ocean, wetland, soil, atmospheric) that must approve any clause affecting their domain.
