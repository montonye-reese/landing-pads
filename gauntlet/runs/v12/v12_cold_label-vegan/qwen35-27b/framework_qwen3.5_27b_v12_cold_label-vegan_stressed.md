# THE TERRA COVENANT
## A Charter for the Stewardship of Earth
**(Final Ratification Draft: Post-Veil of Ignorance)**

**Date of Ratification:** [Year 0 of Transition]
**Status:** Binding Meta-Constitution
**Applicability:** All Inhabitants of Earth, Sentient Species, and Future Generations
**Design Philosophy:** Designed behind a Veil of Ignorance (protecting the most vulnerable)

---

### PREAMBLE

**WE, THE INHABITANTS OF EARTH AND THE STEWARDS OF THE BIOSPHERE,**

Recognizing that we share a singular, closed system where no boundary of air, water, or soil can be truly divided;
Acknowledging that the survival of the whole is the prerequisite for the survival of the part;
Rejecting the doctrine of unlimited growth upon finite resources;
Affirming that human dignity is not a privilege of economics or geography, but a biological right;
Acknowledging that the capacity to suffer grants moral standing to all sentient life;
**And knowing we do not know who we shall be in the next life—poor or rich, human or animal, able-bodied or disabled, in a stable nation or a war zone;**

**DO HEREBY ESTABLISH** the Terra Covenant as the foundational legal and ethical framework for the governance of our common home. This Covenant supersedes all conflicting local, national, and regional laws that violate the Five Pillars of Existence. We pledge not merely to coexist, but to co-thrive in a state of regeneration. We build this not for the strongest, but for the most vulnerable.

---

### ARTICLE I: THE PLANETARY TRUST (ECOLOGICAL INTEGRITY)

**Section 1: The Boundaries of Life.**
1.1. The Earth’s biosphere, atmosphere, hydrosphere, and cryosphere are declared **Common Heritage**. No state, corporation, or individual may claim sovereign ownership of the global commons.
1.2. All industrial and agricultural activity must operate within **Planetary Boundaries** as defined by the Stewardship Council. These boundaries include limits on carbon emission, biodiversity loss, freshwater usage, and chemical toxicity.
1.3. **Prohibition of Irreversibility.** No project shall be approved that risks irreversible damage to planetary systems.

**Section 2: Regeneration.**
2.1. Resource extraction is recognized as a debt to the future. A **Regenerative Tax** shall be levied on all finite resource extraction.
2.2. 100% of revenue generated from the Regenerative Tax shall be dedicated to ecosystem restoration, decarbonization, and climate adaptation infrastructure.

**Section 3: Climate Reparations (Loss and Damage).**
3.1. Nations that exceed their carbon budget or cause irreversible harm to vulnerable regions must pay a **Climate Reparations Fund**.
3.2. This Fund is ring-fenced exclusively for displaced populations in vulnerable nations and cannot be used for general budget or debt.
3.3. Environmental degradation causing harm to human health or life constitutes a violation of this Covenant.

---

### ARTICLE II: THE FLOOR OF DIGNITY (SOCIAL SECURITY)

**Section 1: Immediate Implementation.**
1.1. The following are guaranteed as **Universal Entitlements** from birth to death, regardless of citizenship or economic status, **effective immediately upon ratification**:
    *   (a) Nutritious Food and Clean Water.
    *   (b) Safe Shelter and Sanitation.
    *   (c) Preventative and Curative Healthcare.
    *   (d) Basic Education and Digital Literacy.
1.2. These entitlements are **non-cancellable**. Failure to provide them constitutes a systemic breach of the Covenant by the governing jurisdiction.

**Section 2: Accessibility as a Right.**
2.1. All infrastructure (housing, transport, digital) must be designed for **100% Accessibility** by default, not as an add-on.
2.2. High-cost care for the severely disabled is covered without means testing.
2.3. The economy must be designed around the needs of the disabled; a system that works for the most vulnerable works for all.

**Section 3: Wealth Distribution.**
3.1. The ratio of wealth and income inequality within any jurisdiction is capped at a maximum of 1:50.
3.2. Extreme wealth accumulation beyond the threshold of necessity is subject to progressive taxation, the revenue of which funds the Minimum Standard.
3.3. The **Universal Basic Asset** program grants every adult citizen ownership shares in the digital and physical infrastructure of the economy.

**Section 4: Mobility.**
4.1. The right to migrate for safety, climate survival, or opportunity is recognized as fundamental.
4.2. All borders are permeable for the purpose of humanitarian aid and climate displacement.

---

### ARTICLE III: COGNITIVE SOVEREIGNTY (TRUTH & KNOWLEDGE)

**Section 1: Access to Information.**
1.1. Scientific data, public records, and educational materials essential to public health and safety must be **Open Source**.
1.2. Proprietary algorithms controlling information distribution (social media, search engines) are subject to public audit to prevent manipulation, polarization, or disinformation.

**Section 2: Protection of the Mind.**
2.1. **Surveillance Capitalism is Prohibited.** No entity shall harvest private human experience for the purpose of behavioral modification or prediction markets.
2.2. No entity shall engage in the manipulation of human cognition for commercial or political gain (e.g., dark patterns, addictive design, deepfakes used for coercion).
2.3. Neuro-rights are protected. Data regarding brain activity, mental state, or biometric emotion is considered **Private Property** of the individual.

**Section 3: Education.**
3.1. The global curriculum prioritizes Systems Thinking, Empathy, Logic, and Ecological Literacy.
3.2. Literacy in the Terra Covenant itself is a mandatory requirement for all civic leadership roles.

---

### ARTICLE IV: POLYCENTRIC GOVERNANCE (STRUCTURE OF POWER)

**Section 1: Nested Jurisdiction.**
1.1. Authority is decentralized to the **lowest effective level**.
    *   **Local/National:** Manage daily laws, culture, policing, and local economics.
    *   **Regional:** Manage bioregions (watersheds, airsheds) and cross-border logistics.
    *   **Global Stewardship Council (GSC):** Manage *only* the Five Pillars of Existence (Ecology, Dignity, Truth, Economy, Sentience).

**Section 2: The Stewardship Council.**
2.1. The GSC holds **Veto Power** over local actions violating the Covenant (Articles I, II, VII).
2.2. The GSC holds **No Police Power**. Enforcement is carried out via trade embargoes, suspension of aid, and resource flow restriction, not military force.
2.3. GSC members are selected via a global lottery stratified by demographic, serving limited terms to prevent corruption.

**Section 3: Representation.**
3.1. **Non-Human Guardians:** The GSC must include representatives with legal standing to advocate for specific species, selected by independent scientific and ethical bodies.
3.2. These Guardians hold veto power over projects that threaten critical habitats, even if human economic gain is high.

**Section 4: Emergency Protocol.**
4.1. In a declared planetary emergency (pandemic, ecological collapse), the GSC can bypass standard transition periods for life-saving measures.
4.2. Activation requires 75% citizen assembly approval, but allows immediate action.
4.3. Emergency powers expire automatically within 12 months unless re-ratified.

---

### ARTICLE V: THE REGENERATIVE FLOW ECONOMY (SYSTEMS)

**Section 1: Metric of Success.**
1.1. **GDP is Abolished.** Economic success shall be measured by the **Planetary Health Index (PHI)**, which accounts for environmental damage, social well-being, and resource efficiency.
1.2. Entities that lower the local PHI are penalized; entities that restore it are subsidized.

**Section 2: Currency and Value.**
2.1. **Terra Credits.** Currency is decoupled from debt and anchored to **Physical Capacity** (Energy or Ecological Service).
2.2. The total money supply cannot exceed the biosphere’s regenerative capacity.

**Section 3: Ownership and Land.**
3.1. **Stewardship Leases.** Land cannot be owned; it can only be leased. Leases are contingent on ecological health. If land degrades, leases are revoked.
3.2. The Commons (Oceans, Atmosphere, Genetic Code, Orbit) are **Inalienable**. They cannot be privatized.

**Section 4: Transition Protocol.**
4.1. **Survival Trigger:** If global GDP contraction exceeds 15% in any year during transition, the economic caps pause automatically to prevent collapse.
4.2. **Phased Gates:**
    *   *Phase 1 (Years 0-5):* Voluntary Incentives (Green Infrastructure Fund).
    *   *Phase 2 (Years 6-10):* Soft Cap (Tariffs on non-compliance).
    *   *Phase 3 (Years 11-15):* Hard Cap (Systemic Interlock Enforcement).

**Section 5: Production and Labor.**
5.1. **Full Lifecycle Responsibility.** Manufacturers retain ownership of materials. Products must be designed for 100% recyclability or compostability.
5.2. **Universal Basic Assets.** Automation revenue is redistributed to citizens. Decoupling survival from labor is mandatory.

---

### ARTICLE VI: THE SENTIENCE PRINCIPLE (NON-HUMAN RIGHTS)

**Section 1: Moral Standing.**
1.1. **Sentience as a Criterion.** Moral standing is granted to any being capable of experiencing pain, suffering, or subjective well-being (mammals, birds, cephalopods, and other vertebrates).
1.2. **Intrinsic Value.** These beings possess **Intrinsic Value**. They are not merely assets to be managed for human benefit.

**Section 2: Graduated Rights.**
2.1. **Tier 1 (Full Rights):** Humans, great apes, dolphins, elephants.
2.2. **Tier 2 (High Protection):** Birds, mammals.
2.3. **Tier 3 (Basic Protection):** Fish, invertebrates.
2.4. **Safeguards:** Indigenous communities and religious groups are granted exemptions for traditional hunting/sacrifice up to local sustainable limits, subject to review.

**Section 3: Prohibitions.**
3.1. **Industrial Farming.** The use of animals for mass food production (factory farming) is prohibited.
3.2. **Entertainment.** Animal captivity for entertainment (zoos, circuses, blood sports) is prohibited.

---

### ARTICLE VII: ENFORCEMENT & HUMANITARIAN EXEMPTIONS

**Section 1: Systemic Interlock.**
1.1. The GSC controls the distributional infrastructure of the Commons (rare earth mining rights, orbital slots, carbon credit markets, data backbone).
1.2. Non-compliant nations lose access to this infrastructure (Systemic Interlock).

**Section 2: Humanitarian Exemptions (The Innocent Bystander Clause).**
2.1. **No Sanction Shall Starve a Civilian.** No embargo or infrastructure cut-off shall block the flow of food, medicine, or clean water to civilians in non-compliant zones.
2.2. The GSC must fund independent humanitarian corridors into Rogue States, paid for by the Regenerative Tax of compliant nations.
2.3. Compliance is penalized at the systemic level, not the biological level.

**Section 3: Ratification and Evolution.**
3.1. Approval requires a **Double Majority**: A majority of Nations AND a majority of the Global Population.
3.2. Every five years, a **Sortition Assembly** meets to audit the Stewardship Council and propose amendments.
3.3. This Covenant is designed to protect the most vulnerable. If it fails to do so, it is in breach.
