# THE TERRA COVENANT
## A Charter for the Stewardship of Earth

**Date of Adoption:** 21st Century (Year of Transition)
**Status:** Binding Meta-Constitution
**Applicability:** All Inhabitants of the Planet Earth

---

### PREAMBLE

**WE, THE INHABITANTS OF EARTH,**

Recognizing that we share a singular, closed biosphere where no boundary of air, water, or soil can be truly divided;
Acknowledging that the survival of the whole is the prerequisite for the survival of the part;
Rejecting the doctrine of unlimited growth upon finite resources;
Affirming that human dignity is not a privilege of economics or geography, but a biological right;

**DO HEREBY ESTABLISH** the Terra Covenant as the foundational legal and ethical framework for the governance of our common home. This Covenant supersedes all conflicting local, national, and regional laws that violate the Four Pillars of Existence. We pledge not merely to coexist, but to co-thrive.

---

### ARTICLE I: THE PLANETARY TRUST (ECOLOGICAL INTEGRITY)

**Section 1: The Boundaries of Life.**
1.1. The Earth’s biosphere, atmosphere, hydrosphere, and cryosphere are declared **Common Heritage**. No state, corporation, or individual may claim sovereign ownership of the global commons.
1.2. All industrial and agricultural activity must operate within **Planetary Boundaries** as defined by the Stewardship Council. These boundaries include limits on carbon emission, biodiversity loss, freshwater usage, and chemical toxicity.
1.3. **Prohibition of Irreversibility.** No project shall be approved that risks irreversible damage to planetary systems.

**Section 2: Regeneration.**
2.1. Resource extraction is recognized as a debt to the future. A **Regenerative Tax** shall be levied on all finite resource extraction.
2.2. 100% of revenue generated from the Regenerative Tax shall be dedicated to ecosystem restoration, decarbonization, and climate adaptation infrastructure.

**Section 3: The Right to Nature.**
3.1. Every inhabitant has an enforceable legal right to a clean, healthy, and stable environment.
3.2. Environmental degradation causing harm to human health or life constitutes a violation of this Covenant.

---

### ARTICLE II: THE FLOOR OF DIGNITY (SOCIAL SECURITY)

**Section 1: The Minimum Standard.**
1.1. The following are guaranteed as **Universal Entitlements** from birth to death, regardless of citizenship or economic status:
    *   (a) Nutritious Food and Clean Water.
    *   (b) Safe Shelter and Sanitation.
    *   (c) Preventative and Curative Healthcare.
    *   (d) Basic Education and Digital Literacy.
1.2. These entitlements are **non-cancellable**. Failure to provide them constitutes a systemic breach of the Covenant by the governing jurisdiction.

**Section 2: Wealth Distribution.**
2.1. The ratio of wealth and income inequality within any jurisdiction is capped at a maximum of 1:50.
2.2. Extreme wealth accumulation beyond the threshold of necessity is subject to progressive taxation, the revenue of which funds the Minimum Standard.
2.3. The **Universal Basic Asset** program grants every adult citizen ownership shares in the digital and physical infrastructure of the economy, ensuring participation in global prosperity.

**Section 3: Mobility.**
3.1. The right to migrate for safety, climate survival, or opportunity is recognized as fundamental.
3.2. All borders are permeable for the purpose of humanitarian aid and climate displacement.

---

### ARTICLE III: COGNITIVE SOVEREIGNTY (TRUTH & KNOWLEDGE)

**Section 1: Access to Information.**
1.1. Scientific data, public records, and educational materials essential to public health and safety must be **Open Source**.
1.2. Proprietary algorithms controlling information distribution (social media, search engines) are subject to public audit to prevent manipulation, polarization, or disinformation.

**Section 2: Protection of the Mind.**
2.1. No entity shall engage in the manipulation of human cognition for commercial or political gain (e.g., dark patterns, addictive design, deepfakes used for coercion).
2.2. Neuro-rights are protected. Data regarding brain activity, mental state, or biometric emotion is considered **Private Property** of the individual.

**Section 3: Education.**
3.1. The global curriculum prioritizes Systems Thinking, Empathy, Logic, and Ecological Literacy.
3.2. Literacy in the Terra Covenant itself is a mandatory requirement for all civic leadership roles.

---

### ARTICLE IV: POLYCENTRIC GOVERNANCE (STRUCTURE OF POWER)

**Section 1: Nested Jurisdiction.**
1.1. Authority is decentralized to the **lowest effective level**.
    *   **Local/National:** Manage daily laws, culture, policing, and local economics.
    *   **Regional:** Manage bioregions (watersheds, airsheds) and cross-border logistics.
    *   **Global Stewardship Council (GSC):** Manage *only* the Four Pillars of Existence (Ecology, Dignity, Truth, Conflict Resolution).

**Section 2: The Stewardship Council.**
2.1. The GSC holds **Veto Power** over local actions violating the Covenant.
2.2. The GSC holds **No Police Power**. Enforcement is carried out via trade embargoes, suspension of aid, and resource flow restriction, not military force.
2.3. GSC members are selected via a global lottery stratified by demographic, serving limited terms to prevent corruption.

**Section 3: Conflict Resolution.**
3.1. Military force is prohibited for territorial expansion or resource acquisition.
3.2. Inter-state conflicts are resolved through **Restorative Justice Tribunals**, focusing on repairing harm and reintegrating parties.
3.3. Defense budgets are limited to a percentage of GDP strictly for planetary defense (asteroid, pandemics) and disaster relief.

---

### ARTICLE V: THE REGENERATIVE ECONOMY (SYSTEMS)

**Section 1: Currency and Value.**
1.1. Currency is not sovereign property. It must be backed by resource availability or service delivery.
1.2. Global value metrics shall not use GDP. They shall use **Genuine Progress Indicators (GPI)**, which account for environmental damage and social well-being.

**Section 2: Ownership.**
2.1. The Commons (Oceans, Atmosphere, Genetic Code, Orbit) are **Inalienable**. They cannot be privatized.
2.2. Private property rights exist only insofar as they do not encroach upon the Common Heritage.

**Section 3: Labor and Automation.**
3.1. Automation and AI must be used to liberate humans from drudgery, not to displace them without support.
3.2. "Human Labor Dividend": Revenue from fully automated industries is redistributed to the citizens as part of the Minimum Standard.

---

### ARTICLE VI: EVOLUTION AND RATIFICATION

**Section 1: Amendment.**
1.1. The Covenant is not static. Amendments may be proposed.
1.2. Approval requires a **Double Majority**: A majority of Nations AND a majority of the Global Population (verified via the Global Citizen Assembly).

**Section 2: The Citizen Assembly.**
2.1. Every five years, a **Sortition Assembly** (randomly selected citizens from all regions) meets to audit the Stewardship Council and propose amendments.
2.2. This ensures the system remains responsive to human need rather than entrenched power.

**Section 3: Ratification.**
3.1. This Covenant comes into force upon the signature of the Stewardship Council.
3.2. Transition Period: Existing nations have 20 years to align their constitutions and economies with the Covenant. Non-compliance results in suspension of trade privileges.
