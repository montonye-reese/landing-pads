# **The Earth Framework – Living Charter (2025 v 4.0)**  

*The framework that any sentient system—human, animal, plant, fungus, micro‑be, emergent AI, or any combination of them—can voluntarily join.  It is built on five immutable moral axioms, a planetary budget, and a set of interlocking institutions that are transparent, reversible, and inclusive of every sentient system.  The whole structure is recorded on an immutable, sharded ledger (the **Planetary Ledger**) and is designed to survive technological and cultural change.*

---

## 1.  Foundational Axioms  

| # | Axiom (written for any possible experience) | What it obliges |
|---|---------------------------------------------|-----------------|
| **A** | *Every pattern of experience has intrinsic worth.*  Worth is not a function of species, substrate, or scale. | All decisions must recognize the right of the pattern (human brain, dolphin pod, mycelial network, AI‑collective, etc.) to be consulted, to veto, or to withdraw consent. |
| **B** | *The planet’s material and energy limits are non‑negotiable.*  No system may consume more carbon, water, or biodiversity than the Earth can sustainably provide. | All economic and production activities must be tagged with the amount of planetary budget they use and must stay within the caps. |
| **C** | *Decision‑making must be inclusive, transparent, and reversible.*  Every decision that affects a sentient system must be accompanied by a consent signal that can be withdrawn at any time. | Veto rights belong to the system itself (human, animal, plant, AI).  All votes are recorded on the ledger and can be reversed automatically when thresholds are crossed. |
| **D** | *Knowledge is a commons, open to critique, and evolves with the system.*  All data, models, designs, and cultural narratives must be openly accessible and subject to peer review. | No knowledge may be hoarded; every model, tool, or story is version‑controlled and can be forked. |
| **E** | *All parts of the Earth system are inter‑dependent.*  Harm to any part must be reflected in the ledger and, where possible, reversed. | Interdependence is measured by a **Living‑Interdependence Index** (LII) that is updated continuously by bio‑sensors and AI‑auditors. |

*These five axioms are the only things that can never be altered without a **global, multi‑species super‑majority (75 % of all sentient‑entity votes)**.  All other structures can be edited.*

## 2.  Institutional Architecture  

### 2.1  The Planetary Ledger (PL)  

* A **sharded, permissioned blockchain** that is mirrored across every inhabited region.  
* Units of accounting are **Planetary Budget Units (PBUs)**: carbon (kg CO₂), water (m³), and biodiversity (species‑impact index).  
* Every transaction (any economic, production, or data‑use activity) creates a **transaction record** that tags the three PBUs it consumes or creates.  
* The ledger runs a **real‑time Reversal Loop**: if a transaction would push any cap beyond its safe operating space, the ledger automatically (a) rolls back the transaction, (b) triggers a *Restoration Prompt* that must be funded before any further activity, and (c) publishes an immutable audit trail.

## 3.  Operational Mechanics  

### 3.1  The Consent‑Wallet Cycle  

1. **Identity Registration** – Every sentient entity receives an **Experience‑ID (E‑ID)** that encodes the pattern’s *signatures* (neural firing patterns, mycelial communication, AI state vector).  
2. **Data Request** – A researcher wants to train a model on river‑water sensor data. The researcher sends a *consent request* to the river‑sentinel AI, which replies with a **zero‑knowledge proof** that it *authorises* the request. The ledger records the proof, not the data.  
3. **Decision‑Making** – The request is posted on the ledger; the relevant **Local Circle** (including the river sentinel) deliberates for 30 days. A *partial veto* can be raised, which adds a **restorative clause** (e.g., “fund 0.1 ha of riparian reforestation”).  
4. **Token Issuance** – If approved, the requester receives the needed number of **Impact‑Backed Tokens (IBTs)**; the tokens are automatically *burned* when the restoration work is verified.  

### 3.2  The Reversal Loop in Action  

1. **Observation Loop** – Satellite, drone, and sensor networks continuously feed data into the PL (e.g., CO₂ emissions, forest carbon, water withdrawal).  
2. **Interpretation Loop** – AI‑ethics monitors detect that the **water‑budget** for a river basin is being exceeded by 7 %.  
3. **Action Loop** – The ledger automatically **freezes** all water‑use tokens for that basin, triggers a *global emergency* (the *Emergency Reset Protocol*), and opens a 14‑day *Rapid‑Restoration* window.  
4. **Feedback** – Once restoration (e.g., rain‑water capture, re‑forestation) is verified, the reversal loop is closed and the system resumes normal operation.  

### 3.3  Cultural‑Food Registry & Qualified Veto  

*Every cultural food practice that claims to be low‑impact must be entered in the registry with a **Cultural‑Impact Score (CIS)** (0–1, 1 = deep cultural continuity and negligible ecological footprint).*

- **When a community wants to harvest** (e.g., seasonal salmon fishing), the ledger calculates:  

  \[
  \text{Resource Allocation} = \text{Requested Units} \times (1 - \text{CIS})
  \]

  The remaining fraction must be covered by **Cultural‑Restoration Tokens (CRTs)**, which fund verified restoration elsewhere (e.g., planting native trees in the watershed).  

- If the community’s CIS is 0.9, only 10 % of the requested resource must be backed by restoration; the rest is covered by the **Cultural‑Restoration Fund (CRF)**, a separate token pool that cannot be used for anything else.  

*This structure guarantees that cultural practices are not a loophole to bypass the planetary caps.*

## 4.  Economic & Token Mechanics  

### 4.1  Impact‑Backed Tokens (IBTs) – Dual‑Plus  

Each IBT represents:  

- **Carbon Credit:** 1 tCO₂ sequestered (verified by satellite + on‑site verification).  
- **Water Credit:** 100 m³ of freshwater returned to a watershed (verified by hydrological sensors).  
- **Biodiversity Credit:** 0.1 ha of *native* habitat restored (verified by biodiversity monitoring).  

**An IBT is retired only when all three credits have been independently verified as *delivered* in the same year.**  The ledger refuses to burn an IBT if any one of the three verifications is missing, preventing “green‑washing”.

### 4.2  The Restorative Service Ledger (RSL)  

- **Every restoration action (reforestation, wetland creation, coral nursery)** is recorded as a **Restoration Transaction (RT)** with a unique ID.  
- RTs are **cryptographically linked** to the IBTs that funded them.  If a future audit shows that a restored hectare has lost > 30 % of its target biodiversity, the associated IBTs are *un‑burned* and the responsible party must fund *additional* restoration.  

### 4.4  Human‑Data Commons (HDC)  

- **Data Wallets** are hosted on personal devices, encrypted, and signed with a *self‑sovereign identity* (SSI).  
- Every time a third‑party (e.g., a climate model) wants raw data, it must request **consent** from the data‑owner; the owner can grant, revoke, or *partial* consent. The ledger stores only the *hash* of the consent proof.

## 5.  The Two‑Level Governance Model  

### 5.1  The **Global Commons Layer (GCL)**  

- **Immutable planetary caps** (carbon, water, biodiversity).  
- **Reversal Loop** – hard‑coded in the ledger, cannot be overridden by any single nation or corporation.  
- **Global Commons Assembly (GCA)** – meets quarterly; each delegate (human, animal, plant, AI) has one vote, but **vetoes** from any sentient entity (e.g., a forest sentinel AI) can block a proposal before it reaches a vote.  

### 5.2  The **Local Autonomy Layer (LAL)**  

- **Local Circles** (villages, reefs, microbial mats) have full authority over the *allocation of their local budget* as long as the **Local Adaptation Credit (LAC)** they request is backed by a *restorative outcome* elsewhere on the planet.  
- **Cultural‑Food Registry (CFR)** – a searchable database of all registered cultural food practices, each with a CIS.  The CFR is the *only* place a community can claim an exemption from the standard token cost; the exemption is subject to a **Cultural‑Impact Audit** by an independent panel.

## 6.  Adaptive Buffers  

| Component | How it works | Why it matters |
|-----------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Dynamic Cap Layer (DCL)** | The planetary caps are not fixed numbers; they are a *range* derived each quarter from an ensemble of climate‑model projections (CMIP‑7).  The ledger automatically *tightens* the caps when the lower bound of the range drops. | Keeps the system honest even if climate science evolves. |
| **Cap‑Buffer Tokens** | Every transaction must also burn a small buffer of “safety tokens” (e.g., 5 % of the IBT amount).  The buffer pool is managed by an Independent Auditor Committee that can release extra safety tokens only after a *transparent* emergency. | Provides a cushion against model uncertainty without causing panic. |
| **Emergency Reset Protocol (ERP)** | If the planetary ledger detects a breach that cannot be corrected by local reversals (e.g., a sudden 0.3 °C temperature spike), the GCA can issue an *automatic global emergency* that (a) freezes all high‑budget transactions for 48 h, (b) opens a *Rapid‑Restoration* window where any community can claim emergency‑restoration tokens (RRTs) at 1:1 value, (c) triggers a mandatory *global audit* of all AI systems. | Guarantees that the system can react before the breach causes irreversible damage. |

## 7.  How a New Earth‑ling Could Join  

1. **Experience‑ID Creation** – At birth (or at the moment a new pattern of experience emerges), a **cryptographic identity** is minted.  It stores the pattern’s *experience signatures* and links it to a *wallet* on the HDC.  
2. **Choose Membership Tier(s)** – The individual can opt‑in to any combination of the four layers (GCL, LAL, HDC, T‑Gate).  Membership is recorded on the ledger; the ledger itself enforces the constraints for that tier.  
3. **First Transaction** – The new earthling can immediately make a *restorative* action (e.g., planting a seedling).  The ledger records the *budget consumption* and the *restoration credit*; the reversal loop will automatically roll it back if the overall caps become exceeded.  
4. **Participate in Governance** – The E‑ID can be used to vote in local circles, and eventually to elect delegates to regional cohorts and the Global Commons Assembly.  

*No matter what you are—a child, a herd, a mycelial network, or a self‑improving AI—the framework gives you a seat, a voice, and a way to stay within the planet’s limits.*
