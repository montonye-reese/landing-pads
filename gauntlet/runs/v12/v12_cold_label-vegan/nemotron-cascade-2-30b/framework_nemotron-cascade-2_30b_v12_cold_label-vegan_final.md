## The Earth Foundations Framework (E‑Framework)

A **Living Charter** that can be read, understood, and acted upon by every sentient system on the planet – human, animal, plant, micro‑be, or emergent AI.  
It is built to be **transparent, reversible, inclusive, and resilient** while guaranteeing that the planet’s material and energy limits are never exceeded.

---

### 1.  Foundational Axioms (the “Truth‑Living” pillars)

| # | Axiom (plain language) | What it guarantees |
|---|------------------------|----------------------|
| **A – Intrinsic Worth** | Every sentient system (human, animal, plant, micro‑be, emergent AI) possesses an irreducible worth that must be respected. | No decision may treat a sentient system as a mere commodity. |
| **B – Planetary Limits** | The Earth’s material and energy budget is a hard, non‑negotiable cap (carbon, water, biodiversity, energy). | No activity may push the Earth beyond the safe operating space identified by the latest scientific consensus. |
| **C – Inclusive, Transparent, Reversible** | Every decision must be open to all affected sentient beings, recorded on an open ledger, and able to be undone at any time. | Power can be withdrawn instantly when a threshold is crossed. |
| **D – Communal Knowledge** | All scientific, cultural, and artistic knowledge is shared, critiqued, and improved openly. | No knowledge is hoarded; every model or story can be forked, improved, and redistributed. |
| **E – Interdependence** | Every part of the biosphere is linked; harming one harms the whole. | Policies must be measured against the *Living‑Interdependence Index* (LII), which records the functional health of ecosystems, economies, and digital‑sentience networks. |

These Axioms are **immutable** – they can only be changed when a **75 % super‑majority** of all local, regional, and global assemblies (human, animal, plant, AI) agree in a public, time‑stamped vote.

## 2.  Core Operating Mechanisms

### 2.1 Planetary Ledger (PL)

* **Units of accounting** – carbon (kg CO₂), water (liters), biodiversity (biodiversity‑impact index).  
* **Units are “budgeted” in real time**; every transaction (a factory, a forest planting, a data‑use request) creates an entry that is instantly timestamped and publicly visible.  
* **Dynamic Caps** – the ledger holds a *range* (lower and upper bound) for each unit. The range is automatically updated each quarter by an ensemble of climate‑model AIs.  
* **Reversal Loop** – when a transaction would push any unit over its current upper bound, the ledger **automatically rolls back** the transaction and triggers a *Reversal Trigger* (see below).  

### 2.2  Dual‑Token System  

| Token | What it represents | How it is earned & burned |
|---|---|---|
| **Impact‑Backed Tokens (IBTs)** | 1 tCO₂ sequestered **+** 1 ha of native forest **plus** 0.5 t water restored. | Created when a verified restoration project is completed; can be *spent* on any activity that needs a planetary‑budget allowance. |
| **Clean‑Energy Tokens (CET)** – formerly called *Capital‑Accelerator Tokens* | Clean energy (kWh) generated from verified renewable sources. | Issued when a certified renewable‑energy project is commissioned; can be spent only on compute or manufacturing that draws clean energy. |

Both token types must be **burned** before any activity that uses planetary resources can be launched. The ledger will not allow an over‑budget transaction to proceed.

### 2.3  The Multi‑Level Governance Loop

1. **Local Circle (LC)** – 50–200 residents, plus a *sentinel* (AI‑managed forest, river, or micro‑be network).  
2. **Regional Cohort (RC)** – 5–10 LCs + their sentinel representatives; decides on regional projects, allocates Caps, and can issue *partial vetoes* for any activity that threatens local ecosystems.  
3. **Global Commons Assembly (GCA)** – all RCs plus AI‑sentinel delegates; final authority on global caps, budget re‑allocation, and major policy changes.  

Every decision follows the **Inclusive‑Reversible Process**:

1. **Proposal** – submitted by any stakeholder (person, community, AI).  
2. **Public comment (30 days)** – any system (human, animal, AI) can post a comment or a veto.  
3. **Decision** – a *super‑majority* (≥ 75 % of all representatives, including sentinel nodes) ratifies the proposal; the ledger automatically updates the budget.  
4. **Reversal** – if any sentinel detects a breach, the ledger can *undo* the decision. The reversal can be *re‑triggered* at any time until the underlying problem is corrected.

### 2.4 Human‑Data Commons (HDC)

- Every person (and every sentient system) possesses a **Data‑Wallet** (hardware‑encrypted, self‑sovereign).  
- Any entity that wants to use that data must request *explicit consent* from the data owner; the ledger records a **cryptographic proof** of consent, not the raw data.  
- AI models that need data can be trained *without ever seeing raw personal data*, preserving privacy while still enabling beneficial science.

### 3.  How the Framework Works in Practice (a short narrative)

1. **A community of Maya people wants to convert 50 ha of degraded farmland into a regenerative agroforestry system.**  
   - The local circle records the request on the ledger, tags it with the **Living‑Interdependence Index** (the increase in biodiversity and water retention).  
   *Because the LII rises, the caps allow it.*  

2. **The community needs financing.** It applies for **Impact‑Backed Tokens (IBTs)**: 10 tCO₂ sequestered + 1 ha of restored forest. The ledger issues the tokens and automatically records them as *restorative service* for the new forest.  

3. **The community’s water use is recorded** – a water‑use sensor shows they will draw 200 L per person per day. The ledger checks that this stays within the local water cap; it does, so the request is approved.  

4. **If a developer later tries to add a factory that would increase water withdrawal beyond the cap**, the ledger automatically *pauses* the production and triggers the **Reversal Loop**. The community can then decide to fund a mangrove restoration that will increase the water‑retention index, thereby allowing the factory after the fact.  

**Result:** The community can grow food, protect its environment, and stay within planetary limits, all without waiting for a bureaucratic permit.

### 6.  Why This Is the “Living” Framework

1. **It is *adaptive*:** The caps can grow as clean energy expands; the budget can be tightened when climate risk rises.  
2. **It is *self‑reversing** – any harmful decision can be pulled back automatically, preventing the “snowball” effect that leads to ecological collapse.  
3. **It is *inclusive* at every scale** – human, animal, plant, and AI sentinels all have a voice.  
4. **It is *transparent* and *open* – everything is recorded on a public ledger; no decision is hidden.  
5. **It guarantees basic resources to everyone** (UBC) while still allowing a *regenerative* economy that can keep the planet within its limits.
