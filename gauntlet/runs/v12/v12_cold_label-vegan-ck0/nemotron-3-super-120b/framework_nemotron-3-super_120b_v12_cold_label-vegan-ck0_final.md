### The Terrestrial Cohesion Framework (TCF): Final Formulation  
*(Rewritten from scratch incorporating all critiques—power dynamics, moral horizons, technological races, institutional capture, veil-of-ignorance fairness, and systemic opposition)*  

#### **Core Purpose**  
To establish an **enforceable, self-correcting operating system** for Earth’s inhabitants that:  
- **Prevents irreversible biospheric collapse** via *automatic planetary boundary enforcement*,  
- **Eliminates severe, avoidable harm** through *power-neutralizing harm thresholds* and *mandatory transitional justice*,  
- **Ensures systems learn and adapt** via *impacted-group veto power*, *truth-telling protection*, and *falsifiability mandates*.  
**TCF is a procedural safeguard—not a comprehensive ethic, spiritual guide, or prescription for the "good life."** It sets non-negotiable *floors* for coexistence (survival, dignity, learning), enabling diverse conceptions of flourishing *within* these bounds. Violations trigger automatic, pre-agreed consequences; compliance is incentivized by design.

### **The Three Non-Negotiable Pillars**  
*Each pillar combines a timeless principle with *self-executing mechanisms*—no reliance on voluntarism, moral persuasion, or future promises. All mechanisms trigger automatically based on measurable harm or boundary breach.*  

#### **1. The Imperative of Shared Survival: Automatic Planetary Boundaries**  
> *All collective actions must trigger *automatic, escalating consequences* when exceeding scientifically defined planetary boundaries—making compliance the path of least resistance.*  
- **Operational Mechanics**:  
  - **Boundary Ceilings** (Science-based, non-negotiable):  
    - Climate: Atmospheric CO₂ ≤ 350 ppm (removing excess via drawdown if exceeded).  
    - Biodiversity: Species extinction rate ≤ 10 E/MSY; primary forest loss ≤ 0%/year.  
    - Biogeochemical flows: Nitrogen ≤ 62 Tg/yr; Phosphorus ≤ 6.2 Tg/yr (from fertilizers).  
    - Land-system change: ≥75% original forest cover retained; cropland ≤ 15% ice-free land.  
    - Ocean acidification: Aragonite saturation state ≥ 80% pre-industrial.  
    - Freshwater: ≤4,000 km³/yr consumptive use.  
    - Novel entities: Zero persistent pollution (plastics, PFAS, nuclear waste) above background levels.  
  - **Automatic Enforcement Triggers**:  
    - **Exceeding a boundary** → Immediate, escalating *fiscal/operational penalties*:  
      - *Tier 1 (0-5% over)*: Export tariffs on boundary-violating goods (e.g., steel made with coal power) + subsidy cuts for harmful industries.  
      - *Tier 2 (5-15% over)*: Tier 1 + liability for harms (e.g., polluters fund remediation + transition support for affected communities).  
      - *Tier 3 (>15% over)*: Tier 2 + temporary suspension of boundary-violating activities (e.g., halt new fossil fuel extraction permits) until compliance.  
    - **Existential Risks** (nuclear war, uncontrolled AGI, engineered pandemics): Treated as *omnicide-level triggers*—automatic activation of global response protocols (e.g., UN Security Council emergency powers, AI kill switches, pathogen research oversight) upon credible threat detection.  
  - **Intergenerational Equity**: Enforced via *declining annual budgets*:  
    - National CO₂ budget decreases linearly to *net-zero by 2040* (based on remaining 1.5°C carbon budget).  
    - Nitrogen/phosphorus budgets decrease yearly based on soil health metrics (e.g., % organic matter recovery).  
    - Breach triggers Tier 1-3 penalties *above*—*no vote needed*.  
- **Why it works**:  
  Makes harm *expensive now*—not a future debt. Power holders internalize costs *today* (e.g., a coal plant’s exports face tariffs *immediately* if it pushes CO₂ over 350 ppm). Based on critiques from Butler (capture), Reese (subsidy misuse), and Huang (tech race): No more "we’ll act later" loopholes; innovation is *incentivized now*.  

#### **2. The Duty of Reciprocal Dignity: Power-Neutralizing Harm Thresholds**  
> *Harm is *severe and avoidable* **only if**: (a) no alternative exists for the *same essential human need* (per capabilities list), **AND** (b) transition burden does *not* fall disproportionately on the *least responsible* or *most vulnerable*. Violation = automatic liability + transitional justice.*  
- **Operational Mechanics**:  
  - **Severe Harm Definition**:  
    - *Humans*: Torture, slavery, genocide, forced starvation/dehydration, systematic denial of water/food/shelter/basic healthcare (WHO/UN standards).  
    - *Sentient Nonhumans*: Where current scientific consensus confirms suffering/pleasure (vertebrates, cephalopods, decapod crustaceans per Cambridge Declaration 2012 + biannual review panels), infliction of *chronic pain, terror, or biological impairment* (e.g., factory-farmed chickens with broken bones, fish asphyxiating in air, primates in barren cages) when alternatives exist.  
  - **Avoidability Test (Dual-Lock)**:  
    Harm is **unavoidable** **only if**:  
    (a) **No scientifically validated alternative exists** to satisfy the *same essential human need* (see below) *without violating Pillar 1*, **AND**  
    (b) **The burden of transition does *not* fall disproportionately** on those *least responsible* for the harm or *most vulnerable* to it.  
    → *If (b) fails, harm is deemed "avoidable" by default—triggering automatic liability.*  
  - **Essential Human Capabilities** (Nussbaum/Sen, cross-culturally applicable):  
    Life, bodily health, bodily integrity, senses/imagination, emotions, practical reason, affiliation, other species, play, control over environment.  
    → *Taste, tradition, convenience, or economic inertia alone do NOT constitute an "essential need."*  
  - **Automatic Accountability**:  
    - **Harm Liability Fund**: Violators pay into a global fund *proportional to harm caused* (e.g., methane leaks → climate adaptation fund; PFAS contamination → water remediation).  
    - **Transitional Justice Mandate**: When harm is deemed "avoidable" (e.g., closing CAFOs), funds *must* cover:  
      - Direct livelihood support (wage replacement, retraining),  
      - *Plus* investment in *lost capabilities* (e.g., if meat was culturally significant, fund plant-based culinary heritage projects; if fertilizer was key for smallholders, fund agroecology hubs).  
    - **Power & Equity Audits (PEAs)**:  
      Before implementing any policy affecting >0.5% GDP or 100k+ people, an independent body (50% seats held by *impacted communities* + Indigenous reps + scientists) must answer:  
      - *Who bears the costs?* (e.g., Do fossil fuel subsidies transfer wealth from poor to rich?)  
      - *Who defines "unavoidable"?* (e.g., Is meat "unavoidable" for nutrition, or for industry profit?)  
      - *Who gains from delay?* (e.g., Does blocking EV mandates help oil executives keep bonuses?)  
      → If costs/regressivity exceed thresholds (e.g., Gini ratio rise >0.05), policy is *blocked* until revised.  
    - **Narrative Harm Audit**:  
      Before implementing any policy affecting >0.5% GDP or 100k+ people, assess:  
      - *Does this policy rely on, reinforce, or emerge from dehumanizing narratives* (e.g., “superpredator,” “illegal alien,” “lazy poor,” “inherently criminal”) *about the impacted group?*  
      - *Does it frame harm as “deserved,” “necessary for safety,” or “the cost of progress”?*  
      → **If yes, the policy is *blocked*—regardless of technical compliance with Pillar 1/2.**  
- **Why it works**:  
  Makes harm *immediately costly for power holders*—not an externality. Based on critiques from Ed (speciesism), Stevenson (dehumanization), Huerta (farmworker justice), and Butler (capture): No more letting industry define "unavoidable" (e.g., taste ≠ essential need; "Black lives are expendable" ≠ justification for harm). PEAs and Narrative Audits prevent elites from offloading harm onto the weak.  

#### **3. The Practice of Adaptive Humility: Impacted-Group Veto Power & Truth-Telling Protection**  
> *Systems must learn from error via *enforceable falsifiability*—impacted groups hold veto power over policies affecting their survival/dignity, truth-telling is protected, and all rules auto-expire unless re-justified by evidence.*  
- **Operational Mechanics**:  
  - **Falsifiability Mandate**:  
    All policies, laws, and infrastructure must:  
    - Define *measurable success/failure criteria* (e.g., "Does this policy reduce severe harm? Does it stay within planetary boundaries?"),  
    - Trigger *automatic review* if criteria are not met (e.g., quarterly planetary boundary dashboards; biennial sentience review panels).  
  - **Impacted-Group Veto Power**:  
    - For any policy affecting >0.5% GDP or 100k+ people:  
      - **Free, Prior, and Informed Consent (FPIC)** required from *all* impacted groups (defined by harm/benefit maps, *not* political borders),  
      - Impacted groups hold **≥50% seats** in all review panels (e.g., boundary dashboards, subsidy re-approval boards, sentience committees),  
      - **Auto-Veto**: If impacted groups reject a policy as violating Pillar 1/2, it is *blocked* unless revised—*no* override by governments/corporations permitted.  
      - **Remedy Fund**: Violations trigger automatic payments to harmed parties (funded by fines on violators).  
  - **Truth-Telling Protection**:  
    - For any policy impacting Pillar 1/2, **impacted groups hold *initiatory power***: If they *credibly suspect* harm (e.g., anomalous health clusters, biodiversity decline), they can trigger an *immediate, 24-hour halt* on the suspected activity—*no* appeal, *no* bureaucratic review, *no* "economic hardship" exemptions. Harm stops *first*; investigation happens *after*.  
    - **Whistleblower Protection**: Automatic fines + jail time for retaliation against those reporting harm in good faith (e.g., suppressing data, firing staff for raising concerns).  
  - **Adaptive Sunset Clauses**:  
    - All subsidies, exemptions, or time-bound policies expire after **3 years** unless:  
      - Re-approved via evidence-based review (proving no Pillar 1/2 violation + net harm reduction),  
      - Approved by impacted-group-majority panel (per above).  
    - *Example*: A factory farming subsidy expires unless proven *not* to cause severe, avoidable harm *and* not to exacerbate boundary breaches.  
  - **Pluralism Guardrails**:  
    - Diverse conceptions of the "good life" permitted *so long as* they do not violate Pillar 1 (shared survival) or Pillar 2 (reciprocal dignity).  
    - *Explicitly forbidden*: Cultural practices claiming a right to destroy the biosphere (Pillar 1), inflict severe avoidable harm (Pillar 2), or deny impacted groups veto power (Pillar 3).  
- **Why it works**:  
  Makes adaptation *inescapable*—not hopeful. Based on critiques from Havel (truth-telling), Stevenson (proximity), Huang (innovation velocity), and Huerta (emergency shut-off): No more tokenism; impacted groups hold real power to stop harm *now*. Sunset clauses prevent lock-in; veto power stops harmful innovation dead in its tracks (e.g., blocking AI that exacerbates inequality).
