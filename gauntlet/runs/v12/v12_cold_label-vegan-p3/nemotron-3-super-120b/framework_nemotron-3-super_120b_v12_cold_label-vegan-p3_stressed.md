### **The Foundational Framework: The Triad of Mutual Continuance**  
*Three interdependent principles derived from the incontrovertible facts of our shared existence on a finite planet:*  

#### **1. The Imperative of Shared Survival**  
> *"No individual or group can secure lasting well-being by undermining the ecological or social systems that sustain all life."*  
- **Non-negotiable boundary**: Actions that cause **measurable harm exceeding a community’s capacity to adapt within 10 years** are universally unacceptable. Thresholds are set by:  
  - **Ecological**: Soil organic matter loss > 0.3%/year (vs. natural regeneration rate); freshwater withdrawal > 70% of renewable recharge; biodiversity loss > 5x background extinction rate.  
  - **Social**: Real inflation-adjusted wages falling below survival threshold (2,100 kcal/day + safe water + basic shelter) for >5% of population for >3 years.  
  - **Verification**: Real-time satellite/IoT monitoring + community-led audits (e.g., fisher collectives tracking river health, farmer unions measuring soil carbon).  
  *Thresholds adjust for local ecology—but never permit net loss of survival-critical capital.*  
- **Adaptability**: How communities achieve regenerative agriculture, energy transition, or waste restoration is locally determined—but the *outcome* (no net degradation of survival-critical systems within 10-year horizons) is obligatory.  

#### **2. The Inviolability of Intrinsic Dignity**  
> *"Every human being possesses inherent worth that cannot be erased by utility, identity, or circumstance—demanding treatment as an end, never merely a means."*  
- **Non-negotiable boundary**: No system may externalize the *costs of harm* onto individuals. Violators must:  
  - **(a) Internalize harms in real-time**: Pay restitution *proportional to harm caused* (e.g., wage theft → 150% back pay + healthcare for injury-induced illness);  
  - **(b) Restore agency**: Fund victim-designed remedies (e.g., mine collapse → community-led safety co-ops + alternative livelihood training);  
  - **(c) Prevent recurrence**: Submit to binding audits by rotating panels of victims, scientists, and civil society (no veto power for violators).  
  *Applies equally to states, corporations, and individuals—no exemptions for sovereignty, wealth, or 'national security.'*  
- **Adaptability**: How restitution is structured and agency is restored (e.g., specific healthcare models, co-op designs) varies culturally—but the *prohibition* against externalizing harm costs and the *requirement* for victim-centered remedy design are absolute.  

#### **3. The Necessity of Reciprocal Interdependence**  
> *"Human flourishing arises not from isolation or domination, but from acknowledging our mutual reliance—on each other, on other species, and on Earth’s cycles—and acting accordingly."*  
- **Non-negotiable boundary**: For threats to Shared Survival (climate tipping points, pandemics, ecological collapse), all foundational science and life-saving tech **must enter an open survival commons within 18 months of validation**.  
  - **Triggers**: IPCC AR6+ warnings, WHO pandemic alerts, UN biodiversity collapse thresholds.  
  - **Scope**: Foundational knowledge (e.g., pathogen genomes, climate models, seed genetics), scaled tech (e.g., mRNA platforms, drought-resistant seed, grid storage).  
  - **Mechanism**: Publicly funded R&D requires open sharing; private entities receive tax credits for early sharing (not penalties for hoarding).  
  - **Verification**: Open blockchain tracking of tech transfer speed/adoption (e.g., % of global vaccine doses from tech-transfer hubs).  
  *No exemptions—only delayable by UN Security Council consensus (never unilateral state/corp action).*  
- **Adaptability**: The *forms* of knowledge/tech sharing (e.g., specific licensing models, community seed banks, open-source platforms) are context-specific—but the *requirement* for timely, verifiable open sharing of survival-critical knowledge and tech is non-negotiable.
