The Foundational Framework: The Triad of Mutual Continuance  
Three interdependent principles derived from the incontrovertible facts of our shared existence on a finite planet:  

**1. The Imperative of Shared Survival**  
> *"No individual or group can secure lasting well-being by undermining the ecological or social systems that sustain all life."*  
- **Why it’s foundational**: Human survival depends on stable climate, breathable water, arable soil, and biodiversity—systems that ignore borders. Harm to these systems is not theoretical; it manifests as immediate, measurable threats to health, food/water security, and social stability. Ignoring this guarantees eventual collapse, as seen in historical civilizations that exceeded ecological thresholds (e.g., soil depletion triggering famine, pollution causing irreversible ecosystem loss). Continuance requires acting *before* harm exceeds a system’s capacity to absorb or reverse it.  
- **Non-negotiable boundary**: Actions that cause **measurable harm exceeding a community’s capacity to adapt within a 10-year horizon** are universally unacceptable. Harm is measured by:  
  - **Ecological**: Soil organic matter loss > 0.3%/year (vs. natural regeneration rate); freshwater withdrawal > 70% of renewable recharge rate; biodiversity loss > 5x background extinction rate.  
  - **Social**: Real inflation-adjusted wages falling below survival threshold (2,100 kcal/day + safe water + basic shelter) for >5% of population for >3 years; trust in institutions falling below 30% (per verified community surveys) for >5 years.  
  - **Verification priority**: Harm detection begins with **lived-experience sentinels** (e.g., farmworkers reporting wells drying by July, fishers noting collapses in catch, Indigenous land observers tracking medicinal plant loss)—weighted at 60% in harm-assessment dashboards—supplemented by real-time satellite/IoT data (40%) and subsidy-flow tracking. Thresholds activate *immediately* if:  
    (a) Measured harm exceeds recovery rates, *OR*  
    (b) Public subsidies or market rules directly incentivize harm exceeding recovery rates (e.g., livestock subsidies driving stocking rates that degrade soil; crop insurance favoring monocultures over polycultures; tax breaks for factory farms), *OR*  
    (c) Intergenerational harm persists (e.g., wealth gaps, health disparities, or environmental racism suppressing survival capacity across ≥2 generations, verified via longitudinal data).  
  *Thresholds adjust for local ecology—but never permit net loss of survival-critical capital. Irreversible harm (e.g., species extinction, aquifer collapse, permanent trust destruction) is never permitted, regardless of claimed benefits.*  
- **Adaptability**: How communities achieve net-positive ecological and social outcomes (e.g., regenerative agriculture, energy transition, waste restoration) is locally determined—but the *outcome* (no net degradation of survival-critical systems within 10-year horizons, verified by harm-assessment dashboards) is obligatory.  

**2. The Inviolability of Intrinsic Dignity**  
> *"Every human being possesses inherent worth that cannot be erased by utility, identity, or circumstance—demanding treatment as an end, never merely a means."*  
- **Why it’s foundational**: Denying dignity (via wage theft, torture, dehumanization, extreme deprivation, or denial of basic needs) destroys the trust, cooperation, and innovation capacity essential for solving shared survival challenges. Conversely, recognizing dignity enables resilient societies where people invest in collective futures. History shows systems that instrumentalize humans (e.g., slavery, exploitative labor regimes, denial of healthcare) inevitably generate instability, violence, and reduced adaptive capacity—proving dignity is not a luxury but a survival prerequisite.  
- **Non-negotiable boundary**: No system may externalize the *costs of harm* onto individuals—or permit conditions that prevent survival-critical stewardship. Violators must:  
  - **(a) Internalize harms in real-time**: Pay restitution *proportional to harm caused* (e.g., wage theft → 150% back pay + healthcare for trauma-induced illness; pollution exposure → funded healthcare for asthma/cancer linked to emissions);  
  - **(b) Restore agency**: Fund *victim-designed remedies* (e.g., mine collapse → community-led safety co-ops + alternative livelihood training; water contamination → community-built filtration units + watershed restoration jobs);  
  - **(c) Prevent recurrence**: Submit to binding audits by rotating panels of victims, scientists, and civil society (no veto power for violators);  
  - **(d) Cease harmful practices immediately** when economic incentives or historical exclusion drive harm (e.g., halt wage theft; stop subsidies locking farmers into extractive cycles; end practices rooted in past exclusion like redlining or Indigenous land theft).  
  *Applies equally to states, corporations, and individuals—no exemptions for sovereignty, wealth, 'national security,' 'economic necessity,' 'tradition,' or 'historical precedent.' Restitution and agency restoration funds must be held in victim-controlled trusts (managed by elected victim representatives), paid in kind (direct goods/services like water filters or arable land), and verified solely by recipient panels (e.g., 'Did this remedy *actually* restore your capacity to work/safely tend the land?'). Any suppression of truth-telling or externalization of harm triggers automatic triple fines + permanent exclusion from framework participation.*  
- **Adaptability**: How restitution is structured and agency is restored (e.g., specific healthcare models, co-op designs, water access solutions) varies culturally—but the *prohibition* against externalizing harm costs, the *requirement* for victim-centered remedy design, and the *mandate* to prevent recurrence via ceased harmful practices and victim-controlled remedies are absolute.  

**3. The Necessity of Reciprocal Interdependence**  
> *"Human flourishing arises not from isolation or domination, but from acknowledging our mutual reliance—on each other, on other species, and on Earth’s cycles—and acting accordingly."*  
- **Why it’s foundational**: No one creates food, knowledge, or oxygen alone. We depend on strangers’ labor (farmers, teachers, healthcare workers), microbial ecosystems in our guts, forests regulating rain, and shared cultural inheritance. Ignoring this breeds fragility (e.g., just-in-time supply chains collapsing during pandemics; monocropping increasing pest vulnerability); honoring it builds adaptive capacity (e.g., diverse crop rotations stabilizing yields; open-source science accelerating crisis response). Continuance requires systems where waste becomes food, knowledge flows freely, and trust enables rapid collective action—especially for threats like pandemics or climate tipping points where delay multiplies harm.  
- **Non-negotiable boundary**: For threats to Shared Survival (climate tipping points, pandemics, ecological collapse), all foundational science and life-saving tech **must enter an open survival commons within a timeframe inversely proportional to harm velocity**:  
  - **High harm velocity** (e.g., exponential pandemic spread, rapid ice-sheet collapse >10mm/yr, wildfire spread rate >5km/hr): Open sharing required within **6 months** of validation.  
  - **Medium harm velocity** (e.g., gradual soil degradation, slow-onset malnutrition, biodiversity loss at 5x background rate): Open sharing required within **18 months** of validation.  
  - **Low harm velocity** (e.g., background species extinction <1x background rate): Open sharing required within **36 months** of validation.  
  - **Growth-velocity adjustment**: If verified growth velocity >5%/yr in the sector developing the tech (indicating rapid innovation investment), sharing timelines extend *proportionally*—*provided* harm-reduction velocity (e.g., soil carbon gain per R&D dollar, lives saved per vaccine dollar) is tracked and shown to be increasing.  
  - **Truth-flow mandate**: All survival-critical tech sharing *must* weight **frontline steward knowledge** (e.g., Indigenous fire management, traditional water conservation, generational pest observation) and **truth about historical exclusion** (e.g., how redlining created asthma hotspots, how colonial land theft drives current water insecurity) *equally* with foundational science in survival-commons sharing—and actively integrate both into tech development within 6 months of validation.  
  - **Verification**: Open blockchain tracking of tech sharing speed/adoption vs. harm velocity/growth velocity/truth-integration metrics (e.g., % of global vaccine doses from tech-transfer hubs within harm-velocity-defined windows; % of climate-resilient seeds developed with farmworker/Indigenous input; reduction in harm metrics post-implementation of truth-integrated systems).  
  *No exemptions—only delayable by unanimous consensus of three independent victim panels (e.g., flood-survivors, pandemic-affected communities, Indigenous land defenders) who confirm sharing would cause immediate, irreversible harm (e.g., enabling imminent genocide). Economic, political, or technological convenience never justifies delay.*  
- **Adaptability**: The *forms* of knowledge/tech sharing (e.g., specific licensing models, community seed banks, open-source platforms) are context-specific—but the *requirement* for timely, verifiable open sharing of survival-critical knowledge and tech, weighted by harm velocity, growth velocity, and truth-flow mandates, is non-negotiable.
