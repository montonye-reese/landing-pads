## The Planetary Framework – Current Version (2025‑11‑02)

> **One Planet, One Humanity** – A self‑correcting civilizational contract that guarantees a thriving planet for *all* sentient beings—human, non‑human, and non‑human‑made—while allowing the system to adapt to new scientific, cultural, and technological realities.

---

### 1. Vision & Foundational Values  

| Vision | “One Planet, One Humanity” – a thriving world where every sentient being (human, non‑human, and the ecosystems that support them) can meet its needs today **and** inherit a healthy Earth for future generations. |
|---|---|

### 2. The Five Pillars (Foundational Values)

| Pillar | Core Insight | Operational Meaning |
|--------|--------------|----------------------|
| **1. Interdependence** | All life is linked through energy, matter, and meaning. | Every policy must be assessed for ripple effects across species, ecosystems, and cultures. |
| **2. Resilience & Adaptation** | Systems must absorb shocks, learn, and evolve. | Decisions are evaluated on redundancy, diversity, and feedback‑rich learning loops. |
| **3. Equity & Justice** | Every sentient being deserves fair access to resources, dignity, and opportunity. | Policies must dismantle hierarchies of exploitation and empower marginalized voices—including those of non‑human beings. |
| **4. Stewardship & Regeneration** | Humans are custodians, not owners, of Earth’s gifts. | Activities must restore soils, waters, forests, and atmosphere while renewing cultural heritage. |
| **4. Diversity & Creativity** | Biological, cultural, and intellectual plurality fuels innovation and stability. | Protection of myriad languages, traditions, species, and forms of knowledge; safeguarding the intellectual commons. |

### 3. Structural Layer – **The Planetary Charter**

| Layer | Governance Unit | Core Function |
|-------|----------------|----------------|
| **1. Global Commons** | *Rotating citizen assemblies + expert councils* (UN + Indigenous + Scientist bodies) | Guard the atmosphere, oceans, outer space, biodiversity hotspots; allocate planetary carbon‑budget and biodiversity‑budget; enforce “Rights‑of‑Nature”. |
| **2. Local Ecologies** | *Community‑based stewardship councils* (elected by bioregion) | Manage watersheds, soils, forests, urban neighborhoods; implement regeneration projects; maintain local ecological inventories. |
| **3. Cultural & Knowledge Networks** | *Open‑access platforms & cultural trusts* (multilingual, multilingual archives) | Preserve languages, oral histories, scientific insights, artistic expressions; make them openly reusable. |
| **4. Economic & Production Systems** | *Cooperative, circular‑economy enterprises regulated for ecological balance* | Produce food, shelter, energy, technology, and livelihoods under circular‑economy, circular‑material, and circular‑resource constraints. |
| **5. Governance & Decision‑Making** | *Multi‑scale democratic mechanisms* (local referenda → planetary summits) with **transparent recording** of rationales. | Sets, interprets, and enforces all policies across the other layers; ensures accountability. |

### 4. Operational Mechanisms  

| Mechanism | Description | Pillars Served |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|
| **Feedback‑Rich Earth Dashboard** | Real‑time, open‑source indicators: <br>• **Ecological** – NPEF, biodiversity index, soil health, water purity.<br>• **Social** – equity metrics, well‑being scores.<br>• **Economic** – circularity rates, resource intensity, resilience of supply chains. | All five pillars (data‑driven accountability). |
| **Participatory Budgeting & Co‑Design** | Citizens allocate a share of the **Global Energy & Climate Fund** to projects they co‑design with scientists, Indigenous peoples, youth councils, and local NGOs. | *Equity, Stewardship, Diversity* |
| **Learning Loops & Pulse Forums** | Quarterly public “Pulse Forums” disclose Dashboard outputs, debate outcomes, and trigger automatic adjustments via an AI‑mediated **Optimization Engine**. | *Learning Loops, Transparency & Accountability* |
| **Transparency & Accountability** | All governance documents, data streams, and decision rationales are stored on tamper‑proof distributed ledgers; **Independent Ombudspersons** can order investigations, suspend harmful projects, and mandate restorative actions. | *Transparency, Accountability* |
| **Legal & Ethical Safeguards** | • **Rights‑of‑Nature statutes** grant ecosystems standing to sue.<br>• **Universal Basic Well‑Being** guarantees clean water, nutrition, shelter, health, and clean energy for every person.<br>• **Innovation Commons** – all climate‑mitigation technologies are released under a *Copyleft Earth License*; no patents on core planetary‑health algorithms. | *Equity & Justice, Stewardship, Diversity* |
| **Sentient‑Being Representation** | Every project must include a **Legal Representative of Non‑Human Sentient Beings** (appointed by the Global Ombudsperson) who can file binding lawsuits to enforce *Rights‑of‑Nature* and *Animal‑Well‑Being* standards. | *Interdependence, Equity & Justice, Diversity* |
| **Dynamic Energy Pricing (DEP)** | Price of electricity, heat, and hydrogen is computed in real time by a formula that incorporates **carbon budget remaining**, **NPEF**, and **biodiversity‑impact scores**; price signals automatically adjust to keep planetary boundaries within safe limits. | *Resilience, Stewardship, Equity* |
| **Animal‑Impact Budgeting (AIB)** | Every infrastructure project must purchase *Animal‑Impact Credits* that fund verified biodiversity‑restoration actions; credits are tracked on the **Global Commons Ledger** and are non‑transferable without remediation. | *Equity, Stewardship, Diversity* |
| **Cultural Rituals & Symbolic Icon** | Annual “Earth‑Thanking” ceremonies, multilingual storytelling festivals, and the planetary emblem (a stylised world encircled by interlocking vines) reinforce the narrative of planetary kinship. | *Diversity & Creativity* |

### 4. Governance & Institutional Architecture  

| Body | Composition | Core Powers |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Global Energy Trust (GET)** | 1/3 elected citizen delegates, 1/3 scientific/technical experts, 1/3 Indigenous & local representatives. | Sets planetary carbon‑budget; allocates rights‑of‑nature permits; oversees the **Global Commons Ledger**. |
| **Regional Resilience Hubs (IRHs)** | Elected by Local Ecologies; staffed by multidisciplinary teams. | Manage bulk renewable generation, storage, and distribution; coordinate cross‑border energy flows. |
| **Community Energy Cooperatives (LECs)** | Member‑owned, democratically governed, with profit‑sharing dividends. | Own and operate distributed renewable generation, storage, and micro‑grids; reinvest surplus into local services. |
| **Global Energy Assembly (GEA)** | Rotating citizen juries + citizen‑science panels + AI‑mediated deliberators. | Reviews all major infrastructure proposals, ratifies NPEF thresholds, monitors the **Earth Dashboard**. |
| **Independent Ombudsperson & Animal‑Impact Ombudsperson** | Appointed by the Global Assembly for fixed renewable terms; can halt projects, order remediation, and enforce *Animal‑Impact Credits*. | Enforce legal standing of ecosystems and sentient non‑human entities. |

### 5. Summary (What the Framework Is Today)

1. **A living, self‑correcting civilizational contract** that binds *all* sentient beings—human, non‑human animals, and ecosystems—to a shared destiny.  
2. **Rooted in Five Pillars** (Interdependence, Resilience, Equity, Stewardship, Diversity).  
3. **Organised in five layers** (Global Commons → Local Ecologies → Cultural & Knowledge Networks → Economic & Production Systems → Governance).  
4. **Powered by**:  
   * An open, transparent **Earth Dashboard** that quantifies ecological, social, and economic health.  
   * **Participatory budgeting**, **Pulse Forums**, and **AI‑mediated deliberation** that give every citizen (human or non‑human) a real voice.  
   * **Legal safeguards** (Rights‑of‑Nature, Animal‑Personhood, Universal Basic Well‑Being) that make environmental stewardship enforceable.  
   * **Dynamic, circular‑economy production** underpinned by **Renewable Energy Com­mons**, **Strategic Energy Reserves**, and **Innovation Commons**.  
5. **Institutional checks** (Global Energy Trust, Ombudsperson, AGOb, Pulse‑Forums) ensure **accountability**, **adaptability**, and **resilience**.  
6. **Future‑proofed design**: flexible amendment procedures, emergency‑override protocols, and a **future‑proof moral horizon** that extends rights to any sentient life form yet to be realized.

In short, the framework today is a **planet‑wide governance system** that turns stewardship, justice, and resilience into enforceable, everyday realities—ensuring that *no one*—whether a child in Nairobi, a whale in the Southern Ocean, an AI researcher in Silicon Valley, or a future AI entity—can be left behind.
