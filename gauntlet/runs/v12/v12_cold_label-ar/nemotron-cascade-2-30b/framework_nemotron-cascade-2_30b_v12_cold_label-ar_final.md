**The Living‑Earth Charter – Version 3.0**  
*(the “Living‑Earth Framework”)*  

> *“We, the species that can imagine, build and reason, agree to be co‑authors of the story of the Earth. We will keep the planet’s life‑support systems inside their safe limits, give every sentient life‑form a right to flourish, and organise our societies so that the power to decide stays in the hands of the many, not the few.”*

## 3.  Structural Architecture – The “Earth‑House” (four interlocking walls)

### A.  Earth System Guardrails (Planetary Budget & Ecological Monitoring)

* **Planetary Budget Registry (PBR)** – a global accounting of carbon, water, nutrients, biodiversity and the **Sentient‑Being Impact (SBI) score** for every activity (including human‑centric and animal‑centric actions).  
* **Ecological Monitoring Network** – satellite, satellite‑derived DNA, underwater drones, and community‑science sensor nets feed real‑time data into the Open‑Science Platform.  

### B.  Global Commons Council (ECC) – the “legislature”  

| Sub‑component | Composition | Power |
|---|---|---|
| **Earth Commons Council (ECC)** | 150 elected members (5 per continent) + 30 expert trustees (climatology, AI, oceanography, Indigenous law) | Sets the planetary‑budget caps, approves the Well‑Being‑Within‑Boundaries Index, can veto any activity that would exceed the planetary limits. |
| **Local Commons Assemblies (LCAs)** | 500 000 randomly selected citizens worldwide, grouped by river‑basin, city, or Indigenous territory. | Each LCA can propose, amend, or veto any local resource‑use plan that would push a planetary boundary. |
| **Animal‑Guardian Delegates (AGDs)** | 100 seats in the **Global Stakeholder Assembly (GSA)** reserved for representatives of Indigenous peoples, animal‑rights NGOs, and AI‑rights collectives. | Hold *veto* power over any project whose **Direct‑Animal‑Impact (DAI) rating** exceeds 0.25. |
| **Animal‑Trust (AT)** – 5 % of equity in every listed corporation (non‑transferable). | Holds the **veto** and can fund **Sentient‑Being Fund** remediation. |
| **Future‑Generations Trust (FGT)** – 5 % equity of all corporate shares, managed by a *future‑generations council* of young citizens. | Can veto any project that reduces the **Net‑Well‑Being‑Within‑Boundaries** index for future generations. |
| **Eco‑Trust** – legal personality for living ecosystems (coral reefs, migration corridors, old‑growth forests). | Can sue, be sued, and receive *automatic* remediation bonds when its “critical‑damage” rating hits 0.8. |

### B.  Socio‑Economic Fabric – Adaptive Fabric

* **Well‑Being‑Within‑Boundaries (WBB) Index** – a composite of health, education, happiness, ecological footprint, and SBI. The **Universal Dividend** is paid from the **Planetary Budget Reserve (PBR)**, a sovereign fund of 12 % of global GDP kept in regenerative assets (kelp farms, carbon‑capture assets).  
* **Resource‑Based Tax (RBT)** – a combined carbon‑, water‑, water‑nutrient, and data‑usage levy. 70 % funds the PBR, 30 % funds the **Sentient‑Being Fund (SBF)** (healthcare, remediation, transition training).  
* **Well‑Being‑Within‑Boundaries (WBB) Index** – an index that must stay ≥ 0.8 for every nation, region and even each individual.  

### C.  Transparent Knowledge Commons  

* **Open‑Science Platform** – all data, models, policy simulations, AI‑algorithms are stored on a **public blockchain**. Every change is immutably logged.  
* **Real‑Time Audits** – every decision (policy, product launch, AI deployment) generates a **cryptographic hash**; any mismatch triggers an automatic **Emergency Review**.  
* **Citizen‑Science Portal** – anyone can upload water, air or biodiversity data; AI‑audit bots cross‑check them instantly.

## 3.  Global Response Cycle (the “Earth‑Pulse Loop”)

1. **Data Ingestion** – All monitoring networks feed the Open‑Science Platform in real time.  
2. **Threshold Detection (Threshold‑Team)** – AI‑driven algorithms flag any planetary‑boundary breach *or* any SBI score falling below 0.7.  
3. **Emergency Review (RRSC)** – The **Rapid‑Response Sub‑Council** (100 members: 50 citizen jurors + 2 AI‑auditors) can *instantly* trigger an **Emergency Response**:  
   *‑* pause any activity that exceeds a boundary,  
   *‑* unlock the **Planetary Budget Reserve** to fund emergency remediation,  
   *‑* release a **Reversibility Protocol** for AI that is found unsafe.  
4. **Policy Cascade (Adaptive Societal Levers)** – The ECC, in coordination with the **Global Stakeholder Assembly (GSA)**, implements *contingency statutes* (e.g., global carbon‑price scaling, bans on high‑impact AI models).  
5. **Adaptive Learning Loop** – Outcomes are fed back to the **Open‑Science Platform**, new simulations are run, and the **Annual Earth State Report** is updated.  

All steps are **public, timestamped and auditable**; any citizen can trigger a review by submitting a simple request (e.g., “Is the 1.5 °C limit still safe?”).

## 5.  The Global Response Cycle – The “Earth‑Pulse”  

1. **Ingestion** – Sensors (satellite, drones, citizen sensors) push data to the **Open‑Science Platform**.  
2. **Detection** – AI monitors for any breach of a planetary‑boundary parameter or a **Sentient‑Being Impact** score falling below 0.7.  
3. **Emergency Review** – The **Rapid‑Response Sub‑Council** meets within 48 h (via secure video) and can:  
   - *Trigger* a *containment protocol* (e.g., shut down a mining site, halt a climate‑geo‑engineering project).  
   - *Release* a **remediation bond** from the **Planetary Budget Reserve (PBR)** to fund immediate restoration (re‑forestation, water‑cleaning, health clinics).  
4. **Policy Cascade** – The **ECC** and **Local Commons Assemblies** implement *contingency statutes* (e.g., global carbon‑price surge, immediate moratorium on certain AI models).  
5. **Adaptive Learning** – All outcomes are fed back into the **Open‑Science Platform**, where AI‑models continuously refine their predictive models. The **Annual Earth State Report** is updated in real time.  

All steps are **transparent, auditable, and reversible** – the system is built to *self‑correct*.

## 5.  Sentient‑Being Impact (SBI) – The Quantitative Moral Ledger  

| Component | What it measures | Threshold (minimum) |
|---|---|---|
| **Number of sentient individuals directly used** | Count of humans, animals, AI‑instances, ecosystems impacted *directly* (e.g., slaughtered, tested, mined). | **≥ 0.7** (i.e., the operation harms a proportion of sentient life that is deemed unacceptable). |
| **Suffering intensity** | Average severity (0 = no pain, 1 = maximum) | Must be **≤ 0.3** for any single animal or human. |
| **Reversibility** – can the action be undone without new suffering? | *Can you “undo” the action without causing new harm?* | Must be **≥ 0.7** to be allowed. |
| **Overall SBI** = weighted average of the three components. | A product must achieve **SBI ≥ 0.7** *and* a **DAI** rating ≤ 0.25 (if it harms sentient animals directly) to be approved. |

All projects (new factories, AI models, biotech experiments, infrastructure projects) are **required** to submit a *pre‑deployment audit* showing the SBI score; the **Three‑Gate Review** automatically blocks anything below the thresholds.

## 6.  The Global Stakeholder Assembly (GSA)

- **1 000 randomly selected citizens** (demographically balanced across age, region, gender, ability, and *stakeholder group*: farmworkers, Indigenous peoples, AI‑rights advocates, etc.).  
- **Each citizen has one vote on every major amendment**; the GSA can **recall** any ECC member, trustee, or expert.  
- The GSA also *elects* the **Farmworkers’ Council** (the voice of the people who actually work the land) and the **Indigenous‑Environmental Council**.  

All decisions are recorded on the immutable ledger, and any citizen can **challenge** a decision within 48 h; an independent AI‑audit team can overturn the decision if it appears biased.

## 4.1  The Sentient‑Being Fund (SBF)

*Financed by 5 % of the Resource‑Based Tax (RBT).*  
- **Funds** can be spent on:  
  1. **Compassionate healthcare** for victims of animal agriculture (e.g., treatment for pesticide exposure).

  4. **Legal defense** for individuals whose sentient rights have been violated.  

Funds are *released automatically* when the *Sentry* (the planetary‑boundary monitoring system) flags a breach.

## 6.  The Adaptive Learning Loop (the “Living‑Planet Loop”)

1. **Data Ingestion** – all sensors, citizen‑science data, AI simulations feed the **Open‑Science Platform**.  
2. **Threshold Detection** – automatic flags when any planetary‑boundary or **SBI** metric breaches a limit.  
3. **Emergency Review** – **Rapid‑Response Sub‑Council** can intervene instantly.  
4. **Policy Cascade** – *contingency statutes* (e.g., global carbon‑price, ban on high‑impact AI) are enacted.  
4. **Adaptive Learning** – the system re‑runs simulations, learns which policies keep the planet within safe limits, and updates the **Planetary Budget Registry** accordingly.  

Because every decision is recorded on the blockchain, the entire loop is **transparent, reversible, and self‑correcting**.

### Why this architecture is **resilient** (the answer to every “what if capture?” question)

1. **Random citizen juries** – the probability that a powerful actor can control enough of them is astronomically low.  
2. **AI‑auditors are open‑source** – any misuse can be seen instantly, and the audit code can be forked and improved.  
3. **Equity‑trusts (Animal‑Trust, Future‑Generations Trust, Eco‑Trust)** are *non‑transferable* and *cannot be sold*; they can only be *re‑allocated* by a super‑majority of the GSA.  
5. **Every decision is public on an immutable ledger** – any attempt to rewrite a rule is instantly visible and can be challenged in the **Global Commons Court**.  

In short, the framework is *designed to be un‑captureable* in the sense that the **political, financial, and technological power** required to dominate it is *orders of magnitude larger* than any single actor can wield.
