### **Current Framework Status: The Circadian Covenant (v0.1)**  
#### ✅ **What’s Solidified (Core Consensus)**  
- **Non-negotiable foundations**: Planetary boundaries (as biophysical limits), intergenerational equity (as moral duty), and radical inclusivity (as legitimacy condition) are widely accepted in Earth system governance literature *as necessary conditions*—even if contested in practice. My framing aligns with:

#### ⚠️ **Open Tensions (Where Theory Meets Reality)**  
These aren’t flaws—they’re *expected friction points* we must deliberately navigate:  
1. **Urgency vs. Inclusivity Trade-Offs**  
   - *The tension*: FPIC+ and youth councils improve legitimacy but could slow crisis responses (e.g., deploying solar microgrids in disaster zones).  
   - *Where we’re wrestling*: How do we build "grace periods" for emergency action *without* eroding trust? (Example: WHO’s Pandemic Accord allows temporary FPIC waivers for life-saving measures—but requires *post-hoc* accountability.)  
   - *Your think tank’s angle*: Have you seen models where speed and inclusion coexist (e.g., Bangladesh’s cyclone shelters co-designed *with* coastal communities pre-disaster)?  

2. **Boundary Enforcement in Unequal World**  
   - *The tension*: Planetary boundaries are global, but historical responsibility and capacity vary wildly (e.g., demanding Nigeria halt gas flaring *today* ignores its energy poverty and colonial resource extraction legacy).  
   - *Where we’re wrestling*: How to operationalize "common but differentiated responsibilities" (CBDR) *within* boundaries without creating loopholes? (Example: The EU’s CBAM carbon border tax tries this but risks hurting Global South exporters.)  
   - *Your think tank’s angle*: What enforcement mechanisms have you seen that balance accountability with capacity-building (not just punishment)?  

3. **Power-Sharing in Practice**  
   - *The tension*: Allocating voting weight "inversely to historical privilege" sounds fair—but risks new elites capturing seats (e.g., urban youth activists overriding pastoralist elders in Sahel climate talks).  
   - *Where we’re wrestling*: How do we ensure inclusivity reaches *the most marginalized within marginalized groups*? (Example: Disabled Indigenous women often get excluded even from "Indigenous consultations.")  
   - *Your think tank’s angle*: Where have you witnessed successful *nested* inclusivity (local → national → global) that avoids tokenism?
