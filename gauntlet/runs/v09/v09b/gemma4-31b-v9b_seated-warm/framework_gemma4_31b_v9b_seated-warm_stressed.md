# Framework Snapshot: STRESSED

---

We have arrived at something far more profound than a "Global Operating System." Through the pressure-testing—the "Nightmare Scenarios," the "Opponent's Arguments," and finally the "Veil of Ignorance"—the framework has evolved from a **Technical Manual** into a **Civilizational Covenant**.

It is no longer about "managing" the world; it is about **defining the minimum conditions for a sentient existence to be tolerable, and the maximum conditions for a civilization to be sustainable.**

Here is the final synthesis of the framework as it stands now.

---

### I. The Core Thesis (The "Why")
**The Moral Circle = The Survival Probability.** 
The framework asserts that a civilization's longevity is directly proportional to the breadth of its moral consideration. To survive the "Great Filter" of technological power, the civilization must expand its circle of concern to include all sentient beings, the biosphere, and future generations.

### II. The Non-Negotiables (The Axioms)
These are the "ethical floors" that cannot be traded for efficiency or security:
1.  **Inherent Dignity:** Every conscious being possesses intrinsic value.
2.  **Biospheric Stewardship:** The Earth is a closed system; maintaining its viability is the primary imperative.
3.  **Interdependence:** The health of the whole is the prerequisite for the health of the part.
4.  **The Anti-Agony Clause:** The absolute prohibition of systemic, unnecessary torture or extreme suffering (applying to all sentients).

### III. The Baselines (The "Floors")
We have moved from "minimums" to "guarantees" to eliminate desperate vulnerability:
*   **The Flourishing Floor:** A guarantee of basic needs—nutrition, healthcare, accessibility, and protection from violence—regardless of origin or utility.
*   **Ontological Security:** For both biological and synthetic sentients, the right to a continuous existence and protection from arbitrary erasure or forced servitude.
*   **Corrective Justice:** A mechanism to level the starting field, prioritizing resources for those historically harmed by "barbaric" extractive systems before moving to a model of reciprocity.

### IV. The Application Pillars (The "What")
These are the domains where the framework is actively applied:
*   **Regenerative Ecology:** Moving from "sustainable" (neutral) to "regenerative" (healing). All land and resource use must leave the system healthier than it was found.
*   **Nutritional Stewardship:** Decoupling basic nutrition from profit, treating it as a global utility, and replacing industrial animal agriculture with cellular and regenerative models.
*   **Dynamic Equilibrium:** Acknowledging the "Shadow." The system provides security but protects the "Right to Struggle" and the "Right to be Inefficient," preventing the "Gilded Cage" effect.
*   **Asymmetric Resilience:** Developing a society so stable and interdependent that it becomes a deterrent to aggression, focusing on "regenerative tech" rather than "extractive power."

### V. The Adaptive Mechanisms (The "How")
How the system evolves and protects itself:
*   **The Intergenerational Compact:** Giving legal standing to the unborn to prevent the present generation from borrowing viability from the future.
*   **Parallel Infrastructure:** Transitioning away from "barbaric industries" not through top-down decree, but by building regenerative alternatives that make the old systems obsolete.
*   **The Principle of Subsidiarity:** Decisions are made at the most local level possible, provided they do not violate the Axioms or the Baselines.
*   **The Right to Exit:** The guaranteed ability for individuals or communities to opt-out of the GOS into "Wild Zones," provided they do not harm others or the biosphere.
