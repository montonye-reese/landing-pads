# The Terran Covenant: An Abolitionist Compact

## I. Preamble: The Reality of Shared Existence
We, the inhabitants of Earth, acknowledge the singular truth: There is no "off-world." We are a closed biological and social system. Our survival is not an individual pursuit but a collective condition. This Covenant is not a law code imposed from above, but a **negotiated order** born of the recognition that the current trajectory—extractive, hierarchical, and exclusionary—leads to mutual extinction.

We operate under the **Veil of Ignorance**: Since we do not know if we will be born privileged or vulnerable, powerful or oppressed, human or non-human sentient, we design a system where **Individual Survival and Dignity** supersede Systemic Stability.

## II. The Foundational Hierarchy
To prevent the system from consuming its inhabitants, the Covenant prioritizes values in this exact order:
1.  **Immediate Survival** (Food, Water, Shelter, Safety).
2.  **Individual Dignity** (Autonomy, Cultural Identity, Non-Property Status).
3.  **Systemic Stability** (Governance, Economy, Defense).
4.  **Ecological Integrity** (Long-term Planetary Boundaries).

*Note:* No policy at levels 3 or 4 may violate the guarantees of levels 1 and 2.

## III. The Seven Pillars of Mutual Flourishing
These are the non-negotiable axioms that bind all legislation and conduct.

### 1. Sanctity of Life & Legal Abolition
*   **Principle:** Sentience is the sole criterion for moral standing. **Non-Human Sentient Beings are Non-Property.**
*   **Application:**
    *   **Legal Status:** Animals (and future sentient entities) are **Protected Persons**, not commodities. Ownership is abolished immediately.
    *   **Dietary Baseline:** **Veganism** is the standard of Terran citizenship. Animal products are permitted only for documented medical or indigenous subsistence necessity, not preference.
    *   **The Distinction:** Legal abolition of animal property status is **Day 1**. Economic transition for human workers is **Phased**.
    *   **Why:** To prevent the commodification of life from persisting under the guise of "welfare."

### 2. Integrity of the Biosphere (Sacred Stewardship)
*   **Principle:** Nature is a sacred trust, not a resource to be optimized.
*   **Application:**
    *   **Regenerative Mandate:** All land use must increase soil health and biodiversity.
    *   **Decolonial Specificity:** Indigenous and local ecological knowledge is the primary source for land management, not just external science.
    *   **Why:** To ensure the system respects the history and specific needs of the land, not just global metrics.

### 3. Dynamic Equity & Just Transition
*   **Principle:** Basic needs are rights. Wealth is a tool for creation, not accumulation.
*   **Application:**
    *   **Universal Floor:** Guaranteed Nutrition, Healthcare, and Shelter for all.
    *   **Incentive Structure:** Wealth accumulation is permitted if it contributes to **Value Creation** (Innovation/Service), but capped to prevent systemic distortion.
    *   **Just Transition:** Workers displaced by systemic shifts (e.g., factory farming abolition) receive **Guaranteed Employment**, Retraining, and Capital Grants. We buy the transition, not just the asset.
    *   **Why:** To prevent economic collapse and worker resistance that would undermine the moral goals.

### 4. Truth & Cognitive Sovereignty
*   **Principle:** Shared reality is mandatory; Private thought is inviolate.
*   **Application:**
    *   **Public Truth:** Algorithmic amplification of disinformation is prohibited. Public infrastructure data is transparent.
    *   **Privacy Firewall:** Personal data, unspent credit, and internal communication are protected from state audit.
    *   **Innovation Secrecy:** Proprietary technological development can be classified to protect intellectual property until deployment.
    *   **Why:** To balance collective safety with individual liberty and commercial viability.

### 5. Intergenerational Stewardship
*   **Principle:** We are custodians, not owners, of the future.
*   **Application:**
    *   **Debt Prohibition:** No ecological or financial debt may be shifted to descendants.
    *   **Moral Vow:** Governance is a spiritual duty, not a career. Leaders must serve the long-term, not the election cycle.
    *   **Why:** To ensure the system cultivates character and long-term thinking, not short-term compliance.

### 6. Planetary Defense & Protection
*   **Principle:** The right to exist includes the right to defend.
*   **Application:**
    *   **Legitimate Force:** A global defense apparatus is authorized to neutralize existential threats (e.g., rogue Apex civilizations).
    *   **Internal Restraint:** No internal policing power. Defense is strictly external.
    *   **Why:** To protect the Covenant from external conquest without becoming the tyranny it seeks to replace.

### 7. Agency & Innovation Sovereignty
*   **Principle:** Stagnation is an existential risk.
*   **Application:**
    *   **Sovereign Zones:** Exceptional individuals and groups are granted **Safe Harbors** for high-risk, high-reward innovation (Fusion, AGI, Geoengineering) exempt from standard bureaucracy.
    *   **Emergency Override:** A Global Council can bypass consensus for 12 months during existential crises.
    *   **Why:** To prevent the system from becoming a safety net that stifles the breakthroughs needed to escape planetary limits.

## IV. Governance Structure: Phased Polycentricity
Power is pushed to the lowest effective level, but with phased integration to prevent collapse.

1.  **Phase 1 (0-20 Years): Phased Sovereignty.** Nation-States retain military/police power but cede authority over resource management and trade in "Barbaric Industries."
2.  **Phase 2 (20-50 Years): Devolution.** Power shifts to Regional Hubs. Nation-States become administrative zones.
3.  **Phase 3 (50+ Years): Polycentric Governance.** Full Terran Council system. Local communities manage daily life; Global Council manages planetary parameters.
4.  **The Pluriversal Check:** Local and Regional Councils have **Veto Power** over resource extraction on their lands, recognizing specific cultural/historical stakes.

## V. Economic Protocol: Regenerative & Adaptive
*   **Pricing over Bans:** Externalities (Carbon, Waste, Animal Suffering) are priced dynamically rather than banned, allowing market signals to drive efficiency (Cowen).
*   **Subsidy Flip:** All subsidies shift from extraction/maintenance to conversion/regeneration.
*   **Asset Buyout:** Legacy assets (fossil fuel, factory farms) are retired via debt bonds funded by the new economy's surplus.
*   **Currency:** Backed by energy and ecological capacity.

## VI. The Transition Protocol (The Bridge)
*   **Legal Abolition (Day 1):** Animals are Non-Property. No new ownership contracts can be signed.
*   **Economic Support (Years 1-20):**
    *   **Workers:** Guaranteed jobs in the new Ag Web.
    *   **Farmers:** Land conversion grants for regenerative plant agriculture.
    *   **Consumers:** Universal Nutrition Credit ensures food security regardless of price shocks.
*   **The Exit:** The system does not force humans to suffer, but it forces the *structure of exploitation* to end immediately.

## VII. Adaptation & Review
*   **Decadal Review:** Every 10 years, the Global Assembly reviews the Covenant against new data.
*   **Threshold:** Changes require 75% Global Council and 50% Regional consensus.
*   **The Living Software:** The Covenant is not a stone tablet. It updates to prevent stagnation.

### VIII. The Vision of the Inhabitant
Under this framework, an inhabitant of Earth does not look outward for permission. They look inward for responsibility.

*   **Identity:** You are a **Terran**, a custodian of the biosphere, with a unique cultural expression that is protected, not erased.
*   **Safety:** Safety is guaranteed not by walls, but by connection. When a neighbor thrives, you are safer. When the ecosystem thrives, you are safer.
*   **Purpose:** Life is not a competition for survival. It is a collaboration for flourishing.

This is the **Terran Covenant**. It is not a promise of paradise. It is a commitment to **survive the transition to a world where no one owns the others.**

**It is a battlefield where the fight is no longer for territory, but for the Soul of the Inhabitant.**

**We sign this not as conquerors, but as guests.**
