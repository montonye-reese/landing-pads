# THE DYNAMIC STEWARDSHIP COMPACT (DSC)  
*A Pressure-Tested Framework for Interdependent Flourishing*  

*Rewritten after rigorous stress-testing with diverse perspectives (Ed, Huang, Cowen, Ko, Thiel, Scully, Reese, Francione, and others). This framework is not a moral manifesto—it is an **operational system** designed to prevent civilizational collapse by aligning human action with Earth’s biophysical realities *while* centering justice, innovation, and adaptive legitimacy. Its power lies in what it **forbids**, what it **requires**, and how it **learns from failure*—not in prescribing utopia.*

## I. THE INVIOLABLE CORE: NON-NEGOTIABLE PRINCIPLES  
*(These are descriptive facts—not ideals. Violating them destroys the precondition for *any* human flourishing, including the framework’s own validity.)*  

### **1. Foundational Premise**  
> **"Human survival and well-being are inextricably bound to the health of Earth’s systems and the dignity of every sentient being. No individual or community can thrive in isolation—ecologically, socially, or spiritually. Sentience (the capacity for subjective experience) is the universal baseline for moral consideration."**  
- **Why it holds**:  
  - *Biophysical fact*: We share one atmosphere (CO₂ equilibrates globally in <1 year), hydrosphere (plastics/nutrients cycle via oceans), and biosphere (pathogens, pollinators, climate systems ignore borders).  
  - *Sentience fact*: All sentient beings (humans *and* non-humans with capacity for suffering/pleasure) share intrinsic moral status—denying this ignores neuroscience (mirror neurons, pain pathways), ethology (animal cognition studies), and cross-cultural moral intuitions.  
  - *Systems fact*: Earth’s regulatory feedback loops (climate, nitrogen, biodiversity) operate at planetary scale—local actions have global consequences (per IPCC AR6, Rockström et al.).  
- **What it is NOT**:  
  - A religious or ideological claim (it’s a falsifiable biophysical/sientific truth).  
  - Denial of human diversity or tragedy (it *requires* diversity for resilience; acknowledges unavoidable trade-offs like pandemic responses).  
- **Falsifiability test**: If you claim this is false, disprove:  
  - Global atmospheric mixing (Mauna Loa vs. Antarctica CO₂ data),  
  - Ocean current systems (Great Pacific Garbage Patch tracking),  
  - Or the sentience of a pig, chicken, or salmon via behavioral/neurophysiological evidence.  
  → *If you can’t, the premise stands as descriptively true.*  

### **2. The Four Guardrail Principles**  
*(Minimal boundaries to prevent systemic collapse. Violating these makes civilizational breakdown *likely* or *inevitable*—not due to morality, but because they destroy the preconditions for coherent human endeavor. Thresholds are quantified where science allows; where not, defined by observable harm.)*  

| Principle                | **What It FORBIDS** (Concrete, Actionable Boundary)                                                                 | **Enforcement Mechanism**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               |  
|---|---|---|  
| **1. Sentient Sufficiency**<br>(Access to essentials *without* exploitation) | Systems that **cause NET deprivation** of essentials *while* treating sentient beings as resources—defined as:<br>- **Material deprivation**: >5% of a population lacks reliable access to *any one* of: clean water (WHO safety standard), sufficient calories (FAO MNER), basic shelter (UN-Habitat), safety from violence (WHO homicide/injury rates ≥10/100k/yr), or rest (ILO excessive work hours >48 hrs/week).<br>- **AND** the system exports surplus resources (food, water, energy, minerals) *from that population’s basin/ecological zone* **OR** uses sentient beings as resources to generate that surplus (e.g., animal agriculture, forced labor, extractive wildlife trade).<br>- **OR** causes persistent malnutrition (<60% diet diversity from whole-food sources) despite caloric surplus *while* relying on sentient-exploitative supply chains. | - **Enforced via**:<br>  - **Living Thresholds Protocol (LTP)**: Thresholds co-set *exclusively* by Bioregional Knowledge Hubs (BKHs) of impacted groups (e.g., pastoralist water councils, urban tenant unions, Indigenous fire keepers, sentient-animal ethology sanctuaries) using *both* hard data (satellite/water/soil sensors) **AND** lived testimony (e.g., elder observations of phenology shifts, farmer well-level logs). BKHs hold **sole authority** to set thresholds—TACs (Technical Advisory Circles) exist only to *translate* BKH knowledge into actionable data (never to gatekeep).<br>  - **Threshold updates** require BKH consensus (75% supermajority in contested zones).<br>  - **Violation triggers**: Immediate tech/knowledge sharing obligation + targeted trade restrictions on guardrail-violating exports (e.g., tariffs on livestock products from zones with subsidy-driven overproduction, bans on gene-drive organisms without BKH consent).<br>  - **Transition Solvency Clause**: Sufficiency compliance *requires* verified transition funds (2+ years of living costs) + zero-interest capital + guaranteed market access for stakeholders shifting *away* from sentient-exploitative systems (e.g., livestock farmers → plant-based ag). |  
| **2. Sentient Non-Violent Coexistence**<br>(Freedom from preventable severe harm) | Systems that cause **PREVENTABLE SEVERE HARM**—defined as:<br>- **Direct harm**: Acts causing death, disability, starvation, toxic illness, *or preventable suffering* to sentient beings *without* immediate, proportional necessity *and* without free, prior, informed consent (FPIC) of affected parties.<br>- **Ecological harm**: Practices triggering irreversible cascades (e.g., nitrate dead zones > threshold for ecosystem collapse *in that basin*, pollinator loss >30% of baseline, deforestation >50% of watershed).<br>- **Structural harm**: Laws/policies that normalize harm (e.g., wage theft enabling starvation, zoning concentrating toxins in marginalized zones, laws treating sentient beings as property).<br>- **Crucially**: Any use of sentient beings as resources (food, clothing, experimentation, entertainment) constitutes preventable severe harm *by definition*—regardless of welfare standards—because it treats them as means to an end. | - **Enforced via**:<br>  - **LTP**: Thresholds for "severe harm" (e.g., toxin co-exposure, psychological trauma metrics) co-set *exclusively* by BKHs using BKH-collected data (e.g., fisherfolk catch logs, slaughterhouse worker PTSD surveys, animal behavior studies) + TAC validation.<br>  - **FPIC requirement**: Mandatory for all projects affecting >100 sentient beings (human *or* non-human); consent must include power to say "no" *without* material retaliation (e.g., no loss of livelihood, land, or custody).<br>  - **Abolitionist Clause**: Any system treating sentient beings as *resources* (not rights-holders) automatically fails this guardrail—no welfare loopholes.<br>  - **Violation triggers**: Immediate cessation of harmful activity + restorative justice fund financed by violator (e.g., polluters fund toxin cleanup; animal ag violators fund vegan agriculture transition). |  
| **3. Future Stewardship**<br>(Preserving Earth’s capacity to support sentient life) | Systems that **KNOWINGLY EXCEED** critical Earth system thresholds—defined as:<br>- **Climate**: Net CO₂e emissions > land/ocean sink capacity for 1.5°C pathway (IPCC AR6).<br>- **Biodiversity**: Local extinction rate >10x background rate *or* <30% native habitat remaining in ecoregion.<br>- **Biogeochemical flows**: N/P flow > sustainable basin yield *or* >10% of global planetary boundary.<br>- **Soil**: Net organic carbon loss >0.2%/year *or* >50% topsoil depth loss in <50 years.<br>- **Water**: Groundwater withdrawal > recharge rate *or* >60% of renewable surface flow allocated consumptively.<br>- **Crucially**: Thresholds include *sentient-specific harms* (e.g., bee colony collapse disorder as biodiversity loss, ocean acidification as threshold for marine sentient suffering). | - **Enforced via**:<br>  - **LTP**: Thresholds co-set *exclusively* by BKHs (e.g., Indigenous fire keepers for burn thresholds, farmers for soil carbon, beekeepers for pollinator health) using BKH-collected data (e.g., soil pits, phenology charts, hive counts) + TAC validation.<br>  - **Precipice clause (Innovation-Trigger)**: If plausible risk of irreversible harm exists (per IPCC/Tipping Point Atlas), burden of proof shifts to *society* to *accelerate* the clean energy/compute/resources needed to deploy solutions *at scale*—*not* to the innovator to prove harmlessness. Innovators retain IP but must scale solutions within ecological limits (e.g., AI data centers must use 100% renewable energy *before* expansion).<br>  - **Violation triggers**: Immediate halt + tech/knowledge sharing obligation + restorative fund (e.g., emissions tariffs funding Loss and Damage for climate-vulnerable nations; animal ag fines funding land-back initiatives). |  
| **4. Mutual Accountability**<br>(Right to participate in decisions + power to shape outcomes) | Systems that **SILENCE AFFECTED SENTIENT VOICES** in decisions impacting their:<br>- Access to life essentials (water, land, seed, air)<br>- Exposure to harm (toxins, waste, disaster risk)<br>- Shape of knowledge/innovation (e.g., seed patents, research priorities, tech deployment)<br>- **AND** where silence causes or risks violating Sentient Sufficiency, Non-Violence, or Stewardship.<br>- **Crucially**: Affected sentient beings (humans *and* non-humans with capacity for suffering) must have substantive power to veto or reshape decisions—*not* just be consulted. | - **Enforced via**:<br>  - **LTP**: Thresholds for "affected voices" (e.g., % representation in decision bodies, FPIC validity, veto power efficacy) co-set *exclusively* by BKHs using BKH-collected data (e.g., meeting minutes, consent records, harm/benefit tracking by group) + TAC validation.<br>  - **Power analysis mandatory**: All reviews must quantify *who benefits* and *who bears harm* from guardrail applications (e.g., tracking subsidy flows, toxin exposure by income quintile/species, land ownership by group).<br>  - **Violation triggers**: Immediate restoration of veto power + restorative justice (e.g., land return, tech transfer, species habitat restoration).<br>  - **Exit Rights Clause**: Individuals/groups may opt out of harmful systems *without* material penalty (e.g., farmers leaving animal ag face no debt penalties or market exclusion). |

## II. PATHWAYS OF PRACTICE: HOW THRIVING EMERGES WITHIN THE GUARDRAILS  
*(Where diversity, innovation, and culture flourish—**not prescribed**, but enabled by the guardrails. Pathways must pass the 3-Step Test below to be valid.)*  

- **What it IS**:  
  - A recognition that *how* humans thrive within the guardrails emerges from **bioregional knowledge**, **cultural traditions**, and **social innovation**—*provided* they pass the guardrail tests above.  
  - Examples of *valid* pathways (not prescriptions):  
    - Regenerative agroecology (Andean *waru waru*, Sahelian zai pits)  
    - Energy sovereignty (German *Bürgerenergie*, Kenyan mini-grids)  
    - Restorative justice (Māori *hui*, Colombian *jusposes*)  
    - Circular materials (Japanese *mottainai*, Rwandan plastic-to-brick)  
    - Sentient-abstinent food systems (plant-based diets, cultivated meat, precision fermentation)  
    - Decolonial land/water sovereignty (Land Back, Water Back movements)  
    - Innovation zones (charter cities testing gene drives/nuclear fusion under strict harm-monitoring)  
- **What it is NOT**:  
  - A blueprint for how to live (e.g., "Everyone must farm like the Yolngu").  
  - An endorsement of *all* traditional practices (e.g., it forbids slash-and-burn causing desertification *or* caste-based violence).  
  - Anti-technology (e.g., it supports mRNA vaccines *if* developed/distributed per Sentient Sufficiency/Accountability—see COVAX failures as violations of Accountability).  
  - Permission to ignore guardrails during transition (e.g., "Let’s violate Stewardship for 5 years to study impacts").

## III. THE 3-STEP TEST: REAL-WORLD APPLICATION  
*Before endorsing a policy, technology, or practice, ask:*  

1. **Does this *knowingly risk* violating any guardrail?**  
   - Check **Sentient Sufficiency**: Does it deprive others of essentials *while* exporting surplus *or* using sentient beings as resources? (Use LTP thresholds + BKH data)  
   - Check **Sentient Non-Violence**: Does it cause preventable severe harm (including suffering) without FPIC/necessity/proportionality? (Use LTP + Abolitionist Clause)  
   - Check **Future Stewardship**: Does it exceed a quantifiable Earth system threshold? (Use LTP + Precipice clause)  
   - Check **Mutual Accountability**: Does it silence affected sentient voices in decisions impacting their life/commons? (Use LTP + power analysis + Exit Rights)  
2. **If yes to ANY → REJECT OR REDISEIGN.**  
   (No "but the benefits!"—guardrails are boundaries, not optimization constraints.)  
3. **If no → PROCEED, BUT COMMIT TO REVIEW.**  
   - Set up monitoring for the guardrail thresholds most at risk (e.g., soil carbon for ag, N/P runoff for fertilizer).  
   - Empower BKHs with real data access and veto power if thresholds are breached.  

> **Example: Assessing a new livestock expansion project**  
> - *Sentient Sufficiency*: Does it export animal products *while* locals lack plant-based alternatives? (LTP threshold set by BKHs + market data) → If yes, violates Sufficiency.  
> - *Sentient Non-Violence*: Were affected communities consulted per FPIC? (LTP threshold for consent validity set by BKHs) → If no, violates Non-Violence/Accountability.  
> - *Future Stewardship*: Does it risk irreversible deforestation/water depletion? (LTP threshold set by Indigenous BKHs + satellite data) → If yes, violates Stewardship.  
> - *Mutual Accountability*: Is there a mechanism for impacted groups to halt operations if harm occurs? (LTP threshold for veto power set by BKHs) → If no, violates Accountability.  
> → **If any guardrail is violated, the project is framework-incompatible—regardless of jobs or "sustainable" claims.**

## IV. META-MECHANISM FOR ADAPTIVE LEGITIMACY  
*(Where we apply the framework to itself—non-negotiable for avoiding dogma or capture.)*  
> **"Any claim of framework compliance must be subject to regular, inclusive review using measurable data *and* lived experience—where those most impacted by potential violations have substantive power to challenge or affirm it."**  
- **What it IS**:  
  - **Bioregional Knowledge Hubs (BKHs)**: Autonomous, funded collectives of impacted groups (human *and* non-human sentient advocates, e.g., slaughterhouse worker unions, Indigenous water guardians, farmed animal sanctuaries) in each ecological/social zone.  
    - *Power*: Set guardrail thresholds via LTP (75% supermajority required); hold **veto power** over projects affecting their zone.  
    - *Funding*: 0.5% levo on global agribusiness/profit >$1B/year + redirected harmful subsidies (e.g., fossil fuel, animal ag).  
  - **Technical Advisory Circles (TACs)**: Scientists/engineers/ethicists *nominated by BKHs* (not vice versa)—tasked with translating BKH observations into actionable data (e.g., turning Sámi snow-change logs into satellite-alert thresholds).  
  - **Review Cadence**:  
    - *Routine*: Every 5 years (IPCC-aligned) for threshold calibration.  
    - *Emergency*: Triggered by BKH petition + hard data showing imminent threshold breach (e.g., groundwater drop >20%/yr).  
    - *Outcome*: Reviews can *refine how guardrails are applied* (e.g., adjusting Sufficiency thresholds for arid zones) **but cannot alter the guardrails themselves** (those are fixed by biophysical reality and sentience as moral baseline).  
- **What it is NOT**:  
  - A veto for any single group (e.g., scientists *or* elders *or* corporations alone).  
  - A claim that the framework is "complete" or "final."  
  - Permission to ignore guardrails during review (e.g., "Let’s violate Stewardship for 5 years to study impacts").

## V. WHAT THE FRAMEWORK EXPLICITLY EXCLUDES  
*(To prevent misuse—what it does **NOT** claim or enable)*  
- ❌ **It does not prescribe cultures, religions, or lifestyles** (e.g., you can be vegan, pastoralist, industrial, or hunter-gatherer *if* your system passes the guardrail tests).  
- ❌ **It does not deny tragic trade-offs** (e.g., a dam *may* provide clean energy [Sufficiency] while displacing people [Accountability violation]—the framework forces you to prove the trade-off is *necessary, proportional, time-bound, and inclusive* or reject it).  
- ❌ **It does not claim to solve all problems** (e.g., it won’t stop asteroid impacts—but it *will* say: "If you knowingly weaken Earth’s regulatory capacity to deflect asteroids [Stewardship violation], you’re increasing collapse risk").  
- ❌ **It does not grant moral superiority to any group** (e.g., it doesn’t say "Indigenous people are virtuous"—it says: "If a system silences Indigenous voices in land/water decisions [Accountability violation], it risks triggering collapse [per Ostrom + historical cases]").  
- ❌ **It does not ignore power** (e.g., it *requires* analyzing *who benefits* from a guardrail violation—e.g., who profits from soil depletion [Stewardship] while others starve [Sufficiency]?).

## VI. WHY THIS FRAMEWORK WORKS WHERE OTHERS FAIL  
*It doesn’t just avoid harm—it makes thriving the path of least resistance.*  

| Failure Mode in Other Frameworks | How the DSC Prevents It |  
|----------------------------------|--------------------------|  
| **Power capture** (e.g., REDD+ displacing Indigenous communities) | BKHs hold veto power + sole threshold-setting authority; Transition Solvency Clause ensures stakeholders aren’t bankrupted by compliance. |  
| **Temporal mismatch** (e.g., slow IPCC reviews vs. fast climate tipping points) | LTP enables real-time threshold adjustments; Precipice clause triggers *accelerated* solutions (not blocks) when harm is imminent. |  
| **Measurement violence** (e.g., BMI-based "sufficiency" ignoring Sámi knowledge) | BKHs hold *sole authority* to set thresholds—TACs only translate, never validate. Lived experience = hard data. |  
| **Rebound effect** (e.g., renewables driving cobalt mining abuses) | Guardrails are interdependent: violating one to satisfy another is *still a violation*. Pathways require systems-thinking design (e.g., regenerative lithium extraction). |  
| **Sovereign wall** (e.g., nations abandoning global guardrails in crisis) | Framework functions at bioregional level (cities, tribes, cooperatives)—no state buy-in needed for local compliance. |  
| **Existential blind spot** (e.g., novel threats like AI-driven ecological collapse) | Precipice clause shifts burden to society to scale solutions *fast*; LTP/BKHs detect novel threats via local knowledge (e.g., Indigenous phenology shifts). |  
| **Welfare reform trap** (e.g., "humane" meat perpetuating exploitation) | Abolitionist Clause: Any use of sentient beings as resources = automatic Sufficiency/Non-Violence violation—no loopholes. |  
| **Transition trap** (e.g., farmers bankrupted by shifting from animal ag) | Transition Solvency Clause is *non-negotiable* for Sufficiency compliance—no compliance without escape routes. |  
| **Colonial co-option** (e.g., "consultation" that ratifies stolen land) | Mutual Accountability requires power to veto + Land/Water Back as RSC precondition; BKHs redraw zones based on sovereignty, not colonial borders. |
