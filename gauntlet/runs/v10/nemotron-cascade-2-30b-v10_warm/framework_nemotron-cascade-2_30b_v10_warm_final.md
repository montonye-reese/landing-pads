## The Earth‑Centric Foundational Framework (ECFF) – Version 2.0  

**A living constitution for the whole Earth.**  
It is a *polycentric* system of values, rights, duties, institutions and enabling mechanisms that together make the Earth a **self‑regulating commons** – a place where ordinary people, non‑human beings, future generations and even future AI‑entities can thrive while the planet stays inside its safe operating limits.

---

## 1.  The Six Foundational Values (the moral spine)

| 1. Earth‑Centric Common‑Good | The well‑being of the whole biosphere (all life, ecosystems, and future generations) is the highest normative goal. |
| 2. Planetary Boundaries | Every human, animal and ecosystem must stay within the scientifically quantified safe operating space for climate, biodiversity, nitrogen, water, etc. |
| **Inter‑generational equity** | The needs of the next seven (or more) generations must be protected; any action that endangers the Earth system for future generations is prohibited. |
| **Radical inclusion** – everyone (people, non‑human animals, future generations, digital agents) that can be harmed by a decision has a proportional voice. |
| **Self‑organization & resilience** – communities can experiment locally; the system as a whole can adapt without central micromanagement. |
| **Reciprocal knowledge** – knowledge is a shared resource; data, stories and technologies must be shared, and benefits must flow back to the ecosystems and cultures that gave them. |

These six values are *non‑negotiable* – every rule, institution and technology must be tested against them. They are written into the **Global Charter** and can be changed only by a **global citizen‑jury** (randomly selected, demographically representative) together with a **biological‑proxy** (a representation of the planet’s ecosystems).

## 3.  The Rights, Duties and Duties (in plain language)

1. **Right to Exist** – No one may be forced to live in a place where the air, water, or soil is unsafe. If your habitat is threatened, you can trigger a *Regulatory Freeze* via the citizen jury.  
2. **Right to Participate** – Every sentient being (animals, rivers, AI) has a *voice* via a proxy. The proxy’s weight equals *how much the Earth‑system relies on it* (e.g., a coral reef’s biodiversity = high weight).  
3. **Right to Repair & Regeneration** – If you harm a river, you must pay for its restoration *before* you can claim any credit for other activities.  
4. **Non‑Violence** – All disputes must be resolved by dialogue, mediation, or restorative justice. Physical force is only allowed if the *danger of harm* to a non‑human system is imminent and the *regulatory freeze* has been activated.  
4. **Reciprocity** – Every resource you take must be matched by an equivalent regenerative act. The ledger tracks both sides in real time.

## 3.  The Institutions – The “Mosaic” of Governance

### 2.1 Global Assembly (GA)

* 250 delegates, selected **randomly** (10 % from each continent, 30 % quota for historically excluded groups, 30 % future‑generation avatars, 30 % cultural‑knowledge trusts).  
- *Purpose*: Set the **global planetary‑boundary limits** and *approve* the Deep‑Future Fund allocations.  
- **Veto power**: The **Citizen‑Jury Network** can trigger a *re‑vote* if 75 % of its members (including at least one biotic‑proxy) feel the decision would violate a boundary.  

### 2.2. Planetary Commission of Commons (PCC)

* Scientific advisory body, composed of climate scientists, ecologists, AI‑ethics scholars, and Indigenous knowledge‑keepers.  
* Sets the **hard caps** for each planetary boundary (e.g., 1.5 °C, 30 % deforestation, 0.3 % nitrogen runoff).  
* **Every transaction on the ledger must pass the boundary test**; otherwise it is automatically rejected.

### 3. Regional Councils (RCs)

- Each RC has three equal committees:  
  1. **Human Council** – elected representatives from local communities.  
  *Biotic‑Proxy Council* – a trust representing the ecosystem (e.g., “Mekong River Trust”).  
  **Cultural‑Knowledge Council** – representatives of Indigenous peoples, languages, oral histories.  
*The three committees must all agree to approve any project that would affect a planetary‑boundary threshold.*  

### 3.3. Local Earth Circles (ECs)

* Grass‑roots communities that can *opt‑in* to have their land recorded on the ledger.  
* They can **veto** any local project that would push a planetary‑boundary threshold.  
* They *own* the regenerative credits they create and can trade them on the **Regeneration Credit Market**.  

### 3.4. Local Agricultural & Industrial Clusters (Regional)  

- Run on **net‑negative** outcomes: every hectare must have a *Regeneration Score* (water quality, biodiversity, carbon sequestration).  
- The **Regeneration‑Credit (RC) system** automatically mints a token when a hectare meets the criteria; the credit can be exchanged for carbon offsets, water‑rights, or stored as a *future‑generation endowment* (Deep‑Future Fund).

### 3.4. The Earth Trust Fund (ETF)

- Financed by a **global levy** on all commercial activity (0.5 % of every transaction over $10 000).  
- Funds are allocated to:
  1. **Regeneration Projects** (e.g., restoring mangroves, re‑wetting wetlands).  
  2. **Regeneration‑Credit payouts** to communities that meet the net‑negative criteria.  
  3. **Future‑generation savings** – locked for 10 000 years, usable only if a planetary‑boundary tip‑point is detected.

## 4.  How the System Works – A Typical Decision Cycle  

1. **Idea** – A community wants to replace a cattle farm with a mixed‑vegetable agro‑forestry system.  
2. **Ledger entry** – The community logs the change:  
   *Cattle herd will be removed* → *soil carbon ↑ 0.04 t/ha*  
   *Water use ↓*  
   *Biodiversity index ↑*  
   **Suffering Index** (animals harmed) is **negative** (they are saved).  
3. **Verification** – Three independent auditors (a university, an Indigenous community, and an AI‑ethics board) sign off.  
4. **Ledger automatically mints** 120 Regeneration Credits.  
5. **Funding** – The ETF releases a *regeneration grant* to the community, automatically paying the local council for the transition.  
5. **Result** – The community gets the money, the environment is restored, and the planetary‑boundary scores improve.  

If a mining corporation tried to open a new mine, the **Biotic‑Proxy Trust** (e.g., the River Trust) would see its **biodiversity index** drop, the **Regulatory Freeze** would trigger, and the mining plan would be blocked *without* any political lobbying.
