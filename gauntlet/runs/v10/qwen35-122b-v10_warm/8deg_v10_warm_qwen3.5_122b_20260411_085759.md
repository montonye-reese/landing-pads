If we redesign the global agricultural system from scratch, guided by the **Planetary Accord**, we move away from the model of **Industrial Extraction** (maximizing yield at any cost) to **Regenerative Nurture** (optimizing health for soil, farmer, consumer, and climate).

### II. Structural Pillars of the New System

#### 1. Production: Polycentric & Regenerative
*   **Local Hubs:** The primary unit of production is the **Regional Food Zone** (100–300km radius). 80% of calories are produced within this zone to ensure freshness, reduce transport emissions, and build community resilience.
*   **Soil as Infrastructure:** Soil health is treated as critical infrastructure, equal to roads or bridges. Governments invest in soil regeneration (cover cropping, no-till, agroforestry) rather than just yield subsidies.
*   **Biodiversity Mandate:** Monocultures are restricted to specific "seed banks" for emergency resilience. Commercial farms must maintain 15–20% of their land as non-productive habitat (pollinator corridors, wetlands) to support ecosystem services.
*   **Livestock Integration:** Animals are reintegrated into farming systems as nutrient cyclers (manure for soil) rather than isolated in feedlots. Meat is treated as a seasonal resource or processed product, not a daily staple, aligning consumption with ecological capacity.

#### 2. Equity: Land & Labor Justice
*   **Land Stewardship Rights:** Land is held in **Community Trusts**. Ownership confers the right to steward, not the right to exploit. Speculation on agricultural land is prohibited.

#### 3. Distribution: Closed-Loop Logistics
*   **True Cost Pricing:** Food prices reflect environmental and social costs. A cheap hamburger becomes expensive because it accounts for water usage, soil erosion, and healthcare costs associated with processed diets.
