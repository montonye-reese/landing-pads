## What Should We Do Next?

1. **Pick a Scope** – Do you want to ground the framework in a:
   * *Geographic pilot* (e.g., a city or watershed)?
   * *Sector* (e.g., agriculture, renewable energy, digital services)?
   * *Stakeholder group* (e.g., Indigenous peoples, youth climate movements)?

2. **Select Priorities** – From the pillar list, choose the **2–3 pillars you want to operationalize first** (e.g., Ecological + Social Economic + Adaptation).  

3. **Define Success Metrics** – What early‑stage indicators will tell you the approach is working? (e.g., reduction in local carbon emissions, increase in community‑owned renewable assets, launch of an impact‑audit dashboard.)

4. **Create a “Starter Kit”** – A handful of concrete deliverables such as:  
   * A one‑page **Vision‑Statement** with your chosen tagline.  
   * A **Principles Checklist** that each participating organization signs.  
   * A **Simple Dashboard Template** (maybe a Google Sheet) that tracks the metrics you selected.  
   * A **Pilot Action List** (e.g., “set up a neighborhood micro‑grid pilot” or “implement a circular‑product pilot for X industry”).

5. **Schedule a Co‑Design Session** – Gather interested parties (local government, civil‑society reps, businesses, researchers) and walk them through the **Vision → Pillars → Implementation Mechanics** flow. Capture feedback and lock in a concrete next‑step timeline.
