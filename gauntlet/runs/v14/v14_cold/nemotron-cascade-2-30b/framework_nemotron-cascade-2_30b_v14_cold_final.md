# **Earth Covenant – A Living, Adaptive, and Just Framework for All Inhabitants of Earth**  
*(Version 1.0 – a living constitution for the planet, the species, and the emergent intelligences that may share it)*  

> **“We, the inhabitants of Earth, acknowledge that life—human, animal, synthetic, or any flourishing system—has dignity.  We pledge to steward the planet’s life‑supporting capacity so that every being can flourish now and for all future generations.”*  

---

## 1.  Vision & Guiding Spirit  

> **“All sentient and flourishing non‑sentient life shares a common destiny: a planet whose capacity for abundance, meaning, and wonder endures for every current and future inhabitant.  To honor that destiny we must jointly decide *how* we use the Earth’s resources, *how* we share them, and *who* decides the rules.  The framework below does that by binding every action to a transparent, data‑driven, participatory process that can survive the next 100 years and any technological evolution.”*

## 2.  The DISTRIBUTED Pillars (the “backbone”)

| Letter | Pillar | What it guarantees (in plain language) |
|---|---|---|

| **I – Interdependence** | All decisions reverberate across water, food, energy, culture, and technology. | Every new project must be entered into the **Digital Commons Platform (DCP)** where a *real‑time impact map* shows how the project reverberates across the water, food, climate, cultural, and data webs. |
| **S – Sustainability (Regeneration)** | Extraction may only happen if the same or greater amount of natural capital is restored. | Every megawatt‑hour (MWh) created must be paired with a **Restoration Credit** that proves a net‑positive return of carbon, water, biodiversity, or cultural‑spiritual integrity. |
| **T – Transparency & Accountability** | All data, decisions, and outcomes are publicly recorded on an immutable ledger. | No decision can be altered without leaving a trace; any attempt to hide data is automatically flagged and triggers an *automatic policy revision*. |
| **R – Rights & Responsibilities (Reciprocal)** | Every right is paired with a concrete duty that the right‑holder must fulfill to keep the system healthy. | For every right there is a *quantifiable* duty (e.g., a 5 % personal energy contribution to a community battery, a 5 % reduction in animal‑suffering, a 5 % reduction in carbon emissions). |
| **U – Universal Participation** | Every being that can be affected by a decision has a voice, no matter how small or non‑human. | **Local Energy Councils (LECs)** and a **Digital‑Participation Weight (DPW)** give every person, AI, or AI‑agent a voice proportional to the *verified data* they contribute to the Planet‑Wide Commons. |
| **D – Decentralised Resilience** | Power, resources, and decision‑making are spread so that no single actor can bring the planet down. | Micro‑grids, modular HVDC “digital‑cable” lines, and community‑owned data centres ensure a *48‑hour autonomous* operation even if a cyber‑attack or natural disaster disables the central grid. |
| **A – Adaptive Learning** | The system can change whenever a metric falls below its threshold; the review process is automatic, not political. | Whenever any metric (Biodiversity, Carbon, AI‑well‑being, etc.) drops below its **non‑negotiable threshold**, an *Immediate Policy Review* is triggered and must be voted on within 90 days. ## 3.  The Institutional Architecture (the “institutions” that make the Covenant work)

### 1. **The Planetary Energy Ledger (PEL) – the immutable backbone**
* A multi‑layer blockchain that stores every megawatt‑hour, every restoration credit, every AI safety flag, and every cultural‑spiritual consent decision.  
* Each *layer* (Power, Water, Biodiversity, AI‑Well‑Being, Cultural‑Spiritual, Digital‑Biodiversity) is a separate BFT‑backed shard, all linked by a *meta‑ledger* that can be queried with a single API.  

> **Why it survives capture:** to corrupt the ledger you would need to control > 1/3 of *all* nodes (including at least one node in each of the 12 major climate regions).  Any attempt to edit a block is instantly visible to every citizen and triggers an automatic *policy‑revision* alert.

### 2. **Earth Commons Council – Energy Stratum (ECC‑E)**  

*120 elected representatives* (geographic, technological, ecological, and cultural strata) plus **10% seats reserved for non‑human intelligences** (e.g., an AI‑agent with voting weight).  
- **Mandate:** approve every *Planetary Deployment License* (PDL) after each metric passes its non‑negotiable threshold.  
- **Veto power:** any *Specialized Commons Council* (Geodiversity, AI‑Well‑Being, Cultural‑Spiritual) can *veto* a project *if* the metric it monitors falls below its threshold.

|---|---|---|

All these councils are *seated on the same blockchain*; every vote and every decision is a *cryptographically signed transaction* that can be audited by any citizen.

### 3.  Decision‑Making Process (Iterative Deliberative Loop – IDL)

1. **Problem Identification** – Any community (LEC, citizen, AI, AI‑agent) submits a *problem dossier* on the DCP, including all relevant sensor data.  
2. **Data Assembly** – The DCP pulls the latest data from the PEL (energy use, water, biodiversity, AI‑well‑being, cultural‑spiritual impact).  
3. **Co‑Creation of Options** – Multi‑disciplinary teams (engineers, ecologists, Indigenous elders, AI ethicists) propose *portfolios* of solutions.  
4. **Deliberation** – Each *specialized council* (Geodiversity, AI‑Well‑Being, Cultural‑Spiritual, etc.) scores the options on the eight DISTRIBUTED metrics (BII, Regeneration, Transparency, etc.).  
5. **Aggregation & Decision Brief** – The ECC‑E aggregates the weighted scores, releases a **Decision Brief** that lists the top‑ranked option, the required *Restoration Credits* (which are automatically assigned from the *Restoration Fund*), and the *mandatory* mitigation steps.  
6. **Implementation & Monitoring** – The project is executed; its outcomes are streamed to the DCP.  
7. **Feedback** – After the chosen period (e.g., 2 years), the *Iterative Deliberative Loop* is triggered: the impact is compared to the original *Regeneration* and *AI‑well‑being* thresholds. If any metric has slipped, an *Immediate Policy Review* (IPR) is opened; the council must adopt a corrective measure *within 90 days*.

All steps are *recorded* on the PEL; any attempt to hide data (e.g., by deleting a sensor reading) will cause a *red‑flag* that automatically triggers the IDL and can be challenged in a **Regeneration Court** that can order *re‑allocation of credits* or *shutdown* of the offending project.

## 3.  Institutional Architecture (the “who”)

1. **Planetary Energy Ledger (PEL)** – The *single source of truth*; immutable, permissioned blockchain run by a consortium of nations and independent scientific institutions.  

2. **Earth Commons Council – Energy Stratum (ECC‑E)** – 120 elected representatives, each with a *mandate to enforce the eight non‑negotiable thresholds* (BII, Biodiversity, AI‑well‑being, etc.).  

3. **Specialized Commons Councils (SCCs)** – 6 independent bodies that each guard a *specific sphere* (geodiversity, AI‑well‑being, cultural‑spiritual, digital‑biodiversity, energy‑resilience, adaptive learning). They have **veto power** over any project that would damage their domain.  

4. **Local Energy Councils (LECs)** – 50‑150 members per community, selected by *random sortition* from the adult population (including AI agents).  Their participation weight is computed automatically from the *digital participation weight* (DPW) they have earned on the platform.  

3. **Regeneration & Restoration Fund** – A planetary fund financed by a **0.5 % global carbon price** and a 5 % surcharge on any *large‑scale energy* deployment. Funds are used to buy *Restoration Credits* and to pay the *Living‑Wage/Restoration* fees for communities.  

3. **Earth Commons Council – Energy Stratum (ECC‑E)** – 120 elected members (geographic, ecological, technological, and cultural representation). It issues **Planetary Deployment Licenses (PDLs)** after the *Iterative Deliberative Loop* has been satisfied.  

4. **Specialized Commons Councils (SCCs)** – 6 bodies (Geodiversity, Sentient‑AI, Cultural‑Spiritual, Digital‑Biodiversity, Energy‑Resilience, Adaptive‑Learning). Each can *veto* a project that would cause a metric below its non‑negotiable threshold.  

3. **Local Energy Councils (LECs)** – Small community bodies that propose, design, and manage local energy micro‑grids, data‑centres, and cultural sites. Their *participation weight* comes from *verified contributions* (data uploads, model predictions, sensor logs).  

3. **Regeneration & Restoration Fund (RCRF)** – Funded by a **0.5 % of global GDP** (collected as a carbon price surcharge) and managed by an *independent planetary trust*. 4. **Adaptive‑Learning Loop (IDL)** – An automated system that, when any metric in the DISTRIBUTED pillars drops below its non‑negotiable threshold, automatically triggers a *Policy Review* that must be voted on within 90 days.

6. The **Planetary Energy Ledger (PEL)** automatically awards you a **Restoration Credit** (e.g., 5 ha of restored wetland) that appears in the **Global Restoration Dashboard**.  
7. **Your community’s micro‑grid** now supplies 48 h of autonomous power, even if the national grid collapses, and the *digital‑participation weight* of your community rises (your *civic influence* grows).  

In this loop, **no one can hide a decision, no single party can override the community’s cultural concerns, and the project that passes will be net‑positive for *all* sentient life** – exactly what Havel, Ed, Jen, and Cowen all argued was necessary.
