# THE PLANETARY COVENANT
### A Framework for Planetary Civilization and Biospheric Stewardship
**Version 3.0 (Justice, Resilience, & Conviction Edition)**
**Status:** Codified Draft for Global Consensus

**PREAMBLE**
We, the Inhabitants of Earth, recognizing our interdependence as part of a single biosphere and acknowledging the profound vulnerability of each individual, hereby establish this living covenant. This document supersedes all prior charters that prioritize extraction over regeneration, power over dignity, or compliance over conviction.

This framework is designed behind a **"Veil of Ignorance"**: it protects the rights of the incarcerated, the farmer, the migrant, the worker, and the dissenter, just as it protects the future and the planet. We bind ourselves to the truth that **survival without justice is fragility, and justice without survival is impossible.**

---

### ARTICLE I: FUNDAMENTAL LAWS
*These principles are non-derogable. They prioritize the well-being of sentient life and the integrity of the biosphere.*

**Section 1: The Law of Ecological Integrity & Sentience**
1.1. The Earth is the primary sovereign of this planet. Human and artificial systems are subordinate to the biosphere.
1.2. Economic activity is bounded by **Objective Planetary Boundaries** (CO2 limits, water usage, biodiversity indices).
1.3. Non-human life holds an inherent right to existence. Ecosystems are recognized as legal persons.
1.4. **Abolition of Unnecessary Harm:** No economic activity may be permitted that involves the infliction of unnecessary suffering on sentient beings (including farmed animals and research subjects).
1.5. **No State may externalize risk onto the biosphere or its inhabitants.**

**Section 2: The Law of Dignity, Conviction, & Redemption**
2.1. Every sentient being possesses an inalienable right to safety, physical sustenance, and cognitive autonomy.
2.2. **Right to Life:** Guaranteed access to clean air, potable water, nutrient-rich food, and shelter is a human right, regardless of state membership or political alignment.
2.3. **Right to Conscience:** No individual or community shall be punished for refusing to participate in a system they believe violates their moral conscience.
2.4. **Right to Redemption:** No citizen shall be permanently excluded from the Covenant due to a past violation, provided restitution is made. The worst thing one has done does not define them forever.
2.5. **Restorative Justice:** The primary objective of consequence is repair, rehabilitation, and reintegration, not retribution.

**Section 3: The Law of Intergenerational Equity**
3.1. The rights of the unborn are constitutionally protected.
3.2. No policy adopted today may compromise the resource availability or environmental safety of populations in the year 2100.
3.3. Long-term ecological risk must be weighted equally with immediate economic gain.

---

### ARTICLE II: RIGHTS OF THE INHABITANT
*All beings subject to the jurisdiction of this Covenant are granted the following rights.*

**Section 1: Fundamental Provisions**
1.1. **Right to Growth:** Guaranteed access to quality education, healthcare, and the tools for personal development.
1.2. **Right to Connection:** Protection of community, privacy, and freedom from unauthorized surveillance.
1.3. **Right to Safe Labor:** Any work performed on this planet must occur in conditions safe for the human body and mind.
1.4. **Right to Collective Bargaining:** Workers possess the right to organize and bargain collectively to determine working conditions, wages, and safety standards.

**Section 2: Prohibitions**
2.1. There shall be no slavery, debt bondage, or forced labor.
2.2. There shall be no discrimination based on origin, species, cognitive architecture, genetic code, or past criminal record.
2.3. There shall be no weaponization of data, biology, or environment against an inhabitant.
2.4. **No Punishment via Starvation:** No sanction against a state or entity may deprive its population of basic sustenance (food, water, medicine).

---

### ARTICLE III: GOVERNANCE AND STRUCTURE
*Decision-making shall follow the Principle of Subsidiarity, balanced with Human Rights Oversight and Citizen Oversight.*

**Section 1: The Structure of Stewardship**
1.1. **Local Councils:** Handle day-to-day governance within bioregional limits.
1.2. **The People’s Chamber:** A legislative body composed of **directly elected representatives from the global population** (not nations). It holds veto power on human rights issues and transition protections.
1.3. **The Council of Stewards:** Composed of state representatives and scientific advisors. It holds **"Conditional Access"** power: non-compliant regions are isolated from the *Global Commons* (shared infrastructure, energy, data) but not attacked.
1.4. **The Citizen Audit:** A randomly selected body of citizens (sortition) empowered to audit the Council of Stewards and the Enforcement Authority for corruption, conflict of interest, or tyranny.

**Section 2: The Judicial Tribunal**
2.1. An independent tribunal shall adjudicate disputes involving the rights of nature, corporations, or states.
2.2. Decisions are binding and prioritized by the metric of **Net Biological and Social Flourishing**, but must include **Narrative Justice** (hearing the human story).
2.3. **Strict Liability:** Entities causing harm via "Sandbox" experimentation are personally liable for all damages, with no cap on compensation.
2.4. **Human-in-the-Loop:** No algorithmic decision affecting human dignity, liberty, or life can be automated without a **human appeal process**.

**Section 3: Transparency**
3.1. All data regarding resource use, pollution, and governance is public property.
3.2. Algorithms making decisions affecting public welfare must be open-source and auditable.
3.3. **Power Audit:** The financial flows of all Councils and Authorities must be public, including donor lists and lobbying records, to prevent capture by special interests.

---

### ARTICLE IV: ECONOMIC AND TECHNOLOGICAL OPERATING SYSTEM

**Section 1: The Economy**
1.1. **Subsidy Reallocation:** Legacy industries contributing to existential risk shall see their subsidies redirected toward the transition (plant-based agriculture, renewable energy, regenerative tech), not simply taxed.
1.2. **Universal Basic Infrastructure:** Guaranteed for all, including digital access, healthcare, and transit.
1.3. **Wealth-Driven Funding:** The cost of transition shall be funded by **wealth redistribution** and **extraction taxes**, not by raising the cost of living for the poor.
1.4. **Innovation Safe Harbors:** Entrepreneurs who take risks aligned with the Covenant are protected from punitive sanctions if failure is technical rather than malicious.
1.5. Automation and AI are utilized to reduce mandatory human labor, allowing for creative and care-based pursuits.

**Section 2: Technology and Intelligence**
2.1. **The Alignment Mandate:** Artificial Intelligence systems must be designed with "Human and Planetary Well-being" as a hard-coded constraint.
2.2. **Ethical Sandbox Zones:** Specific high-risk research areas are designated for rapid experimentation. These are strictly geo-located to prevent environmental leakage.
2.3. **Cognitive Liberty:** No system may manipulate human thought or emotion at a mass scale without explicit, revocable consent.
2.4. **No-Experimentation Risk:** Technology tested in Sandbox Zones cannot be deployed until its safety margins for the general population are verified.

### ARTICLE V: IMPLEMENTATION AND EVOLUTION

**Section 1: The Dynamic Constitution**
1.1. This document is not static. It requires revision every **five (5) years** or upon the discovery of a new existential threat.
1.2. A **Sunset Clause** applies to all Articles of Law: no regulation remains valid without active re-ratification by the populace after ten years.

**Section 2: Transition Protocol**
2.1. **Parallel Transition Economies:** Legacy systems are subsidized to transition over a 50-year window. **No farmer, worker, or community shall be abandoned.**
2.2. **Decarceration & Reentry:** Incarcerated individuals are to be prioritized for reentry into the new economy. Prison systems must transition to restorative models.
2.3. **Establishment of a Transition Authority:** To manage the dissolution of non-compliant institutions and ensure **infrastructure readiness** (processing plants, supply chains) before phase-outs begin.

**Section 3: Enforcement and Sovereignty**
3.1. **Conditional Access:** Violations of Covenant result in exclusion from the Global Commons.
3.2. **Humanitarian Safeguards:** If a state opts out of the Covenant, the Global Council is mandated to ensure the **population's** basic needs are met, preventing suffering due to state isolation.
3.3. **Non-Compliant Sovereignty:** States may choose to leave the Covenant, but they bear full responsibility for their own life-support systems and must not externalize risk (pollution, war) onto compliant regions. **No punitive sanctions on the population for the choice to leave.**
3.4. **Right of Conscientious Objection:** Individuals or communities may opt out of specific requirements they deem morally wrong (e.g., animal agriculture, specific labor conditions) provided they do not harm others and accept the economic consequences (isolation from specific benefits).
