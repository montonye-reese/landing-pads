# THE PLANETARY COVENANT
### A Framework for Planetary Civilization and Biospheric Stewardship
**Version 2.5 (Justice & Resilience Edition)**
**Status:** Codified Draft for Global Consensus

**PREAMBLE**
We, the Inhabitants of Earth, recognizing our interdependence as part of a single biosphere and acknowledging the profound vulnerability of each individual to the failures of state and system, hereby establish this living covenant. This document supersedes all prior charters that prioritize extraction over regeneration, or power over dignity. We bind ourselves to the truth that survival without justice is fragility, and that justice without survival is impossible. This framework is designed behind a "Veil of Ignorance," ensuring that no citizen is permitted to suffer due to political circumstance, market forces, or systemic transition.

---

### ARTICLE I: FUNDAMENTAL LAWS
*These principles are non-derogable and shall serve as the supreme authority for all policy, economic, and social frameworks.*

**Section 1: The Law of Ecological Integrity (Hard Metrics)**
1.1. The Earth is the primary sovereign of this planet. Human and artificial systems are subordinate to the biosphere.
1.2. Economic activity is bounded by **Objective Planetary Boundaries** (CO2 limits, water usage, biodiversity indices).
1.3. Non-human life holds an inherent right to existence. Ecosystems are recognized as legal persons with standing in judicial review.
1.4. **No state or entity may externalize risk onto the biosphere.**

**Section 2: The Law of Dignity and Agency (Universal Floor)**
2.1. Every sentient being possesses an inalienable right to safety, physical sustenance, and cognitive autonomy.
2.2. **Right to Existence:** Guaranteed access to clean air, potable water, nutrient-rich food, and sustainable shelter is a human right, regardless of state membership or political alignment.
2.3. **Right to Transition:** Citizens displaced by necessary economic shifts are entitled to income support, retraining, and housing before their role is eliminated.
2.4. Justice is restorative. The primary objective is repair and rehabilitation.

**Section 3: The Law of Intergenerational Equity**
3.1. The rights of the unborn are constitutionally protected.
3.2. No policy adopted today may compromise the resource availability or environmental safety of populations in the year 2100.
3.3. Long-term ecological risk must be weighted equally with immediate economic gain.

---

### ARTICLE II: RIGHTS OF THE INHABITANT
*All beings subject to the jurisdiction of this Covenant are granted the following rights.*

**Section 1: Fundamental Provisions**
1.1. **Right to Life:** Guaranteed access to clean air, potable water, nutrient-rich food, and sustainable shelter.
1.2. **Right to Growth:** Guaranteed access to quality education, healthcare, and the tools for personal development.
1.3. **Right to Connection:** Protection of community, privacy, and freedom from unauthorized surveillance.
1.4. **Right to Safety from Risk:** No individual shall be collateral damage of experimental technology or economic transition.

**Section 2: Prohibitions**
2.1. There shall be no slavery, debt bondage, or forced labor.
2.2. There shall be no discrimination based on origin, species, cognitive architecture, or genetic code.
2.3. There shall be no weaponization of data, biology, or environment against an inhabitant.

---

### ARTICLE III: GOVERNANCE AND STRUCTURE
*Decision-making shall follow the Principle of Subsidiarity, balanced with Human Rights Oversight.*

**Section 1: The Structure of Stewardship**
1.1. **Local Councils:** Handle day-to-day governance within bioregional limits.
1.2. **The People’s Chamber:** A legislative body composed of **directly elected representatives from the global population** (not nations). It holds veto power on human rights issues and transition protections.
1.3. **The Council of Stewards:** Composed of state representatives and scientific advisors. It holds "Conditional Access" power: non-compliant regions are isolated from the Global Commons (shared infrastructure, energy, data) but not attacked.

**Section 2: The Judicial Tribunal**
2.1. An independent tribunal shall adjudicate disputes involving the rights of nature, corporations, or states.
2.2. Decisions are binding and prioritized by the metric of **Net Biological and Social Flourishing**.
2.3. **Strict Liability:** Entities causing harm via "Sandbox" experimentation are personally liable for all damages, with no cap on compensation.

**Section 3: Transparency**
3.1. All data regarding resource use, pollution, and governance is public property.
3.2. Algorithms making decisions affecting public welfare must be open-source and auditable.

---

### ARTICLE IV: ECONOMIC AND TECHNOLOGICAL OPERATING SYSTEM

**Section 1: The Economy**
1.1. Value is decoupled from infinite extraction. Economic activity is bounded by planetary carrying capacity.
1.2. **Universal Basic Infrastructure** is guaranteed for all, including digital access, healthcare, and transit.
1.3. **Transition Tax:** Legacy industries contributing to existential risk are taxed to fund retraining and new infrastructure, not to punish workers.
1.4. Automation and AI are utilized to reduce mandatory human labor, allowing for creative and care-based pursuits.

**Section 2: Technology and Intelligence**
2.1. **The Alignment Mandate:** Artificial Intelligence systems must be designed with "Human and Planetary Well-being" as a hard-coded constraint.
2.2. **Ethical Sandbox Zones:** Specific high-risk research areas are designated for rapid experimentation. These are strictly geo-located to prevent environmental leakage.
2.3. **Cognitive Liberty:** No system may manipulate human thought or emotion at a mass scale without explicit, revocable consent.
2.4. **No-Experimentation Risk:** Technology tested in Sandbox Zones cannot be deployed until its safety margins for the general population are verified.

---

### ARTICLE V: IMPLEMENTATION AND EVOLUTION

**Section 1: The Dynamic Constitution**
1.1. This document is not static. It requires revision every **five (5) years** or upon the discovery of a new existential threat.
1.2. A **Sunset Clause** applies to all Articles of Law: no regulation remains valid without active re-ratification by the populace after ten years.

**Section 2: Transition Protocol**
2.1. **Parallel Transition Economies:** Legacy systems are taxed heavily and priced out gradually over a 50-year window, not banned immediately.
2.2. Establishment of a Transition Authority to manage the dissolution of non-compliant institutions.

**Section 3: Enforcement and Sovereignty**
3.1. **Conditional Access:** Violations of Covenant results in exclusion from the Global Commons (shared resources, data, energy grids).
3.2. **Humanitarian Safeguards:** If a state opts out of the Covenant, the Global Council is mandated to ensure the **population's** basic needs are met, preventing suffering due to state isolation.
3.3. **Non-Compliant Sovereignty:** States may choose to leave the Covenant, but they bear full responsibility for their own life-support systems and must not externalize risk (pollution, war) onto compliant regions.

---

### ARTICLE VI: RATIFICATION AND SIGNATURE
*This text enters into force upon the ratification of 51% of Earth's sovereign entities and the Global Population, following a 24-month implementation period for preparation.*
