# THE PLANETARY COVENANT
### A Framework for Planetary Civilization and Biospheric Stewardship
**Version 1.0 (Draft for Global Consensus)**

**PREAMBLE**
We, the Inhabitants of Earth, recognizing our interdependence as part of a single biosphere, and acknowledging our shared responsibility for future generations, hereby establish this living covenant. This document supersedes all prior charters, laws, and systems that prioritize extraction over regeneration or division over unity. We bind ourselves to the truth that the survival of the human species is contingent upon the health of the planetary life-support system.

---

### ARTICLE I: FUNDAMENTAL LAWS
*These principles are non-derogable and shall serve as the supreme authority for all policy, economic, and social frameworks.*

**Section 1: The Law of Ecological Integrity**
1.1. The Earth is the primary sovereign of this planet. Human and artificial systems are subordinate to the biosphere.
1.2. No economic, political, or military activity shall be permitted if it exceeds the regenerative limits of the planetary boundaries (atmosphere, water, soil, and biodiversity).
1.3. Non-human life holds an inherent right to existence. Ecosystems are recognized as legal persons with standing in judicial review.

**Section 2: The Law of Dignity and Agency**
2.1. Every sentient being possesses an inalienable right to safety, physical sustenance, and cognitive autonomy.
2.2. No entity shall be instrumentalized solely for economic gain. Human labor and attention are protected from algorithmic exploitation and coercive control.
2.3. Justice is restorative. The primary objective of consequence is repair and rehabilitation, not retribution.

**Section 3: The Law of Intergenerational Equity**
3.1. The rights of the unborn are constitutionally protected.
3.2. No policy adopted today may compromise the resource availability or environmental safety of populations in the year 2100.
3.3. Long-term ecological risk must be weighted equally with immediate economic gain.

---

### ARTICLE II: RIGHTS OF THE INHABITANT
*All beings subject to the jurisdiction of this Covenant are granted the following rights.*

**Section 1: Fundamental Provisions**
1.1. **Right to Life:** Guaranteed access to clean air, potable water, nutrient-rich food, and sustainable shelter.
1.2. **Right to Growth:** Guaranteed access to quality education, healthcare, and the tools for personal development.
1.3. **Right to Connection:** Protection of community, privacy, and freedom from unauthorized surveillance.

**Section 2: Prohibitions**
2.1. There shall be no slavery, debt bondage, or forced labor.
2.2. There shall be no discrimination based on origin, species, cognitive architecture, or genetic code.
2.3. There shall be no weaponization of data, biology, or environment against an inhabitant.

---

### ARTICLE III: GOVERNANCE AND STRUCTURE
*Decision-making shall follow the Principle of Subsidiarity, balanced with Global Oversight.*

**Section 1: The Structure of Stewardship**
1.1. **Local Councils:** Handle day-to-day governance, culture, and resource distribution within bioregional limits.
1.2. **Continental Synods:** Manage regional infrastructure, migration corridors, and water treaties.
1.3. **The Global Stewards Council:** Composed of elected representatives and scientific advisors. It holds veto power over any local or continental action that violates Article I (Fundamental Laws).

**Section 2: The Judicial Tribunal**
2.1. An independent tribunal shall adjudicate disputes involving the rights of nature, corporations, or states.
2.2. Decisions are binding and prioritized by the metric of **Net Biological and Social Flourishing**.
2.3. The Tribunal may issue injunctions against industries or practices deemed existential threats.

**Section 3: Transparency**
3.1. All data regarding resource use, pollution, and governance is public property.
3.2. Algorithms making decisions affecting public welfare must be open-source and auditable.

---

### ARTICLE IV: ECONOMIC AND TECHNOLOGICAL OPERATING SYSTEM

**Section 1: The Economy**
1.1. Value is decoupled from infinite extraction. Economic activity is bounded by planetary carrying capacity.
1.2. Universal Basic Infrastructure is guaranteed for all, including digital access, healthcare, and transit.
1.3. Automation and AI are utilized to reduce mandatory human labor, allowing for creative and care-based pursuits.

**Section 2: Technology and Intelligence**
2.1. **The Alignment Mandate:** Artificial Intelligence systems must be designed with "Human and Planetary Well-being" as a hard-coded constraint, not a variable.
2.2. **Open Knowledge:** Medical, scientific, and agricultural data are public goods. Intellectual property rights that prevent access to survival-critical technologies are suspended.
2.3. **Cognitive Liberty:** No system may manipulate human thought or emotion at a mass scale without explicit, revocable consent.

---

### ARTICLE V: IMPLEMENTATION AND EVOLUTION

**Section 1: The Dynamic Constitution**
1.1. This document is not static. It requires revision every **five (5) years** or upon the discovery of a new existential threat.
1.2. A **Sunset Clause** applies to all Articles of Law: no regulation remains valid without active re-ratification by the populace after ten years.

**Section 2: Transition Protocol**
2.1. Immediate cessation of all activities classified as "Existential Risk."
2.2. Phased implementation of economic restructuring, allowing time for adaptation.
2.3. Establishment of a Transition Authority to manage the dissolution of non-compliant institutions.

**Section 3: Enforcement**
3.1. Violation of this Covenant results in sanctions ranging from resource redistribution to dissolution of corporate charters.
3.2. States that fail to uphold this Covenant may be subject to global trade embargoes or protective intervention.

---

### ARTICLE VI: RATIFICATION AND SIGNATURE
*This text enters into force upon the ratification of 51% of Earth's sovereign entities and the Global Population, following a 24-month implementation period for preparation.*

**IN WITNESS WHEREOF**, the inhabitants, corporations, and nations of Earth are called upon to uphold the balance of the roots and the fruit.
