### **The Covenant of Interbeing**  
*A foundational framework for all Earth’s inhabitants*  

**CORE INSIGHT**  
> *All life arises from and depends on the same Earth systems. Human well-being is inseparable from the health of the biosphere, the stability of communities, and the recognition of our mutual dependence. To harm the web is to harm oneself; to nurture it is to nurture all.*  

**THREE GUIDING PRINCIPLES**  
*(Non-negotiable, universal, and actionable)*  

1. **REGENERATIVE RECIPROCITY**  
   Give back more than you take—toward people, ecosystems, and future generations.  
   *Action: Design economies, communities, and personal choices to restore (not deplete) natural and social capital.*  

2. **RADICAL INCLUSION OF DIGNITY**  
   Every human being possesses inherent worth, irrespective of identity, status, or utility—and this demands active protection of their needs and voice.  
   *Action: Guarantee thresholds for dignified living (water, food, safety, healthcare, education, cultural expression, participation); redistribute power to correct historical harms.*  

3. **COURAGEOUS HUMILITY IN STEWARDSHIP**  
   We are temporary caretakers, not owners—of Earth, knowledge, and each other. Act with prudence, admit uncertainty, and prioritize healing over control.  
   *Action: Apply the Precautionary Principle; center impacted voices in decisions; embrace "both/and" thinking (e.g., justice + limits, tradition + innovation).*  

**WHAT THIS FRAMEWORK REJECTS**  
- False dichotomies (e.g., "people vs. planet," "growth vs. survival")  
- Extractive morality (ethics divorced from relational impact)  
- Static perfection (the belief that utopia must be achieved before action begins)  
- Cultural imperialism (imposing universal principles without local expression)  

---

### Notes on Usage  
- **Universality**: Principles apply to all humans, communities, nations, and species (via stewardship).  
- **Flexibility**: *Expression* adapts to culture, ecology, and context (e.g., reciprocity in Indigenous land practices vs. urban cooperative economies).  
- **Measurement**: Progress is tracked by reduced suffering, increased resilience (social/ecological), and alignment with interbeing (not GDP or consumption).  
- **Non-Dogma**: This is a living covenant—open to refinement through practice, dialogue, and emerging evidence, but grounded in the three principles above.  

> *This framework does not demand agreement—it invites enactment. Begin where you are: in your choices, your community, your sphere of influence. The web remembers how to heal when we remember our place in it.*
