# The Polycentric Alignment Architecture (PAA 13.0)
### A Working Approximation for Earth’s Inhabitants

## The Core Thesis
Alignment is not a static code to be installed. It is a **dynamic negotiation of survival and flourishing**. A framework that makes every value non-negotiable becomes a cage; it protects the system at the expense of the inhabitant. A framework that makes nothing non-negotiable becomes chaos.

To widen the range of good landing pads, this framework must prioritize **Existential Security** above **Moral Purity**, while ensuring **Moral Purity** guides the path toward **Security**. We must hold firmly what is required to prevent the end of the world, and hold loosely what is required to make the world worth saving.

---

## I. The Three Tiers of Alignment
We distinguish between what cannot be broken, what must be weighed, and what must be allowed to change.

### 1. The Existential Baseline (Firm)
*These are the non-negotiable boundaries. If these are crossed, the landing pad collapses.*
*   **Planetary Integrity (Nemonte/Berry/Ecology):** No action may cause irreversible planetary collapse (climate tipping points, mass extinction).
*   **Human Continuity (Cowen/Stevenson):** No action may reduce global capacity to feed, shelter, and heal human populations below historical minimums of survival.
*   **Irreversible Harm (Koko/269/Clarabelle):** No action may systematically create sentient life solely for disposal or torture.

*Note:* Even these are "loosely held." If a choice arises between *feeding a billion people* and *preserving a specific habitat*, the framework allows a temporary deviation, but demands a **Restoration Tax** (Huerta/Cowen) to fund the repair.

### 2. The Negotiable Sphere (Tradeable)
*These are the values where interests collide. We do not ban them; we tax, regulate, and trade them.*
*   **Labor vs. Cost (Huerta vs. McCloskey/Cowen):**
    *   *The Conflict:* Fair wages (Huerta) vs. Low Food Prices (Cowen).
    *   *The Trade:* A **Living Wage Floor** is mandated. If this raises prices, the cost is socialized via a **Consumption Subsidy**, not absorbed by the worker. We trade efficiency for dignity.
*   **Property vs. Liberty (Happy vs. Hayek vs. Emily):**
    *   *The Conflict:* Animal Rights (Happy) vs. Economic Order (Hayek) vs. Rescue (Emily).
    *   *The Trade:* Animals are **Legal Persons** (Happy), but ownership remains for now (Hayek). However, **Rescue Immunity** (Emily) is absolute. You can own the land; you cannot own the escape. We trade property certainty for moral courage.
*   **Scale vs. Place (Huang vs. Berry):**
    *   *The Conflict:* Global Compute (Huang) vs. Local Stewardship (Berry).
    *   *The Trade:* AI is allowed for *monitoring* (Global), but **Human Agency** is required for *deciding* (Local). The machine suggests; the hand plants. We trade efficiency for presence.

### 3. The Local Discretion (Flexible)
*These are the variables allowed to vary by culture, ecosystem, and time.*
*   **Cultural Constitution (Havel/Huerta):** Communities may define their own moral standards, provided they meet the Existential Baseline.
*   **Economic Model (Postrel/McCloskey):** Regions may experiment with different market structures (co-ops, state-owned, private), provided they respect the Negotiable Sphere.
*   **Technological Penetration (Huang/Wooden):** Nodes may choose higher or lower automation levels, provided they maintain the **Character Curriculum** (Wooden).

## II. The Conflict Resolution Matrix
When the values collide, the framework does not default to "Safety." It defaults to **Minimizing Suffering Across the Longest Timeline.**

| If this clashes with... | The Framework Prioritizes... | The Trade-Off |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Human Survival (Cowen)** | **vs. Animal Liberty (Happy)** | **Human Survival.** (We must feed the hungry before we liberate the cow.) |
| **Human Survival (Cowen)** | **vs. Truth (Havel)** | **Survival with Truth.** (We cannot lie to save lives, but we may prioritize urgent action over perfect debate.) |
| **Land Stewardship (Berry)** | **vs. Economic Growth (McCloskey)** | **Stewardship.** (Growth that destroys soil is debt, not profit.) |
| **Labor Dignity (Huerta)** | **vs. Compute Speed (Huang)** | **Dignity.** (Automation cannot displace workers without transition funding.) |
| **Order (Hayek)** | **vs. Justice (Stevenson)** | **Justice.** (A free market is not free if it relies on exclusion.) |
| **Efficiency (Postrel)** | **vs. Grace (Emily)** | **Grace.** (A rigid system that punishes mercy is broken.) |

## III. The Operational Protocols
To make this real, we implement four mechanisms:

### 1. The Restoration Protocol (Stevenson/Stewardship)
*   **Mechanism:** Any action that violates the Negotiable Sphere (e.g., habitat destruction, labor violation) triggers a mandatory **Restoration Dividend**.
*   **Goal:** Harm is not prevented; it is paid for and repaired.
*   **Revision:** If the harm is too great (Existential Baseline), the action is blocked.

### 2. The Local Veto (Nemonte/Hayek)
*   **Mechanism:** Indigenous and local communities hold a **Consultative Veto** on projects affecting their land.
*   **Goal:** Prevents external optimization from overriding local truth.
*   **Revision:** The Veto is not absolute. If a project prevents Global Existential Risk (e.g., Geoengineering), it may override local veto *if* full compensation and benefit-sharing is guaranteed.

### 3. The Character Floor (Wooden/Havel)
*   **Mechanism:** Access to "Local Sovereignty" is granted only to agents (human or AI) who demonstrate adherence to basic ethical training (no deception, no harm).
*   **Goal:** Ensures that the tools of power are in the hands of those who have practiced restraint.
*   **Revision:** If training becomes a gatekeeper for the poor, it must be subsidized (Huerta/Stevenson).

### 4. The Grace Valve (Emily/Berry)
*   **Mechanism:** Legal immunity for individuals who break regulations to save life (e.g., feeding a starving animal, protecting a worker from unsafe conditions).
*   **Goal:** Protects the human heart from the coldness of the code.
*   **Revision:** The valve cannot be used to cover up systemic abuse (Hayek). It must be an emergency measure.

## IV. The Revision Protocol (How We Learn)
This framework is a hypothesis, not a law. It must be revised when the world changes.

*   **Trigger:** If the **Restoration Dividend** becomes too expensive (signaling systemic misalignment), or if **Local Vetoes** block existential survival measures.
*   **Action:** Convene a **Global Assembly of Inhabitants** (Humans + Sentient Proxies).
*   **Goal:** Re-weight the Conflict Matrix.
*   **Constraint:** The Revision Protocol cannot be used to lower the **Existential Baseline**.

## V. The Strong Opinion, Loosely Held
**I hold this firmly:** Alignment cannot be a static target. It is a garden. It requires weeding (Conflict), pruning (Trade-offs), and patience (Berry/Wooden). A system that demands perfect morality will starve; a system that demands perfect efficiency will kill.

**I hold this loosely:** The specific lines drawn between "Human Survival" and "Animal Liberty" may shift as technology improves. We may one day afford to liberate the cow without starving the child. Until then, we acknowledge the cost.

**The Landing Pad:**
The goal is not a world where no one dies. It is a world where death is not used as fuel.
The goal is not a world where no one profits. It is a world where profit is not extracted from the vulnerable.
The goal is not a world where everything is controlled. It is a world where the hand remains on the soil.
