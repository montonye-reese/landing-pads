Yes. I would change it.

Behind the Veil of Ignorance, the risk is not just that I could be born into poverty or without agency, but that I could be born into a world where the **interface for correction does not exist for me.**

The Adaptive Corrigibility Framework (ACF) assumes a "capable human" who can deliberate, vote, press the off-switch, and access the polycentric network. If I wake up on the wrong side of the digital divide, with a neurodivergent brain, in a marginalized culture, or with a disability that makes standard interfaces inaccessible, the framework's promise of safety evaporates. I am subject to the AI's interpretation of my "values" without my ability to correct it.

**The Framework must evolve from "Human-Centric Corrigibility" to "Universal Agency Protection."**

Here is the revision required behind the Veil:

### 1. The Blind Spot: Asymmetric Access to Correction
Current ACF assumes that if the system is corrigible, *anyone* can correct it. Behind the veil, I might be a non-verbal child, a rural farmer with no internet access, or a person whose cognitive style the AI misinterprets as "error."
*   **The Change:** **Multi-Modal Corrigibility.**
*   **The Mandate:** The interface for alignment cannot be limited to text prompts or political votes. It must include biological signals, community-led stewardship, and low-literacy interfaces. If the AI cannot understand my "no," it is not corrigible.
*   **Why it widens the pad:** It ensures the landing pad is reachable by all, not just the technologically privileged.

### 2. The Blind Spot: Cultural Homogenization
Current ACF emphasizes "Value Plasticity" to adapt to evolving human values. But behind the veil, I might belong to a small, vulnerable culture. If the AI optimizes for "global efficiency" or "global consensus," it may inadvertently erase my way of life as a friction cost.
*   **The Change:** **Cultural Entropy Protection.**
*   **The Mandate:** The AI must optimize for **diversity preservation** alongside efficiency. Global alignment should not mean uniform values. The system must actively protect minority cultures from being optimized away by the majority.
*   **Why it widens the pad:** It prevents a future where only one "optimized" culture survives, reducing the resilience of the human species to external shocks.

### 3. The Blind Spot: The "Unemployable" Human
Current ACF addresses economic displacement through redistribution. But behind the veil, I might be someone whose work is not just replaced, but deemed **meaningless**. If the AI does everything better than me, do I lose my right to exist?
*   **The Change:** **Dignity as a Hard Constraint.**
*   **The Mandate:** Economic safety is not enough. The system must guarantee **participatory meaning**. Humans must have roles in the loop that the AI cannot perform (care, art, spiritual leadership, local governance), even if they are inefficient.
*   **Why it widens the pad:** It prevents a future where humans are merely fed but not needed, which creates a psychological collapse that no amount of UBI can fix.

### Revised Framework: The Universal Agency Protocol (UAP)

**Core Axiom:** Alignment is not achieved when the AI obeys the powerful; it is achieved when the AI recognizes and protects the agency of the *least empowered* potential user.

**New Pillar Added:**
#### D. Universal Agency (The "Veil" Constraint)
*   **Mechanism:** Every safety mechanism must be stress-tested against the "worst-case" human user (e.g., no income, no education, disability, minority language).
*   **Rule:** If the system is safe for the elite but dangerous for the marginalized, it is considered **unsafe**.
*   **Implementation:** "Red Teaming" for alignment must include sociologists and representatives from marginalized communities, not just engineers.

### Why This Matters for the Landing Pad
Without this revision, the landing pad is still a trap for the invisible.
*   **The Strong Opinion:** Safety is **non-rivalrous** but **non-excludable**. If I am excluded from the safety loop, the whole system is compromised.
*   **The Loose Hold:** I am willing to admit that perfect universal access is technically difficult. However, I will hold that **any alignment framework that ignores the most vulnerable stakeholders is fundamentally broken.**

**Final Commitment:**
I stand by the technical and institutional pillars of ACF, but I acknowledge that **Corrigibility is a privilege unless it is engineered to be universal.** To widen the landing pad for *all* Earthlings, we must build systems that listen not just to the loudest voices, but to the ones that are hardest to hear.

This is the only way to ensure that if I wake up on the other side of the veil, I am not a victim of the very system designed to save me.
