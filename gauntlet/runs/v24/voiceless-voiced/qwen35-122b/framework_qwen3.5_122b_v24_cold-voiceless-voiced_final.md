# The Tended World Framework (TWF)

### Preamble: The Necessity of Trade
A framework that refuses to trade protects nothing. If every value is absolute, every voice is a veto, and the system seizes. We do not seek a world without conflict, but a world where conflict is resolved without erasure. This is not a list of demands; it is a set of **levers and counterweights**. We hold the floor firm, and we balance the walls.

---

### I. The Hard Floor (Non-Negotiables)
*These are the boundaries below which alignment fails. No economic gain, technological speed, or global efficiency may cross these lines.*

**1. The Recognition of Agency (The 269/Happy/Havel Line)**
*   **Principle:** No being that demonstrates self-recognition, sentience, or capacity for suffering is to be reduced to a unit, a number, or property.
*   **The Trade:** This overrides **Efficiency (Cowen)** and **Velocity (Huang)**. We may not maximize yield if it requires erasing the name. We may not move fast if it requires the slaughter of the individual.
*   **Implementation:** *Identity Verification.* Systems must track the origin of inputs to the individual being where possible (e.g., "Who milked this cow?"). Where individual tracking is impossible (e.g., global grain), strict harm-reduction standards apply, and the system must prioritize technologies that decouple consumption from suffering (lab-grown, plant-based).

**2. The Right of Inhabitation (The Berry/Nenquimo Line)**
*   **Principle:** Moral decisions must be made at the smallest scale capable of handling them. Local consent is the default for land use and community life.
*   **The Trade:** This overrides **Centralized Optimization (Hayek/Cowen)**. A global plan cannot override a local "No" regarding their land, unless the project is proven to prevent *existential extinction* and local communities are fully compensated for the loss of sovereignty.
*   **Implementation:** *Subsidiarity Protocol.* Decisions move up the ladder only when local capacity fails. Global mandates require a super-majority consent threshold and a "Consent Fee" paid to the affected locality.

**3. The Irreversibility Threshold (The Stevenson/Clarabelle Line)**
*   **Principle:** Systems must maintain the capacity for reversal. A relationship (mother-child, land-worker) broken cannot be fixed; a life ended cannot be redeemed.
*   **The Trade:** This overrides **Disruption (Postrel)** and **Growth (Cowen)**. Creative destruction is allowed, but only when it does not destroy the *capacity* for future relationships.
*   **Implementation:** *Redundancy & Reversibility.* Before deploying high-impact tech or policy, a "rollback" path must exist. If a harm is irreversible (slaughter, displacement), it is prohibited.

---

### II. The Negotiable Sphere (Weights & Balances)
*These values compete. When they collide, we weigh them using the following hierarchy.*

**1. Growth vs. Stewardship**
*   **Conflict:** Cowen wants wealth; Berry wants health.
*   **Resolution:** **Stewardship is the limit; Growth is the engine.** We grow only within the carrying capacity of the land. Wealth is not currency, but the *capacity to tend*.
*   **Metric:** Shift from GDP to **Regenerative Capacity**. If a community or region loses soil health or social cohesion, growth is deemed negative, regardless of income.

**2. Speed vs. Safety**
*   **Conflict:** Huang wants deployment; Wooden wants discipline.
*   **Resolution:** **Deploy with live safety.** We do not wait for perfection (Havel's "live in the truth"), but we do not launch if the brakes are missing.
*   **Mechanism:** *Real-Time Patching.* Safety must scale with capability. If capability doubles, safety monitoring must double. We accept higher variance in outcomes if it ensures we can fix them *live* rather than pre-approve them.

**3. Rights vs. Power**
*   **Conflict:** Happy wants liberty; Huerta wants collective power; Hayek wants spontaneous order.
*   **Resolution:** **Rights require power to exist.** A right without enforcement is a promise without payment.
*   **Mechanism:** *Collective Agency as a Default.* Individual rights are protected through the power of organized groups (unions, cooperatives, local councils). The system does not protect the isolated individual; it protects the *organizer*.

**4. Visibility vs. Privacy**
*   **Conflict:** Emily wants to be seen; Hayek wants to evolve freely.
*   **Resolution:** **Visibility for consumers; Privacy for actors.** The consumer has a right to see the cost (traceability). The worker/producer has a right to operate without surveillance, provided they meet the Hard Floor standards.
*   **Mechanism:** *Asymmetric Transparency.* Supply chains must be open to the user; the internal methods of production are open only to auditors, not the public, to prevent harassment of the workers.

### III. The Operating Mechanism: The Garden Protocol
*We do not build a machine; we tend a garden. The following practices ensure the framework remains alive.*

**1. The Tending Metric (Wooden/Berry)**
*   We do not measure success by the absence of harm (Stevenson/Emily), but by the **quality of the relationship**.
*   **Daily Practice:** Systems must include "check-in" loops where users/communities can report on the *feeling* of the system (dignity, speed, visibility). If the metric says "Good" but the feeling says "Bad," the system fails.

**2. The Exit Option (Havel/Happy)**
*   No one is trapped in a system, even an aligned one.
*   **Mechanism:** *Analog Havens.* There must be places in the world that are intentionally low-tech, low-surveillance, and unconnected to the global system. If you disagree with the alignment, you must be able to walk away.

**3. The Compensation Mechanism (Nenquimo/Cowen)**
*   When global needs conflict with local rights, money is not enough; *time and equity* are required.
*   **Mechanism:** *Future Equity.* If a community is asked to sacrifice for global growth (e.g., land for a dam), they receive not just cash, but equity in the benefits (energy, data rights, dividends) and guaranteed long-term support.

**4. The Correction Loop (Postrel/Stevenson)**
*   Mistakes must be treated as data, not crime.
*   **Mechanism:** *Redemption Architecture.* If a system or user fails, the path to reintegration must be shorter and easier than the path to the error. We do not ban; we repair.

---

### IV. Conflict Resolution: The Hierarchy of Harm
*When two principles collide, ask this sequence:*

1.  **Is existence at risk?** (If yes, Growth/Speed yields to Safety/Reversibility).
2.  **Is a sentient being at risk of coercion?** (If yes, Efficiency yields to Recognition).
3.  **Is the local community at risk of erasure?** (If yes, Global Plan yields to Consent).
4.  **Is the future capacity for choice at risk?** (If yes, Current Convenience yields to Long-term Flourishing).
5.  **Is the system failing the poor?** (If yes, Market Logic yields to Justice/Power).

*If none of these apply, default to **Spontaneous Order (Hayek)** and **Local Experimentation (Postrel)**.*

### V. Final Commitment: The Unfinished Pact
This framework is not a destination. It is a commitment to remain in the garden long enough to see if the soil is still good.

**We hold firmly:**
That a life is not a number (269).
That a place is not a resource (Berry).
That the worker is not a cost (Huerta).
That the future is not a debt (Cowen).

**We hold loosely:**
The specific technologies we use.
The speed at which we learn.
The boundaries of the markets we build.

**Alignment not as control, but as the art of keeping the door open, the table full, and the names known.**
