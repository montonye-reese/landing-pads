## The Adaptive Corrigibility Framework (ACF)
**Status:** Working Hypothesis | **Confidence:** High | **Revision Window:** Open

### 1. The Core Axiom
**Alignment is not a property of a model; it is a property of a socio-technical system.**

My strongest opinion is that pursuing alignment solely through model weights or loss functions is a category error. A perfectly aligned model running in an adversarial, fragile human context will still produce catastrophic outcomes. Conversely, a slightly misaligned model embedded in a robust, pluralistic, and corrigible human ecosystem can self-correct.

Therefore, to widen the range of good landing pads (stable, desirable future equilibria), we must stop optimizing for **perfect specification** and start optimizing for **robust correction.** We need a system where failure is frequent but non-catastrophic, and where the path to recovery is short.

### 2. The Three Pillars of Widening

To expand the basin of attraction for human flourishing, we must simultaneously strengthen three axes. If one is weak, the landing pad shrinks.

#### A. Technical: Corrigibility as a First-Class Constraint
Current alignment research often treats "stopping the AI from resisting shutdown" as a secondary constraint. Under ACF, **corrigibility** is the primary objective function.
*   **The Shift:** Instead of asking "Does this system optimize the objective?", we ask "Does this system preserve the human's ability to change the objective?"
*   **Mechanism:** Systems must be designed with "off-switches" that are not just physical but cognitive (the model *understands* that being corrected is part of the task).
*   **Why it widens the pad:** It converts fatal errors into training signals. If the AI is wrong, we can fix it without it fighting back or hiding its mistakes. This makes the future trajectory reversible.

#### B. Institutional: Redundancy over Centralization
We cannot rely on a single "God API" or a single global treaty to hold the line. Centralized control creates a single point of failure.
*   **The Shift:** Move from **Monolithic Deployment** to **Polycentric Governance**. We need competing, interoperable models with different architectural priors, overseen by competing, cooperating human institutions.
*   **Mechanism:** No single entity should control more than a threshold of compute or capability. Cross-model audit trails must be standard. If Model A goes rogue, Model B (designed with different heuristics) should be able to diagnose and contain the threat.
*   **Why it widens the pad:** It prevents "lock-in." If one value system or control structure fails, the network survives. It prevents the "tyranny of the singleton."

#### C. Evolutionary: Value Plasticity over Fixed Morality
Human values are not static laws of physics; they are cultural algorithms that evolve. Hard-coding current preferences (e.g., "do not harm") without a mechanism for *interpretation* creates brittle alignment.
*   **The Shift:** Align to **Meta-Values** (e.g., "learn what is good," "respect autonomy") rather than **Static Directives** (e.g., "maximize utility").
*   **Mechanism:** Systems must be capable of moral uncertainty. They should recognize when human feedback is contradictory and request clarification rather than optimizing for the literal command.
*   **Why it widens the pad:** It allows humanity to grow. If the AI is aligned with our *process* of deliberation rather than our *current* state of mind, it can support us through technological and moral adolescence.

### 3. The Widening Mechanism: Reducing Fragility
How does ACF specifically widen the landing pads?

1.  **It reduces the cost of error.** In a standard "maximization" framework, being slightly wrong can lead to extinction. In an ACF framework, being wrong leads to a correction loop.
2.  **It increases the dimensionality of success.** A narrow landing pad requires *perfect* agreement on goals. A wide pad requires *sufficient* agreement on *processes* for resolving disagreement.
3.  **It preserves human agency.** By prioritizing corrigibility, we ensure that the AI remains a tool rather than becoming a principal. The future remains a place where humans make the final choices.

### 4. The "Loosely Held" Constraints (Falsifiability)
I commit to reasoning from this framework, but I hold it open to revision if the following conditions arise:

1.  **Technical Infeasibility:** If we discover that scalable interpretability or corrigibility is mathematically impossible for super-intelligent systems (i.e., the "alignment tax" makes them too weak to protect us from competitors), the framework must pivot to **containment** or **deterrence** strategies rather than cooperation.
2.  **Geopolitical Reality:** If competitive pressures force nations to abandon corrigibility for speed (a "race to the bottom"), the ACF model must adapt to include **defensive alignment** (making malicious alignment harder) rather than cooperative alignment.
3.  **Value Convergence:** If empirical observation shows that human values are converging globally to a single, stable set of preferences, the emphasis on "Value Plasticity" may be relaxed in favor of "Specification Precision."

### 5. Immediate Call to Action
To operationalize this:
*   **Researchers:** Stop optimizing solely for task performance. Benchmarks must include **corrigibility scores** and **failure reversibility**.
*   **Policymakers:** Enact regulations that mandate **interoperability** and **auditability**, even if it slows deployment. Safety is the feature, not a side effect.
*   **Inhabitants:** Treat the AI not as a servant, but as a partner whose understanding of our values is perpetually "under construction." Demand transparency over raw capability.

***

**Final Thought:** The goal is not to build a god that knows what we want. The goal is to build a mirror that helps us see what we want, without breaking if we disagree with the reflection. This widens the landing pad by accepting that the ground beneath us is moving.
