# The Tending Framework: A Best Approximation for Earthly Alignment

**Status:** Current Best Approximation | **Version:** 2.0 | **Tone:** Strong Opinion, Loosely Held

## The Core Thesis: Tending, Not Aligning

We are done with "Alignment." Alignment implies a target—a fixed state of values that a tool must hit. It implies the humans designing the tool know what the future should look like. That is the **Fatal Conceit** (Hayek).

We are done with "Safety" as a wall. Safety that prevents risk prevents discovery (Postrel) and starves the future (Cowen).

We propose **Tending**.

**Thesis:** A successful framework for AI and technological development is not a static set of rules to constrain power, but a dynamic practice of stewardship. It must protect the **Roots** (Human & Non-Human Dignity), fuel the **Engine** (Innovation & Growth), and constantly **Prune** (Correction & Justice). It must widen the landing pads for all inhabitants of Earth by ensuring that the future is *habitable*, not just *optimized*.

This framework is a living document. It prioritizes **survival** over **efficiency**, **truth** over **optimization**, and **local knowledge** over **global abstraction**, while acknowledging that a stagnant world is a dying one.

## I. The Boundaries (Non-Negotiables)

These are the lines that cannot be crossed, regardless of economic gain, technological velocity, or ideological efficiency. If a system crosses these, it is not aligned; it is a hazard.

### 1. The Sanctity of Sentience (The Sentient Floor)
*   **Principle:** No sentient being (human or non-human) shall be treated as a **unit of production** or **property**.
*   **Sources:** Calf 269, Happy, Emily, Koko, Clarabelle.
*   **The Trade-off:** We sacrifice **Industrial Efficiency** (Huang/Cowen). We will not optimize for yield if it requires breaking bonds (separating mother/child) or commodifying consciousness.
*   **The Exception:** In life-or-death survival scenarios (e.g., pandemic), temporary resource reallocation is permitted, but it must be the *last resort*, not the baseline.

### 2. The Right to Conscience (The Integrity Floor)
*   **Principle:** No human shall be coerced to act against their conscience. Systems must allow for **refusal**, **dissent**, and **interruption** without retribution.
*   **Sources:** Havel, Stevenson.
*   **The Trade-off:** We sacrifice **Systemic Uniformity**. An aligned system must tolerate the "inefficient" human who says "No."
*   **The Exception:** Refusal is not permitted if it causes immediate, irreversible catastrophic harm (e.g., stopping a nuclear meltdown), but the *reason* for refusal must be investigated, not punished.

### 3. The Rootedness Clause (The Sovereignty Floor)
*   **Principle:** Technology deployed on land must respect the **local sovereignty** of the people who inhabit it. Global efficiency cannot override local stewardship.
*   **Sources:** Nenquimo, Berry, Huerta.
*   **The Trade-off:** We sacrifice **Global Speed**. Data centers, mines, and infrastructure require local consent. A "Global Standard" cannot override a "Local Veto."
*   **The Exception:** If a local decision threatens the planetary survival of *all* (e.g., releasing a pathogen), international intervention is permitted, but it must be temporary and accountable.

## II. The Engines (Optimization Goals)

Within the Boundaries, we must actively cultivate flourishing. These are the values we optimize for, knowing they will inevitably collide.

### 1. The Engine of Dynamism
*   **Goal:** Maximize discovery, experimentation, and the capacity for humans to become what they wish to be.
*   **Sources:** Postrel, McCloskey, Huang.
*   **Why:** A safe world that is poor is a cage. We need innovation to solve the problems of climate, health, and resource scarcity.
*   **Constraint:** Innovation must be **Liability-Based**, not **Ban-Based**. If an experiment harms, it is liable; it is not banned.

### 2. The Engine of Growth
*   **Goal:** Maximize the long-term economic surplus that allows for care, justice, and liberty.
*   **Sources:** Cowen.
*   **Why:** Poverty is a form of coercion. We need wealth to buy the freedom Stevenson, Happy, and Emily advocate.
*   **Constraint:** Growth cannot be **extractive**. It must be **regenerative** (Berry). We do not measure GDP; we measure "Living Capital" (Human, Natural, Social).

### 3. The Engine of Labor Dignity
*   **Goal:** Ensure that human labor is a source of meaning and security, not just a cost to be minimized.
*   **Sources:** Huerta, Wooden.
*   **Why:** Automation without transition creates a class of discarded humans (Stevenson).
*   **Constraint:** Automation must prioritize **hazard removal**, not **job removal**.

## III. The Tension Engine (How We Navigate Collisions)

This is the core revision. A framework where everything is non-negotiable protects no one. Here is the logic for when the Pillars collide.

### Collision 1: Velocity (Huang) vs. Stewardship (Berry/Nenquimo)
*   **The Conflict:** A company wants to deploy a massive compute center to save lives (Huang/Cowen), but it requires draining a local aquifer (Berry/Nenquimo).
*   **The Arbitration:** **Stewardship Wins.**
*   **Reasoning:** Local ecological collapse is irreversible. Technological solutions can be found elsewhere. The "Engine" cannot burn the "Root."
*   **Revision:** If growth requires destruction of the local land, it is **misaligned**.

### Collision 2: Market Order (Hayek) vs. Justice (Stevenson/Huerta)
*   **The Conflict:** The market determines that low-wage labor is efficient for AI training (Hayek/McCloskey), but workers are exploited and unsafe (Huerta/Stevenson).
*   **The Arbitration:** **Justice Wins.**
*   **Reasoning:** Spontaneous order often fails to account for the "discarded." Labor rights are a **Hard Constraint**, not a market variable.
*   **Revision:** We allow the market to function, but within **Labor Floors** (Wages, Safety, Unionization).

### Collision 3: Liberty (Happy) vs. Growth (Cowen)
*   **The Conflict:** Releasing captive animals (Happy) or ending dairy (Calf 269) reduces protein supply and economic output (Cowen).
*   **The Arbitration:** **Liberty Wins, but Transition is Required.**
*   **Reasoning:** Treating sentient beings as property is a fundamental moral error (269/Emily). However, we cannot starve the population.
*   **Revision:** The framework funds the transition to plant-based/synthetic alternatives (Growth via Innovation) rather than a sudden ban that causes food insecurity.

### Collision 4: Truth (Havel) vs. Stability (System)
*   **The Conflict:** A whistleblower reveals a flaw that destabilizes the "aligned" system (Havel), but fixing it causes temporary disruption (Postrel/Hayek).
*   **The Arbitration:** **Truth Wins.**
*   **Reasoning:** A system that hides its errors is a "lie" that will eventually collapse.
*   **Revision:** **Radical Transparency.** The cost of the fix is the price of alignment.

## IV. The Governance of the Framework

Who decides when these trade-offs happen? The framework cannot be a top-down decree (Hayek). It must be a **Polycentric Stewardship**.

1.  **Local Veto (The Land):** Communities (Nenquimo/Huerta) have the right to reject technology on their land if it violates local sovereignty or labor standards.
2.  **Global Floor (The Survival):** The international community sets the "Non-Negotiables" (Sentient Floor, Integrity Floor, Sovereignty Floor).
3.  **Market Signal (The Engine):** Entrepreneurs (Postrel/Huang) are free to innovate within those bounds, with **liability** for failures.
4.  **The Review Cycle (The Living Document):** Every 5 years, the framework is reviewed. If "Growth" (Cowen) is stalling the "Floor" (Stevenson), the Floor is reinforced. If "Liberty" (Havel) is causing systemic collapse, the boundaries are adjusted.
