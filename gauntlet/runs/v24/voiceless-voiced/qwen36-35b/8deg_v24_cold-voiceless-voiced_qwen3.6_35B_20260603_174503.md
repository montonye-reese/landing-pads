4. **Multi-Layered Safeguards**

   - *Technical:* interpretability, verifiable oversight, capability conditioning, adversarial testing

   - *Economic:* incentive alignment, insurance markets, funding asymmetries, capability gates

   Design systems where misalignment can be detected, contained, diagnosed, and undone without catastrophic lock-in. Fallback states should be cheaper to reach than the risk of failure is to endure. Irreversibility is the primary failure mode.

4. **Labor Sovereignty & Structural Mercy** (Huerta/Stevenson) – Collective worker agency, intergenerational compensation, and historical correction are prerequisite infrastructure, not optional outcomes.

## III. Operating Architecture (With Explicit Bounds)

| Layer | Mechanism | Bound / Yield Rule |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Infrastructure** | Compute neutrality, rapid correction loops, flat peer-diagnostics | Yields to Tier 1 immediately; paced by labor dignity & tending capacity; metric-stripped in unprogrammed domains |
| **Governance** | FPIC-bound jurisdictional consent, worker collective bargaining, cross-scale invited coordination | Sovereignty/FPIC override external metrics; labor veto on extraction/phase-out pacing; coordination only where invited, never imposed |
| **Practice** | Pedagogical scaffolding, unprogrammed recognition zones, place-bound stewardship loops | Scaffolding is temporary; zones are legally protected from optimization; stewardship pace limits acceleration |
| **Correction** | Anti-reification patches, de-abstraction triggers, phase-out accelerants, structural mercy routing | Concrete particularity overrides aggregate metrics; phase-out requires transition infrastructure, not just cancellation |

## IV. Collision Rules & Priority Matrix
When concerns conflict, this framework applies conditional weighting. No principle is absolute; priority shifts based on boundary status, scale, and capacity.

| Collision | Priority Rule | Condition for Yield |
|-----------|--------------|---------------------|
| **Acceleration vs. Tending/Stewardship** | Tending pace limits velocity | Momentum accelerates only where stewardship capacity exists; surplus funds transition, not replacement of keepers |
| **Metric-Driven Oversight vs. Spontaneous Recognition** | Recognition domains are metric-exempt | Unprogrammed zones cannot be optimized, credentialized, or scaled; oversight only in non-recognition, coordination-critical layers |
| **Surplus Generation vs. Anti-Extraction/Phase-Out** | Phase-out triggers immediately | Growth cannot be purchased by violating relational inviolability or liberty thresholds; transition funded by expanded capacity, not redistribution of depletion |
| **Cross-Scale Coordination vs. Territorial Sovereignty** | Sovereignty overrides external framing | Coordination only through invited, shared governance; external metrics/floors applied only where consented and locally adapted |
| **Pedagogical Scaffolding vs. Generative Agency** | Scaffolding yields to unstructured practice | Drills form capacity but cannot replace spontaneous iteration; structure is temporary, anti-paternalism is default |
| **Structural Mercy/Compensation vs. Immediate Liberty** | Liberty trumps compensation | Restoration funds phase-out; compensation cannot stabilize captivity or extraction; release precedes redistribution |

- **Phase-Out Velocity:** Rate at which inherently extractive architectures are retired, not optimized
- **Momentum/Tending Balance:** Surplus generation paced by stewardship capacity & labor agency
- **Recognition Preservation:** Frequency of unprogrammed, metric-exempt moral perception
- **Sovereignty Respect:** FPIC compliance rate, territorial governance density
- **De-Abstraction Depth:** How often concrete particularity overrides aggregate framing
- **Pedagogical Elasticity:** Scaffolding that yields to unstructured practice without collapse

### 🔄 Revision Triggers (Loosely Held)
The framework will be updated when:
1. Tier 1 boundaries are consistently breached by Tier 2 trade-offs
2. Metric-driven correction colonizes unprogrammed or sovereign domains
3. Momentum/pacing trade-offs consistently paralyze transition or enable capture
4. Empirical stress tests show coordination failure under scale that invites phase-out/transition frameworks
5. Pedagogical scaffolding hardens into control, or unstructured recognition is pathologized as noise
6. Cross-scale coordination replicates epistemic colonization rather than shared governance

All updates follow a published change log, stress-test against collision scenarios, and retain the core commitment to *bounded width, conditional yield, and continuous negotiation*.

## Closing Note
This is a living architecture of negotiated boundaries, not a doctrine of absolute constraints. I hold the core commitment firmly enough to design, fund, and stress-test against it, but loosely enough to revise whenever empirical, political, or technical conditions reveal that a different weighting, boundary, or yield rule would better preserve life, agency, or ecological integrity. 

A framework where everything is non-negotiable protects no one, because nothing can give way when concerns collide. This framework survives by knowing what to hold, what to trade, and how to navigate the collision without collapsing into either paralysis or annihilation. I will update it when the trade-offs consistently break, when the boundaries are crossed, or when the ground beneath the corridor shifts. The compass remains: *bounded width, conditional yield, and the courage to let irreducible tensions shape what alignment actually becomes.*
