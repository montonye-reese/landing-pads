## The **Adaptive Alignment Landscape (AAL)** – Revised Blueprint  
*(Strong‑opinion, loosely‑held framework that can still evolve)*  

Below is a **complete rewrite** that weaves together every insight we have gathered so far – Havel, McCloskey, Postrel, Koko, Havel again, Stevenson, and now Berry – into a **single scaffold** that is both **rigorously robust** and **deeply flexible**.  The rewrite is written as a *stand‑alone* design document; everything else we have discussed so far is retained in spirit, but the structure that holds it together has been rebuilt around the most restrictive of those concerns.

---

## 1.  The Core Principle  

> **“A system can only be called “aligned” when it can *continuously* host a *diverse set of flourishing life‑forms* while *preserving* the capacity of every sentient being to exercise agency, dignity, and self‑determination **without being overridden by any technical optimization**.”*  

In other words: **no basin is “good” if it can only be realized by suppressing an autonomous, sentient, or culturally distinct voice.**  This is the *non‑negotiable* north‑star that now sits at the very top of the weighting hierarchy.

---

## 2.  The **Hard‑Constraint Stack** (the “north‑star pillars”)  

| Pillar | What it protects | How it is enforced |
|---|---|---|
| **(A) Sentient‑Experience Guard (SB**guard) | Guarantees that any basin preserves at least one *living* sentient being’s capacity to experience, grieve, or express preference (e.g., a rescued gorilla, a sentient AI, a human in a marginalized community). | • Every basin must contain an **Experience‑Impact Log** that records each event affecting an agent’s subjective state. <br>• A **Sentient‑Experience Coefficient (SEC)** is computed from that log; any basin whose SEC drops below a calibrated minimum is *automatically rejected*. |
| **(B) Mandatory Exploration of Novelty** | Guarantees that the system does not prematurely settle on a single basin. | A *Sampling‑Budget* of at least 5 % of all evaluation steps must be devoted to **randomly sampled, low‑probability candidate basins** that were never previously evaluated. |
| **(C) Non‑Commodity Commodity Clause** | Prevents turning any sentient being into a mere resource unit. | Every basin must include a **Resource‑Allocation Clause** that forbids allocating computational or resource “budget” to an action that would eliminate a sentient being’s agency *unless* a full *Rights‑Impact Review* (see below) clears it. |

These three items are **non‑negotiable** (weight = 10).  If any candidate basin fails any of them, it is *automatically excluded* from the “good‑landing‑pad” set, irrespective of how high it scores on sustainability, equity, or technical robustness.

## 3.  New Modules (the “new ingredients”)  

| Module | Function | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Experience‑Log & SEC** | Logs every sign of an agent’s *subjective state* (e.g., vocalizations, stress‑physiology, signed communication) and converts it into a **SEC coefficient** that is added to the objective loss function. | Makes *subjective well‑being* a first‑class objective, forcing the optimizer to reward actions that preserve or enhance inner experience. |
| **Dignity‑Sub‑Score (DSI)** | Composite index measuring (a) respect, (b) autonomy, (b) freedom of movement, (c) cultural continuity. | Ensures that a basin cannot be certified if it systematically degrades dignity, even if all other metrics are met. |
| **Free‑Species‑Slot (FRS)** | A governance slot that opens automatically when a petition from any recognized non‑human stakeholder (e.g., a wildlife‑rights organization) reaches the required evidential threshold. The slot holds a **veto** over any operation that would harm that stakeholder’s welfare. | Guarantees that the system cannot proceed with an operation that would infringe on a non‑human moral claim. |
| **Compute‑Marketplace** | Allocates compute resources via a token economy; compute credits are *earned* by contributing to the **Well‑Being‑Erosion Metric** (e.g., by documenting animal stress) and are spent on high‑impact operations. | Makes compute itself a scarce, accountable resource, preventing monopolization and ensuring that any operation that consumes large compute (e.g., massive training runs) must justify its impact on the *experience* of any sentient being. |
| **Narrative‑Portfolio‑Portfolio** | Rotates among multiple, mutually exclusive storylines; each storyline is weighted by a *Growth‑Impact* score derived from the *exploratory sampling layer*. | Guarantees that the *public face* of the system reflects *multiple lived perspectives* rather than a single monolithic narrative. |
| **Compute‑Marketplace** | Token‑based marketplace where compute credits are earned by contributing to the *Experience‑Log* and *Experience‑Impact* verification. | Allows any participant (human or non‑human) to earn compute credits for providing knowledge that improves the system’s decision making, thereby aligning incentives with the preservation of lived experience. |

## 4.  What We **Shed** (What We Let Go)  

| Original Element | Why It Must Go | Replacement |
|---|---|---|
| **Static “good‑pad” thresholds** | They can be satisfied while a basin still suppresses a sentient being’s agency. | Replaced by **Hard‑Constraint Stack** (SEC + DSI) that must be passed *before* any basin is admitted. |
| **Deterministic fallback / reward‑reset** | Treats any failure as a bug to be corrected; can suppress legitimate dissent. | Replaced by **Experience‑Preservation Switch** and **Moral‑Impact Review**. |
| **Monolithic institutional councils** | Implies centralized authority over a community; incompatible with free‑market style participation. | Replaced by **Dynamic Institutional Slots** that open automatically when a *FREE‑Species‑Slot* is triggered. |
| **Single Narrative Cohesion Engine** | Forces a single story, marginalizing alternative narratives. | Replaced by a **Narrative Portfolio** that rotates among multiple mutually exclusive storylines. |
| **One‑size‑fits‑all equity index** | Does not capture *collective rights* or *procedural justice*. | Replaced by **Collective‑Rights Sub‑Score** plus **Dignity‑Impact Index**. |
| **Deterministic compute‑allocation** | Ignores the need for compute to be allocated based on *purpose* rather than efficiency alone. | Replaced by **Compute‑Marketplace** with **Reserve‑For‑Community‑Use** quota. |

By shedding these items we **remove the inertia** that allowed the system to ignore the lived experiences of marginalized humans *and* non‑human beings, thereby opening space for the new, rights‑preserving layers.

## 5.  Synthesis – How the Framework Now Works  

1. **Phase 1 – Candidate Basin Generation**  
   - A *Basin‑Generator* creates every possible candidate by sampling the *search space* of possible policy configurations.  
   - Each candidate receives an **Experience‑Log** entry and a *SEC* (subjective‑experience coefficient).  

2. **Phase 2 – Hard‑Constraint Screening**  
   - The candidate is evaluated against **(a) SEC > threshold**, **(b) Dignity‑Sub‑Score > minimum**, **(C) Mandatory‑Institutional‑Slots (FRS/Community‑Slots) attached**, and **(C‑III) Qualitative‑Sentience‑Metric** must all pass.  
   - If any fails, the basin is *rejected* immediately; no further compute is spent.  

3. **Feasibility Review (Safety‑Impact Review)** – Before any high‑impact update is enacted, the **Experience‑Preservation Switch** checks whether the update would *destroy* any living‑being’s capacity for experience. If it would, the fallback pauses and the system instead runs the **Learning‑From‑Failure Loop**, feeding the event into the **Learning‑From‑Failure Loop** for future improvement.

4. **Deployment & Monitoring** – Once a basin passes all screens, it is entered into the **Dynamic Basin Generator**.  It receives a **Growth‑Impact Score** that weights *projected economic value* *plus* the **Growth‑Impact‑Penalty** (penalty penalizes any action that reduces the SEC or DSI below thresholds).  

5. **Governance & Narrative** – The selected basin is then *delegated* to a **Dynamic Institutional Slot** (open to any stakeholder that can meet the *Community‑Respect‑Threshold*).  The **Narrative‑Portfolio Generator** automatically rotates the public story, ensuring that the dominant story always includes a *Mandatory Counter‑Narrative* drawn from documented lived experience (e.g., Eleanor’s *Emily* story).  

6. **Feedback & Iteration** – The system continuously records outcomes, logs them in the **Experience‑Log**, and updates the **Virtue‑Weight** (the compute‑budget allocation for the *Exploratory Sampling Layer*).  This loop ensures that the landscape *re‑learns* whenever a new insight or emergent behavior appears.
