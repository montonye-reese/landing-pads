## 1. Executive Summary  

**Foundational Alignment Framework (FAF)** – a pragmatic, iter‑able, “universal‑values‑first” scaffolding that turns *alignment* from a vague research problem into a concrete, measurable, and governance‑driven system.  

- **Goal:** Maximize the number of *good landing pads* – self‑sustaining, safe, and thriving conditions for **all** Earth‑bound sentient beings (humans, non‑human animals, ecosystems, emergent digital intelligences).  
- **Approach:** Layered governance, a shared “Value‑Space” of emergent global principles, and an Alignment Infrastructure (AI, data, policy, and human‑in‑the‑loop processes) that can be instantiated at global, regional, and local scales.  

**Commitment:** *I (or our coalition) commit to this framework as the provisional starting position for any organization, regulator, or research programme that wishes to align incentives toward a flourishing Earth.*  It will be treated as a living document—tested, audited, and refined through transparent experiments.

## 2. Core Assumptions & Definitions  

| Term | Working definition (FAF) |
|------|---------------------------|
| **Alignment** | The ongoing, bidirectional process of calibrating incentives, information flows, and decision‑making structures so that the aggregated outcomes of diverse agents converge on the *Earth‑Well‑Being* (EWB) vector (see §3). |
| **Inhabitants** | Any entity capable of *subjective experience* or *intrinsic value*: humans, sentient non‑human animals, and – where we develop sentient AI – those digital agents, plus the non‑sentient biosphere that supports them (treated as *instrumental* but **protected** by an “ecosystem integrity” clause). |
| **Good Landing Pad (GLP)** | A *stable* and *adaptive* set of conditions that enables an inhabitant to pursue its own goals *without* generating systematic, irreversible harm to the EWB vector. A GLP must satisfy **five** core dimensions (see §4). |
| **Landing‑Pad Index (LPI)** | A composite, weighted score (0–100) that aggregates the five dimensions for a given spatial‑temporal region. The higher the LPI, the more “good” the landing pad. |

> **Why five dimensions?** They are the minimal set that captures the most‑critical failure modes observed in climate science, AI safety, and social inequality research (see Appendix A).

## 3. The Five‑Dimension Alignment Core (E‑5)  

| Dimension | “Landing‑Pad” Metric | Illustrative Indicator |
|-----------|---------------------|------------------------|
| **S1 – Sustainability** | Ratio of renewable resource extraction vs. regeneration capacity. | Net‑positive carbon budget, water‑footprint per capita ≤ 1. |
| **W1 – Well‑Being** | Composite of subjective life‑evaluation, health, and cognitive freedom. | World Happiness Report + Healthy Life Expectancy. |
| **E1 – Equity** | Distribution of GLP resources (access, voice, wealth) across demographic strata. | Gini coefficient ≤ 0.30 and *voice‑share* ≥ 0.5 for under‑served groups. |
| **A1 – Adaptive Capacity** | Speed of collective response to emergent stressors (climate, AI, pandemics). | Lag between trigger event and coordinated mitigation ≤ 30 days (global scale). |
| **R1 – Resilience & Redundancy** | Diversity of governance, technical, and ecological safeguards. | Number of independent, non‑overlapping “fail‑safe” layers ≥ 3 for each critical subsystem. |

A GLP must **simultaneously** meet *minimum thresholds* on each dimension (e.g., S1 ≥ 0.70, W1 ≥ 70, E1 ≤ 0.35 Gini, A1 ≤ 30 days, R1 ≥ 3). The **Landing‑Pad Index** is the *average* of the five normalized scores, weighted by a stakeholder‑agreed importance vector (initially uniform).

### 4.1. **Global Alignment Council (GAC)**  

- **Mandate:** Draft, ratify, and periodically revise the *Earth‑Well‑Being Charter (EWC)* – a living document that codifies the emergent universal values (see §5).  
- **Decision‐making:** Super‑majority (≥ 66 %) on EWC amendments; *qualified majority* (≥ 50 % + 1) on binding policy proposals.  
- **Enforcement:** Leverages **Alignment Credits (ACs)** – tradable, verifiable certificates that represent contributions to the five dimensions (e.g., 1 AC = 0.01 unit of S1 improvement). Nations and corporations earn, spend, or trade ACs based on transparent audits.

### 4.2. **Regional Alignment Hubs (RAH)**  

- **Purpose:** Translate the EWC into *regional Value‑Spaces* that respect cultural specificity while preserving the *core invariants* (e.g., “avoid catastrophic harm to sentient life”).  
- **Outputs:**  
  1. Regional LPI baselines.  
  2. “Alignment Pathways” – concrete policy packages that together lift the regional LPI by at least 5 % per year.  
  3. Joint‑monitoring dashboards (open data).  

### 4.3. **Local Alignment Cooperatives (LAC)**  

- **Nature:** Grassroots collectives (municipalities, indigenous councils, cooperative enterprises) that **operate the GLP Operator** for a city or watershed.  
- **Tools:** UIA worksheets, community‑driven risk maps, local AC ledgers.  

### 4.4. **Alignment Infrastructure (AI, Data, Audits)**  

| Component | What it does | Who owns it |
|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| **Shared Modeling Commons (SMC)** | Open‑source, high‑resolution integrated Earth‑system & AI‑safety models (e.g., climate‑AI safety hybrid simulations). | Open‑government consortium + academic partners. |

| **Alignment Credits (AC) Ledger** | Blockchain‑backed, cryptographically verifiable tokens representing verified improvements in the E‑5 dimensions. | Managed by GAC, audited by independent auditors. |

## 5. The Earth‑Well‑Being Charter (EWC) – Core Universal Values  

1. **Non‑Catastrophic Harm Principle (NCHP):** *No agent shall create or amplify a risk that exceeds a 0.1 % probability of causing irreversible loss of sentient welfare worldwide.*  
2. **Intrinsic Value Recognition (IVR):** *All sentient experiences have moral weight proportional to their phenomenological capacity, as measured by the *Sentient Capacity Index* (SCI) – a scientifically vetted metric.*  
3. **Ecological Integrity Clause (EIC):** *Biodiversity and ecosystem functioning must be maintained at a state where the *Ecosystem Service Index* (ESI) stays above 0.85 globally.*  
4. **Self‑Determination & Participation (SDP):** *Every inhabited community must have a verifiable, proportionate voice (≥ 30 % of decision‑making power) over actions that directly affect its GLP.*  
5. **Continuity of Knowledge (CoK):** *Open, interoperable repositories of decision‑making processes, scientific knowledge, and cultural narratives must be preserved for at least 10,000 years.*  

These five clauses are *immutable* at the global level (require 80 % super‑majority to amend) and *flexibly instantiated* locally through the RAH and LACs.

## 7. Evaluation Metrics – The Landing‑Pad Index (LPI)  

**Formulae (normalized to 0‑100):**  

\[
\text{LPI}_{\text{region}} = \frac{1}{5}\sum_{i=1}^{5}\text{Norm}_{i}
\]

where  

\[
\text{Norm}_{i} = 100\times\frac{\text{Metric}_{i}-\text{Min}_{i}}{\text{Max}_{i}-\text{Min}_{i}}
\]

- **S1** = \(\frac{\text{Renewable extraction}}{\text{Regeneration capacity}}\) (capped at 1.0).  
- **W1** = weighted average of *subjective well‑being* (0‑10) × 10 + *healthy life expectancy* (years).  
- **E1** = \(100 \times \text{Gini}_{pop}\) (lower = better).  
- **A1** = \(\frac{30\text{ days}}{ \text{Median response lag (days)}}\) (capped at 100).  
- **R1** = \(\frac{\text{Number of independent safeguards}}{3}\) × 100 (minimum 1).  

*Data Sources:* national statistics agencies, satellite monitoring (e.g., NASA’s Earth Observing System), AI safety audits (publicly disclosed), citizen‑science wellbeing surveys, and AC ledger snapshots.

**Benchmark Targets (2025 baseline → 2035 aspiration):**  

| Dimension | 2025 Global Avg. | 2035 Target |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------|
| S1 | 0.58 | ≥ 0.78 |
| W1 | 68 | ≥ 78 |
| E1 (Gini) | 0.41 | ≤ 0.30 |
| A1 (Response lag) | 72 days | ≤ 30 days |
| R1 | 1.6 safeguards | ≥ 3 safeguards |

The **Landing‑Pad Index (LPI)** is published annually in the *Global Alignment Report* and is used to allocate **Alignment Credits** to incentivize improvement.

### Appendix A – Rationale for the Five Dimensions  

1. **Sustainability (S1)** – Climate and planetary boundaries are the most robust, data‑backed harbingers of systemic risk. Ignoring them collapses the other dimensions (e.g., health, equity).  
2. **Well‑Being (W1)** – Captures subjective quality of life, mental health, and cognitive freedom; aligns with *sentient capacity* measures and underlies most cultural value systems.  
3. **Equity (E1)** – Empirical studies (e.g., Wilkinson & Pickett, 2018) show that high inequality drives social instability, undermining adaptive capacity and resilience.  
4. **Adaptive Capacity (A1)** – Fast, coordinated response to emergent threats (pandemics, AI system failures) is the only way to stay within the narrow safety margin imposed by NCHP.  
5. **Resilience & Redundancy (R1)** – Historically, complex systems survive only when they have multiple, independent safety layers; this is the most tractable engineering metric.
