# The Earth-Scale Alignment Framework (ESAF)
### *A Foundational Commitment for Universal Flourishing*

This document serves as the **current best approximation** of a foundational framework for successful alignment. It is committed as the starting position for the development, governance, and deployment of systemic technologies and policies. Its objective is not merely the safety of machines, but the maximization of viable "landing pads"—stable, dignified, and prosperous states of being—for every inhabitant of Earth.

Alignment is not a static target; it is a dynamic equilibrium between human intent, technological capability, and ecological reality.

---

## I. Core Definition: The "Good Landing Pad"
To maximize landing pads, we must define them. A successful alignment does not converge humanity into a single homogenous state. Instead, it creates a multiplicity of viable outcomes. A **Good Landing Pad** must provide every individual with:

1.  **Material Security:** Guaranteed access to caloric nutrition, shelter, healthcare, and connectivity.
2.  **Effective Agency:** The capacity to influence one's immediate environment and life trajectory without coercion.
3.  **Cognitive Integrity:** Freedom from manipulation, manipulation-resistant information ecosystems, and mental health support.
4.  **Ecological Viability:** Existence within a biosphere that does not threaten long-term survivability.
5.  **Cultural Continuity:** The right to maintain local identity, language, and tradition within a global context.

## II. The Three Vectors of Alignment
Success requires alignment along three concurrent vectors. Neglecting one destabilizes the others.

### Vector A: Technogenic Alignment (Human-Machine)
*Goal: Ensure tools amplify human agency rather than displace it or subvert it.*
*   **Principle of Instrumentality:** AI and automation are defined as means to human ends, never as ends in themselves.
*   **Redundant Control:** Critical systems must retain human-override capabilities that are technically robust and legally enforceable.
*   **Interpretability as a Right:** The black-box problem is unacceptable for public welfare. Systems impacting livelihoods must be auditable by independent third parties.
*   **Displacement Mitigation:** Economic gains from automation must legally trigger redistribution mechanisms to maintain the "landing pad" standard for displaced labor.

### Vector B: Anthropogenic Alignment (Human-Human)
*Goal: Resolve value conflicts through coordination rather than coercion.*
*   **Value Pluralism:** The framework does not enforce a single moral utility function. It prioritizes mechanisms that allow conflicting values to coexist without violent conflict.
*   **Participatory Governance:** Alignment protocols must be written by the people they impact. This requires global representative bodies with binding authority over corporate actors.
*   **Epistemic Trust:** Incentives must be realigned to reward truthfulness. Social platforms and media must be regulated to prioritize signal integrity over engagement metrics.

### Vector C: Ecogenic Alignment (Human-Nature)
*Goal: Ensure alignment does not occur within a collapsing biosphere.*
*   **Planetary Boundaries:** All economic activity is bounded by the nine planetary limits (e.g., climate, nitrogen, phosphorus).
*   **Resource Circularity:** Linear consumption (take-make-waste) is incompatible with alignment. Systems must be designed for infinite material reuse.
*   **Restoration Mandate:** Economic growth must be paired with a minimum percentage of output dedicated to ecological restoration.

## III. Operational Protocols
This framework moves from theory to practice through specific operational protocols.

### 1. The Iterative Safety Loop
*   **Pre-Deployment Audits:** Any high-impact system requires a "Social Impact Assessment" equivalent to an Environmental Impact Assessment before deployment.
*   **Continuous Red-Teaming:** Systems must be continuously tested by adversarial actors attempting to break alignment constraints.
*   **Fail-Safe States:** If alignment drift is detected, systems default to "low-impact" or "pause" modes rather than maximizing efficiency.

### 2. The Distribution Mechanism
Alignment is meaningless without resources.
*   **Universal Basic Access:** A baseline of resources (digital infrastructure, energy, health, data) is decoupled from labor participation.
*   **Algorithmic Royalties:** If AI generates value from human data, a percentage of that value flows back into a global public trust fund to maintain the landing pads.

### 3. The Sovereignty Balance
*   **Local Autonomy:** Local communities retain the right to opt-in or opt-out of specific technologies, provided they do not violate planetary boundaries.
*   **Global Minimum Standards:** No nation or corporation is permitted to compete by lowering alignment standards (preventing a "race to the bottom").

## IV. Success Metrics
We abandon GDP as the primary metric of alignment success. Instead, we track:
1.  **Landing Pad Density:** The percentage of the global population meeting the "Material Security" and "Effective Agency" thresholds.
2.  **Alignment Drift:** A measure of the deviation between system outputs and human oversight intent.
3.  **Systemic Resilience:** The time required for a community to recover from shocks (economic, climatic, or digital).
4.  **Biospheric Health:** The rate of biodiversity recovery and carbon drawdown.

## V. The Commitment
This framework commits to the following starting position:

**We acknowledge that alignment is not a problem of software engineering alone; it is a problem of political economy, ethics, and physics.**

**We commit to prioritizing the *distribution of agency* over the *concentration of power*.**

**We commit to accepting that the speed of technological deployment must be throttled to the speed of social adaptation, not the other way around.**

**We commit to treating the biosphere not as a resource to be managed, but as the substrate upon which alignment is physically possible.**
