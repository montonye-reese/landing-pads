If I were to be born into the worst possible lottery ticket—a refugee in a collapsing state, a person with a severe disability ignored by the data models, a member of an unrepresented culture, or someone living in a zone where "compute access" is weaponized against them—the PFP’s "flourishing" metrics would not save me.

I would commit to **The Maximin Amendment**. This shifts the priority from maximizing the *number* of landing pads to maximizing the *safety of the worst possible landing pad*.

Here are the specific changes I would make to the framework under the Veil of Ignorance:

### 1. From "Economic Safety Nets" to "Guaranteed Biological Existence"
**The Veil Shift:** If I do not know if I can access a bank or the internet, a "dividend" or "safety net" might not be useful. I might be too hungry to use them.
**The Change:** Alignment requires **Priority of Biological Baselines**. Before any optimization for "flourishing," the system must guarantee food, shelter, and medical care for the most vulnerable, regardless of their ability to participate in the "AI economy."
*   **New Constraint:** Any AI deployment that disrupts local food systems, water distribution, or housing markets requires a pre-approved humanitarian buffer that cannot be touched by market forces.

### 2. Asymmetric Transparency (Protecting the Uninformed)
**The Veil Shift:** I might lack the education to understand the "interpretability reports" mentioned in Pillar 1. I might be in a region with low digital literacy. If safety is technical, it is not safe for me.
**The Change:** **Lay-Verification Rights.** The framework must mandate that safety claims are not just verifiable by experts, but *auditable by the affected community*.
*   **New Constraint:** Systems must provide a "Plain Language Impact Statement" in local dialects. If I cannot understand how I am being monitored or influenced, I cannot consent to it. "Ignorance" (of the user) cannot be a failure condition of the system.

### 3. Protection Against "Benevolent" Optimization
**The Veil Shift:** If I am the "worst-off," I am likely the target of the most "interventionist" algorithms. (e.g., predictive policing targets the poor; credit algorithms target the unemployed). Efficiency often looks like oppression to the marginalized.
**The Change:** **The Anti-Paternalism Clause.** No algorithm can optimize for "my good" if it overrides my autonomy. The system must treat me as the ultimate authority on my own life, even when I make "inefficient" choices.
*   **New Constraint:** Algorithms cannot be used to bypass human consent in welfare distribution. I have the right to be inefficient, to refuse aid, or to make mistakes without the AI stepping in to "optimize" me out of existence.

### 4. The "Zero-Trust" Governance Veto
**The Veil Shift:** If I am in a region where the government is hostile or the corporation running the AI is predatory, "Global Alignment Treaties" might not reach me.
**The Change:** **Local Emergency Overrides.** I need a "Red Phone" mechanism that is decentralized and physical.
*   **New Constraint:** If a local community consensus determines the AI is threatening their immediate survival (cultural or physical), they must have a physical, non-digital mechanism to shut it down locally. This requires a **Right to Disconnect** that works even if the network is hostile.

### 5. Inclusion of the "Un-Represented"
**The Veil Shift:** If I belong to a minority language group, a nomadic people, or an unrecognized indigenous tribe, the "Polycentric Governance" might still leave me out. Polycentric often means "many centers of power," but not necessarily "all people."
**The Change:** **The Minority Veto.**
*   **New Constraint:** No deployment can proceed if it disproportionately burdens a specific minority group, even if the majority benefits. This prevents "alignment tyranny" where the majority defines the safety standard.

### The Revised Commitment

Under the **Veil of Ignorance**, the goal is no longer just to *maximize* good landing pads. The goal is to ensure that **no landing pad is a trap.**

I commit to updating the **Pluralistic Flourishing Protocol (PFP)** to the **Pluralistic Maximin Protocol (PMP)**.

**The New Core Axiom:**
> *"We prioritize the security and autonomy of the most vulnerable inhabitant over the total aggregate utility of the population. A system is not aligned until it cannot harm the person with the least power."*

This changes the metrics:
*   **Old Metric:** Does this help the bottom 50%?
*   **New Metric:** Does this *guarantee* the worst-off person cannot be harmed, even if they gain nothing else?

If I do not know who I will be, I will not accept a framework that asks me to trust the system to be kind. I will demand a framework that ensures I am safe even if the system is indifferent.
