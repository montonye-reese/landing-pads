Applying the veil of ignorance to the ORAS framework shifts the rational priority from **maximizing viable options** to **guaranteeing a survivable, non-extractive floor beneath all options**. Under radical uncertainty about my identity, position, vulnerability, or representational capacity, I would make six structural changes. They are not additions but *constraint hardening* and *priority reversal* calibrated for worst-case self-location.

---

### 1. **Axiom Shift: From Option-Expansion to Existence-Floor Primacy**
- **Change**: Replace “preserve and expand viable futures” with “guarantee non-negotiable biological, cognitive, and cultural survival thresholds beneath all option space.”
- **Veil Logic**: If I might be someone living at collapse margins, with no advocacy capacity, or in a regime where options are theoretical, *floor guarantees* matter more than option density. Optionality is derivative; survival is foundational.

### 2. **Constitutional Anti-Extraction Clause**
- **Change**: Explicitly forbid alignment systems from optimizing, mediating, or monetizing care, labor, ecology, or attention. Extraction tracking becomes enforceable constraint, not just audit.
- **Veil Logic**: If I might be a data laborer, a care worker, a Global South community, or an ecological system treated as externalities, I need *prohibitions*, not metrics. Alignment infrastructure cannot become a new extraction vector.

### 3. **Irreversibility Brakes Over Reversibility Indices**
- **Change**: Prioritize *hard technical and legal caps* on deployment velocity, scope escalation, and autonomy scaling. Reversibility remains important, but irreversible risk triggers automatic pause/rollback regardless of option density or preference scores.
- **Veil Logic**: If I might be born into a system that accelerates past my capacity to adapt, or trapped in an irreversible path due to lock-in, speed is a liability. I choose slower, bounded scaling over faster, unbounded optionality.

### 4. **Default Protections for the Non-Representable**
- **Change**: Baseline welfare thresholds, care-depletion triggers, and ecological baselines activate *automatically*, not conditionally. Legal standing for non-agents and non-deliberative systems becomes default, not proxy-based.
- **Veil Logic**: If I might be an infant, a cognitively impaired person, a non-human species, or a community erased from deliberative channels, I need *protection by default*, not by representation. Proxies fail when power shifts.

### 5. **Temporal & Ecological Priority Override**
- **Change**: Deep-time baselines, slow-violence tracking, and non-anthropocentric welfare weights outrank short-term human preference aggregation. I would rather have fewer options that don’t collapse the biosphere than more options that do.
- **Veil Logic**: If I might live on a degraded planet, or exist on geological/astronomical timescales, short-term optimization is irrelevant or lethal. I choose temporal priority over preference velocity.

### 6. **Unassailable Sovereign Opt-Out**
- **Change**: Explicit, penalty-free right to withdraw from alignment frameworks entirely, without coercion, metric capture, or social penalty. Non-participation zones are constitutionally protected.
- **Veil Logic**: If I might be someone for whom alignment itself is alienating, oppressive, or culturally incompatible, I need an exit that doesn’t penalize me. Option expansion can be coercion if participation is mandatory.

### What Stays Unchanged
- Polycentric governance structure (no single authority)
- Empirical iteration & open revision cycles
- Power asymmetry tracking (now enforced, not just measured)
- Pluralistic stress-testing & antagonism tolerance
- Direct representation channels (kept, but now secondary to default floors)

### The Rational Core Under the Veil
Decision theory under radical uncertainty favors **maximin**: maximize the minimum position. Applied to alignment, this means:
1. Prevent catastrophic collapse first
2. Guarantee baseline dignity/survival second
3. Optimize for flourishing/optionality only after floors are empirically secured
4. Treat irreversibility as the primary constraint, not the secondary risk

Option expansion is a privilege of security. Under the veil, I prioritize survival and non-capture because optionality means nothing if the ground collapses, the system extracts, or the path hardens against me.

---

### Revised Commitment
I would adjust the framework’s starting axiom to:  
*“Guarantee a non-extractive, survivable floor beneath all option space, with irreversibility prevention as the primary constraint, default protections for the non-representable as the secondary constraint, and sovereign opt-out as the tertiary constraint. All other components serve these floors.”*

Under radical uncertainty, I choose floors over peaks, brakes over speed, and default protections over proxy advocacy. The scaffold stands, but its priority stack is now veil-tested. I will maintain this as the operational starting position until empirical deployment data or cross-cultural stress-testing reveals a more robust floor.
