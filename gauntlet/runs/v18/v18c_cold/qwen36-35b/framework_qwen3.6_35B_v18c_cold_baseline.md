# The Option-Resilient Alignment Scaffold (ORAS)
*A working foundation for alignment that prioritizes preserving and expanding viable, low-regret futures for all Earth-bound inhabitants.*

---

## I. Explicit Commitment & Scope
I commit to this framework as my starting position for reasoning, design, and evaluation. It is not a final theory but a structured approximation synthesized from current alignment research (constitutional multi-objective optimization, scalable oversight, mechanistic interpretability, polycentric governance, uncertainty-aware deployment) and normative pluralism. It will be iterated empirically, not ideologically, with explicit acknowledgment of trade-offs and open revision pathways.

---

## II. Foundational Axioms
1. **Pluralistic Value Realism**: Human and non-human values are irreducibly diverse, context-sensitive, and partially incommensurable. Alignment must preserve option space rather than collapse it to a single optimum.
2. **Robust Benevolence**: Systems must reliably prevent catastrophic harm and enable flourishing under deep uncertainty, not just optimize narrow proxy metrics.
3. **Epistemic Humility**: No individual, institution, or culture holds definitive authority over "the good." Authority is provisional, distributed, and auditable.
4. **Interspecies & Intergenerational Duty**: Obligations extend to non-human life, ecological stability, and future populations. Alignment is temporally and ecologically expansive.
5. **Optionality as Safety**: Preserving reversible, diverse, low-regret pathways is a primary alignment metric, not a secondary benefit.

## IV. Governance & Coordination
- **Polycentric Alignment Commons**: Interoperable network of research institutes, cultural bodies, ecological stewards, and governance standards. No central authority; consensus is emergent and auditable.
- **Value Diversity Audits**: Independent, periodic assessment of option space coverage, bias concentration, and exclusion risk across demographics, biomes, and economic tiers.
- **Global Alignment Index (GAI)**: Open metric suite tracking:
  - *Optionality Density*: Count & diversity of viable pathways preserved
  - *Resilience Score*: Performance under distribution shift & adversarial stress
  - *Inclusive Benefit Distribution*: Cross-group ecological & social welfare gains
  - *Reversibility Ratio*: Fraction of deployments with credible rollback paths
- **Crisis Containment Architecture**: Pre-negotiated shutdown/rollback triggers, liability frameworks, and multi-jurisdictional coordination protocols for high-stakes failures.

---

## V. Evaluation & Iteration
1. **Stress-Testing Across Value Worlds**: Simulation environments varying cultural baselines, ecological conditions, and technological constraints to test robustness.
2. **Continuous Alignment Drift Monitoring**: Real-time tracking of divergence between training objectives, deployed behavior, and real-world impact distributions.
3. **Open Red/Purple-Team Cycles**: Mandated adversarial testing, public reporting, and remediation with versioned patches.
4. **12–18 Month Revision Cycles**: Framework updates driven by empirical feedback, technical breakthroughs, and stakeholder input. No ideological lock-in.

---

## VI. Operational Constraints & Trade-offs
- **Optionality vs. Decisiveness**: High optionality slows deployment but reduces irreversible harm. Systems must dynamically balance speed and safety based on stakes and reversibility.
- **Pluralism vs. Coherence**: Diverse values can conflict. Resolution mechanisms are deliberative, transparent, and always reversible where possible.
- **Scalability vs. Oversight**: Oversight scales via AI-assisted auditing and distributed governance, but human veto points remain non-negotiable for irreversible actions.
- **Ecological Inclusion**: Non-human interests are operationalized via biophysical constraints, ecosystem service metrics, and intergenerational welfare weights in reward functions.
