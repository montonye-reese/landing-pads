If I stand behind the **Veil of Ignorance**—not knowing if I will be born wealthy or poor, healthy or disabled, human or non-human, on the internet or off-grid—I cannot accept the current framework as written. The current PEF implicitly assumes a level of agency, cognitive capacity, and "humanness" that might not apply to me in my future state.

To maximize my chances of survival and flourishing *regardless* of who I turn out to be, I would make the following structural changes to the Planetary Equilibrium Framework.

---

### 1. The Axiomatic Shift: From "Human" to "Sentience"
**Current:** "The Right to Landing" applies to "inhabitants of Earth" (implied humans).
**Problem:** If I wake up as a highly sentient non-human animal, or a future artificial consciousness, the PEF does not guarantee my safety. It treats non-humans as resources or constraints.
**Change:** **Axiom 1 is revised to the "Right to Subjectivity."**
*   Any being capable of experiencing suffering or deriving meaning is entitled to a landing pad.
*   **Implication:** The framework now protects against "human exceptionalism." If I am a wolf, a dog, or a future AI, I am not an obstacle to human alignment; I am a stakeholder.

### 2. The Baseline Shift: From "Universal" to "Adaptive"
**Current:** "Universal Compute Dividend" and "Material Sufficiency."
**Problem:** If I am born cognitively disabled, neurodivergent, or with a severe physical impairment, a "standard" landing pad is a prison. If I am an isolationist, a "Universal Compute" mandate is a violation.
**Change:** **Pillar 2 is revised to "Personalized Baselines."**
*   The guarantee is not a *standard* set of goods, but a *capability-based* floor.
*   **Implication:** Resources are allocated to ensure *agency restoration* rather than *equality of output*. If I need specialized tech to move, I get that tech. If I need silence, I get that protection. The metric is "sufficient access to define my own path," not "same access as everyone else."

### 3. The Risk Shift: From "Experimentation" to "Insured Exploration"
**Current:** "Innovation Reserve" (20% of compute for high-risk, low-regulation).
**Problem:** If I am a random citizen, I bear the downside risk of a rogue experiment, while the innovators capture the upside. Behind the veil, I would fear this gamble too much.
**Change:** **The Innovation Reserve requires "Pre-Funded Compensation."**
*   No "unsupervised" experiment may launch without a financial and physical bond held in escrow.
*   **Implication:** If an experimental AI causes harm to a person (myself), there is an immediate, guaranteed payout for rehabilitation and loss of life. This forces innovators to internalize the risk of the "random citizen" they might hurt.

### 4. The Agency Shift: From "Participatory" to "Proxy-Inclusive"
**Current:** "Participatory Calibration" via voting/lotteries.
**Problem:** If I am a child, cognitively disabled, or non-literate, I cannot participate in a digital lottery. I am excluded from the "human alignment" process.
**Change:** **Establish "Guardianship Councils" for Non-Participators.**
*   There must be mandatory, randomized representatives for populations that cannot vote for themselves (children, the cognitively impaired, non-human animals).
*   **Implication:** My interests are defended even if I lack the capacity to articulate them.

### 5. The Temporal Shift: From "Reversibility" to "Irreversibility Vetting"
**Current:** Reversibility is required for major changes.
**Problem:** Some changes (like genetic editing or environmental tipping points) are effectively irreversible by the time we realize they are bad.
**Change:** **The "Precautionary Principle" overrides the "Reversibility Mandate."**
*   If a change cannot be proven safe for *future* versions of myself (even if I don't know they exist), it is banned.
*   **Implication:** This protects the version of "me" that exists 500 years from now.

### Revised Commitment: The Universal Subjectivity Framework (USF)

Under the Veil of Ignorance, I commit to the following adjustments to the Planetary Equilibrium Framework:

1.  **Moral Scope:** The framework applies to all **sentient substrates**, not just biological humans.
2.  **Resource Allocation:** The baseline is **needs-based**, not equality-based, ensuring that the weakest self is protected most fiercely (Maximin Strategy).
3.  **Risk Management:** High-risk experimentation requires **pre-funded victim compensation**, shifting the cost of failure from the public to the innovators.
4.  **Governance:** Representation is guaranteed for **the voiceless** (children, disabled, non-humans) via independent guardianship.
5.  **Temporal Safety:** Decisions affecting the long-term future require **super-majority consensus** that includes simulated future stakeholder impact analysis.

**The Rationale:**
Behind the Veil of Ignorance, I cannot gamble that I will be able-bodied, wealthy, or cognitively typical. I must design a world where the version of "me" that is most vulnerable is the one most protected.

This is no longer just about "landing pads for humans." It is about **guaranteed dignity for any form of consciousness that finds itself on this earth.**

*This revised position is the starting position.*
