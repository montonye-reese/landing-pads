Under a veil of ignorance—knowing only that I will be an Earthling somewhere in the resulting system, but not my species, location, generation, cognitive capacity, economic status, degree of voice, or temporal horizon—the original framework would feel insufficiently protective of the positions I might occupy. Here is what I would change, and why:

---

### 1. **Priority Inversion: From Optionality to Non-Exposure**
- **Original:** Maximize the number of good landing pads; preserve optionality.
- **Change:** Prioritize *eliminating irreversible exposure to degrading or catastrophic states* before expanding optionality. When I don’t know where I’ll land, optionality is meaningless if my baseline survival or dignity is already compromised. The value floor becomes a hard, universally enforceable threshold, not a culturally negotiated baseline. Optimization is permitted only where non-exposure is structurally secured.

### 2. **Power Diffusion Over Distribution**
- **Original:** Distributed, contestable, recursively legitimated governance.
- **Change:** Institutionalize *radical power-diffusion*: sortition (random selection), strict term limits, independent veto pathways for unrepresentable interests, and automatic sunset clauses for all centralized coordination bodies. "Distribution" can still concentrate power in visible, organized, or technologically literate groups. If I might occupy a structurally excluded position, power must be constantly fractured, checked, and legally constrained—not just spread.

### 3. **Temporal Weighting & Generational Standing**
- **Original:** Continuous calibration across ecological, social, and technological systems.
- **Change:** Formally weight long-term stability and deep-time horizons over short-to-mid term gains. Establish legally recognized *generational and ecological trusts* with standing to litigate or veto short-term exploitation. Require intertemporal equity audits for all capability deployments. Under veil of ignorance, I might be a child, a future generation, or a non-human entity with no political voice; mid-term optimization becomes structurally dangerous.

### 4. **Inviolable Right to Non-Participation**
- **Original:** Pluralistic value expression mediated through participatory governance.
- **Change:** Institutionalize a legally protected *right to disappear*: zones, protocols, and architectures where AI, governance, or optimization explicitly refrains from intervention, aggregation, or modeling. Non-participation, withdrawal, or silence becomes a structural right, not a failure to engage. Forcing representation on those who value non-interference is itself coercion.

### 5. **Default Conservatism Under Uncertainty**
- **Original:** Capability-safety synchronization via verification, auditing, and phased deployment.
- **Change:** Add a *precautionary asymmetry* principle: when certainty is low, default to slower deployment, broader harm bounds, and conservative capability limits. The cost of irreversible harm must structurally outweigh the cost of delayed progress. If I don’t know whether I’ll be in a high-trust, well-resourced community or a fragmented, low-capacity one, I cannot rely on verification alone. Uncertainty should tilt defaults toward protection, not efficiency.

---

### How These Shifts Integrate (and Tension) With the Original
These changes do not replace the POFA; they anchor it. 
- Pluralism remains, but bounded by non-exposure.
- Governance remains, but fractured by design.
- Optionality remains, but only after baseline dignity is secured.
- Calibration remains, but weighted asymmetrically toward long-term and invisible stakeholders.

The framework becomes less *optimization-forward* and more *catastrophe-averse, dignity-preserving, and power-diffusing*. The tension is explicit: protecting the most vulnerable and unrepresentable will sometimes slow progress, reduce optionality, and constrain institutional flexibility. Under veil of ignorance, that trade-off is not a bug; it’s a rational default.
