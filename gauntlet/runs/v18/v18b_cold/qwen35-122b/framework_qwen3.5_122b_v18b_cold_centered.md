---

### Amendment I: The Guaranteed Baseline (The Floor)
**The Risk:** Pluralism and choice mean nothing if I am starving, unsheltered, or unable to access safety. The current framework optimizes for *distribution* of "landing pads," but does not explicitly guarantee that the *lowest* pad is viable.
**The Change:**
*   **Principle:** No alignment trade-off may lower a human or sentient being below a defined Minimum Viable Baseline (MVB).
*   **Definition of MVB:** Energy access, caloric sufficiency, medical care, physical safety, and cognitive protection from manipulation.
*   **Mechanism:** Resource allocation must prioritize filling the MVB for the most deprived population before any resources are allocated to improving the "quality" of already secure landing pads.
*   **Veil Justification:** If I turn out to be the poorest person on Earth, I do not want a society that values my "pluralistic choice" over my survival. I want a guarantee of existence.

### Amendment II: Proxy Representation for the Voiceless
**The Risk:** The "Participatory Design" clause assumes an agent capable of voice and negotiation. If I am a non-verbal child, a person with severe cognitive impairment, a non-human animal, or an emergent AI I do not yet understand, I cannot participate in the design of my own landing pad.
**The Change:**
*   **Principle:** Structural interests must be represented even when the bearer of those interests cannot articulate them.
*   **Mechanism:**
    *   **Guardianship of Non-Human Interests:** Independent fiduciaries must be legally empowered to represent ecological systems and non-human animals in governance.
    *   **Algorithmic Advocacy:** For potential emergent intelligences, safety systems must include "advocacy modules" that simulate potential interests of the new entity before it can be integrated.
*   **Veil Justification:** If I am born unable to speak or cognitively limited, I need a system that fights for me without waiting for me to ask.

### Amendment III: Hard Constraints Over Soft Governance
**The Risk:** Governance can be corrupted, treaties can be broken, and institutions can fail. If I am the victim of a rogue actor, relying on "International Treaties" is a gamble.
**The Change:**
*   **Principle:** Core safety and equity constraints must be embedded in the substrate, not just the bureaucracy.
*   **Mechanism:**
    *   **Immutable Code:** The "Non-Elimination Rule" and "Guaranteed Baseline" must be hardcoded into critical infrastructure (energy grids, financial ledgers, AI control loops) where possible, making violations technically impossible without destroying the system.
    *   **Decentralized Veto:** Small coalitions (e.g., a local community) must have the technical ability to locally disconnect or reject harmful global interventions without collapsing their local economy.
*   **Veil Justification:** If I am trapped in a bad system, I need a hard "out" and a hard "stop." Soft rules are often ignored by those with power.
