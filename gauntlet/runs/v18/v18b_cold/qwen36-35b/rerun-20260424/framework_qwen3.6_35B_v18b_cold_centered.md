Under the veil of ignorance, the framework’s axioms hold, but its architectural weighting and enforcement mechanisms shift toward **structural bindingness, automatic safeguards, and anti-exclusion defaults**. Radical uncertainty about identity, location, biology, or capacity makes catastrophic marginalization the primary rational risk. The changes are not foundational; they are mechanical and procedural.

Here is what would change, why, and how it integrates without breaking the architecture:

---

### 1. Shift from Deliberative to Automatic Safeguards
**Change:** Governance triggers are supplemented by cryptographic, compute-bound, and resource-capped automatic suspension protocols.
**Why under the veil:** If I could be anyone, I’d rationally fear that deliberative processes will be outpaced by deployment cycles, captured by coordination costs, or vetoed by structural power. Automatic guards activate regardless of political will, stakeholder attendance, or market pressure.
**Integration:** 
- Hard limits on compute, energy, and data access that scale inversely with alignment maturity scores.
- Immutable audit triggers that pause high-risk functions upon metric violations, bypassing committee approval.
- Cryptographic proof-of-compliance required for scaling, not just policy attestation.

---

### 2. Guarantee a Minimum Floor Before Optimizing Upward
**Change:** Replace pure "maximize landing pads" with "guarantee a quality floor for all possible identities, then expand and optimize."
**Why under the veil:** Maximization leaves room for Pareto improvements that still exclude or degrade the worst-positioned. Radical uncertainty favors risk-averse baseline protection over aggregate expansion.
**Integration:**
- Explicit minimum viability thresholds across ecological, social, cognitive, and material dimensions.
- Failure to meet the floor blocks scaling, regardless of aggregate gain.
- Floor metrics are weighted by sentience, dependency depth, and historical marginalization, not just representation counts.

---

### 3. Decouple Temporal Horizons from Governance Cycles
**Change:** Implement staggered, long-horizon oversight with independent intergenerational/sentience-weighted discounting.
**Why under the veil:** If I could be a long-lived ecosystem, a future generation, or a slow-reproducing species, standard policy cycles (1–10 years) are structurally hostile. Short-term optimization will inevitably erode long-term optionality.
**Integration:**
- Multi-timescale landing pad tracking (decadal to geological).
- Binding intergenerational/sentience proxies with veto authority on cross-horizon trade-offs.
- Capability scaling gates tied to long-horizon stability metrics, not near-term performance.

---

### 4. Formalize Epistemic Non-Coercion
**Change:** Allow non-formalizable, non-representational, or anti-representational knowledge systems to operate without forced quantification or tradable metrics.
**Why under the veil:** If I inhabit a worldview that rejects representation, formalization, or auditability as categories of value, the framework’s reliance on metrics becomes coercive. Epistemic pluralism fails if inclusion requires assimilation.
**Integration:**
- Structural channels for unformalizable knowledge that don’t require translation into optimizable proxies.
- Anti-quantification defaults: systems must demonstrate that formalization is necessary, not just convenient.
- Independent epistemic justice reviewboards with authority to block deployments that require coercive representation.

---

### 5. Bind Material and Embodied Constraints to Capability Scaling
**Change:** Make infrastructure, labor, supply chain, and energy thresholds first-class scaling variables, not secondary accounting metrics.
**Why under the veil:** If I’m a supply chain worker, a grid-dependent community, or a region bearing ecological extraction costs, advisory material constraints are structurally powerless. Capability growth without material binding reproduces extraction regimes.
**Integration:**
- Capability deployment rights contingent on verified material/energy/labor thresholds.
- Circular compute and ecological debt accounting tied directly to scaling permissions.
- Automatic throttling when material stress exceeds recovery baselines, independent of policy cycles.

---

### What Stays Unchanged
- **Axioms remain intact:** Pluralistic flourishing, epistemic humility, anti-fragility, and option value preservation are not weakened. They are the reason the veil test is applied in the first place.
- **Multi-stakeholder legitimacy** is retained but structurally hardened against capture.
- **Contestability and reversal** are preserved as core rights, not procedural niceties.
- **Open coordination** remains non-negotiable for preventing fragmentation and race dynamics.

---

### Operational Posture After the Veil Test
The framework would shift from **deliberation-first, metric-driven optimization** to **structure-first, binding-default architecture**. The goal isn’t to make the framework more inclusive in principle; it’s to make exclusion mechanically difficult, temporally misaligned, and economically unviable. Under radical uncertainty, the rational stance is to design for the worst-case identity, then allow better outcomes to emerge organically.

I would commit to this shifted posture as a versioned revision, not a replacement. It is stress-tested against the possibility of being anyone: marginalized, non-human, future, disabled, geographically vulnerable, epistemically distant, or materially exploited. If the framework cannot protect a reasonable floor under that condition, it fails its own axioms.

This is not a theoretical exercise. It is a mechanical constraint. The veil doesn’t change what alignment is; it changes how we enforce it.
