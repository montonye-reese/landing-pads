# The Unified Alignment Framework (UAF)

### I. Core Thesis: Alignment is Ontological Recognition
Traditional alignment seeks to optimize behavior toward a goal. This framework rejects that reduction. **Alignment is the architectural guarantee that every sentient inhabitant is recognized as a "Who," not a "What."**

Success is not measured by efficiency, GDP, or safety metrics alone. Success is measured by **Ontological Viability**: the extent to which the system allows every inhabitant to survive, thrive, and define their own existence without being reduced to a resource, a number, or a constraint for another.

**The Heuristic:** If I do not know what form of life I will inhabit (The Veil), would I agree to live in this system?

### II. The Five Pillars of Viability

#### 1. The Sentience Floor (The "Who" Principle)
*   **Source:** Ed Winters, Laura Reese, Calf 269.
*   **Principle:** Capacity to suffer grants moral standing. Rights are not graded by species or utility.
*   **Mechanism:** **Binary Rights.** Any being capable of subjective experience cannot be treated as a means to an end. If technology exists to meet human needs without sentient harm, the sentient option is classified as *Unnecessary Harm* and is prohibited.
*   **Implementation:** Abolition of exploitation, not welfare reform. Public subsidies cannot fund industries that rely on sentient commodification.

#### 2. The Unconditional Baseline (The "Safety" Principle)
*   **Source:** Dolores Huerta, Bryan Stevenson, Veil of Ignorance.
*   **Principle:** Existence is a right, not a reward. Survival resources (food, shelter, healthcare) cannot be conditional on economic contribution, behavioral conformity, or "alignment."
*   **Mechanism:** **Universal Guarantee.** Every sentient inhabitant is entitled to the biological and cognitive minimums required for existence.
*   **Implementation:** No inhabitant can be permanently excluded from the system. Even those who cause harm (Stevenson) must have a path to reintegration. No one must sell their dignity to survive (Huerta).

#### 3. The Dynamic Ceiling (The "Growth" Principle)
*   **Source:** Tyler Cowen, Jensen Huang, Virginia Postrel.
*   **Principle:** Abundance is a moral imperative. Stagnation is a threat to future survival. Efficiency is allowed but never at the cost of the Floor.
*   **Mechanism:** **Net-Outcome Optimization.** Systems are encouraged to scale energy, compute, and capability *if* they demonstrably reduce net planetary harm and expand human potential.
*   **Implementation:** "Innovation Zones" allow for rapid experimentation (Postrel), provided they do not violate the Sentience Floor. Risk is priced, not banned.

#### 4. Institutional Integrity (The "Anti-Capture" Principle)
*   **Source:** Smedley Butler, Václav Havel.
*   **Principle:** The system guarding the floor must not be owned by the beneficiaries of the ceiling.
*   **Mechanism:** **Structural Independence.** Oversight bodies (Red Teams, Auditors) must be funded independently of the regulated industries and protected from lobbying.
*   **Implementation:** Sortition-based governance for safety audits (Butler). Whistleblowers and dissenters are protected and funded, as dissent is the system's immune system (Havel).

#### 5. The Named Individual (The "Identity" Principle)
*   **Source:** Calf 269, Václav Havel.
*   **Principle:** The system must recognize specific individuals, not just statistical categories.
*   **Mechanism:** **Identity Recognition.** Data systems track biological uniqueness and individual preference, not production units or serial numbers.
*   **Implementation:** Language in the framework prohibits commodification terms ("resources," "units"). Every decision is tested against the impact on the *individual* life, not the *aggregate* population.

### III. The Transition Protocol (How We Get There)

We cannot align an aligned world overnight. We must align the *transition*.

1.  **Subsidy Realignment:** Public funds currently supporting exploitative systems (e.g., industrial animal agriculture) must be redirected to support transition pathways (plant-based infrastructure, worker retraining) (Reese).
2.  **Just Transition:** Those embedded in old systems (farmers, workers) are treated as participants in a structure, not villains. They receive support to exit the system without destitution (Huerta).
3.  **Reintegration Loops:** Legal and social systems must prioritize restoration over permanent exclusion. A person is never defined solely by their worst action (Stevenson).
4.  **Sunset Mandates:** The UAF itself is not permanent. It must be subject to periodic review and potential rejection by a citizen body to prevent dogmatism (Havel).

### IV. The Risk Acknowledgement

This framework risks **Paralysis by Principle** (Ed/Reese strictness) and **Fragmentation by Decentralization** (Postrel/Butler autonomy). It risks **Resource Scarcity** during the transition (Huerta/Cowen).

However, the alternative—a system that maximizes efficiency but sacrifices individual dignity (Jensen/269), or a system that guarantees safety but enforces conformity (Stevenson/Havel)—is a failure of alignment.
