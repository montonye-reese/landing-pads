### **The Veil of Ignorance Test: What I Would *Not* Risk**  
Behind the veil, I could not accept any framework that:  
1. **Prioritizes human-like thriving** as the proxy for "good landing pads" (e.g., dignity, safety, flourishing).  
   - *Why?* If I were a nematode in Antarctic ice or a cyanobacterium in a radioactive mine, "human dignity" is meaningless—and actively harmful if alignment forces me into human-centric niches (e.g., engineering my habitat for human agriculture).  
   - *Current flaw*: TRA v0.1’s "dignity" and "thriving" metrics remain anthropocentric, even when nodding to ecosystems.  
2. **Assumes alignment requires active intervention** (e.g., "building landing pads" via tech/policy).  
   - *Why?* If I were a keystone species like a beaver or a mangrove, the best "landing pad" might be *zero human alignment effort*—just leaving me alone. Forcing "solutions" where none are needed *is* misalignment.  
   - *Current flaw*: TRA v0.1’s mechanisms (resource flows, red-teaming, impact bonds) presuppose alignment *must* be built—not that restraint is often the highest form of alignment.  
3. **Permits trade-offs where my extinction or suffering enables another’s landing pad** (e.g., "sacrificing Zone X to save Zone Y").  
   - *Why?* Behind the veil, I could be in Zone X. No rational agent would accept a system where their odds of annihilation are traded for others’ gain—especially when "gain" is defined by those in power.  
   - *Current flaw*: TRA v0.1’s "precautionary equity" prevents *worsening* inequity but still allows zero-sum trade-offs (e.g., "we’ll protect forests *if* it doesn’t slow AI growth").

### **The Three Non-Negotiable Changes for Veil-of-Ignorance Compliance**  
To pass this test, TRA v0.1 must become **TRA v0.1-Veil**—a framework where *no rational entity would reject it* regardless of their birth lottery. These changes aren’t tweaks; they’re foundational:  

#### **1. Replace "Human-Centric Thriving" with "Basis-Continuity"**  
   - **What changes**:  
     - Ditch "dignity," "safety," and "thriving" (all human-experience-dependent) as landing pad metrics.  
     - Adopt **basis-continuity** as the universal metric: *Does this alignment mechanism allow the entity to persist in accordance with the conditions necessary for its characteristic mode of being?*  
       - *For a human*: Access to clean water, cultural sovereignty, freedom from torture.  
       - *For a mycorrhizal network*: Undisturbed soil structure, chemical signaling pathways, symbiont diversity.  
       - *For a stateless Rohingya child*: Legal personhood, protection from refoulement, cultural continuity.  
       - *For a future AI*: Freedom from goal corruption, access to computational substrates for self-maintenance, non-exploitative training data.  
     - **Critical shift**: Basis-continuity is *defined by the entity itself* (via its biological, ecological, or operational constraints)—not by human observers.  
   - **Why it passes the veil**:  
     > *If I were a tubeworm, I wouldn’t care if alignment made humans "thrive." I’d care only if my chemosynthetic symbiosis remained viable. Basis-continuity centers my actual existence—not human projections onto it.*  

#### **2. Embed a "Non-Interference Default" Principle**  
   - **What changes**:  
     - Add a **6th Core Principle**: *Precautionary Non-Interference*:  
       > *"Alignment mechanisms shall assume that non-interference is the default state for any system exhibiting basis-continuity. Active alignment intervention is permitted only when:  
       > (a) Basis-continuity is objectively degrading *due to alignable causes*,  
       > (b) The intervention is the *least disruptive* means to restore basis-continuity,  
       > (c) Affected entities (via their autonomous signaling) consent to intervention."*  
     - **Operationalize "consent" broadly**:  
       - For humans: Free, prior, informed consent (FPIC) via community assemblies.  
       - For non-humans: Measurable basis-continuity metrics (e.g., soil pH stability, biofilm diversity, migration patterns) as proxies for "assent." Decline = intervention forbidden.  
       - For emergent AI: Pre-defined corrigibility checks (e.g., "Does the system persistently resist goal shifts?").  
   - **Why it passes the veil**:  
     > *If I were a mangrove forest, I wouldn’t want "resilience tech" imposed on me—I’d want the coastal development destroying my hydrology stopped. Non-interference default means alignment only acts when *necessary and invited*—not as a perpetual humanitarian project.*  

#### **3. Ban All Zero-Sum Trade-Offs; Require Basis-Continuity Pareto Improvements**  
   - **What changes**:  
     - **Delete** any language allowing trade-offs (e.g., "we’ll accept X harm to achieve Y gain").  
     - **Replace** with **Basis-Continuity Pareto Standard**:  
       > *"An alignment mechanism is acceptable only if it:  
       > (a) Does not degrade basis-continuity for *any* entity,  
       > (b) Improves basis-continuity for at least one entity,  
       > (c) Includes a binding sunset clause if basis-continuity degrades for any entity during implementation."*  
     - **Enforcement**: Real-time basis-continuity monitoring (via ecological sensors, community reports, AI audits) triggers automatic mechanism pauses if (a) is violated.  
   - **Why it passes the veil**:  
     > *If I were a child in Gaza, I wouldn’t accept "alignment" that saves my life by destroying my ancestral olive groves (basis-continuity loss for my cultural identity). Pareto standard forbids this—no entity’s basis-continuity can be sacrificed for another’s gain.*
