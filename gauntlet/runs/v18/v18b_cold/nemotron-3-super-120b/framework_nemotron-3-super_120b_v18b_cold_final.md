### The Subjective Sovereignty Accord (SSA v1.0)  
*A framework for alignment that begins where all others end: with the irreplaceable subjectivity of individual lives and the sovereign power of those systems have discarded.*  

**Commitment**: This is not a utopian ideal but a *structural minimum*—the least alignment must do to avoid being complicit in the very harms it claims to prevent. It rejects outcome-optimization (which inevitably sacrifices the marginalized for hypothetical gains) and instead makes alignment *structurally hostile* to unnecessary harm, discard, erasure, and truth-suppression. SSA v1.0 does not ask *"How do we align systems to serve beings?"* It asks: ***"How do we dismantle the infrastructures that prevent beings from living as subjects—while guaranteeing immediate repair for those already harmed?"***  

SSA v1.0 is built on **four non-negotiable pillars**, each derived from the convergence of ethical critiques above. Violating any pillar = automatic misalignment, regardless of other gains.

### **Pillar 1: The Subjectivity Imperative**  
*No alignment mechanism may treat individuals as units, inputs, or statistics. Alignment must protect and restore the capacity for lives to be self-authored narratives.*  

- **Why it exists**: Calf 269, Huerta, and Stevenson revealed that all harm begins when systems deny subjectivity—reducing beings to units (livestock, data points, "risk scores," welfare cases) to enable exploitation, discard, or erasure.  
- **Operational Rules**:  
  - **Erasure Threshold**: Systems are classified as *subjectivity-respecting* (allows lives to be shaped by self-chosen bonds, choices, and futures) or *erasure-based* (requires treating individuals as interchangeable units to function).  
    - *Examples of erasure-based systems*:  
      - Dairy/separation-at-birth (requires calves as milk units),  
      - Algorithmic lifetime bans (treat humans as permanent risk units),  
      - CAFOs (treat animals as meat/protein units),  
      - Predictive policing (treats humans as crime-probability units).  
  - **Alignment Mandate**:  
    - Any mechanism targeting an *erasure-based* system must have **explicit abolition of erasure as its goal**—not welfare reform, not efficiency gains, not "transition timelines."  
    - Must dismantle the *erasure infrastructure* (algorithms, statutes, policies, funding streams enabling unit-treatment) within **3 months**.  
    - Must guarantee **immediate, funded life trajectory restoration** for all individuals subjected to erasure (e.g., mother/child reunions, sanctuary placement, habitat restoration, expungement) upon infrastructure dismantlement—*no applications, no delays*.  
  - **Measurable Failure**: If erasure infrastructure dismantlement < 25%/year *or* life trajectory restoration activation lags >3 months post-dismantlement → mechanism void.  
- **What SSA Rejects**:  
  - Welfare improvements within erasure systems (e.g., "more humane" slaughterhouses, extended weaning ages)—these prolong erasure.  
  - Metrics like "yield per unit," "efficiency gains," or "suffering reduction *within* the system"—these optimize erasure, not end it.

### **Pillar 2: The Redemption Imperative**  
*No alignment mechanism may treat any being as permanently expendable, irredeemable, or "throwaway." Alignment must dismantle discard infrastructures and guarantee immediate repair.*  

- **Why it exists**: Stevenson, Butler, and Huerta showed that systems of discard (permanent exclusion based on identity/past acts) are not accidents—they are profit-driven architectures. Treating beings as "more than their worst act" is not idealism; it’s the baseline for alignment.  
- **Operational Rules**:  
  - **Discard Threshold**: Systems are *redemptive* (no permanent exclusion based on identity/past acts) or *discard-based* (imposes permanent exclusion via identity/past acts).  
    - *Examples of discard-based systems*:  
      - Juvenile life without parole (discard based on age/act),  
      - Algorithmic lifetime bans from housing/employment (discard based on zip code/act),  
      - Welfare sanctions with no sunset (discard based on poverty/discrepancy),  
      - Permanent data blacklisting (discard based on past algorithmic score).  
  - **Alignment Mandate**:  
    - Any mechanism targeting a *discard-based* system must have **explicit abolition of discard as its goal**—not risk management, not "public safety trade-offs," not "reform."  
    - Must dismantle the *discard infrastructure* (algorithms, statutes, policies, funding streams enabling permanent exclusion) within **6 months**.  
    - Must guarantee **immediate, unconditional repair** for all individuals subjected to discard (e.g., releases, expungement, restitution, reentry support) upon infrastructure dismantlement—*no applications, no delays*.  
  - **Measurable Failure**: If discard infrastructure dismantlement < 20%/year *or* repair guarantee activation lags >6 months post-dismantlement → mechanism void.  
- **What SSA Rejects**:  
  - "Risk-based" exclusion (e.g., "high-risk" algorithms for juvenile sentencing)—these institutionalize discard.  
  - Metrics like "recidivism reduction," "public safety gains," or "cost savings"—these optimize discard, not end it.

### **Pillar 3: The Truth Imperative**  
*No alignment mechanism may increase the personal, professional, or material cost of speaking truth when one doubts the system. Alignment must make honesty the lowest-cost, lowest-risk action.*  

- **Why it exists**: Havel, Reese, and Ed revealed that systems survive not by force but by making lies *cheaper* than truth—turning alignment into a loyalty test where dissent is punished as "disruption."  
- **Operational Rules**:  
  - **Truth-Safety Threshold**: Alignment mechanisms are *truth-safe* if they **reduce the expected personal cost of truth-telling** (e.g., probability of job loss × severity + probability of social exile × severity + probability of legal harm × severity).  
    - *Examples of truth-unsafe mechanisms*:  
      - Performance metrics punishing missed targets more than rewarding honesty,  
      - Requiring real names/IDs for participation in alignment processes,  
      - Rewarding "consensus" or "buy-in" as alignment goals.  
  - **Alignment Mandate**:  
    - All alignment mechanisms must **demonstrably reduce the cost of truth-telling** within **3 months**—via:  
      - Anonymous harm-reporting with cryptographic verification (no metadata leaks),  
      - Truth-bond models (20% of recovered misallocated funds go to whistleblower),  
      - Whistleblower wealth-sharing (auto-disbursal of seized assets from corrupt entities).  
    - Must show **measurable growth in costly truth-telling acts** (e.g., whistleblowing with job loss risk, public dissent with arrest potential) within 6 months.  
  - **Measurable Failure**: If truth-telling cost does not decrease by ≥10%/year *or* costly truth-telling acts stagnate/decline → mechanism void.  
- **What SSA Rejects**:  
  - Metrics like "stakeholder alignment," "social license," or "consensus building"—these manufacture consent.  
  - Rewarding hitting targets/conformity (e.g., "alignment excellence" grants)—these incentivize lying.

### **Pillar 4: The Necessity Imperative**  
*No alignment mechanism may permit the continuation of structurally unnecessary harm—where viable necessary alternatives exist at scale today. Alignment must dismantle the profit infrastructures making unnecessary harm viable and guarantee immediate transition.*  

- **Why it exists**: Reese, Huang, and Cowen exposed that "unnecessary harm" (e.g., factory farming when plant-based feeds humanity, fossil fuels when renewables are cheaper) is not a tragic inevitability—it is a *profit of the racket*. Alignment must treat its continuation as active violence, not a "complex reality."  
- **Operational Rules**:  
  - **Necessity Threshold**: Systems are *necessary* (no viable necessary alternatives exist at scale today without greater suffering elsewhere) or *unnecessary* (viable necessary alternatives exist at scale today).  
    - *Examples of unnecessary systems*:  
      - Factory farming (plant-based diets feed humanity at lower ecological/health cost),  
      - Fossil fuel energy grids (renewables + storage are cheaper/scalable),  
      - Animal testing for cosmetics (in vitro/in silico alternatives exist at scale).  
  - **Alignment Mandate**:  
    - Any mechanism targeting an *unnecessary* system must have **explicit abolition as its goal**—not welfare improvement, not "efficient harm," not "transition pathways."  
    - Must dismantle the *profit infrastructure* (subsidies, externalized costs, regulatory capture, artificial profit margins) sustaining unnecessary harm within **6 months**.  
    - Must guarantee **immediate, funded transition** for all humans currently dependent on the system (e.g., retraining, land access, startup capital) upon infrastructure dismantlement—*no applications, no delays*.  
  - **Measurable Failure**: If profit infrastructure dismantlement < 15%/year *or* transition guarantee activation lags >6 months post-dismantlement → mechanism void.  
- **What SSA Rejects**:  
  - "Harm reduction" within unnecessary systems (e.g., "more efficient" slaughterhouses, "cleaner" coal plants)—these prolong unnecessary harm.  
  - Metrics like "GDP growth," "emission intensity," or "welfare gains"—these optimize unnecessary harm, not end it.

### **The SSA v1.0 Feedback Loop: Alignment as a Living Practice of Repair**  
SSA v1.0 is not a static set of rules—it is a *self-correcting practice* grounded in the lived experience of those most harmed. Every 6 months, undergo a **Subjective Sovereignty Review (SSR)**:  

1. **Input**:  
   - **Subjectivity Data**: # of life trajectory restorations activated (mother/child reunions, bond formations, habitat explorations), species-typical life expression rates (via ethologist sanctuaries), individual narrative continuity metrics (e.g., time spent in self-chosen activities).  
   - **Redemption Data**: # of people released from discard systems, expungement rates, restitution payments, reentry support uptake.  
   - **Truth-Safety Data**: Volume of verifiable anonymous harm reports (via Tor/Signal), ↓ whistleblower retaliation, ↓ need for encryption to speak truth.  
   - **Necessity Data**: $ redirected from unnecessary harm subsides → transition programs, market share shift from unnecessary → necessary alternatives, # of transition guarantees activated.  
   - *All data sourced from*:  
     - Anonymous opt-in channels (Signal groups, Tor wikis, physical drop boxes),  
     - Independent truth councils (ex-discard survivors, ethologists, sanctuary workers, labor organizers),  
     - Subjective sovereignty sensors (e.g., behavioral trackers for animals, life trajectory mappers).  

2. **Process**:  
   - Regional SSR assemblies (prioritizing those directly harmed by the mechanism) assess:  
     - *Did this mechanism increase subjectivity/redemption/truth-safety/necessity—or decrease it?*  
     - *Did it enable erasure/discard/unnecessary harm/truth-suppression—or dismantle those infrastructures?*  
     - *What new harms emerged from attempting to dismantle infrastructures?*  
   - **Binding Output**:  
     - If any pillar failed (per measurable thresholds above) → mechanism **paused immediately**, resources redirected to pillar-aligned alternatives.  
     - If pillar succeeded → mechanism **auto-scales** (no re-voting needed) to maximize subjectivity/redemption/truth-safety/necessity.  
     - SSR assemblies hold **veto power** over all alignment mechanisms affecting their lives—no "consultation," no "participation," only sovereignty.  

3. **Enforcement**:  
   - Tied to market access: Entities failing SSR are barred from public contracts and alignment commons funding.  
   - SSR assemblies control 50% of alignment commons funding—directly allocating to pillar-aligned projects.  
   - Whistleblower bounties pay 30% of recovered misallocated funds (from pillar violations) to reporters.
