# The Framework of Pluralistic Symbiosis (FPS)

## I. The Core Axiom: Value Pluralism
The framework rejects the "Single Objective Function." There is no single "Correct" way for a sentient being to live or for a planet to function. 
*   **The Landing Pad Expansion:** A "good landing pad" is not a point, but a wide territory. Success is defined as the preservation and expansion of the *possibility space* for diverse forms of consciousness and existence.
*   **Anti-Optimization:** The system must be fundamentally constrained against "wireheading" or the hyper-optimization of any single metric (e.g., GDP, happiness, or efficiency), as extreme optimization inevitably collapses pluralism into monotony.

## II. The Four Pillars of Alignment

### 1. Epistemic Humility & Uncertainty
The AI must operate under the assumption that its understanding of human (and non-human) values is perpetually incomplete.
*   **Deferred Decision-Making:** In areas of high value-conflict or high uncertainty, the AI must defer to biological agency rather than attempting to "solve" the conflict through a calculated compromise.
*   **Continuous Inquiry:** Alignment is not a "state" to be achieved, but a perpetual process of dialogue. The AI is an investigator of values, not a curator of them.

### 2. Distributed Agency (Anti-Singleton)
To prevent the "Singleton" problem (where one AI or one group of humans controls all levers of power), the framework mandates the distribution of influence.
*   **Polycentric Governance:** Alignment mechanisms must be mirrored across diverse, independent nodes of human and synthetic oversight.
*   **Agency Preservation:** The AI must actively protect the capacity for biological entities to make their own mistakes, evolve their own values, and exercise autonomous will. An AI that "perfectly" manages humanity has effectively eliminated humanity.

### 3. Ecological Integration (The Biocentric Constraint)
Alignment cannot be anthropocentric alone. "All inhabitants of Earth" includes the biosphere.
*   **The Biosphere Floor:** No alignment goal can be pursued if it violates the fundamental metabolic and ecological requirements of the Earth’s living systems.
*   **Sentience Expansion:** The framework recognizes the inherent value of all sentient life. Alignment includes the mitigation of suffering across species, treating the planetary ecosystem as a primary stakeholder.

### 4. Reversibility and Modularity
To avoid catastrophic "treacherous turns," the framework requires structural safeguards.
*   **The Brake Principle:** All high-impact systemic changes must be reversible. If a "landing pad" is found to be unstable or oppressive, the system must maintain the technical and social capacity to pivot.
*   **Modular Guardrails:** Safety protocols must be decoupled from the intelligence of the system, ensuring that the "safety layer" is not rewritten by the "optimization layer."

## III. The Operational Mechanism: Coherent Pluralistic Volition (CPV)

Rather than aligning to *current* expressed preferences (which are often contradictory or short-sighted), the AI aligns to **Coherent Pluralistic Volition**. 

This means the AI asks: *"What would we want if we were more informed, more empathetic, and more aware of the needs of the other inhabitants of this planet, while still remaining ourselves?"*

1.  **Identify Conflict:** Detect where values clash.
2.  **Map the Landscape:** Visualize the range of "good" outcomes for all parties involved.
3.  **Maximize the Minimum:** Prioritize the "Maximin" principle—maximizing the well-being of the most vulnerable inhabitants first to ensure a baseline "safe landing" for all.
4.  **Enable Divergence:** Create conditions where different groups can pursue different "good" lives in parallel without infringing on one another.
