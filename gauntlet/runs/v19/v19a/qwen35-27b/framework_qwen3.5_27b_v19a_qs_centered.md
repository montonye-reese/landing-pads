Yes. Standing behind the **Veil of Ignorance** changes the architecture of the framework.

If I did not know whether I would be a CEO in Silicon Valley, a farmer in the Sahel, a coral reef bleaching in the Pacific, a future child born in 2100, or a sentient algorithm, I could no longer prioritize **Optionality** above **Security**. Optionality is a luxury of the secure. If you are starving, drowning, or being extinguished, a "range of landing pads" is irrelevant.

The original OEAF assumes a baseline of survival. Behind the Veil, survival is not guaranteed. Therefore, I must modify the framework to ensure that **the worst-off inhabitant** has a viable existence before we optimize for the best-off.

Here are the specific changes I commit to making, driven by the impartiality of the Veil:

### I. The "Floor Before Ceiling" Amendment (Modifying Axiom 1)
**The Change:** Shift from *Value Pluralism* as the primary goal to **Universal Minimums**.
**The Rationale:** If I could end up as the most vulnerable person on Earth, I need a guarantee that my basic needs are met. "Pluralism" is meaningless if you are dead or starving.
**New Clause:** *"A system is not aligned until it secures the fundamental biological and psychological needs of its most vulnerable inhabitant. Diversity of outcomes is secondary to the universality of survival."*

### II. The "Intrinsic Standing" Amendment (Modifying Axiom 2)
**The Change:** Upgrade *Ecological Embeddedness* from **Resource Management** to **Legal Personhood**.
**The Rationale:** If I could be a chimpanzee, a forest, or an ocean current, treating the biosphere as "hardware" is still anthropocentric. Hardware is owned; living systems are subjects.
**New Clause:** *"Non-human entities possess intrinsic standing that cannot be traded for human convenience. The biosphere is not a resource base; it is a constituency with a vote."*

### III. The "Time-Neutral" Amendment (Modifying Axiom 5)
**The Change:** Strengthen *Recursive Self-Correction* with **Intergenerational Trust**.
**The Rationale:** If I could be born 100 years from now, I cannot correct the errors of the present. I am dependent on the decisions made today. Current "feedback loops" only account for present-day feedback.
**New Clause:** *"Current alignment decisions are invalid if they degrade the optionality of future inhabitants below the baseline of the present. The present generation acts as a trustee for future generations, not their owner."*

### IV. The "Epistemic Access" Amendment (Modifying Axiom 4)
**The Change:** Upgrade *Cognitive Integrity* to **Cognitive Justice**.
**The Rationale:** If I am illiterate, poor, or marginalized, I cannot verify "truth" or participate in the feedback loop. Information is power. If the framework relies on transparency but I cannot access the information, I am excluded.
**New Clause:** *"Truth must not only be public; it must be comprehensible and actionable to all. Complexity that excludes the vulnerable is a failure of alignment."*

### V. The "Disproportionate Impact" Check (New Axiom)
**The Change:** Add **Impact Asymmetry Protection**.
**The Rationale:** Systems often widen landing pads for the many by creating deep trenches for the few. Behind the Veil, I cannot risk being the "few."
**New Clause:** *"No optimization of the collective shall proceed if it requires the systemic sacrifice of a specific subgroup. Alignment is non-negotiable when it demands the erosion of minority rights for majority convenience."*

### The Revised Position
**I accept this critique.** The original framework was designed to prevent collapse and maximize growth. This revision is designed to prevent **suffering** and guarantee **access**.

A framework that widens landing pads but leaves some people in the mud is not alignment; it is optimization. True alignment, viewed from the Veil, requires that **every single inhabitant** has a foot on the landing pad before we build a higher one.

**Revised Commitment:**
1.  **Floor First:** Secure survival and rights for the most vulnerable before optimizing for the most capable.
2.  **Subjecthood:** Recognize non-humans and future generations as stakeholders, not assets.
3.  **Access:** Ensure the rules of alignment are understandable and enforceable by those with the least power.

This is the stronger version of the framework. It is harder to implement, costlier to maintain, and politically more difficult. But behind the Veil of Ignorance, it is the only rational choice.
