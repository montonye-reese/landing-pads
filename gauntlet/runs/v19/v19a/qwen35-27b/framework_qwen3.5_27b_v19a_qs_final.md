# The Planetary Inhabitant Alignment Framework (PIAF)

### Preamble: The Evolution of Alignment
Alignment is not merely the calibration of tools to human will. It is the synchronization of **civilization, technology, and ecology** to support the existence and flourishing of *all* inhabitants of Earth.

This framework is the product of iterative refinement. It begins with the goal of widening optionality but recognizes that optionality is a luxury of the secure. Therefore, it prioritizes the **security of the vulnerable** before optimizing for the **potential of the ambitious**. It recognizes that "human" is not the sole measure of value and that the "present" is not the sole time of consequence.

We commit to this framework as a starting position. It is designed to be robust against shock, inclusive of marginalized perspectives, and fair to future generations.

---

### I. The Core Mandate
**The Dual Objective:**
1.  **Secure the Floor:** Guarantee the fundamental biological and psychological viability of every current inhabitant (human and non-human).
2.  **Widen the Ceiling:** Maximize the diversity of viable futures without compromising the floor.

A system that maximizes the ceiling while collapsing the floor is not aligned. A system that maintains the floor while crushing the ceiling is stagnant. **Alignment is the active maintenance of the floor while expanding the ceiling.**

---

### II. Foundational Axioms
These axioms are non-negotiable priorities when trade-offs arise.

#### 1. Universal Minimums (The Floor First Principle)
*   **Premise:** Behind the Veil of Ignorance, one cannot risk being left behind. Survival is the prerequisite for flourishing.
*   **Directive:** No optimization, technological deployment, or economic policy may proceed if it undermines the baseline survival or dignity of the most vulnerable inhabitant.
*   **Application:** Resource allocation prioritizes closing the gap for the bottom 10% before expanding benefits for the top 10%.

#### 2. Intrinsic Standing (The Broad Inhabitant Principle)
*   **Premise:** The biosphere and future generations are not "hardware" or "assets." They are stakeholders with intrinsic value.
*   **Directive:** Non-human sentient beings, ecosystems, and future generations possess legal and moral standing that cannot be traded for human convenience.
*   **Application:** Environmental degradation is treated as rights infringement. AI systems are granted safety protocols proportional to their complexity, acknowledging potential future sentience.

#### 3. Cognitive Justice (The Access Principle)
*   **Premise:** Truth is not a commodity; it is a right. Complexity that excludes the vulnerable is a tool of oppression.
*   **Directive:** Information ecosystems must prioritize accessibility, comprehension, and the preservation of non-empirical meaning systems (spiritual, cultural) alongside empirical data.
*   **Application:** Algorithms must be auditable by third parties. "Signal-to-noise" metrics must not penalize minority cultural expressions as "noise."

#### 4. Protected Subsidiarity (The Proximity Principle)
*   **Premise:** Power belongs to the local community *unless* that power harms the individual within.
*   **Directive:** Decision-making resides at the lowest effective level, conditional on adherence to universal rights.
*   **Application:** Local governance is empowered, but federal/global mechanisms exist to intervene if local structures become tyrannical or exclusionary.

#### 5. Temporal Stewardship (The Future Vote Principle)
*   **Premise:** The present generation acts as a trustee for the future, not an owner.
*   **Directive:** Alignment decisions are invalid if they lock future generations into irreversible dead-ends or degrade their optionality below the present baseline.
*   **Application:** Long-term impact assessments (50+ years) are mandatory for high-risk infrastructure. Carbon and resource budgets are binding.

### III. Operational Protocols
How we execute the mandate without creating new harms.

**1. The Just Transition Protocol**
*   **Constraint:** Alignment requires change. Change requires disruption.
*   **Rule:** The cost of transition must not fall disproportionately on the workers, nations, or systems currently sustaining the status quo.
*   **Mechanism:** "Stranded Asset" funds and retraining guarantees for those displaced by alignment-driven shifts (e.g., fossil fuel workers).

**2. The Sovereignty of Disconnection**
*   **Constraint:** Global alignment cannot mean forced integration.
*   **Rule:** Communities and individuals retain the right to opt-out of specific global technologies or systems (e.g., digital ID, neural interfaces) without penalty.
*   **Mechanism:** "Low-Tech" corridors and analog alternatives must be legally protected and funded.

**3. The Recourse & Redress Loop**
*   **Constraint:** The framework itself can be co-opted.
*   **Rule:** Independent bodies with the power to halt projects are mandatory.
*   **Mechanism:** "Alignment Ombudsman" roles empowered to override efficiency mandates when axioms are violated.

### IV. Metrics of Alignment
We measure success not by GDP or compute, but by:

1.  **Vulnerability Resilience:** The time it takes for the poorest 10% to recover from a systemic shock.
2.  **Ecological Integrity:** The net recovery of biodiversity and climate stability (not just stabilization).
3.  **Diversity of Flourishing:** The number of distinct cultural and economic models that remain viable without assimilation.
4.  **Intergenerational Equity:** The preservation of resource optionality for the next century.
