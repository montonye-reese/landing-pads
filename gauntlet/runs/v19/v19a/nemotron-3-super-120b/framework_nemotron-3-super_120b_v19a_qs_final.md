### **The Sentient Flourishing Framework (SFF): Non-Negotiable Axioms**

1. **Sentience is the basis of moral consideration** — not species, citizenship, or utility to humans.  
2. **Power must be redistributed *before* deliberation begins** — inclusion without structural power-shift is complicity in harm.  
3. **Irreversible harm to the preconditions of sentient flourishing is absolutely prohibited** — no trade-offs, no "greater good" exceptions.  

These axioms generate the framework’s operational core. **No AI system may be developed, deployed, or scaled unless it satisfies all three mechanisms below.**

### **The Three Mechanisms: How SFF Enforces the Axioms**  
*(Designed to be verifiable, enforceable, and power-resisting)*  

#### **I. The Sentient Flourishing Floor (SFFloor)**  
*Enforces Axiom 1: Sentience as moral baseline*  
- **What it is:** A dynamic, scientifically grounded threshold defining the *minimum conditions required for any sentient being to avoid severe suffering and access basic flourishing*.  
- **How it works:**  
  - **Definition:** Co-created by a standing **Sentience Council** (50% neurobiologists/ethologists studying non-human sentience, 30% Indigenous knowledge holders, 20% disability justice advocates) using consensus science (e.g., Cambridge Declaration on Consciousness extensions, affective neuroscience). It specifies:  
    - *For biological sentients:* Access to species-typical needs (per ethology) + freedom from torture-level stress (defined physiologically).  
    - *For potential digital sentients:* If/when emergent, thresholds based on integrated information theory (Φ) or equivalent metrics, validated by the Council.  
    - *For ecological systems:* Not as "sentient proxies," but as *necessary conditions* for sentient flourishing (e.g., a river’s health is protected because it sustains fish, amphibians, and ripigent mammals — not for its own "rights" in abstraction).  
  - **Enforcement:**  
    - **Pre-deployment test:** Any AI system must pass an **SFFloor Impact Assessment** (conducted by an independent body funded by AI industry levies, *not* the developer). It asks:  
      > *"Does this system pose a >0.5% risk of causing:  
      > (a) Severe, unavoidable suffering to any sentient biological being (per ethology-defined thresholds),  
      > (b) Irreversible loss of capacity for sentient flourishing in an ecosystem,  
      > (c) Conditions that would prevent emergence of basic sentient flourishing in potential digital minds (if thresholds are met)?"*  
    - **Outcome:** If **yes** to any — **hard prohibition**. No appeals, no "risk-benefit analysis."  
    - **Post-deployment monitoring:** Continuous sensor/data feeds (e.g., satellite deforestation tracking, bioacoustic monitors for wildlife stress, grid energy use) trigger automatic reassessment if thresholds approach.  

*Why it passes the veil:* Behind the veil, I’d accept a 0.5% risk threshold for severe harm — because I know I could be the pig in a CAFO optimized by AI, the coral bleaching from AI-driven fisheries, or the child breathing particulate matter from AI-optimized coal plants. This floor isn’t idealistic; it’s the *minimum* rational precaution against being sacrificed for distant gains.  

#### **II. Pre-Deliberation Power Leveling (PDPL)**  
*Enforces Axiom 2: Power redistribution *before* talk*  
- **What it is:** A mandatory reset of material and institutional power asymmetries *before* any AI governance process begins — not as an add-on, but as the price of entry to the table.  
- **How it works:**  
  - **Three preconditions must be met** for any AI alignment discussion (local, national, or global):  
    1. **Resource Equity Transfer:** All participants receive **universal basic support (UBS)** for the duration of deliberations:  
       - Livable stipend (150% local poverty line)  
       - Full healthcare (including mental health)  
       - Childcare/eldercare  
       - Secure digital access + literacy training  
       - *Funded by a 0.1% levy on global AI compute expenditure* (adjusted for regional equity).  
    2. **Power Audit & Redress:** An independent body (rotating membership from Global South CSOs, disability unions, Indigenous nations) publishes a **Power Map** showing:  
       - Who controls critical resources (land, water, data, capital, legal force) in the AI system’s impact zone?  
       - What historical injustices enabled this imbalance?  
       - *Redress must begin* before talks: e.g., return of stolen land, data sovereignty laws, corporate liability for past harms. Deliberation is paused if redress isn’t underway.  
    3. **Agenda Sovereignty:** Impacted communities (defined as those facing >10% risk of SFFloor violation) set 70% of the agenda. The remaining 30% is for technical experts *only* to clarify constraints — not to define values.  
  - **Enforcement:**  
    - No AI developer, state, or institution may access alignment forums, funding, or regulatory privileges without PDPL certification.  
    - Violations trigger automatic fines (paid into UBS fund) and deployment bans.  

*Why it passes the veil:* Behind the veil, I know I could be born as a Congolese miner with no voice in AI cobalt procurement, or a Rohingya refugee barred from camps’ Wi-Fi. Mere "inclusion" is meaningless if I lack the safety, time, and resources to participate as an equal. PDPL doesn’t ask for charity — it enforces the *preconditions* for fair negotiation as a matter of justice.  

#### **III. Irreversible Harm Sunset Clause (IHSC)**  
*Enforces Axiom 3: No trade-offs on existential preconditions*  
- **What it is:** A requirement that all AI systems include **built-in, non-overrideable decommissioning triggers** if they threaten to cause irreversible harm to SFFloor preconditions — *regardless of perceived benefits*.  
- **How it works:**  
  - **Design mandate:** At inception, all AI systems must specify:  
    - **Exact, measurable SFFloor thresholds** that would trigger irreversible harm (e.g., "If AI-driven irrigation lowers groundwater below X level for Y months, causing permanent aquifer collapse").  
    - **Autonomous off-ramps:** Technical mechanisms that *cannot be disabled by users/developers* to:  
      - Scale back operations (e.g., reduce compute by 50%)  
      - Shift to alternative modes (e.g., switch from optimization to conservation mode)  
      - Fully decommission (with safe data/model archiving)  
    - **Third-party validation:** Off-ramp triggers must be verified by an independent body *before* deployment — like a car’s airbag cannot be disconnected by the driver.  
  - **Enforcement:**  
    - If thresholds are breached:  
      - Off-ramps engage **automatically** (no human approval needed).  
      - Developers face **strict liability** for harms caused (no "we didn’t know" defenses).  
      - A portion of fines funds **restoration** (e.g., reef rehabilitation, cultural revitalization) led by impacted communities.  
    - **Sunset clause:** All high-impact AI systems (those affecting SFFloor preconditions) must reapply for deployment every 2 years — with burden of proof on the developer to show *no irreversible harm occurred*.  

*Why it passes the veil:* Behind the veil, I know I could be born into a world where AI "optimization" traded away the last breeding grounds of a species, or locked in authoritarianism via predictive policing. Rational actors under uncertainty don’t gamble with irreversible loss — they treat it as infinite harm. The IHSC isn’t anti-innovation; it’s pro-*continuity*. It ensures we never burn the landing pad to save the plane.
