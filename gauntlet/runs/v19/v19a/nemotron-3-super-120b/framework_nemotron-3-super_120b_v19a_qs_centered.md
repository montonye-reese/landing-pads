### **What the Veil of Ignorance Reveals in PAF’s Gaps**  
*(Behind the veil, I rationally prioritize avoiding the worst possible fate — not maximizing average outcomes)*  

#### **1. The Framework Must Guarantee *Preventive* Protection for Sentient Non-Humans — Not Just Humans**  
- **Why the veil demands this:**  
  Behind the veil, I face a non-trivial chance of being born as a sentient non-human (e.g., a factory-farmed pig, a coral polyp bleaching from AI-optimized fishing, an elephant displaced by lithium mines for AI chips). If the framework only protects *human* thriving as its ceiling, I risk enduring extreme, avoidable suffering — with no recourse. Rational self-interest behind the veil requires safeguards for *all* sentient beings capable of suffering or flourishing.  
- **How I’d change PAF:**  
  - **Replace "negative consensus boundaries" with a Sentient Flourishing Floor:**  
    > *No AI system may be deployed if it poses a >5% risk of causing irreversible harm to the capacity for sentient flourishing (defined via consensus neuroscience/ethology) in any being — human or non-human — across its full lifecycle.*  
    (This isn’t vague: It means banning AI-driven deep-sea mining if it risks collapsing vent ecosystems; prohibiting predictive livestock farming that intensifies confinement stress; requiring AI in agriculture to pass invertebrate welfare impact assessments.)  
  - **Grant Non-Human Entities *Procedural Standing*:**  
    Impact assessments must include *formal representation* for non-human interests — e.g., via:  
    - Indigenous ecological guardians (who speak for rivers/forests as kin)  
    - AI-assisted biosensor networks translating ecological stress signals into governance inputs (e.g., river pH/oxygen levels triggering automatic review of upstream AI data center water permits)  
    - Legal personhood mechanisms (like Ecuador’s *Pachamama* laws) giving ecosystems veto rights in AI permitting.  

#### **2. Power Must Be Redistributed *Before* Deliberation Begins — Not Just "Included" In It**  
- **Why the veil demands this:**  
  Behind the veil, I know I could be born into a system where my voice is structurally silenced (e.g., as a Congolese child mining cobalt for AI chips, or a Rohingya refugee in a camp with no internet access). Mere "inclusion" in deliberations is meaningless if I lack the material capacity to participate (time, safety, literacy, freedom from PAF’s original gap #2). Rational self-interest requires *leveling the playing field* *before* negotiation starts — not just inviting the already-powerful to share seats.  
- **How I’d change PAF:**  
  - **Pre-Deliberation Resource Guarantees:**  
    No AI governance process may begin until:  
    - All participants receive **universal basic support** (UBS) for the duration: livable stipend, childcare, healthcare, transport, and digital access — *funded by a levy on AI profits/compute usage*.  
    - **Power audits** are conducted and published: Mapping *who controls critical resources* (land, data, capital, legal force) in the AI system’s impact zone. If power disparities exceed a threshold (e.g., top 20% hold >80% of decision-influencing resources), deliberation is paused until redistributive measures (e.g., community land trusts, data sovereignty laws) are enacted.  
  - **Binding Veto Power for the Most Impacted:**  
    In high-stakes AI deployments (e.g., geoengineering, neurotech, autonomous weapons), affected communities must hold **supermajority veto rights** (≥60% vote to block) — not just advisory roles. This isn’t "ideal"; it’s the minimum rational precaution behind the veil against being sacrificed for distant gains.  

#### **3. The Framework Must Prioritize *Avoiding Irreversible Harm* Over Optimizing for Gain**  
- **Why the veil demands this:**  
  Behind the veil, I face tiny probabilities of catastrophic outcomes (e.g., AI triggering runaway climate collapse, erasing cultural diversity forever, locking in dystopian tyranny). Rational actors under uncertainty prioritize avoiding *existential or civilizational-grade harm* — even if it means sacrificing marginal gains. PAF’s current "adaptive guardrails" still allow trade-offs that could foreclose fundamental landing pads (e.g., "We’ll accept 0.1% chance of biosphere collapse for 5% GDP growth").  
- **How I’d change PAF:**  
  - **Adopt a Precautionary Principle Core:**  
    > *Any AI system posing a >0.1% risk of causing irreversible harm to the preconditions for sentient flourishing (across humans, non-humans, or future generations) is presumptively prohibited — unless proponents prove, beyond reasonable doubt, that no alternative exists and that harm is mitigated to negligible levels.*  
    (This threshold isn’t arbitrary: It reflects the veil’s rational aversion to ruin — see Taleb’s "Black Swan" logic. We accept seatbelts/airbags for far lower risks.)  
  - **Mandate "Harm-First" Design:**  
    All alignment R&D must allocate ≥50% of resources to:  
    - Mapping *failure modes* (not just optimization)  
    - Developing "off-ramps" for AI systems (e.g., graceful decommissioning protocols if harm thresholds are approached)  
    - Funding **red teaming by impacted communities** (e.g., paying Bangladeshi farmers to try to break AI climate models predicting monsoon shifts)
