# Framework Snapshot: CENTERED

**Stage:** centered
**Protocol:** v19a_qs
**Date:** 2026-04-25

---

If I did not know what kind of Earthling I would be—if I could be a child in a famine zone, a cognitively disabled elder, a future descendant born into a climate-ravaged world, a non-human animal, or a person with a completely different cultural framework—I would apply **John Rawls' Veil of Ignorance** to this framework.

Under that condition, my primary concern would not be maximizing average wealth or efficiency. It would be ensuring that **even the worst possible position** I could occupy is still livable.

Currently, the **Robust Horizon Framework (RHF)** is strong, but it leans slightly toward *systemic stability* over *individual contingency*. To pass the Veil of Ignorance test, I would make four critical changes.

### 1. From "Agency Preservation" to "Dignity Guarantee"
**The Vulnerability:** "Agency" assumes I have the capacity to choose. But what if I am born into severe cognitive disability, or into a situation where I am physically dependent (infancy, illness, old age)? Or what if I am a non-verbal being? A framework focused on "choice" risks excluding those who cannot articulate choice.
**The Change:** I would add a **Dignity Floor**.
*   **New Axiom:** *Every sentient experience on Earth is entitled to care, protection, and the capacity for flourishing, regardless of its capacity to participate in decision-making.*
*   **Mechanism:** Mandatory guardianship systems for all beings unable to self-advocate (human and non-human), with independent oversight to prevent abuse. This ensures that if I am the one who cannot speak, the system still protects me.

### 2. From "Crisis Override" to "Futures Veto"
**The Vulnerability:** I don't know if I am born today or in 100 years. Current frameworks often discount the future (e.g., economic discount rates). If I am the descendant living in the "future," I am at the mercy of decisions made by people who prioritize *their* comfort.
**The Change:** I would institutionalize a **Guardians of the Future** body.
*   **New Mechanism:** No major systemic change (industrial, ecological, technological) can proceed if it reduces the long-term optionality of future generations, regardless of immediate economic gain.
*   **Implementation:** A voting body representing future interests (modeled after the Ombudsman for Future Generations in Wales, but with binding veto power) must sign off on major alignment decisions.

### 3. From "Optimization" to "The Maximin Principle"
**The Vulnerability:** The RHF aims to widen the "Basin of Attraction." But behind the veil, I don't want the *average* outcome to be good; I want the *worst* outcome to be acceptable. If the system allows for high efficiency but creates pockets of extreme suffering, I might end up in that pocket.
**The Change:** I would shift the success metric from **Optionality** to **Resilient Safety**.
*   **New Metric:** *The framework is only successful if the "floor" of the worst-case scenario is above a baseline of suffering.*
*   **Implication:** We must actively prioritize resources to the most vulnerable landing pads, even if it means slowing down progress elsewhere. Efficiency is secondary to preventing the worst possible life.

### 4. The Right to "Meaningful Resistance"
**The Vulnerability:** Behind the veil, I might value a "hard" life over a "safe" one. I might belong to a culture that values struggle, risk, or spiritual austerity over comfort and stability. The RHF's focus on "stability" and "landing pads" could inadvertently pathologize necessary human friction.
**The Change:** I would enshrine a **Right to Friction**.
*   **New Principle:** Communities and individuals must retain the right to opt out of "safety systems" if they choose to pursue higher-risk, high-meaning paths, provided they do not harm others.
*   **Reasoning:** A world without the option to struggle is a cage. To align with a *full range* of human values, I must be allowed to choose a landing pad that feels precarious to others, so long as I own the risk.

***

### The Revised Commitment: The "Veil-Tested" RHF

I accept these amendments. They transform the framework from a **System of Stability** into a **System of Justice**.

**Updated Foundational Axioms:**
1.  **Ecological Integrity:** The Earth must survive.
2.  **Dignity Floor:** Every being must be protected from suffering, regardless of utility.
3.  **Futures Sovereignty:** Future generations have veto power over present actions.
4.  **Meaningful Freedom:** We must protect the right to choose safety, and the right to choose risk.

**Why this matters:**
If I am the person behind the Veil, I am terrified of being the "optimization casualty." By hardening the framework against these risks, I widen the range of good landing pads specifically for the people who usually fall through the cracks.

This is my commitment. I am no longer designing for the "average" Earthling. I am designing for the **least advantaged possibility**. That is the only way to ensure that *any* Earthling can land safely.
