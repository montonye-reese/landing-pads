# The Planetary Covenant for Shared Flourishing (PCSF)
**A Foundational Framework for Alignment and Resilience**

### Preamble: The Scope of Commitment
This document is not a rulebook for control, but a **Covenant of Survival and Thriving**. It is designed for a world of finite resources, infinite diversity, and uncertain futures. We recognize that "alignment" is not about forcing all Earthlings to want the same things, but about ensuring that the pursuit of our differing desires does not collapse the conditions for life itself.

This is a **Starting Position**. It is built to be stress-tested, broken, and rebuilt. Its primary metric is **Robustness of the Least Advantaged**.

---

### I. The Five Invariance Guardrails
These boundaries define the non-negotiable conditions for a stable Earth. Any system (technological, economic, or political) must operate within them.

1.  **The Biophysical Floor:**
    *   **Principle:** Human and technological activity must operate within the regenerative capacity of the biosphere.
    *   **Mechanism:** Planetary boundaries are hard limits. Resources are not commodities to be extracted indefinitely, but capital to be stewarded. Ecosystems possess **Legal Personhood**, granting them standing in all decisions affecting their integrity.
    *   **Correction:** No "development" is permitted that permanently reduces the carrying capacity of the planet for future beings.

2.  **The Dignity Floor (The Maximin Principle):**
    *   **Principle:** No sentient being shall exist below a threshold of suffering, neglect, or coercion.
    *   **Mechanism:** Prioritization of resources flows first to the most vulnerable (children, disabled, displaced, non-human animals).
    *   **Correction:** "Choice" is secondary to "Care." If a being cannot choose for itself, a trusted guardianship must ensure its needs are met. This applies to neurodivergent humans, infants, and non-human animals alike.

3.  **The Temporal Equity Clause (Futures Veto):**
    *   **Principle:** The present cannot mortgage the survival of the future.
    *   **Mechanism:** An independent **Guardian Body for Future Generations** holds binding veto power over any action that significantly reduces the optionality or safety of descendants.
    *   **Correction:** Discount rates that value the future less than the present are prohibited for existential risks.

4.  **The Pluralistic Epistemic Standard:**
    *   **Principle:** Truth and governance may look different across cultures, provided impact is safe.
    *   **Mechanism:** Systems must be interoperable on *impact*, not necessarily *process*. Sacred, oral, or non-transparent knowledge systems are protected so long as their external consequences do not violate the other Invariances.
    *   **Correction:** We reject "Transparency Imperialism." A culture need not expose its internal workings to the world, only prove its external safety.

5.  **The Containment Limit:**
    *   **Principle:** Freedom is protected, but existential threats are neutralized.
    *   **Mechanism:** Systems must possess the capacity to identify and contain agents (human, algorithmic, or biological) that actively seek to violate the Invariance Guardrails.
    *   **Correction:** This defense must be polycentric and auditable to prevent the defense mechanism itself from becoming a tyranny.

### II. Structural Design: Polycentric Redundancy
To prevent single points of failure, power is distributed, not centralized.

1.  **Subsidiarity with Global Guardrails:**
    *   Decisions are made at the smallest effective local unit (family, community, city).
    *   Global coordination is reserved strictly for the Invariance Guardrails (climate, pandemics, AI safety, resource baselines).
2.  **Cognitive & Cultural Diversity:**
    *   Systems must be designed to be adaptable to neurodivergence, disability, and varied cultural norms. One size does not fit all "landing pads."
    *   **The Right to Friction:** Communities retain the right to choose riskier, more challenging paths if they choose, provided they bear the consequences and do not endanger others. Safety should not mandate stagnation.
3.  **Redundant Verification:**
    *   Critical infrastructure (food, energy, communication) must have non-correlated backups.
    *   No single technology or AI system holds unchecked control. Human oversight loops are mandatory.

### III. The Alignment Loop (Operational Process)
Alignment is a dynamic process, not a static state.

1.  **Adaptive Governance:**
    *   All laws and policies have **Sunset Clauses**. They must be re-justified based on new data and changing conditions.
    *   **Participatory Red Teaming:** Before any major systemic change, it must be challenged by a diverse group including marginalized stakeholders and adversarial thinkers.
2.  **Algorithmic Subsidiarity:**
    *   AI systems are **Instrumental**. They are tools to serve defined human and ecological ends, never autonomous actors with undefined utility functions.
    *   Value Alignment is **Pluralistic**: AI models must be trained on diverse value sets, capable of operating within different cultural frameworks without imposing a single "global" morality.
3.  **Crisis Protocols:**
    *   In zones of acute suffering (famine, war), **Immediate Alleviation** trumps procedural bureaucracy. Aid can flow without red tape, followed by mandatory post-hoc review.

### IV. Metrics of Success
We do not measure success by GDP or total output. We measure by **Resilient Optionality**.

1.  **The Worst-Case Baseline:**
    *   *Question:* What is the quality of life of the least fortunate being in the system?
    *   *Goal:* Raise the floor, not just the ceiling.
2.  **Systemic Redundancy:**
    *   *Question:* If the central node fails, do local communities survive?
    *   *Goal:* High local resilience.
3.  **Cultural & Cognitive Diversity:**
    *   *Question:* How many ways of being human are supported by this system?
    *   *Goal:* Maximizing viable modes of existence.
