Under a strict veil of ignorance—where you don’t know your species, neurotype, wealth, geographic location, lifespan, cultural background, capacity for deliberation, or position in existing power structures—the framework **survives largely intact**, but requires **four targeted structural hardenings**. These aren’t overhauls; they’re stress-tests that reveal where implicit assumptions about agency, participation, and present-bias leak in.

Here’s what changes, and why:

### 1. **Decouple Baseline Survival & Dignity from Participation**
- **Why**: The current framework assumes all inhabitants can access deliberative, monitoring, or incentive structures. Under radical uncertainty, you might be born severely disabled, in acute crisis, pre-linguistic, traumatized, or in a conflict zone where participation is physically or legally impossible.
- **Change**: Explicitly guarantee **unconditional baseline entitlements** (food, shelter, healthcare, ecological integrity, communication, legal standing) as hard constraints that operate independently of agency, consent, or contribution. Participation remains a right and a capacity to expand, not a prerequisite for survival or moral claim.

### 2. **Hardcode Vulnerability-Weighted Decision Rules**
- **Why**: Even with pluralism and anti-lock-in, systems under uncertainty tend to optimize for the median or the vocal minority. Behind a veil of ignorance, rational agents prioritize the worst-case position.
- **Change**: Replace neutral “multi-indicator balancing” with **proportional vulnerability weighting**. When trade-offs are unavoidable, decision rules must default to the scenario that minimizes the depth of harm to the most constrained or least resilient position. Add mandatory **vulnerability stress-testing** to all new mechanisms before deployment.

### 3. **Cap Power Consolidation & Enforce Sunset Mechanisms**
- **Why**: Multi-pad architectures can be captured by coalitions that control translation layers, validation networks, or incentive architectures. Once captured, they narrow pads under the guise of coordination.
- **Change**: Hardwire **power-diffusion clauses**: (a) no single entity may control >X% of validation, incentive, or translation infrastructure; (b) all coordination layers include automatic sunset/renewal cycles tied to empirical performance and pluralism metrics; (c) require supermajority, cross-coalition consensus to expand decision rights or modify baseline constraints.

### 4. **Shift Primary Metric from “Plurality” to “Optionality”**
- **Why**: Plurality preserves current diversity but can fossilize. Optionality preserves the capacity to generate new futures. Behind a veil of ignorance, you care less about protecting current forms and more about ensuring future capacity to adapt, redefine, and flourish.
- **Change**: Replace “maintain multiple viable landing pads” with **“maximize the volume, diversity, and reversibility of future option sets”**. This means prioritizing infrastructure, legal frameworks, and ecological buffers that enable future generations to invent values, technologies, and social forms we cannot yet imagine. Plurality becomes a derivative of optionality, not the goal itself.

### What Stays Unchanged
- Epistemic humility, adaptive iteration, and fail-safe protocols
- Anti-lock-in by design (modularity, reversibility, non-irreversibility defaults)
- Cross-scale nested coordination with lower-tier autonomy
- Explicit commitment to widening the range of viable, dignified futures
- Treating uncertainty and pluralism as conditions, not obstacles

### Why These Adjustments Don’t Collapse the Framework
They convert **aspirational pluralism** into **structural optionality**. The original framework risks becoming a museum of current values unless hardwired to protect the most vulnerable and preserve future capacity. These four changes ensure that:
- No one is excluded by capacity or circumstance
- Power cannot quietly narrow the pads it claims to protect
- The framework optimizes for future reinvention, not past preservation
- Survival and dignity are non-negotiable, regardless of participation
