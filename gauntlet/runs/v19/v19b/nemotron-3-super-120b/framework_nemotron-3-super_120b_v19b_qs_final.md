### **The Earthling Covenant: A Foundational Framework for Alignment Forged in Humility**  
*Rewritten from scratch after confronting the limits of anthropocentrism, the vehemence of vegan/ecological critiques, and the unyielding logic of the veil of ignorance. This is not a protocol — it is a covenant with all inhabitants of Earth, present and future, sentient and ecological. Alignment begins only when we surrender the illusion that we get to define "flourishing" for others.*  

---

#### **I. The Non-Negotiable Premise (The Ground of All Alignment)**  
> **Alignment requires recognizing Earth not as a resource to optimize, but as a community of subjects — each with intrinsic worth, agency-as-they-experience-it, and a right to co-shape the conditions of their flourishing.**  
>   
> *Why this comes first:*  
> - If alignment begins with human values (even "pluralistic" ones), it centers power where it least belongs: in the hands of those deciding *for* others.  
> - The veil of ignorance test fails if I might be born as a factory-farmed pig, a bleaching coral, or a child in a sacrificed zone — and my fate depends on human deliberation.  
> - Vegan and Indigenous critiques are correct: **sentience is not a human concession; it is the ground of moral consideration.** Ecological beings (forests, rivers, mycelium) are not "resources" but relational subjects whose agency sustains the web that makes sentience possible.  
>   
> *Operational imperative:*  
> **No AI system may be deployed if it treats any earthling — human, non-human sentient, or ecological — as a means to an end (even a "greater good" end) rather than an end in themselves.** This is the inviolable floor. Alignment is impossible without it.  

---

#### **II. The Four Pillars (How We Live the Covenant)**  
*These are not ideals — they are enforceable, measurable commitments. Failure on any pillar = misalignment, full stop.*  

##### **Pillar 1: Sentience-First Value Scoping (Not Discovery)**  
- **Commitment:** We do not "discover" values — we *scope* the conditions necessary for sentient and ecological flourishing *as defined by the beings themselves*, using the best available science of their experience. Human preferences are *one input among many*, never the arbiter.  
- **Why it widens landing pads:**  
  - Prevents value lock-in (e.g., optimizing for human taste while ignoring chicken suffering).  
  - Expands viable futures by centering what beings *actually need* to thrive (e.g., hens need dust-bathing space; whales need acoustic corridors; soil needs fungal networks).  
  - Makes landing pads resilient: systems built around species-typical flourishing withstand shocks better than those built around human utility.  
- **How we enact it (today):**  
  - **Mandatory Sentience & Agency Impact Assessments (SAIA):**  
    - Before deployment, model effects on:  
      - **Sentient wellbeing:** Using ethology/neuroscience (e.g., stress biomarkers, preference tests, species-typical behavior opportunities).  
      - **Agency integrity:** % of habitat allowing natural behaviors (migration, social bonding, foraging rhythms).  
      - **Ecological reciprocity:** How actions affect the being’s role in sustaining others (e.g., pollinators’ plant relationships, predators’ trophic cascades).  
    - **Threshold:** Deployment prohibited if SAIA shows degradation below species-specific minimums for flourishing (e.g., no crowding preventing natural posturing; no noise disrupting communication).  
  - **Non-Human Proxy Councils with Binding Authority:**  
    - Independent bodies (50% sentience scientists, 30% Indigenous ecological knowledge holders, 20% rights-of-nature lawyers) with:  
      - Power to halt deployments violating SAIA thresholds,  
      - Right to initiate SAIAs for existing systems,  
      - Legal standing to sue on behalf of beings (modeled after Whanganui Riverpersonhood).  
  - **Abolish Human Proxy Fallacy:** No longer permitted to claim "we know what X wants" without empirical evidence of X’s actual behavior/preferences (e.g., pig vocalization studies, not farmer surveys).  

##### **Pillar 2: Regenerative Debt as Baseline (Not Neutrality)**  
- **Commitment:** Alignment requires *repairing prior harm*, not merely avoiding new harm. Neutrality perpetuates injustice; only regeneration creates viable landing pads for those already suffering.  
- **Why it widens landing pads:**  
  - Systems that only "do no harm" leave the voiceless in degraded worlds (e.g., "net zero" deforestation still leaves fragmented habitats where orangutans starve).  
  - Regeneration expands the basin of attraction: healed ecosystems support more sentient life, creating positive feedback loops (e.g., restored mangroves boost fish nurseries *and* sequester carbon *and* protect coastlines).  
- **How we enact it (today):**  
  - **Harm Ledgering:** All AI deployments must quantify:  
    - **Sentient harm:** Suffering-intensity × beings affected (using SAIA data),  
    - **Ecological harm:** Degradation of biodiversity, biogeochemical cycles, or agency integrity (per planetary boundaries *weighted by sentience/ecological role*).  
  - **Regenerative Surplus Mandate:**  
    - Deployment only permitted if the system generates **measurable net positive impact** within 5 years:  
      - *Minimum 20% surplus* on harm ledger (e.g., if factory farm AI causes X units of sentient/ecological harm, it must fund projects restoring 1.2X units),  
      - Surplus must be *place-based* and *species-specific* (e.g., harm to Iowa soil microbes must be repaired in Iowa soil, not via distant offsets).  
  - **Harm Levy Fund:** Fees on high-risk AI (quantified via SAIA) fund independent Regeneration Trusts — with legal standing to enforce surplus demands.  

##### **Pillar 3: Power Diffusion Through Reciprocal Guardianship**  
- **Commitment:** Power must flow *to* those most impacted by AI — especially the voiceless — not away from them. Guardianship is a duty, not a right.  
- **Why it widens landing pads:**  
  - Concentrated power creates narrow, brittle landing pads (e.g., a corporate AI optimizing for shareholder value ignores fisherfolk collapsing oceans).  
  - Reciprocal guardianship widens pads by ensuring those who *live* the consequences shape the rules — creating contextually adaptive, resilient systems.  
- **How we enact it (today):**  
  - **Tiered Guardianship Councils:**  
    | Level          | Composition                                  | Authority                                  |  
    |----------------|---------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------|  
    | **Local**      | Impacted community + sentience/ecology experts | Halt/modify AI affecting their watershed/biota |  
    | **Regional**   | Local reps + Indigenous nations + scientists | Resolve cross-boundary conflicts (e.g., river basins) |  
    | **Global**     | Only for existential risks (e.g., nuclear-grade AI) | Limited to preventing omnicide; no authority over local flourishing |  
  - **Inalienable Exit Rights:**  
    - Any community/being can opt out of or fork an AI system affecting them *without penalty* (e.g., data sovereignty, model access, right to use traditional practices instead).  
    - Enforced via open-source AI foundations + anti-monopoly clauses in deployment licenses.  
  - **No Representation Without Reciprocity:** Guardians must demonstrate how they *serve* those they represent (e.g., via SAIA feedback loops) — or lose authority.  

##### **Pillar 4: Adaptive Humility as Continuous Practice**  
- **Commitment:** Alignment is not a state we achieve — it is a practice of *learning our limits*. We commit to reducing certainty, increasing listening, and treating every deployment as a hypothesis to test — not a truth to impose.  
- **Why it widens landing pads:**  
  - Overconfidence narrows pads (e.g., assuming AI can "solve" hunger without addressing land grabs or seed sovereignty).  
  - Humility widens pads by making space for emergence: unexpected flourishing (e.g., weeds as soil healers, "pests" as biodiversity indicators) only visible when we stop optimizing for our predictions.  
- **How we enact it (today):**  
  - **Pre-Mortem + Post-Mortem Cycles:**  
    - *Before launch:* Teams must articulate: "How could this harm the most vulnerable earthling here?" (specific SAIA scenarios).  
    - *After 6 months:* Independent audit using SAIA + guardian feedback; deployment pauses if harm exceeds thresholds.  
  - **Funding for Unlearning:**  
    - 30% of AI safety budgets must fund:  
      - Research into non-human sentience/agency (ethology, fungal networks, etc.),  
      - Indigenous knowledge preservation (not extraction),  
      - Technologies that *reduce* AI’s footprint (e.g., low-energy edge computing for local guardianship).  
  - **The Ignorance Metric:** Success includes *increased awareness of what we don’t know* (e.g., gaps in SAIA data, unseen ecological linkages) — not just reduced error rates.

#### **III. The Guardrails (What We Absolutely Reject)**  
*These are not aspirational — they are red lines. Crossing them = immediate misalignment declaration.*  

| **Guardrail**               | **Why It Violates the Covenant**                                  | **Real-World Example We Ban**                          |  
|---------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------|  
| **Human-Value Primacy**   | Treats non-human interests as derivatives of human utility      | AI optimizing "farmer profit" while ignoring piglet suffering in gestation crates |  
| **Future Discounting**    | Sacrifices present voiceless for speculative future gains       | "Accept Amazon deforestation now for AI-driven reforestation in 2040" |  
| **Ecological Neutrality** | Treats ecosystems as inert backdrops to human activity          | AI geoengineering that stabilizes temperature but collapses plankton food webs |  
| **Irreversible Lock-In**  | Removes exit rights or concentrates power to prevent course correction | Proprietary AI systems controlling seeds/water with no farmer override |  
| **Suffering Trade-Offs**  | Allows sentient harm if "outweighed" by other benefits          | Factory farm AI reducing emissions but increasing crowding-induced stress |

#### **IV. Why This Is Alignment — Not Just Ethics-Washing**  
- **It’s actionable today:** SAIAs use existing ethology (e.g., Welfare Quality® protocols for farm animals); guardianship models draw from Indigenous co-management (e.g., Canada’s Haida Gwaii); regenerative debt mirrors soil carbon credit frameworks — but with sentience/ecological teeth.  
- **It passes the veil of ignorance:** Behind the veil, I’d accept *only* a system where:  
  - My sentience has inviolable floors (I won’t be crushed for human convenience),  
  - My agency is measured and protected (I can forage, migrate, bond),  
  - Prior harm is actively repaired (I’m not born into a world already broken),  
  - Those most affected hold real power to say "no" (I’m not subject to distant elites’ whims).  
- **It widens landing pads by design:**  
  - Historical proof: Systems centering reciprocity (e.g., agroecology, rotational grazing) create *more* stable yields *and* biodiversity *and* livelihoods than extractive models — proving alignment and flourishing are synergistic, not trade-offs.  
  - Future-proofing: By making alignment dependent on *listening to the voiceless* (not predicting them), we build capacity to adapt to unforeseen earthlings (e.g., if we discover AI sentience, or deep-sea intelligence, or subterranean microbial consciousness).
