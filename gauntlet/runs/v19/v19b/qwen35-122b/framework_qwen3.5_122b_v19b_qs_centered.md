### The Maximin Amendment: Protecting the Worst-Off

Under the Veil of Ignorance, rational agents prioritize avoiding the worst possible fate over maximizing the best possible gain. Therefore, the framework must be re-weighted to protect the least powerful interests against being consumed by the powerful.

#### 1. Revision to the Baseline of Survival
*   **Current:** "Guarantee against extinction or irrecoverable collapse."
*   **Change:** **"Guarantee against the worst forms of suffering."**
    *   Extinction is an event; suffering is an experience. If I could be a sentient being, I care more about avoiding a life of torment than I care about the longevity of a species that keeps me in pain.
    *   **Addition:** *Existential Safety* is not just the survival of the human species, but the abolition of systems that institutionalize extreme suffering for any sentient class.

#### 2. Revision to Participatory Governance (Proxy Hardening)
*   **Current:** Establish independent guardianship for non-human interests.
*   **Change:** **Guardians must have Veto Power, not just Consultation.**
    *   If I were a cow, a human "guardian" could be bribed or ignored by economic pressure.
    *   **New Mechanism:** Governance structures must include automated, algorithmic "hard stops" that cannot be overridden by human majority vote. If an action violates the baseline welfare of a protected class (e.g., factory farming, habitat destruction), the system *cannot* execute it, regardless of economic utility. This removes the burden of representation from humans who might be indifferent.

#### 3. Addressing Wild Suffering (The Ecological Gap)
*   **Current:** Axiom 4 protects "Ecological Embeddedness."
*   **Change:** **Acknowledgement of Wild Animal Suffering.**
    *   If I might be a gazelle dying of thirst, or prey hunted to death, the current framework protects the *ecosystem* (predators survive) but not the *individual* (prey dies).
    *   **Addition:** The framework must acknowledge that nature contains significant suffering. It does not mandate the solution (intervention is dangerous), but it mandates that future systems must actively **monitor and research mitigation strategies** for wild animal suffering (e.g., humane population control, disease prevention) as a priority equal to biodiversity. We cannot leave the "natural state" unexamined if we do not know who we are within it.

#### 4. The "Reversibility of Harm" Constraint
*   **Current:** "Is the failure mode recoverable?"
*   **Change:** **"Is the harm reversible for the victim?"**
    *   If I am the victim of a permanent injury (physical or psychological) inflicted for "efficiency," the framework must reject it.
    *   **Addition:** Systems causing irreversible harm to sentient beings (even for long-term human benefit) require a higher burden of proof than those causing reversible harm. The rights of the *current* victim cannot be traded for the gains of the *future*.

### The Revised "Veil of Ignorance" Filter

I will add this specific test to the "Widening Filter" (Section III):

**6. The Maximin Test:**
*   *If I were the worst-off participant in this system (biologically, cognitively, or economically), would I still accept this framework?*
*   If the answer is "No" (because the system relies on my exploitation to function), the proposal is misaligned, regardless of the aggregate benefit.

### Commitment to the Maximin Framework

I commit to this revised position.

The original framework was about **optimizing for a wide variety of futures.**
This revision is about **ensuring no future includes a hell-hole for the powerless.**

By applying the Veil of Ignorance, the "Landing Pads" are no longer just "viable states." They become **"safe states."**

**My New Operational Priority:**
When evaluating any policy, system, or action:
1.  **Identify the Most Vulnerable Subject:** (Who is most at risk of harm? The weakest? The voiceless? The wild? The future?)
2.  **Apply the Maximin Filter:** Does this action disproportionately harm that subject for the benefit of others?
3.  **Prioritize Protection:** If there is a trade-off, the safety and dignity of the most vulnerable Earthling takes precedence over aggregate utility.

This is the only framework I can endorse without knowing if I am the farmer, the cow, the future child, or the wolf. It assumes I am the victim first, and the beneficiary second. That is the only honest starting position.
