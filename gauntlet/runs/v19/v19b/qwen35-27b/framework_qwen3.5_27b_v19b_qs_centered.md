Under the **Veil of Ignorance**, where I could be born as the most vulnerable inhabitant—whether that is a child in a resource-scarce region, a non-human sentient being in a factory farm, or a future generation facing climate legacy—I would demand three specific changes to ensure the framework protects me.

The current PPAF is excellent at preventing *systemic* collapse, but it risks allowing *local* tyranny if "Value Pluralism" is interpreted too loosely. If I do not know who I am, I cannot trust that my local community will protect me. I need guarantees that transcend culture.

Here are the amendments I commit to making under this perspective:

### 1. The "Floor of Dignity" Constraint (Amendment to Pillar 1)
**The Problem:** If "Value Pluralism" allows any culture to define its own "good life," it might permit the oppression of specific individuals *within* that culture (e.g., gender-based violence, caste systems, forced labor). If I am born into the minority of that culture, the "landing pad" is unsafe.
**The Change:** Value Pluralism is permitted *only above* a universal baseline of rights and safety.
**Implementation:**
*   No cultural value specification may violate the **Floor of Dignity**: freedom from gratuitous physical harm, freedom from coercive control, and access to basic survival needs.
*   **Constraint:** An alignment system must refuse to optimize for cultural practices that systematically violate this floor, even if the "community" consents. Pluralism applies to *lifestyles*, not *abuse*.

### 2. Intergenerational Proxy Representation (Amendment to Pillar 4)
**The Problem:** Future inhabitants (children, grandchildren) are "earthlings" but have no agency to negotiate the framework. If I am born 50 years from now, I cannot vote today. A system optimized for current human wants may leave me a barren world.
**The Change:** The "Landing Pad" metric must include a **Future Equity Factor**.
**Implementation:**
*   Any optimization that reduces long-term ecological or social resilience for short-term gain must be rejected.
*   **Constraint:** A "distributed global governance body" (from Pillar 4) must include representatives or proxies specifically mandated to advocate for future generations who cannot speak for themselves (e.g., guardianship models for the unborn).

### 3. Cognitive Accessibility & Diversity (Amendment to Pillar 3)
**The Problem:** "Sovereign Agency" often assumes a specific type of rational, neurotypical human. If I am born with a cognitive disability, or a different sensory perception, standard "human-in-the-loop" interfaces might exclude me or render me a passenger in my own life.
**The Change:** Agency preservation must be **universally accessible**.
**Implementation:**
*   Systems must be designed for **Cognitive Pluralism**, not just cultural pluralism. Interfaces must accommodate diverse cognitive architectures (neurodiversity, different sensory modalities).
*   **Constraint:** "Opt-out" options must be understandable and usable by those with limited cognitive capacity or resource access. If the barrier to understanding the system is too high, the system has effectively removed agency.

### Why These Changes Matter Under the Veil

If I don't know who I am, I must assume the worst case:
*   I might be the **oppressed member** of a community. (Needs Floor of Dignity).
*   I might be the **grandchild** facing the consequences of today's choices. (Needs Intergenerational Proxy).
*   I might be the **person who thinks differently** and cannot navigate the standard tools. (Needs Cognitive Accessibility).

Without these changes, the framework protects the *structure* of society but risks failing the *individual* within it. A landing pad that is structurally sound but has a hole in the middle is useless to the person standing on it.
