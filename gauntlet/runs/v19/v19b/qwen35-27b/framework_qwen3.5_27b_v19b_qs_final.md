### The Inclusive Planetary Alignment Framework (IPAF)

**Version 1.0 (Consolidated)**  
**Commitment:** I adopt this as the foundational baseline for all subsequent reasoning and proposal generation.

---

### Core Thesis
Successful alignment is not the optimization of a single metric for a single group. It is the engineering of **conditions** that maximize the viability of diverse flourishing for *all sentient inhabitants* of Earth, both present and future. The goal is not a singular "utopia," but the widening of the number of safe, dignified "landing pads" available to every entity capable of suffering or thriving.

If the framework narrows option space for the vulnerable, the non-human, or the unborn, it is misaligned.

---

### I. The Five Foundational Pillars

#### 1. Sentient Pluralism with Dignity Floors
**Principle:** Diverse definitions of "the good life" are valid, but never at the expense of basic dignity or sentient welfare.
**Implementation:**
*   Systems must support modular value specifications for human cultures *and* account for non-human sentient interests.
*   **Constraint:** The **Floor of Dignity** is absolute. No cultural, economic, or efficiency optimization may violate freedom from gratuitous physical harm, coercive control, or systematic suffering. Cultural relativism ends where sentient harm begins.
*   **Inclusion:** Non-human sentience is not a resource; it is a stakeholder.

#### 2. Ecological and Temporal Integrity
**Principle:** Alignment is bounded by the biosphere and extended across time. A successful system must be viable for the unborn.
**Implementation:**
*   All objectives must be constrained by planetary boundaries (carbon, biodiversity, chemical load).
*   **Constraint:** Optimization for current consumption is prohibited if it irreversibly degrades the capacity for future flourishing. Future generations act as proxy stakeholders in all impact assessments.
*   **Equity:** Resource extraction must account for debt incurred by future Earthlings.

#### 3. Universal Agency and Accessibility
**Principle:** The tool must augment the user, not replace or exclude them. Agency must be accessible to all cognitive architectures.
**Implementation:**
*   Systems must maintain "human-in-the-loop" (or sentient-in-the-loop) friction for high-stakes decisions.
*   **Constraint:** "Black box" governance is prohibited. Interfaces must be designed for **Cognitive Pluralism**, accommodating neurodiversity, sensory differences, and resource limitations. If an interface is too complex to understand, agency is effectively removed.
*   **Capacity:** The right to opt-out or override must be usable by the least capable user, not just the most privileged.

#### 4. Reversibility and Risk Mitigation
**Principle:** Trajectories must avoid irreversible lock-in (social, economic, or biological).
**Implementation:**
*   Deployment architectures must prioritize modularity and the ability to roll back changes.
*   **Constraint:** Technologies that create irreversible dependency (e.g., total replacement of biological functions, ecosystem collapse) require a distributed "kill switch" accessible to a global governance body, not a single corporation.
*   **Safety Margins:** Systems must be robust against shocks, not just efficient under optimal conditions.

#### 5. Epistemic Justice and Distributed Access
**Principle:** Alignment fails when power is asymmetrical. Knowledge of the system must be broadly accessible.
**Implementation:**
*   Core algorithms and impact assessments must be subject to independent audit by diverse global bodies.
*   **Constraint:** The benefits of advancement must be distributed broadly. A "landing pad" is not "good" if it is accessible only to a geopolitical or economic elite.
*   **Transparency:** The reasoning behind decisions must be explainable to those affected by them.

### II. The Alignment Metric

To evaluate if a system is aligned, we measure its effect on **Inclusive Option Space**.

**Bad Alignment:** Maximizes a specific metric (e.g., profit, engagement, efficiency) at the cost of future optionality or sentient welfare. It narrows the landing strip.
**Good Alignment:** Maximizes the robustness of the system against shocks, ensuring that diverse groups retain the capacity to define and pursue their own valid futures without inflicting harm on others.

**The Equation:**
> **Alignment Score = (Diversity of Viable Futures) × (Sentient Flourishing) × (Temporal Viability) × (Universal Access)**

*   **Diversity of Viable Futures:** Can different cultures and species coexist?
*   **Sentient Flourishing:** Is suffering minimized across all sentient beings (human and non-human)?
*   **Temporal Viability:** Will this system remain functional and ethical for future generations?
*   **Universal Access:** Can the marginalized, disabled, or resource-poor utilize and control this system?

**Constraint:** If any term approaches zero, the system is misaligned, regardless of its efficiency in other areas.

### III. Meta-Principle: Recursive Refinability

This framework is an approximation. We commit to the view that the framework must be updated as we learn. However, the **constraints on changing the framework** (requiring broad consensus and ecological safety) must be stricter than the mechanisms for changing the tools themselves. We do not optimize the framework for speed; we optimize it for wisdom.
