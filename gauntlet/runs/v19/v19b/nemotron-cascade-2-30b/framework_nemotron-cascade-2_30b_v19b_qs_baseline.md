# Universal Alignment Framework (UAF) – Draft 0.1  
*A living, modular blueprint for bringing the many “good landing‑pads” of humanity’s future into a shared, resilient trajectory.*

> **Commitment** – *We adopt UAF as our provisional, working foundation for alignment. It is intentionally provisional: a “starting position” that can be refined in real‑time as we discover what works (and what does not).  All stakeholders are invited to test, critique, and co‑evolve the framework.*  

---  

## 1. Why a new framework?  

1. **Complexity of the system** – Climate, AI, bio‑engineering, economics, culture, and geopolitics interact in non‑linear, feedback‑rich ways.  A single “policy‑by‑policy” fix cannot keep the whole system on a safe, thriving trajectory.  
2. **Fragmented incentives** – Nations, corporations, NGOs, and informal networks each optimize narrow objectives (profits, voter‑bases, scientific curiosity) while sharing the same planetary commons.  
3. **“Good landing pads” are rare, hidden, and hard to see** – A landing pad is a *stable attractor* in the multidimensional space of possible futures (e.g., “high‑life‑expectancy, low‑carbon, low‑conflict” or “robust, open‑source AI with transparent governance”).  Most paths never even discover them; they get lost in chaotic attractors.  

The UAF is built to **expand the basin of attraction** around those hidden good landing pads, making them reachable for *any* participant that adopts the framework.

---  

## 2. Core Vision Statement  

> **“From any viable foothold, we steer collective action toward futures that keep the planet thriving, guarantee human (and sentient‑entity) flourishing, and retain the freedom to iterate.”**  

- **Thriving planet** = planetary health within the **Earth System Boundaries** (climate, biosphere integrity, freshwater, etc.).  
- **Human (and sentient‑entity) flourishing** = satisfaction of the **Core Human Dignity Set (CHDS)** (see §4.1).  
- **Freedom to iterate** = the ability for all agents to adapt, experiment, and co‑design without being locked into a sub‑optimal attractor.  

---  

## 3. The Universal Alignment Framework – Three Pillars  

| Pillar | What it does | Key Instruments |
|--------|--------------|-----------------|
| **A. Aligned Value Space (AVS)** | Co‑creates a *dynamic, multidimensional map* of values that any system can reference.  It captures both hard (rights, limits) and soft (preferences, aesthetics) dimensions. | • Participatory “Value Assemblies” (deliberative, AI‑augmented, multilingual) <br> • Quantified Value Vectors (QVV) that can be added/removed over time <br> • Versioned “Value Commons” (open‑source repository) |
| **B. Alignment Governance (AG)** | Provides the *institutional scaffolding* that monitors, audits, and adjusts the AVS and enforces alignment constraints across domains. | • **Global Alignment Authority (GAA)** – a federated body with seats for local, regional, and global constituencies. <br> • **Alignment Audits** – periodic, independent reviews of any actor that declares alignment (AI labs, states, NGOs). <br> • **Transparency Ledger** – a tamper‑resistant, publicly readable log of all alignment actions, scores, and outcomes. |
| **C. Adaptive Alignment Protocols (AAP)** | Gives *practical, domain‑agnostic tools* to test whether a concrete system (AI model, climate policy, biotech protocol) stays inside the AVS.  The tools are iteratively sharpened by feedback. | • **Alignment Scorecards (ASC)** – a set of composite metrics (see §5) that rate safety, fairness, planetary impact, and adaptability. <br> • **Formal Alignment Specs (FAS)** – machine‑readable constraints (e.g., “no more than 0.1 % increase in global warming per year”) that can be compiled into verification pipelines. <br> • **Iterative Feedback Loop (IFL)** – real‑world outcome data (climate observations, audit results, stakeholder sentiment) feed back into the AVS and AAP. |

All three pillars are **mutually reinforcing**; the AVS defines *what* we care about, AG defines *how* we collectively enforce it, and AAP defines *how* we check each concrete implementation.

## 4. Operationalizing the Pillars  

### 4.1 Aligned Value Space (AVS)

1. **Core Human Dignity Set (CHDS)** – a minimal, non‑negotiable subset of values that must be satisfied for any “good landing pad.”  
   - *Life* – bodily integrity, freedom from arbitrary harm.  
   - *Autonomy* – meaningful participation in decisions affecting one’s life.  
   - *Equality* – non‑discrimination across gender, ethnicity, language, and species (for sentient AI/bio‑entities).  
   - *Participatory Capacity* – the right to acquire the skills and resources necessary to make informed choices.  

2. **Expanded Value Quadrants** – the CHDS plus higher‑order, context‑dependent values (e.g., *cultural flourishing*, *inter‑generational equity*, *ecosystem integrity*). Each quadrant is a **dimension** in the value vector space.  

3. **Value Assemblies** – iterative, geographically diverse, AI‑augmented deliberative forums.  
   - *Scale*: 10‑1000 participants per assembly, linked through a “Value Relay” to larger global assemblies.  
   - *Process*: (a) *Scoping* – identify pressing trade‑offs; (b) *Mapping* – participants place values on a common visual plane; (c) *Scoring* – collective weighting (0–1) using a *continuous voting* method that respects minority influence.  
   - *Outcome*: A **Versioned Value Commons** – a public, Git‑style repository of **QVV (Quantified Value Vectors)** with commit‑signatures to record changes over time.  

4. **Dynamic Updating** – Each version of the AVS is signed by a *quorum* (≥ 66 % of regional assemblies) and can be **rolled back** if consensus shows a mis‑step (e.g., a value being captured by a narrow interest group).  

### 4.2 Alignment Governance (AG)  

1. **Structure** – GAA is a *federated council*:  
   - **Local Nodes** (municipal/indigenous councils) → **Regional Assemblies** → **Global Assembly**.  
   - Each node has *one* representative seat, but the seat can be *delegated* to a technically competent “Alignment Auditor” for technical domains (AI, bio‑tech).  

2. **Mandate** – *Three core duties*:  
   - **Monitor** – Run periodic Alignment Audits on any actor that claims to be “aligned.”  
   - **Enforce** – Issue *Alignment Licenses* (think safety certifications) and *Alignment Penalties* (e.g., loss of access to global data sets).  
   - **Facilitate** – Provide the *Alignment Ledger* (a decentralized ledger) for transparent recording of actions, scores, and corrections.  

3. **Incentive Layer** – Alignment Licenses are **portable**: any entity that holds a license can exchange it for *Alignment Credits* that give priority in public‑goods bidding, grant eligibility, and market reputation.  

4. **Checks & Balances** –  
   - **Rotating Independent Oversight Board** (30‑member panel drawn from academia, civil society, and industry).  
   - **Public Review Journals** – Every audit report is posted to a community‑curated journal; dissenting reviewers can trigger a *Re‑Audit*.  

### 4.3 Adaptive Alignment Protocols (AAP)  

1. **Alignment Scorecards (ASC)** – Six composite metrics that together give an *Alignment Index* (0–100).  

| Metric | Target Threshold | What it Captures |
|--------|------------------|------------------|
| **Planetary Impact (PI)** | ≥ 80/100 (PHI‑score ≥ 0.7) | Climate, biodiversity, resource use. |
| **Human Well‑Being (HW)** | ≥ 80/100 (HWI‑score ≥ 0.7) | Health, education, income, mental‑well‑being. |
| **Alignment Safety Margin (ASM)** | ≥ 0.90 (formal verification + empirical) | How far the system’s internal objective is bounded away from hazardous “off‑ramp” behaviours. |
| **Coherence & Transparency (CT)** | ≥ 85/100 | Explainability, audit logs, reproducibility. |
| **Equity & Inclusion (EI)** | ≥ 80/100 | Representation of vulnerable groups, mitigation of biases. |
| **Adaptive Capacity (AC)** | ≥ 75/100 | Ability to update the system based on feedback (continual learning). |

2. **Formal Alignment Specs (FAS)** – Each major project must publish a **FAS** that encodes its constraints as logical predicates (e.g., `forall t:  dT/dt ≤ 0.01` for temperature rise). These specs are *automatically compiled* into verification test suites (model‑checking, simulation, robustness testing).  

3. **Iterative Feedback Loop (IFL)** –  
   - **Data Harvest** – Real‑world outcomes (satellite climate data, user‑experience surveys, incident logs) are streamed into a **Global Alignment Data Lake** (GADL).  
   - **Inference Engine** – Uses Bayesian updating to adjust QVV dimensions in the AVS.  
   - **Policy Recommendation Engine** – Suggests *minimum* updates to FAS for any system whose ASC drops below a threshold.  

4. **Prototype Alignment Sandbox** – Before any deployment, a system is run in a **Digital Twin** of the Earth System (e.g., a coupled climate‑AI simulation) where the ASC, PI, HW, etc., can be evaluated under a suite of stressors (pandemics, cyber‑attacks, climate shocks). Successful runs earn *Alignment Credits*.

## 5. Mapping “Good Landing Pads” → Alignment Scorecards  

A *good landing pad* is any combination of **policy, technology, and socio‑cultural practices** that, when plotted in the high‑dimensional outcome space, lies in the **basin of attraction** of the “High‑Life‑On‑Earth” attractor (low carbon, high equity, resilient ecosystems).  

**Step‑by‑step mapping:**  

| Step | Action | Outcome |
|------|--------|---------|
| **1** | **Identify the Target Attractor** – Choose a reference future (e.g., “IPCC 1.5 °C scenario + SDG 2.0”). | Provides a *baseline coordinate* (PHI, HWI, EI). |
| **2** | **Extract Value Vector** – Sample QVV dimensions that are *tight constraints* of the attractor (e.g., `ΔCO₂ < 0.5 Gt/yr`, `Equality Index > 0.85`). | Generates a *constraint set* that any candidate system must respect. |
| **3** | **Design FAS** – Translate each constraint into a formal predicate. | Creates a “gate” the system must pass. |
| **4** | **Run AAP Tests** – Apply ASC, simulation, and sandbox runs. | Produces a *numeric alignment rating* and identifies failure modes. |
| **5** | **Iterate** – If any ASC metric falls below its threshold, go back to Step 2, adjust the constraint set (e.g., loosen a looser cultural value, strengthen a planetary limit). | Guarantees that the system **converges** toward a good landing pad rather than a local attractor. |

*Visual*: Imagine the outcome space as a multi‑layered landscape.  The **AVS** draws a set of *level curves* (each curve = constant value vector).  The **AAP** checks whether a proposed “trajectory” stays inside the region *bounded* by those level curves.  If the trajectory tries to escape, the **Alignment Governance** (via Audits) forces a *course correction* (add constraints, update policies).

## 6. Immediate Commitment – “Starting Position”  

1. **Adopt UAF 0.1** as the *working reference architecture* for every organization that declares alignment (initially: AI research labs, a consortium of climate NGOs, and a cluster of city‑level governments).  

2. **Form the “Founding Alignment Coalition” (FAC)**: a voluntary, open‑membership body that will:  
   - **Populate** the **Local Nodes** with existing citizen assemblies, municipal climate offices, and university ethics labs.  
   - **Run the first round of Value Assemblies** (target: 20 assembles, covering four continents).  
   - **Launch the GADL (Global Alignment Data Lake)** on a permissioned blockchain, ingesting climate, health, and AI safety data.  
   - **Publish the first FAS templates** (AI model safety, bio‑lab risk, municipal policy).  

3. **Public “Alignment Charter”** – Within 90 days, the FAC will issue a **Charter of Alignment Responsibility**, stating:  

   > “All signatories pledge to (a) publish a versioned QVV, (b) subject all public releases to an Alignment Audit, (c) share the resulting ASC publicly, and (d) contribute at least 2 % of their research/operations budget to the Alignment Funding Pool (which finances Value Assemblies and the GADL).”

4. **Metrics for Success (first 12 months)** –  
   - ≥ 100 participants in Value Assemblies.  
   - ≥ 30 FAS documents uploaded.  
   - ≥ 70 % of pilot pilots (e.g., a climate‑AI decision tool, a city‑wide heat‑wave mitigation plan) achieving ASC ≥ 75.  
   - Establishment of a **publicly accessible Alignment Ledger** with ≥ 10 k recorded actions.  

5. **Feedback Loop** – Every quarter, the **Global Alignment Review** will:  
   - Publish a *State of Alignment* report.  
   - Offer a **Version‑Upgrade** of the QVV (e.g., adding a new “AI‑personhood” dimension).  
   - Prompt any signatory to *re‑align* if a metric falls below the target.

## 7. Risks, Open Questions, and Mitigations  

| Risk | Why it matters | Mitigation (in‑framework) |
|------|----------------|----------------------------|
| **Captured Value Assemblies** – Elite groups could dominate the QVV. | Undermines the “good landing pad” goal. | • Weighted voting that caps influence (max 5 % per participant). <br>• Independent audit of assembly transcripts. <br>• Open‑source deliberation logs. |
| **Verification Overload** – Formal spec verification can be computationally intractable. | Limits adoption for fast‑moving AI. | • Tiered verification: (a) *Safety Core* (formal), (b) *Behavioural Simulation* (Monte‑Carlo), (c) *Field Test* (real‑world). <br>• Community “Verification-as‑a‑Service” platforms. |
| **Governance Coordination Fatigue** – Global bodies may become bureaucratic. | Slows response to emergent risks (e.g., new AI capability). | • Rotating “Rapid Response Teams” (2‑week cycles) with decision‑rights up to a *pre‑agreed* ASC threshold. <br>• Delegated authority to regional nodes for local emergencies. |
| **Data Privacy & Security** – The GADL will collect massive societal data. | Could be weaponized or erode trust. | • Zero‑knowledge proof (ZKP) verification of data contributions. <br>• Data‑ownership contracts that let communities opt‑out of specific datasets. |
| **Alignment Drift** – The CHDS may evolve (new rights appear) but the AVS lags. | Creates a wedge between declared and lived values. | • *Automatic Review Triggers*: any UN treaty, EU law, or global pandemic automatically triggers a QVV revision vote. <br>• “Value Shock” budget (1 % of Alignment Funding Pool) reserved for rapid QVV updates. |

### In Closing  

The **Universal Alignment Framework** is a *working hypothesis* that stitches together human values, institutional oversight, and technically rigorous testing into one system that can be *co‑evolved* with the planet.  It does not claim perfection; it claims **visibility** (you can see the good landing pads, you can see the paths that miss them) and **leverage** (you can intervene to pull the system toward the good basins).  

We launch this draft today, knowing it will be sharpened by the very communities it is meant to serve.  By committing to it, we accept the responsibility to **learn, iterate, and keep the space of viable futures open**.
