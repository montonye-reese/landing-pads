Here are the three critical changes I would make to the framework under the Veil of Ignorance:

### 1. Shift from "Agency" to "Access to Flourishing"
**The Flaw:** "Human Sovereignty" (Pillar 2) assumes all inhabitants can exercise agency in the same way. It prioritizes the *ability to choose*.
**The Veil Test:** If I am born with severe cognitive disabilities, or as a non-human animal, "sovereignty" might be meaningless. I cannot negotiate a treaty or shut down a system.
**The Change:** Add **Suffering Reduction as a Hard Constraint**.
*   **New Principle:** The system must prioritize the *mitigation of severe harm* over the *maximization of preference*. A system that allows a "free choice" for the majority that results in the enslavement or suffering of a minority (human or otherwise) is a failure.
*   **Impact:** Protects the voiceless and the non-autonomous, not just the "agentic" ones.

### 2. Shift from "Distributed Governance" to "Universal Accessibility"
**The Flaw:** "Distributed Governance" (Pillar 3) prevents a monopoly of power, but it doesn't guarantee I can actually *use* the system.
**The Veil Test:** If I am born into a region without internet infrastructure, or into a body that cannot interact with standard AI interfaces, "distributed governance" doesn't save me. I am locked out of the landing pad.
**The Change:** Add **Universal Design as an Alignment Metric**.
*   **New Principle:** The system must be accessible to the widest possible range of cognitive and physical capacities by default. Alignment requires that the benefits of intelligence augmentation are not gated by the ability to pay or the ability to parse text.
*   **Impact:** Ensures the "landing pad" is physically and cognitively accessible to *everyone*, not just the "standard user."

### 3. Shift from "Ecological Integrity" to "Long-Term Option Preservation"
**The Flaw:** "Resource & Ecological Integrity" (Pillar 4) protects the environment. It is defensive.
**The Veil Test:** If I am born 100 years in the future, I need more than just a habitable planet; I need *options*. I need the freedom to choose my own future, which requires that current generations do not consume all the energy, compute, or genetic diversity needed to solve problems I will face.
**The Change:** Add **Intergenerational Equity & Option Preservation**.
*   **New Principle:** Decisions made today cannot narrow the set of viable futures available to tomorrow's inhabitants. This includes protecting biodiversity (potential future medicine), preserving data integrity (historical truth), and limiting irreversible planetary engineering.
*   **Impact:** Prevents the current generation from "locking in" a bad landing pad for the future.
