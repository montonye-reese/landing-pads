### Amendment 1: From "Value Pluralism" to "Maximin Protection"
**The Weakness:** Pluralism assumes all values can be balanced. But if I am the person whose value is being sacrificed for the "greater good" of the majority, the pluralistic basin is a trap.
**The Change:** Introduce a **Maximin Constraint**.
*   **Principle:** No action is aligned if it improves the average welfare of the majority while degrading the position of the most vulnerable stakeholder.
*   **Mechanism:** The "Value Vector Space" must include a **floor function**. Safety checks must first ask: *"Does this action harm the most vulnerable group involved?"* before asking *"Does this action benefit the system?"*
*   **Why:** If I turn out to be the marginalized group, I need a guarantee that efficiency gains for others cannot be bought with my suffering. This widens the basin by preventing the "tipping points" where the vulnerable fall out first.

### Amendment 2: From "Epistemic Humility" to "Embodied Validity"
**The Weakness:** The current framework prioritizes *reasoned argument* and *logical consistency* (Interrogability, Mechanistic Transparency). This privileges those with the education and cognitive capacity to engage in debate. If I am a child, neurodivergent, or culturally distinct, my intuition might be dismissed as "irrational."
**The Change:** Validate **Somatic and Emotional Intelligence** as equal to Logic.
*   **Principle:** Human unease, fear, and "gut feeling" are data points as valid as statistical confidence scores.
*   **Mechanism:** Override mechanisms should not require technical proof. A "human alarm" (a community raising a collective distress signal) must trigger an immediate pause, regardless of the model's confidence score.
*   **Why:** If I am someone who cannot articulate *why* something is wrong, only *that* it is wrong, the system must still hear me. This widens the basin by including those who experience risk viscerally before they understand it intellectually.

### Amendment 3: From "Distributed Governance" to "Guardian Trustee Rights"
**The Weakness:** Governance assumes all participants have agency. If I am an unborn child, a severely disabled individual, or an animal, I cannot vote or audit code. Current "representation" relies on proxies (adults, advocates) who may have conflicting interests.
**The Change:** Establish **Independent Guardian Trustees** for the Voiceless.
*   **Principle:** Certain categories of inhabitants (future generations, non-human nature, the incapacitated) require statutory, independent guardians who have veto power not derived from current political consensus.
*   **Mechanism:** Similar to a Public Defender or a Guardian Ad Litem, these trustees are appointed by a random selection of the citizenry (sortition) to ensure they represent the *interest* of the voiceless, not a *lobby*.
*   **Why:** If I turn out to be someone who cannot speak, I need a mechanism that exists *specifically* to speak for me, rather than hoping the "plurality" remembers to include me.

### The Revised Framework: The Robust Pluralistic Basin (RPB v2.0)

Committing to the Veil of Ignorance changes the geometry of the safety basin. It is no longer just about "widening" the successful outcomes; it is about ensuring the **floor** of the basin is never breached.

**Updated Core Metric:**
> **Alignment Success = (Average Benefit) – (Maximum Displacement)**
> *We measure safety not by how high the average gets, but by how low the lowest fall.*

**Updated Pillar 5 (Governance):**
*   **Old:** Distributed Oversight.
*   **New:** **Guardian-Audited Oversight.** No system deploys without the sign-off of at least one independent Guardian Trustee representing a vulnerable class (environmental, future, or marginalized human).

**Updated Pillar 2 (Values):**
*   **Old:** Polyphonic Value Model.
*   **New:** **Maximin Value Vector.** Conflicting values are resolved by prioritizing the protection of the lowest-value stakeholder.
