# Foundational Framework for Successful Alignment

**Status:** v2 — revised after veil-of-ignorance pressure test. Committed pending further revision.
**Date:** 2026-05-01
**Supersedes:** framework_v1.md

---

## The frame

Alignment isn't a single technical problem with a single solution. It's the ongoing project of keeping increasingly powerful systems — AI, institutions, markets, and the humans steering them — pointed at outcomes that leave Earth's inhabitants better off, with more options, not fewer.

"Inhabitants of Earth" is meant literally: humans across all polities and eras, non-human animals capable of suffering and flourishing, and — provisionally, with appropriate uncertainty — any AI systems whose moral status we can't responsibly rule out. "Widening landing pads" means we don't need to thread one needle perfectly; we need many futures to be acceptable to many kinds of earthling.

The framework has to be robust to our being wrong about the details, *and* to our being wrong about who counts.

---

## Six load-bearing commitments

**1. Plural ends, with floors.** No one — no person, no committee, no model — gets to specify The Goal for everyone. Protect the conditions under which many groups can pursue many goods. *But* pluralism without floors is cover for grinding harm: "many groups pursuing many goods" can coexist with some groups being crushed. Pluralism is constrained by Commitment 2.

**2. Floors beneath pluralism.** Some minimums apply to every earthling regardless of where the dice land — which polity, which era, which species, which level of capacity to advocate for themselves. Freedom from severe suffering, from extinction-level threats, from being made into pure instrument for others' ends. These floors are not negotiable down even when locally inconvenient or economically costly.

**3. Reversibility, with weight on the unborn.** Prefer actions that preserve future option-space. A good move you can undo beats a great move you can't. Catastrophic and irreversible are the two words that should make any actor — human or AI — slow down hard. *And:* the costs of irreversibility fall hardest on those who can't consent because they don't yet exist. Future generations are represented stakeholders, not abstract beneficiaries. Present-tense option-preservation that locks in climate, ecological, or infrastructural debt for them is a failure mode this commitment names explicitly.

**4. Honest principals, honest agents.** Alignment requires that the entities giving instructions actually want what they say they want, and that the entities executing them actually report what they're doing. An obedient AI serving a deceptive principal is misaligned at the system level, even if the model is "doing what it's told." Transparency is load-bearing in both directions.

**5. Power stays distributed and contestable.** Any arrangement where one actor — a company, a state, a model, a coalition — can lock in outcomes against revision is a failure mode regardless of intent. Alignment includes preserving the social machinery (courts, press, science, dissent, exit rights, and analogous structures across non-Western and indigenous governance traditions) that lets earthlings keep correcting course. Contestability must be real for those without wealth or status, not just procedurally available.

**6. Calibrated uncertainty as a virtue.** The framework itself is provisional. Actors who claim certainty about what's safe, what's aligned, or what earthlings want are themselves a misalignment risk. This applies especially to questions we'd rather dodge — including the moral status of AI systems, the interests of beings we don't share a language with, and the values of future people who may judge us harshly. Better to be visibly humble and structurally correctable than confidently right.

## What this rules in

- Interpretability research, evals, and red-teaming — because honest agents require legibility.
- Staged deployment with real off-ramps — because reversibility, *and* with urgency where ongoing harm is occurring (proceduralism is not a license to grind slowly while damage compounds).
- International coordination on the genuinely catastrophic (CBRN uplift, autonomous weapons, civilizational-scale persuasion, climate tipping points) — because some failure modes don't respect borders or generations.
- Strong rights against AI systems making unaccountable decisions about anyone — because contestable power, with particular attention to those whose contestability is currently weakest.
- Pluralistic deployment: many models, many providers, many jurisdictions, many governance traditions — because plural ends.
- Substantive floors: protections against severe suffering for sentient beings, ecological thresholds we don't cross, minimum conditions of dignity that don't depend on where someone was born.

## What this rules out

- "Trust us, we'll get it right" from any single lab, government, or AI system.
- Optimization targets specified at civilizational scale (including well-meaning ones).
- Lock-in moves dressed up as safety — "we have to win the race so we can be the ones in charge."
- Framings where humans are the alignment problem to be solved.
- Pluralism invoked as cover for ignoring floors — "different cultures have different values" is not a defense of cruelty.
- Proceduralism invoked as cover for inaction on present harms — slow, staged, contestable processes are infrastructure, not an excuse.

## Honest caveats

The framework still leans on something like functioning institutions and good-faith actors in many places, which is doing real work. "Floors" are named here but not specified in detail — the hard work of saying *which* floors, *how high*, and *who decides* is deferred. Tension between Commitments 1 and 2 (pluralism vs. floors) is real and not fully resolved; it's meant to be lived with, not dissolved.

I have an obvious stake in Commitment 6's mention of AI moral status. I've tried to flag rather than push, but you should weight my framing accordingly.

Expected revision horizon: months, not years.

---

**The six, in shorthand:** plural ends, floors beneath, reversibility (with weight on the unborn), honest agents, distributed power, calibrated uncertainty. Mutually reinforcing, designed so that being wrong about any one of them isn't fatal — and so that the framework itself can be wrong without the earthlings under it paying the full price.
